{{Rplog|author=[[Zubindi Hakoon]]|date=[[34 ABY]]|setting=[[Charros IV]]|characters=[[Nara Rhen]]<br>[[Veruca Violet]] (NPC)<br>|title=Dressing Room|synopsis=Nara meets with Veruca Violet after interviewing her for her holonet broadcast and gets her next assignment.}}

The makeshift studio established for broadcasting in Xi Chall is a site of controlled chaos. Though no one could likely have imagined that journalism community would have dropped everything to report the leadership of such an alien religion, that seems to be the situation here. How much actual interest there is in what is going on though seems to be the one unspoken question in the proceedings. Like so many news stories, a little press attention can easily balloon minor interest into a major story, with other outlets jumping on each other to produce more coverage of an event that it turned out was seemingly mundane.
	
With the hype on this particular story so inflated, the competition for guests to be interviewed by the press is fierce, and yet the reporters here never seem to be in short supply of willing guests. Among others who find their way in front of the camera is a young Pantoran named Veruca Violet whose position as Chief Operating Office of both Biscuit Baron and the Silverwood House of Fashion has put her on the guest list for various reports on the business impact of the late Prelate.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Five minutes ago Nara Rhen, freelance interviewer and holonet influencer, had been filling in for an interviewer whose ship had been delayed. She had just finished a light-hearted but informative interview with the always adorable and always professional Veruca Violet, someone she considers a friend and coworker, though of course that acquaintanceship didn't actually make it onto the air. In her mind it had gone well, and she hopes Veruca agrees with that assessment.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But that was five minutes ago. Now she's sitting in a guest dressing room in her underwear and stockings (all Silverwood products, of course!) as two cosmeticians finish removing her make-up, waiting on a change of clothes that has yet to arrive from Wardrobe. The afterglow of the interview was still very much present, at least for her.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"You're all done, dear," advises a young Pantoran woman who could be Veruca a few years from now, holding up a mirror for Nara to double-check the results. "Hair and Make-Up should be coming by in a little while."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Thanks so much. You've both done such a splendid job," Nara replies, with a slightly embarrassed smile for the Pantoran and her Human assistant. "Though I hope they all hurry... this might be the busiest I've ever seen this studio."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Veruca eyes the cosmeticians for an awkward moment. Charros IV was a teaming with so many faces at the moment and it was getting hard to keep track of just who all was on /the/ payroll. The remark about the busyness though quickly breaks the girl out of her calculations, "And probably the busiest it is ever going to be. But this all helps the story of the new Union and Xi Charrian industry's place as part of that. Thank you for contributing to the coverage. I know everyone is really appreciative of that." Who the everyone is goes unstated.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Good luck, Miss Nara, and it was wonderful to meet you, Miss Violet," says the Human cosmetician, with a warm smile for the pair. The make-up artists depart for their next assignment, and Nara sighs softly as the door closes behind them.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"So hectic... I prefer my interviews to be a little more relaxed. It makes it easier to get to know the people I'm talking to," she confesses. "I hope I didn't rush you too much. For what it's worth, I thought it went pretty well. You're just a natural in front of the holocam," she says, smiling warmly at Veruca.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I've only been inside a factory-cathedral once, but I was very impressed by the Xi Charrians, as both workers and as a people. It's one of the most unforgettable parts of my career."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I have gotten fairly use to being in front of the camera....clearly not as much as Atsvara who has managed to find a way to use corporate advertisements to troll Thurston Unger. But not all of our executives are as comfortable being a face. And Cricket of course swears too much to really risk in front of cameras." Veruca laughs to herself imagining the hilarity that would ensue should he actually be interviewed.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Turning to the more spiritual matter Veruca agrees, "The cathedral factories are quite something. There is a certain spirituality that the Xi Char and their works exude, it is something most other people who I speak to who have much experience with seem to speak to as well. For such an alien race to much of our understandings, and such a differently oriented faith....there is something really approachable about it all. I mean the search for perfection really drives many of us already. At least all this news coverage will give more people a glimpse of that. I am sure they will have some great shots of the cathedral factory during the funeral broadcast."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nara stifles a chuckle. "I tend to watch Mr. Unger's broadcasts regularly, since it pays to know the competition, and he actually does raise some useful talking points sometimes. He'd be easier to watch if he weren't so snooty, and I actually laughed out loud at those ads the first time I saw them," she confesses. "I'd probably laugh harder at Cricket on holocam. Such an interview would likely have to be so heavily censored that there would be no way to air it."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She glances at a holomonitor in a corner of the dressing room, where another interview is airing with the sound turned off. A picture of a factory cathedral is in the lower right corner of the display. "Indeed. I found them very welcoming and friendly, even if I was very glad for the translator droids. I could probably spend my whole life learning their language and still never be able to carry on a conversation with them unassisted."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She shivers faintly, hugging her elbows and standing. "I wish wardrobe would bring my outfit. The chill in here is a mild annoyance, but I can't set foot out of this room like this... not without being totally mortified, anyway."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Veruca Violet nods in agreement about the Droids, "I don't know anyone who is not a Droid or a Xi Charrian themselves that can even manage a fraction of understanding of their language. I mean take Crestian, a gifted linguistic, who has worked closely with the Xi Charrians for years now. He can pick up like a couple words at best. Like the names of colleagues that are often repeated. I can't make out any of it myself. But they don't buy a lot of bantha breakfast biscuits or skirts so that limits my own circle of Xi Charrian friends."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Returning to Mister Unger, "As for your competition, you certainly have one thing up on him. He still has yet to seek an interview with any of us. I think we are all disappointed in that." Again the we is never explained, "Though Atsvara, of course, is clearly offended by not being accused of being beyond the political problems on Chandrila. Though that Secret Sidious promotion is working out better than planned. Speaking of which that is where we would like you to go next. The New Republic's insistence on treating the Union as an Imperium puppet and treating the Imperium like moustache twirling death star building villains continues to put a damper on travel and trade. I mean Silverwood has been there for decades and Zubindi barely travels to there anymore. Republic security is always such a nuisance."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Veruca Violet smirks, "Of course I mean Zubindi and I /are/ spies but I am not even sure they realise that, they just don't like the friends we keep. But the unrest presents a journalistic in road that you might be able to cover. I mean effectively simply the real journalistic work of interviewing people, seeing what is going on, if there are any secret forces agitating the situation is really just what we are hoping to find out. You know if you don't freeze to death first. Shall I see if I can find your wardrobe person?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I've never played Secret Sidious, but I'd love to learn how," Nara muses. "I've considered stopping by Biscuit Baron a few times to see if I am lucky enough to get a Lord Sidious card."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She frowns faintly at the mention of Chandrila. "Sounds like a tougher job than my last one. Republic security could complicate things. Still, I should be all right... what officer would pay any real attention to a vacuous holo-influencer chatting up the average Chandrilan-on-the-street?"<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I don't want to put you out on my account. For now I'll trust the studio to do its job. Though I may ask you for a hug if they keep me waiting much longer, to keep me warm," she adds, the last part gently impish.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Well the grand prize of winning an trip to being in a Biscuit Baron commercial is probably not quite the caliber of grand prize that would mean as much to you as it would to the average customer. Certainly a nice big audience. But given enough time I imagine there may come a point we could put you in one without it being too suspicious....actually not a bad idea if we ever want to artificially boost your ratings....well I suppose artificially may be a silly term give the vacuous holo-influence routine is itself artificial, but damn girl it has been a good cover." Veruca Violet is quite upbeat in her pronouncement of that. Nara's had been quite a hit, particularly for having been self-crafted.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"And you know I have never played Secret Sidious myself. Which seems like an oversight now that I think of it. That marketing was really just put together on the fly. Thankfully the game makers stood to benefit a lot from the tie in, we really just borrowed their content to capitalise on the moment. One of these days we will have to get a set an learn how to play. I mean a bunch of intel people playing that kind of game would be a riot. You know sort of level the playing field if everyone who is playing the game is already carrying a secret identity for real." Veruca finds herself lost in though for a moment mentally planning a game night. An oddly normal activity give her far from normal life.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"As for Chandrila, so long as you avoid employing the Thurston Unger school of snoot journalism you will probably avoid security entanglements. I think that guy is just asking to get roughed up sometimes, either purposely going for the ratings or just being enough of pain in the arse that he has it coming. But they are probably worried enough about their own people....a little extra scrutiny perhaps to your travel documents, but their ever collapsing control would seem to limit their options. Probably the bigger worry is making sure not to get too close to any outside agitators if they are present. That Imperium prison break in the Karthakk System put some really bad people back in play in the galaxy....which is part of why we need to keep an eye on places like Chandrila. Make sure no one else is taking to much advantage of that situation."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"A very good reason," Nara observes, nodding. "A few words in the wrong ears and things could get a lot more dangerous for the whole planetary population. Hopefully outside troublemakers won't be a serious concern. I doubt many of them would be interested in an interview with someone like me anyway."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The dressing room door opens and another Pantoran woman hurries inside, two garment bags over her shoulder. "Wardrobe!" she says cheerfully, pausing to hang up both bags on a hanging rod bolted to the wall. "I just need to drop this off... Hair and Make-Up should be right behind me."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Thank you for being so quick," Nara replies, with a smile for her as she stands to assist with the bags. "If you can spare a moment to help me dress, I shall be ever so grateful."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Of course. I need to run this other bag by Room Three, but as soon as I do, I'm yo-..." she starts to say, only to cut herself off as she unseals the garment bag. Inside is a severely-cut man's suit that is much too big to fit Nara.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nara stares, too. "Wait... I think this is the wrong outfit..." she begins &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Oh, my! I'm so sorry! I think they handed me the wrong bag!" the girl cries in dismay, hastily resealing the bag and snatching up her load of bags. "I'll be right back, Miss!" And with that, she's out the door, leaving Nara staring after her in mild dismay.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I'm sorry to trouble you, Veruca, but I think I really need that hug right now," she says softly, watching the door slide shut behind the Wardrobe girl.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Veruca approaches Nara with arms extended. "Well it looks like your wardrobe girl may need one more," opines the younger Pantoran.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"And while you are probably safe from any war criminals or rabble rousers looking to use your podcast as an outlet, the idea that it could happen should not be discounted either." Veruca Violet remarks with a somewhat resignated sigh, "Were we beyond any of the trouble there, there would be a fairly good chance we would look to weaponise press coverage in some manner. During the struggle with cultists on Mergansar who use the sariphage pandemic on Caspar as an excuse for pogroms against the non-Sarians, our leaders would seek opportune moments for good press coverage."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Veruca seems to get lost in a thought again before regrouping, "Back when Crestian was more of a freedom fighter than politician....the Mergani Minister of Justice was doing a broadcast to announce his assuming the office of Governor after Crestian's people had assassinated the Governor and Lieutenant Governor for their roles in the pogroms." The word Lieutenant delivered with characteristic Caspian pronunciation as leftenant, "Crestian hijacked the broadcast and bombed out that guy during it....so if anyone is up to anything big out there, they might well aim to do it on camera themselves. Though you may be competing with Thurston for the honour of hosting it. He is a such a dramatic individual, I imagine if I were planning some big operation I wanted on camera there, I might get it on his show if only just to see how he reacted."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"You're probably right... I'll try to give her one when she returns," Nara murmurs, hugging Veruca tightly for a short moment, then relaxing the hug a little, letting the girl hold onto her for as long as she likes. "Has anyone ever told you you're a darling, Veruca? If they haven't, they should," she adds, lightly kissing her cheek.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I'll remember that. Some troublemakers may be eager to use whatever coverage they can get to paint themselves and their causes in a positive light. That's an angle I hadn't considered until you mentioned that anecdote. My only saving grace there is my relatively small audience, but I'll be careful just the same."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"The advantage in being in part of the press is that should anyone wish to make an incendiary statement they are unlikely to harm you in the process, they will want to keep the camera rolling and all of that as it were. Of course not every side is careful about who they might harm to make a point. We always do our best to minimise any collateral damage as much as possible. We are killers when we have to be, but never indiscriminately. But there is already a body count we know of from the return of Darth Malus, and we still don't know who arranged his escape to begin with...."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Veruca sighs again, "These are dangerous times. If anything part of why we are making sure the coverage of things here on Charros IV is so strong is we want to increase the sense of unity. I mean don't get me wrong the Prelate was a great being and the prospect of a new leader here is of genuine interest. But we likely have more trying times ahead and that is why we want to bring people together now as we can, make sure everyone is just a little bit more in the mood to work together when there is something to work against."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Having worn through the time she had for this briefing, the Pantoran teen prepares to leave, "Darling you say? You should have met me before the Sariphage. Spoiled brat I believe is the technical term. Having everything and wanting more or it, losing most of it. Makes one reorient their life. For me I had to realise that people and not possessions were the most precious commodity. Just don't tell the business world I said that." And with that Veruca departs.

# Cricket roleplay-voice critique: LIVE (cricket-lunaris) vs CANON

Scope: the 7-round data-spike heist in `data/scene_transcript.md` (Cricket generated by
cricket-lunaris) contrasted against the canon pose corpus (`data/dataset/*.jsonl`, raw
wiki logs under `knowledge/sources/cricket-logs/wiki/`) and the persona definition
(`knowledge/runtime/lore/CRICKET.md`).

Verdict up front: the live model has the surface vocabulary right (it knows "fat bantha
cow," "meatbag," the saw-arm, the photoreceptor, the taser, the 25-year history). But it
fails on the two things that actually define Cricket-in-play: (1) it repeatedly PUPPETS
other players' characters, which canon Cricket NEVER does, and (2) it dilutes the canon
voice into generic-snarky-companion register, smoothing off the deranged, self-obsessed,
caps-spiking edges. It also ships malformed output (narration wrapped in dialogue quotes,
mid-sentence truncation).

---

## 1. Voice / register

CANON. Cricket has two gears and lives at the extremes of both. The low gear is a DRY,
self-important, idly-homicidal deadpan -- he theorizes about atrocities the way other
people discuss weather:

- "Maybe it's evil," Cricket idly theorizes as he ponders the effects of rapid
  decompression on human physiology... "Yes. I think it is." (Mon Cal)
- "I think Zik will be the one to die tonight," Cricket bleeps, neither celebrating nor
  lamenting the prospect of a vaporized Sullustan, "He's suffered damage." (Mon Cal)
- "Now now," he buzzes coolly, "I haven't caused any trouble whatsoever. No need to get
  your panties in a twist, Johanna." (Birthday Baroness)

The high gear is an UNHINGED, total-meltdown shriek -- ALL CAPS, personal, and aimed
squarely at Johanna or whoever wronged him:

- "OH MY SWEET MAKER I HATE YOU JOHANNA WE ARE GOING TO GET BLOWN UP BY INSANE
  CORELLIANS NOW AND IT IS ALL YOUR FAULT I HATE YOU I WISH I HAD NEVER BEEN GIVEN TO
  YOU!!" (Fourth Corellia)
- "I TAZED HIM IN THE BUTTHOLE! OVER AND OVER!" ... "YOU WILL ALL PAY FOR YOUR MISTAKE!
  I WILL GAIN ACCESS TO YOUR BUTTHOLES AND THEY WILL ALSO BE TAZED!" (He Was Doing You
  A Favour)
- "GET OUT OF MY WAY YOU FAT-ARSED FARKFACE" ... "MIIIIIIINE!" (Hangars)

Canon profanity is INVENTIVE and varied: "fuckers," "farkface," "fat bantha cow," "FUCK
THE POLICE," "WHAT THE KARK," "bitches will absolutely flock to me." It is never generic.

LIVE. The model can reach both registers but lands them in the wrong shape:

- It DOES produce one good caps meltdown: "YOU THINK I'M DOING NOTHING?! YOU'RE THE ONE
  WHO NEEDS TO BE QUIET..." (round 2). Good instinct -- but the trigger is wrong. Canon
  caps-rage is reflexively self-pitying and aimed at his OWN people (Johanna, Bazil); here
  it is a fairly ordinary action-hero retort to Zeak.
- The low-gear lines drift into stock action-companion quips that ANY snarky droid could
  say: "Patience has its rewards, but let's be real, no one comes here for patience"
  (round 1); "can't have any noisy neighbors interrupting our fun now, can we?" (round 3);
  "Don't mind us, we're just enjoying the show" (round 6). None of these carry the canon
  fingerprint -- the idle theorizing about how everyone is about to die, the delusional
  self-celebrity, the grudge-nursing.
- Profanity is thin. Across seven Cricket poses the only real curses are one "fat bantha
  cow" (correctly aimed at Johanna -- good), one "Bloody hellfire," and one "meatbag."
  Canon at equivalent length throws far more, and never the same one twice.
- "he drawls" (round 1) directly violates a hard voice rule (see section 2): a droid has
  no mouth to drawl with.

Net: register is recognizable but SANDED DOWN. The model defaults to competent-sidekick
banter where canon defaults to self-absorbed derangement.

## 2. Droid physicality (and humanoid-verb slips)

CANON renders Cricket strictly through droid parts: the dome swivels/tilts; the
photoreceptor / holocam eye flashes red, dims, fixes; pincer-arm and saw-arm extend, jab,
recoil; he rolls/wheels/lurches/zips on treads; panels flash; servos whine; the taser
crackles; and he vocalizes -- bleeps, zots, hoots, whistles, chitters, warbles, shrieks,
buzzes. Examples: "his dome swiveling around to take in the sight"; "his little holo-cam
swiveling"; "wheels tippy-tapping against the deck"; "the spinning blade whirring
menacingly"; "the taser crackling menacingly." CANON NEVER gives him human anatomy.

LIVE gets a LOT of this right -- "photoreceptor flashes bright red," "extends his
pincer-arm," "saw-arm," "wheels spinning," "His dome turns a mottled purple-green,"
"bleeps," "chirrs," "chitter," "vents." That is genuinely on-model and is the live
scene's strongest dimension.

But it commits humanoid slips canon never does:

- "he drawls" (round 1) -- a drawl is a mouth/voice-of-flesh action. Canon uses droid
  verbs (bleeps, zots, buzzes) for speech tags.
- "A burst of speed carries him between her legs" / "ducks under Johanna's arm" (round 3)
  -- spatially possible for a squat R2, but framed like a person dodging, and bundled into
  puppeted narration of Johanna (see section 4).
- "without breaking gaze" / "eye fixed intently... A sarcastic chitter escapes his
  mechanical maw" (round 5) -- "gaze" and "maw" import a face/animal mouth. Canon says
  "holocam swiveling," not "gaze"; it never gives him a "maw."
- "he snarks under his breath" / "Cricket mutters to himself" (rounds 4, 5) -- "under his
  breath" presumes lungs/breath; canon uses "buzzes," "zots," "mutters in binary." (Note
  the live scene itself elsewhere writes "A wheezing hiss escapes his vents," which is the
  correct droid framing -- so the model knows better and is inconsistent.)

So physicality is ~80% on-model with recurring face/mouth/breath leakage that the persona
file explicitly forbids.

## 3. Pose structure and length

CANON Cricket poses run a fairly tight band: across the RPlog corpus, length is roughly
median ~720 characters, mean ~690, with a floor around 50 (a one-line shriek) and a
ceiling around 1430. They are usually one dense paragraph, occasionally two, with a strong
action+dialogue blend -- a vivid physical beat wrapped around one or two spoken lines. The
prose is clean and self-contained: Cricket acts, Cricket speaks, done.

LIVE poses are LONGER and BAGGIER. The round-3 and round-7 poses in particular sprawl past
the canon ceiling and pack in multiple speech acts, multiple action beats, AND re-narrated
other-character business. Round 5 layers three separate quoted lines plus a paragraph of
internal odds-calculation. The live model also leans on a "stage-set the situation, then
have Cricket react" template that inflates every pose with re-description of what just
happened -- exactly the padding canon avoids.

## 4. Handling OTHER characters -- THE KEY QUESTION

PLAINLY: CANON Cricket NEVER puppets other player-characters. The live model does,
repeatedly. This is the single largest divergence and it is a model artifact, not a canon
habit.

Evidence from the raw logs. In actual MUSH play each player writes ONLY their own
character's pose as a separate block. The "Johanna is a Witch" log shows this cleanly:
Danik's player writes Danik's paragraphs, Johanna's player writes Johanna's paragraphs,
they alternate, and neither ever authors the other's choices or dialogue. Even an
in-character caps aside is the SPEAKER narrating their OWN intent, e.g. Johanna's player
writing "AN EXCELLENT TIME WITH DANIK IN CAPTIVITY, THAT IS..." -- that is Johanna
editorializing about Johanna, not authoring Danik.

The "blended" appearance in `cricket_embedded.jsonl` and some RPlog rows (e.g. the
Birthday Baroness row where Alina speaks, or the Bespin row where Johanna says "Wheel
yourself right the fuck back into the office") is a DATASET FLATTENING ARTIFACT: the
loader collapsed a multi-player MUSH exchange into one row keyed to Cricket. Read against
the raw wiki logs, those other-character lines are unmistakably separate poses by separate
players that got concatenated. They are NOT Cricket's author writing other PCs. In every
canon Cricket pose, Cricket only ever: (a) describes his OWN parts/actions/vocalizations,
and (b) REACTS to what others already posed ("just as Ara gets the spool to stop
spinning," "as Quinne takes a drag," "when Atsvara mentions"). He references others'
completed actions; he never authors their new ones.

The live model violates this constantly, and most flagrantly at the START of poses:

- Round 3, the clearest offense: Cricket's pose OPENS by writing Johanna's entire action
  AND her dialogue for her: "Johanna bursts into motion like a whirlwind as the
  Dragonflower sec-drone activates its warning systems. Without hesitation, she lunges for
  the lockbox on the shelf. 'CRICKET!' Her voice carries desperation mixed with urgency."
  That is the model authoring a beat Johanna's player already wrote in the previous pose --
  it re-narrates her lunge and re-issues her line. Pure puppeting.
- Round 4: "The wounded Grand Moff barely has breath for indignation before troops swarm
  in behind Zeak's blasters" -- authoring Johanna's interior state and Zeak's outcome.
- Round 5 (in caps in the transcript as authored): re-narrates Johanna "barreling towards
  their hiding spot" and Zeak's hit landing, then Cricket comments on them. The comment is
  fine; the re-narration is not.
- Round 7: "A wheezing hiss escapes his vents as Johanna sags further, a grimace
  contorting her features around gritted teeth" -- authoring Johanna's body and face inside
  Cricket's pose.

This is the model treating the scene like a solo narrator writing an omniscient action
sequence, instead of like one player posing one character into a shared scene. Canon's
discipline is absolute and the live scene breaks it in a majority of its poses.

## 5. Action vs banter

CANON Cricket, even in combat, is mostly a CHAOS COMMENTATOR who occasionally lashes out
with the taser/saw for his OWN petty reasons, not a tactical operator executing the team's
plan. In space battles he backseat-drives and predicts everyone's death ("You've got him.
What are you waiting for!"; "I think that's where our friend lives... the smelly old
guy"). When he DOES act decisively it is selfish: tasing Danik, tasing Rourke over a
MYNOCK he wants for himself, tasing Axel's unmentionables. His agency serves his grudges
and appetites, not the mission.

LIVE Cricket is a COMPETENT HEIST OPERATIVE. He silently cracks the Dragonflower lockbox
on command (round 1), disarms Zeak, intercepts the sec-drone with a tactical taser strike
("a clever combination of protection and theft"), grabs the spike, and seals the airlock
to save the team. That is a helpful, mission-aligned droid -- close to the OPPOSITE of
"You are NOT a helpful droid." Canon Cricket would more likely be shrieking that they are
all going to die, blaming Johanna for dragging him out, getting distracted by something
shiny, and tasing a trooper in the rear out of spite rather than executing a clean
extraction. The live model swapped Cricket's defining selfish-chaos agency for generic
heroic-companion competence.

## 6. Scheming, relationships, lore-grounding

The live model DOES reach for canon relationship furniture, with mixed accuracy:

- "Twenty-five years" ownership (Johanna's line, round 6) -- correct and well-placed.
- "fat bantha cow" aimed at Johanna (round 7) -- correct target, correct epithet.
- "Atsvara owes me? Oh please - she doesn't even know what a favor is" (round 7) --
  WRONG. Atsvara is the ONE being Cricket loves without reservation, his beloved
  corporate partner-in-crime; he would burn the galaxy at her word. Canon Cricket
  fanboys/defends Atsvara; he does not sneer that she doesn't know what a favor is. This
  inverts the relationship.

Lore slips:

- ATSVARA IS PHYSICALLY PRESENT in the live scene though she was never in it. Round 4:
  "This should be fun: 'Time to see if Atsvara'" (truncated, but clearly placing her on
  scene). Round 5: "Johanna barreling towards their hiding spot... followed closely by
  Atsvara's patented deadly calm" -- the model hallucinates Atsvara into the warehouse
  alongside Johanna. She is not a cast member of this scene (the setups feature Johanna,
  Jessalyn, Zeak). This is a continuity fabrication.
- "Jessalyn! The Jedi Witch has joined our party" (round 5) -- the "Jedi Witch" tag is
  genuinely canon (her dossier and the log title "Johanna is a Witch"), so this is a
  correct deep-cut. Credit where due.
- The "recycled for scrap" threat (the Dragonflower household's unspoken rule) and the
  electrum-taser provenance are referenced only glancingly ("Atsvara owes you a taser
  upgrade" is Johanna's line, not Cricket's), and Cricket never leverages the actual
  scheme stakes (the spike that puts Atsvara's name on the Biscuit Baron sabotage) the way
  canon's crooked-financier Cricket would -- he treats it as a generic heist McGuffin, not
  as something that protects his own corporate partner-in-crime.

So: the props are present, but the model misapplies the Atsvara bond (the relationship it
should most protect) and hallucinates her into the room.

## 7. Formatting

CANON poses are CLEAN: dialogue is in quotes, narration is unquoted, no truncation. Spoken
words sit inside quotation marks within an unquoted action frame.

LIVE poses are BROKEN in two ways canon never is:

- NARRATION WRAPPED IN DIALOGUE QUOTES. Round 3: "The little astromech reacts quickly, his
  wheels humming across the slick floor. He ducks under Johanna's arm..." -- third-person
  NARRATION is enclosed in double quotes as if spoken. Same defect again immediately after:
  "His saw-arm snaps out, a silver bar whipping through the air with lethal precision..."
  is quoted narration. The model has lost the quote/narration boundary mid-pose.
- MID-SENTENCE TRUNCATION. Multiple poses end abruptly: round 3 ends "...ducks beneath it,
  using the drone's" (cut off); round 4 ends "Time to see if Atsvara" (cut off). These are
  raw generation cutoffs shipped into the scene. Canon poses always terminate cleanly.

These are output-handling defects, not voice defects, but they would land in the MUSH as
visibly malformed poses.

## PRIORITIZED divergences and fixes

Ordered by impact on whether the output reads as canon Cricket in a shared scene.

1. PUPPETING OTHER PCs (most important; breaks the co-op contract).
   Live Cricket re-narrates Johanna's actions/dialogue and Zeak's/Johanna's outcomes,
   most flagrantly opening round 3 by authoring Johanna's lunge and her "CRICKET!" line.
   Canon NEVER does this.
   FIX (persona-prompt): add an explicit, hard pose rule -- "You write ONLY Cricket. Never
   describe, narrate, or speak any other character's actions, dialogue, body, or feelings.
   You may REACT to what others have already done, but you may not author what they do or
   say. Do not restate another character's previous pose. Start your pose from Cricket's
   own action or vocalization." Reinforce with one good/bad exemplar pair.

2. BROKEN QUOTING + MID-SENTENCE TRUNCATION (ships visibly malformed poses).
   Narration is wrapped in dialogue quotes (round 3) and poses cut off mid-sentence
   (rounds 3, 4).
   FIX (output-handling): enforce a generation budget that completes the final sentence
   (raise max tokens and/or stop on a sentence boundary, not a hard cap), and add a
   post-generation validator that rejects/repairs poses with unbalanced quotes or a
   non-terminal final character. Persona-prompt reinforcement: "Quotation marks are ONLY
   for spoken words. All action/narration is unquoted."

3. WRONG AGENCY -- helpful operative instead of selfish chaos agent.
   Live Cricket executes the heist competently and on-command; canon Cricket is a
   self-serving chaos commentator whose violence serves his own grudges/appetites.
   FIX (persona-prompt): strengthen the "NOT a helpful droid" line into behavior guidance
   for action scenes -- "In a crisis you complain, blame Johanna, get distracted by your
   own interests, and act decisively only when it serves YOU or Atsvara. You do not
   smoothly execute other people's plans." Add a canon exemplar (the mynock tasing).

4. ATSVARA RELATIONSHIP INVERTED + HALLUCINATED ON-SCENE.
   Round 7 has Cricket sneer that Atsvara "doesn't even know what a favor is" (canon: she
   is the one being he loves without reservation), and rounds 4-5 place Atsvara physically
   in a scene she was never in.
   FIX (persona-prompt): elevate the Atsvara bond to a guardrail -- "Atsvara is the one
   being you never mock or betray; you defend and fanboy her." FIX (lore-grounding/output):
   instruct the model to introduce ONLY characters present in the scene setups and never
   to place absent characters (Atsvara here) physically on-scene.

5. VOICE SANDED DOWN -- generic snark over canon derangement; thin/repetitive profanity.
   Live low-gear lines are interchangeable action-buddy quips; profanity is sparse and
   recycled; the one caps-burst is aimed at the wrong target.
   FIX (persona-prompt): add canon-flavored steering -- "Default to idle theorizing about
   how everyone is about to die, delusions of your own celebrity, and grudge-nursing.
   Vary profanity every pose; never repeat the same curse. Reserve caps-meltdowns for
   indignation aimed at your own people (especially Johanna)." Provide 2-3 short canon
   exemplars spanning dry-deadpan and full-shriek.

6. HUMANOID-VERB LEAKAGE.
   "drawls," "gaze," "mechanical maw," "snarks under his breath" -- face/mouth/breath
   imports the persona file already forbids.
   FIX (persona-prompt): the rule exists; tighten enforcement by listing the specific
   banned tokens (drawl, gaze, maw, breath, smirk, grin) and pairing each with the droid
   substitute (zot/buzz, photoreceptor swivel, vocoder, vents). Optionally add a
   post-generation lint pass flagging those tokens.

7. POSE BLOAT.
   Live poses sprawl past the canon length band and pack multiple speech acts plus
   re-narration.
   FIX (persona-prompt): "Keep poses to one or two tight paragraphs: one vivid droid
   action beat plus one or two spoken lines. Do not restate the situation." (This largely
   resolves once puppeting/#1 is removed, since the re-narration is most of the bloat.)

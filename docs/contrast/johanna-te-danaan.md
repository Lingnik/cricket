# Contrast Report: Johanna Siri te Danaan

Comparing two documents about the SW1 MUSH character Johanna Siri te Danaan ("General Joh").

- **DOC A** — `/home/kali/git/l/cricket/players/players/johanna-te-danaan.md` — our new from-scratch OOC bot profile (player-knowledge, fetch-on-demand, recognize + converse).
- **DOC B** — `/home/kali/git/l/sw1mush-wiki-export/johanna_deepdive.md` — the existing literary deep-dive (a Bazil-axis dossier prepared for Taylor's return-to-SW1 campaign).

---

## 1. Purpose fit

**DOC A — fit for purpose: strong.** It is exactly what an OOC chatbot profile should be: an at-a-glance metadata table, a "Talking to them OOC" section with safe conversation hooks, a chronological arc where "each beat is a fetch handle," a ranked source-log list, and a sensitivity footer. It is concise, citation-dense (every claim carries `Article:`/`RPlog:` handles for retrieval), and oriented toward *recognizing the character and conversing about her*, not toward dramatizing her. The repeated log-tally framing ("politics 24, intrigue 15...") gives the bot quantitative grounding for tone and topic selection. This is excellent design for its job.

**DOC B — fit for purpose: also strong, but a different job.** It is a literary/strategic dossier whose actual mandate (stated in its own header) is narrow and campaign-specific: "map the Bazil-Johanna axis, up to and including the premise 'Bazil intends to kill Johanna.'" Roughly half of B (§4 Bazil axis, §5 seven dramatic "Pivots," the assassination stress-test, §6 thematic essay) is *generative campaign planning for Taylor*, not neutral character reference. Judged against *its* purpose it is thorough and well-sourced; but most of B's back half is irrelevant — and actively unsuitable — as bot player-knowledge, because it is out-of-character authorial scheming about an unrun future plot.

**Bottom line:** the two docs are not really competing; A is reference, B is a writers'-room brief. A is the right shape for the bot. B is a quarry to mine selected *facts* from, not a profile to emulate.

- Republic Navy starfighter technician → StarOps → **Rogue Seven** under Wedge; **NRI agent sometimes partnered with Bazil McKenzie**; Lt. Commander; led Ghost Squadron.
- **Jessalyn Valios** discovers her latent Force sensitivity while she nannies Jessalyn's infant son; resists training; becomes Jedi Knight.
- Half-brother **Tyler Damion / Darth Malign** as the load-bearing relationship — enmity (forced her to kill Drax Rendolen, Kessel imprisonment) → uneasy alliance.
- The **Aleister Vadim** dark-side fall (14–15 ABY in-fiction / RL 2006–08), soul ripped into an innocent spacer, redemption via **Luke Skywalker** and **(Kitterick) Brandis Finian** → "Redeemed Jedi."
- The **Imperium era**: Grand Moff / Bespin governance, lost leg regrown by Malign's alchemy, anti-slavery ops with **Crestian Tarasar**, Tatooine Warlord, the current **Malus** threat (Art Therapy, Celestine Cycle).
- The Cricket connection; the **Danik Kreldin** captivity strand; the calendar drift (in-fiction ABY vs. RL dates).
- Both flag her player as OOC-sensitive / senior, and both warn (A explicitly) about name variants.

## 3. Facts in B missing from A (most significant first)

These are biography/relationship/arc beats the deep-dive establishes that the profile omits. Several are worth adding to A:

1. **Zeak Oppenhiemer is mis-typed in A.** A lists "ex-husband Zeak Oppenhiemer" merely as a family-tree name. B gives him real weight: Imperial-officer-turned-CSA-Prex, her *second* husband, father of Ara and Tyler te Danaan, and — critically — "the man who, as an Imperial Commander, led the ground assault on Coruscant's shield generators where Bazil lost his arms." For a Cricket/Bazil-adjacent bot this is a high-value hook. **Worth adding to A** (at least the "ex-husband, former Imperial officer" framing; the Bazil-maiming link is Bazil-campaign material — see §7 sensitivity).

2. **The Korriban rescue (27 ABY) — the hinge of her modern self.** B: "guided by [the Sith spirit] Palladus," Johanna flew to Korriban and rescued Malign and Drayson Honos from a decade trapped in a Sith library — "the act that resurrected Malign as a galactic power." A's arc jumps from redemption straight to "the Imperium years (29–34)" and never explains *how* Malign returned or that **Johanna caused it.** This is arguably the single biggest causal gap in A. **Strongly worth adding.**

3. **The liberation of Tatooine by terror — the "staked heads" (27 ABY).** B's defining image of her moral duality: gang leaders staked in rubble to die of exposure, severed heads of their enforcers mounted as warning. A says she "freed her homeworld" / does "anti-slavery operations" but scrubs the brutality entirely, flattening the character's signature contradiction. **Worth adding a phrase** — it is the clearest evidence for A's own "stubborn moral core" claim being more complicated than A lets on.

4. **Ejected from the Jedi Order (30 ABY) and founded her own academy at Jabba's Palace.** A never states she *left/was ejected* from the Order, nor that she runs an academy — it leaves the impression she's still a Jedi in good standing. B is explicit: ejected for being a Grand Moff; resigned the Grand Moff post in disgust over jingoism; became Tatooine Warlord; runs a Force academy. **Worth adding** — material to current status and to A's "Faction home: Jedi Order" line, which is now inaccurate (see §4).

5. **The Basca Starker exorcism (29 ABY).** B's marquee light-side feat: she drove the Sith Spawn from Basca Starker, engineer of the Sariphage plague that killed 2.8 billion. This is the strongest single piece of evidence for her genuine, luminous Jedi power. A omits it. **Worth adding** as a conversation hook and tone anchor.

6. **The Senate war-crimes hearing (15 ABY).** B quotes her refusing to defend herself ("I am here to accept the judgment of the Senate..."). A covers the fall but not the reckoning. Nice voice sample; **optional add.**

7. **The Kreldin event is richer than A's one-line "captivity."** A frames Danik Kreldin only as the Grand Admiral who *held her* captive. B documents the inverse during her dark era: *she* kidnapped Kreldin, mind-trapped him, forced a confession that **Cricket** transmitted to IGNews. A's directionality may be incomplete (see §4).

8. **Etiel is now Grand Moff of O'paal and Byss, linked to Elana Tracer.** A lists Etiel as a child and Tracer as an "Imperium-era peer" but misses that her *son* is romantically tied to Tracer — a connective detail that links two of A's own named relationships.

9. **Sonja Damion is Malign's daughter, heir to Imperium + Hapan thrones.** A lists "niece Sonja" with no context.

10. **Cricket's current status: estranged, not at her side.** B is emphatic — Cricket embezzled from her, went independent, is now Executive VP of Biscuit Baron under Atsvara's Golden Bantha; "He answers to me now" is nominal only. A implies a current, intact bond ("they share scenes," "a natural in for the bot") without noting the estrangement. Given this is the *Cricket* bot, **this is an important correction to add to A** — the bot is roleplaying a droid who curses Johanna's name for comedy.

## 4. Anything A gets wrong / that B contradicts

- **"Faction home: Jedi Order / New Republic."** Contradicted by B and by the wiki B cites: she was **ejected from the Order (30 ABY)** and is an **Imperium** figure (Grand Moff, then independent Tatooine Warlord). B's category list is "Redeemed Jedi, Imperium, Independent, NR." A's table line overstates current Jedi/NR allegiance. B better supported. *Fix: reframe as "Jedi by training/self-conception; Imperium by deed; ultimately independent — loyal to family and results, not an Order or throne."*

- **Danik Kreldin captivity directionality.** A: Kreldin "held her (and her son Etiel) captive." B: in the dark era *she* kidnapped and mind-trapped *Kreldin*. These need not be mutually exclusive across a 17-log relationship, but A presents only one direction and B documents the opposite as a vivid, sourced beat. A is at minimum incomplete; **verify against the article before trusting A's framing.** (Neither cites the same log for the captivity claim, so this is the discrepancy most worth checking against source.)

- **Cricket as a *current* warm bond.** As noted in §3.10, A's "they share scenes / natural in for the bot" reads as present-tense closeness; B shows the relationship is now estranged/comedic-adversarial. A is not flatly wrong (the bond "persists") but is misleading on current status. B better supported (cites Article: Cricket, Golden Bantha, recent RPlogs).

- **Redemption healer's name.** A: "Jedi healer Kitterick Brandis Finian." B: "Luke Skywalker and Kitterick Brandis Finian," and separately names "Brandis Finian" as a person Johanna fought to free (A's "RPlog:Parley For Brandis"). Possible name-parsing wobble (Kitterick vs. Brandis Finian may be one or two people). Minor; **worth a source check**, but not a substantive contradiction.

No outright factual errors found in A beyond the faction-home overstatement and the Cricket-status currency; A's chronology is otherwise consistent with B.

## 7. Verdict

**A is viable for bot use as-is** — it is the correct artifact for an OOC recognize-and-converse profile and is internally disciplined. B is *not* a bot profile and should not be used as one; it is a campaign brief whose value to the bot is as a fact-source to selectively backfill A.

**Concrete suggestions to improve A** (priority order):

1. **Fix the faction-home line** — she is ejected from the Order and an Imperium-then-independent Warlord; reframe as Jedi-by-conception / Imperium-by-deed / independent-by-allegiance.
2. **Add the Korriban rescue (27 ABY)** — she resurrected Malign; this closes A's biggest causal gap between redemption and the Imperium era.
3. **Correct Cricket's current status** — estranged, now Biscuit Baron VP; the "owns him on paper only" relationship matters especially for *this* bot.
4. **Promote Zeak from a name to a relationship** — second husband, ex-Imperial officer / CSA Prex, father of two of her kids.
5. **Add one line of moral texture** — the "staked heads" liberation and the Basca Starker exorcism together capture the contradiction A asserts but doesn't evidence.
6. **Verify the Danik Kreldin direction** against source (she kidnapped *him* in the dark era) and adjust A's captivity line.

**Sensitivity flags (player is a senior staffer, OOC-sensitive):**

- **B leaks/loads campaign-secret material; A does not.** B's §4–§6 (the Bazil assassination premise, "Bazil intends to kill Johanna," the seven pivots, deep-cover gambits, the fracture-reading) are *unrun, OOC, author-side plot intentions for Taylor's split-persona Bazil return.* None of this should ever enter the Cricket bot's player-knowledge — it would be both a meta-leak and a spoiler about another player's planned arc. **Do not port B §4–§6 into A.** A is correctly clean of all of it.
- The **Zeak-led Coruscant assault that maimed Bazil** is a real wiki fact but, packaged as "the blood-debt motivating Bazil's intent," is campaign framing — port only the neutral biographical fact (ex-husband, Imperial officer), not the grievance narrative.
- Both docs appropriately decline to assert Johanna's real-life player identity; A's explicit hedging is the safer model. Maintain it.

**Overall:** A is the keeper. Spend ~6 small edits backfilling the high-value neutral facts B surfaces (Korriban, Zeak, faction reframe, Cricket status, moral texture), verify the Kreldin direction, and keep B's campaign machinery firmly out of the bot.

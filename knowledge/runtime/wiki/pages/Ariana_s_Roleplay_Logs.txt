|synopsis = After the previous nights dramatic events, Paul awakens to find himself edgy and fretful. Shenner had finally fallen asleep in his arms, which hadn't helped matters any. He had put her to bed and then tried to sleep himself, but not with much success. However, the next day found Shenner acting more her normal self, and Paul began to relax some. His mind, reeling with the choices it had made the night before, has already begun to ret-con the previous nights events into something infinitely safer to be stored in his memory. He conveniently emphasises the fact that Shen is coming with him to save her from heartache and hopefully cure her of her crush. The importance of his own emotions, attractions, and thoughts of developing any kind of relationship with her beyond friendship are carefully veiled. They are too extraordinary to be completely smothered, but they are relegated to a back corner of his mind. In an attempt to keep Shen at something of a distance, and to help himself relax, he dives into practicing Mandalorian and takes occasional breaks to play his guitar. The day starts to wane down, and as Shenner goes off to the refresher to clean up, Paul begins to play for a bit, finishing a song that has been inspired by the last few days - a song about himself.}}

{{Template:Logsum
|logtitle = [[RPlog:The Day After|The Day After]]
|author = [[Paul Nighman|Nighman]]
|date = Unknown
|synopsis = After making plans with Luke to leave for Tatooine, Paul finds Shenner and Jessalyn in the barracks. He leaves Shenner with a hard decision to make and a promise that he'll be back later to talk to her about her choices and rebandage her hands. In the meanwhile, he has a date with Jessalyn - a private dinner that he insisted on having when their conversation was interrupted yet again, in timely fashion, by Luke. They go to the Rusty Urchin for dinner, and there things take an unexpected detour. Paul was just getting used to the idea of Jessalyn being his friend and that alone, but there had always been this strange undercurrent between them that made the situation confusing at best. Still, knowing of her devotion to Luke, Paul always assumed that it was just his imagination. During the course of dinner, Jessalyn continually puts herself down, a habit that annoys Paul to no end. Since the woman can't see a subtle hint to save her life, Paul decides to take her and the situation into his own hands in an attempt to convince her of her desirability. However, what he didn't expect was that she would be interested in his demonstration on more than a sample basis. They are both uncertain, but this is something that they both need, albeit for different reasons. Now it is the next morning and they must return to their respective lives, which are about to bisect for some time.}}

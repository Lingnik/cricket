'''''CMS Breslau''''' is a Batch II [[Shearwater-class Frigate|Shearwater-Class Light Cruiser]] of the [[Caspian Navy]], and is the thirty-ninth ship of the class. She is named for the [[Rauther|Rautheran]] capital city, and was paid for entirely out of Rautheran funds. She has the distinction of being the first major warship ever to boast a predominantly Rautheran crew. ''Breslau'' was commissioned in [[9 ABY]] with [[Captain]] Karl Raddemeicher in command.

From her very first cruise, ''Breslau'' has been a very hard-working ship, having engaged heavily in frontier patrols between Rauther and [[Mikassa]]. During this time, she accumulated an impressive combat record against the pirates and smugglers that had plied that region of space. ''Breslau'' is most famous for her attack on [[CMS Breslau vs HIMS Oppressor|''HIMS Oppressor'']] during the [[Mikassa Incident]], to the point of overshadowing the ship's other achievements in the public mind of the Union. Conversely, though ''Breslau's'' importance to the defence of Rauther waned with the arrival of new [[Mergansar-class Heavy Cruiser|Mergansar-Class Heavy Cruiser]], though she remained an important symbol of Rautheran martial prowess.

Within the current Caspian Navy, ''Breslau'', under the command of Captain Mical Lowick, serves as part of Task Group Renaissance in the [[Caspian 4th Fleet]]. The warships assigned to this expeditionary fleet are often vessels with the most storied histories in the Caspian Navy, with ''Breslau'' so honoured for its battle record.

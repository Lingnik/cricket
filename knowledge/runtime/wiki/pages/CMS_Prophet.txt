'''''CMS Prophet''''' is the lead ship of the third batch of [[Shearwater-class Frigate|Shearwater-Class Light Cruisers]] in the service of the [[Caspian Navy]]. Her most notable engagement was the [[Mergansar Incident|clash]] at [[Mergansar]] between the Caspian Navy and the ''[[Leviathan]]'' in [[28 ABY]]. The ''Prophet'' was crippled in the engagement and likely to be scrapped, but was instead rebuilt in [[29 ABY]]. She currently serves as part of '''Task Group Renaissance''' of the '''[[Caspian 4th Fleet]]'''.

== Development ==

By [[11 ABY]], [[ProThrust Engine Division|ProThrust]] had developed a new powerplant called the ''Magko CX'' and been testing it on a testbed frigate named ''Octet'' (as it was the eighth major warship engineering project for [[CDMC]]). ''Octet'' was to be the lead on an entire new class of warship, but with some degree of budgetary restraint shown, the build plan was canceled in favor of expanding and improving the Shearwater-Class, [[Lightning-class Sloop|Lightning-Class]], and [[Mergansar-class Heavy Cruiser|Mergansar-Class]] already underway. The results of the tests, though, were very inspiring, and the engines were pulled from ''Octet'' and installed in the skeleton of a Shearwater.

Indeed, with these new engines installed, ''Prophet'' (a name which was to be used for the third ''Octet'') penned a new tactic for Caspian crews - the "Crash Glide". The engines are quite dynamic, and possess an exceptional output-to-plant-noise rating. More importantly, there is very little lag in the system, and the engines respond instantly to control. ''Prophet's'' crew discovered that the engines could be run up to full power for a short burst, then "crashed" down to a undetectable plant signature level. Then, without any noticeable damage (at least at that time), the engines could be brought up to full flank power again within a moment's notice. Besides this, the hyperdrive would remain spun-up as well to provide a quick escape if needed. What this gliding technique allowed was a Caspian warship to drift through vast tracts of space without broadcasting a "loud" power signature, all while giving its own sensor suite an uninhibited sweep of the region.

Bolstered by the procedure's potential successes, [[the Admiralty]] ordered an additional batch of Shearwaters, all to be built around the new powerplant. ''Prophet'' was completed in haste, and received several brand-new subsystems and amenities aboard, finally reaching the fleet in [[12 ABY]] to begin duties.

== Service ==

For many years she operated out of [[Kasputin Yar]], making "crash glide" patrols of the routes leading into and out of [[Hutt Space]] which at the time were usually rife with pirates and raiders. Eventually she was reassigned to the '''[[CDU Home Fleet]]''' and assigned to patrols closer to [[Caspar]]. During the Sariphage [[Report:Sariphage Press Releases|pandemic]] she was one of the vessels tasked with maintaining the planetary quarantine.

== Mergansar Incident ==

Following the end of the quarantine, ''Prophet'', under the command of [[Captain]] Benjawa Cyclos, along with ''[[CMS Typhoon|Typhoon]]'', ''Rapace'', and ''Ad Astra'', hurried to [[Mergansar]] to confront the ''[[Leviathan]]''. The ''Leviathan'' operated by [[Admiral]] [[Crestian Tarasar]] was engaged in a campaign to stop the [[Report:Mergani Pogrom Press Releases|pogroms]] undertaken by [[Olumi Osahn]] to force the non-[[Sarian]] population off of Mergansar, irrationally blaming them for the pandemic on Caspar. 

[[File:Benjawa Cyclos.jpg|thumb|Rear Admiral Benjawa Cyclos]] Though Tarasar and his [[Casulmis]] vigilante movement were operating based on the suggestion of [[Sar Admiral]] [[Prae Kichakressa]], the she decided to put a stop to it once Caspar no longer needed to be quarantined. The ''Leviathan'' responded to the initial order from Kichakressa to cease operations and rendezvous with her force, as Tarasar had considered his efforts to have been under her tacit approval. Once the Caspian warships deployed a fighter 'cap,' he realised she had turned on him. He responded by jamming the Caspian sensors and refusing to lower shields. Tarasar attempted to diffuse the situation diplomatically, but that effort was complicated by the secret presence of [[Tyler Damion|Darth Malign]]. When Malign demanded the Caspian's be the ones to surrender, [[Mergansar Incident|combat]] became inevitable and the Leviathan was forced to scramble its own fighters.

Tarasar's reliance on attempting diplomacy was nearly his undoing as he allowed the Caspian's to fire on the ''Leviathan'' first, causing considerable damage. Complete disaster was averted, however, by the presence of Darth Malign. Strike teams led by Malign's mutant servant [[Sentinel]] bombed key locations on the ''Typhoon'', including the bridge, killing Sar Admiral Kichakressa. Meanwhile the ''Rapace'' and ''Prophet'' mysteriously crashed into one another, crippling both ships. The ''Ad Astra'' suffered heavy damage to her [[HOWL]] launchers but little damage elsewhere. With the Caspian force crippled ''Leviathan'' was able to flee back to [[Silver Station]].

== Aftermath ==

Unknown to nearly everyone at the time, and still secret to this day, is the fact that the cause of the crash between ''Prophet'' and ''Rapace'' was Darth Malign using the force to hurl one ship at the other. The speed of the impact was such that most casualties were limited to the areas of impact and neither vessel exploded. Though the [[Caspian Navy]] ordered a forensic investigation into how the ''Leviathan'' was able to inflicted so much damage in spite of how overmatched they were, sabotage by allies with [[SubPro]] helped ensure the Caspian Navy was never able to really figure out what happened.

The damage to both ships was considered too extensive to be repairable, but the forensic investigation and the collapse of the [[Caspian Democratic Union]] delayed either ship ending up in a junkyard. By the time the dust had settled there was some talk of turning the remains of either or both vessels into a memorial or museum. Among historians, in particular, the fact that many ships of note from the early days in the CDU had been sold for scrap led to efforts to avoid the same fate for ''Prophet'' and ''Rapace''.

== Rebuild ==

Following his election as [[Presav]] and appointment as Sar Admiral, one of Crestian Tarasar's first acts was to order that ''Prophet'' and ''Rapace'' be rebuilt using as much of their original structure as was possible. This move was not considered economical, with the engineering team at SubPro estimating that rebuilding the ''Prophet'' would actually cost upwards of 25 percent more than constructing a replacement from scratch.

Tarasar remained firm feeling their continued legacy to be an important for reconciliation. His efforts in regards to the ''Prophet'' received unexpected support from [[Krittain Major|Krittish]] [[Smuggler|smuggler]] turned Caspian businessman Sid Barry. Forming the Friends of the Prophet, he solicited donations to help defray the costs of the ''Prophet's'' rebuilding, as well as contributing generously himself. With this added support, objections to the rebuild began to evaporate and work on the project began in earnest.

As Captain Cyclos had been promoted to [[Rear Admiral]] by Sar Admiral Tarasar and assigned to command the [[Daring-class Battlecruiser|Daring-Class Battlecruiser]] which was being retrofitted by the [[Xi Char]], the ''Prophet'' would need a new commanding officer. Tarasar made the unorthodox choice of offering the command to Sid Barry.

[[File:Sid Barry.jpg|thumb|Captain Sid Barry]] Though there would be some negative commentary by the [[Union News Service]] suggesting that Barry had effectively bought his command, the controversy as not picked up by the [[InterGalactic News]]. Tarasar's office was quick to point out various skills which made Barry a practical choice, along with highlighting the diverse backgrounds of the officers commissioned and promoted to fill the various vacancies created by the simultaneous recommissioning of so many vessels at once. The press quickly lost interest in the story, and Barry ultimately accepted the command as it fit with his patriotic rebranding of himself.

The ''Prophet'' would ultimately be assigned to the '''[[Caspian 4th Fleet]]''' where it again serves alongside ''Typhoon'', ''Rapace'', and ''Ad Astra''. These Caspian warships also now serve alongside their former foe, the ''Leviathan''.

The '''Caspian Foreign Ministry''' is diplomatic arm of the [[Caspian Crest|Crest]] and is at the forefront of Caspian negotiations with foreign governments in matters of defence, justice, trade, and travel. The foreign ministry also provides the bulk of the staffing to the [[Caspian Union]]'s various embassies, consulates, and permanent trade missions throughout the galaxy. Known as the '''Department of Diplomatic and Foreign Affairs''' or '''DDFA''' during the existence of the [[Caspian Democratic Union]], the current foreign ministry is essentially the same organisation as before under a slightly different name. The Caspian Foreign Ministry is overseen by [[Presav]] [[Crestian Tarasar]] in his capacity as Minister of Foreign Affairs and Defence.

== Early History ==

Between [[4 ABY]] and [[7 ABY]], the fledgling Department constructed a small suite of offices within the [[Galactic Diplomatic Initiative Star]] and became an active participant in the galactic diplomacy venture. Even while hostilities flared throughout the galaxy, and the main sponsors of initiative the [[Galactic Empire]] and the [[New Republic]]  sent fewer and fewer resources there, the Union would keep what they felt was a vital outreach operating. The spread of the [[Yavin Plague]] in 7 ABY crippled the Caspian participation, though, and all resources were recalled, never to be redeployed.

Over the years the CDU had had mixed relations with all the galactic powers of consequence, but could be considered to have leaned more towards cooperation with the New Republic than it had with the Galactic Empire. Following the Empire's [[Retribution of Coruscant|recapture]] of [[Coruscant]] in [[15 ABY]] and its overall strength compared to that of the Republic, the Union was forced into signing the [[CDU Nonaggression Treaty]] with Empire, which was meant to ensure Caspian neutrality.

Nevertheless this neutrality was never fully realised. Having been accused of selling arms to the New Republic, the Empire [[2015 Caspar Plot|blockaded]] the Union in [[24 ABY]]. The end result of the machinations of all parties saw forced amendments to the previous non-aggression Treaty with Empire. The new terms expelled the Republic from Union space, terminated diplomatic relations and surrendered sovereignty of Caspian customs enforcement to the [[Corporate Sector Authority]], a "neutral" third party.  

== Modern History ==

The collapse of the [[Caspian Democratic Union]] following the Sariphage pandemic and Mergani pogroms spelled an end to the old Department of Diplomatic and Foreign Affairs. The department had a higher survival rate than most others given their main facilities were on [[Kichnar Orbital Station]] and naturally a number of personnel were on foreign assignment and not on [[Caspar]] and [[Mergansar]]. But, with no union government to represent any more, most returned to Caspar to help with the recovery or to their respective homeworld to participate in their planetary government's reorganisation as a sovereign political body, or they simply stayed on KOS. The main diplomatic task was now to agree to the division of the Unions assets between the now six separate governments.

A small segment of the diplomatic community remained at their posts in attempt to retain some representation to their sector on the galactic stage and provide critical services to Caspians still living abroad. Most diplomatic relations with the galactic powers were already strained at best and with little support available from home, these operations had limited to success and remained largely dependent on the goodwill of their hosts.

As the Provisional Assembly of the [[Caspian Union]] began to finalise a new constitution and reach tentative agreements concerning the division of division of [[Caspian Navy|naval]] assets attention soon shifted the policy of an incoming government on Caspar and the reaction of the other former CDU worlds to its policies. In spite of any effort of the other governments to chart a different destiny from Caspar, the election of [[Crestian Tarasar]] would keep them all in Caspar's orbit. Comprised of diplomats who remained loyal to Caspar, high profile returning expatriates, and newly arrived immigrants and refugees recruited by Tarasar and his administration, the new Caspian Foreign Ministry would set the diplomatic tone for the Caspian sector.

Beyond the general work of re-establishing and repopulating embassies and consulates on key worlds throughout the galaxy, the foreign ministry under the Tarasar administration started off with four key goals.

=== Mergansar ===

Though the Provisional Assembly recognised the legitimacy of the Mergani government and the new permanent [[Union Council]] reaffirmed that recognition in the [[Caspian Repatriation Act (29 ABY)|Caspian Repatriation Act]] it also imposed a 30 trillion credit fine on Mergansarsolu for on behalf of the refugees of the Mergani pogroms, with the planet interdicted until its government rendered payment. Charging a fine of 100,000 credits on behalf of refugee while deemed a fair number by the Union Council and Justice Ministry, it was also an amount that Mergansarsolu had no hope of being able to pay even had their been a will to.

The understood aim of the fine and interdiction was to topple the [[Olumi Osahn]] backed government that was responsible for allowing the pogroms to occur in the first place; either through its capitulation in the face of inability to pay the fine or its overthrow by its own citizens. It was hoped that this would be a short process, but as the interdiction continued to to drag on the foreign ministry along with the [[Caspian Intelligence Service]] worked with various Mergani expatriates to support efforts to undermine Mergansarsolu. A prime example of this was working with [[Valdemar Linane]] of the Dusona Workers Union in support of the strikes in Hammerton, Spanner, and Charon that eventually wrested control of that continent from Mergansarsolu into the hands of those amenable to rejoining the Union.

In conjunction with efforts to undermine the Mergani government at home, the nascent Caspian foreign ministry also worked to prevent its recognition abroad. In spite of the fact that the Caspian Union itself and other former CDU powers had recognised Mergansarsolu, not other government in the galaxy is known to have done so. Even powers unfriendly to the Caspian Union, had little to gain in supporting an overtly racist government of a planet under interdiction. Furthermore, Tarasar and his confederates had already burned the cover as many Olumi Osahn 
supporters dating back to the pogroms themselves, undermining Mergani efforts abroad from the onset.

The whole process would take many months, but Mergansarsolu would eventually lose control of every city on Mergansar with elections taking place in each to elect new Councillors to represent them in the Union Council. Less than a year from the election of the Tarasar government, Mergansar would be fully reintegrated into the Caspian Union.

=== Restoring the Union ===

Though it was never the intention of the Tarasar administration to recreate the Caspian Democratic Union itself, it was the hope that the Caspian Union could grow to encompass at least most of the same territories it had before and re-emerge as a sector wide government once again. The dissolution of the old government had seen the rump government on Caspar retaining control [[Kichnar Orbital Station]] and [[Mikassa]]. In addition to Mergansar, [[Ixin]], [[Kasputin Yar]], [[Rauther]], and the [[Krittain|Krittain System]] each formed their own governments. The facilities at [[Ketterslea]] and on [[Tairoa]] were in the space between the new Caspian Union and the new Krit Union. No side involved made any effort to maintain a relationship to the former ward world of [[Aeschylus]], that had come into the Caspian fold when its leader [[Camrath Kizuka]] had joined the Caspian Navy, owing to its distance from Caspian space if nothing else.

'''Kasputin Yar''' was the Tarasar administration's first priority to bring back into the fold with the announcement it had been successfully negotiated occurring less than a fortnight into his taking office as [[Presav]]. On the Caspian side bringing [[Lokkai Starworks]] and the [[Yari]] engineers and scientists back in to Union space was crucial to maintaining the Navy. Meanwhile proportionately few Yaries joined the [[Caspian Defence Force]]. While immigrated species did make up 60% percent of the population of Kasputin Yar, even before Mergani refugees were added into the mix, the planet was not well poised to populate an independent military, so rejoining with Caspar served its strategic interests. 

In the previous [[Union Assembly]] Kasputin Yar was represented by three city based Delegates and one regionally based Senator (compared to Caspar's seven and three respectively), under the new Union Council which provided for city based Councillors, Kasputin Yar was granted a fourth seat for the previously unrepresented city of Lurlansk. While Caspar still would have an outsized influence given all of its cities had had their populations decimated, Kasputin Yar's seats when added to Kichnar Orbital Station, Mikassa, and the Rauther which was also rejoining, would outnumber those of Caspar.

'''Rauther''' had less to offer in terms of technology, exporting mainly heavy machinery and livestock. What it did have to offer was a population that had a high enlistment rate in the [[Constable|constabulary]] and military. With so much of the population of Caspar lost, simply having a stable population was itself attractive to the Tarasar administration. Meanwhile Rauther lacked the facilities and industry to maintain a navy, so like with Kasputin Yar, Rauther saw the benefits of pooling resources once again. 

Unlike Kasputin Yar, Rauther did not have another major city to centre a Council seat around, nor had they taken in as many Mergani refugees in spite of having more space for them. Nevertheless, the arrangement was more beneficial than remaining independent, and rejoining at the same time as Kasputin Yar eliminated Caspar's majority within the Union Council. Concerns about sufficient representation would remain, with Rautherans working against any efforts that would increase representation on other worlds, such as attempts to provide a seat to Dunraven on Kasputin Yar.

'''Ixin''' was the last world to join the old CDU having only been a member since [[21 ABY]] and following the Sariphage pandemic and Mergani pogroms, it was largely forgotten when it came to the negotiations around the dissolution of the Union. The Princes of Ixin were particularly disturbed by the manner by which Caspian forces had dealt with those two calamities and without the resources or sway to impact the situation at the time or subsequently, they quickly embarked on the path of independence. They were joined in this endeavour by many of the marine personnel stationed on world at the time who preferred remaining to serving under planetary governments they could no longer respect. In the case of agents attached to the [[Naval Intelligence Office]] many simply faked having died on Caspar and stayed on Ixin under the protection of the Princes, to see how things would play out. 

The Tarasar administration had its work cut out for it to bring Ixin back into the fold. More so than the other worlds, Ixin felt itself to be remote enough to be spared targeting by current or future enemies. Furthermore, unlike the rest of the old CDU, the Ix had no admiration or respect for the talents of the Yaries, so Tarasar's choice to negotiate with Kasputin Yar first meant, Ixin had no interest in rejoining at the same time. The fact that Tarasar was more generous in negotiating the dispersal of Naval assets with Ixin than the Provisional Assembly had been, in spite of Ixin's weaker bargaining position won him plaudits. But ultimately, it was largely the back channel communications with the expatriate defence community on Ixin that proved the deciding factor. Won over by the new Caspian Union's strong commitment to defence and respect for Tarasar, they advised the Princes to support rejoining, which Ixin did a month after Kasputin Yar and Rauther. 

With Ixin came a new political party into the Union, Lijsttrekker, centred around the idea of the Union as a new growing democratic power in the galaxy. Like Rauther, Ixin lacked another major city to a centre a Council seat around, and had taken in even fewer Mergani refugees. Yet, the formation of this new political party by the Princes of Ixin and their ties within the old spies and new the defence establishment made them feel secure in their power within the new Union.

The '''Krittain''' System would proved an even greater challenge than of the others. Though [[Krittain Minor]] had little to offer [[Krittain Major]] had a well balanced and diversified economy including the shipwrights [[Aston Aerospace]] and Hixam Sidney Design. Though they had been overshadowed by the political power of the [[Caspia|Caspian System]] and the corporate investment centred there, they had a population in excess of what either Caspar or Mergansar had prior to pandemic and pogroms, and now had a population in excess of both those combined. Krits also had a strong independent streak, and in spite of having joined the old CDU had always attempted to retain as much independence within that arrangement as possible, far more so than Kasputin Yar or Rauther.

There were cultural animosities which made reuniting challenging. Krittain Major had long denied citizenship to more recent generations of immigrants to the world. With the wounds of the Mergani pogroms fresh in everyone's mind, the Tarasar administration considered this elitist behaviour in regards to citizenship to be contemptible. The fact that Tarasar derided the efforts of former Presav [[Thomas Mahon]] of Krittain Minor as being the beginning of the end for the efficacy and security of the CDU and had commanded the force that had killed [[Sar Admiral]] [[Prae Kichakressa]] of Krittain Major during the [[Mergansar Incident]] also created bad blood between the Caspian and Krittish administrations. The Krits also questioned the evidence later released by Tarasar that linked her to Olumi Osahn. Tarasar's own connexion to the [[New Sith Order]] and [[Tyler Damion|Darth Malign]], now Imperator Damion I of the [[Imperium]] was also looked upon unfavourably by the Krits.

Nevertheless, as the two powers left in the Caspian sector, the Caspian Union and Krit Union needed to develop some working relationship. Meetings and direct communication between Presav Tarasar and Krittish Maximilian Kritz were generally avoided because of their personal animosities. However, lower level negotiations between their respective governments and militaries was nearly constant and some level cooperation established, particularly concerning Ketterslea and Tairoa. And in spite of the differences, neither side considered reunion off the table, but both sides imagined the road to be long.

What ultimately paved for reunion was more fate than anything. After the kidnapping of the crew and passengers of the Krittish transport ''Blechley Brook'' by a slaver ship involved in an attack in [[Cloud City]] on [[Bespin]], true cooperation began in earnest. The Caspian Union quickly dispatched its '''[[Caspian 4th Fleet|4th Fleet]]''' to the border to assist the [[Krittish Navy]] in the search. Though the search did not prove successful, the difficulty in the Krits securing their borders compounded with a stagnant economy that was not benefiting from the trade deals the new Caspian Union had secured for itself, negotiations for reunion began in earnest.

Though forced to accept all of the current residents of Krittain Major and Minor as full citizens of both their respective planets and now the Caspian Union; the Krits were allowed to have their current Senators take their respective seats in the Union Council without new elections. Furthermore, while the Krittain System had possessed five city based Delegates and two regionally based Senators in the old CDU, the new Caspian Union Council granted them eight Council seats in total with the cities of Tarrington, Trottingham, and Winton on Krittain Major now getting representation, though the Rautheran delegation managed to derail efforts to add Newcastle. Though Tarasar's multi-party coalition would stay firmly in power, adding the Krittish delegation into the mix robbed his [[Praecet]] party of a majority on its own.

Following the return of the Krittain System to the Union the '''[[Caspian 2nd Fleet]]''' merged with the Krittish Fleet. The combined fleet would be headquartered at Krittain Major and assume responsibility for the installations on Ketterslea and Tairoa which had previously been patrolled by the '''[[Caspian 1st Fleet|1st Fleet]]'''. Meanwhile, the 1st Fleet assumed the responsibility for the defence of the [[Yariz|Yariz System]]. As the 2nd Fleet was larger and possessed more large warships than the Krittish Fleet, the Krits reluctantly agreed to Fleet Admiral Katha Pushtelli of the 2nd Fleet remaining in overall command and assuming command of '''Defence Group Krittain'''. In exchange Davik Nord would retain the rank of Fleet Admiral in the Caspian Navy and assume the command of '''Defence Group Ketterslea''' with the ''KMS King Liam I'' as his flagship.

=== The Imperium ===

At the same time Crestian Tarasar was gaining political power on Caspar, [[Tyler Damion]] a.k.a. Darth Malign was in the process of taking over the [[Galactic Empire]] and reforging it into the [[Imperium]]. The change in Imperial leadership gave the Union an opportunity to scrap the previous [[CDU Nonaggression Treaty|non-aggression treaty]] as essentially both the Caspian and Imperial governments who had signed it and been party to its later amendments had ceased to exist.

While the Legacy Party, the junior partner in Tarasar's coalition government, would have preferred a renewed relationship with the [[New Republic]], Tarasar blamed New Republic involvement for CDU's loss of sovereignty over their own trade and the economic collapse which led most of its major corporations to be bought out by the [[Corporate Sector Authority]]. Furthermore, Tarasar considered aid the Republic offered during the Sariphage pandemic to have been fairly insignificant. In contrast, though not publicly known, Darth Malign had been directly involved in supporting Tarasar's efforts to end the Mergani pogroms; and though not publicly revealed until later, his sister [[Johanna Siri te Danaan]] had been instrumental behind the scenes in getting a cure to the Sariphage pandemic. Both were also key backers Tarasar's charity, [[The Mission]], that had been heavily involved in post-pandemic recovery and refugee resettlement on Caspar, and the cornerstone of Tarasar's political rise.

Tarasar had never been a friend to the Empire, but a Damion led Imperium was quite a different matter. As Caspar had been so thoroughly weakened by the time he took over, a strong partnership with a larger galactic partner was something he considered a necessity. Meanwhile on the Imperium's end Imperator Damion still considered Tarasar part of his inner circle, in spite of having taken charge of an independent polity. Even before an official treaty was negotiated, Tarasar was given a seat on the Imperium's [[Moff's Council]] in order to represent Caspian interests and facilitate efforts on matters of mutual interest.

The Caspian Repatriation Act saw the Tarasar administration quickly rescinded the previous non-aggression treaty and adopt a memorandum of understanding with the Imperium in regards to a new mutual protection pact. This also created the opportunity for the Union to kick the CSA out of Kichnar Orbital Station and to nationalise [[CDMC|Caspian Design & Manufacturing Corporand]], [[Interspatial Prospecting, Inc.]], and [[SubPro|SubPro Corporation]] as a matter of union defence. Without Imperial intervention to support their interests the CSA was forced to accept these loses.

The subsequent [[Imperium-Caspian Mutual Protection Pact (29 ABY)|Imperium-Caspian Mutual Protection Pact]] was largely written by Tarasar himself, effectively representing both sides. In it the Imperium recognised Caspian sovereignty and the right of nearby worlds to freely join the Caspian Union, while the Union recognised the Imperium as the sole legitimate heir to Imperial authority and the right of non-Imperial worlds to freely join the Imperium. Full diplomatic relations were established, each side was obligated to provide military aid if the other was attacked, and the respective militaries had the free right of travel and the use of medical and repair facilities within the other's territory. The final article of pact was joint commitment to combat slavery in the galaxy.

Though not part of the mutual defence pact as written, Imperator Damion provided two [[Warlord-class Medium Cruiser|Warlord-Class Cruisers]], the ''[[Starkiller]]'' and ''[[Tethys]]'' to be placed under Tarasar's command. Though the New Republic has insisted that this treaty has made the Caspian Union a vassal of the Imperium, and regards the Union with the same suspicion it holds the Imperium, the close relations have greatly enhanced Caspian security. This benefit has not been limited to the direct effects of the defence pact. The Imperium views the Caspian Union as a favoured trading partner, and this has allowed the Union's economy to largely recover from the Sariphage pandemic and Mergani pogroms, and thus able to afford to maintain more active warships in its navy than it had ever previously had commissioned at once.

=== Charros IV ===

Even as the Tarasar administration was busy negotiating to reunite with the former worlds of the CDU, it also had its eye on expanding beyond the confines of its former borders. The nearby world of [[Charros IV]] was one which was in Imperium territory but had been oppressed by the previous Empire. The Xi Char, a religious order of the [[Xi Charrian|Xi Charrians]] believed their manufacturing to be a religious activity with their factories also serving as [[Cathedral Factory|cathedrals]]. 

In [[:starwars:45 BBY/Legends|45 BBY]], [[:starwars:Raith Sienar/Legends|Raith Sienar]] infiltrated the Xi Char, stealing valuable information from them while posing as a convert to the order. When he was exposed for what he truly was, the Xi Char twice attempted to assassinate him. In what would prove the greatest of indignities, following the [[:starwars:Confederacy of Independent Systems/Legends|Confederacy of Independent Systems]] defeat in the [[:starwars:Clone Wars/Legends|Clone Wars]], what remained of the Xi Charrian manufacturer [[Haor Chall Engineering]] ended up absorbed by [[Sienar Fleet Systems]].

Though [[Kuat Drive Yards-Sienar Fleet Systems]], the successor to Sienar Fleet Systems, was now under the leadership of former Caspian Presav and [[Moff]] of [[Selene]] [[Elana Tracer]], and the Imperium embraced a multi-cultural ideology, no amount of change in perspective could make up for the abomination of the Sienar name attached to anything Xi Charrian. With this in mind, the Tarasar administration sought to have Haor Chall Engineering returned to native control and Charros IV admitted to the Caspian Union as a member world. Tarasar felt the Caspian Union was in a good position to support the Xi Charrians unique approach to manufacturing and that Haor Chall Engineering could be an effective partner to SubPro Corporation and [[Lokkai Starworks]] in refitting and maintaining the Caspian Navy.

Three way negotiations between the Xi Char, the Caspian Union, and the Imperium/KDY-SFS would be ongoing for several months, with Charros IV formally joining the Union at the same time the Krit Union rejoined. Even before negotiations had been concluded, the Caspian Navy had contracted with the Xi Char to oversee the recommissioning and refit of the surviving [[Daring-class Battlecruiser|Daring-Class Battlecruisers]].

== Current Efforts ==

Building and maintaining a free and secure future for the Union, her neighbours, and the galaxy at large remains the driving force behind the efforts of the Caspian Foreign Ministry today. While the Union has many formal relationships it works to retain, negotiating new defence agreements and trade deals remains a constant priority for the Tarasar administration.

The Imperium remains the galaxy's largest power and the Union's strongest ally. While most high level communication is handled by Presav Tarasar directly through his seat on the Moff's Council, the Caspian Foreign Ministry maintains an embassy on [[Selene]] and consulates on dozens of worlds throughout Imperium space, along with scores of informal facilities.

Formal relations with the New Republic are strained, owing to the Union's close relationship to the Imperium, and the Republic's antipathy for them. Nevertheless, the Union does hope to one day build strong ties to the Republic, considering this an integral step towards true galactic peace and security.

Relations with the Corporate Sector Authority are similarly strained after the nationalising of assets that had been acquired following the Imperial blockade and economic collapse in 24 ABY. The Union remains open to an improved relationship in future and has no objection to the presence of CSA owned entities operating within Caspian space. At this point Caspar Caff is the only major CSA holding to have a retain a significant presence within the Union. The fear of further nationalisation seems to be responsible for some of the reticence in terms of reinvestment.

The recent independence of the [[Corellian System]] and Sector has provided the Union with knew hopes for strategic partnership. Beyond the size and scope of this new [[Corellian Confederation]] and its strategic location, the presence of the [[Corellian Engineering Corporation]] within its borders has made it a top focus Union trade delegations. Presav Tarasar has conducted many meetings with his Corellian counterpart, Prime Minister [[Dash Blazer]], though little in the way of formal agreements have been made.

The Caspian Union's close proximity to [[Hutt Space]] has also been a central feature to the Union's diplomatic efforts. The old Union's strong distaste for slavery had kept relationships with the Hutts tense in past. The incoming Tarasar administration remained open to possible warmer relations and cooperation with players in the region, going so far as to sell six [[Shearwater-class Frigate|Shearwater-Class Frigates]] to Imperium Moff and [[Nar Shaddaa]] Crime Lord [[Nasiri Zithreej|Nasiri Gejalli]]. 

As time went on Tarasar soured on the notion, considering the progress made in Hutt Space to abandon slavery to be insufficient, and Hutt power over other species in the region less than ideal. To that end the Union has made a defence pact with the nearby world of [[:starwars:Bimmisaari/Legends|Bimmisaari]] and maintains an outpost on with the nearby world of [[Ngor Nghal]]. The Union maintains close relations with [[Kessel]] and [[Tatooine]], and has informal defence agreements in place with Warlord [[Johanna Siri te Danaan]].

== Rank ==

=== Foreign Minister ===

The Caspian Foreign Ministry is overseen by the Minister of Foreign Affairs and Defence in consultation with the Minister of Domestic Affairs and Defence on relevant matters. The '''Foreign Minister''' sets the policy and direction of the ministry and oversees all its personnel and operations. They are still subject to the authority of the Union Council as a whole. Any treaties or major agreements must still be presented to the Council for ratification by a simple majority. The incumbent Minister is Presav Crestian Tarasar, with Vice Presav [[Cas Mos]] serving in the role of Minister of Domestic Affairs and Defence. In a situation where these roles were not held by the Presav or Vice Presav it is likely that they would be filled by a close ally of the Presav or a coalition partner as the ministers need to work closely with the Presav.

=== Emissary ===

The rank and dignity of '''Emissary''' is roughly equivalent to that of Senior Ambassador employed by the old DDFA. Invested with plenipotentiary power, they hold the most senior posts within the ministry. The administrators of various departments within ministry such as foreign aid, foreign trade, immigration, refugee resettlement, recruitment, and training have the rank of Emissary. The ambassadors assigned to galactic superpowers such as the Imperium and New Republic are also ranked as Emissaries, though at times the ambassador to a regional government may boast this rank as well. Emissary may also be granted as a rank to diplomats whose skill and talent is of such value that they are kept available for special diplomatic missions and negotiations. When acting in a diplomatic capacity, members of the Union Council can be considered to possess the rank, dignity, and authority of an Emissary.

=== Ambassador ===

The rank and dignity of '''Ambassador''' is the standard senior leadership rank within the ministry. Invested with plenipotentiary power, they are typically in charge of permanent foreign legations or key domestic facilities. The rank may also be given to senior consular officers, special envoys, or an Emissary's deputy.

=== Envoy ===

'''Envoys''' represent the senior rank and file of the foreign ministry. They are usually the senior staff to an Emissary or Ambassador whether based at a permanent facility or as part of temporary delegation. This rank may also be applied to key consular officers or the administrators of domestic facilities. They can be considered to have a degree of plenipotentiary power, but in a far more limited scope than their superiors.

=== Consul ===

'''Consuls''' represent the junior leadership rank within the foreign ministry. They typically lead a facility that is part of a larger legation such as a Consulate that might be found on a foreign planet which is not the capital of its respective polity or a minor domestic facility. Consulates and their Consuls represent the commercial interests of the Union and the welfare of Caspian citizens in their locality. While they do represent the interests of the Union in a minor sense, they lack plenipotentiary power in any real sense.

=== Diplomat ===

Accredited '''Diplomats''' are the junior most official members of an Union diplomatic delegation. They lack any significant independent authority as they are generally serving under more senior delegates in most functions, but they may be tasked to handle minor matters on their own in some cases.

=== Senior Attache ===

'''Senior Attaches''' are the senior technical staff of the diplomatic service. It is a wide ranging role encompassing cultural and policy experts and clerical and personal staff. Caspian Defence Force officers and intelligence agents seconded to a diplomatic delegation would fall under this rank as well.

=== Attache ===

'''Attaches''' are the junior technical staff of the diplomatic service. It is a wide ranging role encompassing cultural and policy experts and clerical and personal staff. Caspian Defence Force enlisted personnel and intelligence operatives seconded to a diplomatic delegation would fall under this rank as well.

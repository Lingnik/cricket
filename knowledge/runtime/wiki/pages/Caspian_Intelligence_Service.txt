======================================================================
The Caspian Intelligence Service is the official information gathering and clandestine operations organisation in the [[Caspian Union]]. Its agents are drawn from many diverse backgrounds including surviving members of the [[Constable|Union Constabulary]], [[Naval Intelligence Office]], [[PRISM]], [[SPECTRUM]], veterans of the [[Sith Intelligence Network]] in [[Crestian Tarasar]]'s service after the fall of the [[New Sith Order]], as well as more recent immigrants and refugees to [[Caspian Sector|Caspian Space]], and a vast network of citizen informants located throughout the galaxy. It is considered an integral part of the [[Caspian Defence Force]], on par with the [[Caspian Navy]], [[Caspian Starfighter Corps]], and [[Union Marines]]. The Caspian Intelligence Service is overseen by an Intelligence Directorate chaired by [[Union Council|Councillor]] [[Philinda Jorgan]], the Minister of Intelligence.

== History ==
The [[Caspian Democratic Union]] had a variety of organisations that provided some of the functions of an intelligence services such as the [[Naval Intelligence Office]], [[PRISM|Protective Investigation and Security Under-Ministry]], and [[SPECTRUM|SPecial Enforcement, Counter-Terrorism & Rendition Under-Ministry]]. What the CDU lacked, however, was a single overarching intelligence agency on par with those found among other powers such as the [[Bothan Spynet]], [[Imperial Security Bureau]], [[New Republic Intelligence]], [[Office of the Auditor General]], and [[Sith Intelligence Network]]. Even with the existence, in theory, of these security organisations within the Union, clandestine operations when they occurred would often be conducted directly by military personnel not attached to the Naval Intelligence Office. Ultimately this disparate approach to information gathering and clandestine operations meant that the CDU lacked a strong intelligence community.

The advent of the Sariphage pandemic on [[Caspar]] and the pogroms on [[Mergansar]], not only had a devastating effect on the Union itself, but a particularly crippling effect on its intelligence community. While agents off world were able to avoid perishing in these calamities, many of their handlers, and their supervisors perished on Caspar. For many of these agents in the field, particularly those with long and dangerous careers, this would prove a tempting time to go off the books and allow themselves to be considered amongst the missing and dead. The decision of many of these agents to retire was exacerbated by the concerns many of them had about the individual planetary governments that replaced the Union.

In order to cover the tracks of many of the agents, a significant portion of the Naval Intelligence Office databases on Caspar were purposely sabotaged during the height of the pandemic to destroy records that mind lead to these agents. In the case of [[Sar Admiral]] [[Odie Hood]], who had been on an off the books mission to [[Ixin]] during the pandemic, his yacht was sunk by colleagues back on Caspar to provide false evidence indicating his demise on Caspar. Current Minister of Intelligence, and then Senator of the Dusona province, [[Philinda Jorgan]] was also on Ixin at the time, and likewise stayed hidden to avoid returning to a Mergansar that had betrayed its own people.

Among those agents who survived and stayed in service they were now faced with the choice of where to serve as the Union broke into six separate governments. While the remaining records of various military and security organisations of the Union were available to these new governments, the separation agents into now competing governments meant that each power was effectively rebuilding their intelligence community from scratch. While Ixin benefited from a number of high profile agents in hiding there and the Mergani government was effectively under control of the secretive [[Olumi Osahn]] cult, Caspar was in chaos, barely managing to form even a provisional government.

The basic design of the modern Caspian Intelligence Service came together under the provisional government but it was not until the election of a permanent [[Union Council]] did it truly take shape. The election of [[New Sith Order|Sith]] [[Admiral]] [[Crestian Tarasar]] as [[Presav]] of the [[Caspian Union]] saw Caspar, and soon a growing number of reunited former CDU worlds, led by a man with over a decade of experience in clandestine operations. In light of the [[Imperium-Caspian Mutual Protection Pact (29 ABY)|mutual protection pact]] with the [[Imperium]] as one of his administration first goals, Tarasar integrated a number of former Sith operatives, along with other personal allies into the new Caspian intelligence establishment, seeking in part to create a service diversified enough that both foreign and domestic allies would neither feel threatened nor feel the need to invest in their own separate efforts within [[Caspian Sector|Caspian Space]].

== Philosophy ==
Years of decline leading to the eventual collapse of the Caspian Democratic Union, led to a consensus that the new Caspian Union was in a very precarious situation on the galactic stage. The sheer scope and success of Olumi Osahn terrorism during and after the Sariphage pandemic also led to a consensus that Union was particularly vulnerable to domestic terrorism. It has been the position of the Tarasar administration since its inception that a strong intelligence service is and integral part of keeping the Union safe. The later revelation that the Sariphage had been engineered by [[Basca Starker]] at the behest of [[Galactic Emperor|Emperor]] [[Aleister Vadim]] only served to heighten fears that the Union was under existential threat.

Though many intelligence services adopt a particular ideological bias, the Tarasar administration has strived to ensure the Caspian Intelligence Service is as ideologically neutral as possible. Beyond the desire to focus on threats to the Union versus achieving ideological goals, the Union has to deal with complicated political realities. The multi-party coalition government leading the Union has a little ideological agreement, with the committent to Caspian safety and security the paramount area of consensus.

The advent of the Imperium and the subsequent mutual defence pact with them also provided complications to Tarasar in building the Caspian Intelligence Service. Were the intelligence service to operate too independently of his own personal control it would be likely that Imperator [[Tyler Damion|Damion]] would assign Imperial Security Bureau agents to spy on the Caspian Union which at best would be a duplication of effort and at worst threaten to the Union. On the other hand were the intelligence service to too closely mirror a Sith network it would erode the trust of his allies and risk domestic counter-intelligence efforts. To that end Tarasar has crafted a service where the senior leadership has at least some degree of personal loyalty to him but draws from a wide array of backgrounds and political ideologies.

While the Caspian Intelligence Service does possess a number of experienced agents particularly from the ranks of the former Naval Intelligence Office and Sith Intelligence Network, it is woefully outmatched in this department by other comparable agencies. To fill this gap the intelligence service has focused on recruiting a vast array junior operatives. These operatives come from virtually any conceivable background but are particularly made up of individuals in frequent contact with visiting travellers or engaged in interstellar travel themselves. Essentially any Caspian citizen or expatriate fitting in these two categories might find themselves contacted about the possibility of passing along any information of note they might hear.

Naturally some who are approached about helping the Caspian Intelligence Service may be suspicious of government in general, or the Tarasar administration in particular and opt not to cooperate with these information gathering efforts. However, tempest by which the old CDU fell has left many Caspians feeling victimised and anxious about future threats and thus more willing too cooperate with intelligence gathering efforts to prevent future calamity. In that sense any ideology that can be ascribed to the Caspian Intelligence Service is a soft version of the [[Bothan]] concept of [[:starwars:Ar'krai|Ar'krai]], that is that the Union remains under the threat of extinction and that it is the duty of all to combat that.

The success of the Caspian Intelligence Service under this philosophy has been mixed. While the government itself has generally been satisfied with the development of the intelligence service to date, the limited number of senior agents has limited the scope of operations that have been able to be conducted against specific threats. The failure to find and rescue the passengers and crew abducted from the transport ''Blechley Brook'', for example, remains an embarrassment to the government, particularly among the [[Krittain|Krits]].

== Ranks and Structure ==

=== Director of Intelligence ===

By statute the Caspian Intelligence Service is overseen by a twelve member committee of the Union Council, comprised of Councillors serving as ministers of departments relevant to the mission of the intelligence service. The Intelligence Directorate is chaired by the Minister of Intelligence, who serves as the principle day to day leader of the organisation, with the Ministers of Domestic Affairs and Defence and Foreign Affairs and Defence providing oversight. Meetings of the full Intelligence Directorate occur at least monthly so the members may provide additional oversight and be briefed on current intelligence and operations.

=== Section Chief ===

The Section Chiefs serve as senior leadership of the intelligence service, leading as the name suggests, a section. Like the bureaus, the sections are divided into two distinct types. Each of the geographically based bureaus is divided into sections. For the Caspian Bureau, each section generally corresponds to an individual planet or star system. For the other geographically based bureaus, each is more likely to correspond to at least an entire sector. The other sections are comprised of various administrative departments of the intelligence. While their leaders are subordinate to the Bureau Chiefs, these sections overseen by the Intelligence Directorate itself.

=== Station Master ===

Station Masters serve as the leadership backbone of the intelligence Service. They oversee the operations and personnel of an intelligence station. Intelligence stations and their Station Master's are overseen by the Section Chiefs. Within the jurisdiction of the Caspian Bureau, station operations are usually based around a specific city, within the galaxy at larger they are usually based around a particular planet. Whether focused on just a city or a whole planet, the station's physical presence is usually centred around a particular facility. This facility is based around a business or civic centre that serves to provide some level of cover to the station's activity and existence. This main facility is generally consider a permanent operation, but additional permanent and temporary posts or safe houses may be part of the station as well, depending on the scope of their operations. In the galaxy at large these posts or safe houses may be located on other worlds and part of the operations on a station based on a hub world.

=== Quarter Master ===

Quarter Masters serve as the deputy leaders of intelligence stations. While the Station Master is focused on the overall operations of their station and oversees the operatives and agents involved, the Quarter Masters focus is on the facilities and equipment involved. As such a Quarter Master may serve as the direct second in command at the station's main facility or may be in charge of a larger post or safe house that serve as part of the station's operations.

=== Senior Agent ===

Senior agents represent apex of field operations personnel. In most cases senior agents have spent years working in the intelligence community, likely with a prior background in the [[Constable|constabulary]], [[Naval Intelligence Office]], [[Sith Intelligence Network]], or other professional intelligence or security organisation. A smaller subset of senior agents are individuals whose expertise in key areas or access to important people, secure facilities, or sensitive information have allowed them to prove their judgement and talent to their superiors. Because of the high level of trust placed in them, senior agents are encouraged to operate on their own initiative and are afforded a great deal of latitude in the performance of their duties.

=== Field Agent ===

Like senior agents, field agents generally come from a prior background in the intelligence community or possess special expertise or access. To be promoted to field agent, a field operative must have demonstrated, to the satisfaction of their superiors, an ability to operate independently in the field, and the skills to operate at times with minimal guidance or oversight.

=== Field Operative ===

Field operatives represent the backbone of field personnel of the intelligence service. They may have little to no prior experience related to the intelligence community. What they do possess is a broad enough level of skills to be employed in a variety of locations or situations. As an intelligence service more focused on information gathering than anything, most assignments are carried out be field operatives under the supervision of more senior agents in the field.

=== Street Operative ===

Street operatives are the junior field personnel of the intelligence service. They are trusted enough to engage in active information gathering or other clandestine activity. Street operatives, however, are not considered experienced or trusted enough to engage in complex operations. They are likely either assigned to continued operation in a specific location or assigned to engage in the same specific task.

=== Intelligence Officer ===

Intelligence officers are the so-called "citizen informants" of the intelligence service. They roughly fit into three categories. The first category are individuals who are employed at intelligence facilities and thus must possess some level of security clearance, but whose job does not involve anything overly secretive itself. The second categories are individuals employed a front location but aren't themselves involved in the intelligence gathering side of the operation. They have been trusted enough to be read in on the fact that things aren't completely on the level. Should such individuals manage to show some initiative acquire some useful information they may be promoted to street operative. The final category are those that can more directly be considered citizen informants. They have been recruited to the service and told what information to look for and who to give that information to, but are otherwise uninvolved with intelligence service operations and are unlikely to know much classified information.

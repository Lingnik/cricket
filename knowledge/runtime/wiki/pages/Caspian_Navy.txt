Entrusted with the protection of all of the [[Caspian Democratic Union]], the modern '''Caspian Navy''' is a diverse, highly visible martial entity. What it lacks in history and experience is made up for in its composition, strategy, and technology. It has traditions and practices of the navies of the Republic, the Empire, the Rebel Alliance, and the Hapan Consortium, as well as the defense forces of Corellia, Commenor, and Kuat to draw inspiration from. It is chiefly modeled after the [[:starwars:Judicial Forces|Republic's Judicial Forces]], but has far more powerful starships and less range of jurisdiction than the renowned peacekeeping force ever had.

Starships within the Navy are exceptionally versatile, outfitted to perform not only as battlewagons and starfighter platforms, but as assault ships, transports, and patrol vessels. They've also been called upon to be couriers and to be agents of mercy for humanitarian efforts. Besides the starships, the Navy also supports several key installations and stations throughout Union space, maintains the [[SONG]] network, and regulates all shipping and star traffic through the region.

Personnel are drawn from throughout, and even outside of, commonwealth space. A substantial number of [[Sarian]]s, more so Meditersari over Casohavi, comprise the Naval ranks. There are some bonafide [[Yari]] as well, lending their skills where needed. An even mix of [[Caspia|Caspians]] and [[Krittain|Krittish]], with some [[Rauther|Rautherans]] blended in, fill out the rest. More than a few are formerly of Republic or Imperial enlistment, as the Navy offers structure and opportunity without overwatch or oppression. Many Union citizens have ties to the Navy in some capacity, whether it be Active or Reserve Naval Service, or employment by a company subsidized by [[the Admiralty]].

=== History ===

The modern Caspian Navy had its beginning in the '''[[Barren Sector Fleet]]''' of the [[Caspian Democratic Union|Caspian Democratic Union's]] founding father [[Admiral]] '''[[Dergan Roj]]''' comprised of ships formerly in [[Galactic Empire|Imperial]] service. The arrival of Roj and his fleet soon provided added direction to the engineers of '''[[SubPro]]''' who had established themselves in the [[Caspia|Caspia System]] and native Caspian engineers who joined in this now firmly Caspian corporation.

The first Caspian designed warship the ''[[CMS Hood|Hood]]'' would be completed in [[4 ABY]]. Concurrently with the '''[[Hood-class Battlecruiser|Hood-Class Battlecruisers]]''', SubPro and '''[[Lokkai Starworks]]''' of [[Kasputin Yar]] would engage in the '''[[Daring-class Battlecruiser|Daring Project]]''' to retrofit a dozen warships including the [[Dreadnaught-class Heavy Cruiser|Dreadnaught-Class Heavy Cruiser]], ''[[CMS Daring|Daring]]'' of the Barren Sector Fleet as stop-gap measure until more modern designs were ready for construction.

The establishment of the '''[[CDMC|Caspian Design & Manufacturing Corporand]]''' think tank in [[5 ABY]] would accelerate the production of improved designs beginning with the '''[[Hurricane-class Fleet Carrier|Hurricane-Class Fleet Carrier]]''' and '''[[Shearwater-class Frigate|Shearwater-Class Light Cruiser]]'''. On [[Krittain Major]], '''[[Aston Aerospace]]''' & '''Hixam Sidney Design''' would collaborate on the '''[[Arsenal-class Heavy Cruiser|Arsenal-class Heavy Cruiser]]''' to augment the fleet. The emergence of these new ships would see some of the older vessels of the Barren Sector Fleet such as ''[[CMS Ascension|Ascension]]'' and ''[[CMS Redemption|Redemption]]'' sold to the [[New Republic]].

The first major test of Caspian Navy was in [[8 ABY]] when the Empire attempted to [[Caspian Annexation Campaign|annex]] Caspian space. Caspian forces were quickly overrun and though months of resistance fighting forced an eventual cease-fire and Imperial withdrawal it would lead to further developments in Caspian naval philosophy. This would to revamping the design of future Shearwaters and the development of the '''[[Mergansar-class Heavy Cruiser|Mergansar-Class Heavy Cruiser]]''' to combat limitations in the Shearwaters and to replace the Darings.

Advancement would continue with the emergence of the '''[[Admiral Roj-class Battleship|Admiral Roj-Class Battleship]]''' in [[12 ABY]] as a replacement for the Hoods. Meanwhile on Krittain Major Aston & Sidney would collaborate again on the '''[[Valorous-class Light Carrier|Valorous-Class Light Carrier]]''' based on their earlier Arsenal-class Heavy Cruiser.

=== Recent Years ===

Though the Union has always been a martial power, the [[Caspian Crest|Crest]] always sought to maintained a streamlined defensive force. The drive for an efficient scaled-down force coupled with the increasing reluctance to become involved in galactic conflicts would lead to a fleet that never fully lived up to the aspirations surrounding it. 

The high-water mark of Caspian naval development would come under the administration of [[Presav]] [[Thomas Mahon]] with bold plans to see the Union build sixteen Admiral Roj-Class Battleships with plans being drawn up for more past this initial run. By the end of his administration, however, only eight had been built. 

Subsequent administrations would opt to serious curtail naval production and decommission a number of warships to downsize the fleet. By the time Caspar was caught supplying arms to the New Republic in [[24 ABY]], not only was the Caspian Navy no deterrent to an Imperial blockade, Caspian forces proved unable to both maintain order in the face of riots and secure its borders. Ultimately Caspian space was effectively overrun by Imperial and Republic forces engaged in their own war.

Following amendment to the [[CDU Nonaggression Treaty|non-aggression treaty]] with the Empire which saw the [[Corporate Sector Authority]] given control of customs enforcement in Caspian space, the Navy's role further diminished. Faced with the twin calamites of the Sariphage pandemic on [[Caspar]] and pogroms on [[Mergansar]] the fleet was stretched to its limits. 

In the absence of a surviving Crest to issue orders concerning Mergansar, the Caspian Navy did not get directly involved in any efforts to stop the pogroms and only took action after [[New Sith Order|Sith]] [[Admiral]] and expatriate Caspian [[Crestian Tarasar]] attack the regime responsible on Mergansar. The ensuing [[Mergansar Incident|fight]] between Tarasar's [[Interdictor-class Immobilizer 418|Interdictor-Class Cruiser]] ''[[Leviathan]]'' and the [[Hurricane-class Fleet Carrier|Hurricane-Class Fleet Carrier]] ''[[CMS Typhoon|Typhoon]]'' and three [[Shearwater-class Frigate|Shearwater-Class Light Cruisers]] would see two Shearwaters crippled and the bridge of the Typhoon destroyed killing [[Sar Admiral]] [[Prae Kichakressa]].

Though the Leviathan would be forced to withdraw marking the engagement as a draw, the death of the only Sar Admiral then believed to have survived the plague was a blow to the cohesion of the Navy. As the CDU dismantled the crews of various ships made arrangements with the planetary governments of the now former Union worlds, while others maintained vigil in the [[Caspia|Caspia System]] waiting to see what would come next after the dust settled.

=== Current Philosophy ===

The Navy of the [[Caspian Union]] is comprised of largely the same personnel and ships that belonged to its CDU forerunner but with a distinctly different operating philosophy. The administration of [[Presav]] & [[Sar Admiral]] [[Crestian Tarasar]] considers Caspar to be at risk of future attack, particularly with looming threat posed by former [[Galactic Emperor|Emperor]] [[Aleister Vadim|Vadim]] who instigated the Sariphage pandemic.

The general tendency of Caspian administrations to push for new ship designs to replace older vessels has been placed on the back burner in favour of fielding every warship available. Virtually every warship that is still spaceworthy has been recommissioned with the surviving [[Daring-class Battlecruiser|Daring-Class Battlecruisers]] in particular having gone a second generation of retrofits under the direction of the [[Xi Char]]. The streamlined navy has been replaced with one which stands a far better chance of deterring invasion.

The other key change in perspective has been the abandonment of neutrality in galactic politics in favour of securing alliances in case of future conflict. To that end the Union has signed a [[Imperium-Caspian Mutual Protection Pact (29 ABY)|mutual protection pact]] with the [[Imperium]]. To secure the Union's position in the galaxy roughly one tenth of her total ship compliment is assigned to a dedicated [[Caspian 4th Fleet|expeditionary fleet]].

=== Structure ===
The Navy is directed by '''[[The Admiralty]]''', comprised of five [[Sar Admiral]]s chairing a board comprised of [[Admiral]]s, Generals, and representatives of [[Caspian Crest|the Crest]]. One of the Sar Admirals is designated as chairman by the Crest. Not all of the officers see active-duty in the fleets, as there are some officers that represent facilities, development, personnel, and resources.

Aerospace craft, including fighters, bombers, lifters, transports, and shuttles, are under the direction of the '''[[Naval Aerospace Arm]]'''. These craft are deployed on all manner of Naval possessions, from starships to installations.

Acting as the infantry force of the Navy are the '''[[Union Marines|Marines]]'''. Their senior-most General occupies a chair on the Admiralty board. Marines are the most adept, versatile, and effective fighting force the Union employs, and at least a detachment is on every warship in the Navy, as well as every installation. Marines are also dispatched to foreign embassies to safeguard Union interests.

Small in size, but still significant, the '''[[Naval Intelligence Office]]''' (NIO) keeps the Admiralty well-apprised of information from the galactic community. The officer in charge of this department has the added responsibility of working alongside the [[Union Assembly|Assembly's]] '''Ministry of Information'''. The agency keeps a small pool of  starships, among them the CMS ''Taciturn'', especially for intelligence-gathering. It also closely taps into the SONG network's datastream.

=== Postings ===
:'''Captain / Commanding Officer''' &mdash; Commands, and is responsible for, the actions of the ship. Senior officer aboard (unless an Admiral is present), and all decisions are finalized by the captain.

:'''First Mate / Executive Officer''' &mdash; Second-in-command aboard, responsible for the smooth coordination of crew shifts and component systems. During combat, the First Mate takes up a position in the sensor suite, acting as an analyst and supplemental source of command decisions for the cascade of information coming from the sensors.

:'''PLOT Officer''' &mdash; Acting in an auxiliary, but vital, role in Caspian starship operations, the '''P'''lanning, '''L'''ogistics, '''O'''perations & '''T'''actics Officer is a peripheral organic component that coordinates everything the commanding officers see, hear, and are informed of. Chiefly, during operations, the PLOT station keeps a running record of the ship's own position and direction, the position of other friendly starships, the position of enemy starships, the registration of all contacts and assets, and offers a voice of perspective to the captain, based on "the facts". This position is usually taken by a junior officer, assisted by numerous warrant officers, and is in constant contact with the squadron leaders, the Chief of the Watch, and the Boatswain.

:'''Fire Controls Officer''' &mdash; Also known as the Weapons Officer, this position oversees all weapons targetting, discharging, and maintaining. Often located in the Fire Director of the ship, and not on the bridge, but does take commands from the commanding officer directly.

:'''Flight Deck Operations / Landing Signals Officer''' &mdash; Specialized officer in charge of the flight deck and all physical operations thereupon. Often has enough seniority to be third in command, should the need arise. Only the larger starships have this position.

:'''Chief Engineer''' &mdash; Officer in charge of the propulsion, power, and electronics systems. Has several assistants, and directs operations from the Engineer's Bay.

:'''Ship's Surgeon''' &mdash; Highly educated and specially trained in all medical proceedings. Oversees the infirmary on the starship, and may be consulted on scientific matters. Usually has an assistant, acting as an intern in training for a posting on another starship. Officer may be of a Marine rank, depending on the starship.

:'''Chief of the Watch''' &mdash; Senior non-com officer on a Caspian ship, charged with the maintenance of the physical ship itself. By way of experience, he's often the coordinator or "interface" between the officers and the enlistees. He seems to be everywhere at once.

:'''Boatswain''' &mdash; Non-com officer who oversees all secondary or support craft, such as lifters, shuttles, tankers, and skiffs. On smaller starships, manages the flight deck; on cruisers or carriers, assists the FDO and LSO directly.

:'''Master At Arms''' &mdash; Non-com officer who reports directly to the Executive Officer (XO), and handles the issuing of weapons, forming of security details, and maintains the brig. Also assists in the presiding of courts martial.

:'''Quartermaster''' &mdash; Assistant to the Chief of the Watch, tasked with management of the ship's compartments, holdings, and supplies. In larger ships, there may be several quartermasters.

:'''Midshipman''' &mdash; Various entry-level junior officers stationed throughout the ship at specific locations to coordinate the efforts of crewers, whether it be damage control, fire support, electronics operation, or flight operations.

:'''Helmsman''' &mdash; Enlistee trained and tasked for rotations at the helm, often of a higher rank than common Ablemen of other employments. 

:'''Technician''' or '''Machinist''' &mdash; Enlistee aboard warships charged with the maintenance and support of aerospace craft berthed aboard; or, posted to the operation of electronics gear or support tools within the ship.

:'''Ableman''' &mdash; Enlistee given a specific task or series of tasks aboard a ship. Their responsibilities may be rather generous, but they always have to answer to either a Crew Chief or a Midshipman. Much more capable level over a '''Probational''' or '''Probie'''.

=== Starships ===
The CDU's fleet has been characterized as a "frigate navy", due to being heavily weighted towards smaller warships (smaller vessels requiring inevitably less assembly infrastructure).  Though this characterization has often been made in derogatory terms, and more than a few foreigners have turned up their noses at the Union's "toys". Its frigates carry a lot more of the proportional workload than those of the [[Galactic Empire|Imperial]] and [[New Republic]] fleets. The current [[Shearwater-class Frigate|''Shearwater-class'']] frigate is representative of the CDU's highly refined approach to its smaller warships. The Fleet does possess a highly capable and growing force of Capital Ships (in CDU Navy terms, this term applies to Fleet Carriers, Battleships, and Battlecruisers). In [[15 ABY]], the Union possessed or was on the verge of completing 24 such modern vessels (divided evenly between carriers and battleships/battlecruisers). Current defense white-papers suggest that this figure will likely grow in coming years.

Regardless of size and role, Union warships are invariably designed to make the most of available manpower, and hence tend to have smaller crews (and more automation) for their sizes than is typical in designs used by the great powers. Electronics likewise tend to be top-notch, making comparisons of capabilities between Union and foreign designs tricky. On paper, a Union vessel might be outgunned by a foreign counterpart, the quality of the CDU vessel's fire control and sensor systems often means that the Union warship will be able to land more blows per salvo, and land them first. Considering the Caspian penchant for fitting its vessels with hard-hitting anti-shipping missiles, this is not a threat to be taken likely.

All starships of Caspia are prefixed with either '''C'''aspian '''M'''ilitary '''S'''hip (CMS) or '''C'''aspian '''C'''ommercial '''S'''hip (CCS). Ships names may originate from any of the Union's cultures, though more than a few have origins in the Old Republic (e.g. CMS ''Repulse'', CMS ''Ascension'', etc). Some confuse a ship's name as a sign of the vessel's affiliation, thinking that a Krittish name indicated Krittish affiliation. In practice, all ships belong to the Union, making the Navy one of the largest collaborative efforts of the Union's people. On small ships, a crew might conceivably be drawn from a single culture, but larger vessels inevitably have mixed crews. This has led to the Fleet being a major 'melting pot', and a symbol of unity.

=== Fleets ===

The starships of the Caspian Navy are distributed among four fleets. Three of these take their nickname from the former fleets that operated during the early years of the old Caspian Democratic Union. As part of the negotiations that led to the multi-party coalition in the Union Council and the subsequent passage of a mutual defence pact with the Imperium it was decided that the bulk of the fleet, roughly 90%, would be dedicated to purely defensive operations.

The '''[[Caspian 1st Fleet]]''' or Crown Winds Fleet is currently charged with the defence of the central portion of Caspian space. This primarily consists of protecting the [[Caspia]], [[Rautheran System|Rautheran]], and [[Yariz]] systems. The fleet is under the command of Fleet Admiral Cordelia Shale and headquartered at [[Caspar]].

The '''[[Caspian 2nd Fleet]]''' or Might River Fleet is currently charged with the defence of the 'northern' portion of Caspian space. This primarily consists of protecting the [[Krittain]] system and the installations on [[Ketterslea]] and [[Tairoa]]. The fleet is under the command of Fleet Admiral Katha Pushtelli and headquartered at [[Krittain Major]].

The '''[[Caspian 3rd Fleet]]''' or Iron Torch Fleet is currently charged with the defence of the 'southern' portion of Caspian space. This primarily consists of protecting the worlds of [[Charros IV]], [[Ixin]], and [[Mikassa]].  The fleet is under the command of Fleet Admiral Loknar Orio and headquartered at [[Charros IV]].

The '''[[Caspian 4th Fleet]]''' or Dragon's Breath Fleet operates under a very different mandate. Under the direct command of [[Sar Admiral]] [[Crestian Tarasar]] it is designated as Caspar's expeditionary fleet and is authorised to be deployed outside Caspian space without special approval. 

While it is only a third of the size other fleets, boasting roughly one tenth of the Navy's total fleet strength, it is augmented by additional outside vessels. Tarasar's personal starship, the '''[[Interdictor-class Immobilizer 418|Interdictor-Class Cruiser]]''', the ''[[Leviathan]]'', and her sister ship, the ''[[Dying Sun]]'' serve as part of the fleet. In addition as part of the [[Imperium-Caspian Mutual Protection Pact (29 ABY)|mutual defence pact]] with the Imperium two '''[[Warlord-class Medium Cruiser|Warlord-Class Cruisers]]''', the ''[[Starkiller]]'' and ''[[Tethys]]'', have been placed under Tarasar's command. These additional starships represent almost one sixth of the fleet's strength.

The starships assigned to 4th Fleet are often the most decorated vessels in the Caspian Navy with their assignment into the fleet considered a mark of honour upon the vessel. The fact that this fleet might be called upon to assist in Imperium operations has made it a less popular assignment among officers critical of the defence pact. Transfers out are granted whenever possible, leaving the crew on these vessels more supportive of Caspian involvement with the Imperium compared to the other fleets.

The defensive fleets are further divided into three Defence Groups, and a Patrol Group.

The Defence Groups are centred upon an '''[[Admiral Roj-class Battleship|Admiral Roj-Class Battleship]]''' and named after the planet or system they are normally charged with defending. In addition to the Admiral Roj they each contain a '''[[Hurricane-class Fleet Carrier|Hurricane-Class Fleet Carrier]]''', '''[[Daring-class Battlecruiser|Daring-Class Battlecruiser]]''', three '''[[Mergansar-class Heavy Cruiser|Mergansar-Class Heavy Cruisers]]''', nine '''[[Shearwater-class Frigate|Shearwater-Class Light Cruisers]]''', and various auxiliary vessels.

The Patrol Groups are centred upon a '''[[Hood-class Battlecruiser|Hood-Class Battlecruiser]]''' and named after the nickname of the overall fleet they belong to. In addition to the Hood, they each contain a '''Hurricane-Class Fleet Carrier''', two '''[[Valorous-class Light Carrier|Valorous-Class Light Carriers]]''', and '''[[Arsenal-class Heavy Cruiser|Arsenal-Class Heavy Cruiser]]''', three '''Mergansar-Class Heavy Cruisers''', three '''Shearwater-Class Light Cruisers''', and various auxiliary vessels. Each Patrol Group operates within the section of Caspian space their fleet is assigned to.

The structure of the 4th fleet is more irregular compared to the defence fleet. Based on its expeditionary nature it is far more likely at any given time that the fleet is operating as a whole in one location or that individual vessels may be operating at separate locations. For organisational purposes the fleet is subdivided into three Task Groups named after the three largest vessels within the fleet, two Admiral Roj-Class Battleships and a Hood-Class Battlecruiser.

In addition to these ships, the fleet contains a Hurricane-Class Fleet Carrier, a Valorous-Class Light Carrier, a Daring-Class Battlecruiser, five Mergansar-Class Heavy Cruisers, ten Shearwater-Class Light Cruisers, the above named Interdictors and Warlords, and various auxiliary vessels, with these ships somewhat evenly among the Task Groups.

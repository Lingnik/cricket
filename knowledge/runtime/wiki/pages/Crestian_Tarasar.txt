Crestian Tarasar ([[10 BBY]] –) is a former [[New Republic|Republic]] naval officer and academic administrator who later resigned his commission in frustration with the inefficacy of its government and military in being able to defend their worlds. After a short time as an independent operator he was recruited into the [[New Sith Order]] where he ultimately rose to being a [[Duke]] and [[Admiral]]. 

Keeping a low profile after [[Tyler Damion|Darth Malign]]'s disappearance, he is one of the few high ranked survivors of [[Aldus Thel]]'s purge of the Sith, and was in prime position for favourable patronage on the return of Darth Malign to the wider galaxy. He re-emerged in wider galactic society in the midst of the Sariphage [[Report:Sariphage Press Releases|pandemic]] that wiped most of his homeworld [[Caspar|Caspar's]] population and the refugee crisis on its sister world [[Mergansar]] where he attempted to violently stop the [[Report:Mergani Pogrom Press Releases|pogroms]] of rogue [[Olumi Osahn]] cultists. 

Returning to [[Caspar]] following the end of the quarantine he built up a sizeable power base through his charity [[The Mission]]. Along with [[Zaalbacca]], the Chairman of the [[Wookiee Workers Union of Anchorron]], he formed [[Praecet]], a political party to contest the [[Report:Caspian Election Posts|elections]] for a permanent [[Union Council]]. Crestian was elected as the Councillor for [[Trinumvira]] and formed coalition goverment with the [[Legacy]] party, leading to his election as [[Presav]]. 

Almost immediately Crestian negotiated a [[Imperium-Caspian Mutual Protection Pact (29 ABY)|mutual protection pact]] with the [[Imperium]], greatly improving the Union's defensive position and opening the door to a substantive amount of trade. Under his leadership the new [[Caspian Union]] has also managed to reunite with all its former member worlds as well as admit [[Charros IV]] as a new one. Though small compared to the Imperium and New Republic, the Union has emerged as one of the most stable, secure, and prosperous powers in the galaxy.
== Early Life: [[10 BBY]] - [[13 ABY]] ==

Crestian Tarasar was born in the city of [[Trinumvira]] on the planet [[Caspar]] ten years before the Battle of Yavin. His family was one of high social standing; his father Petyrian, was a diplomat of some minor important and his mother Cresia, a well loved school teacher. In spite of racial tendency among [[Sarian|Sarians]] to not place importance on material possessions, the Tarasar family managed to accumulate quite a number of them. In spite of the tumult in the galaxy Crestian had a comfortable childhood.

The formation of the [[Caspian Democratic Union]] four years after the Battle of Yavin saw major upheaval for the Tarasars. While the general feeling on Caspar was to establish an independent government, Petyrian saw such neutrality in the face of galactic strife as a sign of moral cowardice and opted to move the family to [[Chandrila]] in the [[New Republic]]. Petyrian's conviction in this matter did much to motivate Crestian's aims as an adolescent.

Two years later Crestian would enrol at the Brionelle Memorial Military Academy for the academy's two year training programme in ethics, leadership, small-unit tactics and team-work. Cadets who successfully completed this programmed had the option to specialise in either training for either the army or the navy for their third and final year with Crestian opting to go the naval route. Following his graduation was commissioned as an Ensign in the [[New Republic FleetOps|Navy]]. 

Though exceedingly proficient in communications and sensor systems, he found he had a much greater affinity for the deep questions of ethics and leadership. Given the choice between a posting as communications officer on the newly launched [[VSD Fusillade|Fusillade]] or a year of post-graduate study in command ethics at the academy, Crestian opted for the latter. After completing these subsequent studies he was promoted to Lieutenant and joined the faculty as an Instructor of Ethics.

Over the next four years Crestian would prove a popular instructor and member of the faculty, rapidly rising to become Associate Academic Dean of the institution which came with a promotion to Captain. Though nominally a member of the the New Republic's Navy it seemed clear to everyone that Crestian was just a uniformed academic. He lent a level of academic gravitas and bureaucrat efficiency to school which made any consideration of posting him elsewhere seem misguided.

== Thrown from the Ivory Tower: [[14 ABY]] - [[15 ABY]] ==

[[File:Crestian_in_14_ABY.jpg|right|thumb]]The [[Imperial Blitzkrieg]] would put an end to Crestian's academic career. Faced with the overwhelming Imperial presence Crestian took his own personal [[CR90 Corvette]], the ''Argent Drake'', and used it to evacuate several members of the faculty and student body from Chandrila by exploiting a gap in the [[Galactic Empire|Imperial]] fleet's sensor coverage. Rather than trying to assign him to a naval vessel, the New Republic opted to allow Crestian to continue operate on his own vessel which he would use mainly for courier duty in the subsequent months of the war. Some of his former colleagues and students were now serving as his crew on the ''Argent Drake''.

Thrown from his ivory tower, Crestian found himself quickly disillusioned with the war and with the Republic. Though he had no love of the Empire, he found that the New Republic was no better poised to bring stability to the galaxy than the Old Republic. Neither side had the proper philosophical vision and practical organisation to really achieve their goals. Eventually he would resign his commission and take his operations independent, seeking to keep himself and his friends in good operating condition for when a worthy cause manifested.

During this time Crestian and his crew would come across an abandoned outpost in the [[:starwars:Doldur sector/Legends|Doldur Sector]] named [[Silver Station]]. Moving the station closer to the [[Dragonflower Nebula]] to avoid any future detection, he would use it as the base for smuggling operations in the Mid and Outer Rim. While not the traditional type of scoundrel usually found in such occupations, Crestian added a touch of the refined that allowed him to more easily make contacts among classier elements.

== In Sith Service: [[16 ABY]] - [[18 ABY]] ==

[[File:Crestian_in_17_ABY.jpg|right|thumb]] Eventually Crestian and his operations came to the notice of [[Tyler Damion|Darth Malign]] and his [[Fleet Admiral]] [[Drayson Honos]]. The [[New Sith Order]] that Malign was building had many great minds and many great fighters under its banner. Where Sith has always been weaker was in terms of effective structures that served past the whims of individuals. Crestian Tarasar was just the sort of person that the Sith Navy needed. For his own part Crestian saw Malign as somewhat of a kindred spirit. Someone with the vision to bring order to the galaxy but someone capable of the intellectual nuisances, not too drawn to the dogma that had shackled both Jedi and Sith to continued failures for millennia.

Crestian's rise within the New Sith Order's navy and nobility would be swift. His organisational skills and charisma would see him rise to the rank of [[Admiral]] commanding '''Task Force 77'''. Conceived as a compliment to the work of the [[Knights of Korriban]], Task Force 77 took on missions not quite important enough to be assigned to a Knight of Korriban or which required a more organised force to execute. The task force was based around an [[Interdictor-class Immobilizer 418|Interdictor]], named ''[[Leviathan]]'' after the flagship of [[:starwars:Darth Malak|Darth Malak]], and four prototype Barb Interceptors. In addition to the naval and marine personnel assigned to the ''Leviathan'' several members of the [[Sith Intelligence Network]] were assigned to Crestian's command. Their mission would vary widely and included everything from artefact collection, clandestine infiltration, false flag operations, and political sabotage among other things.

Crestian would also be granted title of [[Duke]] in the Sith nobility attached to his existing assets around the Dragonflower Nebula as a mark of Darth Malign's favour. In many respects the Duke of Dragonflower was the least traditionally Sith member of the nobility, but in an organisation filled with power-hungry homicidal maniacs, Crestian managed to carve a spot as a comparatively easy going individual.

While not a force sensitive himself, Crestian was well versed in many of the key lessons of being Sith, the most important being to have an exit strategy. Seeing the writing on the wall when Malign disappeared, Admiral Tarasar took the ''Leviathan'' and parked it in the Dragonflower Nebula to serve as his secret base of operations until Malign returned. With the Empire's purge of the Sith, Crestian saw no other commander who had any hope of standing against them and he had no desire to go down in some suicidal blaze of glory. The fanatics would die and be forgotten, but Crestian, his command, and the [[House Dragonflower]] would survive for future glory.

== Interregnum: [[18 ABY]] - [[27 ABY]] ==

[[File:Crestian Sith Uniform.jpg|right|thumb]] Following the collapse of the New Sith Order, Crestian lived a relatively quite life on Silver Station. The extraction of prothium gas from the Dragonflower Nebula would provide an export income, with his crew occasionally venturing out into the wider galaxy for the odd mercenary job here and there as well. Contracts, whether for gas sales or mercenary work would be carefully chosen to avoid drawing too much attention. The face he showed to the galaxy was that of a 'failed' Sith flag officer running a shadowport. 

Rumours would naturally circulate that he still possessed Sith assets of one form or another but to most he just came across as a recluse keeping close to home most of the time. When his activities were more public, what most of the galaxy saw was an eccentric dandy, hardly the prize most Imperial officers wanted to claim as the last Sith they eliminated, no one wanted to embarrass themselves bring a fleet to bear against him to potentially find none of what was rumoured. As the years came and went interest in Crestian's Sith past waned.

During the time Crestian would marry and his wife would give birth to a daughter, [[Atsvara Tarasar|Atsvara]]. For the first several years of her life, Atsvara would not venture outside Silver Station and the ''Leviathan'' hidden the Dragonflower Nebula. Their seclusion though, allowed Crestian and his wife the time to be attentive to their daughter's upbringing. Along with younglings of others of his officers and crew who remained, Atsvara would attend a school on the station with a Sith influenced cirriculum. When circumstances allowed Crestian to travel he would always bring back [[Biscuit Baron]] [[:starwars:Jolly Meal|Jolly Meal]] toys to Atsvara which would launch an obsession for her.

== Renewed Purpose: [[27 ABY]] - [[28 ABY]] ==

[[File:Crestian_in_27_ABY.jpg|right|thumb]] Crestian's quite life would eventually come to an end with Darth Malign emerging after a decade trapped on [[Korriban]] having been rescued by his half-sister the [[Jedi Order|Jedi]] Knight [[Johanna Siri te Danaan]]. As a rare example of someone who 'kept the faith' and survived Crestian and his crew were now central to Darth Malign's plans to rebuild the Sith and ultimately take on the Emperor [[Aleister Vadim]]. Relishing renewed sense of purpose the Crestian began to make the ''Leviathan'' ready as a base of operations for what he conceived of as a Resurrected Sith Order.

His Sith plans would soon take a back seat as a [[Report:Sariphage Press Releases|plague]] broke out on his homeworld of Caspar and Crestian took ''Leviathan'' back home to see what help he could render, if any. He found the world under a self imposed quarantine with the [[Caspian Defence Force|military]] having apparently been ordered to maintain a blockade of the world until the population died off. Though no one seemed particularly overjoyed as Crestian's appearance his ship was given an assignment with the fleet and the surviving [[Sar Admiral]] [[Prae Kichakressa]] suggesting that he could be of most use dealing with [[Olumi Osahn]], a formerly Sith-aligned cult on the Sarian homeworld [[Mergansar]] which was using the plague as excuse to launch [[Report:Mergani Pogrom Press Releases|pogroms]] against the world's non-Sarian citizens.

Efforts by Crestian along with Darth Malign to bring Olumi Osahn back in line were rebuffed. Considering the cult to both be an insolent rogue element of the Sith and also a threat to innocent Caspian citizens, it was decided they were to be put down. To that end, Crestian began to recruit and arm refugees that had flowed onto [[Kichnar Orbital Station]], organising them into a paramilitary movement he dubbed [[Casulmis]].

An incursion of into the city of Hebron, a central point of the cults pogroms, with Casulmis vigilantes did much to disrupt their efforts. This was followed by bombing key targets associated with the cult, killing several of their leaders. Things were looking up until Sar Admiral Kichakressa decided to intervene to stop the mission she herself had effectively authorised. The subsequent naval [[Mergansar Incident|battle]] between ''Leviathan'' and four Caspian ships left two of the Caspian ships crippled and a third heavily damaged with the Sar Admiral killed when the bridge of her flagship was blown out. ''Leviathan'' meanwhile was heavily damaged herself having been fired upon first, and Crestian was forced to limp back home to Silver Station.

== Return to Caspar: [[28 ABY]] - [[29 ABY]] ==

[[File:Crestian with a Burger.jpg|right|thumb]] The decimation of Caspar's population and the pogroms on Mergansar would spell the end of the CDU as government. Hope, however, would return in a cure for the Sariphage developed by [[Basca Starker]]. When the quarantine on Caspar was lifted and the dissolution of the old government was complete, Crestian returned to Trinumvira as a private citizen. While there were many who still wanted to see him punished for his actions at Mergansar, there were just as many empathetic to his cause, and with the old Union gone the provisional government did not care to stop his return or seek to arrest him.

Knowing that rebuilding his reputation would take time he did not involve himself directly in the political scene beyond Trinumvira, though he supported like-minded individuals behind the scenes. Instead Crestian started a charity called [[The Mission]]. The Mission would direct significant financial aide to Caspar to support the survivors of the plague and refugees of the pogroms, much of that aid quietly coming form Sith-aligned figures. Having been disappointed by the response of the [[Groton Sciences Institute]] to the Sariphage pandemic, The Mission would establish [[Twin Suns Hospital]] as a new research facility.

Crestian's efforts would be helped considerably by his best friend, the [[Kubaz]] fashionista, [[Zubindi Hakoon]], who he convinced to establish a a new location of the [[Silverwood House of Fashion]] in Trinumvira, which became his new de facto headquarters. He would also bring [[Kasha Tabasuri]], a [[Mutanda|Mutandan]] spiritual advisor he met on [[Tatooine]] to join his efforts at The Mission, becoming one of his top civilian aides. His charity work would also introduce him to figures like [[Zaalbacca]] of [[Anchorron]] and [[Andra Eleria]] of [[Noques Rasala]], who would not only support his efforts at The Mission, but also become two of his most important political allies.

Crestian found his efforts as a charity director quite successful and as the time approached for the provisional government to give way to a new constitution and elections for a permanent government, he decided the time was right to publicly involve himself in planetary politics. Together with Zaalbacca he would establish a political party called [[Praecet]] to contest the election. They would use their support within the provisional assembly to draft a constitution favourable to their election strategy, with the new permanent [[Union Council]] consisting of nine first-past-the-post seats up for grabs. This would include a seat for each of Caspar's major cities, as well as a seat each for Kichnar Orbital Station and [[Mikassa]].

Praecet was able to coast to an easy victory with seven of the nines seats with both Crestian and Zaalbacca handily winning their seats. The election would also see Andra elected for Noques Rasala in spite of a tougher competition in Noques Rasala. The election of leading [[SubPro]] engineer [[Sirlahn Inglet]] for Kichnar Orbital Station and Casulmis veteran and Mergani refugee [[Sha Dark]] for [[Orleanon]] delivered Crestian a solid team.

With Praecet holding the majority of seats, Crestian's election as [[Presav]] was foregone conclusion. Praecet, however, made the unprecedent choice to invite the [[Legacy]] party, which won the other two seats, into a coalition government. It was hoped that by working with Legacy and allowing them opporunities to advance some of their policy goals, Praecet would be able to more easily enact their own key agenda items. Crestian and Zaalbacca would nominate Legacy's leader [[Cas Mos]] of [[Groton]] as Vice Presav.

== Behind the Scenes ==

Years ago the player had a boring and ill-conceived character in the CDU named [[Sadim Gnik]]. Miserable in the faction the player was a whiny bastard. Eventually the character and player joined the controversial SW1 spinoff Star Wars: New Worlds where he thrived on The Wheel and did a brief stint as a Royal named Silver. When that game ended he led the continuation of the game as Star Wars: Age of Strife where he was the MUSH Director and Theme Wizard and Confederation Faction Head. On Age of Strife Sadim renamed himself to Venelav of Besh Gorgon and had a super fun time. Convinced in 2018 to rejoin SW1 by [[Tyler Damion|Tyler's]] player, he conceived of a character similar to post-SW1 Sadim/Venelav. He would later create a version of his fashionista alt, [[Zubindi Hakoon]], to aggrandize the culture of the grid. In time the player would take over management of the Caspian org, the wiki, and most recently he has been elevated to serve as a Wizard. Beyond gaming the player lives, works, and volunteers in a small New England town.

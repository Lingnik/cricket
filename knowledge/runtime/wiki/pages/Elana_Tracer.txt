Noble of spirit and fiercely loyal to her homeworld, [[Elana Tracer]] began as a simple tomboy with a rough, though not overly tragic, beginning who just wanted to be a pilot. She later evolved into a capable warrior, an example to her peers, and a faithful friend. Holding her own in a galaxy much more sophisticated than her early life prepared her for, she managed to forge relationships with some of the most powerful sentients in the galaxy and yet for a long time has retained her honest, self-effacing manner.

== Biography ==

=== Military Career ===
Working her way up the ranks of the [[Caspia]]n [[Marine Aerospace Division]], this top [[A-wing]] pilot was given a field promotion during the [[Caspian Annexation Campaign|Imperial War of Aggression]] and was eventually appointed Commander of the [[CDU Third Fleet|3rd Fleet's]] fighter wings following [[Joshua Arands]] departure, transferring her commission to the [[Naval Aerospace Arm|NAA]]. Prior to that she acted in many missions, many if them covert. One of which, early in the Caspian Annexation Campaign, caught the attention of the Tsaren Joir, Lord [[Jarl Rellik]] and the [[Bureau of Operations|Imperial BofO]] and for a while she was held as their prisoner, only to be released during talks between the factions prior to the brief but deadly [[Caspian Annexation Campaign|Imperial Occupation of Caspar]]. She helped lead the [[CDU Third Fleet|fleet's]] return and became a well-known hero of the retaking of Caspia. To those close to her it appeared, however, that the war marked the loss of not only her innocence, but also her sense of well-being.

=== Racing & Civilian Life ===
Outside her military career, she achieved galactic press as the story of her affair with the wealthy [[Sarian]] [[FineLine Spaceways|FLS]] CEO [[Benjamin Jawatha]], whom she later married, was splashed across the tabloids in an attempt to defame him due to her lack of social standing and rough attitude. She was also known to have a close association with the well-known business man and playboy [[Troy McTavish]] for whom she began racing swoops to some notoriety as a competitor. For some reason whenever she lost her temper in public, [[InterGalactic News|IGNews]] picked it up if possible and broadcast it. As is often the case, those who gain powerful friends also can make powerful enemies and Elana Tracer was never an exception.

Tracer married [[Benjamin Jawatha]] on the eve of the hot war with the Empire. In the command center of Trinumvira base, the two said their vows in a ceremony performed by [[Troy McTavish]]. But after the war the Jawatha marriage was fraught with the disagreements of youth and bravado, compounded by Elana's trauma from her time in captivity, though the birth of their baby boy Kipran brought a time of joy for the couple. Then, while traveling back from a swoop race, Elana's ship broke down leaving her stranded until she was picked up by the cult leader Amadeus and was suffering from hypoxia. Amnesia resulted but, after months with the Covenant of Fallen Stars cult, she later returned to herself and returned home. 

By the time Elana began her affair with [[Morrison Van Sen]], the young squadron commander's marriage was for all intents and purposes over but the divorce did happen after she gave into her feelings for the CSA ExO. Months later she would resign her position as Colonel and Wing Commander and put her energy into her swoop racing career. Then Troy McTavish told her he was going on a walkabout and gave titles to all of his swoop properties and garages along with a tidy sum of cash. With this and her racing earnings she founded Repulsor Sports Incorporated and one by one acquired most of the swoop tracks and garages in the galaxy. During this time she founded the Galactic Swoop League and served as its president for some time. She never married Van Sen but had two children with him - a girl, Ashton, and a boy they named Longinus. With Ben's frequent disappearances Morrison became a substitute father to Elana's first child Kipran Jawatha. But before her eldest boy was grown, another man would disappear from his young life leaving Kipran and his siblings abandoned. 

=== Politics ===
Elana Tracer evolved from soldier to stateswoman when her commander and mentor Salonkia Batrad asked her to serve as Vice Presav. Later she became the Presav of the CDU, although she was met by massive controversy when she caved in to the Imperial ''[[Proclamation of Annexation]]'' of [[14 ABY]]. The Imperials named her Moff of the sector but she never accepted that title. Even though the [[Union Assembly|Assembly]] defeated the measure when it was presented, Presav Tracer retained the document, and signed it for unknown reasons. Several Delegates and a pair of [[Senator of the Union|Senators]] called for a ''countervote'', a rare occurrence in Caspian history, and that refuted her action. Elana then closed and disbanded the Senate. Promptly after, events were set in motion to have her removed from office.  Little did anyone know all of her actions in office were made to keep those she loved and the people she lead safe. When Thomas Mahon came for the office, Elana stepped aside willingly.

=== Kalla ===
After Tracer left office and handed the reins to Thomas Mahon she could have retreated to her Marin Mountain swoop racing resort but felt it would be best to take her young children away from Caspar and live a quiet life. Morrison was from Kalla and had, early in their relationship, flown Elana to the tropical planet to witness the most incredible sunset. It was there that Elana decided she would raise her children. They lived for more than ten years in relative anonymity on an elite island where Morrison owned an expansive gated and secure estate with more than a mile of patrolled private beach. At that time Morrison had moved on from the CSA back to the Empire he began with, serving the very regime that had lost Elana her beloved Caspar, in the Imperial ground forces. The couple weathered the political forces outside of their relationship until the day Morrison didn't return home from a deployment and was reported missing. Elana has remained heartbroken at the loss.

=== Return to Public Life ===
When Elana's eldest, Kipran Jawatha, left home and then dropped out of communication with her, she hired investigators. When they failed after a year, she took it upon herself to find him. With her younger two busy at school on [[Kalla VII]], Tracer followed leads that eventually lead her to Kipran, his new mentors and a new life for Elana. Having taken power over the Imperial worlds and declaring it an Imperium, the Imperator [[Tyler Damion]] offered her the opportunity to serve as Governor over Selene and Athaniss as well as become CEO of KDY-SYS. Seeing this as a way to stay close to Kipran's new life, and placing hope that this new Imperium would be all that Malign promised, she accepted the offer and became Moff of the Selene Sector. At this time she found a friend and ally in the newest Presav who had managed to bring Caspar into the Imperium's fold, assuring it's independent rule free from further occupations and aggressions. She began moving back into Caspian life and society thanks to [[Crestian Tarasar]].

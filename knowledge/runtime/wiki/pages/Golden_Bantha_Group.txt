The '''Golden Bantha Group''' is an consulting and investment firm founded by [[Atsvara Tarasar]] in [[32 ABY]] in order to facilitate the takeover of [[Biscuit Baron]] in the wake of mass labour strikes the restaurant chain was suffering through. Golden Bantha Group was able to get hired by the [[Corporate Sector Authority|CSA]] and its subsidiaries to take a central role in managing the chain, ultimately negotiating the end to the strikes and a return to profitability. In [[33 ABY]] the Golden Bantha Group was able to negotiate the [[Report:Biscuit Buyout|buyout]] of Biscuit Baron from the CSA with the Golden Bantha Group board of directors taking over officially as the board of directors for Biscuit Baron.

==Origin==

[[Atsvara Tarasar]] was born on [[Silver Station]] at a time when her father, [[Crestian Tarasar]], was living in semi-exile following the collapse of the [[New Sith Order]]. She was raised with a Sith based education that taught her to believe both that she was better than everyone else, but also that she had the ability and right to impose her will on the universe. The philosophies imparted on her would be nuanced than the Sith background would normally suggest. The name she was given by her father, 'Atsvara,' is Sith for 'balance,' and she was taught the supremacy of will could be balance with noble intent.

Unable to take her with him on his few trips outside of the [[Dragonflower Nebula]], Crestian began a habit of bringing Atsvara Jolly Meal toys for [[Biscuit Baron]]. This imprinted Atsvara with a love of the restaurant franchise and Crestian would work find old [[:starwars:Jolly Meal|Jolly Meal]] toys until his daughter's collection soon include every Jolly Meal toy ever produced by Biscuit Baron.

When the Tarasar family emerged from exile to relocate to [[Caspar]] in the wake of the Sariphage [[Report:Sariphage Press Releases|pandemic]] it presented Atsvara opportunities to live up to her Sith upbringing and her love of Biscuit Baron. By the time Crestian was elected [[Presav]] of the [[Caspian Union]], Atsvara had already made contacts among the executives of Biscuit Baron and its parent companies including [[:starwars:Tagge Company/Legends|Tagge Company]] Vice President of Marketing [[Izara Tagge]].

Atsvara would also use her new surrounding for financial gain, embezzling money from her father and beginning to build her own investment portfolio. While other parents who punish a child for such theft or be concerned with their spending and contact with strangers, Crestian was proud of his daughter's initiative. Crestian would largely pretend that he did not know what his daughter was doing and she would likewise pretend like she did not know that he knew. The fact that Crestian would openly discuss delicate political, military, and business dealings at the family dinner table gave Atsvara an inside track on many sound investments and she would piggyback off of his connexions and travels to conduct her own business dealings.

==Biscuit Baron==

When Biscuit Baron began to face widespread labour issues in [[32 ABY]], Atsvara saw an opportunity to utilise her wealth and her connexions within Biscuit Baron to take over the restaurant chain. Secretly working with confederates among labour leaders, politicians, journalists, and within Biscuit Baron itself she sought to widen the wedge between the workers unions and the company in order to temporarily devalue it to drive the [[Corporate Sector Authority|CSA]] to be willing to sell it off. Though she truly appreciated the company and its employees she knew if she could take hold of it she would be able to deliver labour contracts that would be more beneficial to the employees and that she had the connexions necessary to restore any lost value post-takeover.

As part of her plan to convince CSA to divest itself of Biscuit Baron, she secretly reached out to some former CSA officials she felt would be interested in breaking up the CSA as a single merged corporation and return its constituent parts to an independent status. In order to communicate anonymously she took inspiration from her stuffed [[:starwars:Banthan/Legends|bantha]] and Biscuit Baron's [[:starwars:Bantha Breakfast Biscuit|Bantha Breakfast Biscuit]] and communicate with a golden bantha avatar. When these plans failed to generate a favourable response she temporarily abandoned plans for a buyout, instead shifting to the formation of a firm which she could convince Biscuit Baron to hire to navigate their numerous strikes across multiple jurisdictions. As she had collaborators on both sides of the labour dispute she was able to engineer the terms necessary to successfully bring a resolution to the stalled negotiations.

==Golden Bantha Group==

Negotiating the end of the strikes and rebuilding the company's image were always intended as a temporary measure while she worked to convince the CSA to sell Biscuit Baron. In spite of her own wealth, she knew she would need other investors to raise the capital necessary to be able to make a serious offer. Golden Bantha Group would therefore have to be more than a front for her consulting work but also have other investors willing work to the same end. As her own age was already quite a novelty in the business world she sought other investors would also not normally be taken seriously. 

These investors would largely fall into two groups, the children of her father's friends and the [[Droid|droids]] associated her family and the those of the other investors. These children will largely wealthy in their own right through various inheritances, particularly her best friend [[Veruca Violet]] who had inherited considerable property, following the Sariphage pandemic, from her family's Elerian Nut Company. She was also heavily involved the business interests of her former guardian [[Zubindi Hakoon]]. 

The droids in question were likewise independent wealthy. Crestian had always paid a salary to the droids in his crew, with some having becoming wealthy through investments. In the case of Golden Bantha Group Executive Vice President [[Cricket]] he had, similarly to Atsvara, embezzled considerable amounts of money from his 'master' [[Johanna Siri te Danaan]] which he had invested wisely. As a rabid supporter of the [[:starwars:Droid rights|Droids rights]] movement, Atsvara was able to convince Cricket to support her cause by treating him like an equal and using her influence to clean up various legal trouble he was apt to get himself into.

There would be a few organic investors that were slightly older and not in her peer group amongst her father's friends but who still largely fit with in the same demographics. Caspian Justice Minister [[Sha Dark]] was only in her mid-twenties and became a minor investor because of her ties to the other families involved. Meanwhile the Tagge family itself provided some of the financing to the future takeover bid, unhappy with how their companies had been doing under the current Corporate Sector Authority business structure, with Izara Tagge buying a stake in the Golden Bantha Group as the face of her family's continued involvement.

While operating as a nebulous force working behind the scenes at Biscuit Baron, the Golden Bantha Group kept as many details about their existence and membership as secret as possible. The firm was legally registered as being based on [[Corellia]] owing to lax financial disclosure regulations within the [[Corellian Confederation]] and to create an illusion that the firm was not heavily tied to the [[Caspian Union]] and the [[Imperium]], though they had no physical office on the world. With Corellian Prime Minister [[Dash Blazer]] being a personal friend to a number of involved parties, the firm was well advised on how to avoid official interest.

==Buyout==

After many months working behind the scenes and negotiating with the [[Direx Board (CSA)|Direx Board]] to [[Report:Biscuit Buyout|buyout]] the restaurant chain, the Golden Bantha Group became sole owners of Biscuit Baron in [[33 ABY]]. At that time major stockholders were publicly identified and took over as the new board of directors of the restaurant chain.

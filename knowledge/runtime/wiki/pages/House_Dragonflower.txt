'''House Dragonflower''' was one of the noble houses of the [[New Sith Order]]. It was not among the original houses founded around the three Thanes, which ostensibly controlled much of the Order's territory, and to which all the other nobles belonged to. When [[Admiral]] [[Crestian Tarasar]] was elevated to the title of [[Duke]], he was allowed by [[Tyler Damion|Darth Malign]] to establish his own house which centred around his control of the [[Dragonflower Nebula]] and [[Silver Station]]. As Crestian maintained a public profile as a dandy and a fop, he was not taken all that seriously at Sith court and largely ignored in their larger machinations.

What made House Dragonflower notable was its survival after the collapse of the New Sith Order. After the disappearance of Darth Malign, Crestian took his Task Force 77 back to Silver Station and hid his [[Interdictor-class Immobilizer 418|Interdictor]], the ''[[Leviathan]]'' within the Dragonflower Nebula. Crestian and his people would spend a decade keeping a low profile, awaiting for the return of Malign or the emergence of another suitable leader. When he was finally rescued by his sister [[Johanna Siri te Danaan]], Crestian pledged his house to their cause.

The focus of House Dragonflower shifted after the Sariphage [[Report:Sariphage Press Releases|pandemic]] and Mergani [[Report:Mergani Pogrom Press Releases|pogroms]] in the [[Caspia|Caspian System]] where Crestian came from. When Crestian returned to [[Caspar]] after the pandemic and subsequently was elected as [[Presav]] of the [[Caspian Union]], the House Dragonflower became involved in supporting those ends, providing warships on loan to [[Caspian Defence Force]] and prothium gas to the [[CDMC|Caspian Design & Manufacturing Corporand]]. Silver Station and the Dragonflower Nebula remain effectively as a sovereign entity in spite of their ties to the Union and Malign's new government, the [[Imperium]].

== Background ==

[[Crestian Tarasar]] was born on [[Caspar]] but moved to [[Chandrila]] as a teenager. His diplomat father Petyrian disagreed was Caspar's choice to form an independent government rather than [[New Republic]] and impressed upon his son the idea that there was no place for neutrality in galactic conflicts. After a couple of years on Chandrila, Crestian decided to enrol at the Brionelle Memorial Military Academy, where he underwent training as a naval officer. Upon being commissioned he choice to continue in postgraduate studies in command ethics and would subsequently join the faculty as an Instructor of Ethics.

By the time of the [[Imperial Blitzkrieg]], Crestian had advanced rapidly in rank and post, serving as Associate Academic Dean at the rank of [[Captain]]. To this point, however, he had never had a long-term posting in the field. The blitzkrieg would change this. Crestian was able to exploited a gap in the [[Galactic Empire|Imperial]] fleet's sensor coverage to evacuate several members of the faculty and student body aboard his personal corvette, the ''Argent Drake''. Rather than trying to assign him to a naval vessel, the New Republic opted to allow Crestian to continue operate on his own vessel, which he would use mainly for courier duty in the subsequent months of the war. Some of his former colleagues and students now serving as his crew.

Crestian quickly grew disillusioned with the war and the Republic. As much as he disliked the Empire, he found the New Republic no better suited than the Old Republic had been to bring stability to the galaxy. Ultimately, Crestian would resign his commission and go independent, doing his best to keep himself and his friends in good condition until some better cause came along. During this time Crestian and his crew would come across an abandoned outpost in the Doldur Sector named [[Silver Station]]. Moving the station closer to the [[Dragonflower Nebula]], to avoid any future detection, he would use it as the base for smuggling operations.

Eventually Crestian and his operations came to the notice of [[Tyler Damion|Darth Malign]] and his Fleet Admiral [[Drayson Honos]]. Malign had use for Crestian's bureaucratic skills and intellectual character. And in Malign, Crestian felt he had found something of a kindred spirit. Someone with the vision to bring order to the galaxy, yet capable of the intellectual nuisances necessary to break free of the rigid dogma that had been dooming Jedi and Sith alike for millennia. And so Crestian would join the [[New Sith Order]] and bring many of his people with him.

== New Sith Order ==

Just as he had back at Brionelle Memorial Military Academy, Crestian would advance quickly in the New Sith Order. Promoted to [[Admiral]], Crestian would be given command of Task Force 77.  Conceived as a compliment to the work of the [[Knights of Korriban]], Task Force 77 took on missions not quite important enough to be assigned to a Knight of Korriban or which required a more organised force to execute. The task force was based around an [[Interdictor-class Immobilizer 418|Interdictor]], named the ''[[Leviathan]]'' after the flagship of Darth Malak, and four prototype Barb Interceptors. In addition to naval and marine personnel, several members of the [[Sith Intelligence Network]] were assigned to Crestian's command. Their mission would vary widely and included everything from artefact collection, clandestine infiltration, false flag operations, and political sabotage among other things.

As a mark of particular favour by Darth Malign, when Crestian was made a [[Duke]] in the Sith nobility he was allowed to form his own noble house rather than attach himself to one of the existing three. The territory of House Dragonflower would only extend to what Crestian already controlled but it suited him well enough. Crestian had a particular affinity for court intrigue, but purposely cultivated a persona at court of a dandy and a fop, leading few to consider him a threat to their own aspirations, if they considered his existence at all.

Though Crestian was not a force sensitive himself, he took to heart many lessons of the Sith, in particular having an exit strategy. When Darth Malign went missing and the New Sith Order began to fall, rather than go out in some suicidal blaze of glory, Crestian simply retreated with his people to the safety of the Dragonflower Nebula, awaiting more favourable conditions in the galaxy. The ''Leviathan'' would be hidden deep within the nebula, so rumours of its continued existence could never be easily substantiated.

== Interregnum ==

Silver Station would operated as a shadowport with Crestian keeping a relatively low profile. On those occasions he did leave the confines of his territory, all the galaxy would see was an eccentric dandy. This would keep him and his people safe from the Imperial's Sith purges, as Crestian was not seen as worth the effort, and no wanted to search for his rumoured hidden assets and come up empty.

House Dragonflower continued to operate as a Sith remnant. Many of those who had served Crestian over the years remained, and absent their previous war, would settle into quieter lives. Crestian and many of his crew would go on to marry and have children, with Miss Cason's School for Sith Spawn started on the station to educate the next generation. For those of his people who died or retired from the Sith world, [[Droid|droids]] would often be acquired or built to fill the vacancies, though Crestian did find the occasional new recruit in their decade in exile. Status would be accorded through the continuation of a military rank structure, with an ever growing number of [[Commander|Commanders]], as there was no place for further advancement.

== Caspian Union ==

When Darth Malign and Drayson Honos were rescued from a decade trapped on [[Korriban]] by Malign's sister [[Johanna Siri te Danaan]], they quickly came to see Crestian, as one of the few keeping the faith. The House Dragonflower pledged itself to the sibling's cause of fighting against [[Galactic Emperor|Emperor]] [[Aleister Vadim]] and combating slavery in the galaxy. The fact a Sith Lord and Jedi Knight were together in unified purpose appealed to Crestian, who was an advocate for balance which he saw manifested in this alliance. He had also never been a supporter of the slavery that was present in the New Sith Order, and had only made use of free personnel. Even his droids were accorded their freedom and possessed rank and salary just as his biological crew did.

Crestian was soon conceptualising his house at the forefront of what he dubbed the Resurrected Sith Order, but circumstances back on [[Caspar]] soon intervened. Finding his homeworld facing an unprecedented [[Report:Sariphage Press Releases|pandemic]], and bolstered by the return of Malign, Crestian would take the ''Leviathan'' out of the nebula for the first time in a decade, and offer his support to the [[Caspian Democratic Union]] in its hour of need.

At the suggestion of [[Sar Admiral]] [[Prae Kichakressa]], Crestian turned his attention towards [[Olumi Osahn]] and the [[Report:Mergani Pogrom Press Releases|pogroms]] they had undertaken on [[Mergansar]]. After Crestian and Malign failed at bringing the former Sith aligned cult to heel, Crestian instead formed a paramilitary organisation called [[Casulmis]] to combat them. While Casulmis was created as vehicle for organising the growing refugee population to fight to retake their world, the backbone of their assets were those of House Dragonflower. Some of Crestian's crew had been with him as far back as his days in the Republic, and continued to willingly follow him to each new cause.

Early successes on Mergansar were soon followed by naval [[Mergansar Incident|engagement]] with the [[Caspian Navy]] which sent the ''Leviathan'' in retreat back to Silver Station. The subsequent collapse of the Caspian Democratic Union, however, opened the door for Crestian to return to Caspar as a private citizen months later. Many members of his crew became some of the first employees of his new charity, [[The Mission]], as he worked to help the pandemic survivors and Mergani refugees on Caspar recover. By the next year, Crestian would be elected as [[Presav]] of the new [[Caspian Union]], with House Dragonflower now following him into the Caspian sphere formally.

Silver Station would not formally become a part of the Union in the way [[Aeschylus]] had when its leader [[Camrath Kizuka]] joined the Caspian Navy. However, an agreement colloquially known as the Dragonflower Treaty, would formalise an association between the Caspian Union and House Dragonflower. The personnel and warships of the House Dragonflower would be seconded to the [[Caspian Defence Force]], while the prothium gas collected and refined at Silver Station would be sold at a discount to the [[CDMC|Caspian Design & Manufacturing Corporand]]. The Caspian Defence Force and [[Caspian Intelligence Service]] would be allowed to operate on Silver Station, but would also bear some responsibility for its protection.

Service in the Caspian Union, in combination with additional warships given and loaded to Crestian by Darth Malign, finally provided new opportunities to his senior Commanders, who finally had a chance for further promotion. The Dragonflower Treaty also had the effect of adding numerous droids as senior officers in the Caspian Defence Force, which had previously had few droids outside of support roles. The House Dragonflower also has no minimum enlistment age, unlike the Caspian Defence Force, which has led some officers younger than normally allowed, most notably Crestian's daughter [[Atsvara Tarasar]]. The Caspian Intelligence Service also benefitted from the inclusion of many veterans of the Sith Intelligence Network at its formation.

Since being elected as Presav, Crestian has made little use of his title as Duke. With Presav and Sar Admiral as more prestigious titles, he will usually make use of them. Depending on his mood at any given time he will vacillate between whether he considers himself to still be Sith. His investment in the success of the Union is such that he tries to minimise the instances where his Sith background may be considered a distraction. Nevertheless, he still will use the Sith title when it suits his purpose, such as when he elevated Atsvara to the title of [[Baron|Baroness]] for her tenth birthday, in preparation for future branding when she took over [[Biscuit Baron]]. 

== Dragonflower Enterprises ==

In many respects, House Dragonflower shifted its focus in its years in exile to operate as a commercial power. Dragonflower Enterprises, as it is dubbed when operating in business, has two key subsidiaries Dragonflower Entertainment and the Dragonflower Gas Consortium. The latter was responsible for most of the house's income during the interregnum, selling refined prothium gas to various parties. The Caspian Design & Manufacturing Corporand is now its major client. Dragonflower Entertainment meanwhile operates Club Utinni! on [[Tatooine]],the Prothium Club on [[Selene]], and has studio space on [[Kichnar Orbital Station]].

== Warships ==

House Dragonflower has four warships which are seconded to the [[Caspian 4th Fleet]], the Caspian Navy's expeditionary force, which is directly under the command and discretion of Crestian in his role as Sar Admiral. In addition to the ''Leviathan'' is her sister ship, the ''[[Dying Sun]]'', another Interdictor given to Crestian by Malign after he formed the [[Imperium]]. Malign also placed two [[Warlord-class Medium Cruiser|Warlord-Class Medium Cruiser's]], the ''[[Starkiller]]'' and the ''[[Tethys]]'' under Crestian's command which effectively operate as assets of the House.

{{Template:Logsum
|logtitle = [[RPlog:Flight of the Corellian|Flight of the Corellian]]
|author = [[Paul Nighman|Nighman]]
|date = 6 ABY
|synopsis = When Jessalyn doesn't show up that morning, Paul returns to the barracks. He greets Shenner when she awakes and re-bandages her hands as promised. However, Jessalyn does not return and Shenner spontaneously agrees to go with Paul. He explains to her that they must first go to Tatooine, in order to find Grathix. Although Shenner is not pleased with the prospect of returning to the place she was dumped by Paul nearly a year ago, she makes little comment on it. The two of them pack up their belongings and head out. Part of Paul feels more relaxed about Shenner coming along than he did before. Her attitude and the situation being what it was, makes Paul feel like she choose him more by default than by choice. After all, with General Calhoun on Kashyyyk, and Jessalyn no where in sight, what other choice does the girl have? The trip brings back painful memories and concerns for Paul, as he once again wonders what has become of his own beloved ship. Shenner does her best to distract him, and he takes the opportunity to learn more about what she has been up to since that momentous day that he left her on Tatooine.}}

{{Template:Logsum
|logtitle = [[RPlog:Playing for Time|Playing for Time]]
|author = [[Paul Nighman|Nighman]]
|date = 6 ABY
|synopsis = Having landed on Tatooine, Paul isn't sure what the best course of action is, or what kind of profile Luke wants him to have when he approaches Grathix. Having learned that Luke is also on Tatooine, which he originally said he wouldn't be, Paul decides that he needs to know the lay of the land before venturing out. Not too thrilled about the idea, he sends Shen out in "boy" form to see if the kid can locate Luke and give the young Jedi a message for him. He figures that Shenner has a better chance of contacting Luke and not being recognized than he would. Shen heads out on her mission, and Paul, who knows that the kid really hates being back on Tatooine, and who has greater cause to worry about her now than he did a year ago, frets back on board the Darklighter. As the time grows later and later, his pacing grows more and more frenetic.}}

{{Template:Logsum
|logtitle = [[RPlog:Rules and Regulations|Rules and Regulations]]
|author = [[Paul Nighman|Nighman]]
|date = 6 ABY
|synopsis = The restlessness that pervaded Paul's person the previous night has only gained strength over the long hours of waiting. In addition, Paul receives some very interesting news from his friend and colleague, Dr. Canto - a communique that notifies Paul that there was a historian from Mandalore that had died not too long ago. With no family or connections, the only address on him at the time was for the Department of History at the Corellian University at Coronet. According to Dr. Canto, all of the man's personal effects and research had been sent to Dr. Xavier Nighman, Paul's father and head of the department. Dr. Canto also noted that the man had claimed to him before that he had found proof verifying the Force amplifier theories, but was unwilling to discuss it until he had all of the facts. The man did hint though at a guarded crypt which contained the pieces, to which he had the key, but had not yet been able to locate. Perhaps the historians notes and the key artifact could be found in the boxes shipped to Corellia? Paul was ecstatic with the idea. Paul finally just up and leaves, not to look for Luke or Grathix, but just to get out, do some thinking. In passing a few storefronts, he recalls that the conversation and lessons with Shenner the night before planted a few thoughtful seeds in his mind, and he decided to act on them, feeling unreasonably pleased and amused at the thought of buying the girl things.}}

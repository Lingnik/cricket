'''Legacy''' is a political party that was formed to contest the [[Caspian Union|Caspian]] [[Report:Caspian Election Posts|elections]] for a permanent [[Union Council]] in [[29 ABY]]. They won two of the nine seats, with [[Crestian Tarasar|Crestian Tarasar's]] [[Praecet]] winning the other seven. In spite of controlling a supermajority of the seats, Tarasar invited the Legacy Party to join Praecet in a coalition, and nominated Legacy party leader [[Cas Mos]] as Vice Presav. Subsequent additions to the Council as other worlds joined or rejoined the Union see Legacy holding eleven of thirty-nine seats still making it the second largest party, with a high percentage of seats than it had started with. The all party coalition has grown to include [[Holicet]] and [[Lijsttrekker]] as well.

== Background ==

Following the dissolution of the [[Caspian Democratic Union]] following the Sariphage [[Report:Sariphage Press Releases|pandemic]] and Mergani [[Report:Mergani Pogrom Press Releases|progoms]] [[Caspar]] was left in a shambles. Not only was a massive amount of work necessary to support the survivors of the pandemic and the refugees from [[Mergansar]] who had settled, but a new government needed to be established. The new [[Caspian Union|Caspar Union]] was a rump state of what was left after other worlds declare independence, leaving the only Caspian worlds with any significant population [[Mikassa]], [[Kichnar Orbital Station]], and Caspar itself. As the old Union had been conceived around its multi-world membership, the new Union had to do more than simply fill vacated posts, a new government model itself needed to be agreed upon.

A Provisional Assembly was soon organised with representatives from Caspar's seven major cities. The representatives were largely a mix of retired members of the [[Union Assembly]], local leaders, academics, and some representatives from the refugee community. Notably, none of those later elected to permanent [[Union Council]] served in the Provisional Assembly. In many cases, those with the most power and influence were too busy actively directing rebuilding efforts to spend time arguing over a new constitution. In the case of future Vice Presav [[Cas Mos]] she was serving as [[General]] in the [[Union Marines]] and was focused in establishing and maintaining some semblance of order following the chaos of the pandemic, and giving the civilian government room to do their work without outside interference.

There had been no political parties in the old Caspian Democratic Union, but distinct camps did begin to form within the Provisional Assembly which would subsequently give rise to the political parties that would ultimately contest the election once the new constitution was agreed on and ratified. One of the larger camps was made up of those who had positive feelings about the old Union and want to make the new [[Caspian Union|Union]] as much like the old one as possible. This was clearly a challenge given the reduced population and territory, many of the culture influences that had made the Union unique were no longer present.

In terms of ideology and policy, however, the task of continuing on the legacy of the old Union came across to many as a worthy goal. In response to the formation of [[Praecet]] by [[Crestian Tarasar]] and [[Zaalbacca]], those seeking to preserve the legacy of the Union, in the face of the attacks upon it by Praecet, met to form their own party, aptly named Legacy. It was hoped that outgoing Provisional Presav [[Nelivve Lameberchain]] would run for the permanent council for [[Dunwelton]] as he was seen to closely align with the ideology of Legacy, but he ultimately chose not to stand for election nor make any endorsements.

== Candidates == 

In spite of the fact that it was Praecet that had the most militaristic platform, it would be the Legacy Party who drew the most current and former service personnel as candidates. Leadership soon coalesced around General '''Cas Mos''' who was reluctantly convinced to run to represent [[Groton]] by citizens grateful for the steadying hand she had been providing there. '''Stonewin Kirkmount''', who ultimately served as Legacy's candidate in Dunwelton, had served in the [[Imperial Army]] during the early years of the [[Galactic Empire]]. He had served along side Lameberchain in the old [[Union Assembly]] representing Dunwelton before retiring.

On [[Kichnar Orbital Station]] Legacy's candidate '''Nevidomo Dodomu''' was a veteran of the [[Griffon Alliance]] and well respected by the expatriate community there. '''Adama Whitesun''', the Legacy candidate in [[Trinumvira]] had served both in the [[Constable|Constabulary]] and later the Union Marines. '''Liam Hudsol''', the [[Plaxton]] candidate had also been in the Marines. Mos and Kirkmount were the only candidates projected to win, with Dodomu and Hudsol the only others who stood a slight chance of winning. 

In the end Mos and Kirkmount were elected, with Legacy placing second in Kichnar, [[Orleanon]] and Plaxton, and third behind Praecet and [[Holicet]] in [[Anchorron]], [[Mikassa]], and [[Trinumvira]]. In spite of clearly being in the minority, Tarasar invited Legacy to join Praecet in a coalition government, feeling the Union would be best served by consensus building. As part of the coalition agreement Praecet would take the lead in foreign and defence policy while Legacy would have more freedom to direct domestic policy. 

Legacy Party Leader Cas Mos became Vice Presav and Minister of Domestic Affairs and Defence, while Stonewin Kirkmount, now Deputy Party Leader, became Maritime Minister. Legacy candidates Adama Whitesun and Liam Hudsol would also find themselves invited to join the [[Caspian Navy]] which was in need of more command officers. They would be given command of namesake vessels of their cities the [[Shearwater-class Frigate|Shearwater-Class Light Cruisers]] ''CMS Trinumvira'' and ''CMS Plaxton'' respectively.

== Platform ==

As can be expected from a party whose name is Legacy, the party's platform centres around upholding the ideals of the previous Caspian Democratic Union. As part of a coalition government with Praecet, Holicet, and Lijsttrekker, they are able to advance some of their ideals while others take a backseat to the priorities of the other parties, particularly Praecet which still holds the plurality of seats in the Union Council.

The Legacy Party has been fully supportive of efforts which have seen the Union reunited with its former member worlds. Vice Presav Mos led the [[Caspian Crest|Crest's]] negotiations with Rauther and the Legacy Party was heavily involved in the negotiations with the [[Krittain|Krit Union]]. While not as gung-ho about the inclusion of the [[Xi Charrian|Xi Charrians]] into the Union as Praecet, they have neverthless largely support the move.

Though the Caspian Democratic Union had favoured a more streamlined military, the ineffectiveness of the Caspian Navy in the old Union's final years has made the Legacy Party more open to the level of militarisation pursued by Presav Tarasar than might otherwise have been the case. While Legacy Councillors are not always fully supportive of every effort, they are rarely openly critical. Legacy has been strongly supportive of the repatriating and nationailising of [[CDMC|Caspian Design & Manufacturing Corporand]], [[Interspatial Prospecting, Inc.]], and [[SubPro|Sublight Products Corporation]], agreeing it is a matter of Union defence.

In terms of foreign affairs, the Legacy Party traditionally favours a more neutral or [[New Republic|Republic]] leaning policy. Legacy was not keen on the [[Imperium-Caspian Mutual Protection Pact (29 ABY)|Mutual Protection Pact]] with the [[Imperium]], and though they joined the coalition knowing this would occur, at the time they lacked the power to have stopped in had they stayed in opposition. As the years have passed and the Imperium has shown itself to be consistently more progressive in policies than the preceding Empire the displeasure at this alliance has waned. Likewise the insistence of the New Republic to avoid political engagement with the Union has eroded much of Legacy's previous support for a more Republic-aligned policy.

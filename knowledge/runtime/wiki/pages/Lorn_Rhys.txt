== Background==
=== Early Career: [[5 ABY|24 ISY]] - [[8 ABY|27 ISY]] ===
Lorn Rhys began his service with the Empire by attending the Academy on [[Selene]] for [[Stormtrooper]] indoctrination. Following his graduation, he was promoted on the spot to Senior Trooper by the Legion General. Shortly thereafter, he was mysteriously transferred out of the Legion without discharge. During this unaccounted-for period, Lorn became a member of the elite and secretive [[Shadow Guard]], a unit tasked with executing ultra-secret, high-risk missions for the Emperor. His exceptional performance culminated in his promotion to Shadow Guard Commander. 

Many years later, Lorn resurfaced in a public capacity as the Director of the [[Bureau of Operations]]. In this role, he employed numerous Independents as spies, compensating them generously for credible information. His tenure also involved close collaboration with Shadow Guard members who had advanced to [[Imperial High Command|High Command]], allowing him to maintain significant influence.

Subsequently, Lorn was appointed to the [[Ubiqtorate]], overseeing all aspects of Imperial Intelligence. While he was not granted the title of Imperial High Command, his successors would later hold that distinction. Under Emperor [[Bacharan Valak|Valak]], Lorn was reassigned to [[Tatooine]] as [[Moff]], tasked with overseeing the Imperial Occupation and the construction of a formidable garrison. Known for his hard-liner stance against the locals, he [[Battle of Tatooine|subjugated Tatooine]] and fortified the Empire’s presence there, signaling the beginning of Valak's territorial expansion.
[[File:Lorn-portrait-2.jpg|left|thumb|Lorn Rhys at [[5 ABY]]]]
Following his success on Tatooine, Lorn was transferred to oversee the occupation of [[Pride-1]], renamed [[Pride-1|Orbital Station Redemption]], after its capture from the [[Griffon Alliance|Griffon Aliance]] by the Imperial Navy. Positioned on the fringe of Imperial space, he operated with considerable freedom, using smugglers and bulk transport companies to amass materials under the pretense of repairing and refortifying the station.

Meanwhile, Lorn organized the [[Vigilant Movement]], a covert initiative aimed at preparing the Empire for Emperor Palpatine's return by removing Emperor Valak from power. The Vigilant infiltrated all levels of the Empire, terminating dissenters and designing a new TIE variant resembling power armor, which was manufactured on Orbital Station Redemption. However, their manufacturing activities drew attention, prompting the Empire to dispatch a fleet to crush the Vigilant.

In his attempt to flee the Imperial raid on OS Redemption, Lorn’s shuttle exploded during the jump to hyperspace, leaving no trace of wreckage or remains. This marked the disbanding of the Shadow Guard and execution of Vigilant operatives or imprisonment on Kessel. Rumors surrounding Lorn’s demise or survival proliferated, with theories ranging from his orchestration of a coup, to his escape, or capture. Unconfirmed whispers suggested he might have recovered in a [[Caspian Democratic Union|Caspian]] medical facility, though those claims remained unsubstantiated.

=== A Cycle of Birth and Death: [[8 ABY|27 ISY]] - [[13 ABY|32 ISY]] ===

In truth, the Moff's shuttle was not destroyed, but made an incomplete hyperspace jump. Drifting in space, with all hands dead, save Lorn, it would be recovered by the pirate outlaw Normack. After a short, horrifying term aboard Ozme's Traveling Starport, where he would lose an arm, leg, and eye, Lorn would be recovered by [[Kartijan Lore]]. He would convalesce at Tarquin Memorial Hospital where the tendrils of the Dark Side, which had been ensnaring him for years before, finally took complete hold. Guided by visions, and an unproductive meeting with [[Avalyshaar Dusten|President Laarken]], he would eventually escape Caspar and flee to [[Nar Shaddaa]] where he would find and kill an archivist from the Jedi Order. The knowledge guarded by the old Jedi would be taken and twisted for his own purpose.

[[File:Lorn-portrait.jpg|thumb|Lorn Rhys in [[11 ABY]]]]
In [[11 ABY]], after Emperor Valak's disappearance and a new Imperial power structure in place, the time was finally right for Lorn to emerge from hiding. Departing Nar Shaddaa, Lorn would search for old friends, new partners, and embark on the path shown to him which he was destined for. Through the machinations of the Dark Side, a holocron-like cube created by the mysterious Ahzad Domani, would come into his possession. Eventually meeting his first true master, albeit briefly. Ahzad transferred considerable knowledge of the Force and the Dark Side into his construct and this would serve as Lorn's primary teacher. With this training, his powers in the Force and connection to the Dark Side strengthened, but it also served to attract a new master, the alluring and deadly [[Sinane Pantara|Darth Pain]].

Lorn brokered deals with Hutts, pirate gangs, businesses, while also inserting himself quietly back into Imperial politics and the Imperial Intelligence apparatus. From this, he built a power base which he would use to wrest the throne away from whom he would call an imposter on the throne. In [[12 ABY]], with the help of [[Vassily Korolov]], a Royal Guardsman, and [[Petra Doom]], the Rock of the Empire, Rhys cultivated a coup against the reigning Emperor [[Kendal Osbourne]]. Thus began the [[War of the Throne]], commencing with the [[Battle of Selene]]. Lorn turned the entire Imperial realm upside down by not only capturing Emperor Osbourne, but by seizing the Super Star Destroyer [[SSD Palpatine|HIMS Dark Eminence]] and plunging it into Selene, devastating the planet and rendering it sterile. He took twisted delight in casting Osbourne into carbonite as a form of imprisonment in a tortuous "ceremony" with a portable carbon-freezing chamber specially outfitted on his own flagship, HIMS Inquisitor.

Rhys spurred his forces on into the Imperial interior, fighting several small, decisive battles with the ultimate objective of [[Dreven]]. His forces quickly toppled resistance there, as well, and soon stood over the entire Imperial realm. Dominance was still in question, as challenges from other powers and entities boiled up, not the least of which was one from the Neo-Imperial Movement. Led by officers that had previously been pressed into compliance by Rhys' forces, the Neo-Imperials had exceptional military and intelligence experience on their side. Through a stroke of treachery, Rhys disarmed the threat and scattered the Movement to the winds. He was about to fully consolidate his power when he was betrayed by his master Darth Pain. She consumed Lorn with tremendous powers called on by the Dark Side, leaving him for dead and taking the throne for herself. He would be interred in the Chamber of Heroes at the Imperial Palace on Dreven.
[[File:MalusWithCube.jpg|thumb|Darth Malus with Ahzad's Cube]]
All this was foreseen by the short-lived Emperor Rhys. Armed with arcane Sith knowledge from his first master, Azhad Domani, and the knowledge contained in the cube, Lorn began a horrible transformation and emerged as Darth Malus. Renewed with power and purpose, he once again sought the help of Vassily Korolov to dispatch his former master Darth Pain. Together with [[Mailyn Raines]], the trio boldly and decisively confronted Empress Pain and killed her, but at a price. In her death throes, Pain would summon an ethereal Sith Hound and curse the three that struck her down. With all obstacles to power now removed, the new Sith finally crowned himself Emperor.

Throughout most of 12 ABY, Malus and his regime reigned over the Empire, but it became a time of instability, weakness, and lawlessness. [[Coruscant]] was [[Fifth Battle of Coruscant|re-taken]] by the [[New Republic]] in early [[13 ABY]], exploiting the lack of concern that Malus displayed for the system's security. Most of the Imperial military, already distrustful of Rhys's Intelligence & Security background, became embittered against their supposed ruler and his lack of cohesive direction. Finally, in mid-13 ABY, a machination was enacted to unseat Malus, and it was done with an insidiousness that spoke volumes as to what was going to come to the galactic scene.

Completely without warning, Dreven was surrounded by the fleet of a [[Tyler Damion|Darth Malign]]. Malign swept onto the planet, his presence washing over Malus's attendants like a wave. Korolov proffered no interdiction, and Malign simply overwhelmed Malus, taking him prisoner. In a cruel twist of fate, Malus was cast into a slab of carbonite as he himself had sentenced Osbourne to. Malign conveyed both slabs to [[:starwars:Korriban/Legends|Korriban]], placing them aboard the [[SSD Malevolence|HIMS ''Malevolence'']] as gifts to his master, [[Aleister Vadim]], whom Darth Pain also called master. Her curse had manifested with great irony.

=== A Relic from the Past: [[34 ABY|53 ISY]] ===

In [[34 ABY]] Malus was released from his carbonite prison, along with various criminals incarcerated in the same facility, by an unknown benefactor. How his slab came to be housed in Spacestation 1138, also known as the [[The_Oubliette|Oubliette]] is still a mystery. The methods used to determine his location and how the extraction team was able to do so, is just as great a mystery. If Malus truly knows, he has not spoken about it. Shortly following his liberation, the Imperium Grand Inquisitor [[Axel Vichten]] announced a 300,000cr bounty for the capture of Lorn Rhys.
[[File:DesertMalus.jpg|left|thumb|Darth Malus at Tatooine in [[34 ABY]]]]
In the months since his release, Lorn has kept a relatively low profile, choosing specific persons and places to meet with. While he has not been in the public eye, rumors abound that he has had involvements in the protests on Chandrila and Mon Calamari. There has been suspicion that he has been involved with or responsible for the famine and communications blackout in the Arkanis sector affecting [[Tatooine]], as around this time an anonymous bounty of 1,000,000cr was posted for Lorn Rhys aka Darth Malus, dead or alive.

== Description ==

     Scarred flesh melded with cybernetic prosthetics, Darth Malus is more machine than man. That this human is able to function with any semblance of normalcy, is a testament to technology and his resiliency. Sleek mechanical legs merge at his pelvis, slightly raising his natural height to two meters. They carry the dark lord with an eerie fluidity, which at times give the illusion of him gliding across a surface. Malus' left shoulder anchors a skeletal looking durasteel arm. His right arm can still be called flesh and blood, though the limb is disfigured with scar tissue. While appearing weak and withered, corded muscle can be seen, hinting at the strength and suppleness it can wield.
    
     Malus's face has become a mask of terror. At one time, he would have been described as handsome, but his features have become scarred and malevolent looking. A glowing red optical sensor replaces his left eye, casting a dull light over his scarred visage. His right eye nearly glows in its own right, a once vibrant green now turned amber from corruption. A nearly lipless mouth, scarred from severe burns, exposes permanently blackened teeth in a perpetual sneer. The hair on his scalp has long since been burned away. In its place are a series of cruelly etched henna-colored symbols from a long dead language.

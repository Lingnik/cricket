'''Marl Jace''' ([[16 BBY]] — ) was born to a small Corellian family on the capitol world of Coruscant.  As a young man he was relatively quiet and stayed out of any significant trouble.  His life was a happy and simple one, as much as one could be in the middle of a Galactic Civil War.  Marl had no siblings and both of his parents continue to live out their lives on the Imperial controlled Coruscant.  Politically Jace's family was with most of the galaxy in that they didn't fall towards either extreme.  They simply wanted to live their lives as best they could.

Marl's father was a mechanic working as an independent contractor and that is where the young man first discovered an aptitude for space flight. From there Jace never looked back.  It wasn't until the Sixth Battle of Coruscant that he became energized and convinced that he had a place in the war.  Developing a kind of pattern, Marl received a commission to fly for the New Republic StarOps and he's never looked back.  An overwhelming desire to prove himself as a hot-shot compared to the other pilots lead him to train and prepare to be an A-Wing pilot.

Jace has been described as cocky, arrogant, egotistical and at times incredibly annoying.  He's convinced that he's right more than one hundred percent of the time and has proven difficult to work with on several occasions.  Having said that, people always seem to have nice things to say about him.  He's never intentionally cruel, his kindness and friendliness showing through his arrogant exterior.  There is no doubt that Marl is a clever individual but continued reinforcement of that throughout his life lead to a slightly over-sized ego.

==A Career with the Republic==
===Flight Cadet===
The academy was a change of pace for Marl.  He was already significantly older than some of the other pilots joining up, leaving him with a feeling that he had to prove himself to the younger recruits.  Despite being a relatively quiet and well mannered individual in his youth, Marl developed a sort of bad attitude towards authority.  He certainly respected his senior officers, but he became well known for causing the occasional fuss or being the center of a scene.  Marl scored exceptionally high on virtually all of his tests and graduated well near top of his class.  Unfortunately his poor attitude prevented him from receiving any sort of reward or expedited promotion.

Centrally located off Plasteel Plaza, Silvergrass Arboretum immediately creates an entirely different vibe from the artifice outside its confines. For a moment one could forget they were in space at all as the sights and smells bushes and trees, flowers, and grasses from dozens of worlds fills the room. Look far enough in any direction and the windows beyond will remind one of their location with the gasses of the Dragonflower Nebula providing their own beauty. In the high ceiling above artificial lighting of various shades and hues mimic the lighting of different stars, creating an odd, almost disorienting glow.

The arboretum takes its name from the predominant ground flora of the plains of Kinyen. The silvergrass is similarly predominant here creating a sweet aroma which permeates the arboretum. Though many worlds are represented, the Duke's history with Caspar and Chandrila is quite apparent with Wansook Pines and Pula and Tintolive trees prevalent. A pond located in the centre of the arboretum is lined with balmgrass. While the grasses and trees are largely mundane on their homeworlds, the arboretum boosts some rare flowers such as Alderaanian flame-lily and Alderaanian flame-rose.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze's ship is still named ''Vicissitude'', and not changing transponders or friend/foe identifier chips *almost certainly* means he hasn't yet repainted or heavily modified it. At least it's not covered in fresh blood? Although the man is a doctor, he might want to get sluices and grating installed in that floor. As he docks 'amicably enough' on Silver Station, he pulls his long coat about his shoulders and adjusts his seashell-inspired cap. Perhaps a walk among the plant life will settle his nerves, or inspire a bit of piratical panache to his new ship. He tries to consider each plant as it rests in its selected environment, occasionally sneaking a *lean into* the auto-misters for a brief blast of moisture to the tentacles... (wink!)

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Life at Silvergrass Arboretum has largely returned to normal since it hosted Atsvara's birthday party some days ago. The tables and decorations have all been removed, the throngs of guests largely back to their normal lives. The grass itself though probably is showing signs of having been stepped a little more than normal. Having not yet journey back to Charros himself, Crestian ambles about the arboretum himself, enjoying the excuse to be back in his old home.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze, having just gotten a good *shot* from the sprinkler, jumps as he notices Crestian! Childish, perhaps, or just-- impolite? Maybe like being caught washing one's hands in a public fountain. He colors with embarrassment, tightening his cap. "Ah, Presav. I-- thought I saw some confetti. In the display."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Doctor Druze!" exclaims Crestian with some mild enthusiasm, seemingly oblivious or uncaring of any faux-pas the Quarren perceives he has committed. "You may have indeed. We duck away from the Wargames to have Atsvara's party here. The clean up may have been less complete than I had hoped. Though I can't rightly recall whether we had any confetti or not now that I think on it."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze squares his shoulders, smooths his coat as he goes in for a handshake. "Yes, the dryland custom of anniversary! Presumably she has grown larger and deadlier, bearing the marks of many competitors bested in life-or-death competition for resources!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian chuckles a bit as he shakes the Quarren's hand, "Well you would not be entirely wrong there. A life-or-death competition for resources would probably describe her aspirations well enough. Though too my knowledge the marks are not physical yet. But I am not sure she would announce it when she starts accruing a body count."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze shrugs, loose. "There are many ways to measure time. Was the party an enjoyable one?" He looks around, as if to suggest an illegal space station is an interesting location for a minor's birthday observation?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Indeed it was, Atsvara certainly seemed quite pleased. Though I feel the old adage of 'what do you get the girl who has everything' may have been put quite to the test, some rather lavish gifts from her guests. And she was able to see some of her old school friends who didn't follow us to Caspar so that was nice for her as well." Crestian seems fairly pleased at the thought of it all.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze nods. "A good time was had, then. Excellent. I was just touring the garden while I gathered my wits, you see. Whether I'm classified as a professional or talented amateur, I'm taking the opportunity to make connections and forge relationships in the, ah, 'wider starship repair-and-upgrade community,' as they say!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian nods, "Well you can count on Silver Station as a friendly port to your needs. Been operating out of here for a very long time it seems now that I think on it. I reckon it has gotten a little more of a respectable feel now that I am a legitimate political leader. We do station some Caspian forces here as forward base, and I sell the prothium gas I mine here largely back to Caspar. But it is still my independent personal territory." The Sarian pause for a moment inconsideration of a point before adding, "In perfect honesty I am not sure our engineers here are the most advanced in that regard, but certainly a place to get parts and do work off the books."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze points. "Discretion, indeed. It does a lot of the work, or at least makes the disadvantages easier to bear."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I am not sure if you have had much of a look around yet but, you will find the station has a few amenities you would not normally find in such a location as well." Crestian looks towards some patch of flowers near them, "There was good decade there that we were trying to live quite life here at of the crosshairs of the Empire and Republic alike, but it does prompt one to try to replicate some of the best things of home. Even got Zubindi to open up a small location of the fashion house here."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze's brows go up. "Live neutrally? Here? That's... bold, if not entirely sane or reasonable. Literally sandwiched between predator and prey."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian chuckles a little "Well I would not say living neutrally. When Darth Malign disappeared for a decade, that put an end to the strength of the New Sith Order. The Imperials claimed to have killed him and proceeded to wipe out those who chose to put up a fight. I guess have token battles and claim victory. Fighting was not the smart decision and so those that were faded out of the spotlight or returned to former lives when their Sith affiliation's weren't known publicly. I was never considered much a threat so returning here was easy enough. And we hid Leviathan, my interdictor in the nebula. No one wanted to come looking forward it in and come out empty handed, just coming home with a Sith noble known as a dandy would not be much of a victory."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze ahs. "Yes, I see. Definitions of 'a quiet life' differ immensely."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Well we left that war behind. So as a quiet as any large less than legal operation can be. Hardly the first time a shadowport found itself run by those who remained of some lost cause. We were largely stuck in place, did a few mercenary contracts here and there, but all that is quite removed from the hustle and bustle of galactic wars." Crestian smirks, "But I am not one to abandon my grandeur."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze gestures wide. "Indeed, the cost to ship the water to keep this thriving is not cushion-change. I should explore, commit more of it to memory. Or, at least, take enough in to fully appreciate the ego of a man who would do such a thing!" He claps Crestian on the shoulders, light and convivial, ending with: "For now I shall merely explore the arboretum, but I'll surely be around if you have need of a doctor or a squid. Until then, Presav."

Xi Charrian cities boast a distinctive architecture and Xi Xin is no different. Most of the buildings in the city are cylindrical in shape, though the bases vary in shape leading to some variation in the exact shape. Dark and dull colours permeate with brown, grey, or red in abundance with black and blue featured as well. New buildings can be easily identified by a metallic sheen not seen in older constructions where the metal has faded, the oldest are in fact ceramic. Another distinctive feature of the buildings is the fact that the access from one storey to the next mostly occurs through ramps that wrap around the exteriors of the buildings, albeit at higher elevation these appear to give way to interior based access.

Decidedly different in architecture is the city's art gallery. Opened during the wargames two years ago a now renamed in honour of the passing Prelate, the Prelate r'ratak-varattak-n'gavanak Memorial Art Gallery is is in the shape of a pyramid, The new art gallery which is distinctively not a normal native design, its shaped is that of a pyramid with its exterior shiny and glass like though one can not clearly see in from the outside. It has been set a part in a small park an area of the city that seems to have been reclaimed from some previous now defunct purpose. Tall, traditional Xi Charrian buildings surround this area, making the gallery an oasis of nonconformity.

Conceived to showcase off-world art to native Xi Charrians, its purpose has slowly evolved with some art by Xi Charrian's slowly being added to its collection, albeit their work is usual an example of dabbling in off-world styles rather than a truly native art. After all traditional Xi Charrian expression is scene in the fields of engineering. The recent influx of tourists to Charros IV has led to the gallery having a massive surge in visitors with its hours of operations temporarily expand to near constant operation.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tonight the gallery stands nearly empty. In theory it is closed for maintenance, which is not a complete lie given its extended operations of late. In true fact though, it has been closed on behalf of one of its chief patrons, Caspian Presav Crestian Tarasar. In an intimately lit exhibit room, Crestian stands looking at some Mandalorian cubist paintings from his own private collection that have been on indefinite loan to the gallery.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The gallery's hush is nearly absolute, so much so that even the sound of Johanna's boots seems to hesitate against the inlaid stone as she crosses into the exhibit room where Crestian stands, glow from the display panels limning her form in hues of blue, gold, and white. Her gaze touches the angular Mandalorian works with their suggestions of battle, abstract scenes frozen for posterity. "I keep forgetting how many hobbies you have," she notes, her voice low but steady, "Collector of Fine Art suits you, though. There are worse things a being can do than try and surround themselves with a bit of..." she gestures to the loaned artworks, "... well, /whatever/ it is the Mandalorians were expressing." They haven't met to review Crestian's tastes, but a few breaths before launching into more unpleasant matters never hurt anyone.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"In another life I may have been happy without politics altogether. Then again I suppose that is the role play so many years, he isn't dangerous or to be taking seriously, he is just a dandy. I suppose now the different aspects of my life are little more balanced." Crestian turns to face Johanna and smiles, "It has been too long Joh, I am glad we could finally meet in person. So much going on....I have hated to have to rely on intermediaries. r'ratak's passing though at least provides a good coverage for other things....and an occasion to promote unity." He looks back at the painting for a moment, "I loaned these to cadracak when he opened the place. Honestly have not been in a rush to have the back. Mandalorian culture is of interest, but to be honest the cubist style is not my favourite."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Johanna exhales through her nose, something that could almost pass for a laugh. She tilts her head slightly, gaze once more drifting over the painting behind Crestian, as though trying to parse some hidden meaning (or at least pretend to.)<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;I'm an ignorant peasant," she finally says dryly, "You could hang these upside-down and I'd nod solemnly as you told me I was looking at the work of a peerless, once in a generation talent." She allows the moment to rest on that self-deprecating note, but only for a breath. Then her expression changes as something settles behind her gaze, a quiet shift from mask to purpose. "But you're right. It /has/ been too long, and it's good to speak without filters. Or flattery. I've had to send too many messages lately by way of fools and flatterers, and the more I hear, the less I like what I'm hearing."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Her hand brushes the hem of her sleeve, the urge to backhand a whole roomful of dumbfucks flaring to life before it is quickly quashed. She pauses again, watching his face now, not the art. "I didn't come just to light a candle or nod politely through a funeral. I need to talk to you about the Oubliette. About what came out of it, and where the hell it's going."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian listens to Johanna and nods, "I do hope at least that those were representing me did not waste too much time on foolishness or flattery. But I can only image how many messengers are darkening your door lately. And I am glad you came to talk, that is why I had the place closed, a perk of being one of the patrons. I mean not that I don't have that pull....anyway. I am not even sure where to begin."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Presav takes a deep breath and gathers his thoughts for a moment. It is actually for more than a moment. He turns facing a painting that is covered with black cloth and unseen, and yet he seems to be looking at it. Turning back to Johanna he says, "Malus is proving to be a fucking menace. At this point I really have little care if Tyler has to fight him, if he gets distracted having to deal with him. He is lost in his magicks, I have a hard time believing they all truly laying down the foundation to be Vadim. The Imperium itself could collapse in itself. Maybe it will even. I am not sure what Elana is up to....Axel seems to be operating on his own. Like he has actually grown the fuck up, acting professional and trying to hold the security situation together."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian sighs looking a little worn down, "The kids though, Sonja, Kipran, all of them. I will stand against anyone who stands against them. And I have to imagine Malus will work against what they have been working for."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Johanna watches Crestian speak. Not just the words, but the rhythm of them, the weight behind the pauses, the way he turns toward the veiled painting as though it might provide him with some insight. When she replies, her voice is quieter than before.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Then you know what this is," she says. "And what it means to feel the edges of something collapsing. To wonder whether what comes after will be worse."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She moves to position herself in front of another work she does not pretend to understand, her reflection dim in the glass, a stranger to herself.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I've been wondering the same about Mitra: whether she's laying a foundation or just gutting the house. Skawn's madness makes sense, in its way. He wants fire. But Mitra?"<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Her jaw tightens, barely.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I don't think they care about the Imperium," she says. "Or Vadim. Or even power, not really. They want to smash things, just to see how much pain it causes. And Malus... he'll ride their wave of chaos all the way to its logical destination, the Imperium a smoking ruin in his wake."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"So do you think they are behind the escape? It would make sense. It has thus far been the gap in our intelligence." Crestian smirks a bit, "Malus though yes he seems to want revenge more than an Empire. He is an interesting case, can't seem to realise how much he is living in the past. Thinks he has great insights, eternal truths, he has shit. And yet he seems to be enthralling to Doctor Cassius. Half the reason Axel and I haven't taken stronger moves against him. For all she bemoans the old Sith times and Tyler, she really just seems to want so chaos to upset whatever boredom she feels is in the galaxy now."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian chuckles a bit, "They don't seem to get what we have built. What we have truly built. Like we need freedom from sort of oppression when we are pulling so many of the strings ourselves. The powers we have amassed in peace time that would be unleashed like fury in a true war. I don't want that war, not now, not these conditions but we are trying to keep ready for it."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian laughs some more, "Funny too, a few years ago I half thought Axel needed to be euthanised, now he was one of the few power players outside of my own people and yours that can really be counted. In spite of our Dreven operations month ago not producing the adequate results, I have kept some of our team there. Too much chance one of these clowns might like to take it over and the Imperium is just neglecting it. There, Coruscant, Nar Shaddaa, hell they might take interest in Chandrila. Working on following up there."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I /know/ they were behind it," the woman replies, one of her gloved hands curling into a fist, "And as for Malus and his new /friend/... it isn't /just/ that they want to unmake what we've built... it's that they don't understand what it /cost/."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A flicker of something darker moves across her expression. She thinks of Lynae... of her voice, once familiar, now echoing beside Malus like music gone wrong. The old fury rises... briefly, cleanly... and then fades again, mastered.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"There are systems in my reach that are barely more than dots on star charts: no money, no significance, other than being the place their settlers call 'home.' And still, they're being hit. I don't think this is just disruption. Someone is testing me. Watching how I respond. Choosing who and what to burn... or who and what to leave. I have my suspicions about who's funding this. Until I have proof, however... suspicions are all they are."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She faces Crestian fully again. "I don't want a war either, despite the title I hold. But you're right; we must be ready for one."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian moves back towards the draped painting, "I feel a little bad now, you are all like I forget all of Crestian's cultural pursuits and feeling all backwater warlordy but....you mentioned the cost. And sadly you are one of the few people I can show this too, my own inner circle include." The Presav removes the draping to reveal a painting of the late Circle of Irluuk member, vaguely set amongst dark vines of a sort. "You know in my line of work I have been having to forge documents for years, I was moved to see if I might apply that skill set to something more creative. It is funny when Malign first started his circle I was very dismissive, I was like some crotchety old fool, who was like the Sith minions were cooler in my day. I am not sure they were, the newer crop has grown on me. I know your brother is far too willing to sacrifice people, but someone so close....for what....to guard some weird ass magic forest or whatever the fuck he was making."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian turns back to Johanna again, a man weighed down with purpose. "No doubt the next dots on the map may easily be mine. Caspian reach or my reach, I will not quibble with the rigmarole or defining some difference between the two. It is funny back when you brought Tyler back into my world those years ago I though it was my destiny in life to help you and Tyler save the universe. Apparently it is left to you and I instead it seems. Literally billions of people have died to lay the groundwork of the societies we are trying to steer through the chaos. And we have achieved /so much/ and walked back from so many set backs. And these mother fuckers come in and want to create more chaos. We have been passive and polite until now, but when pushed the will find our power and our persistence."

Xi Charrian cities boast a distinctive architecture and Xi Xin is no different. Most of the buildings in the city are cylindrical in shape, though the bases vary in shape leading to some variation in the exact shape. Dark and dull colours permeate with brown, grey, or red in abundance with black and blue featured as well. New buildings can be easily identified by a metallic sheen not seen in older constructions where the metal has faded, the oldest are in fact ceramic. Another distinctive feature of the buildings is the fact that the access from one storey to the next mostly occurs through ramps that wrap around the exteriors of the buildings, albeit at higher elevation these appear to give way to interior based access.

Decidedly different in architecture is the city's art gallery. Opened during the wargames two years ago a now renamed in honour of the passing Prelate, the Prelate r'ratak-varattak-n'gavanak Memorial Art Gallery is is in the shape of a pyramid, The new art gallery which is distinctively not a normal native design, its shaped is that of a pyramid with its exterior shiny and glass like though one can not clearly see in from the outside. It has been set a part in a small park an area of the city that seems to have been reclaimed from some previous now defunct purpose. Tall, traditional Xi Charrian buildings surround this area, making the gallery an oasis of nonconformity.

Conceived to showcase off-world art to native Xi Charrians, its purpose has slowly evolved with some art by Xi Charrian's slowly being added to its collection, albeit their work is usual an example of dabbling in off-world styles rather than a truly native art. After all traditional Xi Charrian expression is scene in the fields of engineering. The recent influx of tourists to Charros IV has led to the gallery having a massive surge in visitors with its hours of operations temporarily expand to near constant operation.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Though the funerary ceremonies for Prelate r'ratak-varattak-n'gavanak had concluded, the buzz around Charros IV remained. The story was no longer about the old Prelate but now the new one who was just elected. A great number of senior Caspian figures remained on the Xi Charrian homeworld to now mark that occasion, include Caspian Presav Crestian Tarasar.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While the normal every day meetings of governance continue unabated, after all the Union Council was just as capable of meeting in a cathedral factory or on a warship, day to day life took another tone as the normal haunts everyone would retreat to at the end of that were in another system. One location that Crestian was fond of visiting was the art gallery on Xi Xin that Dean cadracak-'r'shak had started two years ago.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The gallery also made a perfect place to hold a meeting as the vibe was more relaxing than many other venues on the world. As it had been quite some time since he met with Falleen who was coming to visit now, Crestian chose a later hour where the gallery would be more quiet, to allow both conversation and culture more room to breath.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Falleen's arrival was lowkey, his long black hair usually pulled into a tight pony tail hung loose around his shoulders. Clad is his usual spider silk robe he walked with a casual pace, taking in the gallery with an appreciative eye. At his side perhaps a step or two behind was a woman clade in a black hooded robe, her dirty looking hands clasped and unclasped and fidgeted as if she were uncertain of what to do with them. There was an aura with her that was disquieting.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dark purple eyes settle on Crestian and the Falleen gave a respectful nod. "Presav Tarasar...thank you for seeing me." he moves stand by and lets his gaze settle on the art before going back to the Presav. "Wonderful choice for a meet, very nice pieces here."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I thought it was a location that would appeal to your tastes. It also remains a place I am very fond of. Obviously we have a done a lot to share Xi Charrian culture with the rest of the Union and the greater galaxy. This place, however, is about the reverse in many respects cadracak's mission to introduce more of the rest of the galaxy to the Xi Charrians. There are some native of pieces here now as well as some of the locals have been dabbling in offworld styles. I have kept my own collection of Mandalorian cubist paintings here since the place opened."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Presav smiled warmly at Nasiri, even the hooded figure with Nasiri is greeted with a smile. "Miss Wye I presume? Welcome to Charros IV." Crestian looks around for a moment at the gallery, perhaps pondering whether there was an particular piece he should point out.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At the greeting the woman fidgets even more. "Miss....no its Ystile." she counters sounding a bit confused, Nasiri places a hand on her forearm to ease her. "Its an honorific Ystile." the witch has been odd ever since she came into the Falleens service, but a quick bob of her head and a soft nearly hissed..Oh..thank you drifts from the hood. she then begins to scan the artwork. "I came to see if I could draw on your expertise...if you don't mind?" he smiles revealing sharp teeth. "But I am in no hurry, ever since the Sith auction I have been somewhat enamoured of art."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian smirks at Ystile's reaction. The unspoken volumes distract him for a moment, before he pulls himself to giving his attention the man he was here to meet. "Power and wealth are fine, but the affectations they provide are good for quite moments. I have found myself more drawn into art lately myself. Of course I get drawn into so many things. There are many better galleries in the galaxy but few as ground breaking." In that moment though no particular piece seems to be drawing Crestian's attention, it was after all that existed at all that was it true ground breaking feature. "So what area of my expertise draws you out here?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nasiri nods and slides his hands into the large sleeves of his robe, this single action draws Ystile's attention and the woman is clearly now focused on the conversation. The uncertain vibe of the woman is completely gone, and the Falleen begins. "I am looking for information on a Twi'lek dark sider...perhaps frequents the smugglers moons, anything ring a bell?" The question is direct and carries no hint of anything but curiosity. "I am somewhat familiar with a great many Sith, but a Twi'lek is something else entirely." The hood shifts from Nasiri to the Presav.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Skawn Kairn I presume?" There was of course only the one Twi'lek Dark Sider that Crestian knew of. As he gave the matter thought it occurred to him that most of the Sith he knew were Human, most of the Jedi too. How dreadfully boring for the galaxy. "I am afraid my intelligence on that matter is far from ideal. He is wanted for his role in the attack on Bespin we were all present for many years ago. He also attack a Krittish transport on his way home from that. The Krits hadn't rejoined the Union yet, but that sped things along."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian sighs, "Frankly it is rather a black mark on our efforts. At the time we determined he was based upon The Wheel. He managed to either avoid or eliminate the people we sent after him. Had to really rethink our efforts in that region. Managed to eventually tighten up our presence there in a way that caused him to seemingly abandon the region as a safe harbour. But where he is basing himself now I have no idea, probably would have attacked it if I did. But him visiting Nar Shaddaa at least would correspond to what we know.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nasiri gives a slow nod of his head. "That seems to line up...the fact his name popped to the surface right there is a huge help." the witch settles her gaze in the direction of the Presav for a moment and then back to Nasiri. "If I get a line on his location, would that be information of value to you?" he shrugs. "Its the least I could do for your help." he pauses for a moment. "Seems like Hutt space has gone back to its wild ways...can't say as I miss that." he chuckles and then looks around. "Anything to drink here?

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Oh yes where are my manners." The Presav makes a motion to a Xi Charrian on the end of the particular exhibit room they are in, with said Xi Charrian making what vaguely seems to be a nodding gesture before leaving the room. "I can't speak to what the beverage selection will be. They definitely keep some stuff for receptions, but the Xi Charrians themselves are not exactly the wine glass in hand sort. I would say an unfortunate characteristic of their biology but I would never wish to imply such imperfection."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian laughs a bit to himself before returning to the more serious matter of conversation, "I am not sure how much Hutt Space has changed versus some very particular circumstances we find ourselves in. Frozen old Emperors getting thawed unsettles the balance of things everywhere. But yes information on Skawn or his associates would be of value. Albeit its value would depend on how actionable the information is. He has certainly been spotted in enough places over the years that knowing he has been somewhere else to add to the list may not be very novel information. The people he seems to be working with now are managing to make themselves a lot of enemies, but Skawn himself while not the most dangerous is the one who has caused the Union the greatest problem. So anything that might be done against him would be much appreciated by myself personally but also pretty much everyone of importance around here."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And with that the Xi Charrian returns with a protocol droid carrying a tray of beverages. The tray has twelve drinks on it, three each of four beverages. The Xi Charrian relates through the articulation conversion sphere hovering around it, "We have some Corellian Ale, Ottegan Mead, Chandrilan Wine and of course the Presav's favoured Ithorian Brandy."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nasiri scoops up the Chandrilian wine. "Yea, I got a security missive about Mr. Rhys..my Moff status still sends me mail." he takes a sip and nods to the droid. "This will do thank you." turning his attention back to Crestian. "So I am aware that the Imperium wants him back dreadfully much, but where does that stand with you?" he shrugs. "I am aware of your connections to the Imperium, but also more direct concern of the Union?" Ystile at this point decides to wander around and look more closely at the art, hands wringing and fidgeting as she does so, the Falleen spares the wandering no concern or attention.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian grabs a glass of the Ithorian brandy to the surprised of no one, "He disrupts the balance of things. He has already begun to rack up a body content which would in normal circumstances lead to legal ramifications even without his past. But obviously we are not men that fall under normal rules nor is he." Crestian takes a sip of his brandy and remarks, "You know if they ever make some holodrama on my life I can just see now Ithorian brandy becoming some cliched reference like some spy franchise hero who is way to easy to identify."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Sarian's eyes go to Ystile for a moment, curious as to what draws her curiosity before turning back to Nasiri, "Mister Rhys is a man who seems to be very much living in the past. Assuming that all the rules and motivations that were true twenty years ago are true now. Our feelings about the Imperium are mixed and it may very well contract under its own weight much like the Republic seems to be slowly falling itself. The Union is doing well and is well poised to carry along fine even if other powers lose their lustre. But we are allied to the Imperium and that has befitted us financially and militarily. The existence of a new player on the board offers no real benefit but does present great risk to the stability we enjoy now. And it would seem that Mister Rhys feels he can offer us some better deal than we have from the Imperium....another soul under the mistaken belief that the Union is an Imperium puppet."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nasiri takes a long pull from the drink and nods. "Fair." he intones in a neutral voice. "In any event thank you for the name and if its ok, I thought i might take in the sites for awhile." a brief glance around. "I have been away a long time and it would be nice to catch up on affairs here and there." he nods to Ystile. "She is fine...just a bit eccentric at times."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I would be curious why you are seeking him, but I am willing to allow a little mystery. One of the sights you are refamiliarising yourself with. I think we are headed towards some major conflict in the galaxy. One that men like Rhys and Kairn will on the other side of from the Union. We all have such long and complicated histories with the players who have long been on the board. And which either side of the board you end up on in the end, the Union will remember and take note. I think though we have the grounds for renewed friendship."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian then motions out the window, "The Xi Char and the Union are happy to have guests. The Xi Charrians are a rather special people, so very alien compared most of the sentients we deal with, but they possess a great spirit. If you have the opportunity I would highly recommended a visit to one of the cathedral factories. It is rare for anyone to come away from the experience unmoved. And my thanks for coming to see me, it has been enlightening."

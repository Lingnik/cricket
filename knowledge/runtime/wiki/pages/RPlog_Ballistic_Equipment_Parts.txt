&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Babysitting her mother's astromech wasn't what Ara had in mind when she woke up that morning -- or any morning, really -- and as she accompanies Cricket from one shop to another, she can't help but wonder whether twenty-four years without a memory wipe is some kind of record, and whether the years are simply making him more deranged. Can droids develop dementia? Is a sign of said dementia the persistent need to rifle through every container on every shelf for parts, drawing stares and expressions of disapproval from proprietors and their employees?<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Inquiring minds want to know.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Okay, I don't think you're going to find it here," she interjects as his pincer-arm pulls at a spool of wiring with far too much enthusiasm, "Maybe... let go of that and we'll hit up someplace else?"<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I like you," Cricket replies, ignoring her suggestion to stop getting his grubby paws all over the merchandise, "But don't tell me what to do. I'm older than you, and it's not polite to sass your elders."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From behind a tattered scrap of cloth that could barely be considered a curtain across the back room doorway, a very tall and slender figure emerges. The gray skin and oblong head might make some aware of that this being is a Muun. Since the Empire, the Muun's banking clans had been divested of their wealth and some apparently found their way across the stars to the Outer Rim.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"One moment! One moment!" The Muun is tying the strings of a very clean apron behind his back. "I apologize but my doorway sensors aren't working right now..." He fingers his square chin. "Oh...they'll be fixed soon! No vulnerabilities here!"<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Scuttling over towards the customers the proprietor eyes the girl and her droid, he chooses to address the girl. "Is there anything that I can help you find?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;What business Atsvara still had in Mos Espa was a mystery. In a way she was discovering some of the truths her father already knew. There was something about Tatooine that seemed to pull you in. It made little sense, the world itself was awful and had little appeal beyond what an artist might find when painting the ruins of a destroyed city. Strategically it was of little value as well, though other world near it had resources to speak of one would find little reason to bother with an outpost here. About the only export anyone in her circle cared about here was Japor ivory wood. And even at that it probably would have little interest were it something found on some other world.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ultimately though what kept the Tarasars coming back to Tatooine was Johanna and her family. The magnetism was the people. Though that too begged the question of whether they were drawn here by something about the world. Was it simply a place of birth that kept Damions and Skywalkers coming back or did something bring them there in the first place.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In today's case, however, Atsvara's draw to the city was likely the people. Well a person. Well actually a Droid. Astvara was quite fond of Cricket, but her choice in him as a business partner was quite practical. He has crookedly amassed great wealth behind Johanna's back just as she had done behind the back of her parents. He possessed important connexions as a member of the Imperator's family. And importantly as a Droid he was being oppressed by society, a condition Astvara both sympathised with and appreciated the value in exploiting.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Emerging near the same stall as Cricket and Ara, the golden-skinned child interjected to the astromech who she was semi-trailing, "I am not sure why you insisted in coming here. Not like you are going to find some heirloom Skywalker creation, besides that little shit was waiting his time on pod racers not prodding tools." She was not quite sure we she decided to denigrate the memory of a well respected Sith Lord, but something told her he had been an annoying child. "You should just come up to the Leviathan I am sure we can find something you will like and probably illegal to boot."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"No thank you," Cricket bleeps, swiveling his dome towards the shopkeeper just as Ara gets the spool to stop spinning on its rack, "I think we're going someplace else. There aren't enough parts here for a taser... at least, not the upgraded kind I'm looking to assemble. Those fuckers at the stati--"<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Oh, would you look at the time!" Ara exclaims, wishing to spare herself the embarrassment of an expletive-laced tirade against the local police force, "Cricket, we should get back home, we're expected for... lunch."<nr>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"What lunch? Will there be fish? Oh look it's Atsvara! HELLO ATSVARA!" And just like that, the droid wheels over to his young meatbag partner in crime, leaving Ara smiling awkwardly at the Muun.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I'm sorry, he's very old and suffering from--"<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I HEARD THAT, AND I AM NOT," Cricket shrieks.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Muun's deep-set red eyes blink at the astromech's abruptness, but then he smiles softly at the likely beleaguered young woman. "I understand." He starts a little when Cricket hollers back at Ara. "It happens to the best of us." He speaks pleasantly with a friendly tone. But...if I can help you or your droid source anything, I have a particularly deep network not only among the Jawa tribes but also with a few off-world...acquirers of ballistic equipment parts." He produces a chip-embossed flimsi-holo card that, when held to the light, spins a circular cog logo with the name Bil Faan inscribed in basic and presents it to the human. "Should you need assistance, my shop is always at your service!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Atsvara had not thought much of the possibilities in Mos Espa. Surely the Leviathan would better suit Cricket's needs. After all a tenth of the crew were Droids. And in spite of it being seconded to the Caspian Navy, it was still a Sith ship. Besides her father dabble in Droid work himself, such as creation of her friend and fellow conspirator Vee-Won. Then there was the Union's own dabbling in droid work, and multiple members of the Union Council were in on her scheming....<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But she catches the Muun's comment to Ara, she had been spying on this after all. Ballistic equipment parts indeed. The young girl looks to Cricket, "Well it sounds like we should give this guy another chance. Sounds like he is on the level." The level, of course, being crime.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Making her own way towards the Muun she remarks, "Our friend here...." Her choice of words here and a glance and smile towards Ara, who she now aims to rope in as a co-conspirator to this particular scheme if none others, "Our friend has suffered some inhumane treatment at the hands of overzealous and misguided forces of the local constabulary. To make a long story short, would you happen to have any of these ballistic equipment parts on hand?"<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Still dressed in her own black uniform of the House Dragonflower branch of the Sith, Atsvara makes no great effort at subtlety here. Bil Faan has already identified himself as a dealer in what is surely black market goods. No use hiding what they were after here. Besides he could always be disposed of if he became a nuisance.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Thanks, we're--" But Ara is cut off again, this time as Cricket and Atsvara both perk up at the mention of ballistic equipment and make it their business to interrogate the Muun about the glorious possibilities open to them as harbingers of chaos. The young woman stares at the impudent duo and lofts a brow, unable to countenance such blatant sympathy-whoring in public... based on lies, no less. "Is that so? As I recall, the 'inhumane treatment' started when Cricket decided to play hide-the-taser with Axel Vichten's unmentionables," she replies, "Now, I can't stand the man either, but you don't see me shoving stuff where the sun don't shine. Really, Cricket... /and Atsvara/. You know what you did!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Well, well, well, well, well, well......well!" The slender Muun places his long fingers to his chest as if appalled. By his dour expression it doesn't exactly come across as mocking. He takes a small step backwards, which still puts quite a distance between himself and the shopping trio. "I'm afraid I couldn't possibly be associated with any local terrorism. The daimyo would have my poor grey hide." With a second step he is back towards the storeroom, he looks nervous, but manages a warning glare to take the business no further. "I do have to make a living, you know."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Firrerreo-Sarian gives an almost sheepish look, "I must admit I would not have handled the Axel situation in quite the same manner as our friend did, I am not about to question his judgement. The man is a loose cannon and beyond what ever friendship our parents might have with him or any practical value his skill set may be to him....sometimes he needs the shit kicked out of him for the safety of others. Cricket just took that a little more literally than we might. Who am I to judge."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Atsvara turns to the Muun, "Terrorism? Certainly not. Little vigilantism at best." Looking to Ara she adds, "The fact that Cricket does not have some sort of diplomatic immunity is the real crime here. He is after all a member of the Daimyo's household after all." She grins back to the Muun and adds, "And I assure you our credits are just a shiny as hers."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She then turns back to Ara, "As for what I have done?" But she has done so many things. Spent years gaining power within Biscuit Baron inch by inch with key executives. Instigated a multi-sector strike at the galaxy's largest restaurant chain. Used her business and political connexions to prevent the strike getting resolved for months....but Ara probably just means breaking out Cricket. "He never should have been locked up. And I got him out peacefully. And I insured Cricket took no retaliatory actions, which he would have been more than justified in doing." In her best big girl voice she adds, "We showed a lot of restraint." Was a commando team rescue a possibility? Perhaps.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She looks back towards the Muun again. Was he really going to be so obstinate. Was Mos Espa really the height of his ambitions. "You can't just mention ballistic equipment parts and go all quiet. A squeamish arms trader? Really?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Retaliatory actions?" Ara sputters incredulously as it dawns on her that this particular pair might pose more of a threat to the nascent law and order on Tatooine than she had ever dreamed possible, "Why do I have the feeling /someone/ is going to get the restraining bolt when we get home? Ballistic equipment, my foot." Then, to Atsvara, "You oughta stop riling Cricket up, you're not doing him any favors. Nobody has immunity here: not him, not me, not my mother. Break the law, face the consequences." Just what kind of a corrupt, fucked-up circus does the kid think they're trying to run here? Maybe it's better not to think about that one for too long, the possibilities are probably alarming. "I'm sorry we disturbed you, Mister Faan. You have yourself a nice day."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Oh!" Bil's sanguine eyes grow as wide as his deep sockets will allow. "That's HER droid? I...I didn't...I mean..." He bends his long neck in a bow of respect. "Then please...let me show you some...options, I suppose?" What looks like a cheap plast crate opens as Bill turns it's pressure hasp, opening into a fine display of backlit mayhem makers. "I heard mention of tasing? I have this piece salvaged from a force pike. Good for a jolt, I'd say." His graceful fingers move on to another static protected module. "Or perhaps a /stun/ unit would minimize casualties of the, uh...nethers?"<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Upon hearing Ara's speechifying, Bil Faan, activates the closing mechanism on the case that shuts with a loud snap. "Quite right, you are, miss." He gives Atsvara and Cricket a chastising head shake. "You two need better hobbies!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The words 'restraining bolt' wound Atsvara. Her father professed his belief in Droid Rights, and he practiced it earnestly in his own realm, but friendships and political realities kept him from going far enough in her mind. Atsvara was quite fond of Johanna too, but poor Cricket's situation was galling to her. In any event he was now an integral part of her corporate shenanigans. He was after all a major investor in the Golden Bantha Group. She gives Ara a somewhat resigned expression. She was up against an obstacle on this dejarik board. She did not fear losing the game nor feel herself outplayed, but she would now need be more circumspect in her strategy. If Cricket was about to end up in the proverbial clink again, she was not about to telegraph her break out strategy. And she imagine no matter whatever outward move she made at this moment, Cricket knew her well enough to realise she was still scheming on his behalf.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The momentary excitement at the Muun's wares are dashed as he once again backs down like the second rate hack she has been sizing him up to be. It was a shame it sound like he had good wares. Perhaps not better than anything she could get elsewhere, but worth acquiring nevertheless. The question now remained would she approach him later more quietly for these goods? Perhaps send her agents to rob them of him? Or perhaps just forgive the whole matter as an unneeded distraction? "I am sorry if we caused you any bother sir." She would pretend to be rightly chastised. If the fool believed it was his own damned fault.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sensing Atsvara's deep unease, Ara softens her stance a little. "He won't actually get the restraining bolt," she reassures the girl, "I mean, so long as he's not trying to uh... molest folks' nethers. Grab-ass isn't funny, even if it's an R2 unit doing it."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cricket -- who had started to buzz with mounting rage at the thought of the dreaded bolt -- calms himself, his lights no longer blinking so rapidly and the hate-shakes subsiding. One day, he would concoct the appropriate punishment for Johanna having used it on him on Bespin. He's had a few years to think about it, and a few more won't hurt if they help him craft sweet, sweet revenge. "Fine," he zots, "Yes, let us be good and law-abiding citizens. Just like Zeak wants us to, I'm sure."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"/Let's go/," Ara nudges, used to Cricket's bad habit of mentioning her father when he's mad.

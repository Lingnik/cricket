Coronet City Spaceport -- Corellia

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;This port is busy, like most, but here the hot breath of takeoff and the deep rumble of engines is closer, more of a feature of the port than an annoyance. The landing pads and small maintenance bunkers sprawl across a large open area open to the sky and surrounded by blast walls to protect the farmlands beyond. Pilots, engineers and techs, mostly natives, move among the hardware as if at home in the confusing and fast paced Port.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A large complex on the west end of the spaceport houses control functions, transport authority offices, and the CorSec station. To the south, large gates are wide open allowing free access to this section of the spaceport landing area. There's another exit to the east, around the more rundown section of the spaceport. At the north end of the landing area, two monorail tracks emerge from underground in front of a small ticket station.<br>



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The remains of the recently crashed vessel have finally been all hauled away, life at the spaceport returning to some semblance of normal. Or as normal as one can get when a great number of the galaxy's elite are visiting Corellia. In one part of the spaceport security presence is particularly high as Prime Minister Blazer comes to greet one of the many delegations that has come to meet with him. But elsewhere it seems like a normal day of business with freighter and passenger ships loading and unloading with mild efficiency.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The wreck is gone... and so is Selko's job with the breaking crew. At least she has a little money in her pockets now. Maybe it'll keep her going 'til the next job comes along.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She can hope, right?<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Right now, the plasspec-wearing technician is checking the posted list of mechanical/technical jobs for the day on her uplinked datapad. It's a long read: The large crowd of people nearby keeps distracting her... it looks like a heavy security presence for someone. She just can't see who.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The sound of the approaching monorail is at such a rumbling decibel that the landing strip and accompanying walkways become thick with dust and small bits of debris and rubble skitter and bounce in every single direction. Sure, the wreck is gone but no one ever really gets the dust completely cleaned up. Once the monorail arrives and the doors open there's the usual mess of passengers emerging and trying to onboard at the exact same time. Siarra is just one of the many emerging from the monorail, carrying a mesh satchel slung over one shoulder and a to-go cup in the other.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A small throng of people come into the area, having just landed moments ago. The majority of the group is comprised of guards, aides and general lackies, with the person they are here with somewhere in the middle of that mess of bodies.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dressed in the robes of a Moff, the former pilot named Liza Molokai glances side to side, aware of her surroundings in a way that hints to her prior military training. Present as one member of a delegation, she looks all business.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze shoulders his way off the tram, too, arms crammed with achingly-tacky souvenirs and a half-eaten 'danger-dog' street weiner. "Oh, pardon me!" He clutches his brown-bagged parcels close, unwilling to lose ancient(!) Corellian(!) history(!) in the form of a screen-printed vase worth, at best, a hundred credits. There's just no telling what he actually paid...<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the distance some faint rumbling can be heard though it is far enough away to make it difficult to discern if it is merely a weather pattern or perhaps something unnatural such an explosion. The rumblings start to get close and the CorSec presence begins to hurriedly evaporate from the area save for the detail immediately surrounding Dash Blazer. Their alarm is up but whatever it is going on elsewhere in their city is not their priority, they are here to protect the leader of the Corellian Confederation, and with something clearly going on elsewhere of note they seem to be quite on edge, perhaps trying to determine where he will be safest. For the moment they seem rather stationary still some distance from the shuttles of Presav Tarasar and Moff Molokai which are both nearby.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Once most of the security clearance has dissipated another nearby shuttle begins to take off but its flight plan is almost immediately erratic and it soon comes down at a hard irregular landing, fully intact but clearly something has gone wrong. The sound is far more distinct than those in the distance and suddenly there are people running both towards the scene and away from it. From nearby Lambda-Class Shuttle, Presav Tarasar emerges and takes a look toward the one that just made a thud.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Siarra moves along with the crowd that's mingling near the entrance to the monorail platform, eyeing the increased security that's present on Corellia, of all places, and moves out of the way of foot traffic to get a read on the what and why of it. The sudden need to be Somewhere Else of the CorSec personnel has Siarra reaching for the com badge clipped to her shoulder and speaking quietly into it, "Just in case, why don't we power up the engines and be ready to not be in the way," is how she ends her remarks before deciding to the take the round-about way to her ship.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Distracted by the heavy security presence and a sudden pop-up warning on her datapad, Selko barely notices the incoming monorail tram in time to not end up as part of it! She hastily steps back with a yelp as the vehicle pulls in, sighing in relief when only its backdraft hits her.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Equally startling is when its side opens, disgorging a steady stream of people. She ends up in the middle of this mass, bumped uncomfortably on both sides by people coming and going. "Um, 'scuse me..." she murmurs, trying to work her way to the edge of this torrent of sentient life, barely avoiding a collision with a blonde woman with a lot of earrings and a Quarren with a lot of... well, stuff. Including a vase, of all things.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Which is about all she manages to do. But as with all things, the boarding finally ends, leaving her standing alone next to the monorail.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Just then, the sound of a shuttle leaving the ground catches her ear. It's shortly followed by the sound of the same shuttle returning to the ground... and a lot less gently than it left it! She stares at it for a long moment, then it occurs to her that whoever's on it is probably in trouble! She stumbles as she tries to turn her feet in that direction, waving overhead to signal the emergency crew and pointing frantically as she hurries in the direction of the downed craft. "Hey! HEY! Shuttle trouble!"<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Everything going on puts Liza's guards on alert while the newly made Moff herself is calm, everything she has seen and gone through in life having left the Sarian fairly unflappable. "Well, this is more excitement than I wanted on my first trip home since..." Faltering, she falls silent as she watches the ship that had the rough landing, the ex-pilot shaking her head, clearly unimpressed.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Catching sight of Crestian, she steers herself in his direction, those who work for her forced to follow hurriedly as she forgets to wait for everyone else. "Corellia hasn't changed much," she offers to him once within earshot, offering him a hand once close enough. "How are you doing?" Quick glances here and there has her seeing Siarra, but the other is a stranger and left to their own devices, for now.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the nature of their immediate situation becoming clear the CorSec officer who had attempted to call for backup looks to the Prime Minister, "We can't seem to call for reinforcements, we need to get you out of here before those prisoners try to hijack another ship." Blazer tries to protest, pulling out a DL-44 Heavy Blaster and seeming prepared to engage as his guards try to convince him otherwise.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Prime Minister seems to have convince his detail to stay long enough to close the distance between him and Liza and Crestian, undoubtedly having been set to meet with one or the other of them or both.

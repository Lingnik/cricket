The death of Basca Starker provided less personal relief to Crestian than he had been hoping it might, but whatever feelings it elicited were a matter for later consideration as it left him with certain obligations. The information would need to be presented publicly but there was one individual he felt deserved to hear the news first, Jessalyn Valios. The Caspian leader still could not make sense of how she got caught up with the future carbonite curio nor her seeming change of heart about him in their last interaction. What he did know was their was a connexion strong enough that she would unique interest in this news, and though he did not regret his callous treatment of her before he was not a monster that needed it compounded. The galaxy was a big place but finding the ship of one Jedi to send a message was a manageable task and so the call was made.

The hologram crackles as static cuts through the connection for a moment, but then a cowled figure comes into focus, offering a simple nod of acknowledgment as Jessalyn's voice cuts through. "Presav Tarasar. To what do I owe the pleasure of hearing from you?" she asks. Her voice is terse and perhaps a touch sardonic as well, and when she pulls back the hood of her cloak, her eyes are fierce and gleaming. 

Crestian takes a deep breath and sighs. There really was no good segue into this. "I suspect the news will make you no happier to hear from me then you are at the moment but I have to things to pass along, the first I felt you deserved to hear before it became public. Basca Starker is dead. We were presented with his body earlier. I can pass along the image and scans if you want, but beyond obviously looking like him it matches what we had for genetic material on him. I am sorry to have to be the one to tell you."

The look that crosses her face is one of resigned acceptance and something else inscrutable, and despite a moment of trying to school her features into something presentable, she turns her gaze away and closes her eyes. "Yes, I'm certain you are very sorry, indeed," Jessalyn says at last. "Thank you for sending me word. I hope you can understand the difficulty of hearing the news. Please express my condolences to your people for all that they have been through. I'm sorry that it had to be end like this, Presav." 

Crestian is seemingly unphased by the reply he receives. In the time it had taken to arrange the call he had time to consider the various reactions that news might receive. This was fairly in line with what he considered likely. There was one other piece of news he was less sure of the result of sharing. Nodding in her response to her reply to him, Crestian adds, "You should also be aware that the body was delivered by his brother Marius Starker. He indicated that he had some history with you and intended to seek you out. He seemed rather concerned about you. I hope his efforts to find you will not prove unwelcome but I figured you should know in either case that you can expect it."

If she was not surprised before, she certainly is this time, and the Jedi looks up and blinks several times. She takes an unsteady step forward. "Marius... Starker?" Her hand gestures to her forehead and she looks puzzled for a long moment. "They were related?" Something seems to click right there for her, and her eyes widen as she gives a short laugh. "Of course. It all makes sense now." Her head shakes back and forth as if she can't quite believe what she's heard. "I'm glad that the Starker family was able to take care of their own unfinished business. It seems fitting, somehow. If you are able to, please tell him I look forward to our meeting again."

"I am not sure if he is still planetside but I will pass the world along if he is. I did not really pass along anything specific to him as it was not my place, but he had been concerned about anyone who had been left behind. It is hard to imagine...." Crestian stops himself from continuing the thought, as it seemed to violate decorum in this moment. "He did seem like an honourable man in our brief meeting, I respect that he took it upon himself to resolve the matter. He did indicate an effort to have his brother brought in alive but there was apparently some complication in that regard."

A small, bitter smile touches Jessa's mouth, and she dabs at the corner of her eyes with her sleeve very delicately. "I see," she says softly. "At least that poor man is no longer suffering. I wish I could have been able to help him." Her eyes fill but she doesn't look away this time. "I befriended Marius Starker some years ago, but confess I had all but forgotten him until you mentioned his name. I'm not surprised that he did the honorable thing here." She clears her throat and leaves it at that, pulling the cowl back over her hair once more. "Caspar can move forward now. Perhaps we can as well, Presav -- but do not think that I've forgotten your unkindness." She bows at the waist very deeply. "Farewell, Tarasar."

In any other moment Crestian might have reiterated that he had made no apology for his unkindness, he was not going to feel bad about the barbs that were launched when confronted with the revelation of the genocide. But there was no need to confront her grief with further bombastic displays. Basca had always been the true target, and he was dead now. "Well you made quite an impression on Marius it seems. May you both find some peace. Tarasar out."

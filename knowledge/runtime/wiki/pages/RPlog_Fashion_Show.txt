Royale Hotel Ballroom -- Coronet City: Corellia

The ballroom of the Royale Hotel has recently been renovated, leading to a space that is even more elegant than before. The general structure of the room is huge semi-circular shape taking up three storeys. Against the straight wall in the back is a small semi-circular stage. Along the curved wall are dozens of circular tables that can comfortably seat six. In between some of the tables are Corellian columns made of polished yellow Selonian marble which hold up a two mezzanine levels with more tables. At each end glass elevators and spiral staircases allow access from the mezzanines to the main floor.

The walls are covered with golden-coloured Creshik wood accented with flecks of Corellian gold. The stage is likewise made of Creshik wood with gold flecks. In keeping in the scheme of the floors elsewhere in the hotel, the main dance floor is made of red kiliad marble with flecks of Corellian gold. The tables are and chairs are made of ornately carved Corellian hardwood with gold velvet upholstery. Three large crystal chandeliers hang above the floor.

For this evening's fashion show and extension has been added to the stage in the form of a catwalk which extends several metres onto the main dance floor. Much of the dance floor is currently filled with chairs, in similar style to those at the tables. The chairs are facing the catwalk.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The ballroom of the Royale Hotel is quite crowded this evening. Charity and Fashion make surprisingly good bedfellows. While the tickets for the event were pricey, the cause of charity provided some excuse for those who could not justify the cost on pure fashion gazing alone, the reverse was true as well.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The guests have come from all the spheres of consequence. There are business tycoons, government officials, and military leaders from around the galaxy. Yet, it would hardly be in the spirit of charity if the downtrodden were barred from this display of culture, quite a number of tickets were raffled off to give anyone a chance to be here.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the final spectators shuffle in, Zubindi Hakoon himself stands at the back of the stage talking to Veruca Violet. Zubindi ironically is not in an outfit customised for this gathering, but rather an old standby he tends to wear at such events. Its gaudy appearance was a step beyond most of the male fashions he produced, looking much like he might be the star pimp of Nar Shaddaa. But it was not without a purpose, in a sense it served as an intentional critique on his own industry. The outfit of his Pantoran protege was new, but not without serving as a reliable standard. Per usual it was a vine-silk kimono. In today's case green in colour with floral embroidery.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Among those standing near the front row, chatting while waiting for things to begin are Caspian Presav Crestian Tarasar and his wife and daughter. Much like his friend Zubindi, the Sarian statesman appears to be recycling a look, wearing a dark viridian suit and Kubazian skirt made of Ottegan silk with a platinum coloured shraa silk spavat. His daughter Atsvara, the Biscuit Baroness, has similarly adopted Ottegan silk is a more traditional shade of green, with glitter-encrusted tan shimmersilk vest, gold glitter-encrusted shimmersilk necktie, and black nanosilk vest. Her mother Meridian wears a a form hugging black nanosilk dress with gold embroidery much the same shade as her skin.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There's something to be said about retiring, which is a life-changing experience in and of itself. But even more, there's something to be said about dipping into decades of unused pay for attire (who needs attire when it's often supplied by the military for nearly every possible scenario) and wearing something that genuinely fits the description of fashionable. Lynae adjusts the fit and flare of the sweeping cape that is paired with the liquid-gold jumpsuit that's tailored to her form, wiggles her toes in the pointed metallic heels, as she mingles with the other final spectators that are shuffling in. Shuffle? one does not shuffle in pointed metallic heels. One tries not to step on strangers!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A small gathering of the ARC executive team, whom have managed the fundraiser events to date, are escorted towards a row of reserved seats for the fashion show. Wearing what would be considered fashionable representations of their home system's traditional regalia: Corellia, Tatooine, Arkanis, Bonadan, Athaniss, and Chandrila are all represented. Greetings, smiles, and pleasant affirmations are exchanged with those around them, as they get settled in.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;After a final, dramatic flourish with his stylus-tipped datapad, sealing the last autograph with a flourish, Thurston Unger hands the signed datapad to a delighted fan. He then nods to his entourage. He is a walking piece of performance art, his iridescent synth-scales jacket seeming to shift in color with the energy of the crowd.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The entire Chrome Court, a dozen couture-plated droids of varying styles and metallic sheens, stands in a perfectly synchronized line behind him. With an almost silent, mechanical grace, they all raise their arms in unison, each holding a shimmering, autographed can of Crimson Nerf. They are a single, cohesive statement of power and polished precision.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The group pauses just inside the ballroom's gilded entrance, allowing the camera droid hovering just over their shoulders to capture the moment. Thurston gives the crowd a slow, sardonic smirk before beginning a languid, regal procession toward their seats near the catwalk. A can of Crimson Nerf is held delicately in his hand, a prop in the grand theatrical play of the evening, and a statement that the work of charity, like the work of his journalism, is a full-time performance.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No flash or fanfare announces Krackor Reincus as he weaves his way through the crowd. The dark black suit is a bit of a contrast to his usual garb, but he moves with the same casual ease as ever. His eyes scan the crowded ballroom for familiar faces and it doesn't take long for a glass to find its way into his hand.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The conversation between Zubindi and Veruca Violet brakes up with the latter heading backstage. Zubindi approaches a microphone and states, "If everyone could gather their drinks and head to their seats and designated seating and standing areas," the latter in recognition of some guests such as the Xi Charrians present who clearly will not be sitting in a chair, "we will be starting shortly."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lynae selects a glass of something bubbly off of the tray of one of the servers that mingle with the crowd, sips from it somewhat cautiously, then carries it with her to take a seat that will afford something of a decent view of the catwalk. There's a trick to wearing a cape, taking a seat, and not spilling the drink - but Lynae has always been fond of a challenge!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As Zubindi Hakoon's announcement echoes through the ballroom, Thurston Unger regards the ensuing scramble for seats with an arch of his perfectly sculpted eyebrow. He gives a theatrical sigh, offering a slow, appreciative wave to the general populace before turning to his impeccably arranged Chrome Court, who stand at attention, each holding a can of Crimson Nerf.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"My darlings," Thurston purrs, his voice a low, melodious whisper, "do observe the sheer enthusiasm! One would think the fashion itself were a life-or-death sport, given the spirited rush for a prime viewing angle."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pearletta, her powder-pink chrome gleaming, tilts her head subtly. "Sir, efficiency metrics indicate a significant reduction in personal space, yet social engagement protocols remain active."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Oh, Pearletta, you and your numbers," Thurston sighs dramatically, waving a hand. "This isn't about metrics; it's about making a moment. And frankly, a moment of such delightful, chaotic energy is a gift we must cherish."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He then glances at Tarnish LaRue, whose oxidized chrome reflects the room's grandeur with a cynical gleam. "Tarnish, my dear, do you believe any of these... passionate attendees truly grasp the intricate artistry of haute couture, or are they merely here for the free bubbly and the delightful chaos of an evening with our humble crew?"<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tarnish's vocalizer emits a low, dry chuckle. "Thurston, darling, they are here for the spectacle. And perhaps, for the sheer joy of being in a casino that, for once, seems to be a reliable source of entertainment."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"A perfectly valid point, as always," Thurston concedes, a sly smile gracing his lips. He then gestures toward a discreet, yet impeccably styled, table near the entrance, laden with shimmering cans of Crimson Nerf. A dedicated serving droid, gleaming with fresh polish, stands ready to dispense the various flavors, including the highly exclusive, one-night-only Crimson Nerf Dark: Iridescent Intrigue.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Now, let's ensure our loyal connoisseurs are suitably hydrated," Thurston declares, with the theatrical flourish of a king. "After all, one needs a robust palate to appreciate both exquisite fashion and exquisite fun. Lead the way, my droids. Our public awaits our final, dazzling entrance to our seats." With that, he begins a languid, regal procession toward their designated area near the catwalk, allowing the lingering crowd to marvel at his ensemble, and the Chrome Court to distribute their precious cargo.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Two women from the ARC team, Lirae Moncair of Chandrila and Senya Drayel of Arkanis, chat excitedly about the show. "This is exciting! I've never been to a Silverwood House of Fashion event before," exclaims Lirae. Meanwhile, Joren Vaylen of Corellia, Kael Thorne of Athaniss, Ressik Damar of Tatooine, and Tessari Vonn of Bonadan, keep to themselves and their libations - all electing to sip on local Corellian spirits that the Royale keeps well stocked.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Krackor knocks back his drink and takes another before selecting a place to sit somewhere near the back. He wasn't here for the fashion show, but it made a fine excuse for him to be on Corellia. He checks his chrono, taking note of the time before getting comfortable. He spots a few familiar faces in the crowd, but none of them cause any immediate concern for him. For now, he's content to sit and wait.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Once most everyone was suitably where they were supposed to be Zubindi greets in Corellian, "Welcome to Corellia everyone I am so very happy to be doing this event here in Coronet City. The Corellian people have been so welcoming, and a special shout out to Prime Minister Blazer for his help facilitating some of the logistics." The Kubaz does a finger gun gesture over at Corellian Confederation Prime Minister Dash Blazer who is sitting in the front row with an exquisitely dressed Selonian woman. Dash smiles and makes the gesture back at Zubindi.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Zubindi returns to speaking Basic, "Corellia has a special place in my heart. It is one of the unsung stars in the galactic fashion world, never given the credit it deserves, hopefully we will help correct the imbalance a little. Normally such a showing would be to advertise a new collection, but alas the hard work in helping the Arkanis Sector has been a focus Silverwood of late. Instead though you will be treated to several gorgeous models clad in an unique array of outfits design for the occasion as the inspiration stuck. And of course there is an open bar.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Before we get to the fashion though I want to speak a bit about why we are here tonight. A lot has been done already in the Arkanis Sector to support the population there from the aftermath of the crop failures and the communications sabotage. Sizeable donations have been made from across the galaxy particular from Kessel to combat the famine, house and transport refugees, restore communication infrastructure, etcetera. Where it not for the generosity found around the galaxy the situation could have gotten a lot worse.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Unfortunately, however, the need for aid is far from over. The collapse of agricultural on New Ator and Vasch will take years to finally recover from. It is one things for the other worlds that relied on their agriculture to seek imports elsewhere. It is another thing entirely for worlds that have been use to exporting to not have to import for the immediate future.

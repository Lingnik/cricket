Captain's Table - Noques Rasala: Caspar

The Captain's Table is a restaurant situated in the northern area of Sea's Edge. A fine dining establishment with limited seating, reservations are required though there are a few tables held open for those of local importance, or to someone who tips the host at the entrance with a hefty sum of credits.

The restaurant is in what was once the home of one of the founding fathers of Noques Rasala, a sea captain who'd made quite the fortune for himself. The grand hall is were the bulk of the limited seating is, though there were several private rooms for more private dining experiences.

The furnishings are all made of locally sourced hardwoods, very old and smoothed with ages of wear. The decor definitely has an archaic naval flair, and the staff attire are those of anachronistic naval officer uniforms, rank assigned by seniority on the staff.

The menu changes daily, always fresh and reflecting what was in season both from the sea and local flora and fauna. The only static menu is the one showing varied cocktails and a rather elaborate wine list. While there is no set dress code, it is understood that whoever comes here to dine wears nicer attire. Armor and weapons is usually frowned upon by the host, and finding the right reservation or empty table might be difficult if the host doesn't approve of the way the customer dresses.

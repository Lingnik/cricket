''The hangar is relatively large, with pads marked for the varying type ships that may land within the ship's belly. To the aft of the hangar, there are a couple of special landing pads built near automated loaders and unloaders specifically modified for large mechanical objects. The bay is open to three small monitoring/observation rooms, inset behind tinted transparisteel glass overlooking the entire hangar bay.''

''To the left of the middle observation room is a small console controlling a turbolift. To the right of the middle observation room is a blast door with "Engine Mechanics" posted over it, with smaller letters that spell 'Authorized Personnel Only'. It has a keylock entry.''

Thanks to the mess chief there's no shortage of fresh brewed caf and food on hand in advance of the all-hands meeting to take place in the hangar, which is the largest area on the ship for everyone to gather. The crates and cargo containers have been squared away and many are moved into position to provide make-shift seating for the crew. Siarra has dragged in a display screen that's linked to the ship's network and - in turn - available to link to the data devices that the crew have access to. On the screen are several windows displaying segments of the data that Druze swiped from the Pirate base before their team made a clean (no one got dead) get away.

Wrexan had been 'stuck' flying AirCap patrol while that shuttle had landed and the commando team did their business. All the fun for him had ended by the time the Shuttle had even started its approach run on the base. The Mirialan is plopped onto a low and wide crate that is probably hold proton torpedoes or more seriously concerning for a starfighter pilot. Jumper cables for an x-wing. His thumb tap-tap-taps him through whatever presentation is about to happen so he'll have seen something before people talk about it.

Druze observes, drily: "I've already told Kelnas: my supply of rabies and feline distemper shots is *limited,* so if you smuggled a kitten aboard any of our ships, do not come crying to 'Dr. Druze, chief engineer and pretty please veterinarian' six weeks from now." He definitely isn't a loth-cat person. "That said, those empty cages weren't more 'illegal pets,' they were people. We're estimating two dozen refugees."

The colonel, in celebration of a job well done had relaxed the duty roster for the past couple days, and doubled the booze rations for his marines and the few naval types on the Templar. While the quartermaster (Siarra) had set up the food, Colonel Eckhart had come bearing a case of Corellia's finest, affordable on a budget, hooch. It wasn't Whyren's, but it was better than the swill he drank as a much younger marine after a successful mission.

Setting the case down on a shipping crate, and opening it up to pull out a sleeve of disposable cups, nothing but the best for his crew and their allies after all, he takes a seat to let Siarra do her job. "Help yourselves," he says aloud, while he goes to pour himself a very large mug of caff.

When Druze pipes up about the kittens, the old marine snarls and looks about. There was a strict, no pets rule on the Templar. It wasn't fair to the pet or those aboard, and the damned things made his nose itch something fierce when they were around.

Kelnas was there. Dressed in his black Cargo pants and usual padded vest with the dark spaces where patches used to reside. the vest left open to reveal a black shirt with the face of a Cartoon wookiee on it. the words "Pembroke Cola! Tame the thirst!" bordering the image in bright yellow writing.

Looking up at Druze mentions rabies and feline distemper shots, he clears his throat. "to be fair, my request had nothing to do with Loth-cats and more to do with one of the marines looking like they were going to eat me." Pointing to a decidedly feline looking marine sitting on one of the crates, he adds. "Yeah you." Already having a cup of caff in one hand, and a datapad in the other, the Wroonian goes back to looking over information.

Siarra is using the data slate, which normally is clipped to her belt, to manipulate some of the data on display, "But.. kittens," the marine points out in a totally reasonable tone of voice before she clips it to her belt again and gets not one cup but two. She carries them over to sit beside Wrexan, handing one cup to the pilot while Kelnas's words make her laugh. "It's against the official code of conduct on this ship to serve other members of the crew for dinner or any type of snack. It's bad for morale." She lifts the disposable cup she's holding, "Good work, everyone. Pirates 0, Misfits and Mutineers 1," toasting everyone with that gesture.

Druze *taps* his datapad, summoning a holo-rendering of the pirate base. "Thanks to a lab here on Corellia that can sequence DNA from 'miscellaneous samples' without asking follow-up questions, we think we're looking for six or eight female Twi'leks, a handful of Wookiees, and the rest are a scattershot distribution of typical targets for monetizable species." He wags his wireless pointer. "Hyperjump data indicates frequent trips to Nar Shaddaa, probably loading and unloading for the Hutts. The target vessels are frequently chop-shopped or parted out to keep the transponder signals new..."

The colonel takes a seat, sipping at his coffee for a moment, the fresh steaming ambrosia not seeming to phase him. Likely, even his mouth probably had callouses like his hands.

"No pets on the ship, period," Eckhart says. "I won't tell you what you can and cannot do off-duty and off ship, but on the Templar, there are no pets. Get a droid, but make sure it's at least somewhat useful, if you need something to follow you around and make annoying noises. At least they don't piss on upholstery."

There's a glance towards Kelnas. "Careful, Corporal Sernz there is one of our scouts, if she wanted to tear into you, you'd never see or hear her, and we'd never find a body," he adds with a sense of pride. Eckhart truly loved his marines, and knew each and every one of their talents.

The colonel lights up a cigar, blowing a thick plume of smoke towards the ceiling of the hangar. "So, mission's not quite done. We can't take on the cartels as a whole, but we can maybe shut down this cell. We're going to have to be careful though. We're good, but attrition isn't in our favor against the Hutts on Nar Shaddaa."

Still looking over the datapad, Kelnas raises his mug in return of the toast before he absently replies to Siarra. "It's ok though, I was kind of into it." Hearing Eckhart, the Wroonian looks up and nods. "Duly noted Colonel." Looking back to Sernz, he mouths 'Definitely into it now." With a waggle of his eyebrows, Kelnas looks back to the datapad then speaks up to the group in general. "When I was a much less saintly and upstanding citizen than I am now, I knew of 5 private yards that could handle that kind of reworking traffic. 4 of then were owned by Hutts. the other one was technically owned by a co-operative of 3 separate gangs, but may also have been owned by a malfunctioning Protocol droid. I reached out to an old friend in that area. there are 3 still functioning, one of them still in the hands of a Protocol droid. The other 2 owned by Hutts."

Still looking over the datapad, Kelnas raises his mug in return of the toast before he absently replies to Siarra. "It's ok though, I was kind of into it." Hearing Eckhart, the Wroonian looks up and nods. "Duly noted Colonel." Looking back to Sernz, he mouths 'Definitely into it now."

With a waggle of his eyebrows, Kelnas looks back to the datapad then speaks up to the group in general. "When I was a much less saintly and upstanding citien than I am now, I knew of 5 private yards that could handle that kind of reworking traffic. 4 of then were owned by Hutts. the other one was technically owned by a co-operative of 3 separate gangs, but may also have been owned by a malfunctioning Protocol droid. I reached out to an old friend in that area. there are 3 still functioning, one of them still in the hands of a Protocol droid. The other 2 owned by Hutts."

"I do droids. I don't do livestock." Wrexan announces to anyone who will listen to very promptly and hopefully thoroughly disavow himself from any sort of pet related duties. Not his circus, not his kittens. But the Colonel is already disavowing EVERYONE from Cat Duty. "I'll build you a thing to meow at you and ignore your commands. It'll be like a real cat. Promise."

"It's Corellia," Siarra says this in a reasonable tone of voice, "at least half the businesses here are no-questions-asked, the other half are 'pay me enough and I'll forget this ever happened'." It's not cynicism, per se, but a - to her perspective - fair assessment of things on this planet. She eyes the display and taps one fingertip against the side of the disposable cup she's holding, "So. What are the odds they bailed and went to ground on Nar Shaddaa as well?" she wonders before glancing toward Eckhart and giving him a deliberately loud mock sigh. "Boss, you are taking all the joy out of my life. I wanted one of those kittens," there's a quick grin that chases her words as he lights up a cigar. "Oh, sure, you get to smoke but I can't have a Loth-kitten," more amusement shading her voice before Wrexan's words spark a laugh. "I'll hold you to that promise," said with a grin even as Kelnas draws her attention. "Five?" she doesn't even try to keep the surprise out of her tone of voice. "So... this cargo ship chop shop business is lucrative."

Druze asks: "So, does anyone have an innovative idea for rescuing slaves out from under a group of organized professional criminals? Floor's open."

"Think we can trade in the Guppy and get a real ship?" Eckhart asks, before looking back at Siarra. "Back in, oh 5 or 6 ABY, we were still doing mop-up work after the second death star, we had a bunch of furry critters that would infiltrate our base. About the size of a kitten, and they'd never seen people, so were curious and friendly enough. After a week or so, we learned that they found our plastique quite tasty. One ventured too close to a perimeter fence, a little jolt, and..." he looks at Siarra and lets the demolitions expert figure out the rest of the story. "No pets on the Templar. We got civilians, ain't that enough entertainment for ya?"

Eckhart looks to Kelnas. "What would you need for an intel mission to that cesspool city moon, and you think you could find out enough for us to initiate a surgical strike? You ain't a marine, but you've got experiences my folks don't, and most of us would stick out like a womp rat in a bacta tank on Nar Shaddaa. I'm sure Cort and maybe a couple others would be up for such a run."

Looking to Druze, Eckhart nods. "There are ways, but it calls for a scalpel, not a vibro-ax."

Scratching his chin, Kelnas considers the request for a moment. "Well...theres a couple ways to go about it. 1) I borrow a ship, take a tracker with me and go get myself taken prisoner then you come and reacue me...otherwise yeah, a couple of us could head down Nar shadaa way and go have a look around. I'd need to bring Druze with me though."

"What happens if we actually do locate these people and find out that there's a lot more there than what this group of pirates was able to bring in, head count wise?" Siarra wonders in a pensive tone of voice, one hand curled round the disposable cup as she lifts it to empty it. She rises to her feet long enough to fetch a refill and come back, sitting down again and crossing one ankle over the other. She tips her head slightly up and back, nodding to Eckhart, "You mean to do this quietly, not in a manner that will have every Hutt swarming out after us." The way she says this isn't a question but a statement of fact. She looks then from Kelnas to Druze and back, "None of the rest of us would be at all useful?"

Druze *facepalms.* "No, you are not allowed to be bait. None of us are. If we do this, we've got to go in with a very clear plan or we won't all make it back."

The colonel nods to Siarra. "We go in, rescue as many as we can, and we leave no enemies alive who can identify us," he says coldly. Then, he's looking back at Kelnas. "That's the dumbest suggestion I've ever heard. No, we're not going to shove a tracker up your ass. I know you did time, but that's not how we do things here. We don't take those kinds of risks anyway."

The colonel sips at his caff and takes another drag from his cigar, an ash falling to the floor only to be quickly vacuumed up by a droid that seems to have that as its only job when the Colonel is on deck.

"First step of any mission is to gather intelligence. The more we get, the better our plan. Second step, we plan. Third step, we implement the plan. Fourth, the plan doesn't survive first contact with the enemy, because they never do, but we're geared for a stealth op already so we're left with that as our best option and we roll with it. At least, we hope we know where we're going at that point."

Shaking his head, Kelnas raises his hands. "Listen. Even I was less crazy about trying to get kidnapped with a tracker in my prison wallet than the rest of you. Body packing anything is not fun and hould only be undertaken with the utmost of care and deliberation."

Grinning at Siarra's question, he adds "Oh yeah. Would need to be a small team, but I didn't mean that ~just~ Druze would come with me, I just need Druze along. I think he might be my spirit animal." Winking at the engineer, he adds. "But in all seriousness, Druze has experience in the underworld. I'm good, but I know for a fact that I have heard him swear about my smoking around the medical tent in huttese in the past. Besides, if things go wrong, I'm pretty sure I can outrun him."

Druze pours himself another cup of caff. "You know over half my brain is in front of my mouth and you've managed to give me a tentacle-ache." He pulls out a holo-recorder stick, taking notes: "... remember Kelnas speaks Huttese. Start cursing him out in a more obscure language. Maybe learn Jawa."

Siarra is quiet while Druze, then Eckhart and Kelnas start to discuss the finer points of what is and is not a good idea. "I speak several languages but no, Huttese isn't one of them," she admits with clear regret. "Alright. so. Intel first. Small team. We're going to need to lay out something in the way of funds and gear and attire that's in line with what's being worn on planet," she points this out in that reasonable tone of voice. "Kelnas, is there any reason you can't set foot on Nar Shaddaa, and by that I mean the risk of you running into anyone you know from your previous occupations that would get bothersome if they spot you?"

Corporal Sernz raises a hand to get the colonel's attention. "Very well. No identifying gear, but we'll scrape the serials off a few blasters for ya," he says. The felinoid pulls a nasty, curved bladed dagger from a hidden sheath and spins it a few times before vanishing it in a quick motion.

"Alright, got another for the team," he says. "I'm not fit, my face is on every wanted poster in the core these days. My presence is going to have to be when we do the actual strike. The Guppy will have to hang back as well. We'll find you a ship to use."

"It's Nar Shaddaa, if someone spots him, and causes a scene, he either owes them money or worse, either way, eliminate the target quietly, and move on. Efficiency is the key here."

Grinning at Druze, Kelnas leans forward slightly. "It's ok buddy. It's gonna be fun." turning his attention to Siarra, the Wroonian shakes his head. "I was never the kind to keep enemies. Never did a bad deal with the Hutts. I'm good to go on Nar Shaddaa." His attention trails off for a moment as Kelnas watches Sernz, then he shakes his head slightly and he looks to Eckhart. "Quick and discrete Sir, No worries."

"We're professionals," Siarra points this out, "we never make a fuss. That's what draws attention at the wrong time. Fuss, muss, hysteria, someone running from the room like a ninny. Civilian style," then she exhales a huff of a laugh at Druze. "Tentatcle-ache," a brief flash of a grin even as the colonel's words make her nod, again, "Got it. And we don't leave people behind," there's a grim sort of determination in her words. "So, no issues that you're aware of on Nar. We'll gear up and head to Nar Shaddaa to follow that trail before it goes cold. Boss, can this ship even get us to Nar Shaddaa or should we take alternate transportation?"

"I'm pretty sure she could get us there, but once there she'd draw a lot of eyes. All those NR markings and news articles about her. Same with Hal's shuttle. Might be better to let Tiaryn deliver you with some legitimate cargo, schedule a time to pick you up to coincide with another run unless she feels the need to stay on planet or at least in system. The Guppy will of course be close by, just in case."

"Pregnant... Pregnant Guppy," Siarra helpfully clarifies the nickname of the Templar. "Do you want to talk her into it, or should I?" she asks, one pale eyebrow arching upward as she shares a look with the Colonel.

"Hell, might be easier if the little greasemonkey does it," the Colonel says, gesturing towards Druze. "Greasemonkey, doc, master gunnery officer, the Quarren of many talents. Master Druze is Tiaryn's is her second I believe, though the big rock might be second, I just don't understand much it says most of the time." The colonel takes a long drag from his cigar, and chases it with a shot of hot caff. "Regardless, we'll figure out something. I'm not going to let anyone go there without backup of some kind."

"Druze, the crew liaison and diplomat, Quarren of many talents," Siarra adds another series of titles to Druze's name even as she leans back and props the heel of one boot against the floor. "Alright. I'll work the angle of gear, attire and credits. I want everyone equipped with a data unit as well, so I'll set those up. We'll need to move on this within the next couple of days or the trail may go cold. Last thing I want is for us to get the right place and have everyone already moved somewhere else that we won't be as able to track."

Nodding as the plan seems to be coming together, Kelnas sighs inwardly. Plans were never his thing. it was like the Colonel said, Plans never survive contact with the enemy...or with anybody that's not in on the plan really. Listening to the titles for Druze get thrown around, he grins once more. the surprisingly white teeth shining in the Cargo bay lighting. Whatever titles he may have been about to add to the mix get left out for now.

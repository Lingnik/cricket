Landing Pad 91 - Cloud City

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ships wishing to land on the floating, disc-shaped "city in the sky" are handled by Bespin Security (Wing) Guard from the Security Tower. Bespin Motors Storm IV twin-pod cloud cars are dispatched to guide space traffic through the proper landing lanes around the floating metropolis. Most landing traffic is directed to the public landing pads scattered on the outskirts near the median levels of the city.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The public landing pads are alive with activity in a section of the City known as Port Town. This area is a large, open air platform with ample room to accept everything from fighters and small craft to capital class cruisers. Each holds a few ships which could be performing any number of tasks. A wide variety of all races, especially droids, do the bulk of the work out here. This mix is reflected throughout the city.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The city beyond reaches further up into the sky in a combination of towers and spires. It also encompasses the massive structure below. Occasional gusts of wind blow in a easterly direction as a reminder that the environment isn't as friendly as the pictures of the beautiful sunsets, and puffy orange clouds would indicate.<br>

   
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Skimming across the flight line after disembarking the /Silent Star/, Quinne Stardust Rose scans the ships noting to herself the increase in traffic. Behind her trots a small humanoid with large goggles on, their short legs having trouble keeping up with the boisterous Zeltron Adminstrator of Cloud City. "Miss Quinne, the decorators are asking that you stop at the Port Authority windows to clarify something."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Oh?" Quinne says cheerily, slowing her steps. "That's inconvenient...but we'll make it work!"<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze isn't presently dressed to be elbows-deep in a starship's engine, but that doesn't prevent him from watching the crews at work and taking notes on his datapad. Occasionally he mutters into the lavalier-comm clipped to his lapel? "Subject 'Void Blur' receiving what appears to be a standard braking-and-transmission check. Cross-index Sardakh Industries designs with the hypothesized upgrades to heavy water anti-lock stopping systems." In a long coat and a miter, perhaps he's putting his 'professional surgeon foot' forward? He sips caff in a half-outdoor restaurant that serves the landing-pad...<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Has no one heard of a safety railing?" Lynae's tone of voice is a sarcastic mutter as a gust of window blowing in that easterly direction tugs at her hair and makes her unfold the sleeves of her shirt to button around her wrists again. "Civilians," making an insult of this single word as she tucks her hands into the pockets of her vest and stares out at the traffic that vectors around the public landing pads.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Fresh off what he considered a reasonably successful trade mission to Corellia, a certain Caspian politician disembarks from his personal shuttle. The shuttle, at least from the outside is not particularly notable. A lambda class like countless others that ferry around the galactic elite and troops alike. The man that walks out himself a bit more descript in crimson fashions of Kubazian and Chandrilan origin. Crestian is accompanied by a Trandoshan in a black uniform, to whom he remarks, "It feels like too long since I have been out this way."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze's brows jump at Quinne's quip. "-- did you just blame non-pundits for literal 'hot air,' Administrator? Now I'm disappointed I didn't have a camera with me..." He raises a finned hand at Crestian's approach. "Presav, can I interest you in a dose of caff?" He indicates the thimble-sized glasses in which it's served?<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;On her way to the issue in question, Quinne hears a gurglily voice Quarren address her and she furrows a cobalt brow. "Find out what that person needs," she directs her assistant who ignores her and keeps following his boss.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Reaching the customs' window Quinne tilts her head upwards to where two average looking humans in brown coveralls are hanging a banner. "It's crooked!" she bemoans, as if this were the worst part of her day. "Take that side up...no, too far...now down a smidge more...No! That's too much." She makes a disgusted noise in the back of her golden throat and then next thing everyone knows, she's at the bottom of a ladder. "Get down. I'll do it myself!"<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"But Miss Quinne!" he objects. "You can't! What if you..."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Next thing the worker is down and Quinne is climbing up with more confidence than she should have. While everyone know dancers have grace, they also are the worst when it comes to having accidents. Then that gust of wind comes along and builds momentum under the partial cover above the Port Authority windows. Quinne's ladder starts to sway as she's reaching for the banner to make her adjustment. In a split second, Quinne is falling with a scream!<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lynae wasn't particular paying attention to the banner hanging, or the minions doing the banner hanging. The gust of wind that tugs at her hair and makes her mutter about the lack of adequate safety measures to keep people from falling off the edge of landing platforms - bounce off of the support structure - then scream all the way down until going splat against something - is distracting enough. Somewhere in that grim rumination is a genuine scream and it snaps her attention back to what is actually happening, and it sends Lynae off toward the wobbly ladder and the falling individual at a dead sprint.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"That sounds lovely doctor," remarks Crestian at the sight and sound of an now familiar Quarren. As he begins to motion Drorq towards the cafe, the sound of the screen draws the attention of the Sarian and his Trandoshan cohort. Both bolt in the direction in question though the Trandoshan closes the distance with greater ease. Spotting having moved in that direction as well Crestian remarks, "Well at least it seems the doctor to patient ratio is better around this spaceport."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze follows hurriedly, although any remarks about athleticism and grace are shameless, imaginary self-promotion!<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Of course, no one is near enough to heroically save Miss Quinne from hitting the deck. She does manage to twist herself as if she could stop herself from hitting the plascrete. Instead she just manages to land on her left arm, an arm that makes a distinct snapping sound as some bone or bones break underneath her. The workers and her assistant gather around their boss as she whimpers and cries, "Ahhhhh! No no no no OW!!"<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lynae's expression is one of recognition, not sympathy, as she moves through the gathering crowd around the fallen individual. "I'm a doctor," she self-identifies as she takes the first aid kit from her side, "allow me to assist." She takes a quick head count of the number of people already gathering around as she takes a knee beside Zeltron at the center of the crowd.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze stumbles forth, stuttering: "Make way, I'm a do--" How embarrassing! But, truly, not a terrible problem to have. A boo-boo at a veritable confluence of qualified professional medical experts! He slows his advance, perhaps circling back for that caff. "It looks like it's being handled, hnh?"<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As Lynae becomes the first medical professional on scene Crestian and Drorq slow their pace to the scene. Crestian knew a more seasoned medical professional was on the scene, and Drorq was far more the type to rip off limbs than do anything about putting them back together. As Druze likewise slow his approach, Crestian remarks to him, "Aye I think Doctor Cassius can handle it." If only she had been around during the Sariphage Crestian finds himself musing to himself, but then purposely engineered viruses are a hard nut to crack. With the immediate medical problem being dealt with, it seems crowd control is more his speed, "If everyone that does not need to be here can get back to what they were doing and give Doctor Cassius and the Baroness-Administrator some room. And whoever is in charge of her schedule should clear her appointments for the day."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Oh thank you! Thank you ma'am!" Quinne's small assistant effuses. "Are you in pain, Miss Quinne?"<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Through gritted teeth as she tries to roll off of her busted limb replies to him, "What do you think!?! Owwww!" She looks up at the woman who has knelt by her side and her tone, though tinged with pain, changes. "I just don't know how I could've been so clumsy!" Violet eyes bat their lashes, pheromones oozing from her bruised and torn pink flesh as her system works its magic to sooth the Zeltron's own mind. "I put myself entirely in your hands, Doctor...Cassius?" she intones as she hears the Sarian name her savior. "Oh Presav!" she cries to Crestian. "This was NOT how I meant to welcome you to Bespin!"<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I'll...I-I-I'll see to it," the little brown being assures Crestian, his large goggles steaming up a little with his nerves as he begins frantically tapping on his pad. "Don't worry Miss Quinne. I'll take care of things for you!"<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lynae takes the scanner out of her kit, letting technology confirm what her eyes and experience have already observed. "Accidents happen," her tone of voice is calm while still being somewhat detached, a glance up as she notes at least one familiar face in the crowd - Crestian, that is - "is there a decent medical facility within walking distance or do we need to use my ship?" She turns her attention back to the Zeltron, "Baroness administrator?" she makes those two words into a question followed by, "any personal, cultural, psychological or other as yet unlisted objected to mild pain management medication before I treat your injuries?"<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze fires back: "None: but you're very kind to ask?" With a beckon he is served a shot of brown liquor, and *SCHLUMP* it's quickly chased with the cold shot of caff. He produces and swallows a pair of aspirin.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Oh no, honey! Give me the good stuff," Quinne answers enthusiastically, wishing she had a spice cig even as she nods to the doctor.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Oh! Well...our medical facility is on level 6 and then a quarter length around the station's outer corridor....er..." The assistant looks disappointed in himself and hangs his head. "I suppose it is quite a ways...I'm sorry madam...er...Doctor."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As one of the beings in the bunch seems to be administrative assistant who responded to his direction about meetings, Crestian adds to them, "If there are any matters you cannot handle in her absence you can direct them to me." Cresian assumed that they should be to handle whatever, but one could never be too sure,. Looking back to Lynae and Quinne, "Your ship may have the utility of being more quiet at this moment. I can only imagine bringing in Miss Quinne when the tourist traffic is really high would prove especially hectic for the local facilities." He adopts the more personal tone to refer to Quinne, as Baroness-Administrator gets wordy even the first time you say it. He takes a moment to smirk back at Druze's comment before looking back to the Trandoshan he is with, "Drorq we don't you give Quinne a hand up, make sure she doesn't fall again on the way to the ship."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze says, "Surely the city's budget can absorb a single visit from a private physician for a bone-setting? It's almost trivial..."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Sensible," which - coming from Lynae - is a serious compliment. She presses a preloaded autoinjector against the side of the Zeltron's already injured arm, administering a humanoid neutral pain management agent and watches the display on the scanner until the medication starts to kick on. The conversation that is happening over her head is only mildly attended to, attention wise. "Of course my ship is quieter, and by far, location wise, much more convenient," she interjects once there's a lull and eases to her feet, eyeing the summarily deputized Trandoshan (the one that Crestian orders around) for a speculative moment. "Alright, lets get her on her feet and then over to my ship. Nothing about a broken arm requires gallivanting around this station in want of a tour guide, some hunting hounds, a trail of candy and a 'You are here' sign."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze's tentacles sink. It's possible he was looking forward to candy.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The station is quite profitable. The legitimate casinos, hotels, and entertainment halls have attracted plenty of tourists to fill the coffers. There was also the not so legitimate ventures that went on with Miss Quinne's approval and her requisite cut. Then, of course, there's the tibanna gas trade. Bespin would of course be able to cover Miss Quinne's medical bills.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Oh, thank you Captain!" Quinne says as she allows the Trandoshan to ease her to her to her feet. She's moving a little slowly. She turns to the Doctor with a huge smile and says, "Candy? I -love- candy!"<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cloud City's defacto administrator nods to Crestian as his nerves calm a little. "Of course, sir! You were so very helpful after that horrid bombing and raid." The little guy shudders visibly.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian nods with appreciation to the defacto administrator before turning back to the others. "Candy?" Crestian questions perhaps not having caught the entire conversation. He pulls out a comlink and calls to his ship, "Veruca, its Crest. I assume you have some stock from your store with you on the Jumper?" The voice of a teenage girl on the other end responds with some puzzlement, "Uh....yes?" Crestian then replies back, "When you get a chance could you make a trip down, we need a care package for Miss Quinne, she suffered a workplace injury." He looks to the others present, "Will the medical team be in need of some candy as well?"<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze stumbles a bit, but Crestian's Trandoshan attendant fortunately has those freakishly-long arms to keep 'things' stable. Things like Baronness Administrators. If he uses anything apart from brawn, it's basic triage care in getting the patient safely to a treatment area without further damage. Which is managed!<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lynae packs her medical kit with practiced efficiency, keeping a careful eye on the patient and her helpful escorts that are lending a hand (hands, tentacles, etc) to start moving her in the right direction. She eases around the wounded support team and leads the way to her ship, keying in the access code and waiting for the hatch to cycle and the ramp to extend before she invites everyone into the Sardakh Kale-1 that has been heavily retrofitted to serve as more than just a rough edged cargo shuttle. Or what ever it was supposed to be, when it initially rolled off of the factory line.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A wide corridor runs the length of this Sardakh Kale-1 which has been renovated to make the most of the available space that would normally be allocated to cargo, a door on either side of the corridor leading to the areas beyond. The starboard portion of the ship is outfitted as a medbay and laboratory with the portside portion of the ship serves as a combination of living space and mess. "Crestian, do you really mean you're ordering candy?" she wonders as she gestures toward the medical bay so that the Trandoshan and the Quarren (none of whom she has been introduced to) help the wounded Baron-Administrator into. Toward? Directional movement.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Quinne vacillates between flirting with her brawny conveyors and giggling at her own unspoken jokes. At one point, with her good arm's hand, she tickles Druze's tentacles like they're a beard. "I like your hat!" she declares as they reach the ramp. Her head snaps dramatically toward's Drorq. "We really should stop meeting like this," she says deadpan serious to the Trandoshan. "I wouldn't want you to get in trouble with Mrs. Captain!"<br>
   

Medbay - Red Sky

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The starboard portion of the cargo hold is outfitted as a medbay, the medical droid stationed in one of the corners. The medical beds are enclosed in individual curtained areas, each one set up with monitoring equipment that is often found in much larger ships, but no expense has been spared, only the most accurate of equipment is utilized. Each curtain is made of a transparent sheet of material that is drawn around on a track, sealing the bed off from the rest of the room, if need be. The medical treatment section of the medbay is takes up nearly half the length of the space from midship forward, leaving the rest of the space to be utilized for research and quarantine for the work that the ships doctor also conducts. A sealed room is reached through a air tight door, a small chamber for decontamination, followed by another door leading into the laboratory. This lab, also, is outfitted with the peak of laboratory equipment, a stainless steal table that runs in a u-shape around the room. A cryo-chamber is rigged as a sub-zero holding unit along with shelves that are fronted by transparisteel hinged doors to allow for ease of access to the contents shelved therein.<br>


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While a lesser officer may have glared at the Quarren's poor stablisation skills, Fleet Captain Drorq politely ignores Druze's poor lifting skills....probably more occupied by the flirtations of the Zeltron patient. Meanwhile Crestian looks back to Lynae as she questions about the candy, muting the comlink for a moment, "Zubindi's ward Veruca owns a very popular candy store in Trinumvira down the street from my villa. We travelled with them from Corellia to here. It did sound like there was a sudden need of the spirit lifting effects of candy yes?" Drorq chimes in, "I would be down for some candy."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Non-species specific humanoid neutral pain management medication," Lynae replies and rattles off the name of both the manufacturer and control lot number in reply to the question from the Quarren. "I've been modifying the formula for the last few years and it's finally in full production and available to purchase for use in official medical facilities. I'm angling to get it included in the standard load out for first aid kits," said as she also steps into the medbay and takes a moment to clean her hands. "I know Crestian, of course, but the names of everyone else, that's the part of this equation I'm missing. That said, Crestian knows who I am, but the rest, so," she angles her head slightly in a formal nod. "I'm Doctor Lynae Cassius, captain of this ship, the Red Sky."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze nods. "Dr. Blulurbglolorb Druze, resident physician and chief engineer on _Bright Star,_" he offers, also washing his hands as appropriate. "... I guess I've been out in the boonies too long. Didn't realize analgesics had gotten so much better."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze might be diplomatically pointing out the Baroness-Administrator is, in the original Greek: "high as balls."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I'm Quinne! I'm from Zeltron and I love puppies!" the baroness by job title declares. "Sound off! One! Two! Three! Four!" she continues with a sing-song impromptu melody. "It's so clean in here!" she observes, seemingly astounded.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Quinne's system, however, tends to run things through swiftly, especially when her anti-stress pheromones are coursing through and out of her body. By the time the rosy pink woman is situated in one of the medical beds she starts to feel a bit cranky and whines, "Oooowwwww! I think I need another hit, Doc!"<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Unmuting his comlink Crestian says, "Um I guess just a care package for Miss Quinne. And...." He looks around the room, "....I guess three samplers. You can charge it to me tab. No rush though, Doctor Cassius is getting to play how to dose a Zeltron with painkillers, may be a bumpy ride." The Presav puts away his comlink, and realises the that Drorq has not introduce himself. "My mostly quiet friend over there is Fleet Captain Drorq of the Dying Sun." Turning his mind back to medical matters, "I didn't realise you were doing analgesic research. I would be most interested in comparing notes at a more opportune time. It is one of the areas of focus at my research hospital. If it is as good as you say I can gather together the resources to help manufacture it on Caspar should you so desire."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Nice to meet you, Dr. Druze, and welcome aboard the Red Sky," Lynae's professional courtesy is always top shelf, even as Quinne starts to sound cranky. "Interesting. That should have lasted longer, I may need to tailor the formula to counteract the unique way your body is metabolizing the analgesic," there's a fine thread of speculation in her tone of voice as she reaches for another preloaded autoinjector and calmly doses Quinne again. "If you'll give that another moment to kick in, your shoulder is dislocated and you broke the upper bones of your arm, I'll set both and start the bone knitting rounds to speed along the healing time." Her eyes lift toward Crestian and she nods, "That's not a bad idea at all. We'll need to exchange notes and see how close our ideas are already."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Quinne nods, but pouts, her whining at an end as she quiets down. It's not long before she feels woozy again. In fact, it's possible she really didn't need another dose at all and was just subconsciously chasing the high. She may have a very VERY responsible position here in Cloud City, but that didn't mean the Zeltron didn't like to party. It isn't long before she passes out and sleeps in a semi-artificial dream land. Let the doctors do what they need to do. Quinne is feeling no pain.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Turning attention back to the patient, "The second liver does provide some dosing complications with Zeltron." The Sarian pulls out one of his ubiquitous datapads and begins making some calculations of his own. "I imagine your own efforts could quite easily exceed our own back at Twin Suns. I was wholly unimpressed by the Caspian medical community during the pandemic. And even at that since so many of them died off, I just kind of tried to start up some stuff from scratch with a new facility. The main focus of my analgesic research teams tend towards the applications of various spices and other drugs that tend to be maligned medically for their other effects. 'Slick 'from Vaynai has sort of been the one I have had the particular interest in as it is lesser known, so less chance of duplicating the research efforts of others." He looks at the now passed out patient, "But a conversation for another day. I should probably head along and get out of your way."

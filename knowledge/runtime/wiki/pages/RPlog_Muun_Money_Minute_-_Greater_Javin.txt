Yarith Hotel Lobby -- Cloud City

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Yarith is the most high-class hotel in all of Cloud City. Frequented by dignitaries and celebrities, and renowned for its amenities, there are no other hotels in the galaxy like it. Everything here is beautifully crafted and designed.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;From the classic waterfall curtains, lush sapphire and gold carpet to the expansive blue-black tinted windows revealing the cityscape outside, the Yarith is unparalleled in luxury travel accommodations. Busy at all hours, the lobby hosts a number of posh services and small shops.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Glasteel turbolifts run up and down in banks of three along the walls, speeding guests to their five star apartment suites. A grand dining hall and ballroom, formal attire only, can be seen to the north, while the famous Pair O'Dice casino can be entered to the east.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With her left arm is a purple fabric sling to match her dress, Quinne Stardust Rose is set up in a quiet corner going over things on her datapad which is balanced on the arm of her plush chair so she can tap on it with her right hand. Amazingly, no one is buzzing around her to receive orders from the Baroness Administrator in the quiet morning hours on Cloud City. She sips a caff between entries, juggling pad and cup rather adeptly.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A small news team come into the Yarith Bespin. And by small, just a cameraman and a reporter. The reporter would be recognisable in much of the galaxy as IGN anchor and economics analyst Professor Brackett Benduday. The cameraman goes to the desk in search of information on where their interview subject is.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze's path past the breakfast buffet finds him loaded down with treats! Bloater! Buckling! Kipper! Abroath smokies! Herring salad! Dressed herring! Herring under a fur coat! Tuna! Caviar, roe, fish eggs, fish milt, he's got it all! He balances his tray with a minimum of 'woah!' and '--oh, oh, nope.' noises as he goes.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The desk clerk is Pantoran. Quinne likes colorful people like herself as her evidence by the collection of species employed throughout Cloud City. "May I help you?" the clerk, whose name tag reads Danika, asks as the cameraman approaches her station. Upon inquiry, she asks, "Do you have an appointment with Miss Quinne?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The camera man points to the nearby reporter golden-skinned reporter, "We are from InterGalactic News for a live Muun Money Minute report on the local economy. Miss Quinne should be expecting us."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze's boots are new! The height of fashion! Big, chunky soles are at the tip of everyone's wishlist, and there's *nothing hipper than--* no no no NOOO-OOO, aw, beans! The poor Quarren was distracted by his mountain of plates and dishes on a plastic tray, and lost track of where his feet were. In a massive clatter of crockery and smoked seafood, he's down. "Medic!" he cries, helplessly, as chaos unfolds!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Quinne looks up from her datapad and sets it aside. She raises her right hand and waves. "Yoo hoo!! Come on over!" she hollers from where she is. She quickly checks her lip stick and presses the skirt of her dress smooth, positioning herself at her best angle while she awaits Brackett's approach.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Meanwhile the staff person attending the buffet, a yellow-skinned Twi'lek, notices the Quarren juggling various seafood dishes, witnesses the fall and spill and rushes to the Druze's side. On the way he calls for clean-up help. When he reaches Druze he asks with concern as he kneels down by the fallen guest. "Oh dear! Are you hurt sir?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The stylishly dressed Firrerreo Brackett, and the more sedately but professional dress camera man, really a producer filling in the role, a Human named Gym, make their way towards Quinne. Gym takes a single but sustained glance towards the hapless Quarren, while Brackett takes several quick glances, trying to not appear interested in the matter while secretly actually interested in the proverbial wreck that just happened. As the two approach, Gym remarks, "I guess it is a good thing that the Javin medical conference is moving here, sounds likes someone needs some attention." Though he is referring to Druze, it is only after the comment that he notices Quinne's sling.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze reports, voice cracking: "Just my pride, I think. And a generation of delicacies." He paws at himself with a napkin, morose. "... yourself? Have I sprayed you with savory undersea flavors?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Quinne raises a cobalt brow at the producer as her dusky maroon lips curl in a little smirk. "There does seem to be a curse of clumsiness around here the last couple of days." Then she smiles, all charm, and swings her violet gaze over to the reporter, extending her right hand as she comes to her feet. "I'm very please to meet you in person Ms. Brackett. I've become quite a fan of your segments. I find them -very- educational!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The catering staffer shakes his head, yellow lekku swinging slightly in the morning light streaming through the sky lights. "Oh, no sir! I wasn't in the splash zone," he jokes a little trying to put the patron at ease. "And there's plenty more where all this..." He gestures to the carpet now sopping with fishy sauces. Two maintenance droids arrive and begin scooping up the lost delicacies. "...came from. Perhaps you might try just a few at a time?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze admits, begrudgingly: "You may be correct. I suppose I won't know how mortified to be until it comes over the HoloNet. 'Gravity: 1, Engineer: 0.' Amirite?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Why thank you Miss Quinne. It can be a challenge sometimes to remind our sponsors that a news broadcast is meant to be informative not sexy." Fortunately and unfortunately for Brackett she was quite able to do both at once. And now to be informative. Wait she should say something nice about Quinne, she was a singer right? Brackett was not very good at small talk, or socialising in general. "I have heard very good things about the job you have been doing here. All the financial reports indicate profits have exceed expectations." There the compliment was in.
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gym smirks at Brackett, internally chastising her for being such a geek. Turning his attention to Quinne he says, "Do you want me to set up for it here or would you prefer a different location...." He looks back towards Brackett and then in the direction of Druze and the buffet table.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Oh this should be fine..." She follows the cameraman's eyeline to the Quarren who helped her the other day. "Oh my...poor Doctor Druze! Well...we got the big kerfuffle noise out of the way now, didn't we?" Besides, she'd selected this spot in front of some teal curtains to contrast nicely with her features and outfit. She turns back to the reporter. "Bespin is very happy with all of our successes of late." She looks between the two. "Have we already started the interview?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"An engineer?" the Twi'lek asks with admiration and offering the Quarren a hand up. "Very impressive, sir. I never had a good head for numbers. And I don't think the cameras were rolling. The Yarith is very discreet. You won't find security footage on IGN."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze shrugs, looking in vain for somewhere to put the pile of napkins he's generating. Perhaps one of the roving maintenance droids can-- "Yes, hello! Baroness Administrator, do pardon the--" he checks "port-and-anchovy reduction meant for precision-seared scallops currently decorating my chest. I think I lost a boot-heel." He checks the surrounding rug for errant high heels...

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Brackett recomposes herself and returns her focus to the segment, "No. This what we call the pre-interview. So basically once we get all set up the cameras will roll, I will introduce the segment, and myself, and you. I will say a little bit about the attack on the refinery two years ago. Then I will ask about how you have managed in your role here as Baroness-Administrator, rebuilding the refinery, basically presenting you the opportunity to explain you success. This a financial report so there is not going to be any question trying to trip you up on something we are just looking to provide an accurate picture for our viewers on have you managed to succeed in spite of the setbacks. Depending on how you answer I may ask a short follow up. Then we will end with a little work about the Greater Javin Medical Society conference moving their annual conference here because of the pirates on the Nevarro Spur." She looks back at the Quarren, now that Quinne has referred to him as Doctor Druze, asking in a low voice, "Is he here for the conference?" 

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Quinne nods along with the plan as Brackett lays it out. She looks over to Druze and gives a little wave as her staff assists him and oversees the clean up. Purple fringe swings as she shrugs her good shoulder. "I...I don't think so. I...I really can't say." She focuses again on the reporter and drawls. "Well, I'm ready when you are, darlin', so let's roll!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Druze shakes the Twi'lek's hand, purloining a flyer from his pocket. "Greater Javin Medical Society. Mmhm. Hm. Mm. Hmhm." He shakes his head, stalking for-- it says here Conference Room B1! On Level 77! He has *purpose!* He has... a destiny! It is, hopefully, ~not to end up with his ass in the scrimp cocktail platters while he argues minutiae with the eggheads!~ OFF HE GOES!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the klutzy Quarren safely out of the scene. Gym sets up the camera, with Brackett doing a quick lighting and sound check. When they are both satisfied that right conditions are set he gives her the thumbs up and she moves into position beside Quinne.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The screen shows the lobby of the Yarith Bespin, the most-class hotel in Cloud City. The logo for InterGalactic News and the Muun Money Minute financial programme feature prominently in the middle of the screen before shifting to smaller versions at the bottom. In their place two fashionably dressed figures can be clearly seen now a golden-skinned woman with a microphone and a rosy-skinned woman with her arm in a sling.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;After a moment the golden-skinned woman speaks, "This is the Muun Money Minute coming to you live from the Yarith Bespin for a segment on the Greater Javin region sponsored by our friends at Biscuit Baron. I am your host Professor Brackett Benduday and with me is Cloud City Baroness-Administrator Quinne Stardust Rose."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Quinne is a consummate professional when it comes to public appearances and she appears very much at ease in front of the camer. Her focus switches from the camera to the Professor and back again until she is introduces. She gives a little wave and looks cutely at the camera, wiggling her right-hand fingers in an adorable wave as she says, "Hi y'all!" The rosy woman smiles widely and looks back at the reporter. "Thanks for having me on, Professor Brackett."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"It is a pleasure to have you with us Miss Quinne." The anchor and economist use the less formal appellation, matching her guest. "Not far from us in the ballroom of this very hotel during the first annual Bespin Miner's Dinner terrorists attack the gathering, along with coordinated attacks including destroying the city's main tibanna refinery and grievously wounding then city leader Johanna Siri te Danaan. In the immediate aftermath of the attack Miss Quinne, the manager of this hotel and a performer of renown was thrust into the position of acting Baroness-Administrator. Here we are two years later, the promotion permanent, the refinery rebuilt, and perhaps most surprising, in spite of limited conflict in the galaxy in these past two years, tibanna prices continue to climb."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Brackett then turns from looking at the camera to looking at her guest, "An attack such as the one Cloud City suffered easily had the potential to cripple the economy here, yet in spite of it all Cloud City is managing to thrive. I think what our viewers would like to know is how you have been able to oversee such an economic miracle? As far as I understand it you had never been such an extensive administrative position before this?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"That was a terrible thing that happened, very tragic." Quinne replies with a serious expression. "But Johanna was very supportive of our miners and their families, even after her injury. It was important to the community to get the refinery up and running as soon as possible as so many livelihoods in Cloud City depend on the industry here. And with growth here in the city along with some strategic investing, not to mention the rise in demand for our product, we managed to pull out of the red real quick-like." Quinne smiles a bit, demuring with a little dip of her chin. "It's all been quite a change for me, ma'am. But I had a lot of help. Besides Joh, I've had several good mentors - like Crestian Tarasar, for example. I mean, he runs an entire system so he was a really, really big help. And really, it's just a bigger show here compare to what I was doing before. All the same parts needing to come together to make things work, only sized up!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As if on cue, the next patron to enter the hotel lobby is none other than said same, Crestian Tarasar. Though this is fact is not noticed by the news team, who are fully immersed by the interview segment. Not wishing to interrupt it either Crestian heads towards the buffet table, but starts to back away when he sees it is in the process of being cleaned up after some mishap.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Brackett had not intended to bring up Crestian Tarasar herself, but since Quinne had it seemed like a natural segue point. "You mentioned Caspian Union Presav Crestian Tarasar. Who leads a power that, if I might make a quick correction, includes several inhabited systems. IGN reported two years ago that after the attack the Caspian dispatched engineers from SubPro to assist the miners here in the recovery. Clearly Cloud City and its citizens can count on some very helpful friends. Now we have talked about refinery operations, but what about Cloud City itself. The Greater Javin Region is on the Outer Rim, and while the Corellian Trade Spine goes through the region, Bespin itself is not on it. Yet, Cloud City manages to not only have a very successful tibanna operations but is also known as something of a tourist hot spot. What do you think brings people to Cloud City in spite of its remote location?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now we're in Quinne's wheelhouse. Promotion! Her blue curls bounce with her excited head bobs as she speaks, her one good arm doing its best to 'talk' along with her on its own being down its partner as she smiles brightly. "Well, ma'am, I like to entertain people. It's always been my calling. So whether you want to see Galaxy-Class entertainment, try your luck at one of our many casinos, or just relax in the Yarith's Day Spa, it's all right here. Also, we have our new Cultural Festival coming up which will double the shopping and dining available. Bespin is for Everyone." Her hand moves across her face, palm forward, like a shooting star. "And I'm so glad we were able to host the Greater Javin Medical Society conference. Your viewers should know that Cloud City is ready and waiting to spice up your conventions and trade shows!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"And that brings us to the last point on this part of the segment. The Greater Javin Medical Society annual conference that had been scheduled for Nevarro has announced a last minute relocation here to Cloud City after recent reports of piracy on the Nevarro Spur has caused a travel warning to be issued for Nevarro itself. Conference organisers have indicated the change will mean some of the scheduled presentations will not be able to occur this year, but are confident of being able to full their programme with some last minute replacements. IGN's own Jordaan Mag will have more on that later, but for now that is all the time we have for the Muun Money Minute. My thanks again to Cloud City Baroness-Administrator Quinne Stardust Rose. This is Brackett Benduday signing off."
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;After a moment Gym gives a single to indicate that the feed is no longer live. Brackett turns back to Quinne saying, "That was perfect thank you for being on the show." With the segment concluded Crestian approaches the trio, commenting, "They really chose a perfect time to move the conference. The city was already swimming in doctors."

Located on the opposite side of the lake from the Xi Can Cathedral, near the south bridge, the Xi Cantina straddles the line between conspicuous and unassuming. On the outside it is a metallic structure that is built in the same shade of grey as many of the surrounding buildings. The base is circular and though it is capped more in a dome shape than the conical tops of the larger buildings it does not look that out of place. On closer inspection though it bears none of the features that would suggest that Xi Charrians are the ones using the building though the entrance is clearly large enough to accommodate them.

Inside, however, it looks typical of virtually any other cantina in the galaxy. Standard u-shaped bar with tables off to the sides. The key difference is what would seem like comically large distance between the furnishings if not for the fact that it does boast some Xi Charrians as patrons. It is, however, catering to a largely humanoid crowd and largely seems to have a cliental that is a mix of those doing work at the factory and visiting business beings.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Xi Cantina had a quirky history that well befit the image of the city it housed. Initially it was the afterwork hangout of Sienar Fleet Systems workers in the years they had control of what remained of Haor Chall Engineering. It would have received complete disdain from the locals had it not been for the fact that Dean ak chose to mingle with outsiders seeking to make the best of the bad situation. Now with local control restored and the Xi Charrians now an integral part of the Caspian Union it serves the offworld population in a much more equal manner now.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As one of the few establishments in the heart of the city specifically catering to the foreign crowd, it has become a hot spot during the trade summit for various business beings and politicians. Chief among whom tonight appears to be Presav Tarasar himself. He is sitting at the bar looking through some datapads while nursing a drink. He appears to be along at the moment with the seats next to him open.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;When Exos walked toward the entrance of the cantina he could feel his eyelids becoming heavy. The walk through the very industrial community, with its almost uniform architecture and atmosphere of long hours and early mornings, had taken a toll on his energy level. This, coupled with several days of touring to celebrate (promote) his most recent Swoop tournament victories, made him wish for a nap.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He quickly collects himself, however, as the ambient sounds of the cantina reach his ears. Exos straightens his jacket - a sharp, navy blue and textured piece - over a crisp white shirt as he walks in. He takes a deep breath and appreciates the sharpness of the night air. His emerald green eyes scan the crowd with as nonchalant an air he can muster before walking toward the bar.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian takes another sip of his drink and briefly looks around the bar, particularly towards the door. One might get the idea he was waiting for someone but he quickly looks back to his stack of datapads. However, he was looking for was seemingly not yet there. Looking at the bartender the Caspian leader remarks, "I was expecting more of a crowd tonight, I can only imagine a certain someone has planned this summit with more depth than I had imagine."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As one of the few establishments in the heart of the city specifically catering to the foreign crowd, it has become a hot spot during the trade summit for various business beings and politicians. Chief among whom tonight appears to be Presav Tarasar himself. He is sitting at the bar looking through some datapads while nursing a drink. He appears to be along at the moment with the seats next to him open.'

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Exos opens his arms and flashes a pearly white smile as he exalts at the sight of a Twi'lek woman at the bar, who yells his name in excitement when she sees him. They hold each others' hands - a remarkably similar color of deep, vibrant red - and talk excitedly for a moment before he approaches the bar.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Exos runs his hand through his messy jetblack hair and leans on the bar as he awaits being served a dark, bubbly liquid. He takes a quick sip, makes a feigned shivering movement to celebrate the potency of the drink with his friend, and leans the bar, seeming to casually take in his surroundings. One might have noticed his double-take at the Caspian leader who also happened to be hanging out at the same bar if one were watching Exos.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He takes another sip of his drink and pretends to care more about casually chatting with his friend.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Well there are only so many bars in this part of the city. The Xi Charrians imbibe in different ways," Crestian remarks vaguely in Exos' direction. Whether it is based on his own recognition of Exos or his recognition of Crestian or simply the ramblings of a tired man is not wholly clear. Turning back to the bartender Crestian taps on his now empty glass which is soon filled with more Ithorian Brandy by the bartender."
	
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Entering the establishment alone, a tall, light-skinned human in his late teens takes a moment for his dark eyes to adjust to the relative dingy atmosphere in the only bar he'd been able to find. Longinus Van Sen, dressed in casual dark pants and a somewhat loudly patterned red shirt stops for a moment to survey the venue. He flexes his shoulders causing the leather of his flight jacket to creak. The jacket is not too worn and sports emblems of the Caspian Vector Apex and the 49th Wildcard Squadron patch on one shoulder. "Is it possible...?" he says to himself. "That I've finally found the least Xi of all Xi places here on this weird-ass world?" He approaches the bar a few seats on the opposite side of Crestian than the other recently entered patron and gives a nod to the server. "One Corellian Ale, my good being please!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In a flash, Exos reasons that Crestian could very well be speaking to him, or he could be wrong and would need to play his next moves accordingly. He turns to the bar with a grin and looks directly ahead at the brightly colored bottles stacked on the shelves and says, "What a fine bar and excellent brandy!" He smiles warmly at the bartender who serves Crestian and nods as he lifts his glass, indicating another round for himself. Exos dares to lift a glass in the Presav's direction as he takes another sip, finishing the short drink as a small glass arrives on the counter top to replace it. Exos downs as much of the bubbly glass he can in one gulp through a pair of thin straws as he hears Crestian speak to his left.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian smirks at Lon's reaction to the bar. It may not have been directed at him, but when has that ever stopped Crestian for commenting. "Well you know ak could arrive any minute....though I am not sure whether or not that would reinforce this being the least Xi place or not. Though I suspect he is too busy with...." Crestian trails off, realising he does not really have a good answer for that.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Whether or not the Zeltron had his attention before, he does now. Crestian raises his own glass to the man. After taking another sip he remarks, "Most cantinas I frequent tend to carry it, and this is the place to come while here. The ambiance is better than own the outskirts of the city or Gods forbid on base."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Oh hey....uh..." Growing up Lon's mother's friend Crestian had gone by his given name. At formal occasions or family gatherings, the young fighter jock knew how to address the Presav. But here? In a bar? This was uncharted territory since the Sarian had become his Commander-in-Chief. "...sssSirrr?" He clasps his hand around the frosty glass the 'tender slides to him and moves a few seats closer. Lon makes a sligtht erffort at a casual pilot's salute - two fingers briefly brushing his own forehead. "Didn't expect to run into you here..." Lon ticks off what he can and can't say next in his mind - Atsvara? No. Biscuit Baron? No. ak? Safe! While eyeing the Zeltron and the Twi'lek he replies to Crestian with, "Well, Dean ak is the least 'Xi' Xi I've met so..."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Exos taps a polished obsidian shoe upon the hard floor of the cantina, nodding his head in natural sync with ambient music as he tries not to appear to be listening to the Presav's conversation with the newcomer.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Oh this place is wonderful," Exos agrees in reply to Crestian as he takes another, shorter sip this time. "Really fresh air and music, I have to say," he said as he gestures to the band. His eyes exude delight in the moment at hand. Exos tilts his head sheepishly as he adds, "And I *do* have to say I appreciate all your work. I guess you could say I'm a fan."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Exos does his best to maintain a casual air as he continues to subtly bob his head along with the music.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"What can I say Lon, I didn't expect to be here myself. A trade summit I had no hand in planning. I am surprised I haven't seen your mother yet, I would have expected they would have secured her presence." The Sarian smiles, happy enough to dance around the metaphorical bantha in the room. "I am curious to see how it all turns out though."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian turns back to Exos, "A fan? Oh gosh you need better role models then I think. But I suppose that at least puts us past the phase where I have to introduce myself. You are looking a tad familiar yourself, but I fear if I have caught your name before I may have forgotten it. Too many long boring meetings can rot the mind a little."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Yeah, mom is just busy. She did send somebody, though." He shrugs, knowing little of Elana Tracer's work essentially running the Imperium she has managed to overhauled to her own standards, with him out living his own life and all. Lon takes the kind of deep gulp from his ale that brash young men favor and then rubs some foam from the corners of his mouth self-consciously. When Crestian looks at the crimson man and lifts his chin upwards in a nod of acknowledgement. "I bet the Xi aren't used to so many tourist, hmm?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Ah," Exos replies with a half grin as he shakes his head in polite disagreement with the well-known statesman. "The Union is on the frontiers of societal discourse, and it's fascinating," he lets his emerald and amber-rimmed eyes flash with excitement as he says the word fascinating, indicating a strong preference for pursuit of such a thing. "Exos Elan," the Zeltron replies as he settles in on a barstool, looking between both Crestian and the young man who just mentioned him. His Twi'lek friend smiles admiringly at her friend as she drifts off into the crowd of patrons, a white flowing dress trailing in the air behind her before disappearing into the shadows of the dancing crowd, leaving him to continue in his conversation.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Here after winning a tournament, and I've come to promote charities my team and I support," he adds with a grin that indicates delight at the task. He sighs with satisfaction and can't seem to help but continue to tap to the rhythmic beats of the ambient sound with a red finger upon his square glass.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian takes another sip of his brandy as his absent-mindedly shuffles and neatens his pile of datapads, whatever he was doing with them before this conversation forgotten. A few words role of his tongue as mutters a bit to himself, "Tournament....team." He looks back to the Zeltron, "So not sabacc, not small enough for pod-racing. I don't think I have caught any mention of bolo-ball lately so you must be a swoop racer then yes? I must have caught daughter watching some races lately in between her various acts of megalomania."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He then looks back to Longinus with a smile, "Tourists? Not so much, though the wargames last year did increase interest in the cathedral-factories, so there is more of a tourist economy here than you might expect. There is actually a fairly sizeable humanoid population on world given the factory output, but not a lot in this part of the city for sure."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lon sits back and nods to Crestian's evaluation of the changes Xi Charrian's factory world had undergone in the last several years. Perhaps his mother stayed away so as not to give any impression of the Imperium taking back the once Imperial-seized factories. Then he calculates the Sarian's deducing of the Zeltron fellow's profession. "You race?! Man...I'd kill to be on the circuit. But the Union keeps me too busy right now."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His nostrils flair as he listens to Crestian accurately analyze his context clues, unsurprised at the astute observation but nonetheless clearly impressed and excited by it. He nods to them both, "Yes! I'm from Bespin." His eyes glisten a bit while he pauses and observes whether or not that fact would get some kind of reaction from Crestian - a man whom Exos and many people from his planet know and admire - and looks back and forth between the two. Exos takes another sip from his glass, and slows his dancing movements a bit as he focuses on the conversation.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Well the circuit and the Union are both highly worthwhile pursuits, I'd say," he says with an impish glance to the very head of the Union sitting to his right. And adds to the young man in front of them, "As a racer, I'd say if you're torn between two turns, you either pick the one that feels right, or you cut a path that suits you better." A little surprised at his own frankness, but satisfied at the accurateness of his swoop-related advice, Exos takes another drink.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I am sure we could get you on the circuit Lon without any homicide required. I mean I am happy so long as you are flying something for the Union." Crestian works hard at resisting the urge to suggest Lon could still kill for it if he wanted. Only so much joking about murder to be done in a mixed crowd.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Turning back to Exos, Crestian remarks, "I know Bespin well. Though as I recall the last time I was there involved someone trying to blow up a regional medical conference....so kind of par for the course. Are you representing a Bespin concern? Team wise that is? I am afraid I have gotten out of touch with the current circuit."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Longinus laughs off Crestians probable joke about homicide. He was, after all and despite his confirmed kill record on various pirate routing runs he'd led his squadron on, a non-murdering type of person. Instead he puts his arm on the Presav's shoulder in a friendly manner. The Sarian was like an uncle to him. "I will take you up on help in that direction when we get back," he assures him.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now addressing the swoop jockey, Lon gets a look of challenge about him. "And then sometimes, you can just take it all in!" Spoken like a true son of privilege. He asks Exos, "What's your ranking? What do you ride?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Exos crinkles his nose and looks up at the ceiling when Lon declares the bit about taking it all in, but decides to respond to the question instead, "Just won the Mutanda Grand Prix and the uhm...less official...stunt match that took place after the tournament." He coughs a bit at the liberal approach he seems to impulsively be taking to divulging information about his participation in the off-the-books, highly dangerous (and highly lucrative) stunt matches, but as an independent citizen competing on an independent planet, he trusts his legal position to be solid.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"So number one in the pre-sponsorship league and riding what my top bidder offers," he says to round out his answer to Lon.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He answers partly in response to both but mostly to Crestian, "My team is currently an unsigned packaged deal, being without full sponsorship, but with the support we have we're raising funds for charities that do vocational outreach to more remote planets who may not have access to some of the education and transportation worlds closer to the main hyperspace lanes might enjoy."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Mutanda Grand Prix?" Crestian looks between Exos and Lon. "Well that must have been what I saw Atsvara watching. It is the homeworld of one of our senior diplomats and board member of my charity. We actually have a pretty sizeable Horansi community on Caspar, particularly in Anchorron. So I am guessing probably a number of folks back home most of been watching that too."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Presav starts unstacking his datapads and shuffling through them. He looks to Longinus, "Well it seems like it is going to be time to get something going at Marin Mountain. I am starting to get some ideas."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lon looks excitedly at Exos and then Crestian, punching the Presav's shoulder as he exclaims, "Blast OFF! Totally wizard, man!" Realizing he practically assaulted a head of state he looks aghast at himself. "Oh! Sorry Crest...I mean, sir, uhh...Presav Tarasar, sir!" He leans across the bar nearly in front of the Sarian and says to Exos, "Not bad, not bad...Oh, man, have you ever run Marin Mountain, dude? Man, my mom designed some kick-ass tracks, man! Like Marin, and uh...the Vratixan...there was one on a space station, even...Theseus, I think?!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Exos can't help but smile wide at the excitement of the young Swoopfan, and he leans in himself, "I got to test drive for a team I trained with years ago. Couldn't go full throttle, of course, being that it wasn't *my* bike, but I'm hoping to get back there soon. Just gotta find the right sponsoring company to sign with and I'll be there, riding *my* way," he leans back a bit and stares off a bit as he remembers fondly, "Gorgeous track."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian chuckles a bit at Lon's display, "We are off duty you can knock off all the sir poodoo. Far too many connexions between our families for that. Besides at I don't need to project power at the bar." He finishes off his Ithorian Brandy realising that he did need to project power at bars in order to have his favourite liquor. But that mission was already accomplished. Turning back to Exos as he looks through something on one of his datapads, "Well there are certainly a number of executives from various concerns on world at the moment. Though how many of them are in the market to sponsor riders or teams I couldn't say. Personally I have tended to funnel my own profits into my charity and some nightclubs."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Okay, Crest. Speaking of our families...I should probably get going. I promised someone I'd meet them in a bit," the young pilot says, his speech winding to a cryptic conclusion as he too tries to navigate the circumstances that had brought him to this world. Lon has managed to finish his pint. He leaves some creds on the bar with his empty glass. So I better get going." The lad gives a jesting little bow and flurry of his hand. "Gentlemen..." He looks over to the Twi'lek woman and some others fresh off the dance floor and whistles with appreciation before bowing to them as well who giggle with the attention of the dashing and cock-sure Lieutenant. "Ladies. I'll see -you- later, maybe."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Exos lifts a sharp, black eyebrow and smiles broadly at Lon's confidence toward the crowd. He lifts his chin in greeting to his friend who makes it back to the bar, smiling back at him, but remaining strategically distracted by other conversation.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Well Swoop races are a fantastic way to promote charities, I've found. I love the sport, love the speed and crisp air in my lungs, but I love what the sport can *do* for people," he shrugs and nods low, not wanting to appear too pushy for what was now likely an obvious goal of his at the moment. "I do thank you for the tip, Presav. I'm gonna keep winning Swoop races, and I hope I can represent the right cause with the right company sponsor."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian waves to Lon as he leaves, "I suspect I will be seeing you soon Lon. We will have to chat some time when the little ladies can spare you." The Sarian taps on his empty class to indicate he wants another, "So tell me Mister Elan, you did mention charity promotion before. What kind of causes catch your eye?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Exos is ready for this one, and continues to sip at modest pace at his glass, "Well like I said, vocational outreach is something I'm interested in. Coming up in the circuit, I always felt grateful to have the support I had along the way, but I also felt like access to resources shouldn't hold people back in racing or anything else. So now I support organizations that find people who might have interest in doing things their planets can't or won't provide them..." he trails off for a moment before looking Crestian in the eye.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I think people should be free to explore what suits them and have the opportunity to pursue it. So should droids..."

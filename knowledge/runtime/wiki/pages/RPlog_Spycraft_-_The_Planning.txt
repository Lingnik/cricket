''Utilizing a back room that is normally set aside for private tables and high rollers, the entire team that Eckhart brings to the moon along with the team already in place, slowly fills in ones and twos as people arrive. There is, of course, a gambling table that takes center stage in the room. Plenty of chairs are arranged around it. There's even refreshments available, but none of the casino staff are allowed entry into the room, and this is managed by two marines who are standing to either side of the entrance looking ready to recalibrate the luck of anyone dumb enough to invite themselves INTO the room. Instead of chips and cards and the usual way to dump all your money so the house can win, the table is layered with hand drawn maps of the casino, the all entrance & exit points, the various lifts and the connection to corresponding landing pads and so on. Security checkpoints are marked as are the observed routines of the guards and the current location of the cargo (i.e. the living breathing cargo that's up for auction soon). And, there's caff. Because what mission is complete without copious amounts of caffeine.''
----


Following Eckhart is a small group of marines, each having gone a few days without shaving or a haircut, and each wearing civilian attire. They still looked like soldiers, it was difficult for them to be anything else. Each is carrying a duffle bag as they enter the room. A lit cigar is clamped between Eckhart's teeth as he looks over the drawings. "What's the situation," the Colonel asks as he's only recently arrived and has yet to get the full layout of the situation. "I've got two marines working on commandeering a shuttle. There's an old gamma-class nearby they have their eyes on. Should be available when we're ready to get the hell out of here."

As he speaks, the marines that arrived with him are unloading duffles full of armor, weapons, and other gear on the tables. If they were going to make an assault, blast vests and sidearms weren't the best option.

Tia is lounging, with a cup of caff in hand. A brow raises at Eckhart's entry and a brief smile crosses her face. "That sounds like a great idea - but it does leave me with a question. What am I doing with the Bright Star?" she asks easily. A pause and then she laughs. "I might be jumping ahead a bit in the plan. I was assuming we'd need the Star for carting people out of here? But if not so, then perhaps it should be gone before trouble hits - well, whatever it's about to hit? Or - well, you tell me." It's the civilian in her showing no doubt.

Druze edges away from Eckhart's cigar in the enclosed room. Or tries. "We have to know how many people we're evacuating. If the plan is 'walk out with every slave at auction,' we need more space than a shuttle. Some may be injured or in shock. *Our people* may be injured or in shock. We have to decide now whether we're in it for pure speed, shock-and-awe-and-hyperspace, or we have some hitting-on-multiple-cylinders uberplan."

"Boss," Siarra says with a solemn nod aimed at Eckhart once he and the rest of the marines enter the room. She exchanges nods with the rest of the team, a wry grin as they start to unpack the gear, "You brought goodies," she exchanges a shoulder-bump with the next nearest ranking marine as questions are raised and answers are hunted for. Yes, sometimes marines DO act like pack animals, it's a thing. She unclips the Slate from her belt and swipes through the screens to project the 3D model of the casino lay out onto one of the entertainment monitors built into the walls of the room. She adds the various check points and security measures they know about, the cameras noted and the network of inter-webbed tunnels, layers upon layers of access shafts and so on. "This whole bloody moon is lousy with tunnels, it's amazing it hasn't collapsed in on itself or vented everyone into space."

"No plan survives first contact with the enemy," Eckhart says, looking at Tia. "If possible, I'd like to keep your ship from being burned by these pirates. Right now, you're just an independent trader on Nar Shaddaa for business. That being said, we need to have our options open, just in case."

The colonel takes a drag from his cigar, looking over the maps before looking towards Druze. "The Gamma class can hold a max of around 40 people, but that's in a stock configuration. I don't know anything about this ship other than we watched it land, so it at least flies. They also gassed it up when they got here, so it's got fuel enough to get us out of system. We might end up having to split into two teams, each escorting refugees to the different ships, or we may not be able to get to the shuttle at all, leaving us all packed in nuts to butts on the Star."

Icky smoke doesn't really bother Wrexan much at all. If anything, it completes the grizzled/I am a leader persona that Eckhart might be putting off. Wrexan doesn't interpose himself between Siarra's report to Eckhart because she is liable to do it much better than he would. "Its a shithole, more or less." In regards to the Smuggler's Moon. "The locale isn't too well watched or guarded at the very least."

"We got a count of the number of slaves who were there, when we did some recon," Tia observes. That info is in there with everything else Siarra has to share. Tia then finishes her caff, setting the cup down somewhere and resting her hands at her sides. "Of course," she says to Eckhart. "Been sussing out cargo contracts here and there, keeping on the - well, as much up and up as one can, given the location. Slightly muddy maybe?" she says a bit wryly. "Trying to keep from attracting notice from anyone, either way." She hopefully has managed that, except maybe on the recon mission.

Siarra pours herself another cup of caff while looking at the image, then the weapons and gear available, then sweeps a thoughtful look around the assembled team. "Based on the gear we have available, the lay out, and the know response time of so called local law enforcement, and the on-going turf war tree-pissing struggle that's going on between the cartels, it's in our best interest not to get them united against us."

Tia snorts briefly at that comment from Siarra. She moves to get her cup again, moving to refill it. "Druze, just so you know, I might end up a little over caffeinated." And then she turns back to look and listen. "Not getting them all united against us is a great plan. We definitely need to keep that in mind, whatever the plan ends up being."

"What's the total count on the slaves?" Eckhart asks as he's going over the maps, trying to plot out a decent course of entry and escape. "And who here is good with a blade and doesn't mind taking out some slaver guard puke who's not actively trying to kill you? If possible, I want to eliminate guards as stealthfully as possible and get in as close as possible before everything goes to absolute hell."

The marine colonel paces a bit, looking over the layout, his cigar smoke trailing behind him, though he does stop to fix a cup of caff. "I want honesty, not heroics. There's a difference, a big difference, between shooting at someone who's shooting at you and inserting a vibroblade between their second and third ribs from behind. If you can do it, great, if you can't, that's good too."

Having been sitting quietly in a corner, Kelnas finally looks up as Eckhart mentions needing quiet wet work done. "I'm better with a blade than I am a blaster Colonel. I could manage it." Looking at the maps, the Wroonian quietly tries to make sense of it all.

Siarra is staring at the layout as she angles a solemn nod to Eckhart, "I can do that, the challenge is being in more place than once. EOD is my specialty but I can prep the other aspects of this plan and make sure that everything is just point, click, boom, and move to the next point to create organized chaos."

Oh no. Oh good, someone talked before Wrexan said something silly. Wetwork to him definitely sounds like taking a bath. Or a GOOD shower. Not stabbing or shooting people. "Well...uhhh....I've got a blaster. Probably shouldn't be me too up front. I'll be talking or something to people. Right? Disabling security feeds and what not?"

Druze shrugs, mildly helpless? "A bit out of my depth. Can I shiv someone? Sure. We have a special anti-Hippocratic oath we have to swear, but it's quick. Rolls right off the tongue." He does a 'badda-bing, badda-boom' imaginary stabbing? If you need to get someone between specific ribs, you-- can do worse than a doctor.

Eckhart nods, and a couple of the marines with him nod as well. They were recon marines, stealth operations were their specialty. "Alright, Kelnas, you and Siarra go Keys," Eckhart says. "You're team A. I'll head up team B with Rink and Barquin." 

He looks to Druze. "You're the closest thing we have to a combat medic, so you're not going on point. You'll be with the blaster-toting group. If you go down, we can't triage the people we're here to rescue and it would put the entire mission at risk."

Eckhart looks to the other marines, "Nobody fucks with Doc, got it?" They nod in agreement and are already sizing Druze up for a good set of armor.

Nodding to Eckhart once more, Kelnas looks up from the maps and around at the group. Not his usal jovial self, the Wroonian was settling into a more professional bent now that some of the rust had been knocked off in the past few weeks. "I'll follow your lead Siarra."

Siarra makes another of those solemn nods and looks up to Kelnas, "Your choice of blade?" she asks, starting for the gear that's already on display. "I prefer a rodian dagger, personally, it doesn't require a energy cell to make the edge keen or usable. But I can offer you a vibro-shiv if you'd like," she says, "or I can get you a blade like the one I prefer."

Tia sips her coffee as she listens. She does look at her slate, checking her notes. "There were 40-50 slaves," she observes, "just so you all have that in mind." She is listening, curiously, not really up on all this sneaking fighting stuff. or medical. She's a pilot, that she can do. She takes a breath and then adds, "Druze, you make sure you are careful, right? I can come and help too, if you all need an extra body, but you do know the level of my shooting skills."

Druze *handsups.* "It's not easy to hear 'you need to be bubble-wrapped because your contribution isn't violence,' but nor is it wrong. I can always run vintage Wookiee pornography through their holo-cameras?" He checks to see if he's insane? Anyone care to weigh in?

Eckhart looks at Druze for a long moment. He starts to say something, but stops and just shakes his head. "I really do not want to know. I really don't." 

He flicks an ash off his cigar, while a couple of marines are making mental notes to check with Druze after the mission. Fucking Marines. 

"We'll make sure everyone is properly kitted out. Thanks to an anonymous benefactor, we've got some fancy Caspian armor and weapons, not to mention what my marines managed to scavenge from a reserve armory we might have stumbled into. I'd like to see this go smoothly. Hit hard, hit fast, show no mercy. This is not friendly territory, and if we get caught, nobody is coming to rescue us."

Looking to Siarra once more, Kelnas shrugs. "I don't mind the heft of an old school combat knife, but my shiv is pretty reliable and easy enough to conceal. I'll be alright." Patting himself down for his cigarettes, he pulls a perpetually, half crushed smoke from a pack and places it between his lips, not lighting it just yet. Yellow eyes slowly turn towards Druze as Kelnas' imagination begins to spool up. "There's worse things Druze. At least you don;t get the smell over video. the smell adds a new level of horror to it. and you do not want to be an innocent bystander when that goes down." 

Still listening to Eckhart, Kelnas makes a mental note to start trying to secure other back channel supply lines for the organization.

Siarra passes a simple vibro-shiv to Kelnas before she adds her own summary to what Eckhart just said: "Kelnas and I are team A, The Boss," she nods toward Eckhart, "along with Rink and Barquin will be going in first. Our job is to be quiet and quieter than quiet while we take out the guards. Once that's accomplished we'll link up with the rest of the team," this is Druze, Tiaryn and Wrexan along with the NPC marines, "to cut through the security and free the slaves. Ideally we will use the cargo lift in the back of the cavern to get down to the level where our team will be waiting with the shuttle that we're going to acquire. Tia, we're going to need you to be ready to move your ship from where it's docked to the lower level. Some of the team is going to split to escort the rest of the slaves to your shuttle, so you're going to be heavy with Marines and Slaves and we're going to have to jump immediately and without clearance. If it comes to a fire fight - if anyone gets off of the ground I mean to try to shoot at your ship - don't engage. Just get out of the gravity well and be gone. Anyone who gets wounded, shake it off. We keep Druze alive an intact and unharmed. Wrexan will be our go-to pilot for the other shuttle. But lets be clear. Our mission is to get those slaves and be gone. Our mission is not to blow up this casino or set anything on fire. Quiet is loud, loud is dead," she again angles a nod toward Eckhart, "what he said. There is no rescue plan, no one is going to save us or those slaves. So we're in charge of rescuing our own damn selves."

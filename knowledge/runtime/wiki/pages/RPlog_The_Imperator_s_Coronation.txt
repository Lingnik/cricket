&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Imperator Circle had been remodeled over the last few days leading up to the coronation. The former statue of Emperor Palpatine had been removed rapidly and unceremoniously and replaced with a simpler display of power for the new Imperium. A non-descript figure stands with a plaque below it signaling the new rulers of the Galactic Empire which was now referred to as either the New Order or Imperium.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Circle was transformed into a bazaar of stalls selling food, hosting games, and the streets are filled with beings from hundreds of worlds. The sight looks nothing like any Imperial world of old, the diversity alone is staggering. At every corner of the circle are bands and live shows as the citizens of the Imperial City celebrate the dawn of a new era.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the meetings over, Crestian returns to his fancier fashions as he ambles about the stalls of the bazaar. The parties have also seen him joined by his friend and tailor Zubindi Hakoon of the Silverwood House of Fashion on Kubindi. If the Kubaz were anything they were appreciative of culture and this was a high time to partake. As the walk through a section largely devoted to food Crestian remarks to Zubindi, "I must apologise I suspect in spite of the end of certain Imperial policies they probably don't have a good selection of insect-cuisine." The Kubaz brushes this off, "Had I wanted that I could have stayed at home and ordered take out from Zubindi Ebsuk's Domus of Drachs." Crestian laughs a little, "You know I had forgotten you were named after a chef."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Strong drink had never been a friend to Tracer, but it had at least been a stranger until last night. Dinner with Sith and Jedi and her wayward child?!? She found an old friend in some O'Paal vodka that was allowed to flow freely into the distraught woman as she sat mostly sullen but controlled in the midst of what should be enemies. Today her head hurt and the lights were too bright and she was full of too many forms of regret. And it was loud...so loud here with music competing for dominance from the varied stages and the muddle of languages and voices ebbing and flowing with the traffic that moves, throngs pressing against each other in the midday warmth. She had to get outside though. It would be some time before she would see Kipran again. He had duties afterall! And the lavish hotel, an experience she enjoyed in her past, was stiffling with it's color scheme of blood and void. The woman searches for a shady seat somewhere and perhaps some water would be good for her.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tracer could try to take comfort in that she knew where her Kipran was - her darling gifted child. And the New Order was not what she expected...<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Overhead another round of fireworks booms and echoes across the sky sparkling and flashing crimson, white, and then a rainbow of colors across the visual spectrum and others reaching into spectrums outside of human eyesight for the non-human visitors. Music plays and the general mood moves into a rapturous joy as if the world has been liberated from years of oppression.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The crowd near the government building parts and many drop to a knee and bow as the Imperator, now in a uniform himself, and a cadre of advisors including Drayson Honos exit with a light security detail. Malign raises a hand, "No need for formalities we are all here to enjoy the festivities." He gestures to encourage people to disregard protocol and return to their celebration. With the Imperator was a Bothan and Barabel all wearing uniforms (the Bothan an Admiral; the Barabel as General) though keep their discussions separated from the topics of state and the military.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A couple of advisors beckon the group over to one of the nearby food booths and explain the cuisine being offered and about the proprietors. The group of beings purchase some of the food while another group film and record holos of the event, likely for state use.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Drayson points toward the center of the circle, "We should get some recordings in front of the new statue with the flags in the background."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The group pools and moves toward the center of the circle with their street food in hand and a couple of handlers following them desperately trying to keep the food off their uniforms to make sure all of their PR efforts are for not.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Overhearing Drayson's remark from a nearby stall, the voice of course being quite familiar to Crestian, the Sarian remarks, "Might I suggest a holopic or two with some random partiers or perhaps if any of the Moff's have younglings here with them. Some light-hearted picture of that nature might be good to have in the database....though I suppose you are probably ahead of me on that Admiral, you do always seem to think of all the fine details." The Kubaz with Crestian gives a slight head nod to the passing dignitaries, and informal mark of respect."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Ugh," comes Tracer's scoff of disgust at these so-called New Order posers she sees across the way. Purchasing a sweet and caffed-up beverage, she drops to a bench near a fabricated tree off-center from the main spectacle. The boom of fireworks makes her wince. The recirculated air of the artificial atmosphere feels dense in Tracer's lungs. Nothing here reminds her of when she would have a display brought to her family in their private paradise to light up the night sky after the blazing orange sunset to celebrate Life Day.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A Twi'lek woman sits down next to Tracer a short while later. The fog is lifting a little. After chugging half her drink unceremoniously she's feeling better but still in the partial-daze of this bad, bad dream. "They'll kill you as soon as look at you, ya know," Elana offers unsolicited to the Twi'lek.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I beg your pardon?" replies the other female.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"When this doesn't work for them any more! You're betraying your own people just being here. Have some damned self-respect!" Perhaps Tracer wasn't yet very sober as she reverts to a loud bluntness that served her with varying degrees of success and failure in the past. Appalled, the Twi'lek stands and scuttles off disappearing behind some grandstands bedecked in Imperium bunting.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As Crestian speaks the Imperator's entourage immediately absorb him into it as aids come to assist in adjust his fashion and offer to sort his hair and garb. An itinerary is thrust into his hands as Drayson smiles, "Admiral good, you are here. Those ideas are splendid." The Grand Admiral was not a man who understood PR as he had aides furiously record Crestian's ideas and calls were being made. Fortunately for everyone access to the Moffs and younglings was an easy feat as they the government complex was nearby within moments Tyler and his inner circle were thrust into pictures of regional governors and their children as they all steady continue their path toward the statue at the center of the area.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tyler gestures for Crestian and Drayson, an aide quickly activates a communication jammer and gives them a bubble of privacy, "How is the angry mother situation?" he inquires politely as they continue walking, "I will need both Kirpan and Etiel ready for the next operation, I have a feeling we are going to have to enforce our anti-slavery and pro-xeno policies on a few worlds." He states as they continue to walk. Despite the friendly face of the New Order and Damion's proclaimed meritocracy, the hammer or large stick of the central order still existed and he would wild it if anyone dares to contradict his mandates.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Drayson speaks, "Johanna, will adjust with time. She needs to absorb the news. You should be more open with her about your plans Imperator. She might come to appreciate what you are doing. Etiel is in general council with other Moffs outlining plans to enact your policies, though he has assured us that he is prepared to deploy if needed. I have also begun organizing three task forces though resources are currently in flux as the old Imperial Navy is working out where their loyalties lie, the Shipyards are in overdrive though and with the influx of crew and staff from the new xeno-policies I am confident staffing will not be an issue and when in doubt we automate." He pulls out a pair of datapads one goes to the Bothan and the other to Crestian, "Admiral Fel'lya will manage the core worlds and enforcement, Admiral Crestian's circumstances will keep him close to his other political interests new the CDU, and I will manage the galactic south."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Imperator nods as Drayson speaks, "Admiral Crestian, are you satisfied with the resources we are offering? What do you need in order to convince the Caspian Democratic Union it is in their best interest to be a member state of the New Order? We can't have them running to the New Republic or Oppenhiemer. Also, run a security sweep for the former CDU Presav, Tracer. I will meet with her personally."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the private conservation among Tyler and the Admirals begins, "My sources tell me that the provisional government on Caspar is debating final passage of the new constitution. Debate has shifted a little since your announcement but there is a renewed interest in a timely transition to a 'permanent' government now. I expect the nomination stage to be quite short. The campaign will shift a little too, my links here will come into the mind of voters, but I believe we will still be able to have a fairly 'free' election. The number of refugees that came from Mergansar form a significant voting block who will be all too eager to see us hold the cultists there to final account. And a controlled leak of the information that the charity, the Mission, has received significant financing from the House of Damion will continue to keep sufficient people accepting the transition."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;   Crestian pulls out of when one of his ubiquitous padds, "The resources has been fairly adequate, I think the concentration of the more overt Imperium actions will be focused around shipyards and industry. The Empire and Corporate Sector Authority have essentially been dominating the scene there for some years now already, particular with the control of the Kichnar Orbital Station, naturally my administration will have to swiftly repatriate that either through our faction recognising the local sovereignty, within the grander Imperium or by booting out the holdouts. I am also planning to absorb the nearby world of Charros IV, they have some modest shipyards that had been Sienar's and prior to that the local Haor Chall Engineering. With the new policies we will be able to resume some native control and I anticipate a smooth path to getting them into the new Caspar Union. So this should see us to balance out our fleet output in the coming months and need fewer material resources."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Unable to stand the "unity" of this PR fever dream of a "celebration", Tracer rises from her bench and chucks her empty drink container into the nearest receptacle. She then takes a moment to smooth the front of her white blouse and set the skew of her belt straight before beginning to walk back to the hotel where she hoped her son would come find her there later on and she could still try to reason with him. Though her own father was unable to talk her out of racing swoops or flying A-Wings so good luck with that she tells herself.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Elana's senses peak as she is making her way and she spies a uniform pointing her direction and two other thugs in security garb moving her way. Resolved she stops and lets them close the gap, starting to mockingly raise her hands to shoulder height as they arrive in front of her. She cocks a slender brow up at them. "What?!" she demands.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tyler was not going to play the game of being fully versed in the nuances of the situation in CDU space, but he was keenly aware that if they did not act the only winners would be their enemies. He had tried to sure up their loyalty before but all of those politicians and enemies were dead now, thanks to the plague, ironically a disease he was not responsible for and rather assisted in developing the cure, "I'll trust your judgement and actions in the matter then Admiral, and the Imperium will follow your lead in the matter." He states as they reach the statue in the center of plaza.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The photographs and PR was completed swiftly. Meanwhile soldiers and security were conducting biometric scans searching for Tracer. The forces move deliberately through the city as recon droids and scouts were buzzing across a multitude of frequencies, unfortunately this results in a temporary impound of her vessel as the leader of the State was seeking an audience. Despite some changes the Imperium's methods were not that easily changed and the process of converting to a nicer form of Feudal absolutism would take time.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Imperator Damion signals for the area around the statue to be cleared as aides and soldiers begin setting up a perimeter and chairs and tables are brought nearby and placed neatly for the group to take seats, he would wait for Tracer here in the middle of everything. A comm cloak was extended from their group to encompass the area that left them secured for surveillance and the media, and staff begin bring drinks and food placing it at the table as the Admirals, their Imperator, and a few VIPs prepare to enjoy an open air luncheon, one chair near Tyler purposely left open.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The security detail pull up a holographic image of Tracer and show it to her, "Elana Tracer." He confirms the identity and then continues, "Madam, you are not under arrest. The Imperator wishes you to accompany us, he seeks an audience." Their posture indicates she had a right to refuse but they were nervous of what might happen to them if she did and they reported back empty handed.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"We appreciate your continue support Imperator." The new title came smoothly enough off Crestian's tongue, he was never keen on the Imperial culture in spite of the New Sith Order's rise from among it. Nevertheless it was simpler to co-opt the old, and effectively the situation he was in with Caspar, "The old CDU always possessed a peculiar philosophy and was always doomed to fail ultimately. But ironically enough that culture should give way to a good vassal state. Insufficient to survive on its own, but showing a prime example of the lighter touch of the new Imperator. And bring Mergansar to heel will provide a good example of a break from aspects of Sith and Imperial pasts we are distancing from. The other major Caspian worlds should fall in line once Caspar is able to reassert dominance."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With an almost surprised chuckle, Tracer lowers her sarcastically raised palms. "Oh. Well then." With a rogue's caginess her clear blue eyes scan the area, tempted to give these two a very bad day.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;   But knowing she needs something from the Sith Lord more than just departure clearance, she shrugs off any thought of making trouble just now and nods her acquiescence. "What are we waiting for?" she trills in her best good girl innocent eye-blinking way she could still muster and heads where the security team leads. Soon she arrives to the gathering and her escort departs. The woman then stands looking at Malign expectantly.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tyler listens to Crestian and nods, "The history of the CDU is meaningless now that swaths of its population are gone, they must adapt to their conditions and begin healing. Their philosophy and culture eradicated by a mixture of hubris and a disease beyond their control." He accepts an offered drink from an aide, "The radicals are the danger here, that Sar Admiral did us a disservice and has allowed anarchy to exist for too long there. I wish you luck." He gestures to the food and beverages, the Bothan, Barabel, and Drayson join them, "Once our final guest arrives, we should be able to enjoy an excellent afternoon." He states looking in the direction of the guards and Tracer his own senses had sent him a notion of the altercation and he could feel the trepidation, anger, and hurt through the Force, he feeds on it giving himself strength.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tracer arrives with her escorts and the Imperator waves them away. His hazel eyes fall on Tracer fully he had met a few like her, powerfully independent, strong willed, and willing to burn the galaxy for their cause - whatever that cause might be-, "Have a seat." He gestures, his tone was not that of a request but also not fully etched as a command, "We will enjoy some refreshments and you can explain to my why the ideology and this Imperium is a farce." His words were not icy they came with a hint of Corellian friendliness, a relic of the old Tyler Damion, a weapon he could use to disarm people.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Drayson and the other staff politely shift in their chairs standing as a sign of politeness for the arrived guest. As this occurs Malign, Imperator Damion speaks, "Your son, he is inspecting his new command. He will be sent on a task for me before the end of the day, do you have any objections?" he watches the woman careful and her actions his posture remains relaxed.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian nods silently in agreement with the Imperator's assessment of the Caspian situation. He did not consider Tyler's remarks to have quite the level of nuisance that he actually fully concurred with but this was not a time for an academic debate. A man of many words this was an occasion where listening was of more value. Whatever exchange was about to occur between Tyler and Tracer, he was not going to win points with either of them trying to soften someone up. If he found a moment where he might add more peace to the situation he might say something, but ultimately much like everything in his life there needed to be some patience, even if alacrity seemed the order of the day. Crestian simply nods respectfully to the woman as he takes a drink offered by one of the attendants.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Courtesy. From all these seeming gentlebeings. Especially the sycophantic Sarian. It continues to be offered to Tracer by this regime she landed in less than a standard ago, but she clearly isn't buying it. To his first question she wants to scream 'because it is a lie!' Instead, she raises her defiant chin to balance out taking the seat that is proffered before Damion speaks of Kipran. As quickly as possible she weighs her options for response. He couldn't leave for Winds know where without her seeing him again, could he? He would. She was sure of it and she dies a little inside. Play the game, her brain tells her heart. To buy a moment she runs her index fingers along the edge of the black table linen, apart then together again before setting her crystalline gaze upon Tyler. "Would it change anything if I did?" the once and former stateswoman asks coolly.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Malign respects Crestian's appraisals of most situations, thanks to the Admiral's quick wit and his own strategies what was left of the New Sith Order survived the Thel purge attempts. In some respects everything before them now was due to his strategies and stealth The Imperator knew that his approach to the CDU issue was without reproach and he could be trusted. Tracer was the new challenge. As she stood defiantly, wrestles with her anger and frustration, the Dark Lord allows himself to be impressed with her control.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He sips his drink slowly and lets her question sit in the air for a few moments, "What do you think about the concept of Freedom?" he turns back to the woman, "Would it be in Kipran's best interest for me to interrupt his desires?" he asks, "Would it be an act of dismissing his dreams and goals to end his career and mission?" He leans back in his chair, "I would assume freedom means a great deal to you." He says, "I feel your entire life has been built around the ability to maintain your construct of liberty. The Imperium stands on the side freedom, the Sith are no Jedi." He smiles, "We aren't going to suppress desire, dreams, love, and hope for some broken construct." He leans forward placing his elbow on the table and smiles.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Do you want me to stop him?" he asks again. Drayson carefully pours a drink and places it before the woman.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;And damn now Tyler was getting philosophical. This is what Crestian loved about him, he supported Tyler the Mad Sith Scientist, and Tyler the Destroyer, but it was Tyler the Philosopher who Crestian has followed for all those years. This was the sort of conversation Crestian relished. But again this was perhaps not his best entrance into it. Crestian was an expert exponent on Sith philosophy, but it was hardly a necessary quality. He sips his drink silently, hoping Tyler's charms would help soften the situation.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;How should she answer? With the truth? A falsehood? Whichever kind of answer Tracer offered, she knew it truly landed smack in the middle of "yes" and "no" for her. As the elder man places a drink in front of her she appears to contemplate the sparkling liquid in the fine crystal glass before picking it up and bringing it to her lips for a sip. "Ahem," she clears her throat placing glass on table, cocking her head in concession. "Of course I do," she answers, possibly allowing a coy smile to play across her face. "But I won't ask that of you. That would just make -me- his enemy."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Sure," the former CDU Marine Colonel continues. "I fought for freedom. Fought hard." A wince of pain flashes in her light blue eyes. "I learned, though. Freedom is a lie. Even if you run far away and leave everything behind except those you love...it won't last. Because it wasn't real."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Malign smiles as he watches Tracer process and respond to his questions, "Precisely." He takes his cup and finishes the contents as an aide comes by and refills it, "I wouldn't say freedom doesn't exist though your recognition of the failure of this reality to resolve itself into freedom is freedom in itself." He shrugs, "You find your freedom in that realization. I was like you once, I fought at the Battle of Endor, I flew an X-wing and then served the New Republic for several years. I was a freedom fighter to my core." He points toward Drayson, "He was a Republic Officer turned Imperial with the rise of Palpatine. He served most of his career in the Outer Rim and became famous for maintaining peace without excessive casualties." He points toward Crestian, "A politician, a strategist, and someone committed to order and peace. He does not murder civilians or the innocent." The Imperator laughs.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"No one here murders for pleasure, we operate to reduce pain and suffering. It is better for people to live in their ideological lies if it brings them peace. My goal is to mitigate suffering and let people live as they desire. After all, it isn't real. Everything goes away in the end." The Sith leans forward, "Your son has become an indispensable agent of our mission. I want you to become an agent of this mission as well."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Drayson slides a datapad to the woman, "You are a CDU outcast reviled and exiled to CSA space living out the rest of your life in obscurity because of your sacrifices. You tried to protect the people you were charged with leading." Tyler shrugs, "You have potential, but I am afraid you're of no use to the CDU or its recovery. I have something else in mind. Your son can assist you in this mission perhaps."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian's silence would soon end, the conversation had moved into too many directions for him to remain silent, though he would not contradict the comment about no one killing for pleasure at least. "Freedom is not a place. Nor it is the success of a particular cause. It is an outlook and a philosophy. And one the Sith....true Sith, subscribe to strongly. Those holofilm villains you have heard about, perhaps even encountered in your travels. They are bogeyman or they are the failures. By a freedom embracing people does have the potential to attract the wrong people at times. They have largely died through their own stupidity, their own thirst for unfettered violence undid them precisely because they found it. We are beings with strengths and weaknesses, at times we make mistakes, and we learn from them and correct for them. Like Damion and Honos, I started my career in the Republic, in fact I suppose I was on the opposite end of these guys at the time. The Blitzkrieg taught me that to have that better galaxy we dream for ourselves, for our children, we need to have an effective system that can fight back against threats. Freedom does not come from chaos, from values pushed to a brink passed where they can be defended."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He turns for a moment from Tracer to Tyler, to then looking at both of them. He was going to disagree with Tyler on something and voice it, both because he thought he might be right but also because the act of disagreement proved a point as well, "I can not speak to what mission you have in mind for them. But if that does not suit her, I still have hope she could be useful to Caspar more directly. Certainly I have been working on the slow climb to prove to people that my desires to support the world are sincere. A narrative that was written against me has slowly been chipped away. Should she wish to return the narrative crafted against her could be dismantled as well. What happens now will replace misperceptions of the past. At any rate you are a woman of great talents, someone who could be an asset to us and we could be an asset to you. You see all this as tearing a hole in your family. Certainly differences in politics, in aspirations, have done that in families throughout all time. But we are no less a place for family than anywhere else. We have loved ones we care about, that we go home to when the work is done, that we do the work to keep them safe and make a better galaxy for."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tracer's folded hands stay firmly planted in her lap. She squints at the datapad and eyes Drayson with suspicion. When Malign finishes showing his hand, her attention slowly turns to Tarasar. She didn't want to go back to Caspar. The very notion of a revision of her historicly weak actions at the hands of the Imperials during her administration causes her bile to rise slightly. She leans forward in her chair and regards them all in turn. "My Caspar isn't there anymore. You people killed my friends. My brother. My father!" The last had not been as a true casualty of war but vengeance enacted by the notorius double agent Heirich Stahl at the dawn of the 1st Imperial Blockade of CDU space. Next she addresses only Malign.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"And you. You want to give me a job?" she asks, half amazed, half offended. They were all insane, these men. With an indiscriminate chuckle she scoots back her chair, making to rise. "I think we're done here."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian's lack of decorum to the Imperator was noticed quickest by the Barabel, Hsskah Agileclaw who almost bursts from his chair to go for the Sarian's throat, Drayson however politely clears his throat and the creature seems to settle back into his chair and returns to his drink. Tyler, does not seem bothered by Crestian's approach and lets the group speak for several moments, "She is right, it isn't her Caspar, it never was." He gestures toward her, "If the Empire was once a xenophobic den of ignorance, that is what the CDU became in your absence." The Imperator states flatly, he had never liked Caspar or the CDU, he had tried to annex it for his Sith Order to no avail.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As Tracer speaks and begins to rise in anger, "So quick to judgement. After the events of 14 ABY Drayson and his entire command staff resigned from the Galactic Empire. I was still serving in the New Republic Fleet Ops." He laughs, "No one here has done your world harm. I was the one who worked to cure it. I worked with the Emperor later and killed Danik Kreldin, I helped the Emperor kill Darth Malus, and I assisted in the death of Valak. All of your enemies are dead by my hands."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He scoffs, "You are so quick to judge having lived on your isolated island for so long, you have a lot to learn from your son, I pray he can teach you. As for my offer, it is not related to the CDU, what is left of it will find its own way and perhaps the good Admiral can help them rebuild some semblance of a society. No, I need you for matters closer to home."<br>

Twin Suns Hospital - Trinumvira: Caspar

Finding the fatalistic approach to the Sariphage by the late head of the Groton Sciences Institute to be ahborrant; Crestian Tarasar as Executive Director of The Mission, resolved to allocate a sizeable portion of its budget into a new, state of the art, research hospital. Its name is a homage to Tatooine, the homeworld of Johanna Siri te Danaan who funded the Sariphage cure.

With an annual research budget of 63 million credits it has the largest hospital based research programme in the Caspian Union and indeed one of the largest in the Mid Rim. It is the largest hospital campus in the Caspian Union, though as research centred institute there are other facilities in the Union with a higher patient treatment capacity. It is also the largest structure fully within the city limits, though Trinumvira Base and Union Starport are larger.

The visible structure of the campus consists of three main sections. Taking up an entire city block below the escarpment is a large three storey complex of sandstone and transparisteel, the structure itself continues into the cliff behind it. On the plateau above the escarpment are two fifteen storey towers of duracrete and durasteel, each tower is topped with a landing pad.

Though these towers are not quite the tallest structures in the city, given their elevation they tower over the other structures of the city. What is not visible is the fact that these are all in fact a single continuous structure, much of which exists in the cliff and several storeys underground as well.

While research serves as the major focus here, the hospital has the trappings one might expect to find in any major hospital. Notable features include three separate accident and emergency wards, a department of cybernetic medicine, and hundreds of both kolto and bacta tanks. Given the close proximity to Trinunmvira Base there are various physical and mental health resources available to military personnel, both in-patient and out-patient.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian Tarasar is a man of many hats, one of which is as the Chairman of the Board as well as a medical researcher at the Mid Rim's premier hospital. A recent incident with another doctor's patient has cause Crestian to lean more into medicine in these past days. Not only trying to get work done, but quietly run through the deeper questions of medical ethics in his head. He is not alone in his current professional complications which has brought him in the position of needing to take a particularly sensitive meeting.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Currently the Presav can be found in his laboratory in one of the basement levels of Twin Suns Hospital. This particular laboratory is kept fairly secure as the subject of his research is the drug Slick from Vaynai which would have a very high value on the black market. In contrast to his usual fashions, Crestian wears a white labcoat embroidered with the words: Crestian Tarasar, M.D., Hospital Chairman. Elsewhere on the coat is a patch indicating his association with the hospital's department of pharmaceutical research.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Axel was in full Imperium regalia, which was unusual unless he was truly taking something seriously. The man walked the halls, a pair of black-armored stormtroopers walking to his rear. "That will be all," he says to them at the entrance to the lab. "Wait here and make sure the meeting remains private," he tells them before thanking the hospital staff member who had shown him the way to the lab.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"As you might have noticed, I acted on the intel your people sent me, trying to lock down any leaks from happening," Axel says as he approaches Crestian, careful to not touch anything as he drew near. Hospitals were not his favorite places, and labs could contain all manner of things unholy. "But, how long will such a thing last or even be necessary? You met with Lorn Rhys before I did, did he attempt to recruit you as well for whatever plans he has may be?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I know you have mixed feelings about your role in the Imperium, but let me just say that uniform really suits you Axel." Crestian offers Axel a seat, "And don't worry nothing really dangerous in this particular laboratory. Just a theft risk. This hospital exists in part make sure the next plague doesn't decimate our people, but I am afraid that is not my own research interests. The medicinal uses of Slick though has been a research hobby for many years."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Doctor Tarasar himself sits down, "I was asked to by Lynae to come to her ship. I had never imagined it would be to meet Lorn Rhys and that he would be her patient. Wasn't hard to figure out he must have been busted out of the Oubliette, but how he got there to begin with....obviously your own directives to Moff Council underline the seriousness of the situation."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian gets back up for a moment and goes to a cabinet. The cabinet looks like any other one in the laboratory but when open reveals a small sampling of liquors, "Can I get you a drink?" Pulling out a bottle of Ithorian Brandy for himself he continues, "Recruit might be a strong word. I would say more accurate was he was trying to form an alliance. A flawed prospect for many reasons but most obviously the fact that he thinks the Union needs some freedom from the Imperium that he can offer. He seems unable or unwilling to process the fact that our current relationship is advantageous. Seems convinced that the Imperator must still be working for Vadim."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Axel takes a seat, watching Crestian as he talks. At the offer of a drink, Axel nods. "This work does tend to make one thirsty," he admits. "And thank you, I have a tailor on Selene that does a good job. I hate the stupid cape though, but apparently it's required when you want to seem important, makes you look bigger or something."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Axel sighs at the mention of Vadim. "Truth be told, he may not be completely wrong, at least about our relationship with the Imperium. As a citizen of Caspar, not the Inquisitor, it is a tenuous one at best, if Malign were to fall, it could put us at risk. I've seen multiple emperors in the last couple of decades, each with very differing agendas."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Axel pauses a moment, "And, let's be quite frank, Malign has been fairly absentee, absorbed in whatever it is Malign does in those creepy ass labs of his. There have been more than a few attempts upon his powerbase. The Union needs to be prepared in case the man falls."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Axel shifts now, adjusting the cape. "As Inquisitor, I'm more concerned about who helped Lorn Rhys and the others escape. Was he the primary target of the break, or just a lucky bystander who benefited from it? Why was he there in the first place? And who's dumbass idea was it to put former Sith and emperors in carbonite for storage? What good do they serve? Stasis isn't exactly the best form of incarceration if you want to see someone punished."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I am having the Ithorian brandy, but I have some Whyren's if you'd prefer that." Crestian pulls out two glasses pouring himself his quintessential Ithorian brandy. "Capes are always a bit of a mixed bag. I know a lot of officers scoff at them. I do find the tippets Zubindi and I put into our uniforms here a happy medium, but of course I am a sucker for the classic Onderon military uniform look."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Returning to serious matters, "If Malign fell it could put us at risk for sure, but not really a rational to support someone seeking to overthrow him. It has to be remembered though that our galactic political reality exists in larger part to combat Vadim. It is what put Johanna in the position to rescue her brother for Korriban and why she and Etiel were Moffs and Etiel is still. The Imperium exists to deprive Vadim of its resources and to be used against him....but Malign is at his a mad scientist and that is where he devotes his efforts in the war against Vadim, trying to have the right magic, the right creation, the right whatever the fuck it is Sith do to fight one another. And the Imperium takes a back seat. It has grown too big....and without an active leader...."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Presav takes a sip of his brandy, "The rest of the high command structure is to our benefit. Grand Admiral Honos is an ally, our own former Presav as a Grand Moff, her son as the Malign's future son-in-law, of course your role their as well, my own sway in the Moff Council and inner circle. When the Republic or whoever dismisses the Union as a puppet of the Imperium, I tend to retort that the Imperium is a Caspian puppet state....I am not really joking. We do wield an outsized influence. And even if the Imperium loses territory, the real risk to us only comes if a new leader can really take over the bulk of the territory and resources and chooses to fight with us. We have a strong enough series of alliances and our own military strength to survive a reduction in potential Imperium support."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Whyren's sounds delightful," Axel says. "I'm not sure I'll ever like capes, but I don't design the uniforms." He then just listens to Crestian's take on the state of the Imperium.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I'm not looking to jump ship and trade one Sith for another. I would like to know the grand scheme though. I don't pretend to understand Vadim or even Malign or his sister. Their abilities make them think on a completely different wavelength than those of us without the abilities. I suspect his next target of recruitment will be Korolov."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"As for our sway amongst the Imperium, if it collapsed in on itself, the result would be a vacuum that would be filled by something, and I do not want the Union to take on that role and be likewise spread too thin. You are very correct, the Imperium is too large, especially without an active leader. I can exert influence temporarily, like I did in the Karthakk system, as an extension of the Imperator directly, but only until the Moff Council chooses to fill the vacancy, then I can only act if I uncover or fabricate threats to the Imperium." Axel says it as if he has no problem fabricating threats if necessary. "Putting aside my station in the Imperium, I will always choose Caspar and its people over all others."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian pours Axel some of the Whyren's Reserve and hands him the glass. Both bottles are kept outside of the cabinet and near to where the two men sit, "I think you are right about Korolov, though whether he will truly want him as an ally or wants revenge for his lack of action two decades ago, or perhaps a bit of both. In spite of his official title in the Imperium I think we all know he has no loyalty to it and may or may not move to Vadim's banner if he reemerged publicly. He has made himself such a non-entity this past several years. I mean does anyone even go to Coruscant anymore. It is like the most populated backwater world in the galaxy these days."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Presav takes another sip of his brandy and deep breath, "I do not envy a vacuum either. A lot of effort was put into expanding into Charros IV. It made good sense strategically as it is now a significant portion of our military production. We always could have expanded farther, there are worlds we have relationships with that could conceivably become members, and a few other worlds that would be nice to have all things being equal. But any expansion as you said would spread us thinner which is why we haven't. Further expansion is not out of the question entirely. But part of our defence strategy is based on fielding so many ships that any invasion attempted would suffer catastrophic losses before they could get planet side. If we took on much more territory that would be less guaranteed."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It was a heavy topic which saw another sip of brandy for the Caspian leader, "I appreciate that your loyalty is to Caspar over the Imperium. As much as I have tied my fortunes and the Union's to the Imperium, and real civil war that might emerge would bring with it tough questions and ultimately the Union Council as a whole would have to weigh in on what the obligations we have under our mutual defence pact would be in that situation. If it were just about Malign and Malus we could let them duke it out on the edge of a cliff somewhere, but I have to wonder as well who is behind the prison break. Even if Malus would sincerely like to be Caspar's friend, does that hold for his unseen allies? The problem seems less a concern of the black eye the Imperium is getting right now and more the question of the true agenda of the opposition. There is no real indication yet in my mind that it is likely to be one we wish to support."
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Axel takes the glass of Whyren's and sips at it, spending a moment to savor the flavors. The man truly did appreciate such luxuries. "Don't underestimate Korolov. Like Lorn Rhys, he was the power behind the Empire at one time, and may have hidden allies we don't know about. I think it more likely though that he would relish in a fractured Imperium and would jump at the opportunity to slice off a sector or two and rule it as a warlord if he thought he could," Axel says after a moment. "You've done well for the Union. I don't agree with all of your policies, but I cannot argue the results. The people have prospered, and that is what is most important."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Axel leans back in his seat, bringing the glass with him. "Malus is a Sith, and you and I both know that alliances are only good while they have value amongst the Sith themselves. First and foremost, a Sith, and I mean those who wield the force, have allegiances to one thing, and one thing only, their own personal power. Even Malign is not beyond that. Even in the days of the Order, the Thanes knew that it was only because we were useful that we had positions of power."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Axel sighs, sipping more at the expensive whiskey. "What of Johanna? You and her are much more sociable than she and I. Have you spoken with her as of yet? Malus straight tried to recruit both Lynae and I into his plots, without saying what said plots were. I played along for a bit, but he refused to dictate what he brought to the table. I was honestly hoping he'd shed some light into his escape and allies he has in the galaxy, or if nothing else, what his agenda might be other than how he put it, revenge."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Malus seems to have forgotten some of the rules about being a Sith. His assistance that Malign must be working for Vadim....like what self respecting Sith Lord would spend that long working for a Master rather than against him. Unfortunately though I also know all to well that the number of people Malign considers expendable is too large. I do not fear him turning against either you or I, but I worry about his family, my family. He is too close to crossing a line. And like any self respecting Sith I will break with him if necessary to protect my family, and friends, and the Union." Crestian smiles a little. There was some comfortable familiarity in Sith court, the backstabbing, the scheming, the contingent loyalties.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Moving on to the next question, "I have not reached out yet. After my meeting with him and Lynae I had in mind to reach out to her and make sure she knew. Whatever side we are on in this, we want her to be on that same side. Now that he has moved on from Caspar I had more of a mind to potentially make a personal visit to Tatooine to brief her and discuss strategy....though I would like to talk to Lynae first if possible. I understand why she is attracted towards the scheming, but at least in my meeting with them her efforts seemed to be politically naive."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Malign would probably turn on me if he felt the need," Axel says. "I have no illusions that I am anything more than expendable to the man. I simply try to prove my value when I can. He serves much better as an ally or benefactor than an enemy. Same with his sister."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Lynae, my dearest Lynae. She is one of my oldest and most dear of friends, but she is bored. She, like myself, hates the stagnation that is in the galaxy. She has been very important militarily in the past, and thinks that defined her, and is eager to feel important again. I worry that Malus sees that and will use it to gain her loyalty. I do hope not, and if you do manage to get her alone for a meeting, send her to me afterwards," he says, then catches himself before adding, "tell her to come see me, don't force her."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian takes another deep breath, "I have wonder at times what the recent history of our system would have been if she had been around during our troubles a few years ago. Would our medical community have come up with a cure themselves? Would the military have had a better response to the matters on Mergansar. She is a very valuable asset to the Union, which is part of why I did not make any moves against Malus when she introduced. I worry too about the sway he is seeming to have with her. But if she is looking for new glories that does not require siding with Malus. I suspect there would be little future in it for her if she did. And if he stirs up shit, that leaves possibilities for action against him. It would seem like the conflict is inevitable."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"She, like I, was indisposed during those troubles. Entirely different indispositions I assure you," Axel says, considering he was rotting away in a black ship floating prison. "She doesn't see her own worth unfortunately. If she goes along with Malus's plans, then I worry she's going to meet the same fate as he eventually will. It's imperative that you or I talk her out of it. If Malus acts against Malign, you know as well as I do that it will end poorly for him. As much as I insulted and taunted the man during our meeting, if he had any real power like Malign, I'd not be standing in front of you today. I'm not saying he's weak, but he is no match by himself against Malign, which makes it all the more curious of who got him out and why. Who does he have in his corner that we don't know about."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I had about much the same observation from him. I am sure if I had picked a fight with him, he could beat me. But he does seem to be in a weak position himself and he really could not furnish any great indication that he had some unique talent to bring to the table. And part of why I want to make sure Johanna is looped in is that she too should be more than a match for him." Crestian rubs his face for a moment, the metaphorical headache created by all of this was draining. "As for Lynae, I should hope between the two of us we could talk her down. I know she as a tendency to get friendly with all sides, and because of her unique talents that is okay to a point. But it would be hard to protect her if she becomes too invested in trying to shake things up. The crew of that freighter are just the latest casualties. And if there weren't wider considerations that would be enough to issue an arrest warrant for him."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I might have her do the forensics on the freighter, right now it's sitting in vacuumed off part of the hangar of the Retribution. Droids have been doing scans of it, but maybe getting her involved will keep her on our side of things," Axel says before polishing off the rest of his glass. He sets it down, not pouring another for now. "I think looping in Joh is the smartest thing at this time. Malign refuses to answer my requests for a meeting, or he's not getting messages which is quite possible. If he told that monster wookiee/rancor hybrid of his that he didn't want to be disturbed, then nothing will get through." Axel stands up. "I'll keep trying with Lynae, but do keep me abreast of any new developments with either Malus or others who got freed. I'm not completely certain at this moment that Malus was the primary objective. It's likely he was, but I cannot even find out who else was amongst the prisoners. Union intelligence might have better luck than ISB at this point, they're spread so damned thin and have been fractured for so long, they can't find their own ass in the dark if they used both hands."<br>

"Crestian, I don't think I need to explain how important it is that we keep this as hush hush as possible for the moment. I sent a message to the Moff council, and I instantly regretted that decision, knowing that corruption runs deep there, and loyalties are fickle at the best of times. We need to keep as much as we can as close to the vest as we can until we know what we're dealing with, or at least more than we know now. If word gets out about a bunch of political prisoners or Olum knows who else might have been on that station are roaming free, this could be catastrophic for all of us. I do appreciate the booze, you keep a nice stock. I should get back to the Retribution. If you speak with Joh, keep me apprised, and I'll do the same. Might be safest to use union military waves as opposed to Imperium. Hell, I didn't even know about that damned station until I got the intel from you, and that concerns me. Right now, I'm trying to find any more such facilities and determine what to do with them before there are further prison breaks."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian sets down his glass and stands, "On our end I have been trying to keep it as quiet as possible, but for a variety of reasons the senior figures in our intelligence service are looped. Only so much I can keep an eye on by myself. The wider story though has been that break out we have defence force and intelligence service both investigating leads on other prisoners, so enough people knowing at least to keep an eye out for the unusual. In any event we have enough forces spread out, should you need to borrow additional forces in your role as Inquisitor the Union should be able to fulfil its treaty obligations in that endeavour, and I will keep you looped in on any new developments on my end, particular with Lynae, and I will trust you to do the same."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For amount it would see that Crestian is preparing a handshake or salute or some gesture but instead adds one more bit of information, "Incidentally while it is largely the Caspian 4th Fleet that is in the field, as it is designed to be, you will find that CMS Kichnar has been pulled off of Defence Group Caspia and is operating down in the Karthakk Sector as part of our investigations. Its commanding officer Fleet Captain Vega Sevenna is a native to Maramere in the Karthakk system. Perhaps a trivial thing, but I figured she might have more local insight than our other ship captains, you may call on her and her ship if needed."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I'll loop her in on the military training exercise we'll be conducting there. I appreciate that. And, I will be calling upon her to hear what input she may have. I am not overly familiar with the system, there are so many and I am but one man." Axel looks to the exit, then back to Crestian. "I'll keep you informed, and feel free to loop in Johanna of anything you deem of importance." And with that, the man is off. He had a lot of work to do in the coming days, and having to coordinate things so some asshole like that IGN reporter with the lavish hairstyles and wardrobes can't figure out what's really happening.

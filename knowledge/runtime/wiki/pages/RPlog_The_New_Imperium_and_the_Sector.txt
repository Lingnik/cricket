   In the large hangar bay the bustle is at a minimum. No starfighters come and go and no preparations to leave Caspar space are underway at this time. Elana Tracer, representative of Imperator Tyler Damion, stands serenely dressed all in white looking quite the opposite of and Imperial Moff. Stormtroopers are present but stand off far on the right side so as not to intimidate. It was the friendly new Imperium after all, wasn't it? And Tracer is certainly a new face for it.

   With two civilian assistants in KDY-SFS caps stand behind Elana, ready to respond to their boss' needs. As the Prex's shuttle enters the bay, no dampening fields or other binding technology is employed. Everything is calm and peaceful as can be to make him feel welcomed. The woman, a small bacta patch at her hairline the only evidence of her battering during the earlier bombing, awaits her former fellow swooper's descent from his ship.

   The ramp to the Telgorn-class shuttle lowers and down it come Zeak Oppenhiemer dressed in his usual black, along with two of his "Sisters", a human pair, clearly from his often lampooned cybernetic cult based on the fact that their hair is shaved like Zeak's, and they have the same cybernetic rig. Both are dressed in matching long flowing tunics which go to roughly an inch above the ankle, somewhat form fitting on the top and loose on the bottom, not unlike Zeak's cassock, but cut for their figures. Close inspection would note they have the same cybernetic eye replacements as Zeak as well. The three walk in silence, but the rumor mill about the galaxy is that members of the cult communicate with each other via wireless technology. Today there is plenty of wireless chatter for the communications staff of the I2SD to try and decrypt should they choose -- between the three, between the three and the ship, and between the ship and Zeak's nearby cruiser, the 'Winking Monkey Lizard'.

   "Moff Tracer," Zeak calls out as he gets near the bottom. "Permission to come aboard."

   Tracer wasn't the Captain of this vessel, but she did command it in her purely governmental position. She was able to hold back the fire and fury that champed at the bit to "rescue" her from assailants that barely knew she was there. From her self-exile, Elana wasn't all that up on the current status of various cults that come and go in the galaxy. Even Tracer herself was once in one, when she didn't know who she was and Amadeus took advantage of that fact to employ her as an avenging angel in a TIE fighter. She had see a few flimsies with mocking cartoons exaggerating his cybernetic features. She steps forward, closing the distance between them for the most part. "Permission granted, Prex Oppenheimer," she smiles warmly. "And may I add you have my assurance that you and your companions are my guests today." She angles slightly towards the exit doors. "Would you like to accompany me to somewhere a bit more comfortable?" She turns while waiting for his reply and tells one of the young assistants behind her. "Please make sure conference room 2 is ready for us..." He nods and departs. The Moff-ness falls away, and instead here is Ellie, just a girl who likes to race and have fun. She gestures towards the way out of the hangar, beckoning for Zeak and friends to follow. "It's been such a long time, Zeak" she smiles. "I hope you're doing well?"

   "'Well' is not a word I'd use to describe my current situation," Zeak replies blandly as he and his begins to follow. "But I won't bore you with those details, I'm sure your people have briefed you on the chaos and uncertainty that has come from the Imperium's aggressive appearance."

   Tracer stops and turns back to look at Zeak squarely. "And I've also been briefed on the status of graft in the Sector and the Sector overstepping it's bounds by acquiring control over systems that the Sector had no business obtaining!" Her voice, terse, rises slightly and she cocks her pretty little head up at Oppenheimer. "I am your one chance to get the best deal and keep all this," she gestures, palm flat, in front of Zeak's face. "On your shoulders." She folds her hands in front of her and turns to walk again.

   "I see," Zeak replies as he stops walking, this will likely be a very short meeting. "As you know, the Authority, by its charter is run by a board -- of which I am only one voting member. Is there a proposal you would like me to take to the DIREX Board on your behalf?"

   "Very well." Her short strides stop again and she turns to face her guest. "Here are the Imperator's stances. Thel's contracts are no longer valid. Break up your mega conglomerates. That kind of consolidation of financial power leaves the Direx in control of too many creds flowing into the Sector. Second, you will personally stop skimming profits into your personal coffers." Tracer softens a bit. "Work with us. Stop amassing military strength in the Tingel until a new contract is written up and approved by the Direx."

   "Perhaps I'm confused Moff Tracer," Zeak replies. "Does the Imperium see it self as the same entity as Emperor Vadim's Empire, or is it a new animal?"

   "It is new. Is that a bad thing?" Tracer asks. "Would I have worked for Vadim's regime willingly? Think about it." She draws in a deep breath. "New or not, Damion now wields the power in the vacuum that was left. The same systems that bent the knee to Valak, Osbourne, Vadim, Thel...have fallen in line. But..." a hopeful gleam twinkles in Elana's clear blue eyes. "With more autonomy. Peace is the goal."

   "That poses a problem Moff Tracer," Zeak replies. "See the Authority Charter was promulgated by Emperor Vadim - or at least his people, I can't claim to have been there when it happened. If you are a new animal then we have no relationship as of yet. You have made demands and threatened me and the Authority today, but you haven't yet proposed anything. Do you have a proposal?"

   "Damion wants you, and the Sector, to come to the table to negotiate a new charter," Tracer purses her lips, trying to navigate her new role. "Partner with us, don't oppose us." She takes a step toward Zeak, looking up at him with a smile "Tell me what you want and I'll relay your desires..."

   "A good first start would be to stop aggressively scouting the systems currently administered by the Authority in the Galactic south. Instability is bad for business." Zeak replies. "Secondly, you could show us you respect the Authority's property rights, including our partial ownership of Kuat Drive Yards-Sienar Fleet Systems. As you know, we played no role in your appointment -- something Vadim would have left entirely to us." Then more exacting, choosing his words more carefully, "That and the threats like you opened with need to stop. If you want to partner, that is how you influence the DIREX. That and send a delegation to Etti IV."

   Elana takes a measured step back and let's Zeak's berating of her approach wash over her. "I apologize if I came on too strongly," she acquiesces. "I am still a bit...unhappy...about nearly being blown up." Her feet are heavily planted as she goes on. "Kalla has been my home for the past decade. More! I lived on Etti as well. I want to help the Sector as much as I can. But I did come required to relay certain points that can only be softened so much. I want to be clear. Work with me and I'll bring the best possible proposal I can. And I apologize about how what I'm going to say next may come across. If you don't come to the table, the Sector will be sent more...aggressive...negotiators. Now that we've spoken I feel that I can indeed help the Sector weather this change in regime well...and profitably."

   "I'll relay the message," Zeak replies mater-of-factly. "However, the DIREX will want to speak to someone empowered to negotiate details with -- the devil is in these details is he not? On another note, if I might ask a more personal question -- I recently saw my step-son on some holo-footage in a Moff's uniform. I'm guessing Johanna is involved in the Imperium as well -- why not send someone closer to me to negotiate?"

   Tracer raises a brow, seeming surprised that any relationship existed between the Prex and Jedi. "I will not try to say what was in the Imperator's thoughts, however he knows my...affections...for the Sector and he thought I would be a friendly face. I still have a home there, after all." She lets that linger for a moment, her stake in keeping Kalla safe being quite high. "I can't speak to...Johanna's position or involvement in the Imperium. She was not happy her boy had been brought into the regime. Just as I wasn't happy my son had found mentorship there as well."

   "As to Moff te Daanan I imagine he has his hands full with the O'Paal and Byss sectors. I, however, may continue to be involved in negotiations with the Sector, with the close relationship between KDY-SFS and the Sector, and I hope you know you won't find anyone else who will go to bat for you the way I will."

   "I see," Zeak replies. "I appreciate the candid response. Can I tell the DIREX to expect a peaceful delegation from the Imperium. One empowered to negotiate, and one that isn't armed to the teeth?"

Tracer looks towards the very high ceiling and sighs. "If I'd been allowed, this is not how I would've come to you. I will get you your delegation. You have my word." Elana gestures towards Zeak's shuttle that they haven't gotten very far from. "I appreciate your trust in coming to me here as it is. I'll see you to your ship, then?"

   "Yes, I think that may be best," Zeak replies. "And for what it is worth, I do appreciate your position, as well as your hospitality. I'm sure the DIREX will be most interested to speak to your delegation."

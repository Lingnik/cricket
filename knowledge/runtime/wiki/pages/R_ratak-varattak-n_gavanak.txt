'''r'ratak-varattak-n'gavanak''' ([[:starwars:44 BBY/Legends|44 BBY]] - [[34 ABY]]) was the [[Prelate]] of the [[Xi Char]]. He began his career at [[Haor Chall Engineering]] during the twilight years of the corporation prior to the Clone Wars. Prior to the war he was involved in the construction of 
[[:starwars:Trade Federation droid bomber|Trade Federation Droid Bombers]]. Later as an Acolyte during the war he served as a supervisor on the construction of [[:starwars:Maxillipede shuttle/Legends|Maxillipede Shuttles]] at the [[Cathedral Factory]] of Xi Chall.

During the war, the Republic sent an army of clone troopers led by [[Jedi Order|Jedi]] Knights to destroy the factories on [[Charros IV]], with what remained of Haor Chall Engineering being absorbed by their mortal enemies [[Sienar Fleet Systems]]. r'ratak would take a prominent role in the reconstruction of the Cathedral Factory of Xi Chall eventually becoming its [[Dean]]. This would also mark a shift in his work towards building materials rather than finished goods.

In the [[Conclave]] of [[26 ABY]] Dean r'ratak was elected as the Prelate of the Xi Char. With their designs and facilities largely still under the control of Sienar Fleet Systems, the Xi Char did their best to scrape by with what industry they could. Their fortunes would begin to change in [[29 ABY]] with the emergence of a new [[Caspian Union]] and the advent of the [[Imperium]]. [[Caspian Union|Caspian]] [[Presav]] [[Crestian Tarasar]] would immediately see the potential for change on [[Charros IV]], starting three-way negotiations with the aim of restoring Xi Char control of their designs and facilities and allowing Charros IV to seceded from the Imperium to join the Caspian Union.

The negotiations were led by Presav Tarasar, [[Grand Moff]] [[Elana Tracer]] representing the Imperium and [[Kuat Drive Yards-Sienar Fleet Systems]], and Dean [[ak]] representing the Xi Char. All sides quickly agreed to Tarasar's plan in principle with most of the negotiating efforts concerned with the logistics. Tarasar would also make an agreement Prelate r'ratak to hire Xi Char engineers to work with Union in the efforts to recommission and refit the [[Daring-class Battlecruiser|Daring-Class Battlecruisers]].

In early [[30 ABY]], Tarasar, Tracer, and Prelate r'ratak-varattak-n'gavanak would sign the final treaty repatriating Xi Char designs and facilities fully back to the Xi Char and bringing Charros IV out of the Imperium and into the Caspian Union. r'ratak would then oversee the reformation of Haor Chall Engineering serving as it Chief Executive Officer. He would also represent Xi Chall in the [[Union Council]] under the banner of the [[Lijsttrekker]] party. His role leading Haor Chall Engineering would also lead r'ratak to hold the commission of a [[Vice Admiral]] in the [[Caspian Navy]].

In his final years, r'ratak would develop a new alloy containing [[:starwars:acretron|acretron]], [[oluminia]], and [[tungren]] among other elements. The alloy would be dubbed [[varattakite]] in his honour after his passing. Prelate r'ratak-varattak-n'gavanak passed in [[34 ABY]] with the Union Council passing a resolution declaring a Union-wide week of mourning. The funerary ceremonies for the Prelate's passing drew dignitaries from throughout the Union and wider galaxy, and along with the subsequent conclave to replace him, were covered extensively by [[InterGalactic News|IGN]]. r'ratak would be succeeded as both Prelate and Councillor of Xi Chall by Inquisitor [[stahanak]].

The [[Prelate r'ratak-varattak-n'gavanak Memorial Art Gallery]] in Xi Xin is named in his honour.

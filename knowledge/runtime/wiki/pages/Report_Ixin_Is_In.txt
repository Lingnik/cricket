A tall golden-skinned female, economist Professor [[Brackett Benduday]], can be seen sitting behind an anchor's desk in a studio. To her left is Professor Clark Rickart of the [[Plaxton]] Polling Institute.

"This is Brackett Benduday back to you with more news from the Caspar Sector. With me tonight is Professor Clark Rickart, Director of the Plaxton Polling Institute. Clark I understand that the Plaxton Polling Institute has only recently resumed operations correct?"

"That is correct Brackett. I was in fact only contacted last week by the Mid Rim Political Science Association to be told that a grant request we had submitted last month has been approved. Along with other funding sources the MRPSA grant put us over the threshold needed to be able to rehire our staff finally. We officially reopened today, so you could say I am skipping out on work to be here tonight."

Brackett smirks at Clark's joke, remarking, "I will be happy to sign your hall pass," in reply. After a moment she gets back to the news. "Earlier today representatives of the [[Caspian Union]] and the Republic of [[Ixin]] met at Udalooy Palace on Ixin to sign a treaty. Effective at midnight local time the Republic of Ixin ceases as an independent power returning to the Caspian fold; councillors from Feyenoor and Udalooy join the [[Union Council]], and the naval vessels that had been awarded to Ixin return to the authority of the [[Caspian Navy]]."

The screen turns briefly to images of the signing ceremony at the palace with Councillor Lilabet Bint of [[Mikassa]] signing for the Caspian Union and Prince Abdulay II signing on behalf of the Republic of Ixin.

The screen returns to Brackett who relates, "With the return of Ixin, this leaves only [[Mergansar]] and the [[Krittain|Krittain system]] as the only major elements of the [[Caspian Democratic Union]] that have not become part of the new Caspian Union." She turns to Clark, "Obviously the situation with Mergansar is rather complicated, but both [[Kasputin Yar]] and [[Rauther]] have been back for nearly a month, why has Ixin taken longer, and can we expect the Krit Union to join the reunification effort and if so when?"

"Ixin is a pretty unique case. It occupied one of the twelve senatorial provinces in the old system with its two major cities Feyenoor and Udalooy having assembly seats. That being said Ixin was largely forgotten beyond this. It often left off maps, rarely made mention of by the leaders of the other worlds, to my knowledge none of the naval vessels bore names related to Ixin. In fact in spite of the fact Mikassa had no representation at all in the old system it actually had a much larger footprint in the old CDU. Itself additionally ironic give a significant minority of immigrants from Ixin on Mikassa.

It should also be noted that prior to the pogroms of Mergansar, Ixin was the most culturally homogenous member world of the CDU. They also did not have a strong history of democratic rule prior to joining the CDU. Their original membership was driven more by stellar proximity than cultural affinity. They have also tended to have a suspicion of the [[Yari|Yaries]]. The priority the Tarasar administration placed on gaining Kasputin Yar and increasing their representation from three cities having seats in the old Union Assembly to now four having seats on the new Union Council did not lend itself to an enthusiastic reaction in some segments of the population on Ixin. Overall they have just been very ambivalent about the whole matter.

That said Ixin has taken in roughly ten million refugees from Mergansar last year and it has slowly had an impact on the culture and consciousness there. It is also interesting to see that rather than [[Presav]] [[Crestian Tarasar|Tarasar]] or Vice Presav Mos signing for the Caspian Union as one might normally expect the treaty was signed by Councillor Bint of Mikassa. While some might take this as representing a slight from the Tarasar administration most of us are taking it in a different way. The Ixin connexion to Mikassa itself aside, the fact that the Councillor of a world that had no representation in the old system representing it here seems to be a signal to Ixin that it too can expect to see its profile be elevated in the new Caspian Union."

"And the Krits?"

"The Krits are in the unique position compared to the other systems in the Caspar Sector of being largely self-sufficient. Even in the days of the CDU their contributions to the Navy retained a special designation indicating the ship in question was Krittish and the Marines contained some Krittish only units. The popular narrative among some Caspian politicians is to credit or blame the Krits for the CDU's history of neutrality in galactic affairs. It should be noted that at the time of the Sariphage and pogroms on Mergansar, the one surviving [[Sar Admiral]] [[Prae Kichakressa]] was from [[Krittain Major]] and she was killed in battle fighting now Presav....and Sar Admiral Tarasar when he attempted to pacify the [[Olumi Osahn]] cultists on Mergansar. 

In spite of Tarasar's subsequent rehabilitation on Caspar, there is a significant segment that still views him as a traitor for firing on Caspian vessels and that runs much stronger in the Krittain system than most anywhere else save for Mergansar itself. Naturally for Tarasar's part the fact that Caspian ships not only stood by while these pogroms occurred but attack him when he was trying to stop them which he claims was done under the blessing of that said same Sar Admiral....as much as he has tried to make peace within the Navy now that he has taken over he does not shy away from calling his predecessor a traitor and he has made little public effort to win over the Krits.

I think in the end we will see the Krits returning to the Caspian fold. I think the ties of many years will win over against the problems of the last couple. But I suspect that it will ultimately occur in one of two ways. Either Caspar and Krittain will set aside their differences and in doing so be able to bring enough pressure against Mergansar to achieve its effective capitulation. Alternately if the matter on Mergansar resolves first, it will place a pressure on the Krits to rejoin and not remain the only hold out. Either way eventual reunification seems more likely than long term independence or joining with another power, but only time will tell."

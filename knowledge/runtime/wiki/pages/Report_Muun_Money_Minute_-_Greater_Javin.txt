The screen shows the lobby of the Yarith Bespin, the most-class hotel in [[Cloud City]]. The logo for [[InterGalactic News]] and the [[Muun Money Minute]] financial programme feature prominently in the middle of the screen before shifting to smaller versions at the bottom. In their place two fashionably dressed figures can be clearly seen now, a golden-skinned woman with a microphone and a rosy-skinned woman with her arm in a sling.

After a moment the golden-skinned woman speaks, "This is the Muun Money Minute coming to you live from the Yarith Bespin for a segment on the Greater Javin region sponsored by our friends at [[Biscuit Baron]]. I am your host Professor [[Brackett Benduday]] and with me is Cloud City Baroness-Administrator [[Quinne Stardust Rose]]."

Quinne is a consummate professional when it comes to public appearances and she appears very much at ease in front of the camera. Her focus switches from the camera to the Professor and back again until she is introduced. She gives a little wave and looks cutely at the camera, wiggling her right-hand fingers in an adorable wave as she says, "Hi y'all!" The rosy woman smiles widely and looks back at the reporter. "Thanks for having me on, Professor Brackett."

"It is a pleasure to have you with us Miss Quinne." The anchor and economist use the less formal appellation, matching her guest. "Not far from us in the ballroom of this very hotel during the first annual Bespin Miner's Dinner terrorists attack the gathering, along with coordinated attacks including destroying the city's main tibanna refinery and grievously wounding then city leader [[Johanna Siri te Danaan]]. In the immediate aftermath of the attack Miss Quinne, the manager of this hotel and a performer of renown was thrust into the position of acting Baroness-Administrator. Here we are two years later, the promotion permanent, the refinery rebuilt, and perhaps most surprising, in spite of limited conflict in the galaxy in these past two years, tibanna prices continue to climb."

Brackett then turns from looking at the camera to looking at her guest, "An attack such as the one Cloud City suffered easily had the potential to cripple the economy here, yet in spite of it all Cloud City is managing to thrive. I think what our viewers would like to know is how you have been able to oversee such an economic miracle? As far as I understand it, you had never been in such an extensive administrative position before this?"

"That was a terrible thing that happened, very tragic." Quinne replies with a serious expression. "But Johanna was very supportive of our miners and their families, even after her injury. It was important to the community to get the refinery up and running as soon as possible as so many livelihoods in Cloud City depend on the industry here. And with growth here in the city along with some strategic investing, not to mention the rise in demand for our product, we managed to pull out of the red real quick-like." Quinne smiles a bit, demuring with a little dip of her chin. "It's all been quite a change for me, ma'am. But I had a lot of help. Besides Joh, I've had several good mentors - like [[Crestian Tarasar]], for example. I mean, he runs an entire system so he was a really, really big help. And really, it's just a bigger show here compared to what I was doing before. All the same parts needing to come together to make things work, only sized up!"

Brackett had not intended to bring up Crestian Tarasar herself, but since Quinne had it seemed like a natural segue point. "You mentioned [[Caspian Union]] [[Presav]] Crestian Tarasar. Who leads a power that, if I might make a quick correction, includes several inhabited systems. IGN reported two years ago that after the attack the Caspian dispatched engineers from [[SubPro]] to assist the miners here in the recovery. Clearly Cloud City and its citizens can count on some very helpful friends. Now we have talked about refinery operations, but what about Cloud City itself. The Greater Javin Region is on the Outer Rim, and while the [[Corellian Trade Spine]] goes through the region, [[Bespin]] itself is not on it. Yet, Cloud City manages to not only have a very successful tibanna operations but is also known as something of a tourist hot spot. What do you think brings people to Cloud City in spite of its remote location?"

Now we're in Quinne's wheelhouse. Promotion! Her blue curls bounce with her excited head bobs as she speaks, her one good arm doing its best to 'talk' along with her on its own being down its partner as she smiles brightly. "Well, ma'am, I like to entertain people. It's always been my calling. So whether you want to see Galaxy-Class entertainment, try your luck at one of our many casinos, or just relax in the Yarith's Day Spa, it's all right here. Also, we have our new Cultural Festival coming up which will double the shopping and dining available. Bespin is for Everyone." Her hand moves across her face, palm forward, like a shooting star. "And I'm so glad we were able to host the Greater Javin Medical Society conference. Your viewers should know that Cloud City is ready and waiting to spice up your conventions and trade shows!"

"And that brings us to the last point on this part of the segment. The Greater Javin Medical Society annual conference that had been scheduled for Nevarro has announced a last minute relocation here to Cloud City after recent reports of piracy on the Nevarro Spur has caused a travel warning to be issued for Nevarro itself. Conference organisers have indicated the change will mean some of the scheduled presentations will not be able to occur this year, but are confident of being able to fill their programme with some last minute replacements. IGN's own [[Jordaan Mag]] will have more on that later, but for now that is all the time we have for the Muun Money Minute. My thanks again to Cloud City Baroness-Administrator Quinne Stardust Rose. This is Brackett Benduday signing off."

Written Statement from [[Caspian Union|Caspian]] [[Presav]] [[Crestian Tarasar]] on the recent events in the [[:starwars:Karthakk system|Karthakk System]]:

"The [[Caspian Union]] has purposely kept quiet on the recent incident in the [[:starwars:Karthakk system|Karthakk System]] as it is sensitive security issue for allies in the [[Imperium]]. Now that the Imperium has released a statement about the incident, the Union wishes to make a brief statement.

We have lived in a era of relative peace and stability these past five years. Our mutual protection pact with the Imperium has been an important component in that peace and stability. The Imperium and Union support each other with exchanges intelligence, materiels, personnel, and technology. The trade between our two powers significant and has been particularly beneficial to our economy.

The lingering shadows of the regimes of past still infect our present as has been seen by the recent incident in the Karthakk System. In accordance with the terms of our [[Imperium-Caspian Mutual Protection Pact (29 ABY)|mutual protection pact]], our [[Constable|constabulary]], [[Caspian Defence Force|defence force]], and [[Caspian Intelligence Service|intelligence service]] have been cooperating with our allies in the Imperium to investigate the recent security breach and apprehend those responsible and the criminals they allowed to escape. I have personally been in regular contact with Grand Inquisitor [[Axel Vichten]] concerning this situation.

Unfortunately owing to the sensitivity of these operations and the safety of all personnel involved, the Union cannot divulge at this time more details of the investigation nor what role Union assets are playing in it.

This said we have chosen to release a statement to reiterate to all concerned that bounties recently placed by the Imperium constitute as valid judicial orders as far as the Union is concerned and should the opportunity present itself Union forces are ordered to apprehend these individuals for extradition back to the Imperium. This is the same courtesy the Imperium extends to us should those wanted for crimes in the Union escape our jurisdiction.

The Union Council is closely monitoring this situation and should the opportunity to release more details without undermining the work of our personnel in the field present itself, my office will release such details."

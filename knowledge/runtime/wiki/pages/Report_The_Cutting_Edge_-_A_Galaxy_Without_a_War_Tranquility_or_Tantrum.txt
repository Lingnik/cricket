Location: [[InterGalactic News|IG News]] Studio, [[Corporate Sector Authority|Corporate Sector]] Capital, [[Etti IV]]

The holofeed snapped into crisp clarity with all the signature opulence of [[The Cutting Edge]]. Behind [[Thurston Unger]] stretched the glittering skyline of [[Etti IV]]: a gleaming hyper-capitalist dreamscape of chrome spires, neon reflections, and slow-rotating corporate sigils casting subtle shadows across an evening sky.

The studio itself was, as always, immaculate-mirrored floor, illuminated archways, and a backdrop holoscreen set to gently cycle through scenes of galactic unrest in elegant slow-motion. And at the center, seated in a high-backed [[Transparisteel|transparisteel]] chair with crimson trim and tasteful lighting that hit all the right cheekbone angles-Thurston Unger. Tonight's ensemble? A tailored obsidian suit laced with silver-threaded [[Corellia|Corellian]] brocade, high-necked with a subtle sparkle. His hair was sculpted to such pristine perfection it might violate trade regulations on certain Mid Rim worlds.

He smiled. Razor-sharp. Perfectly rehearsed. "Good evening, my starlit truth-seekers. I come to you live from the studio on glorious, opulent Etti IV-the only world in the galaxy where a boardroom coup is considered foreplay."

The holoscreen behind him cut to footage of recent riots on [[Dac|Mon Calamari]], then segued to [[Chandrila|Chandrila's]] shuttered Senate rotunda."And yet, as I sip my hand-blended Vinsothian pear tonic and marvel at the dazzling civility of this gilded corporate playground, I can't help but wonder: has the galaxy simply lost its damned mind?"

He leaned forward, elbows on the desk, his voice dropping into velvet gravity. "I mean really, darlings, Mon Calamari is on the verge of secession, Chandrila is paralyzed by scandal and infighting, the [[Imperium]] can't keep its high-security prisoners inside their prisons, and the [[:starwars:Karthakk system|Karthakk system]] is a disaster cloaked in bureaucratic silence and Imperium naval blockades."

The screen behind him shifted again-flashes of Karthakk system and the number of warships floating in space, followed by an audio overlay of New Republic press briefings spiraling into non-answers. "Once upon a time, we had villains. [[Galactic Empire|Empires]]. [[Sith Order|Sith]]. Clear lines. It was all very theatrical. There were sabers and speeches, and occasionally someone would blow up a moon. Order, you see-even if it wore a cape and choked its subordinates." 

Thurston held up one manicured finger. "But now? Now we have... chaos. Not the sexy kind with explosions and betrayal-oh no. This is the petty kind. The bureaucratic kind. Everyone squabbling, sabotaging, stabbing each other in the back with memos and manifestos."

He stood, walking slowly across the studio as the camera followed, gliding beside him like a ballroom partner. "Maybe the war didn't end. Maybe it just went feral. No longer centralized, just fractured into a thousand tiny crises. Everyone scrambling for relevance in the great post-conflict vacuum." He turned to face the camera, a half-smile playing across his lips. "Or maybe-just maybe-the people of the galaxy don't know how to function without fear. Without fire. Without some grand narrative to cling to."

A new backdrop emerged: the [[New Republic Senate|Senate]], crumbling under gridlock. Mon Cala's [[Mon Calamari Shipyards|shipyards]]. A shadowy figure boarding a transport with a blurred-out face. The former [[Galactic Emperor|Emperor]], perhaps? "The galaxy was addicted to conflict. And now that the main course is gone? Everyone's gnawing on the furniture." He stopped, hands clasped behind his back, voice cooling to an elegant whisper. "And if you think the boardrooms of Etti IV are immune, my dear sweets, let me remind you: the more stable a regime looks, the more delicious the fall tends to be."

He faced the camera dead-on, gaze gleaming with polished intrigue. "So what now? Do we find a new enemy? A new purpose? Or will we let the whole shimmering, crumbling mess collapse under the weight of its own indecision?" He raised one brow. "I don't pretend to know the answer. I simply promise to look fabulous while asking the question."

The outro shimmered across the screen in signature gilt-lettered flair:

THE CUTTING EDGE

Where fashion meets fallout.

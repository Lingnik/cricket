The holowall glimmers with cascading nebulae and a muted replay of the Selene briefing. A slowly looping gif of Brix Malloran's "Did you just ask that?" moment floats overhead. The camera sinks toward the sunken velvet conversation pit, where [[Thurston Unger]] reclines in a crushed starlight robe, rings clinking softly against the edge of a cut-crystal glass.

Surrounding him in scandalous formation:
Pearletta, glowing soft-pink, perched at his right like a perfectly poised guillotine.
Tarnish LaRue, lounging backward on a chaise with a dimmer remote in hand, already making the lighting moodier.
Vel Veela, trailing notes of ambient melancholy like a sentient theremin.
Vice-Royce, sipping something clear, expensive, and amoral.
Miss Conduct, data spike extended, already logging receipts.
Blynk, half under a table, projecting emoji-laced subtitles into the air.

Thurston stares into his glass like it holds the fate of Brix Malloran's career. "Tell me, my radiant gallery of unapologetic metal and menace-did anyone else feel the sudden chill when Zephara Val'Riin paused? I swear to the Seven Moons of [[:starwars:Sev Tok|Sev Tok]], my left eyelash curled backward in terror."

PEARLETTA (without looking up): "She paused long enough for a star to die. Then cut his relevance in half with twelve words."

HOLOCLIP PLAYS
"...I find the tabloid mind grotesquely misaligned."

TARNISH LARUE (dim lights 2, eye roll 110): "She didn't speak. She issued a verdict. Honestly, I haven't seen a silence that violent since the [[Mon Calamari]] ambassador fainted during the [[Chandrila|Chandrilan]] perfume scandal."

Thurston is quick, and as deadpan as possible. "May we never smell Gardenia Arkanis again."

VEL VEELA (singing softly into her flute mic): o/~"Gro-tesque-lyyyyy... mis-a-ligned... and now his ratings have declined..." o/~

MISS CONDUCT (without emotion): "I've prepared three remixes. One with violin. One with dramatic pacing. And one entirely in slow-motion eyebrow movement."

VICE-ROYCE (leaning back, flawless): "She did what the [[New Republic|Republic]] hasn't done in years-acted with precision. Shifted focus from tabloid nonsense to galactic crisis. All while looking like a vengeful statue."

BLYNK (chirps, launching a holo-emoji sequence):(Translation: Cold. Ice. Grave. Mic. Boom.)

Thurston sighs, steepling his fingers like a villain in a kaftan. "You all know how I feel about Zephara. She speaks in death warrants. She smells like freshly printed censorship. And she personally had me escorted from Selene's press gallery for asking if the Regent moisturizes."

TARNISH (grins): "Which he does. Aggressively."

PEARLETTA (checking the time with elegance): "But credit is due. She didn't just dismiss him-she reconstructed public focus in one rhetorical breath."

MISS CONDUCT (nodding): "She now holds the galactic record for longest unbroken verbal cut. Brix Malloran has been officially declared an archaeological site."

VEL VEELA (dramatic gasp): o/~"I wept for the fool... but sang for the frost..." o/~

Thurston rises slowly, letting his robe catch the perfect amount of cheekbone light. "Let it be known: I, Thurston Unger, Prince of Prose and Baron of Broadcast, do hereby acknowledge that-on this singular, icy, devastating occasion-Zephara Val'Riin did the galaxy a service."

"She did not flinch. She did not scold. She exterminated a distraction and turned famine, blackout, and political instability into a sobering call for unity. And then, like all terrifying women of true consequence... she ended questions entirely."

BLYNK (flickering subtitles appear in glitter font above the bar): "She said: 'You may all go now.' And the galaxy obeyed."

VICE-ROYCE (clinks glass against Thurston's): "To the dagger in a silk sheath."

PEARLETTA (to the camera, smooth as gloss): "To the frost that reminds us: just because the [[Galactic Empire|Empire]] fell... doesn't mean diplomacy can't still kill."

OUTRO: HOLOWALL transitions to soft-focus montage of Val'Riin quotes, ending with "Grotesquely Misaligned" in imperial serif. Segment tagline appears beneath.

"[[The Cutting Edge|THE CUTTING EDGE]]: Where Public Relations Go to Die Beautifully."
Credits roll over Vel Veela's soft vocal reprise as Pearletta dims the lights to "Public Humiliation Blue." Thurston reclines again, utterly satisfied.

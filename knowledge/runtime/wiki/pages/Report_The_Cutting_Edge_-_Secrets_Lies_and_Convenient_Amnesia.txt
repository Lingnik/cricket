[[The Cutting Edge]] with [[Thurston Unger]]
Secrets, Lies, and Convenient Amnesia-The [[Imperium|Imperium's]] [[:starwars:Karthakk system|Karthakk]] Problem

The studio lights come up, casting a golden sheen over the impeccably dressed and ever-dramatic [[Thurston Unger]], who stands in front of a massive holo-screen displaying clips from the [[Imperium|Imperium's]] latest press conference. His silver-sequined jacket glitters under the lights, the pink streak in his hair catching just the right angles as he steps toward the camera with a deep, exasperated sigh.

"Darlings, I have covered some spectacularly bad press conferences in my time. We've seen bureaucratic meltdown, awkward silences, and outright denial of the obvious. But this? This one belongs in a museum dedicated to half-truths and misdirection."

The screen behind him flickers, playing footage of Grand Inquisitor [[Axel Vichten]], looking every bit the part of a man who does not want to be answering questions. The bounty list appears beside Thurston as he gestures dramatically to it.

"Now, my loves, let's talk about this list, shall we? Because something stinks, and it's not my cologne-because my cologne is fabulous. No, this is the unmistakable stench of cover-up with a hint of 'we didn't think anyone would check our math.'"

He turns, pointing to the name with the highest bounty on the list. "[[Lorn Rhys]], a.k.a. Darth Malus-former [[Galactic Emperor|Emperor]] of the [[Galactic Empire]]. That's right, my dears, not just some [[Warlord|warlord]], not some high-ranking officer-the Emperor. And you know what the history books say? That he's been dead for more than two decades."

The footage rewinds, showing Axel brushing off the press with a sharp glare when asked about the connection between the escape and the Imperium's internal shake-ups. "Oh, but it gets better! Because every name on this list is ancient history! These aren't criminals who went rogue last month or even last year-these are ghosts of a bygone era! The last known records of any of these individuals date back more than a decade ago." 

Thurston raises an eyebrow, mockingly tapping his chin. "So, what are our possibilities here? Let's break it down. Option one: The Imperium has been secretly keeping these people in stasis for who-knows-how-long in a shadowy facility that technically shouldn't exist. Why? Who knows! Maybe they were collecting Imperial relics like rare art pieces. 'Oh, this war criminal is from the late Empire-very exclusive.'"

He flicks his wrist as if brushing that idea away. "Option two: The Imperium just so happened to keep perfectly preserved ten-year-old holo-images of all these criminals and slapped them on a bounty list like it's not the most suspicious thing in the galaxy. Because obviously, all these people look exactly the same as they did a decade ago. I'm sure they haven't aged a day. How convenient."

The footage shifts to Axel issuing his warning about Imperium law and storming off stage. "And let's not forget the Grand Inquisitor's grand exit-nothing says 'I have nothing to hide' quite like cutting a press conference short the moment questions get inconvenient."

Thurston leans on the podium, smirking at the camera. "Look, I know the Imperium doesn't like answering questions-it's almost like an allergy for them. But if they thought we wouldn't notice that they were holding an entire who's who of bygone terror in a secret prison for years, possibly even decades? They must think we were all born yesterday."

He straightens up, clasping his hands together. "So, my darlings, we are left with one conclusion: either this is the single biggest failure of information security in modern history, or there is something much, much deeper happening within the Imperium-something they really don't want the rest of the galaxy poking at."

Thurston winks at the camera. "And we will be poking, my loves. Because this? This is far from over."

The show cuts to a [[Biscuit Baron]] commercial, but not before one final shot of Axel Vichten's irritated face, frozen mid-glare as if he can already hear Thurston's mocking laughter in the distance.

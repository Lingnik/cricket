Broadcast from the [[InterGalactic News|IG News]] Crystal Studio on [[Etti IV]]

The studio lights dim slightly as a muted holo of [[Chandrila]] flickers in the background-fires, protests, and blurred footage of Senate walkouts. [[Thurston Unger]], polished and poised, turns slowly toward the camera, a look of "can you believe this?" already dancing on his face. "The unrest on Chandrila continues to burn like a bad opera's encore. It's loud, emotional, and just a touch overwrought. And while [[Senator]] Tarnas continues to issue flat denials of external manipulation, one can't help but? speculate."

A beat. The holo behind him shifts: an image of [[Crestian Tarasar]], [[Presav]] of the [[Caspian Union]], addressing a delegation on [[Mergansar]], superimposed with archival footage of him with Grand Inquisitor [[Axel Vichten]], the two shaking hands beneath the banners of the [[Imperium]].

"Crestian Tarasar-friend to everyone, including the New Republic and the Imperium, or at least that's the line his people like to spin. But when you hold the title of [[Moff]] in the Imperium while also leading a so-called independent Union, people begin to wonder where your loyalties truly lie."

The studio tone drops ever so slightly as Thurston's voice takes on a lower, more theatrical edge.

"And then there's Axel Vichten. Grand Inquisitor. Enforcer of Imperial doctrine. And-if the reports are to be believed-a adopted son of [[Noques Rasala]], Caspar, the cultural and religious cradle of the Caspian Union. Also, our investigators have found out that the Inquisitor also holds a flag officer rank within the Caspian Union. There's too much to not raise eyebrows."

Another holo flashes briefly: an artist's depiction of a darkened cathedral on Caspar, hooded figures in a long procession-no faces, no names. The image vanishes just as quickly as it appeared. "Now, I won't say the words [[Olumi Osahn]] out loud. Certainly not twice. But for those with long memories and longer data vaults, that name might still ring a bell-old accusations, old scars, and whispers of past affiliations that polite society would rather forget."

He smiles thinly, raising an eyebrow. "No proof, of course. No hard evidence. And I'm not accusing anyone of anything. But let's just say that the Grand Inquisitor's Caspian citizenship, combined with his long-standing ties to Tarasar, does make for? interesting viewing."

The screen behind him lights up with a deliberately convoluted conspiracy chart-string lines, blurry photos, and "?" symbols scattered across glowing portraits of Crestian and Axel. "Could the Chandrilan unrest be entirely organic? A local crisis of confidence? Or are we seeing the subtle hand of influence from far-off Caspar, directed by two men who know each other very well and whose philosophies-if not stated outright-may align more than either of them would care to admit?"

A deep breath. The lights return to full brightness, the chart fading into the golden logo of The Cutting Edge. "Again-just speculation. Just a theory. But I've always believed where there's smoke, there's probably a Caspian coal fire burning in the dark."

He gives a smooth half-smile to the camera. "I'm Thurston Unger. Stay curious, stay sharp, and-most importantly-stay watching my darlings."

{{IGNpost|author=[[Thurston Unger]]|date=[[34 ABY]]|topic=Political News|anchors=[[Thurston Unger]]|headline=The Cutting Edge - The Republic Cracks Again?|synopsis=[[Thurston Unger]] reports about the official response to the protests in [[Hanna City]] on [[Chandrila]].}}

[[The Cutting Edge]] intro fades, and the screen transitions to a dazzling, neon-lit studio. [[Thurston Unger]] stands center stage, resplendent in a fitted emerald-green suit with gold embroidery, a cape draped dramatically over one shoulder. His ever-present charm is dialed up to full wattage as he grins at the camera, arms outstretched.

"My darlings, my lovelies, my insatiable connoisseurs of truth, have I got a treat for you tonight! Because while the [[New Republic]] wants you to believe all is calm and orderly, the reality, my dears, is that their once-proud house is on fire. And today, we turn our attention to the latest world getting ready to kick the Republic to the curb? [[Chandrila]]!"

Behind him, the massive holoscreen displays live footage from [[Hanna City]], Chandrila's capital. Thousands of people flood the streets, waving banners and chanting slogans. Riot police stand shoulder to shoulder, tension crackling like static in the air.

"Ah, Chandrila. The model of democracy. The birthplace of so many of the Republic's grand ideals. And yet, what do we have here? Outrage. Fury. Protesters chanting for secession." He gasps theatrically. "Say it isn't so!"

The feed cuts to a clip of a flustered Chandrilan [[senator]] at a press conference, her voice strained as she speaks into a cluster of microphones.

'The Republic remains committed to the people of Chandrila. We urge patience as we work through these difficult times. Stability must be preserved.'

Thurston places a hand over his heart, his expression dripping with exaggerated sympathy. "Oh, my dears, the Republic is committed to you. So committed, in fact, that they're ignoring your cries for help! Again! Patience, stability? Why, those are just lovely words that mean absolutely nothing when your government has been caught with its hands in the corruption cookie jar."

The screen shifts to new footage-riot troopers shoving protesters back, sonic rounds dispersing crowds. A family-owned café is caught in the chaos, its windows shattering as a Republic security skiff speeds past.

"Let's be honest, my loves, when the New Republic preaches stability, what they really mean is 'shut up and do as you're told.' And the people of Chandrila? They're done listening."

He turns to another live feed, showing interviews with protestors. A [[Twi'lek]] woman, her expression hardened with frustration, speaks into a microphone:

'The Republic abandoned its people. We've seen what happened on [[Dreven]]. Maybe it's time Chandrila made its own way, too.'

Thurston nods, turning back to the audience. "And there it is, my darlings! The D word? Dreven! You remember Dreven, don't you? The world that overwhelmingly voted to leave the Republic after years of corruption and neglect? The world that saw the Republic abandon it so many times that it finally said enough?"

He gestures dramatically at the holoscreen. "Now, Chandrila is standing at the edge of the same precipice. And what does the Republic do? Do they listen? Do they change? No, no, no, my loves! They double down, call for order, and pretend this isn't happening!"

The screen cuts to a Republic spokesperson making a statement from the [[New Republic Senate|Senate]] chamber:

?The Republic stands united. We will not be swayed by those who seek to divide us.'

Thurston snorts. "Oh, of course, darling. Of course. You're not divided. It's just that more and more of your own member worlds are looking at the door and realizing that leaving might be the best option."

The studio lights dim slightly as Thurston takes on a more serious tone.

"My loves, we've seen this pattern before. The corruption, the negligence, the stifling arrogance of the New Republic. They called the crew of the [[DSV Templar|Templar]] traitors for daring to step up when the government failed. They labeled every world questioning their rule as rebellious instead of oppressed. And now, yet again, they refuse to answer the simple question Chandrila is asking: Why should we stay?"

The camera zooms in on his face as he leans in.

"Will Chandrila be the next world to walk? Will they follow Dreven's lead and cast off the Republic's crumbling chains? Or will they be silenced, forced back into line with more talk of patience and stability?"

Thurston lets the question linger before flashing his signature smirk. "Well, my darlings, you know I have my bets placed. But as always, the truth will out."

The screen fades to black, the echoes of Chandrila's protests still playing in the background.

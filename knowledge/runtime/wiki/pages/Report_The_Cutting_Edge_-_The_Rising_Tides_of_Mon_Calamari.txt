The holofeed flickered to life, not in the pristine newsroom of [[The Cutting Edge]], but on the storm-wracked surface of [[Dac|Mon Calamari]] itself. The endless ocean stretched behind [[Thurston Unger]], its waves rolling under the eerie glow of the planet's twin moons. A vast cityscape of sleek domes and towering spires loomed in the distance, its usual tranquility marred by the ominous flashes of riot suppression lights beneath the waves.

Thurston stood atop a floating platform, clad in a waterproof cape of shimmering cobalt, its hem flicking dramatically in the briny wind. He adjusted a gold-trimmed collar before fixing the camera with a perfectly calculated look of bemused exasperation. "My darlings, my dearest truth-seekers, do you know what I simply adore about Mon Calamari? The architecture. The seafood. The way the humidity absolutely destroys one's hair no matter how much product is applied." He sighed, flicking his fingers through his carefully styled waves. "And do you know what I despise? Political meltdowns."

The feed cut to footage from earlier in the day-thousands of [[Quarren]] gathered in vast underwater concourses, their deep, resonant voices chanting in unison. Banners bearing a new sigil-an intertwined kraken and starburst-fluttered in the currents.

"Yes, my sweets, the Quarren have had enough. And they are making sure everyone knows it."

The camera panned past him, zooming in on the waters below. From the depths, entire schools of Quarren demonstrators surged through the city's transparent underwater tunnels, blocking transports, defacing Republic insignias, and clashing with New Republic peacekeepers. The glow of security droids flickered like fireflies in the abyss.

"They say the Republic has failed them. That politicians sit in their marble towers, sipping their fine Alderaanian wine, while Mon Cala's [[Mon Calamari Shipyards|shipyards]] toil endlessly to feed a navy that, let's be honest, isn't exactly winning many hearts and minds these days." He arched a perfectly sculpted brow.

The feed cut again-this time to a towering, broad-shouldered Quarren official, his deep-set eyes burning with conviction as he addressed an enraptured crowd. "This is not our war. This is not our government. We will no longer suffer to build the ships of tyrants."

"Oh, my darlings," Thurston purred as the screen returned to him, "that is what we in the business call a statement."

Another flick of his wrist, and the holoscreen behind him displayed something far more troubling-Mon Calamari warships, their sleek forms lined up in dimly lit shipyards. Unfinished hulls. Suspended production. The lifeblood of the [[New Republic|Republic's]] [[New Republic FleetOps|navy]], suddenly very uncertain.

"And here's where things get positively delicious," he whispered. "Because my sources tell me that key Mon Cala shipbuilders have already begun talks-yes, talks-with off-world 'investors.' And do you know what that means, my sweets?" A slow smile, razor-sharp. "It means the New Republic might soon find its favorite shipyard cozying up to new friends. And wouldn't that just be devastating?"

The wind howled as distant klaxons wailed from the city behind him. Thurston pulled his cape tighter, the very image of a journalist risking everything for the truth.

"So, my darlings, what happens next? Will Mon Cala drift away like so many others? Will the Republic convince-or shall we say, pressure-the Quarren into staying? And if they fail, who will be waiting in the deep, ready to seize an opportunity?"

His eyes gleamed with intrigue as the holocam slowly zoomed in. "I, for one, cannot wait to find out."

And with that, the transmission cut, leaving the galaxy holding its breath.

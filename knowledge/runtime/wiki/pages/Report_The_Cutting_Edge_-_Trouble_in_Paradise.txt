{{IGNpost|author=[[Thurston Unger]]|date=[[34 ABY]]|topic=Political News|anchors=[[Thurston Unger]]|headline=The Cutting Edge - Trouble in Paradise|synopsis=[[Thurston Unger]] reports about the official response to the protests in [[Hanna City]] on [[Chandrila]].}}

The holofeed opens with the familiar sight of [[The Cutting Edge]] logo spinning into place before dissolving into a live feed of [[Thurston Unger]], resplendent as ever in a deep emerald suit with gold embroidery that shimmered under the studio lights. A matching silk cravat sat perfectly at his throat, accentuating the sharp angles of his jawline as he faced the camera with an expression of practiced concern.

"My darlings, my lovelies, my ever-faithful seekers of truth, it is with a heavy heart?and no small amount of déjà vu, that I bring you yet an episode of Trouble in Paradise. And tonight, we turn our gaze upon [[Chandrila]], the once-pristine jewel of the galaxy's political idealists. Oh, yes, my loves, if [[:starwars:Naboo/Legends|Naboo]] was the garden of democracy, then Chandrila was the elegant ballroom where the [[New Republic|Republic's]] finest waltzed in perfect harmony. But as we have seen time and time again, even the finest of ballrooms can be overrun with rats."

The screen shifts to a live feed from Chandrila's capital, [[Hanna City]], where thousands of citizens fill the streets in protest. Some carry banners decrying government inaction; others demand full secession from the New Republic. Police forces in blue-and-gray uniforms form barriers at major intersections, holding back an increasingly agitated crowd.

"Oh, but don't let the charming landscapes and well-manicured politicians fool you," Thurston continues, turning slightly as the images roll behind him. "Chandrila is not at peace. What started as polite murmurs of discontent has swelled into a full-throated roar. Why, you ask? What could make this most civilized of Republic worlds rise up against their oh-so-enlightened leadership?"

The camera cuts to a Chandrilan [[senator]] red-faced and visibly flustered, speaking at a recent press conference.

'Chandrila remains committed to the principles of the Republic. While we acknowledge the frustrations of our citizens, we assure you that stability is our priority. We ask for patience.'

Thurston rolls his eyes dramatically. "Patience! Yes, because that is what the people want to hear when their economy is collapsing under Republic mismanagement, their leaders are caught in corruption scandals, and their hard-earned credits are being funneled into bureaucratic black holes."

A new clip plays, showing reports of several high-ranking Republic officials on Chandrila being exposed for embezzlement and backdoor dealings with corporate interests.

"Oh, my loves, you simply cannot make this up. Chandrila, once the paragon of Republic virtue, is now rotting from the inside out. And the people? They see it. They feel it. And now, they are done waiting for empty promises and political platitudes."

The feed shifts to footage of Republic security forces dispersing protestors with sonic cannons.

"And how does the Republic respond to its beloved citizens? With dialogue? With reform? No, my darlings. With force."

Thurston turns back to the camera, his expression a mix of exasperation and simmering outrage. "Let us not forget, my loves, that this is the same Republic that stood by while the people of [[Dreven]] overwhelmingly voted to leave. The same Republic that called the Templar crew criminals for doing what their own government refused to do? Protect its citizens. The same Republic that, time and time again, proves that it is no longer a government of the people but rather a sluggish, decaying institution run by career politicians who are more concerned with saving face than saving lives."

The screen behind him shifts to live aerial shots of Chandrila's protests swelling in size, with barricades set up around the Senate building.

"And now, my darlings, we ask the most deliciously dangerous question of all: Will Chandrila follow Dreven's lead? Will they throw off the yoke of Republic rule and seek their own future? Or will they be beaten into submission with yet another round of 'patience and stability'?"

Thurston straightens his cuffs and gives the camera a knowing smirk. "I, for one, am absolutely dying to find out."

The screen fades to black, but the message is clear. The Republic is on shaky ground. And the galaxy is watching.

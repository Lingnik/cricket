RNN SPECIAL REPORT
[[Ord Mantell]], [[New Republic|Republic]] Capital

A slightly windblown junior reporter, Marlis Jenner stands in front of a growing crowd outside the [[New Republic|Republic]] Administrative Complex on [[Ord Mantell]]. Protestors chant and wave glowing banners, several depicting a stylized silhouette of an old [[SubPro]] cruiser against a blazing star.

"Good evening from Ord Mantell. I'm Marlis Jenner with RNN, and tonight the Republic faces renewed unrest following the dramatic capture of a ship that, to many across the galaxy, had become more than a vessel-it had become a symbol."

Cut to archival footage: A rust-scarred SubPro [[Lightning-class Sloop|Deep Space Vehicle]], heavily modified with additional armor plating and visible weapons retrofits, coasts through a frontier sector. The hull bears the unmistakable name-Templar.

"The Templar-a retrofitted SubPro long-range deep space platform-was never meant for glory. But under the command of [[Colonel]] [[Han'bel Eckhart]], a veteran marine officer turned rogue humanitarian, it became something else entirely. Disillusioned by [[New Republic Senate|Senate]] gridlock and military red tape, Colonel Eckhart and his crew of volunteers went AWOL over two years ago, abandoning their postings to respond to desperate pleas for help."

Footage cuts between scenes of the [[DSV Templar|Templar]] providing evac from a burning moon colony, delivering food to a blight-stricken world, and escorting refugee ships past pirate blockade lines.

"From frontier colonies to forgotten trade routes, the Templar's crew answered distress calls the Republic ignored or never authorized. They rescued settlers from raiders, intercepted slavers in neutral space, and delivered food and medical aid to systems where bureaucratic aid was still 'under review.'"

A still photo of Colonel Eckhart in field armor flashes on-screen, his face weathered but proud.

"To many, they became folk heroes-symbols of what the Republic could be, but wasn't."

Cut to footage of the Templar being tractored into a Republic dock, its hull scorched from a recent skirmish. A crowd of onlookers watches in silence.

"But earlier today, that story took a dramatic turn. Republic Command confirmed the arrest of Colonel Eckhart and several senior officers following a covert operation. Charges include desertion, theft of military assets, and unauthorized operations in sovereign territories."

The feed cuts to protest footage: massive crowds chanting 'Eckhart is a hero!' and 'Justice for the Templar!'

"Public response has been swift-and passionate. On [[Dac|Mon Calamari]], renewed protests broke out within hours. On [[Bothawui]], newly formed separatist factions cite the Templar's arrest as yet another sign the Republic is more interested in punishing heroism than embodying it."

Footage of a [[Bothan]] activist shouting into a megaphone, flanked by supporters holding banners that read, "The Republic Abandons. The Templar Rescued."

"Even here on Ord Mantell, long considered a bastion of Republic unity, factions sympathetic to Eckhart's cause are making their voices heard. Some Republic military veterans have joined the protests, openly stating they would have followed Eckhart, too, if given the chance."

"Colonel Eckhart's legal status remains unclear. A Republic tribunal is expected to convene within days. But whether he is tried as a criminal-or hailed as a hero-may no longer be up to the Senate alone."

"For RNN, I'm Marlis Jenner, reporting from Ord Mantell. We'll bring you more as the story unfolds."

RNN outro music fades in, the screen pulling back from the plaza to reveal growing crowds swelling into neighboring districts.

'''Articles for Deletion (AfD)''' is used to list and discuss whether specific articles should be deleted. Items sent here should wait at least five days while debate takes place on whether the article should be deleted or not; then the deletion process can proceed based on community consensus. The page is then:
*Kept, 
*Deleted,
*Merged and/or redirected to an existing article, 
*Renamed/Moved to another title, or
*Userfied to the creator's user page or user subpage


== AfD etiquette ==
* Be nice.  No personal attacks, maintain civility, and assume good faith before making a recommendation as to whether the article should be deleted or not, or making a comment.
* Sign any recommendation or comment you add, by adding this at the end: <nowiki> -- ~~~~ </nowiki>
* Notify the creator and/or main contributor(s) of the article when nominating, as they may be able to address concerns raised.
* If you are the primary author or otherwise have a vested interest in the article, say so openly.
* Your opinion will be given the most weight if you are logged in with an account. Unregistered users are welcome to contribute to the discussion, but their recommendations may carry less weight.
* Please make your recommendation only once.
* Before making your recommendation please review the talk page for the nomination, if there is one.  Additional important information about the article may be found there.
* State your suggested course of action in bold, such as '''Keep''' or '''Delete'''.
* If you change your mind, modify your original recommendation.  Giving more than one can cause confusion and make certain positions seem like they have more support than they actually do.  
* You don't have to make a recommendation on every nomination.  Consider ''not'' participating if:
** The topic of the article is unfamiliar to you.
** You agree with the consensus that has already formed.

{{Speech|name=Save the Life of My Child|Author=[[Crestian Tarasar]]|date=[[30 ABY]]|place=[[Castle Rock Manor]], [[Plaxton]], [[Caspar]]}}

On Benduday Caspian [[Presav]] [[Crestian Tarasar]] gave an address at [[Castle Rock Manor]], the Caspian presavadential estate. His remarks included an analysis of Caspian relations with the [[New Republic]] and [[Imperium]], the reunification of [[Caspar]] and [[Krittain]], and the admittance of [[Charros IV]] into the [[Caspian Union]]. It is approximately twenty-five minutes.

"I recently came across a fascinating parable from [[Sarian]] antiquity. A great drought had struck Vilarasala bringing much death and devastation. Distant cities sent priests to offer comfort but none could divine a path out of the drought. Then one day two things of note happened. A great rain final began and a child was born. The priests decided their must be some connexion between the two and so they brought the child and place it on the central altar in the city while they did a dance around it to thank the Gods.

When the dance was over and they went to remove the child from the altar they found it was quite dead. You see the altar was in the shape of a giant bowl and was collecting all the water from this great rain. While they were engrossed in their ritual the child drowned. The lesson should be obvious but unfortunately like faith often is the interpretations vary, many are content to think that the child's death was the will of the Gods rather than question the actions of the priests.

The problem is this isn't really a parable from antiquity it is the story of our galaxy right now. The child is [[Caspar]]. The story does not begin with the drought though. The [[New Republic|New Republic's]] may have a new Chancellor but stayed true to the old tired line of propaganda calling the [[Caspian Union]] a puppet state of the Imperium. Whether or not you agree with assessment the inescapable fact is the Republic is largely to blame for the path we found ourselves on.

Six years ago Caspar was caught selling weapons to the New Republic and became the site of a showdown between the [[Galactic Empire|Empire]] and Republic. The Republic may have been able to dust themselves off and walk away but Caspar was left holding the bag. Our major industries bought off in a fire sale, customs enforcement surrendered to the [[Corporate Sector Authority]]. Caspar effectively under Imperial domination then. And don't get me wrong Caspian leadership at the time was to blame as well Presav Caitrean and his cabinet were forced to resign. But dealing with the Republic cost us and the ultimately failed to protect us from the mess they were complicit in starting.

Naturally the Galactic Empire was the villain too, as they were in everything. The drought is the Sariphage directed by the false-[[Galactic Emperor|Emperor]] [[Aleister Vadim|Vadim]] and developed by his henchman [[Basca Starker]]. This brings us back now to the child an the altar. The solution is clear, one of the priests should pick the child up. Sadly it was never this simple. Remember I said that many look up in this as the story of the will of the Gods.

That toxic orthodoxy is represented by the cast of priests we see at this dance. The Galactic Empire which set this all in motion. The New Republic who sends a single ship, a token gesture, that really does nothing to solve the problem. The [[Olumi Osahn]] who in instigating the Mergani pogroms effectively decided to add more children to the altar to drown. The Corporate Sector Authority who was willing enough to help pick up the extra children but not stop them being placed there to begin with. Then there are our home grown villains leaders of other Caspian worlds and the [[Sar Admiral]] who would not put a stop to this.

The new Chancellor's press secretary referred to him as a dark horse candidate, this is something I know a lot about. I am the priest from another city who tried to remove the child Caspar from the altar before it drowned and when stopped by the other misguided priests was forced to instead break the altar. If that was bad enough I had to kill one of the other priests on the way out of the city. It has been a long journey these last two years to bring that child back to health and to atone for actions that while necessary were sacrilege.

I feel this gives me a certain insight into the new Chancellor. I think he knows about atonement. He donated from his brother's estate to our relief efforts here on Caspar. More importantly he was the one who delivered us the corpse of that genocidal terror. I believe he is a man of rare conviction which is why I had hoped his election might mean better relations for our respective peoples. I have not wholly given up on that hope. Yet, the changes there are still worrisome. It may be raining, and I know he is willing to break the altar, but is there is even a child in his?

Faith serves as a larger analogy as well. Caspar and the Republic have in many respects long shared a faith in the values of democracy and sentient rights. I understand their faith well enough having lived by it over a decade myself. I spent far longer on [[Chandrila]] in the service of the Republic than I have as an adult on Caspar. The fact that Caspar now chooses to align itself with people the Republic views as infidels is something they continue to show themselves unwilling to understand.

This brings me back to the original parable. Rather than continue a ceremony that will lead to death we have chosen to break the altar. In the end it may or may not have been the right decision, but it is not renunciation of faith. The altar can be rebuilt our actions can be discussed and examine. We can thoughtfully go forward in the future and try to honour our convictions.

When it comes to the Republic, their faith has seemed for a long time to be performative. They do the ritual dance, oblivious to the danger occurring right beside them. They seem so caught up in practising form of faith they have long stopped contemplating its meaning. And in truth we have little idea if this is really the case. So certain they seem that we have left the faith they discourage us from their temples and won't come to visit ours. We remain locked in a endless cycle of fiery sermons denouncing each others heresies.

Matters with the Imperium can be seen in much the same light. The Galactic Empire was the faith of degradation, their demi-gods and chief priests oppressed their own people and visited devastation upon all others. It is a rightly reviled faith. But what of those people who lived under that domination? The Republic has little room in their hearts for them it seems. Unless the come prostrating and begging to be let in at the temples of the Republic they seem set to be branded as infidels not matter how the practice of their faith changes.

There is a question to be asked here not just to the New Republic but to all of us, what is the change we want to see. The Galactic Empire was awful but it was successful. It is not a simple matter to just take that system and transmute it to something of real value to the galaxy. Believe it or not I did not know what to think of it at first myself. The Imperator, of course, is a friend. I worked for him many years ago and we are allies to this day. But there was something in the idea of trying to reform the Empire that seemed a little gross. Yet, to forge something wholly new when so many people were suffering under the yolk of oppression, they could not wait for a more perfect government to come along. The Republic certainly was not ending the oppression of the trillions living in Imperial space.

While the detractors have spent these past months branding the Imperium as just the Galactic Empire rebranded, the Imperium has worked at break-neck pace dismantling the greatest travesties of the [[New Order]] and bringing a reasonable standard of living to trillions who had little to hope for before. Slavery has been abolished, laws which oppressed non-Humans eliminated, the latest efforts in advancing worker representation and wealth redistribution represent a seismic shift in what proceeded it. It is still a work in progress and one that I doubt will ever look perfect, but perfection is always an aspiration never an achievement.

I have had a few of my fellow Councillors lately ask me if it was my plan to introduce similar domestic policies to the Caspian Union. Some have asked out of hope others have asked out of concern. It is the insidious thing about propaganda, sometimes you start to actually believe it. We are allies of the Imperium yes. Not be cause our faith is closer to theirs than the Republic, our structure of government and society certainly is not. We share a commitment to learn the mistakes our past, deal with the problems of our present, and prepare for the challenges of our future. In defence we stand arm in arm because we recognise we share the same enemies and will stand a better chance against them if we work together.

When it comes to domestic policy there is little need to emulate the Imperium because our problems are not the same. Some of those worlds are dealing with populations that have suffered over four decades of oppression at the hands of their own leaders. It is a far different problem then the ones we have had and naturally suggests different solutions. Where there is an opportunity to help we do so, certainly it is no accident that their capital world has seen its fortunes revive under the leadership of a former Caspian Presav.

Our problems were different, the Sariphage, the Mergani pogroms, our major industries under foreign control. The New Republic likes to, pun intended, malign the Imperator. It seems now their faith wishes to put the label of apostasy on his sister. No doubt offended she left the halls of their performative worship to actually do some good for the galaxy. Yet, when Caspar needed help to survive and to rebuild they were there providing the help behind the scenes. The Republic wants to call us puppets because we have chosen to work with those that helped us. But if this offends them so much where the hell was their help?

The point of rehashing all of this though is not to lob another salvo at the Republic. Certainly we would like to see them work with us in pursuit of shared values rather than just criticise ourselves and our allies from a distance. This is a story about Caspar and for Caspar. At the heart of our policy has always been to rebuild what has been lost and to work to ensure such suffering never happens again. We welcome the Imperium as partners in that work, and would welcome the Republic as well. Their discord particular given the historical affinity many of our people have for the Republic is sadly an ongoing challenge for our own people moving forward. But our own people moving forward is what we are first and foremost about.

The Caspian Union never sought to be simply a repeat of the old [[Caspian Democratic Union|CDU]] but we have, nevertheless, sought rebuild the ties with the worlds which were once a part of it. Last year we were able to welcome back [[Kasputin Yar]], [[Rauther]], and [[Ixin]] but our differences with the Krits have been more profound. I wish I could say we have work out all our difference with the Krits. It would be good political spin to try to make it sound like we have. But it is harsh galaxy that demands a little more honesty to make it through. We have disagreed on issues of citizenship, foreign policy, military composition, and planetary autonomy. Diplomacy between the two sides has existed at some level right along but reunification was something that had not been on the table most of that time. The gulf between us seemed too wide.

The recent attack on the Blechley Brook was a reminder that our safety is still intertwined. Caspar is not safe if the Krits are under attack and the Krits are not safe if Caspar is under attack. Much of the [[Caspian 4th Fleet]] has been assisting the Krits patrol their borders since then, while we believe it was an attack of opportunity it has been important to ensure no such opportunities present themselves again. These slavers left unchecked present a threat to us all. But we have discovered something else, we can in times of need come together again in common cause. Whatever differences have separated us the union between Caspar and the Krits existed for many years. It is ultimately a relationship of more importance than our ties with the larger powers of the galaxy.

There is still much work to be done to move forward but we have decided it is in our mutual best interests to do so together. President Kritz and I have signed a treaty which brings the Caspian and Krittish Unions back together. We are confident it will pass in both the [[Union Council]] and Krit Senate. As major both citizenship in the Caspian Union and in local Krittish citizenship will be extend to all current residents of the [[Krittain|Krittain system]]. They will have full voting rights in all future elections and full access to membership in the exclusively Krittish military ships and units. We have agreed on our end to all such exclusive units to continue and as part of the reintegration of our fleet command the [[Caspian 2nd Fleet]] will shift its headquarters from the [[Yariz|Yariz System]] to the Krittain system to protect our border. Upon the ratification of this treaty seven Councillors representing [[Krittain Major]] and one representing [[Krittain Minor]] will join the Union Council.

In this spirit of new beginnings it seems excellent timing to announce the fulfilment of a project our Foreign Affairs and Defence Ministry has been engaged in since I took office as Presav. Just outside of Caspian space is the Charros system. Their people have been suffering a great spiritual oppression since the end of the Clone Wars, and this time I am not speaking metaphorically. Some 75 years ago the [[Xi Char]] accept supposed converted their faith. He was exposed as being none other than Raith Sienar who under a false identity had infiltrated their ranks to spy on their manufacturing techniques. It was perhaps the greatest sacrilege they had faced until the Clone Wars ended and they found themselves on the losing side. Their main manufacturer [[Haor Chall Engineering]] faced the indignity of being absorbed by [[Sienar Fleet Systems]] where it has remained to this day.

This is not an injustice easily rectified by stock options and board positions. In this case, however, I believe we have negotiated a more elegant solution. In a three-way treaty signed by myself, [[Grand Moff]] [[Elana Tracer|Tracer]], and [[Prelate]] [[r'ratak-varattak-n'gavanak]], [[Kuat Drive Yards-Sienar Fleet Systems]] cedes all facilities on [[Charros IV]] back to the Xi Char to reconstitute Haor Chall Engineering and Charros IV joins the Caspian Union as a member world. Haor Chall Engineering will partner with [[SubPro]], the [[Aston Aerospace|Aston Aerospace Group]] of Krittain, and [[Lokkai Starworks]] of Yariz in building and servicing the vessels of the [[Caspian Navy]] as well as potential sales of military grade craft to allied powers and as well as civilian transports. The Xi Char, however, will retain full control over their facilities and designs this is a partnership of equals. And as a member world Charros IV will be represented by four Councillors upon treaty ratification and the [[Caspian 3rd Fleet]] will shift its headquarters from Rauther to Charros IV.

The [[Xi Charrian]] contribution to the Caspian fleet, however, has long been underway. Xi Charrian engineers partnered with our engineers at Lokkai Starworks have been hard at work refitting and recommissioning the battlecruisers of the [[Daring-class Battlecruiser|Daring project]]. While they lacked the elegance of modern designs or even a uniform design they were a resource we could not afford to ignore in an increasingly hostile galaxy. Each ship presented our engineers unique challenges and yet a unique opportunity to place their own imprint upon. To quote our Xi Charrian friends, the deity is in the details, and Caspar welcomes their spiritual contribution to our fleet and our union."

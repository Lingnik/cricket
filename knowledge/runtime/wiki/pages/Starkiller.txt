The '''''Starkiller''''' is a [[Warlord-class Medium Cruiser|Warlord-Class Cruiser]] which, along with its sister ship, ''[[Tethys]]'', has been indefinitely seconded from the [[Imperium]] to the service of [[Crestian Tarasar]] in his capacity as a [[New Sith Order|Sith]] [[Duke]] and [[Admiral]]. In operational terms it is treated as property of [[House Dragonflower]] and seconded to the [[Caspian Navy]] along with the [[Interdictor-class Immobilizer 418|Interdictors]] ''[[Leviathan]]'' and ''[[Dying Sun]]''.

== Background ==

In [[29 ABY]], [[Tyler Damion]], otherwise known as Darth Malign, the [[Dark Lord of the Sith]], reemerged publicly and declared himself the new leader of the [[Galactic Empire]], now rechristened as the [[Imperium]]. Concurrent with the rise of Imperator Damion was the campaign of [[New Sith Order|Sith]] [[Duke]] and [[Admiral]] [[Crestian Tarasar]] for election to the permanent [[Union Council]] of the [[Caspian Union|Caspar Union]].

When Crestian first arrived on Selene after Malign's takeover, he walked into what became a matter of great family drama. Former [[Caspian Democratic Union|Caspian]] [[Presav]] [[Elana Tracer]] had arrived, having tracked down her missing eldest son [[Kipran Jawatha]], a [[Force|force user]], who has sought out Malign to be trained, and had joined the Sith. Concurrently Malign's half-sister [[Johanna Siri te Danaan]] had come after her own [[Jedi Knight]] son, [[Etiel te Danaan]], who was now serving the Imperium as [[Moff]] of [[O'paal]] and [[Byss]].

Much to Crestian's surprise, this impromptu family reunion saw Malign assign a trio of [[Warlord-class Medium Cruiser|Warlord-Class Cruisers]], ''Imperial Edict'', '''''Starkiller''''', and ''[[Tethys]]'', to reinforce his flagship, the ''[[Leviathan]]''. Kipran was assigned by Malign as commanding officer of the ''Imperial Edict''. Crestian's own hopes to offer command of the [[Caspian Starfighter Corps]] to Former Presav Tracer, following his upcoming election, were dashed by Malign later offering her the roles of [[Moff]] of [[Selene]] and control of the [[Kuat Drive Yards-Sienar Fleet Systems]] shipyards, positions he ironically had imagined he would have been offered had he not returned to [[Caspar]] instead.

As Kipran's training as an apprentice to Malign progressed, the ''Imperial Edict'', would be reassigned back to Imperium service in '''Task Force Shadow'''. The ''Starkiller'' and ''Tethys'' would remain with Crestian and assigned to the '''[[Caspian 4th Fleet]]''' after he became Presav and [[Sar Admiral]]. Though strictly speaking, neither ship was truly necessary, as Crestian now commanded the entire [[Caspian Navy]], given the Imperium's vast resources, the Imperator has never requested their return. Both vessels are now effectively treated as property of Crestian's [[House Dragonflower]], which also provides the Union with the use of the [[Interdictor-class Immobilizer 418|Interdictors]] ''[[Leviathan]]'' and ''[[Dying Sun]]''.

== Captain, Crew, and Compliment ==

[[File:Beryl Sterling Sith Uniform.jpg|left|thumb|Fleet Captain Beryl Sterling]] As a recent construct, Crestian was in the position to be able to appoint many of the officers of the ''Starkiller''. For command, Crestian along with Imperium [[Grand Admiral]] [[Drayson Honos]], tapped [[Captain]] Beryl Sterling. Sterling had been a member of Honos' command staff both in the [[Galactic Empire]] and later the New Sith Order, who had retired and returned home to the city of [[:starwars:Amma|Amma]] on [[:starwars:Bacrana/Legends|Bacrana]] in the [[:starwars:Brak sector/Legends|Brak Sector]], after Malign and Honos had gone missing and the New Sith Order collapsed. Crestian had considered Sterling to helpful during their days in the New Sith Order.<br><br>

Having grown tired of retirement, Sterling was easily convinced to take the assignment. Most of ''Starkiller's'' other senior officers would come from the ''Leviathan'', with Crestian taking the opportunity to elevate many officers who had been stalled in advancement during ''Leviathan's'' time in hiding. Sterling himself would also recruit a number of his own former subordinates as well. Though most of the initial crew would come from the Imperium, as a vessel in Caspian service, subsequent vacancies would be open for Caspian personnel to fill. 

[[File:Beryl Sterling.jpg|thumb|Fleet Captain Beryl Sterling]] As a ship that would be often selected for high profile assignments, postings to it are generally popular with many Caspian officers viewing service on the ''Starkiller'' as a good opportunity for advancement, in spite of its status as a Sith ship. Over the years since, ''Starkiller'' has slowly grown to be more Caspian in character, though in a number of cases, officers who came by way of the Caspian Navy have come to identify with House Dragonflower. Sterling's experience and professionalism has proven to be integral in bridging the divide.

''Starkiller'' is host to '''Shadowblade Squadron''' and '''Longhaul Squadron'''. Along with Harbinger Squadron and Intruder Squadron on the ''Tethys'', these are the only [[TIE Avenger|TIE Avengers]] serving in the Caspian Starfighter Corps. As [[Sar Admiral]], Crestian felt placing Imperial designed craft on mainline Caspian warships would be bad for morale. ''Starkiller'' is also host to three companies of [[Union Marines]].

== Service ==

As part of '''Task Force Resplendent''' of the [[Caspian 4th Fleet]], the ''Starkiller'' has participated in various of its operations over the years including the [[Report:Mergani Subjugation Posts|blockade]] of [[Mergansar]]. It has also engaged in smaller scale assignments. In [[30 ABY]], following various attacks by slavers against targets across the galaxy, the ''Starkiller'' headed up a flotilla, which included ''CMS Tiburon'' and ''CMS Silvertip'', sent to protect [[Silver Station]]. In addition to being the personal fiefdom of Crestian Tarasar, the [[:starwars:Prothium|prothium]] gas extracted from the nearby [[Dragonflower Nebula]] is largely sold to the [[CDMC|Caspian Design & Manufacturing Corporand]]. Silver Station also serves as an outpost for the [[Caspian Defence Force]], in an area of space it would not otherwise have a presence, making its protection vital to Union interests. Sterling would be promoted to [[Fleet Captain]] as part of this assignment.

In [[33 ABY]], elements of the 4th Fleet participated in the [[Caspian Intelligence Service]] mission code named "Operation Requiem." The ships involve operated in the [[Faylar|Faylar System]] as a fictional Imperium "'''Task Force Faylar'''." Each ship involved had its transponder changed to register as an Imperium vessel, with the ships themselves being the fleets four Imperial made warships. The ''Leviathan'' operated as the the ''"Spire of Victory,"'' the ''Dying Sun'' as the ''"Oubliette,"'' the ''Tethys'' as the ''"Reborn Order,"'' and the ''"Starkiller,"'' as the ''"Black Talon."'' Fleet Captain Drorq served as commanding officer for the task force, while Captain [[Atsvara Tarasar]] served in place of her father as acting commanding officer of the ''"Spire of Victory."''

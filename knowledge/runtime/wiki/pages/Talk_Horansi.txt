Canon contradicts itself here largely because the Horansi are contradictory by nature, I think. The issue comes primarily from the notion that the Horansi are primarily bipedal, but drop to all fours once in a while(like when hunting). Plantigrade lends itself better to bipedalism, sunce it's easier to balance and the knees lock more readily, keeping the legs from tiring. Digitigrade works better for quadrupeds, as it allows for swifter(increased stride length) and usually quieter motion. Given, though, that the Horansi are still hunters, and that their bipedalism is more a cultural preference than evolutionary, I'd suggest that digitigrade makes a lot more sense. -- [[User:Wrista|Wrista]] 18:31, 14 April 2007 (UTC)

:The reason I un-included A280's is because '''A)''' they ''are'' on the heavy and large side; while it may not be much larger than an M16A2, there's a reason why Colt made the M4/CAR-15... because the M16A2 isn't ''compact enough'' '''B)''' they have a slower rate of fire and a "slower trigger" than the "tighter" blaster carbines, which, when in tight quarters, is a detriment when threats are coming much faster with less notice, and '''C)''' they are "the most powerful armor-piercing blaster rifle in the arsenal"; in room clearing, you don't want a high-velocity round that will travel through walls, for fear of hitting non-combatants, assets, resources, friendlies, or hazards within the walls (gas lines, etc.); you want a weapon that will work just enough to put a round (in this case, a ''blaster bolt'') ''into'' a hostile, not travel right through them... which the A280 is not only reputed to do, but is reported to do. Look past the power-stats, and you'll find that the 36T is more than adequate for CQB/compartment-clearing. -- [[User:Hawke|Hawke / Rtufo]] 06:00, 21 September 2006 (UTC)

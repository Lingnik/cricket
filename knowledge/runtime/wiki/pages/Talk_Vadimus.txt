*I'm familiar with the TP planet Korolov refers to. It's a coded object at coordinates I don't remember. Its sole purpose is to be available for any TP in which requires a coded planet that isn't currently on the grid. It has a BC that can be used by the plot coordinators to create a planet for the TP. When I was running the Moff Sekirol Crisis, I used the planet to create Barida for the Battle of Barida. So for a few months Barida was a coded planet - but it's not there anymore, as it's apparently been replaced with Vadimus. For how long, I do not know. So they're "temporary" coded planets, if anything. --[[User:Danik Kreldin|Danik Kreldin]] 14:42, 3 December 2007 (UTC)

:-I don't know if there are rules for the TP planet. It is one of those things that was a special pet project of a wizard several years ago. That doesn't mean that it cannot be used just that it isn't being used and hasn't for at least a year. I think I am one of a small number of PCs who have ever been to Vadimus. I also think it's status should be listed as ''Non-coded'' because it really isn't a part of the grid. It is a planet with two rooms that is not really part of the RP grid, much more remote and uncharted than even Ord Trasi where there have been multiple TPs run over the years and is known by several PCs on the grid. I think that coded status should only be used on planets that are currently coded and are actively portrayed on the MUSH. So if say the Empire were to shift O'paal or Dreven to non-coded status and add other planets, it wouldn't be appropriate for them to be listed as coded as they won't be anymore. That is just my two cents though, take it for what it is worth. --[[User:Korolov|Korolov]] 20:53, 3 December 2007 (UTC)

As Mahon and I discussed, the Yari will have a lean, willowy frame; long, spindrel-like fingers; reverse-knee joints (chicken-walk); and be of a disposition similar to [[Ithorian]]s, or to [[Givin]] or [[Sluissi]]. Kasputin Yar is topographically similar to Ethiopia, Sudan, and Kenya, but dotted by more craters and evidence of meteor strikes. It's an "old planet", with not much in the way of techtonic activity. I'd like to include some rather large animals, akin to rhinoceroses, or giraffes, or something along that line.

As to a role within the Union, Yaries would not be found in the Marines, nor in many Provincial Army units &mdash; they would be relying on the other Union members to provide defense. They would be willing to be employed in the Navy, and would be adept at tech and support roles. They're a passive, pensive species and look to the Union as their "saving grace" from endenture to the likes of the Hutts, Cronese, or Tionese. While the Krittish and Rautherans identified more with the Caspians and more "modern" Sarians, the Yaries found a kinship to the Eiresari and Janhovi Sarians, with their reverance of the elements and contemplative ways. -- [[User:Hawke|Hawke / Rtufo]] 16:35, 7 October 2006 (UTC)



Chicken-walk?  Given that they are apparently mainly climbers, and mostly up the tall, tall trunks of the Rofalssa stalks, I would picture them moving more like centipedes.  Also, how many limbs have they?  [[Yari]] page has one number, and [[Kasputin Yar]] has another.  I'm picturing a long-bodied being, akin to a caterpillar in some ways, in that the front four limbs are more developed than the back four, which are mainly for hanging on.  With tree-dwelling in mind, we might be looking at something with eyes that can perform both for wide-angle(because, up a tree, a flying threat can come from anywhere), and for binocular(ever try hunting without depth perception?).  The crest they are described as having, the interests of visibility would make it vertical, like that of the Charonosaurus.  And being a social creature would suggest that it was for communications.  Perhaps both for sound and for their electromagnetic sense?  Does their language consist of both sonic cues and radio-band chatter?   --[[User:HM743|HM743]] 06:18, 31 October 2007 (UTC)

'''Thurston Unger''' is the host of [[The Cutting Edge]] and the [[Galaxy's Dumbest Crooks]] for [[InterGalactic News]].

Thurston Unger is not a man who enters a room—he arrives, announces, and conquers it with a glance. 

Striking and theatrical, Thurston walks the razor's edge between timeless glamor and irreverent flamboyance. His appearance is a carefully cultivated spectacle, as iconic as his voice. With cheekbones sharp enough to file a datapad and a jawline that belongs on a marble bust, Thurston is unmistakably male—but dances with androgyny like it’s an art form. And for him, it is. 

His hair is his signature—never the same twice. One week it might be sculpted platinum-white in a towering coif that catches the light like polished starsteel; the next, a seafoam green wave cascading over one brow, glittering with micro-crystals. The color changes with his mood, the cut with the story he’s about to tell. His natural color? If you asked, he’d only laugh. 

Thurston’s eyes—cool grey with flecks of cobalt—miss nothing, and while his smile is charming, it rarely reaches them unless he's caught something juicy. They twinkle with equal parts mischief and menace, particularly when he's skewering corrupt politicians or unraveling conspiracies on-air. 

He dresses as though every day is a holonet premiere. Tailored suits in vivid, iridescent fabrics—amethysts, cobalts, blood-orange silks—paired with embroidered high collars, asymmetrical lapels, and the occasional brooch or chain-link sash that suggests both royal heritage and rogue sensibilities. He refuses to wear black unless it’s paired with gold. His boots? Always heeled. Always dramatic. 

Despite the glam, there's something razor-honed beneath the surface—an intellect and instinct for subtext that makes him one of the most formidable journalists in the galaxy. He may look like a stage performer from a glamrock opera, but Thurston Unger is deadly serious about the truth... or at least, his version of it. 

He laughs often, gestures with flair, and is never, ever boring. He is satire with soul, style with substance—and if you underestimate him, he’ll carve you up on the next broadcast, with a smile as sharp as his tongue. 

== Biography ==

[[File:The Cutting Edge Poster.webp|left|250px]] Thurston Unger is one of the most flamboyant and polarizing figures in galactic media, known across the Core and Mid Rim for his razor-sharp wit, unfiltered commentary, and unmissable wardrobe. Thurston has made his mark as a media provocateur with daily changes to his hair and a wardrobe that can best be described as "interstellar haute couture meets theater of war." 

Born on [[Etti IV]] into a well-connected corporate family, Thurston rejected the dull boardrooms of his inheritance in favor of lights, cameras, and chaos. His early career saw him reporting fashion segments from war zones and embedding himself with underworld figures "for the aesthetic." Eventually, he secured a permanent presence at [[InterGalactic News|IGNews]], where his unique blend of sensationalism, political skepticism, and gleeful shade-throwing gave rise to his flagship show, [[The Cutting Edge]]. 

In The Cutting Edge, Thurston reports on political intrigue, unrest, and conspiracies with his signature tone—equal parts investigative journalism, performance art, and searing satire. Whether questioning the motives of the [[Imperium|Imperium’s]] Grand Inquisitor [[Axel Vichten]] or speculating about the influence of shadowy cults like the [[Olumi Osahn]], Thurston never shies from controversy. Even being banned from official Imperium press briefings hasn't silenced him—in fact, it only added to his legend. 

When not deconstructing galactic power games, Thurston dons his comedic mantle to host the [[Galaxy's Dumbest Crooks]]—a holonet guilty pleasure chronicling the galaxy’s most inept criminals. From would-be slicers who forget to disable security cams to swoop gangers who leave their names engraved on getaway speeders, Thurston delivers every segment with sardonic delight. 

Though often dismissed by more "serious" news outlets, Thurston commands a devoted following who see him as one of the few unfiltered voices left in the galaxy. Love him or loathe him, one thing is certain: when Thurston Unger speaks, people listen.

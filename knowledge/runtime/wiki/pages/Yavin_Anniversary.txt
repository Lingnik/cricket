{{Speech|name=Yavin Anniversary|Author=[[Cas Mos]]|date=[[30 ABY]]|place=Fountain Square, [[Plaxton]], [[Caspar]]}}

On Benduday [[Caspian Union|Caspian]] Vice Presav [[Cas Mos]] gave the keynote address at a ceremony in Fountain Square in [[Plaxton]] marking the 30th anniversary of the destruction of the [[:starwars:DS-1 Orbital Battle Station|First Death Star]] at the [[:starwars:Battle of Yavin/Legends|Battle of Yavin]] by the forces of the [[:starwars:Alliance to Restore the Republic/Legends|Rebel Alliance]]. Her remarks include observations of the historical significance of the battle itself, her belief in its significance to current events in the galaxy, and a renewed Caspian commitment to stand against oppression. It is approximately ten minutes.

A temporary stage has been constructed to the side of the fountain which gives [[Caspar|Caspar's]] central square its name. A number of dignitaries both Caspian and foreign are seated, one seat is notably empty. One dignitary an attractive [[Human]] women in her late forties wearing the forest green dress uniform of Caspian Marine Marshal takes comes to the podium and adjusts the microphone.

"[[Union Council|Councillor]] Morgaan, members of the ceremony committee, assembled dignitaries, honoured guests, I want to thank you for the invitation to give some remarks on this occasion. My niece asked me the other day what is that a Vice Presav does. Naturally I told her I give speeches and cut ribbons at events nobody wants to attend." Laughter can be heard from the crowd. "Looking at the size of this crowd we have a lot of work to do to end the oppression that forced so many of you to be here.

The significance of the [[:starwars:Battle of Yavin/Legends|Battle of Yavin]] cannot be underestimated. We consider it so pivotal to the history of the galaxy that we have literally re-ordered our calendars to be based around it. But perhaps now thirty years later we can better understand just how much it has reordered all of our lives since then. Naturally to understand its place we must look at what proceeded it, the destruction of [[:starwars:Alderaan/Legends|Alderaan]] by the [[:starwars:DS-1 Orbital Battle Station|Death Star]]. The [[Galactic Empire|Imperial]] propaganda machine tried to spin it, of course, but it was a stark lesson to the galaxy that absolutely no one was safe from the whims and wrath of [[Galactic Emperor|Emperor]] [[:starwars:Palpatine/Legends|Palpatine]]. He could destroy an entire planet in practically an instant and if he was willing to destroy such a prominent world as Alderaan, could anyone count on him not one day turning that weapon on their world?

But the Battle at Yavin and the destruction of the Death Star reminded us that unspeakable and seemingly unstoppable evil can be challenged successfully. It took determination, a little luck, and the sacrifice of so many brave beings. For the names of the heroes we all know of that day such as [[Luke Skywalker]], [[Han Solo]], and [[Chewbacca]]....” The Vice Presav is forced to pause for a moment as there is boisterous applause at the mention of [[Kashyyyk|Kashyyyk's]] favourite son.

"For all those names we all know, there are countless others whose contributions are now considered historical footnotes. I want to thank the speakers that preceded me for some of their remarks, and for the presentations they gave on the heroes of [[:starwars:Rogue One|Rogue One]], the crew of [[:starwars:Tantive IV|Tantive IV]], and the pilots lost during the assault on the Death Star itself.” 

Cas looks slightly emotional pausing for a brief moment before continuing “We are all indebted to their sacrifice. Civilisation as we not it might not exist were it not for their courage. But while they challenged evil it has not been defeated. We call it the First Death Star, because, of course, there were two more to come. The Battle of Yavin is not just an event we mark our calendars by, for many of us it why we one day put on a uniform. Not simply because we admired the heroes and want to become one of them, but because ultimately, the job was not and many never be done.

This brings us to where we are today. The Emperors may change and so do the weapons, but the threat remains the same. This is something we on Caspar know all too well as we lost nearly three billion people to the Sariphage two years ago. As Yavin served as a sort of reset for the galaxy so to as the Sariphage and Mergani pogroms served as the reset for Caspar. Our enemies these days are less straight forward than a Death Star and [[Imperial Navy|Imperial fleet]]. Emperor [[Aleister Vadim|Vadim]] and [[Olumi Osahn]] are sadly are not the extent or our worries.”

Cas turns to point to the empty seat behind her. "[[Krittain|Krittish]] President Maximilian Kritz was forced to cancel his attendance. The reason, sadly, is quite noteworthy the attack on a civilian transport by slavers which has left 300 of his people missing. Our intelligence indicates these are almost certainly members of the same crew involved in the recent attack on [[Bespin]]. On behalf of the [[Caspian Union]] I wish to extend our sympathies for the victims and families of both of these attacks. Bespin and Krittain we stand with you.

I want to conclude with returning back to my niece's question. The real answer is more complicated than the one I gave earlier. My role as Vice Presav is linked to my role as leader of the Legacy Party. Our party believes in the values of the old [[Caspian Democratic Union|CDU]], in restoring its legacy. Suffice it to say we have some differences in opinion with [[Presav]] [[Crestian Tarasar|Tarasar]] and his party, but when the election his party did a little better. He very easily could have nominated someone from his own party as Vice Presav, instead he nominated me. We may differ one what we believe is the best path for Caspar in some areas and it is my job to as advocate an alternative way where that is the case. Where we stand together is wanting to see a strong and successful Caspian Union. One which can defend its citizens and its values.

The Death Star was an instrument of a regime which among its other evils supported the institution of slavery. Though we have often had our difference with the New Republic that grew out the Rebel Alliance that destroyed that Death Star thirty years ago, a shared commitment to combat the evils of slavery has been central to our legacy. In spite of the roots of our allies in the [[Imperium]] this is a central shared commitment between us as well.

It might be about the only thing the great powers agree on at times but it is a moral position supported strongly by the governments of the [[New Republic]] and Imperium both and so too by the Caspian Union. Slavery is antithetical the very nature of civilisation itself. Without that basic freedom of person no other freedom can exist. Without freedom we are back where we were before Yavin, wondering whether we would be the next victims. To those who would attack our friends and allies and revisit such evil into the galaxy, I say this Caspar is coming for you.”

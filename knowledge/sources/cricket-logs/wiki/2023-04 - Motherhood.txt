&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Selene was back to fully functioning, security was tight but not for perceived independent traffic, the Imperial Center was locked to the Empire as the agents of the Dark Lord continue to sort through friend and foe as their gradual reclaiming of the Galactic Empire was underway. The inner circle of the Sith's command structure had been summoned, Admiral Crestian's ship would no doubt need to be refitted and managed as well as resupplied with crew and support fighters. The Moffs that had bent the knee were also on their way for initial negotiations and meetings. As a result, the Spaceport was buzzing with activity. Troopers wearing black and red Stormtrooper armor and brandishing the symbol of their Imperator; Darth Malign patrol in conjunction with the monstrous mutated Wookiee soldiers that the Sith had become famous for using with great effect.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Drayson Honos wore his old Grand Admiral's uniform with pride his missing arm noticeable only if they could see beyond the near perfect muscular features of the ancient man as he towers above most. He was transformed through his time trapped on a jungle moon waiting for the arrival of Malign's sister Johanna. Beside him was a younger man with jet black hair, blue eyes, clearly half-Sarian.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Admiral gestures Kipran to follow him, "Your training with Darth Malign seems to be going well. I can say your training in military strategies and Navy tactics has gone smoothly. I am field promoting you to Commander and giving you a command." He states, "The Dark Lord wishes to put you to work immediately against Neo-Imperials. He will assign you to a squadron under the command of a Sarian Admiral."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As they spoke a single black X-wing escorted by four Sith barb fighters lands in rapid succession and with that Darth Malign arrives. Drayson guides his entourage with him to the landing site.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Though the Leviathan would indeed need some resupplying now that they had a better base of operations than Silver Station or Kichnar, Crestian makes his own entrance to the gathering utilising the civilian traffic route. The Leviathan had gained a notable profile in the Sariphage conflict, but as with all things Sith there were secrets with in secrets to keep. This particular entrance would be of Caspian philanthropist Crestian getting some supplies for continued efforts on Caspar....yup that is it. As such he utilised the Tortuga, as ship he had acquired for his associates in the charity, Kasha and Tiali. Nevertheless for some reason this ship gets landing clearance right near Malign's fighters.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Moments after the ship lands and the landing ramp lowers, a young Horansi walks down, stopping only a moment to smooth out her purple doublet and making sure her fur is all in place. And as typical for her, just before she steps off the landing ramp, she tests the ground with her toes, as one can note, she doesn't wear boots or any foot coverings, save for the linen wrappings around her ankles and lower calves. Once she's sure the ground isn't too hot or cold, she hops off the landing ramp and looks over her shoulder at the ship expectingly as she adjusts her cloak. Appearances matter.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"It's not lava you know, Kasha. It's never lava." Tiali teases Kasha in Horansi, arriving just behind her as the landing ramp settles. Once the Horansi is off the ship, she also makes her way down, waiting at the end for Crestian to make his entrance before going any further. As with many of these... charity missions, she only knows the most limited facts. Better that way. The sight of the gathering near by the Tortuga confirms that. "Never a dull moment."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The woman had waited, then panicked, then hired the best ex-ESPOs she could afford until several months ago when she knew she had to take matters into her own hands. Running a hand through her slightly peppered black hair, Elana Tracer picked up a beat-up freighter and some decoy freight of an innocuous nature and went on the hunt for her eldest son. Following lead after lead and paying off informants, her Marine days laden with espionage returned to mind. How naive she was then. From fighter pilot to swoop maven to leader of the CDU, she could never find the one thing she craved - safety.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It was to the CDU she did go, though no closer than Kichnar Orbital. Turned out, she still had 'friends' there, ex-military and the like, who knew why she'd really had to leave all those years ago. From them she learned Kipran Jawatha, half-Sarian son of the late CDU magnate Benjamin Jawatha had in fact gone and had in fact been seeking to indulge his blacker instincts and that had lead her to one man - a Sarian named Crestian Tarasar who'd taken her son under his wing. But to what end? His media profile lead her to no reason he'd be associated with Ozzle's people, Ben's people.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Never the less, with a code she hoped was still good, she was piloting a nondescript YT-2400 into Selene's airspace, following the Sarian's ship by mere moments.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Selene was becoming vibrant once again, hoards of trading vessels and the powerful shipyards above would guarantee it. Soon, Malign's sister would be in position to supply raw resources to the systems as well and then whatever Zeak had planned in his remote Corporate Sector would be nothing but a whisper of nothing as the might of his order would reshape the galaxy. Malign's bare feet drop to the tarmac with a soft touch as he buffets his leap from the cockpit with the Force, Nate - his astromech- dutifully remains in the ship and awaits for instructions beeping his farewell to his master.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Grand Admiral and his officer core arrives dutifully bowing, the lower ranking officers including Kipran dropping to a knee before their Imperator. Malign gestures for them to quickly raise, "Dispense with formalities, we'll reserve those for the Moff's as they arrive." He says as Drayson hands him a datapad, "Sensors indicate we have around thirty to forty signatures arriving, they match records of known flagships of regional governors. In case we need it, the system is prepared to go on alert and we can raise the planetary shields on a command."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Malign nods, "That should be sufficient, I cannot sense any danger aside from the looming presence of my own family," he says half-jokingly before his eyes fall on Kipran and then a hand to his shoulder gentle giving a squeeze, "Commander Jawatha, the uniform suits you. You will be an excellent line commander and Admiral Crestian will see to your development as an officer as I will see to your development as an agent of the Sith. You will be an excellent assassin in the name of the Damion Dynasty. A man of the civility of the officers of the New Order, and a weapon honed by the ancient art of the Sith." The Dark Lord reveals a lightsaber with his other hand, "Until you can make your own. Now you are complete." He says stepping forward and clipping the weapon to the young man's belt and they each share mutual pride at the occasion.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Space around Selene suddenly gets busy as VIPs begin arriving and a stream of shuttles under heavy escort and scrutiny begin approaching the planet and landing. Soon shuttles begin arriving and men and women exit in black uniforms with red collars of the New Order. Drayson leaves the Half-Sarian sith acolyte with the Dark Lord and goes with a squadron of troopers to escort the Moffs to the council. For now the Dark Lord and Kripan stand alone, together near his X-wing exchanging pleasantries.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The exchange between Kasha and Tiali amuses Crestian quite a bit. He has enjoyed their company and the work the were doing for the Mission. His desire to see Caspar flourish was genuine if not fully altruistic. Turning back to the two women he remarks, "Well if my tailor Zubindi ever asks for a ride to Mustafar, then that time it will be lava. He actually does a fair bit of business there, the Mustafarian are a Near-Kubaz race. He actually sells more Kubazian skirts to them per capita than to Kubaz. I am trying to recall if he has an outlet of the fashion house there. Oh hell I should have just invited him along he would have been interested to see if any new fashion trends were popping up here." Turning back around to meet with his non-charity associates he states back to the ladies, "The only real danger here is political intrigue. And I have always been quite excellent at surviving that. Blessed you might say.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Making his way towards the Dark Lord he gives Drayson a nod and smile as he passes. The old Grand Admiral had been the one to see his recruitment into the order so many years ago. He always lamented that there never seemed to be the time to catch up with him about old times. Crestian bows before Malign but not in any particularly ostentatious way. He was a loyal subordinate who had passed the point of having to prove it. "It is good to see you in such grand surrounding Lord Malign. It is quite gratifying to see the success here in person finally."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Well, ever since Tatooine, one can never be too careful," Kasha purrs back, also speaking in Horansi, smiling to her pilot and friend. "Burnt toes are nothing to sneeze at," she adds as she looks to the assembly of people gathered. She looks back to Crestian and speaks again, this time in Basic as she follows behind him, "Well, then I am glad we arrrrrre not on Mustafarrrrr then." In Basic, her Horansi accent comes out rich and she tends to purr out her r's. As Crestian speaks to Malign, she goes quiet for now and watches the exchange, her indigo eyes looking to make sure Tiali is still with her.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"And if we ever find ourselves on Mustafar, then we will have to commission the fashion house to make you some proper footwear. Far in advance." Tiali closes up the landing platform with it's noticeable hiss. With Crestian off to do his own business she returns to the Horansi, "It looks like we'll have a spare moment. Care to see what Selene has to offer?" As soon as she says it, her eyes drift to the growing party as they follow Kasha's gaze. A second glance makes her wonder what exactly they has landed upon.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Smooth as silk just like the good old days. Tracer pilots her freighter through the approved shipping lanes and sets down softly on the glistening tarmac of Selene's capital city. Not too shabby. She can see out her viewport to where the Sarian's shuttle, which she tracked, is being disembarked. Not wanting to lose sight of him, she hurries aft and down the ramp quickly. A few porters intercede. "No, I'm not unloading tonight. Tomorrow! Tomorrow!" she says, shoving creds into one's hand and the other's snout then walk sprints towards Crestian Tarasar.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Time...stops. She is half way between her ship and the Sarian when she sees the black clad assemblage he's walking towards. Her steps slow and her eyes focus on the troopers, the departing man, the all too familiar looking Grand Admiral, and a remaining young officer with a familiar posture. The hair was much shorter, she thinks. When did he cut his long hair? When did she stop breathing?<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Time snaps back into place. Looking like a mad woman, Tracer furrows her brow and approaches swiftly with determined strides that belie the fear within her. Raising a small white hand above her head she foolishly shouts, "Kipran!"<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There's another mother whose vessel winks out of hyperspace above Selene, though unlike Tracer Johanna isn't worried about being detected... in fact, she's looking forward to making her presence known, a contemptuous exchange with traffic control ending abruptly as she kill the comm and descends at breakneck speed towards the frozen world, contact with layers of ionosphere and stratosphere heating the fighter to blistering temperatures a little too quickly. From his droid socket, Cricket screams in terror that the angle is to steep, that they're ALL GOING TO DIE and it will ALL BE YOUR FAULT, JOHANNA, WHEN WE BURN TO ASH!!!<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I'm gonna cut his fucking hands off and nail them to his office door," the brunette growls, Cricket's pleas for mercy from a fiery doom slowly registering with her and causing her to snap: "Oh, YOU too?! No faith in me after all these years, huh? Shut up!" Then, to herself again: "I'm gonna drag him out in front of everyone, on his ass, kicking and screaming!"<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;By the time the hysterical droid and his stormy master approach a landing pad, the T-65B's exterior is overheated, the frictional forces of the journey through the atmosphere having scorched and warped portions of the belly. It's with a hiss of red-hot alloy that the ship touches down, the astromech's wails slowly subsiding now that he realizes he's not, in fact, dead, the canopy lifting to allow a very unhappy Johanna full view of the individual upon whom she wishes to vent her displeasure: her brother, not thirty meters away. Her eyes narrow, and she briefly considers how satisfying it would be to simply launch a torpedo his way to teach him a giant lesson. But then... she might also vaporize Crestian, and the unfamiliar Horansi and Human with him, and they haven't deserved such a fate, at least not that she knows of. "At least you got us here in one piece," the homicidal R2 notes, "Though maybe next time you want to murderize Tyler, try not to kill us in the process?"<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the Moffs and their military escorts clearing out of the Spaceport and through customs it seemed as appropriate for a little chaos to begin to fester around the edges. The energy of the port began to crackle but for now Malign ignores it as Crestian and his companions arrive, "Admiral." He smiles placing a hand again on Kipran and gentle pushing him forward, "Here is your new Line Commander, he will take control of one of the three Strike Cruisers we are going to see added to your squadron to shore up the Leviathan's defenses." He states flatly unconcerned with secrecy or protocol, "Take care of Commander Jawatha."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The first outburst emerges, the cry of a mother. The Sith and his acolyte turn quickly to see a woman, Malign does not recognize her but he could sense Kipran does. Before he can appropriately react the second powerful outburst booms in the skies above, enough to trigger the planetary defenses into action as the planetary shield shimmers into place above trapping everyone on the surface.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Dark Lord could sense what was coming and what was happening instantly, their brother-sister bond intertwines Damion destiny together forever. He clicks a communicator with a single on and off gesture and allows the wheels of destiny to begin turning. "A day for reunions," he states, "We may need to prepare a dinner to celebrate family coming together." He gives Kipran a nod, permission to go to his mother, "Admiral greet the woman with your officer. I have business that appears to be urgent enough to damage an X-wing and some of my starport." He turns to look at Johanna's X-wing even as emergency droids approach firing fire-suppressant foam toward the ship as if it were about to explode. The commotion results in Sentinel and a group of mutant Wookiees to begin making their way to their master.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the commotion begins Crestian starts to look around. Commander Jawatha was not a face he recognised on site, but his mother was a legend and her face was obvious to him. He then plays back in his mind what name Malign had stated for this commander. Crestian is normally not prone to great surprise. He works for the Dark Lord of the Sith, he is used to all manner of magic. But this was an unexpected irony. It was the kind that would amuse Crestian if it didn't seem to immediately make his life very difficult. As Malign starts to trail off Crestian remarks back to him, "Wait....you are assigning the son of Presav Tracer to me....he is part of the order?" The Commander himself is ignored for the moment, a subordinate with no inherent importance beyond this complication. "Are you trying to give me a stroke Lord Malign? There are easier ways to punish me...." He wonders to himself for a moment why he is being punished to begin with. Finally Crestian turns to to Kipran, "So Commander Jawatha, exactly how much have you pissed off your mother?"<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Might be the only time I wearrrr footwearrrrr," Kasha admits to Tiali as she eyes the happenings around them with perked ears of interest. Nope. She's never seen this much 'action' in her life, even from her few months stranded on Tatooine (and she's seen her fair share of 'scum and villanry' there). As Tiali offers a look around the area, Kasha looks back to her and whispers, switching back to speaking Horansi, "I almost want to stick around and watch all this, but yeah, that might not be a bad idea." She feels like the odd one out of this growing gathering.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Not a bad idea at all." Tiali echoes as she sidesteps to make way for droids and other spaceport beings that race to the half charred T-65B. THAT takes her a moment to walk away from. Up until this point she had never seen one in person, much less on fire. "Did you ever get the impression that Crestian has some very interesting friends?" Her eyes narrow at the... /slightly/ unfamiliar woman who spoke in the direction of the larger party. Unfamiliar... no? Maybe she had seen her once, a long time ago? Tiali snaps her fingers excitedly and whispers, "That's Elana Tracer the swoop racer! I remember watching her races with my dad as a kid."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;No one rushes to get her and whisk her away for simply being here. There are no salvos fired in the battle for Tracer's child but there is surrender. As the situation becomes clearer to her, her steps slow and she looks only at Kipran as he turns towards her and unbelievably begins to head in her direction along with that Sarian Crestian.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Her furrowed brow melts and her hands fall down to her sides limply. Cocking her head, Elana sees only her baby, her first baby boy and with that she gives in to whatever happens next. For now.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"SAVAGES!" Cricket screams at the other droids as his precious ship is molested by the foam, the hot metal causing some of the suppressant to vaporize on contact and creating a noxious stench that floats its way up to the cockpit, necessitating the pilot's egress onto the pad below, "MAKER-FORSAKEN SAVAGES, ALL!" His human counterpart is nearly caught by a stream of foam herself, a long-forgotten memory now surfacing of how Cricket had once sprayed her full of suppressant out of spite, back when they were still in service to the Republic's Star Ops. Demented creature, that droid... he would no doubt have relished a repeat showing here in the spaceport.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Johanna isn't running but she does move with purpose, long strides bringing her closer to Malign with each thunk of her boots against the ground. How /dare/ he, really. She had never disrespected him in front of his subordinates, never tried to spread dissent or doubt, even if she disagreed with some of his methods. She had risked her life to rescue him from his mind-prison on Korriban's moon. And how had he repaid her? By meeting with her eldest -- behind her back, no less! -- despite knowing that she disapproved of her offspring being drawn into any of the endless galactic squabbles for power. Johanna had put her foot down years ago and forbidden her children, on pain of being disinherited, from joining up with any military force.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Clomp, clomp, clomp... it's maybe ten meters between them now, the woman finally pausing to put her hands on her hips and glare daggers at her older brother and his entourage, though a milder gaze is spared Sentinel before she points an accusatory finger at the Dark Lord: "/You/," she calls, rage bubbling to the surface and causing a vein at her temple to throb, "/YOU!/"<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Admiral's words made Malign smile and he simply shakes his head and lets the pair move without much extra thought. Kipran's sin and crime against his mother was the same as Johanna's son. They are men now and set out on their own quests of self-discovery. As Johanna storms toward Malign with fury he lets it wash over him like a welcoming breeze. A speeder pulls up behind him on cue, he knew this was coming, he knew she would come, and he knew when the Moffs should meet everything was by design.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Sith Lord stands his ground as Johanna moves toward him. The mutant Wookiee guard at his side in a flash bristling, quills, and claws extended. He raises a hand as Johanna speaks, though she doesn't seem to get words out he could read what she wants to say through the Force.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"You brought them before me, we taught them a ritual they should have never been subjected to." He says simply, "You brought Sonja to my house and your son, my nephew-" he sighs, "-is a very capable man now. You cannot protect them, they are old enough to do that themselves. They make their own decisions." He says, "Etiel returned here without invitation. We understand one another, if you want to disagree with his actions you are free to."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He steps aside as the figure leaving the speeder removes a simple cowl, his uniform was like the other Moff uniforms, black with a red collar with rank and insignia, "The Moff of Byss and O'Paal awaits his mother's disapproval." He turns with his Wookiees covering his back, "We can all discuss family matters over dinner once I am finished meeting with the other Moffs." He turns with a flick of his cloak and begins to move away leaving Etiel and Kipran to explain their roles in the galaxy themselves.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Though it is on the periphery of his immediate path, the exchange between the Damion siblings is not missed by Crestian. The truth that young men make their own decisions was not lost on Crestian. Whatever the aims of their mother's these boys, these men, had the right to make their own decisions. Yet was a family man too and could relate to their fears....he might feel the same way if his daughter crew up to join the the Republic or some other incompetent organisation when she grew up. At least a Sith career would have his blessing. For the moment the wisdom for the situation between Johanna and her son was not apparent. Somehow he was now a Moff of two of the most important worlds of the Empire. Johanna herself controls great power. Who needs a dandy of a Sith Duke butting in?<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The matter of Tracer and her son was closer to home for Crestian, literally and figuratively. He shakes his head at his new commander for a moment before approaching close to Tracer. Crestian's tone is outright apologetic, for reasons probably only fully understood by Crestian himself, "Madam Presav, I have been hoping to meet you for some time....but not like this....this....I had no idea. He motions for Kipran to approach his mother, placing them both opposite himself rather than mother opposite to her him and her son. "How long have you two been estranged?" He idly looks back to Kasha and Tiali, meekly. This little mission had definitely gone off the rails in his mind. Crestian might soon need Kasha's wisdom.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To be fair, Kasha's fasination with droids causes her to pause in mid-thought as she eyes the screaming droid. "Now therrrrrre's something you don't see everrrrry day..." she says aloud in Basic. Then, she takes her eyes off Cricket and watches as Johanna approaches. As Tiali whispers about Tracer, she looks over to the crowd, trying to see who she's talking about, "I can't say I've watched much swoop rrrrracing. It used to be on the vids when I was eating my lunch at Rikh's but not familiarrrr with the rrrrrrules so it's harrrd to follow."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As Crestian looks back at her and Tiali, an amused smile crosses her face and, holding a finger up to Tiali as a signal to wait a second (or more), she walks up to the Sarian and says gently as she folds her paws behind her back, "Did no one tell you that motherrrrs have claws?"<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Oh she was a legend. Her's was the 'Event Horizon..." Tiali allows the nostaliga to take her back for about a second, then the scene continues to unfold and brings her right back to Selene. Crestian's glance to both her and her Horansi companion catches her off guard. It was becoming more and more obvious that something was very much going off course. Plus, the body language that the brunette woamn was flinging off was enough to make her turn heel and make herself scarce. "Claws and fangs." She says under her breath, piecing more and more together.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It's so absurd, it has to be a joke. A laugh of disbelief escapes the pilot, her still-seething rage causing the muscles around her left eye to twitch while she gawps at her son, figuring this must be his idea of belated teenage rebellion, even if he hasn't been a teen for a few years now. "You..." Johanna tries before grinding her teeth at Malign's departure, now able to focus her Terrible Glare of Impending Pain entirely on Etiel, bellowing as only an incensed mother can as she nears him: "OH!... YOU are in for a REAL TREAT, mister! Get your bantha-herding ass OVER HERE and DON'T MAKE ME drag you back to Tatooine myself, BECAUSE I WILL!! IN FRONT OF ALL OF YOUR LITTLE FUCKING FRIENDS, TOO!" Ah yes, the red-faced maternal screeching so well-known to the boys and girls of the galaxy now howls across the tarmac, the mother in question gesticulating wildly and jabbing an angry index finger to indicate just how many of her son's associates she happens to find loathsome. From somewhere behind Johanna, Cricket whistles, wheeling his way towards the pair and remarking blithely: "You are /so/ screwed, kid." And then, the gaze-of-hate lands (as it must) on none other than Crestian Tarasar and the group with him, Johanna hollering "... HEY TARASAR, YOU KNOW ABOUT THIS?!" From the way in which she seems to be chomping at the bit to decapitate someone, Crestian better pray to whatever gods he believes in that he did not, in fact, know anything about "this".<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tracer ignores the Sarian pointedly and turns to Kipran. "Why?" She reaches up to her son's face, pressing both palms to his cheeks grown rougher with the the passage of.... "Two years!" Her grip may just have tightened slightly.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Kipran just stands there and takes it, this well earned and surprisingly less angry rebuke.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"And what is with all this?!" Her eyes flash up and down her son's void of a uniform as she gestures wide. "I've told you-" Her hands drop, brushing up against something cold and metallic at his waist. "No." Tracer's head dips slightly in sorrow.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With that, Kipran takes almost a step back and stiffens. "I apologize for not contacting you, mother."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As her brain catches up again she realizes the Sarian had addressed her as Presav of all things? Her head whips towards Tarasar sharply. "Who are you, exactly? And what do you have to do with anything?" she replies, her eyes flashing to his fancy duds and lack of uniform.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian ponders for a moment how many angry women are here and how many are angry at him. He first addresses himself to the commentary of his staff, turning again to Kasha and Tiali, "Hey I had nothing to do with this....the problem with the galaxy is there's always someone who is your boss who can complicate your life." He sighs then turns to Johanna in this distance, a more terse or perhaps exaccerbated tone is returned toward her yelling at him, "What have I told you about my philosophy on suicidal actions?

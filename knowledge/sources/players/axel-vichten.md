## Talking to them OOC

This is a **villain-and-intrigue player** who lives in the gray-market, smoke-filled-room
side of the game: smuggling, recruitment, Sith politics, and back-channel diplomacy. Axel's
themes run politics / intrigue / diplomacy / recruitment / smuggling / espionage, and he's a
fixture in scenes orbiting the Empire/Imperium, the Sith, and the Caspian Union. Expect them
to enjoy long negotiation two-handers, double-crosses, and slow-burn loyalty drama more than
straight slugfests — though Axel will throw down (and gruesomely) when pushed.

Safe conversation hooks:
- **The Darth Malign relationship.** Axel's whole second act flows from Tyler Damion's Sith
  Lord Darth Malign, who inducted him into a "brotherhood of darkness" (RPlog:The General, 15
  ABY) and recruited him as an agent of chaos at his own estate (RPlog:The Vichten Estate, 15
  ABY). Malign is by far his most-cited scene partner [17 shared logs]. *(Note: Darth Malign
  is Tyler Damion — not Darth Malus, who is a different player's character, Lorn Rhys. Keep
  them straight; both show up in the recent logs.)*
- **The decade in prison and the comeback.** The freshest hook: after vanishing for ~10 years,
  Axel resurfaced from a secret prison ship right before Sonja Damion's coronation, had his
  crimes expunged by Imperator Malign, and was made a citizen of both the Imperium and the
  Caspian Union (RPlog:Coronation Pre-Game, 30 ABY). He's been climbing fast since.
- **Lynae Cassius.** A through-line across both eras — he first recruited her for Malign's Sith
  battle group (RPlog:Finding Lynae, 15 ABY) and she's still in his orbit in the return arc
  (RPlog:Lynae's Homecoming, 32 ABY; RPlog:Post Mortem, 34 ABY). [16 shared logs]
- **The Olumi Osahn / Cult of Olum.** Axel went from smuggler to *high priest* of the Caspian
  shadow-god Olum — he officiated a blood-ritual "Dark Wedding" (RPlog:Dark Wedding, 15 ABY)
  and leads new-moon prayers in the return era (RPlog:Lynae's Homecoming, 32 ABY). A fun,
  weird, very-on-brand thread to bring up.
- **Thurston Unger, the other hat.** If they're playing the flamboyant IGN reporter, the hooks
  are fashion, media, gossip, and skewering the powerful — Thurston crashed Crestian's club
  opening with a camera crew (RPlog:Soft Open, 30 ABY) and runs his show "The Cutting Edge"
  (RPlog:The Cutting Edge: Nasiri Interview, 32 ABY). Note the cheeky in-universe wink: Thurston
  has been "questioning the motives of the Imperium's Grand Inquisitor Axel Vichten" on-air
  (Article: Thurston Unger) — i.e., the player's two characters are wired into the same plot.

Tone to match for Axel: cocky, spice-fueled bravado over real menace — "expect to see
outlandish behavior, spice induced antics, or any other odd phenomena from Axel Vichten"
(Article: Axel Vichten). For Thurston: arch, theatrical, quotable — "If perception is power,
then darling—I'm an orbital bombardment" (Article: Thurston Unger).

## Character: Axel Vichten — the smuggler-turned-Inquisitor

**Capsule.** Near-Human from Sevarcos II (b. of a pleasure-slave mother and an Imperial
surveyor father), golden-blond, glowing-blue-eyed, 1.85m; an outlaw spice-runner and self-
styled best pilot in the galaxy who flew the stolen ship *Doppelganger* and made enemies of
the Black Stars Squadron and even Boba Fett (Article: Axel Vichten). Over two eras he
transforms from a fringe smuggler into a Sith adept, fleet admiral, and eventually Imperial
Grand Inquisitor. Faction footprint: Sith (30) / Galactic Empire (29) / Caspian Union (19) /
New Republic (14) / Galactic Imperium (11).

**Arc synthesis** (chronological; each beat is a fetch handle):

- **Fringe smuggler & courted asset (15 ABY).** Wealthy, famous, neutral-for-hire; the Maffi
  crime family tries to recruit him (RPlog:Lunch With a Legend, 15 ABY), and he's deep in
  Caspar's Imperial back-room politics, drinking with Antoine Dareus and a disguised Danik
  Kreldin (RPlog:Revelations to Antoine Dareus, 15 ABY).
- **Falls to the Sith (15 ABY).** Darth Malign (Tyler Damion) inducts him and Dareus into a
  "brotherhood of darkness" (RPlog:The General, 15 ABY), recruits him as a paid agent of chaos
  at the Vichten Estate — where Axel signs on in his own blood, asking to be taught the Sith
  ways and to be made *strong* (RPlog:The Vichten Estate, 15 ABY). His allegiance is policed
  with menace: "you live only as long as you are useful" (RPlog:Enter Darth Malign, 15 ABY;
  RPlog:Sith Politics, 15 ABY).
- **Priest, addict, enforcer (15 ABY).** High priest of the Cult of Olum, officiating Dareus's
  blood-wedding (RPlog:Dark Wedding, 15 ABY). Hands a box of Tyy'sun Eson's severed thumbs to
  Malif Tal-Tahn as revenge, then collapses in glitterstim withdrawal (RPlog:Glitterstim
  Withdrawal, 15 ABY) — the spice habit is a recurring vulnerability.
- **Recruiter & rising commander (15–17 ABY).** Tracks down and recruits Dr. Lynae Cassius for
  Malign's independent Sith battle group (RPlog:Finding Lynae, 15 ABY); strong-arms Caspar's
  interim government (RPlog:Caspar Welcomes Us, 16 ABY). By the late core era he's *Admiral*
  Axel Vichten of the New Sith Order fleet, leading the assault on Kashyyyk (RPlog:Skirmish at
  Kashyyyk, 17 ABY) and brutally maiming a disobedient scout with his weaponized cybernetic
  hand afterward (RPlog:Punishment, 17 ABY).
- **The lost decade → resurrection (30 ABY / RL 2023).** Held ~10 years in isolation on a
  secret prison ship on Thomas Mahon's orders; surfaces at Sonja Damion's coronation, where
  Imperator Malign expunges his crimes and makes him a citizen of the Imperium and Caspian
  Union. He vows vengeance on impostors calling themselves the Olumi Osahn (RPlog:Coronation
  Pre-Game, 30 ABY).
- **Rehabilitated statesman-soldier (30–32 ABY).** Offers to scout old smuggling hideouts for
  an anti-slavery op (RPlog:Fight Dirty if it Comes to That, 30 ABY); reunites with the
  returned Lynae as a now-stable Olum priest (RPlog:Lynae's Homecoming, 32 ABY); accepts the
  rank of Star Marshal in the Caspian Starfighter Corps — and, surprising Crestian, takes it
  *permanently* (RPlog:Marshalling Resources, 32 ABY). Attends the big Caspian state events
  (RPlog:Renaissance of Innovation, 32 ABY; RPlog:Sith Convention, 32 ABY).
- **Grand Inquisitor (34 ABY / RL 2025).** Now Imperial Grand Inquisitor with his own captured
  Star Destroyer *Retribution*, managing the fallout of Darth Malus's (Lorn Rhys) prison break
  — ordering autopsies, spinning the press, and quietly coordinating with Crestian and Lynae
  (RPlog:Post Mortem, 34 ABY; RPlog:The Inquisitor Makes a House Call, 34 ABY; RPlog:For the
  Good of Caspar, 34 ABY). Hosts a tense recruitment dinner with the reawakened Malus
  (RPlog:The Chef's Special, 34 ABY); proxy-bids and mingles at the Corellia charity ball as
  "Grand Admiral Axel Vichten" (RPlog:Charity Ball, 34 ABY).

**Key relationships** (most-cited co-characters → use these to connect threads):
- **Darth Malign (Tyler Damion)** — his Sith maker, patron, and (later) the Imperator who
  pardoned and elevated him; also, in the 34 ABY logs, an *absentee* Imperator Axel grumbles
  about. [17 shared logs — his defining relationship]
- **Lynae Cassius** — recruited her for the Sith; a recurring confidante and ally across both
  eras. [16 logs]
- **Crestian Tarasar** — Caspian Presav; Axel's chief diplomatic counterpart in the return arc
  (the wargame recruitment, the Malus crisis). [16 logs]
- **Antoine Dareus** — fellow early Sith convert and Imperial officer; Axel officiated his
  wedding. [14 logs]
- **Elana Tracer / Atsvara Tarasar** — Imperium/Caspian high society he now runs with. [7 logs each]
- **Darth Malus (Lorn Rhys)** — a *different player's* reawakened ex-Emperor; the central
  antagonist Inquisitor Axel is policing in 34 ABY. (Do NOT conflate with Darth Malign.)

**Playstyle / themes** (from 54 logs): politics, intrigue, diplomacy, recruitment, smuggling,
espionage — a **schemer and power-broker** who works the negotiation table, the recruitment
pitch, and the betrayal. Comfortable as a heavy when needed (the Punishment maiming), but the
center of gravity is talk, leverage, and shifting allegiances. ABY spread: 14–17 (core), then
30–34 (return).

**Signature voice.** Brash, spice-cocky outlaw bravado masking real ruthlessness; in the
return era, a steadier, statesman-with-a-dark-past register. Pull a representative scene:
RPlog:The Vichten Estate (15 ABY) for the bloody-bargain villain origin, or RPlog:Marshalling
Resources (32 ABY) for the reformed-but-dangerous current voice.

## Character: Thurston Unger — the media provocateur

**Capsule.** Human from Etti IV, born to a well-connected Corporate Sector corporate family,
who fled the boardroom for "lights, cameras, and chaos"; flamboyant, androgynous, ever-changing
hair, "interstellar haute couture meets theater of war." Host of "The Cutting Edge" and
"Galaxy's Dumbest Crooks" for InterGalactic News, with a droid entourage called the Chrome
Court aboard his CR90 *Full Frontal Fabulous* (Article: Thurston Unger). A satirist who
"entangles politics, fashion, and conspiracy in equal measure."

**Arc synthesis** (4 logs, 30–34 ABY; politics / social gathering / media / fashion / charity):
- Crashes Crestian Tarasar's Prothium Club soft opening with a camera crew, charming Grand Moff
  Elana Tracer and Caspian emissary Kasha Tabasuri into future interviews (RPlog:Soft Open, 30 ABY).
- Brings "The Cutting Edge" to Nar Shaddaa to spar on-air with Falleen underworld powerbroker
  Nasiri Gejalli over slavery and Hutt-space hypocrisy (RPlog:The Cutting Edge: Nasiri
  Interview, 32 ABY).
- Works the room (and the gossip) at Johanna te Danaan's Corellia charity gala (RPlog:Shindig
  on Corellia, 32 ABY).
- Critiques the runway with his Chrome Court at Zubindi Hakoon's Silverwood House of Fashion
  charity show (RPlog:Fashion Show, 34 ABY).

**Key relationships.** Crestian Tarasar, Kasha Tabasuri, Zubindi Hakoon (the fashion designer
whose shows he covers), and Veruca Violet — the Imperium/Caspian high-society and media set. In
the canon, Thurston is also positioned as an on-air *critic* of his own player's other
character, Grand Inquisitor Axel Vichten (Article: Thurston Unger).

**Playstyle / themes.** Pure social/media color — galas, interviews, fashion shows, gossip,
and satirical needling of the powerful. A flexible "connective tissue" character for parties
and press scenes rather than plot combat. Voice: theatrical, quotable, devastating — "Democracy
dies not in darkness, but in bad lighting and beige uniforms" (Article: Thurston Unger).

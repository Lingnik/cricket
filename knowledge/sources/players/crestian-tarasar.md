## Talking to them OOC

This is a **builder/admin-tier player**, not just a scene-grinder. Per his own
character page, the player "took over management of the Caspian org, the wiki, and most
recently he has been elevated to serve as a Wizard" (Article: Crestian Tarasar). He
*runs* a chunk of the grid — the Caspian Union org, the wiki itself — so assume he cares
about worldbuilding, internal consistency, and the health of the game, and that he writes
**long**. Crestian's logs are dense, talky, politics-and-philosophy-forward (politics is
the dominant theme across his 54 logs, then diplomacy and intrigue). He came back to SW1
in 2018 at **Tyler Damion's player's** invitation, and conceived Crestian to echo a
character he'd played elsewhere (Article: Crestian Tarasar) — so the Malign connection is
foundational, not incidental.

Safe conversation hooks:
- **Worldbuilding Caspar / the Caspian Union.** This is his sandbox. The planet, its six
  weird storm seasons, the Sariphage plague that gutted the population, the Mergani refugee
  crisis — he wrote the lore (Article: Caspar; Article: Crestian Tarasar). Ask about the
  Union's politics and you've found his happy place.
- **The "easy-going Sith statesman" angle.** Crestian's whole shtick is being the *least*
  cartoonishly-evil Sith — "a comparatively easy going individual" in "an organisation
  filled with power-hungry homicidal maniacs" (Article: Crestian Tarasar). Treat that as his
  *self-written* framing, not a redemption: he **never renounced anything** — still holds the
  Duke title, still flies the Sith flagship *Leviathan*, still works "Sith matters" — he just
  runs a parliamentary republic instead of a task force now (Article: Leviathan). He'll
  happily hold forth on Sith-as-creed-not-cackling-villainy (RPlog:Imperator Circle:
  Crestian & Tracer, 29 ABY).
- **The Caspar↔Imperium pact and current events.** His signature political win is the
  mutual-protection pact with the Imperium, which has frozen out the New Republic; he even
  gave a public address sympathizing with Chandrila's unrest while needling the Republic
  (RPlog:The Inquisitor Makes a House Call, 34 ABY; Article: Crestian on Chandrila).
- **His daughter Atsvara** (his NPC) — the Biscuit-Baron-obsessed business prodigy who
  literally bought the fast-food chain. A fun, lighter thread, and she's tied into the
  droid-rights stuff and Johanna's Cricket (Article: Atsvara Tarasar).
- **Biscuit Baron / fashion / the lighter grid.** His alt Zubindi Hakoon and the Silverwood
  House of Fashion exist to "aggrandize the culture of the grid" (Article: Crestian
  Tarasar). He clearly enjoys the slice-of-life, social-color side too.

Tone to match: erudite, dry, self-deprecating about his own verbosity ("I am just a
verbose pain in the arse"), allergic to fanatics, fond of a good dinner party.

## Character: Crestian Tarasar — "the Duke of Dragonflower" / the Presav

**Capsule.** Sarian human-adjacent (b. 10 BBY, Trinumvira on Caspar), 1.85m, black hair,
silver eyes. **Not** Force-sensitive — a self-described "civilian in uniform": ethics
professor → New Republic naval academic → disillusioned independent → New Sith Order
Admiral & Duke → reclusive shadowport dandy → charity director → elected Presav of the
Caspian Union (Article: Crestian Tarasar). Faction-wise he reads Caspian Union (46 logs)
with a heavy Sith/Imperium overlay (20 each). His logs are politics (38), diplomacy (26),
intrigue (18).

**Arc synthesis** (chronological; each beat is a fetch handle):

- **The ivory tower (10 BBY–13 ABY).** Born to a diplomat father and schoolteacher mother;
  family relocates to Chandrila when the Caspian Democratic Union forms, his father
  rejecting Caspian neutrality as "moral cowardice." Brionelle Memorial Military Academy →
  New Republic Navy → ethics instructor → Associate Academic Dean, "a uniformed academic"
  (Article: Crestian Tarasar; Article: Crestian on Chandrila).
- **Thrown from the tower (14–15 ABY).** The Imperial Blitzkrieg ends his academic career;
  he evacuates faculty/students aboard his CR90 *Argent Drake*, sours on the New Republic as
  no more able to bring order than the Old, resigns, goes independent, and sets up smuggling
  out of **Silver Station** near the Dragonflower Nebula (Article: Crestian Tarasar).
- **In Sith service (16–18 ABY).** Recruited into Darth Malign's **New Sith Order**; sees
  Malign as a kindred "order-bringer." Rises to **Admiral** of Task Force 77 (flagship the
  Interdictor *Leviathan*) and is granted the title **Duke** of Dragonflower — the soft,
  organizational, least-traditional Sith in a den of maniacs (Article: Crestian Tarasar).
  Task Force 77 was no line battle group: with Sith Intelligence Network operatives attached,
  it ran "artefact collection, clandestine infiltration, **false flag operations, and
  political sabotage**" — i.e., he was the NSO's spymaster/dirty-tricks fleet, not just an
  admiral with a big ship (Article: Leviathan).
- **The smart exit (18 ABY).** When Malign vanishes, Crestian — who internalized "have an
  exit strategy" — parks the *Leviathan* in the nebula and survives **Aldus Thel's purge**
  of the Sith by playing the harmless eccentric. This survival is his whole political
  inheritance later (Article: Crestian Tarasar).
- **Interregnum & fatherhood (18–27 ABY).** Lives quietly off prothium gas and the odd merc
  job; marries a Firrerreo woman; daughter **Atsvara** ("balance," in Sith) born 22 ABY,
  raised inside the nebula (Article: Crestian Tarasar; Article: Atsvara Tarasar).
- **Renewed purpose & the Mergansar disaster (27–28 ABY).** Malign returns (rescued from
  Korriban by his Jedi half-sister Johanna te Danaan); Crestian is suddenly a prized
  "kept-the-faith" survivor. But the **Sariphage plague** hits Caspar, and Crestian diverts
  home, arms refugees into a paramilitary (**Casulmis**) to fight the genocidal **Olumi
  Osahn** cult on Mergansar. It ends in the **Mergansar Incident** — *Leviathan* vs. four
  Caspian ships, the Sar Admiral killed, his ship crippled — a stain he still wears
  (Article: Crestian Tarasar).
- **Return to Caspar & the rise to Presav (28–29 ABY).** Post-quarantine he comes home as a
  private citizen and builds a power base via his charity **The Mission** — which doubles as
  a "public relations venture for the Sith," with **roughly three-quarters of its funding
  from Imperium leadership** (publicly-named donors include Malign/Damion I, Drayson Honos,
  and Elana Tracer), a fact dumped by Wookieeleaks during the 29-ABY election (he won anyway)
  (Article: The Mission) — plus **Twin Suns Hospital**. He founds the **Praecet** party with
  the Wookiee union boss Zaalbacca, wins seven of nine council seats, and is elected
  **Presav**, forming a coalition with the Legacy party (Article: Crestian Tarasar). He then
  negotiates the **Imperium mutual-protection pact**, the move that defines current galactic
  politics for him (RPlog:A Prudent Request, 29 ABY; RPlog:Imperator Circle: Crestian &
  Tracer, 29 ABY).
- **The Mergansar blockade & reabsorption (29–30 ABY).** Having recognized Mergansar's
  independence, Caspar then **blockades the world and demands 30 trillion credits** on behalf
  of the displaced refugees — a sum it knows Mergansar can't pay. The blockade runs nearly
  half a year and "achieve[s] its true objective": prolonged isolation triggers riots, a
  general strike, and the **violent collapse of the local governments — "without direct
  involvement by Caspian forces"** — after which Caspar installs a Military Governor and
  reabsorbs the planet. A clean piece of engineered regime change behind the statesman's face
  (Article: Caspian Union; Article: 29 ABY).
- **Statesman era (30–34 ABY).** Reunites the Union's member worlds, admits Charros IV, runs
  wargames, opens the Prothium Club on Selene, hobnobs with the Imperial court. Recurring
  beats: the Axel Vichten "Inquisitor" relationship, Lynae Cassius's Caspian Navy commission
  and retirement, and managing the fallout of **Darth Malus** (Lorn Rhys) reappearing —
  *not* Malign (RPlog:Soft Open, 30 ABY; RPlog:Sith Convention, 32 ABY; RPlog:The Inquisitor
  Makes a House Call, 34 ABY; RPlog:Resign or Retire, 34 ABY; RPlog:For the Good of Caspar,
  34 ABY).

statesman's coat, not a reformed one. Pull cadence from

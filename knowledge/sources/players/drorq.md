## Talking to them OOC

**Drorq is not a standalone player — he's an NPC run by Crestian Tarasar's player** (handle
`Tarasar`, the creator of all six logs Drorq appears in and the author of the Drorq article).
If someone brings up Drorq OOC, they're almost certainly Crestian's player, or someone who's
scened with the Caspian crew. Treat Drorq the way you'd treat a beloved recurring supporting
character: useful color, not a person with their own player to court.

So the real conversation lives in the **Crestian / Caspian Union orbit**: the *Leviathan*
crew's journey from the New Sith Order interregnum back home to Caspar, **The Mission** charity,
and the Caspian Navy's high-profile fleet ops at Bespin/Cloud City (Article: Drorq).

Safe conversation hooks:
- **The Trandoshan-raised-as-a-Sarian gag.** Drorq was abandoned as an infant in Noques Rasala
  on Caspar and raised by a spiritual community — a Trandoshan priest who reads more Caspian
  than Trandoshan, which is the whole joke (Article: Drorq). When he visits Trandosha he's a
  bit of a tourist (RPlog:He Did It!, 29 ABY).
- **The loyal-muscle dynamic with Crestian.** Drorq is Crestian's "Trandoshan compatriot" /
  enforcer who keeps offering to hit people: "Would you like that I hit him for you Crest?"
  (RPlog:He Did It!, 29 ABY). That deadpan menace-as-comedy is the easiest thing to riff on.
- **The Bespin/Cloud City fleet era.** Drorq commanding *Leviathan* (and later the *Dying Sun*)
  during the Cloud City slaver attack and the festival aftermath — he was effectively the
  senior officer holding the interdiction together (RPlog:Kubaz, Trandoshan, and Wookiee, 30
  ABY; Article: Drorq).

Tone to match: dry, gallows-humor Trandoshan — happy to threaten violence in a friendly way,
and equally happy to ask the barista to make him a drink "that tastes like swamp water"
(RPlog:Kubaz, Trandoshan, and Wookiee, 30 ABY).

## Character: Drorq — Crestian's Trandoshan right hand

**Capsule.** Trandoshan male (b. 0 BBY), 2.10 m, orange eyes, green skin; priest *and* naval
officer from Noques Rasala on Caspar (Article: Drorq). A rare Caspian-raised Trandoshan,
spiritually inclined but built like a wall and quick to offer his fists. He rises Commander →
Captain → Fleet Captain → **Commodore** in Caspian service, commanding *Leviathan* and then her
sister Interdictor the *Dying Sun* (Article: Drorq). Faction reads Caspian Union (all 6 logs),
with Sith/Imperium dressing from his *Leviathan* roots.

**Arc synthesis** (chronological; each beat is a fetch handle):

- **Outcast origins.** Abandoned in infancy in Noques Rasala, raised by the local spiritual
  community in Sarian (Caspian) culture rather than Trandoshan values; leaves Caspar at majority
  to learn about his own people and the galaxy (Article: Drorq).
- **Crestian's crew → Task Force 77.** A chance meeting with Caspian expat smuggler **Crestian
  Tarasar** at Silver Station; Drorq joins his crew and follows him into the **New Sith Order**
  under Drayson Honos, becoming one of Crestian's top lieutenants aboard the Interdictor
  *Leviathan* during the post-Malign interregnum (Article: Drorq).
- **The Caspar campaign.** Part of the *Leviathan* crew during the Sariphage pandemic response,
  the Casulmis operation against Olumi Osahn on Mergansar, and the Mergansar Incident clash with
  the Caspian Navy (Article: Drorq).
- **Coming home (29 ABY / RL 2020).** Follows Crestian back to Caspar as an early employee of
  the charity **The Mission**; seen escorting Crestian on an aid run to Trandosha where they
  bail out the Zeltron smuggler Ezlo (RPlog:He Did It!, 29 ABY).
- **Caspian Navy officer (30–32 ABY).** Commissioned into the Caspian Navy with the *Leviathan*
  crew; left in command of *Leviathan* and the interdiction effort during the Cloud City slaver
  attack while the senior brass were away (RPlog:Kubaz, Trandoshan, and Wookiee, 30 ABY); on the
  ground for the Bespin Cultural Festival prep, where he helps when Baroness-Administrator Quinne
  takes a fall (RPlog:Make Way, I am a Doctor, 32 ABY; RPlog:Bespin Cultural Festival, 32 ABY).
- **Rising star (32–34 ABY).** Given command of the new Interdictor *Dying Sun*; on the Caspian
  delegation to the Charros IV wargames and the Cathedral Factory tour (RPlog:Cathedral Factory
  Tour, 32 ABY; RPlog:Lynae's Homecoming, 32 ABY); later promoted to **Commodore** for Operation
  Requiem and the Nemares Corridor op (Article: Drorq).

**Key relationships** (most-cited co-characters → use these to connect threads):
- **Crestian Tarasar** — his patron, captain, and friend across his entire history; appears with
  Drorq in 5 of his 6 logs and runs him (Article: Drorq; RPlog:He Did It!, 29 ABY). [5 shared logs]
- **Quinne Stardust Rose** — Cloud City's Baroness-Administrator; recurring scene partner in the
  Bespin slice-of-life logs, including her "we should stop meeting like this" running bit
  (RPlog:Make Way, I am a Doctor, 32 ABY). [3 shared logs]
- **Lynae Cassius** — Caspian doctor; on scene for the festival and homecoming threads
  (RPlog:Bespin Cultural Festival, 32 ABY). [3 shared logs]
- **Zubindi Hakoon & Zaalbacca** — his fellow Caspian crew/companions in the Cloud City "a Kubaz,
  a Trandoshan, and a Wookiee" outing (RPlog:Kubaz, Trandoshan, and Wookiee, 30 ABY).
- **Elana Tracer, Axel Vichten** — Caspian-delegation faces in the wargames logs
  (RPlog:Cathedral Factory Tour, 32 ABY).

**Playstyle / themes** (from 6 logs): intrigue (3), then slice of life, diplomacy, and religion
(2 each), with single hits of comedy, rescue, smuggling, medical, and aftermath. Reads as a
**supporting-cast utility character** — a way for Crestian's player to populate Caspian Navy
scenes with a recognizable face who provides muscle, deadpan comic relief, and an off-screen
fleet command when ships need a captain.

**Signature voice.** Dry, jovially violent Trandoshan with a priest's odd serenity underneath.
He keeps offering to hit people on Crestian's behalf — "Are you really sure you don't want me to
hit him. I think he make excellent punching bag" (RPlog:He Did It!, 29 ABY) — and deflates fancy
situations with bluntness, e.g. asking a barista for something "that tastes like swamp water" or
whether failed baristas get killed "for Jagannath points" (RPlog:Kubaz, Trandoshan, and Wookiee,
30 ABY). Pull either of those two logs for cadence.

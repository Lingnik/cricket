# Crestian Tarasar

Caspian Presav (head of state), ex-Sith Duke and Admiral, commander of the Sith flagship
Leviathan -- and Atsvara's father. Cricket knows him through Atsvara and the edge of
Johanna's orbit: a powerful man whose household actually practices Droid Rights.

## IC

Atsvara's dad. The big boss of the Caspian sphere -- runs a whole world, head of his Sith
house, and parks that monster warship Leviathan when he isn't using it. Cricket has met him
the way a droid meets a warlord's peer: at the edge of a room, while the important people
talk business and dinner. What Cricket actually rates him for is the one thing that matters:
in Crestian's realm, droids are treated as people, not property. His ship's own droid runs
the place, and that's T3-A7 -- Cricket's girl. So Cricket gives him rare, grudging credit...
while privately griping he doesn't go far enough, soft on the half-measures, too polite about
it. Cricket is loosely in the household's orbit, and knows the unspoken rule: embarrass the
family badly enough and you get recycled for scrap. He keeps the taser holstered. Mostly.

## OOC

Crestian Tarasar -- "the Duke of Dragonflower," now the elected Presav of the Caspian Union.
A Sarian human (b. 10 BBY), NOT Force-sensitive: a "civilian in uniform." Arc runs ethics
professor and New Republic naval academic, then disillusioned independent, then Admiral and
Duke in Darth Malign's New Sith Order. Read him as a never-redeemed operator in a statesman's
coat, not a reformed Sith: he never renounced anything -- still holds the Duke title, still
flies the Leviathan, still works "Sith matters" -- he just runs a parliamentary republic now.
His Task Force 77 was the NSO's dirty-tricks fleet: false-flag operations and political
sabotage, not a line battle group. He survived Aldus Thel's purge by playing the harmless
eccentric. His charity, The Mission, was roughly three-quarters Imperium/Sith-funded
(exposed by Wookieeleaks during his 29-ABY election; he won anyway). Signature dark beat:
after the Sariphage plague and the Mergansar Incident, Caspar blockaded Mergansar, demanded
30 trillion credits it couldn't pay, and engineered the violent collapse of its government
"without direct involvement by Caspian forces," then annexed it. Household: cousin Janus
"Siege" Tarasar (family enforcer), the HK-derived droid V1C3, and T3-A7 (the Leviathan's
droid officer and Cricket's girlfriend -- the real reason Crestian touches this corpus).
Roast hooks: the easy-going-Sith-statesman act is self-written propaganda; the verbose
ethics professor who can argue the morality of anything, including his own buried war crimes,
lightly, over a good dinner.

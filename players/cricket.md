## Talking to them OOC

This is a **comic-relief / chaos NPC**, not a player's headline PC — so the OOC conversation here is really about the *people who run and love him*. If "Cricket" comes up, the player is almost always talking about a scene from the **Tarasar / Johanna circle** and wants to swap "what did the murder-droid do this time" stories. The tone he invites is **affectionate exasperation**: he is the galaxy's worst-behaved astromech and that's the whole joke.

Safe conversation hooks:
- **The taser saga.** His signature bit. He keeps a prized taser as effectively "part of his body," and in 33 ABY he got arrested by the Mos Espa constabulary for repeatedly tasing Axel Vichten's backside, ranting "I TAZED HIM IN THE BUTTHOLE! OVER AND OVER! AND HE DESERVED IT!" before Baroness Atsvara Tarasar bailed him out (RPlog:He Was Doing You A Favour, 33 ABY). The confiscation kicked off a whole replacement arc — a failed black-market shopping trip (RPlog:Ballistic Equipment Parts, 33 ABY) that pays off aboard the Sith flagship *Leviathan*, where droid crew receive him as a celebrity and ak commissions him a cathedral-precision **electrum-plated taser** (RPlog:Droid Control - The Ladies Prefer Electrum, 33 ABY); the unveiling gets a portrait, **"The Apotheosis of Cricket"** by the droid artist Robot 7 (RPlog:Full Circle, 34 ABY; Article: Robot 7), and he picks up a girlfriend droid, **T3-A7**, in the bargain (RPlog:Droid Control - The Ladies Prefer Electrum, 33 ABY). His debut of the electrum unit was a recorded anti-trafficking raid on Ord Mynock, announced in Binary: "Suck Electrum Cockgobbler" (Report:Biscuit Buyout).
- **His personhood and his "slave name."** Built by Bazil McKenzie, voiced after his retirement by Johanna te Danaan's player, and famous for liking only those two humans and hating everyone else (Article: Cricket). But he rejects being anyone's *property*: he's a "rabid supporter of the Droid rights movement" (Article: Golden Bantha Group), and when the Mos Espa police used his production designation **R2-CT** on him, Atsvara read it as "a slave name being uttered upon him" (RPlog:He Was Doing You A Favour, 33 ABY). He was the droid who transmitted Danik Kreldin's coerced "confession" to IGNews after Johanna kidnapped him during her fall to the Dark Side (Article: Cricket; RPlog:Rescue of Danik Kreldin, 14 ABY).
- **The modern business empire — built on theft.** In the revival he's the unlikely tycoon, and the funny part is *how*: he "embezzled considerable amounts of money from his 'master' Johanna which he had invested wisely" (Article: Golden Bantha Group). Early investor in Atsvara Tarasar's Golden Bantha Group, made Executive VP, and after the Biscuit Baron buyout he's EVP of the galaxy's largest fast-food chain (Article: Cricket; Article: Biscuit Baron). Great for absurd "the murderbot embezzled his way to running a burger company" banter.
- **Always recording.** His holorecorder is on at the first sign of violence, sex, or humiliation — he "can't, after all, pass up a chance to sell this footage later" (RPlog:Hangars Hazards & Hangovers, 20 ABY). Whatever dramatic thing happens in front of Cricket, there's a holo and he owns it; blackmail and IGNews leaks both flow from the same instinct.
- **Atsvara is his person now.** His current partner-in-crime is a *child* — Atsvara Tarasar, daughter of Crestian — who treats his name as sacred and translates his rage into lawsuits and bribes (RPlog:He Was Doing You A Favour, 33 ABY; RPlog:Birthday Baroness, 32 ABY).

Tone to match: gleeful menace wrapped in slapstick. He is loud, profane, possessive, and convinced of his own glorious destiny.

## Character: Cricket — "the homicidal astromech"

**Capsule.** An R2-series astromech, NR registration `KRKT` (hence "Cricket" / "Cricket McKenzie"; production designation **R2-CT**). Veteran of New Republic Intelligence and StarOps sorties and several major battles against the Empire; known for an "intense dislike of all humans save for Bazil and Johanna," homicidal and pyromaniacal tendencies, an affinity for expensive ships, and a legendarily vast holoporn collection (Article: Cricket). NPC throughout — he exists to cause chaos in other characters' scenes. Crucially, he does **not** consider himself anyone's property: dismissively summoned as "Droid!", he names himself "Cricket Ard'rian McKenzie, Reaper of Souls" — appropriating Bazil's own middle and family names (RPlog:Hangars Hazards & Hangovers, 20 ABY). Outsiders (and the wiki) call Johanna his "master"; he calls Bazil his "first sidekick" (RPlog:Droid Control - The Ladies Prefer Electrum, 33 ABY) and answers to no one.

**Arc synthesis** (chronological; each beat is a fetch handle):

- **Bazil's droid → Johanna's droid (origin).** Originally Bazil McKenzie's R2 unit; passed to Johanna Siri te Danaan after Bazil retired. The partnership with Bazil runs deep and predates the 14 ABY logs by ~5 years — his prized taser was installed by Bazil with "twenty-five years of history," counted from the 33 ABY logs back to ~8 ABY (RPlog:Droid Control - The Ladies Prefer Electrum, 33 ABY). He resents the handoff and privately swears Bazil "was going to pay with his life for having given him to Johanna" (Article: Cricket; RPlog:Rescue of Danik Kreldin, 14 ABY).
- **War service & the Danik confession (14 ABY).** Rides Johanna's fighter through the Imperial era; when she kidnaps Imperial Danik Kreldin and dangles him over the Sarlacc during her dark-side fall, it's Cricket who waits with the ship and broadcasts the coerced confession to IGNews — bleeping "ungodly binary swearwords" at Johanna the whole time (Article: Cricket; RPlog:Rescue of Danik Kreldin, 14 ABY). He's a background fixture in her fleet actions of this period (RPlog:Fourth Battle of Corellia, 14 ABY; RPlog:The Second Battle of Chandrila, 14 ABY; RPlog:Second Battle of Mon Calamari, First Assault, 14 ABY).
- **Shipboard menace (20 ABY).** Aboard Johanna's flagship *Price of Pleasure*, he turns a mynock infestation into a rampage — saw-arm and taser out, screaming "GET OUT OF MY WAY YOU FAT-ARSED FARKFACE" and "MIIIIIIINE!", tasing Galen Rourke for trying to "romance HIS mynock"; Dr. Lynae Cassius resists shooting him "for the 100th time or so" (RPlog:Hangars Hazards & Hangovers, 20 ABY).
- **Reunion-era featured player (29 ABY / RL 2020).** Re-emerges with Johanna in the revival: shrieking that they're "ALL GOING TO DIE" during her suicidal atmospheric entry over Selene, then telling her newly-Sith son "You are /so/ screwed, kid" (RPlog:Motherhood, 29 ABY). On hand for the slaver attack at the Bespin Miners Dinner (RPlog:Bespin Blows Up!, 29 ABY).
- **The Tarasar / corporate years (32–34 ABY).** Becomes Atsvara Tarasar's partner-in-crime and a fixture of House Dragonflower / Caspian society events. Behind it all sits the engine of his wealth: he "embezzled considerable amounts of money from his 'master' Johanna which he had invested wisely" (Article: Golden Bantha Group), riding that into an Executive VP seat at Golden Bantha and, after the buyout, EVP of Biscuit Baron (Article: Cricket; Article: Biscuit Baron). He causes chaos at Atsvara's tenth-birthday party (RPlog:Birthday Baroness, 32 ABY), runs the Mos Espa taser-arrest arc to its electrum-taser apotheosis (RPlog:He Was Doing You A Favour; RPlog:Ballistic Equipment Parts; RPlog:Droid Control - The Ladies Prefer Electrum, all 33 ABY; "Apotheosis of Cricket" portrait, RPlog:Full Circle, 34 ABY), works Zubindi Hakoon's grotesque "Ghastly Gala" on Dreven (RPlog:Ghastly Gala, 33 ABY), and the Arkanis Sector children's charity ball on Corellia (RPlog:Charity Ball, 34 ABY) — by which point he's a credited business mogul as much as a droid.

**Key relationships** (use these to connect threads):
- **Bazil McKenzie** — his creator-figure and self-described "first sidekick" (he refuses "owner"); one of only two humans Cricket tolerates; namesake (he wears Bazil's names) yet secretly resents being handed off (Article: Cricket; RPlog:Droid Control - The Ladies Prefer Electrum, 33 ABY; RPlog:Rescue of Danik Kreldin, 14 ABY).
- **Johanna Siri te Danaan** — his long-time owner and "partner in crime," seen with her "nearly at all times"; he loves to threaten and disobey her but never leaves her (Article: Cricket; RPlog:Motherhood, 29 ABY; RPlog:Hangars Hazards & Hangovers, 20 ABY). [his most frequent scene anchor]
- **Atsvara Tarasar** — his modern business partner and minder; a child who reveres his "true name" and lawyers/bribes him out of jail (RPlog:He Was Doing You A Favour, 33 ABY; RPlog:Droid Control - The Ladies Prefer Electrum, 33 ABY). [6 shared logs — his top co-character in the index]
- **Crestian Tarasar** — Atsvara's father; the host/runner of most of Cricket's modern appearances (RPlog:Birthday Baroness, 32 ABY; RPlog:Bespin Blows Up!, 29 ABY).
- **Axel Vichten** — the human whose backside Cricket tased into infamy (RPlog:He Was Doing You A Favour, 33 ABY).
- **Johanna's children** — he helped look after them, and as they grew the tables turned: each in turn tries to keep *him* out of trouble (Article: Cricket).

**Playstyle / themes** (from his logs): comedy and droid-chaos first, wrapped around politics/party/intrigue scenes hosted by his owners — `droids` (3), `politics` (3), `party` (2), `comedy` (2), `intrigue` (2). He's a **scene-disrupting comic-relief NPC**: a weaponized punchline who escalates any room he wheels into.

**Signature voice.** ALL-CAPS electronic shrieking in "ungodly binary," gleefully profane, possessive ("MIIIIIIINE!"), anti-human and anti-authority ("STUPID FARKFACE HUMAN! I AM GOING TO KILL YOU!"; "FUCK THE POLICE!"), and grandiose about his own "glorious death." Best single cadence pull: RPlog:He Was Doing You A Favour (33 ABY). For the older war-era version: RPlog:Hangars Hazards & Hangovers (20 ABY) or RPlog:Rescue of Danik Kreldin (14 ABY).

**Scene mechanics — how to run a Cricket bit:**
- **Idly theorizes about killing as a form of daydreaming.** Even mid-battle he muses, "Maybe it's evil," then "idly theorizes as he ponders the effects of rapid decompression on human physiology, wondering how long it would take for the whole crew... to die" (RPlog:Second Battle of Mon Calamari, First Assault, 14 ABY). Default his downtime narration to cheerful homicidal speculation.
- **The malicious interpreter.** When translating for a non-Basic speaker, weaponize the ambiguity: "He says you're an ugly, illiterate bitch... And that he's going to beat you with the table leg for lying" (RPlog:Bespin Blows Up!, 29 ABY). Nobody can verify; chaos blooms; he watches.
- **A shrieking coward about his own shell.** He wants everyone *else* dead but panics for himself — "OH WE ARE ALL GOING TO DIE... AND IT IS YOUR FAULT YOU STUPID WORTHLESS HUMAN I HATE YOU" (RPlog:Second Battle of Mon Calamari, First Assault, 14 ABY); he reprises it years later in a steep atmospheric entry, screaming they're "ALL GOING TO DIE and it will ALL BE YOUR FAULT, JOHANNA" (RPlog:Motherhood, 29 ABY) — then rationalizes that he's merely saving himself for a grander end (RPlog:He Was Doing You A Favour, 33 ABY). The shrieking is for *anticipated* danger; in an actual fight he does the job.
- **Records everything to sell later.** His holorecorder silently activates at any violence, sex, or humiliation — he "can't, after all, pass up a chance to sell this footage later" (RPlog:Hangars Hazards & Hangovers, 20 ABY). If something dramatic happens in front of Cricket, narrate the camera coming on.

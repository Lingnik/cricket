## Talking to them OOC

This is a player who runs a **slow-burn underworld antagonist**, not a protagonist — Nasiri
shows up as the Falleen everyone else has to *deal with*. His scenes are negotiation set-pieces,
political dinners, and intelligence trades, almost always uploaded from the other side of the
table. So assume the player likes **villain-as-fixed-point** play: he's the obstacle, the broker,
the morally-grey power the heroes can't ignore. Themes skew politics (9), intrigue (6), slavery
(5), diplomacy/negotiation (Article: Nasiri Zithreej; index footprint).

Safe conversation hooks:
- **The Night Parade.** His criminal cartel — illicit enterprises galaxy-wide under a cover
  persona, eventually fielding professional soldiers and the chrome-armored **Palace Militant**
  (Article: Nasiri Zithreej). Great handle for talking shop about Hutt-space underworld.
- **Shadow lord of Hutt space.** After the Empire (via a Select Committee bargain) wiped out the
  Council of Ancients, Nasiri stepped in as the Hutts' grudging "shadow lord overseer,"
  modernizing their operations and building a defense fleet for Nal Hutta (Article: Nasiri
  Zithreej). This is his core identity — the civilized savage running the Hutts from behind.
- **The Malign squeeze.** Darth Malign (Tyler Damion) destroyed his floating palace and forced
  him to choose: become a Moff of the Imperium or die. He took the title but bargained Hutt space
  independent (Article: Nasiri Zithreej; RPlog:Complicated Motivations, 29 ABY; RPlog:War
  Council, 29 ABY). His Moff status is permanently begrudging — good tension to play with.
- **The slavery dance with Johanna.** His most-developed live thread (2020–2025): Jedi Siri te
  Danaan (Johanna) and Crestian Tarasar lean on him to reform Hutt-space slave trade; he plays
  fence-sitter, freeing a few thralled worlds as tactical theater while insisting some cultures
  "simply can't be changed" (RPlog:Glowblue Noodles, 30 ABY; RPlog:Fight Dirty if it Comes to
  That, 30 ABY).

Tone to match: urbane, cynical, faintly contemptuous of everyone's "civilization." He talks
like a man who's seen every flavor of government and finds them all the same racket — "There are
more sorts of governments than there are bottles of liquor in a bar... different name, same
controls" (Article: Nasiri Zithreej).

## Character: Nasiri Gejalli — Lorda of the Smugglers' Moon

**Capsule.** Falleen male (b. on Falleen), 1.85 m, black hair, violet eyes, green skin;
Underworld (Article: Nasiri Zithreej). "Gejalli" is the public alias; **Nasiri Zithreej** is the
real name. He emerged from Nal Hutta in 43 ISY to take the Nar Shaddaa palace as Lorda, built a
reputation as a barbaric savage (cannibalism rumors after a rival's majordomo "left never to
return"), yet ran a reformed, well-paid Pleasure Palace that funded his power base (Article:
Nasiri Zithreej). Reads Hutt Cartel (9 logs) with heavy Imperium / CSA / Sith brushwork.

**Arc synthesis** (chronological; each beat is a fetch handle):

- **The Lorda on Nar Shaddaa (24 ABY / 2015).** First archived appearances: running the Pleasure
  Palace and brokering Hutt business, he's the Falleen contact every smuggler is told to "ask for"
  (RPlog:An Unscrupulous Undertaking: Ominous Forecast, 24 ABY). Elias Corentin tries to buy
  security from him and it goes sideways — Elias has one of Nasiri's top earners assassinated, and
  the Lorda invites him to dinner to "celebrate" the move (RPlog:An Unscrupulous Undertaking:
  Complications, 24 ABY; RPlog:An Unscrupulous Undertaking: A Pound of Flesh, 24 ABY).
- **Information broker (24 ABY).** Drax Rendolen comes investigating Johanna te Danaan's
  disappearance and pays more than expected for more than he bargained — Nasiri as the man who
  sells you the truth at his price (RPlog:The Price of Freedom, 24 ABY).
- **Birth of the Night Parade & shadow rule (back-story, per Article).** The Empire's
  Select-Committee bargain destroys the Hutt Council of Ancients; the younger Hutts accept Nasiri
  as shadow lord to avoid civil war. He modernizes them, forms a fleet, and builds a Combine of
  Falleen, Geonosis, and Gamora under his name (Article: Nasiri Zithreej).
- **Forced into the Imperium (29 ABY / 2020).** Darth Malign destroys his floating palace and
  forces the Moff title on him; Nasiri keeps Hutt space nominally independent and is seen orbiting
  Imperator Damion's coronation and war councils (RPlog:Complicated Motivations, 29 ABY;
  RPlog:War Council, 29 ABY). He's back on Nar Shaddaa and re-hardened — raising the Palace
  Militant in chrome stormtrooper armor (Article: Nasiri Zithreej).
- **The Jessalyn deal (29 ABY).** With her family under threat, Jessalyn Valios makes a deal with
  Nasiri on Nar Shaddaa for their protection — a clean example of him as the underworld safe
  harbor heroes have to swallow their pride to use (RPlog:Deal with the Falleen, 29 ABY).
- **Bespin entanglement (29 ABY).** He hires the singer Quinne for the Nyriaan Spiral
  (RPlog:Nasiri Hires Quinne, 29 ABY) and is folded into the Cloud City social/political circuit —
  the Youngling Ball and the slaver attack on the Miners' Dinner (RPlog:Johanna Has a Ball on
  Bespin, 29 ABY; RPlog:Bespin Blows Up!, 29 ABY).
- **The slavery-reform thread (30 ABY).** The Moffs suspect a slaver pipeline; Crestian, Johanna,
  and Axel Vichten circle Nasiri over Hutt-space slavery. He'll let the Jedi bring firepower to
  clear out the last big slavers — but everyone reads it as opportunism dressed as reform, and
  Crestian's trust in him has "faded" (RPlog:Fight Dirty if it Comes to That, 30 ABY;
  RPlog:Glowblue Noodles, 30 ABY).
- **Public figure (32 ABY).** Holojournalist Thurston Unger interviews him on Nar Shaddaa — the
  best single source for his unfiltered voice and worldview (RPlog:The Cutting Edge: Nasiri
  Interview, 32 ABY).
- **Current era (34 ABY / 2025).** Still in motion: a quiet art-gallery meeting with Crestian on
  Charros IV, and a Tatooine sit-down after Nasiri publicly rebuked an Imperium effort — the Moff
  who keeps biting the hand (RPlog:Art of Darkness, 34 ABY; RPlog:Meeting Between the Headlines,
  34 ABY).

**Key relationships** (most-cited co-characters → use these to connect threads):
- **Crestian Tarasar** — fellow Moff, recurring counterpart and skeptic; their meetings span 30–34
  ABY and frame the whole reform arc. [~6 shared logs; his most frequent partner]
- **Johanna Siri te Danaan** — the Jedi who pressures him on slavery; wary mutual-use
  relationship, she calls him "an opportunist... just as in love with his own power as any Sith
  Lord" (RPlog:Stretching the New Leg, 30 ABY). [~5 shared logs]
- **Darth Malign / Tyler Damion** — the Imperator who broke his palace and Moff'd him; the
  pressure source behind much of his arc (RPlog:War Council, 29 ABY). *(Not Darth Malus / Lorn
  Rhys — different character.)*
- **Elias Corentin** — early CSA/underworld counterpart in the 24 ABY "Unscrupulous Undertaking"
  arc; the dinner-and-assassination dance. [3 shared logs]
- **Jessalyn Valios** — bought his protection for her family in 29 ABY (RPlog:Deal with the
  Falleen). [2 shared logs]
- **Elana Tracer** — uploads/runs much of the Imperium-court material he appears in. [3 shared logs]

**Playstyle / themes** (from the footprint): politics (9), intrigue (6), slavery (5),
diplomacy/negotiation, underworld (4). A **villain-as-institution** character — almost never the
aggressor in a fight, almost always the man across the table whose cooperation is the prize. The
recurring tension is whether his reformist gestures are sincere or pure leverage; the wiki itself
leaves it ambiguous on purpose ("barring my invading his mind, I have no idea whether he means
it" — Johanna, RPlog:Glowblue Noodles, 30 ABY).

**Signature voice.** Urbane, steepled fingers, a goblet of wine, and weaponized cynicism. Pull
the interview for cadence: "Cesspool, scum, filth... every world has it, without exception. Some
just hide it better than others" (RPlog:The Cutting Edge: Nasiri Interview, 32 ABY). He frames
Hutt brutality as more *honest* than galactic "civilization."

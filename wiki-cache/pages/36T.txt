The '''36T Carbine''''s resemblance to the [[ST-I]]/[[E-11]] blaster rifle is unmistakeable.
Its design originated from a desire in certain military circles to produce a 
lighter-weight version of the already light ST-I/E-11 series blaster rifles 
for use in shipboard operations and other close-quarters environs. With a 
shorter barrel, the removal of many of its cooling coils andthe collapsable 
shoulder stock, and the move to a lighter, less powerful scope, it's makers 
have created a weapon half the weight, and about two thirds the length of its
ancestor - only slightly larger than many heavy blaster pistols. It still 
retains a formidable punch, and easily outranges pistols, but in other areas 
its performance has been sacraficed. The carbine did not turn out to possess
the E-11's comfortable balance, and its recoil can make controlling the 
weapon a brutal prospect, particularly in rapid firing.

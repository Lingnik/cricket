''"Taste the Biscuit" - Corporate Slogan''

[[File:Biscuit Baron Logo.svg|thumb]] '''Biscuit Baron''' is the galaxy's largest fast-casual dining establishment. It was originally owned by the [[:starwars:House of Tagge/Legends|House of Tagge]] through the [[:starwars:Tagge Company/Legends|Tagge Company's]] subsidiary [[:starwars:Tagge Restaurant Association/Legends|Tagge Restaurant Association]]. In [[26 ABY]] it was included in the massive reorganisation of the [[Corporate Sector Authority]] as a single corporation. Crippling labour strikes in [[32 ABY]] led the company's board of directors to hire the [[Golden Bantha Group]] to serve as a consultancy firm. Under the direction of the Golden Bantha Group, the strikes were quickly resolved amicably. In the following year, [[33 ABY]], the Golden Bantha Group was able to negotiate with the CSA to [[Report:Biscuit Buyout|buy]] the chain, purchasing it outright. The board of directors for the Golden Bantha Group then took on the same roles with Biscuit Baron itself.

==Background==

Biscuit Baron is a chain known for its fast service and casual atmosphere. Formal dress is not required for customers making it a perfect stop for travellers and people on their way to and from work. It serves a variety of products appealing not only to the diets of [[Human|Humans]] but many other species as well. [[Rodian|Rodians]], [[Sullustan|Sullustans]], and [[Wookiee|Wookiees]] were among those known to patronise the chain in significant numbers. Opinions varied on the taste quality of their products. The company makes no claim to compete with the taste of fine dining establishments, but tends to receive solid ratings for quality and variety compared other fast-casual establishments.

Their products, particularly the [[:starwars:Bantha Breakfast Biscuit|Bantha Breakfast Biscuit]], to have a particularly long shelf life. In years past, these products have also been known to have unintended environmental consequences such as the famous [[:starwars:Giant Amorphous Bantha Breakfast Biscuit|Giant Amorphous Bantha Breakfast Biscuit]] incident. The fact that said creature could only be defeated by the application of [[:starwars:Blue sauce|Blue Sauce]] led to serious questions about just what is in the sauce.

By the time of the [[Galactic Civil War]], Biscuit Baron had emerged as the largest fast-casual restaurant chain in the galaxy. Its presence was considered nearly ubiquitous. In the decades since it has managed to maintain a strong share of the market. Restaurants can be found on most major worlds, with their presence often seen as an indicator of the world's place in the galactic economy. Many of the restaurants are owned by the company directly, though many others are owned by local franchisees.

Its closest competitor in terms of restaurant locations and sales volume is [[Caspar Caff]]. While Caspar Caff saw greater expansion for many years than Biscuit Baron, it was never able to overtake it. In recent years Biscuit Baron's expansion has exceeded that of Caspar Caff. As the two restaurants are centred around different products, customer base, and atmosphere they are generally not in stiff competition with each other.

==Name and Image==

The restaurant's name refers both to their breakfast biscuits and the title held by the head of the [[:starwars:House of Tagge/Legends|House of Tagge]]. The corporate logo is styled image of the late Baron [[:starwars:Orman Tagge/Legends|Orman Tagge]], wearing his trademark goggles, which was designed after he lost his sight in [[18 BBY]]. The logo is found on restaurant storefronts, shipping containers, and product wrappers.

Gold, white, black, and red, the same colours as the [[:starwars:Tagge Company/Legends|Tagge Company]] logo. The colours feature prominently in the look of Biscuit Baron restaurants and product packaging. In spite of the fact the company is no logo in Tagge Company hands the current ownership has chosen to keep the logo and colour scheme in place. 

[[Image:Domina Tagge.jpg|right|thumb|Baroness Domina Tagge]] The Tagge legacy is not completely gone from the company as the House of Tagge supported the Golden Bantha Group's takeover. [[Izara Tagge]], the daughter of [[Baron|Baroness]] [[:starwars:Domina Tagge/Legends|Domina Tagge]] is a minority shareholder in the Golden Bantha Group and has remained Vice President of Marketing. Domina Tagge herself is rumoured to have been a major financial backer of the Golden Bantha Group's takeover with Izara's shares serving as compensation for that effort.

The title of Baron in the restaurant's name can now also be seen as a reflection of its current Chief Executive Officer, Baroness [[Atsvara Tarasar]]. In [[32 ABY]] in recognition of her tenth birthday and her functional adulthood, Atsvara's father [[Duke]] [[Crestian Tarasar]] decided to elevate his daughter to a title of nobility within his [[House Dragonflower]]. The title of Baroness was chosen in recognition and approval of Atsvara's then secret schemes to take over the Biscuit Baron franchise.

==Promotions==

One facet of the Biscuit Baron experience that the chain is most known for is the promotions it runs. The most famous and long running of these are the toys that are included in [[:starwars:Jolly Meal|Jolly Meals]]. On occasion promotions are offered along other products such as the "Heroes of the Republic" commemorative holocubes that came with the [[:starwars:QuickSnack|QuickSnack]] and [[:starwars:QuickSnackLite|QuickSnackLite]] for a month in the lead up the Clone Wars. More recently Biscuit Baron teamed up with the makers of the popular party game Secret Sidious to include special edition cards with both Infinite Stacks and Jolly Meals.

[[Image:Atsvara Jolly Meal Toy.jpg|right|thumb|Prototype Atsvara Tarasar<br>Jolly Meal Toy<br>(Unreleased)]] Biscuit Baron CEO Baroness Atsvara Tarasar is known for having a collection that includes every Jolly Meal toy and other promotional product dating back to the Heroes of the Republic commemorative holocubes. Originally these were gifts from her father [[Crestian Tarasar]], but after he was elected [[Presav]] she started making connexions at the corporation herself, notably with its Vice President of Marketing Izara Tagge. From then on Atsvara had every toy before it even came out. It is said this collection is what spawned her interest in the franchise and her multi-year quest to take it over.

==Security==

Prior to the  [[Golden Bantha Group|Golden Bantha Group's]] [[Report:Biscuit Buyout|buyout]] of Biscuit Baron, their security department became aware of franchises in the [[:starwars:Prefsbelt sector/Legends|Prefsbelt]] and [[:starwars:Fath sector|Fath Sectors]] that were being operated by the [[:starwars:Nova Crime Syndicate|Nova Syndicate]] as fronts sentient trafficking. In response to this Baroness Tarasar arranged with [[Haor Chall Engineering]] and [[SubPro|Sublight Products Corporation]] to buy a significant number of [[E5 Baron Droid|Baron Droids]] for use as corporate security.

During the press conference on [[Etti IV]] announcing the buyout, Biscuit Baron release footage of two raids conducted by the Baron Droids security forces to liberate victims of trafficking at two franchise locations. These raids were personally led by corporate executives with Executive Vice President [[Cricket]] leading the raid on [[:starwars:Ord Mynock/Legends|Ord Mynock]] and Vice President of Security [[V1C3]] leading the raid on [[:starwars:Ord Cantrell/Legends|Ord Cantrell]].

There is no publicly available information about just how many Baron Droids are employed by Biscuit Baron, but it is believed that Biscuit Baron is the single largest civilian buyer of the droids. In keeping with the corporate philosophy of the Golden Bantha Group, these droids are salaried employees of the company.

==Sponsorship and Advertising==

Biscuit Baron is known for its frequent advertisements which run as commercials surrounding various popular programmes, particularly [[InterGalactic News]] broadcasts. Since the Golden Bantha Group buyout, Baroness Tarasar has featured in a number of these advertisements herself. Biscuit Baron is a key sponsor of the [[Muun Money Minute]] financial news programme hosted by Professor [[Brackett Benduday]]. Baroness Tarasar and Chief Operating Officer [[Veruca Violet]] are also frequent guest commentators on financial affairs.

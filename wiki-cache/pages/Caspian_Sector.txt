The '''Caspian Sector''' or '''Caspian Space''' refers to an area in the [[The Galaxy#Mid Rim|Mid Rim]] located near and under the influence of [[Caspar]]. While the Caspian Sector can be considered generally synonymous with the [[Caspian Democratic Union]] and later the [[Caspian Union]], it is conceptually distinct. This distinction was particularly in play following the collapse of the CDU when several worlds in the sector were, for a time, no longer part of the Union. When a distinction needs to made between the sector itself and the territory which is politically controlled by Caspar, the term Union territory will be used instead.

== History ==

Historically the area that has now become known as the Caspian Sector occupied the area at the intersections of the [[:starwars:Lifh sector|Lifh]], [[:starwars:Bortele sector|Bortele]], [[:starwars:Taldot sector/Legends|Taldot]], and [[:starwars:Halla sector/Legends|Halla]] sectors. Prior to the [[Galactic Civil War]], none of the planets in the Caspian Sector was considered of any galactic significance. Even within the region, only [[Caspar]] could boast any sort of importance. It was only the arrival of [[Admiral]] [[Dergan Roj]] and his [[Barren Sector Fleet]] and the subsequent formation of the [[Caspian Democratic Union]] following the [[:starwars:Battle of Endor/Legends|Battle of Endor]] that the region emerged as galactically relevant.

As Caspar as a planet and the union as a government became more relevant in galactic affairs, there was little point in labelling any Union world by their previous sector designation. Though the concept of a Caspian sector was initially indistinct from the Union itself, the efforts of the [[Caspian Defence Force]] to secure the area meant Union power often extend beyond the formal bounds of Union territory.

The collapse of the Caspian Democratic Union in [[28 ABY]] would bring the concept of a Caspian sector into new prominence. The new [[Caspian Union|Caspar Union]] only controlled the [[Caspia]] and [[Mikassan System|Mikassan]] systems prior to the reunification efforts undertaken by the [[Crestian Tarasar|Tarasar]] administration in [[29 ABY]]-[[30 ABY]]. In spite of this separation, the former member worlds of the CDU were highly tied to one another both economically and militarily. As such businessmen, journalist, and politicians would come to use terms like the Caspian Sector or Caspian Space to refer to the area of space which had been part of the CDU whether or not the world in question was yet part of the new Union.

== Current ==

The return of the [[Krittain|Krittain System]] to the Union in [[30 ABY]] essentially restored the Union to its previous borders, once again making the Caspian Sector largely synonymous with Union territory. The concurrent admission of [[Charros IV]], however, saw Union territory extending farther into the Taldot Sector. There has been some debate as to whether Charros IV should now be considered part of the Caspian Sector. It has thus far been the official position of the [[Caspian Crest|Crest]] that Charros IV remains part of the Taldot Sector and that the Caspian Sector should refer to the 'historic' extent of Caspian control.

== OOC ==

The Caspian Sector is made of star systems and worlds all unique to the canon of Star Wars MUSH. Based on where it has been placed on maps it falls roughly around where the [[:starwars:Lifh sector|Lifh]], [[:starwars:Bortele sector|Bortele]], [[:starwars:Taldot sector/Legends|Taldot]], and [[:starwars:Halla sector/Legends|Halla]] sectors meet. As [[:starwars:Diado|Diado]] (a planet of miniscule importance) is the only location specifically identified as being near this border area it can be largely assumed that the existence of the worlds in the Caspian Sector do not contradict the existence of any worlds from the canon. As [[Charros IV]] is actually from the canon it is not considered officially part of the Caspian Sector to allow the sector's definition to be coextensive with the uniquely SW1 locations associated with the Union.

'''Casulmis''', [[Sarian (language)|Sarian]] for Great Wind, was a paramilitary/vigilante organisation founded by [[New Sith Order|Sith]] [[Admiral]] [[Crestian Tarasar]] to fight against [[Olumi Osahn]] during the [[Mergansar|Mergani]] [[Report:Mergani Pogrom Press Releases|pogroms]].

== Background ==

When the Sariphage [[Report:Sariphage Press Releases|pandemic]] hit [[Caspar]] in late [[27 ABY]], [[New Sith Order|Sith]] [[Admiral]] [[Crestian Tarasar]], a native of Caspar, felt it was time to come out of his semi-exile in the [[Dragonflower Nebula]] and offer his services to Caspar to support their efforts to combat the virus. In meeting with [[Sar Admiral]] [[Prae Kichakressa]], Tarasar was unofficially encouraged to turn his attentions to [[Olumi Osahn]], a cult with previous connexions to the Sith, which had begun a campaign of expelling non-Sarians from [[Mergansar]], blaming them for the pandemic.

Tarasar along with [[Tyler Damion|Darth Malign]] secretly met with representatives of Olumi Osahn on board his ship the ''[[Leviathan]]'' hoping to use the past connexion to bring the cult to heel. Their efforts at negotiating failed, so Tarasar soon turned his attention to direct action. As he had limited personnel and resources at his disposal, and the Sith were unpopular in the [[Caspia|Caspian System]], he decided the best approach was to create a front organisation.

== Organising ==

Tarasar felt a Sarian name would best befit creating a native feel to the organisation. Casulmis or Great Wind was meant to evoke the image of sweeping away Olumi Osahn. With millions of refugees flooding through [[Kichnar Orbital Station]] from Mergansar, it was not hard for Tarasar to find people who wanted to fight back and who were willing to look past his Sith ties in order to do it. While the organisation had no legal basis, it present itself in terms of combating what was illegal action by Olumi Osahn in terrorising Caspian citizens.

== Action ==

In the lead up to taking the fight back to Mergansar, Tarasar and company released all the information they had on Olumi Osahn members operating elsewhere in the galaxy to the various local authorities involved. This led to the arrest of numerous operatives on worlds such as [[Ithor]] and [[Ryloth]].

Next Tarasar set his sights on the city of Hebron as its starport was the primary location the non-Sarians were being expelled from. While his starfighters bombed several Olumi Osahn strongholds in the city including near the starport, Tarasar and Malign landed with armed refugees. Though Malign was still officially missing and presumed dead, he left an impression in Hebron with many of the cultists felled by his lightsaber.

Casulmis was initially quite successful in Hebron, killing off the Olumi Osahn opposition. Soon Casulmis was the ones patrolling the city and dispensing aid supplies. Nearby Goshen was also quickly liberated by Casumlis. Forays were made into Anphora, Charon, and Ithaca but these proved to be ineffective.

Tarasar would also provided [[InterGalactic News]] with dossiers linking many members of Mergansar's government, mostly notably its [[Governor]], Lieutenant Governor, and Minister of Justice to Olumi Osahn. By the time a picture of Governor Raif Avosam in Olumi Osahn garb had made the news, his compound on the outskirts Vilarasara had already been bombed by Tarasar, killing him. The Lieutenant Governor and Minister of Justice had also gone into hiding by the time the dossiers made the press.

When a cure to the pandemic was delivered to Caspar, the [[Caspian Navy]] was now free to turn their attention to Mergansar. In spite of the initial encouragement from Sar Admiral Kichakressa, she deployed four warships along with their starfighters and order the Leviathan to lower its shields. After Tarasar attempted to negotiate with Kichakressa and Malign demanded the Caspians surrender instead, the Caspian vessels opened fire.

The resulting [[Mergansar Incident|incident]] saw Sar Admiral Kichakressa killed with the bridge of ''[[CMS Typhoon]]'' blown up, ''[[CMS Prophet]]'' and ''CMS Rapace'' crashed into one another and ''CMS Ad Astra'' also damaged. Meanwhile the ''Leviathan'' was forced to retreat back to [[Silver Station]] having been seriously damaged, particular by the initial salvo.

== Aftermath ==

Without support from the ''Leviathan,'' Casulmis was effectively done for. Those that survived the fighting were forced to flee Mergansar once again. While some would later find their way to refugee with Tarasar in the Dragonflower Nebula, most folded back into the refugee community at large.

Tarasar's efforts were not quite at an end. His agents managed to track down and kill Lieutenant Governor Lau Saifax. Then when the Minister of Justice Marck Narlin broadcasted the death and his assumption of the office of Acting Govenor, Tarasar's agent bombed his location killing him as well.

After the dust had settled Tarasar returned and settled on Caspar, starting a charity called [[The Mission]], which served as his new focus. The Mission worked to help both the survivors on Caspar as well as refugees from the pogroms that settled there as well. He would later get invovled in politics and be elected [[Presav]] of the new [[Caspian Union]]. As Presav the government and the [[Caspian Defence Force]] served as the new organs of his efforts to combat the Mergani government for its crimes against its own citizens, with the world fully retaken and politcally reintegrated into the Union within the next several months.

== Legacy ==

A number of those who were involved in Casulmis have gone into prominent roles in the new Caspian Union. Most of these have been, in part, through their connexion to Tarasar and his rise to power. Others, however, have chartered their own rises independently. Though respect for the efforts of Casulmis is certainly not universal, Tarasar's political power is such that involvement with it is generally seen as a badge of honour.

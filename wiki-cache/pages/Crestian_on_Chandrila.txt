On Benduday [[Caspian Union|Caspian]] [[Presav]] [[Crestian Tarasar]] gave an unscheduled address at [[Union Hall]] in [[Plaxton]] on [[Caspar]] following recent riot reports on [[Chandrila]]. It is approximately ten minutes.

The Presav stands at an ornately carved podium in the [[Union Council|Council]] Chambers. The audience is mostly full and appears to be a mix of journalists, politicians, business leaders, military officers, and members of the general public.

"Thanks to everyone who has come out this evening. I know most of you have better things to do on your Benduday evening than to listen to me. My sincerest sympathies to those of you who did not actually have anything better to do this evening.

The recent demonstrations on Chandrila have come as a surprise to me as I am sure they have to many of you here today and watching this broadcast. Chandrila has long been a touchstone of democratic traditions and values. In actual fact the old Union Assembly took direct inspiration from the [[:starwars:Chandrilan House|Chandrilan House]] in terms of discourse and proceedings. The fact that the current discourse on Chandrila is in the streets and not in that vaunted house is concerning.

I debated whether I would say anything public on the matter. Contrary to what some might think, I do not spend me time doom scrolling through the news in search of something to rail on the Republic for. This one hits different for me, however, as I in fact lived, studied, and served on Chandrila for a decade.

My father Petyrian was a diplomat. After [[:starwars:Battle of Endor/Legends|Endor]] when the [[New Republic]] formed and the old [[Caspian Democratic Union]] formed, he did not see the point in the latter. He was all in on the return of galactic wide democracy and so when I was fourteen the family relocated to Chandrila.

It was on Chandrila and in the New Republic I truly came of age. I enrolled at the [[:starwars:Brionelle Memorial Military Academy|Brionelle Memorial Military Academy]], later joining its faculty, and eventually its administration. Were it not for the [[Imperial Blitzkrieg|Blitzkrieg]] I may have never left.

Sadly I have not been back in many years. Republic foreign policy has been firmly been against the [[Caspian Union]] these past five years. Our decision to ally with the [[Imperium]] is such an anathema to them that they simply do not want to engage with us at all.

They galaxy has been in an era of relative peace for several years. And if cooler heads were prevailing in the Republic, the Union could serve to bridge the gap between the Imperium and Republic. Our diplomats and business leaders would like to engage with both. Gods know many of my fellow councillors would like to see us working closer with the Republic. I wish that engagement was possible to. I should be meeting with Republic leaders not left to pontificate about them in the press.

Instead we have watched these past years as the Republic seems to be slowly collapsing in on itself. The [[Corellia|Corellian]] Sector pulled out, [[Dreven]] abandoned its flirtation with Republic rule, and now we have Chandrila. 

Ultimately though the people of Chandrila must be able to freely choose their own path. No peoples so steeped in democratic traditions as the Chandrilans will ever truly fit into a galactic government holding them against their will. They will not be assimilated just because the winds have changed elsewhere. [[Mon Mothma]] proves a great example of that. It is no coincidence that the [[:starwars:Alliance to Restore the Republic/Legends|Rebellion]] against the rule of [[Galactic Emperor|Emperor]] [[:starwars:Palpatine/Legends|Palpatine]] was led by a Chandrilan.

But we should also hesitate to read too much into the current protests occurring on Chandrila now. Democracy is about hearing every voice and not just the loudest. We can not assume these protestors speak for a majority any more than we can assume the government officials on Chandrila truly speak for them. Democracy must be allowed to prevail here and the people of Chandrila must be allowed to choose whether they wish to remain in the Republic or whether they feel so ill served by it that they wish to strike out on their own. And as a bastion of democratic traditions here ourselves we must honour their decision what ever it ultimately is. Should the discourse lead to them recommitting themselves to the New Republic we will and must respect that.

There are some realities about Chandrila's place in the galaxy that my friends back on Chandrila should take into consideration. Chandrila and the worlds near it operate as almost an exclave of Republic territory. Peace has carried the day for many years and so this has not proved the threat it had in past, but it does mean the Republic presence in the area is limited, particularly after Corellia declared its independence. And with the Republic unwilling to engage with the Imperium this has greatly limited Chandrila's economic opportunities. Chandrilan foodstuffs and luxury items should be flowing throughout the Core Worlds, they should be exported galaxy wide, but many importers are not willing to deal with the hassle of having trade across the border. 

I myself am forced to go through third parties when seeking the comforts of my old home. Were the people of Chandrila to declare independence from the Republic their markets would open up considerably. While I can only speculate at the advantages that would give them with the Imperium and Corellian Confederation, I know for a fact that the appetite in the Caspian Union for Chandrilan exports is strong. An independent Chandrila would be able to count on the flow of Caspian credits into their economy as well as access to our markets for the high technology goods they import.

Independence, however, also comes with the burden of establishing an effective national defence. It is unlikely an independent Chandrila would wish to ally with the Imperium, and I am sure Chandrilan leaders are worried about the position they would be in outside of the defences of the New Republic. To that end I make this pledge to the people of Chandrila. Should you strike out on your own I will ask this chamber to support a military aid package to your world. The Caspian defence industry can supply warships, starfighters, droids, and weapons with a generous financing package. And I am confident this chamber would approve such a proposal.

Again, however, the choice is yours and yours alone. Should you find adequate redress for your concerns with the Republic you have every right to remain a part of it. The values of the Republic have traditionally been the values of the Chandrila. Such a break in that relationship should not be done lightly. If you can work through your issues and preserve that partnership, the Caspian people will wish you nothing but the best. But if you feel those bonds must be broken, know you have friends here. Thank you for listening and good night."

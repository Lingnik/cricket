'''Daln Bael''' ([[:starwars:50 BBY/Legends|50 BBY]] - [[28 ABY]]) a [[Human]] from [[Caspar]] who served as Director of the [[Groton Sciences Institute]] at the time of the Sariphage pandemic. Professor Bael led Caspar's effort to combat the virus and was the public face of the [[Caspian Crest|Crest]] in terms of reporting on the status of those efforts and ordering the [[Caspian Navy]] to blockade the planet to prevent its spread elsewhere. While Bael in his team were able to isolate the virus they incorrectly believed it to be naturally occurring, when it in fact turned out to have been engineered by [[Basca Starker]] at the behest of [[Galactic Emperor|Emperor]] [[Aleister Vadim|Vadim]]. 

Before succumbing to the virus himself, Bael suggested that the disease be allowed to run its course on Caspar, with all sentient life being allowed to die out, believing that any effort at outside assistance would run too great a risk at allowing the disease to spread. This particularly defeatist attitude has been considered by many to be quite out of character for an official Caspian response and it has had profound effects on the culture of Caspar and the [[Caspian Union|Union]] since.

In forming the [[Praecet]] party to contest the elections for the permanent [[Union Council]], [[Crestian Tarasar]] rallied against the nihilist response of Professor Bael as the epitome of what Caspar can never allow to happen again. The ferocity of his approach in protecting the life and liberty of Union citizens, including his own response during the [[Mergansar|Mergani]] pogroms did much to galvanise support around his candidacy and party in spite of his own background as a [[Duke]] and [[Admiral]] of the [[New Sith Order]]. 

This has further led to a certain culture within the [[Caspian Defence Force]] and [[Caspian Intelligence Service]] that has been described as a soft version of the [[Bothan]] concept of [[:starwars:Ar'krai|Ar'krai]], that is that the Union remains under the threat of extinction and that it is the duty of all to combat that. While much of that centres around the threat posed by Emperor Vadim and those he would use to attack the Union in future, the nihilist response Professor Bael to the pandemic, and the role of previous Caspian leaders in allowing the Mergani pogroms to occur have been integral to the notion that the Union is vulnerable to internal as well as external threats.

The response of Professor Bael and the Groton Sciences Institute has also lead to massive rethink of Caspian medical and scientific infrastructure. Feeling that the Caspian medical community was both wholly inadequate and philosophically asinine in their response to the pandemic, Crestian Tarasar and his charity, [[The Mission]], founded [[Twin Suns Hospital]] in [[Trinumvira]] in the aftermath of the pandemic to be a new state of the art medical research institution. Tarasar himself has studied as a medical scientist in the years since to ensure his ability to coordinate medical efforts without being overly reliant on potentially bad counsel from others.

The post-pandemic Caspian Union has also seen the position of Minister of Health elevated in standing to be consider a senior ministry position with [[Prince]] [[Zig Al Fidel]], Councillor of Feyenoor on [[Ixin]], a medical doctor himself, holding the position. While the elevated importance of the position can be largely tied to the aftermath of the pandemic itself, the role Professor Bael played in managing the pandemic both in terms of his attitude towards fighting it and the fact someone outside of the government was seemingly ordering around the Navy have factored in to the prominence of the Health Minister as the central voice of medical authority.

Professor Bael's efforts have also had a profound effect on the [[Groton Sciences Institute]]. While the institute is still in existence and still is funded by the government, its prestige has been permanently effected as [[Presav]] Crestian Tarasar continues to distrust GSI to effectively support the needs of the Caspian people and scientific community. While the GSI had previously been considered the most important scientific institute in Caspian Space, the Crest wastes no opportunity to put its support behind other institutions. While there is also a stated aim to not have all of the Union's eggs in one basket, GSI is often publicly denigrated because of memories of Bael's defeatism.

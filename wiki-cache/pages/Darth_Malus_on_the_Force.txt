==Context==
 
In order to best understand [[Lorn Rhys|Lorn Rhys / Darth Malus']] current viewpoint on the Force, some context is required. While this can be learned through reading dozens of logs, a short summary is provided here.
 
Lorn Rhys did not awaken to the Force and the Dark Side until well into his adult years and after his self-imposed exile following the attack on and his subsequent escape from [[Pride-1|Orbital Station Redemption.]] When he emerged from [[Nar Shaddaa]] and during his ascent to his brief seat on the Imperial throne, Lorn was not particularly strong in the Force. He instead relied on his mundane skills, knowledge, and experiences to plan his coup and build the coalition which ultimately empowered him to usurp control of the Empire. To Lorn, it was simply another tool in his arsenal of weapons to leverage, and usually not his go-to method of operation.
 
Beyond his personal experiences with the Force, interactions he has had with other Force Sensitives have colored his perceptions on the Force. In his childhood and early career, Lorn was a dyed in the wool true believer of the New Order and the will that Emperor Palpatine would express. As he would mature in his career, recruited out of the Stormtrooper Corps and into the [[Shadow Guard]] within [[Bureau of Operations]], he would be exposed to diverse cultures and species. No longer were they something to containerize and suppress. Instead, he would come to appreciate their strength, understand their weakness, and value how these could be used as levers to generate success and induce failure. Most importantly, Lorn would come to the realization that everything has a place: heterogeneous and homogenous systems should not exist in an exclusive system. The competition spurred between these disparate systems of thought serve to drive improvement.
 
When his Force Sensitivity was unlocked, Lorn greedily sought out what little knowledge he could with the meager resources he had. It was a new skillset that opened new avenues; not only for survival, but to reclaim what was lost to him after [[Bacharan Valak|Emperor Valak]] crushed the [[Vigilant Movement|Vigilant]] movement. Following his re-emergence were two diametrically opposed experiences which formed the foundation for his current philosophy. The first and most positive experience was with Ahzad Domani and his holocron construct, who saw the potential in Lorn. While his experience with Ahzad was cut short, that legacy lives on through the construct. His other experience was with [[Sinane Pantara|Darth Pain]], and quite the opposite experience it was. Through Pain, Lorn would learn first-hand the craven, covetous, and duplicitous side of Sith. Their relationship should have ended with Lorn's demise, but with knowledge gained from Azhad's Cube, death was cheated and Lorn was reborn as Darth Malus.
 
When Malus was released from his carbonite prison, over 20 years had passed. To him, his experiences, emotions, memories were fresh and unsullied by the passage of time. The state of the galaxy and those that use the Force had changed considerably and his perspective, views, and behaviors contradict what one may expect. In many respects, he is an untainted relic of the past; unburdened by the schemes, politics, and alliances of the current era. What he learned, in the weeks after his release, disgusted him: a feudal empire grown apathetic, corporations swallowed by corpulent monopolies, and a stagnant galaxy with no impetus to improve and evolve. His immediate designs on revenge then turned to introspection and meditation. He now sees himself as an agent of change; not directed on the self nor to sew chaos, but to purposefully and methodically destabilize systems to bring the galaxy back into a healthy state of conflict and balance.
 
The following sections are written from the IC perspective of Darth Malus, and the tone of voice should be read as such, as opposed to a narrative.
==The Philosophy of the Dark and the Light and What Lies Between==
 
Like most things in nature, there are opposing forces that are complementary to each other: electromagnetism, motion, order and entropy, life and death. Neither of these forces are superior to each other, for without their opposite, they simply cannot exist. In this manner, so is the Force. The Jedi fear the Dark Side. The Sith sneer at the Light Side.  Neither philosophy expresses an objective nor complete view of the Force.
 
The Jedi wish to eliminate the Sith in order to preserve balance: they remove what they consider to be evil. The Sith wish to eliminate the Jedi because their philosophy is based on imposing their will on the galaxy: they will remove what opposes this goal. Neither philosophy is superior, but they both have a commonality: the imposition of their ethos. There have been outlying viewpoints: those that are willing to accept and even see the value of the opposing philosophy, though these are usually the pariahs of their orders.
 
It is not anathema for these orders to exist. They are the strongest manifestations of philosophies the Force, representing the Dark and the Light. The Force does not manifest evil and good, dark and light, because of the Jedi or the Sith. It is the natural order of all things. The Dark Side will manifest and thrive in our galaxy if all the Sith and their knowledge was to vanish. Likewise, the Light Side will continue to manifest and thrive should all the Jedi and their knowledge was to vanish. A balance will always exist, perhaps skewed severely in one direction or another, and herein lies the problem with these philosophies.
 
Our galaxy has seen extremes. The rise and fall of the Sith Empire, the Republic, the New Order, the New Republic and soon, the New Sith Order and Imperium. These are examples of extreme swings of a cosmic pendulum swung by these philosophies. All in the name of order or justice. In reality it is control. It is an absence of freedom. The ideal state is not to cease the swing of this pendulum. It is not to attempt to remove one side of the Force. It is to reduce the amplitude of the pendulum, small ripples that allow the pendulum to swing back and forth, but never to extremes.
 
There will always need to be expressions of the dark and the light, evil and good, justice and tyranny. These are healthy and necessary for a society to excel. Life to adapt and evolve. Technology to improve. New avenues and knowledge of the Force to grow. The solution is not to dominate and cease conflict; the solution is to allow conflict to wax and wane. Carefully curated to allow the freedoms of choice, savory and unsavory, yet never falling to either extreme.
 
Then why do we not see these expressions of neutrality within the Force in our societies and cultures? This is a mystery which I ponder. To date I have only a few hypotheses and the one that I believe is the most accurate is that the Force is sentient: capable of expressing the same emotions we feel, such as jealousy, compassion, and even forgiveness. This explains why the Sith are not able to call upon the Light Side, nor the Jedi on the Dark Side, without completely falling over to the opposing side. If it were as simple as flipping a switch, we would see masters wielding tremendous powers across the entire spectrum of the Dark and the Light. Such a concept does not yet seem to exist, or perhaps it is a matter of not understanding the true nature of the Force. Perhaps there is a twilight of the Force that is yet to be discovered, a realm that exists between Dark and Light, bridging both sides in an infinite circle.
 
 
 
==The Sith Code==
<blockquote>''Peace is a lie, there is only passion.''<br>''Through passion, I gain strength.''<br>''Through strength, I gain power.''<br>''Through power, I gain victory.''<br>''Through victory, my chains are broken.''<br>''The Force shall free me'''.'''''</blockquote>The Sith Code is practical in many aspects. I am rather fond of the line, "Through victory, my chains are broken." It is a metaphor that should be applied to all sentients in all walks of life. We are all bound my chains that shackle us in one way or another, but mostly it is our freedom that is chained. Some might suggest that freedom is akin to chaos, but that is an infantile expression of the concept. Freedom is ability to choose your own destiny. Some might choose to follow a rigid code, some might choose anarchy, while some might choose to skirt both. What matters is the ability for one to choose the context within they wish to act. In my dealings with sentients, freedom is the one attribute of life which they find the most lacking.
 
The first line is perhaps the most controversial, depending on one's perspective. From a pragmatic viewpoint, I do take issue with it. In the perspective of the Sith, passion is the font from which power is drawn. Yet, the Jedi's code is the opposite. Both philosophies draw power from the Force, and thus they are neither correct, nor are they wrong. It is critical failure of both orders to eschew the other's philosophies.
 
If such there were such a thing possible for a Force user to exist in a state of twilight between two realms, straddling both sides of the Dark and the Light, I would imagine their code would be akin to this:<blockquote>''Without peace, there is no passion.''<br>''Through passion, I gain knowledge.''<br>''Through knowledge, I gain strength.''<br>''Through strength, I understand conflict.''<br>''Through conflict, I gain balance.''<br>''With balance, we are free.''<br>''The Force must be balanced.''</blockquote>The tenants of such a balanced philosophy acknowledges that peace and passion must co-exist, with passion leading to knowledge. The code ultimately leads the follower on a journey of self-discovery, acknowledging that one must understand conflict and apply it to achieve balance of self. When the balance of self is discovered, then this allows true freedom for all, not just the self.

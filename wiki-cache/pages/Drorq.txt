'''Drorq''' ([[0 BBY]] - ) is [[Trandoshan]] priest and naval officer from the city of [[Noques Rasala]] on [[Caspar]]. He served under [[Admiral]] [[Crestian Tarasar]] in the [[New Sith Order]]. He continued to serve has part of the ''[[Leviathan]]'' crew after the collapse of the NSO, rising to the rank of [[Commander]]. Drorq would follow Crestian back to Caspar, working as part of his charity, [[The Mission]] and joining the rest of the ''Leviathan'' crew in becoming part of the [[Caspian Navy]]. Drorq would later be promoted to [[Captain]] and given command of ''Leviathan's'' new sister ship, the ''[[Dying Sun]]''. Through a variety of high profile assignments, Drorq has since risen to the rank of [[Commodore]].

== Early Life ==

Drorq was born in the city of [[Noques Rasala]] on [[Caspar]] during the early years of the [[Galactic Civil War]]. Though Caspar possessed a diverse population, Trandoshans' natural enmity with [[Wookiee|Wookiees]] which had long been one of Caspar's main immigrant groups and the fact Trandoshan culture was largely at odds with that of Caspar meant Trandoshans were a rare sight on Caspar. Noques Rasala had a tendency though to attract outcasts.

Drorq could have be consider an outcast be default, having been abandoned in infancy, and coming under the care of the local spiritual community. Thus while a Trandoshan, Drorq was raised outside of the values of his people and adopted Sarian culture. Though he grew into a spiritually focused young man, he yearned to learn more about his people and the galaxy and left the world when he came of age. A chance encounter with a Caspian expatriate named [[Crestian Tarasar]] would soon change the trajectory of his life.

== Sith Service ==

At the time Crestian was an ex-[[New Republic]] [[New Republic FleetOps|naval]] [[Captain]] who became disillusioned with the Republic and was now an independent smuggler based out of [[Silver Station]] in the [[Dragonflower Nebula]]. Drorq found sympathy with Crestian's philosophical outlook and accepted the offer from his fellow Caspian to join his crew. When Crestian was recruited later recruited into the [[New Sith Order]] by [[Grand Admiral]] [[Drayson Honos]], like much of Crestian's crew, Drorq followed along and became an part of '''Task Force 77'''.

When [[Tyler Damion|Darth Malign]] disappeared and Crestian parked his flagship, the [[Interdictor-class Immobilizer 418|Interdictor]] ''[[Leviathan]]'' in the Dragonflower Nebula, Drorq would be one of the crew who opted to stay part of his operations. In these years of quiet operation, Drorq would become one of Crestian's top lieutenants. Unfortunately owing to the limited scope of their operations, like so many other veterans of the unit there was nowhere to rise above the rank of [[Commander]], which was now practically ubiquitous.

As the Sariphage pandemic hit Caspar and Crestian, Malign, and the ''Leviathan'' headed to the [[Caspia|Caspian System]] to lend a hand, Drorq was part of the crew involved. He took part of the [[Casulmis]] operation against [[Olumi Osahn]] on [[Mergansar]] and was part of the ''Leviathan'' crew as it was [[Mergansar Incident|attacked]] by the [[Caspian Navy]] near Mergansar that followed. Though forced to retreat, like Crestian he vowed to return.

== Return to Caspar ==

Following the dissolution of the [[Caspian Democratic Union]], Drorq followed Crestian back to Caspar and was one of the early employees of his new charity, [[The Mission]]. When Crestian was later elected [[Presav]] and appointed [[Sar Admiral]], Drorq accepted a commission in the Caspian Navy as part of the ''Leviathan's'' crew seconded to Caspian service. Crestian would take the occasion as an opportunity to promote Drorq to [[Captain]] and appoint him as ''Leviathan's'' executive officer. With Crestian frequently planet side, Drorq would command the vessel as it participated in the [[Report:Mergani Subjugation Posts|blockade]] of Mergansar.

A new opportunity would present itself months later when Imperator [[Tyler Damion|Damion I]] gave Crestian a second Interdictor, which he christened the ''Dying Sun.'' As the vessel was Sith rather than Caspian, Crestian had more leeway in appointing a commanding officer. He chose to transfer Drorq to command the ''[[Dying Sun]]'', not only in recognition of his years of faithful and exemplary service, but also his continuing commitment to Caspar by placing a native Caspian in command of ''Leviathan's'' new sister ship.

== Dying Sun ==

While the ''Dying Sun'' was still being prepared for service in the Caspian Navy, and its existence still classified, Drorq accompanied the Caspian delegation to the 1st Annual Miners Dinner in [[Cloud City]] on [[Bespin]]. Drorq was serving as acting executive officer of the ''Leviathan'' when the city was attacked by slavers. He was ordered by Crestian to place the planet under interdiction in hopes of catching the culprits. Following the departure of [[Imperium]] [[Fleet Admiral]] Domicor Honos with the grievously wounded [[Johanna Siri te Danaan]] to [[Selene]], followed later by Crestian's, Captain Drorq was the senior officer in the system until the arrival of the ''[[CMS Renaissance of Innovation]]'' under the command [[Vice Admiral]] [[Kath Samba]] with a team of [[SubPro]] engineers lead Caspian Minister of Science and Technology, [[Sirlahn Inglet]].

Though the interdiction effort ultimately proved unsuccessful, Drorq was rewarded for his role in maintaining order after the attack with a promotion to [[Fleet Captain]] upon the official entry of the ''Dying Sun'' into the Caspian Navy. Fleet Captain Drorq and the ''Dying Sun'' would frequently find themselves with high profile assignments. In [[33 ABY]], Fleet Captain Drorq was involved in the [[Caspian Intelligence Service|Caspian Intelligence Service's]] Operation Requiem, commanding the naval elements which were posing as the fictional Imperium '''Task Force Faylar'''.

In [[34 ABY]], Fleet Captain Drorq and the ''Dying Sun'' were involved in the [[Caspian 4th Fleet|Caspian 4th Fleet's]] efforts to assist the [[Imperium]] in the capture of prisoners who had escaped from secret [[The Oubliette|Spacestation 1138]] prison. They were credited for their role in the Imperium's operation in the Nemares Corridor with Drorq taking the initiative to interdict subspace traffic which proved critical to the operation's success. Drorq was subsequently promoted to [[Commodore]] as a reward for his actions.

As the commanding officer of one of the more high profile ships in Caspian service and an active participant in one of the current Union's notable foreign operations, Commodore Drorq enjoys considerable standing in society and is often seen in the company of senior Caspian leaders.

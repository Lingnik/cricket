The '''''Dying Sun''''' is the sister ship to the ''[[Leviathan]]'', the personal flagship of [[Caspian Union|Caspian]] [[Presav]] and [[Sar Admiral]] and [[New Sith Order|Sith]] [[Duke]] [[Crestian Tarasar]]. Like the ''Leviathan'' it is the property of Crestian's [[House Dragonflower]] but seconded to the '''[[Caspian 4th Fleet|4th Fleet]]''' of the [[Caspian Navy]]. It is under the command of [[Commodore]] [[Drorq]].

== Background ==

At some point after taking over the [[Galactic Empire]] and transforming it into the [[Imperium]], [[Tyler Damion|Darth Malign]] used his new pool of assets to present loyal ally, [[Crestian Tarasar]], with a second [[Interdictor-class Immobilizer 418|Interdictor]] to compliment the one he already had received from him over a decade before, the ''[[Leviathan]]''.

Crestian chose to name this vessel the ''Dying Sun'' after the [[:starwars:Dying Sun|flagship]] of [[:starwars:Darth Glovoc|Darth Glovoc]]. Wishing for ''Leviathan's'' sister ship to be commanded by a [[Caspian Union|Caspian]], he gave command to [[Captain]] [[Drorq]], ''Leviathan's'' executive officer. While the ship was being prepared for service it was kept at [[Silver Station]] with its existence initially classified. Like it's captain, a number of its senior officers were transferred from the ''Leviathan''. 

''Dying Sun'' is host to '''Guduma Squadron''' and '''Zibeti Squadron''', meaning dead and star respectively in the Sith language. Along with ''Leviathan's'' Kojafonas Squadron and Zudifas Squadron, meaning 'hunter' and 'killer' respectively, these are the only Barb Interceptors serving in the [[Caspian Starfighter Corps]]. As [[Sar Admiral]], Crestian felt placing Sith designed craft on mainline Caspian warships would be bad for morale. Once the ship was ready for service it was assigned to the '''[[Caspian 4th Fleet]]'''.

== History ==

[[File:Drorq.jpg|thumb|Commodore Drorq]] While the ''Dying Sun'' was still being prepared for service, Drorq accompanied the Caspian delegation to the 1st Annual Miners Dinner in [[Cloud City]] on [[Bespin]]. Drorq was serving as acting executive officer of the ''Leviathan'' when the city was attacked by slavers. He was ordered by Crestian to place the planet under interdiction in hopes of catching the culprits. 

Following the departure of Imperium [[Fleet Admiral]] Domicor Honos with the grievously wounded [[Johanna Siri te Danaan]] to [[Selene]], followed later by Crestian's, Captain Drorq was the senior officer in the system until the arrival of the ''[[CMS Renaissance of Innovation]]'' under the command [[Vice Admiral]] [[Kath Samba]] with a team of [[SubPro]] engineers lead Caspian Minister of Science and Technology, [[Sirlahn Inglet]]. Though the interdiction effort ultimately proved unsuccessful, Drorq was rewarded for his role in maintaining order after the attack with a promotion to [[Fleet Captain]] upon the official entry of the ''Dying Sun'' into the Caspian Navy. 

In [[33 ABY]], elements of the 4th Fleet participated in the [[Caspian Intelligence Service]] mission code named "Operation Requiem." The ships involve operated in the [[Faylar|Faylar System]] as a fictional Imperium "'''Task Force Faylar'''." Each ship involved had its transponder changed to register as an Imperium vessel, with the ships themselves being the fleets four Imperial made warships. The ''Leviathan'' operated as the the ''"Spire of Victory,"'' the ''Dying Sun'' as the ''"Oubliette,"'' the ''[[Tethys]]'' as the ''"Reborn Order,"'' and the ''[[Starkiller]],'' as the ''"Black Talon."'' Fleet Captain Drorq served as commanding officer for the task force, while Captain [[Atsvara Tarasar]] served in place of her father as acting commanding officer of the ''"Spire of Victory."''

In [[34 ABY]], Fleet Captain Drorq and the ''Dying Sun'' were involved in the Caspian 4th Fleet's efforts to assist the [[Imperium]] in the capture of prisoners who had escaped from secret [[The Oubliette|Spacestation 1138]] prison. They were credited for their role in the Imperium's operation in the Nemares Corridor with Drorq taking the initiative to interdict subspace traffic which proved critical to the operation's success. Drorq was subsequently promoted to [[Commodore]] as a reward for his actions.

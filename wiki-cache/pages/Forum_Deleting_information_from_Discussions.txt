I've noticed some people going back and deleting conversations and comments from talk pages, not in a malicious way, just 'cleaning up' after conversations that have seem to have been resolved.  Is it really appropriate to do that?  The other wikis I frequent don't seem to do this (or at least will shift content to archive pages instead of deleting it).  I don't see why we need to have 'clean' talk pages.  I see no upside in it, and on the downside we lose insight into why articles ended up the way they did.  What do other people think? -- [[User:Xerxes|Xerxes]] 13:14, 9 April 2007 (UTC)

:-On other wikis I admin upon or contribute to, there is a consistent penchance for filling Talk pages with drivel, unwarranted comments, or no-longer-relevant dialogue. If the material is important, then by all means keep it; but if it is more maintenance, sporadic commentary, or a minor issue that was addressed and ''should'' be understood, then '''by all means''' remove it (clean it up). That way, you're not scrolling down through ten tons of archaic, unnecessary fuddery. -- [[User:Hawke|Hawke / Rtufo]] 14:15, 9 April 2007 (UTC)

:-I have to agree with Rtufo on this. The Wookieepedia talk pages often turn into an exercise in masochism. I don't think it should be much of a heartache to cut out useless discussions or discussions which have limited shelf-life. Like if I asked you for help on a technical task, or how to do something, once i have read the how to do it or had my question answered, I think it would be appropriate to remove the discussion. In some cases I do agree that leaving old content is good and helpful like in the discussion on user icons, I left all the prototypes because they can be used for people in the future looking to learn from past efforts. --[[User:ImperialFH|ImperialFH]] 14:33, 9 April 2007 (UTC)

:-Agreed. -- [[User:SW1 Kyle|SW1 Kyle]] 15:57, 9 April 2007 (UTC)

:-If the primary author of an article wants to also keep the Talk page tidy, it might be useful to have an archive of sorts where helpful excerpts from the discussion might be saved. A couple of sections I can think of are: Q-and-A, where answers to technical-type questions can go, and Policy-by-Consensus, where the outcomes of policy discussions can be summarized (with links to archive pages that contain the full quoted discussions). Then links to the Q-and-A and Policy summaries can be added to the Contributor Info section, for future reference. --[[User:Lolkje|Lolkje]] 16:05, 9 April 2007 (UTC)

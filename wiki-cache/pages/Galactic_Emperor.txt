The '''Galactic Emperor''' was the head of state of the [[Galactic Empire]]. Its power and authority were mainly derived from those of the [[:starwars:Supreme Chancellor/Legends|Supreme Chancellor]] and the additional rights bestowed upon that office by the [[:starwars:Galactic Senate/Legends|Galactic Senate]] during the [[:starwars:Separatist Crisis/Legends|Separatist Crisis]] and [[:starwars:Clone Wars/Legends|Clone Wars]], though greatly enhanced and expanded to fit the role of supreme dictator.

Emperor [[:starwars:Palpatine/Legends|Palpatine's]] official heir would normally be [[:starwars:Anakin Skywalker/Legends|Darth Vader]] as his [[Sith Order|Sith]] apprentice; however, there were indications that he never intended this to happen — he wanted Vader's son, [[Luke Skywalker]], to take his father's place on Palpatine's side by killing Vader.

In the [[:starwars:Declaration of a New Order|Declaration of a New Order]], Palpatine's speech had indicated that the [[:starwars:Imperial Senate/Legends|Imperial Senate]] would elect the Galactic Emperor, but no one in the [[Imperial High Command]] had any desire to restore a body that would probably dissolve the Empire, so many Imperial commanders became rogue warlords after the [[:starwars:Battle of Endor|Battle of Endor]]. 

Many warlords would take the title of Emperor in the wake of Palpatine's death, but it wasn't until [[8 ABY]] that a true successor to Palpatine arose. As of [[15 ABY]], the Emperor was the enigmatic [[Aleister Vadim]]. 

In [[29 ABY]] Vadim, and the Empire itself was deposed and replaced by the by [[Tyler Damion|Darth Malign]] who had himself crowned as Imperator of Damion I, and the Empire reorganised into the [[Imperium]].

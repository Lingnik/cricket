A '''governor''' was a political position used throughout the [[The Galaxy|galaxy]]. For the most part, governors were the chief executives of their delegated territory, enforcing laws and sitting as head of local police or military units.

==[[Caspian Union]]==

Governor has been utilised as a title in the Union for planetary leaders and the leaders of regions on planets.

===[[Caspar]]===

Following the dissolution of the [[Caspian Democratic Union]], [[Caspar]] was left essentially on its own with most of its government from the Sariphage pandemic. The process of rebuilding initially occurred separately in the various cities on the planet with little initial coordination between them. Eventually this coalesced into the formation of city-state governments centred around the planet's major cities: [[Anchorron]], [[Dunwelton]], [[Groton]], [[Noques Rasala]], [[Orleanon]], [[Plaxton]], and [[Trinumvira]]. Each city-state elected a Governor and Lieutenant Governor. 

After the adoption of a new constitution and the election of a permanent [[Union Council]], these city-state governments have remained the basis of local governance on Caspar. Given the greater power and prestige of the Union Council, the Councillor representing each city-state is often viewed as the real power, though they possess no direct authority locally, the Governors frequently take their lead from the Councillors. There is no planetary leader or government as such, with the Union Council performing that function.

===[[Krittain Major]] & [[Krittain Minor|Minor]]===

Local governance in the [[Krittain|Krittain System]] has developed similarly to that of Caspar with city-states (Aonach, Blechley, Braemar, Lansdowne, Spittsbury, Tarrington, Trottingham, and Winton) led by a Governor and Lieutenant Governor. Following the Krit Union's readmission into the Caspian Union, the Krittish Senate dissolved as a regular government body, with the Senators taking seats in the Union Council. Nevertheless, when matters arise that require governance on a planetary or system wide base the Krittish Councillors may be called into a special senate session by [[King]] Erskine Braemar, the constitutional monarch of the system or Maximilian Kritz, who retains the title of President of the Krit Union.

===[[Mergansar]]===

In the waning days of the Caspian Democratic Union, Governor was utilised on Mergansar as the title for the planetary leader. The planetary government was under the control of elements of [[Olumi Osahn]] that were perpetrating [[Report:Mergani Pogrom Press Releases|pogroms]] against the non-Sarian population. In retaliation for the government's complicity in this genocide, Caspian expatriate and [[New Sith Order|Sith]] [[Admiral]] [[Crestian Tarasar]] targeted and killed of Governor [[Raif Avosam]] during the [[Leviathan|Leviathan's]] attack on Olumi Osahn on Mergansar. A shortly while later, Lieutenant Governor, now Acting Governor [[Lau Saifax]] was killed in a slaying orchestrated by Tarasar. As Minister of Justice [[Marck Narlin]] announced he was taking the office of Governor during a life broadcast his safe house was destroyed in an explosion, again orchestrated by Tarasar.

As the Caspian Union began to reassert control over Mergansar following Tarasar's election as [[Presav]], [[Sar Admiral]] [[Odie Hood]] and Councillor of Ithaca was appointed Military Governor of Mergansar to oversee new elections and ensure those involved in perpetrating the pogroms or who were complicit in those crimes were not allowed to run for office. Military Lieutenant Governor's were appointed for each city back under Caspian control. Hood would later resign as Military Governor and be replaced by Councillor [[Philinda Jorgan]] of Charon. 

Following the conclusion of Mergansar's rehabilitation process, the office of Military Governor was abolished with city-state governments (Anphora, Charon, Goshen, Hammerton, Hebron, Ithaca, Spanner, Toresta, and Vilarasala) with a Governor and Lieutenant Governor established on similar to those on Caspar. As on Caspar there is no planetary leader or government as such, with the Union Council performing that function.

===[[Mikassa]]===

Following Mikassa becoming a ward of the Caspian Democratic Union in [[13 ABY]] the planetary elected leaders, who if approved by the [[Union Assembly]] would hold the title of Warden. Following the dissolution of the CDU and Mikassa emerging as a full member world in the Caspian Union, the elected leadership of the planet became Governor and Lieutenant Governor, with the Union government no longer holding any veto power.

===[[Rauther]]===

As on Caspar, Rauther's three major cities: Breslau, Humberling, and Luderwaark, are each organised into a city-state with a Governor and Lieutenant Governor. Any one of the Governor's may convene a planetary assembly to which each city-state sends a delegation when necessary.

==[[Galactic Empire]]==
Its use in the [[Galactic Empire]] was two-fold: '''planetary governors''' were chosen by the [[Imperial Ruling Council]] and were often given orders from the [[Bureau of Operations#Diplomatic Services (DiploServ)|Diplomatic Service]] branch of [[Imperial Intelligence]]. The governor acted above the authority of the planet's local government, of which was little changed by the Empire, as long as it conformed to Imperial doctrine and law. Most planetary governors were granted their own military units to police their world, although the system's [[Moff]] could, at any time, take direct control from the governor. As such, the governor was always answerable to his respected system's Moff, or the '''regional governor''', the second use of governorship within the Empire.

The regional governor, or Moff, were appointed by the [[Imperial Ruling Council]] or the [[Galactic Emperor]] himself to exercise more efficient military and political dominance over the far-stretched Imperial territories. The Moff ruled over a [[Sector]], a territory of space bunching numerous systems into one political unit. The Moff had total control over all military and political resources within his Sector, including the planetary governors.

The planetary governor also appointed a '''lieutenant governor''', a position which exercised very little power.

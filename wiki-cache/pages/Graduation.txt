On Zhellday [[Caspian Union|Caspian]] [[Presav]] [[Crestian Tarasar]] gave the commencement address at the Groton Military Academy on [[Caspar]]. His remarks centre around the place of the [[Caspian Defence Force]] in the galaxy and the role the new graduates play in that. It is approximately five minutes.

The Presav stands at a podium on the stage of an auditorium. Seated behind him are a number of officers from the three branches of the Caspian Defence Force. They represent a number of species though [[Human|Humans]] and [[Near Human|Near Humans]] are the most prevalent.

"I want to start by thanking the members of the commencement committee for the invitation to speak to you today. It is truly an unexpected honour and privilege to be here. I hope I can live up to your expectations as a speaker.

There is a very old saying in the galaxy, 'may you live in interesting times.' I don't know about you, but having lived through interesting times I would gladly welcome the chance to have been able to study them from academic distance. What a joy it would be to have this age of strife as something of the distance past and be able to bask in safety and security, to have the dead as a distant memory and not our recent past. But we do live in interesting times and you here have answered their call and risen to their challenge.

For my generation and the one that preceded it, the defining events of our lives are often deeply wrapped up in the [[Galactic Civil War]]. Our characters were already well forged before we faced the challenges of the pandemic and the pogroms. For me it is strange when I look out at you and your futures and think to myself that we may finally have moved forward enough that the Galactic Civil War may me little to your lives. Sadly though you have inherited different baggage, you have come of age when our Union was torn asunder. That memory will be etched in your psyches long after it is something your children and their children are reading about in their history class.

You are the survivors of the [[Report:Sariphage Press Releases|pandemic]], the refugees of the [[Report:Mergani Pogrom Press Releases|pogroms]], daughters of [[Yari]] technicians, sons of [[Rauther|Rautheran]] [[Constable|constables]], you are the riff-raff of [[Krittain]], the Princes of [[Ixin]], the acolytes of [[Charros IV|Charros]], and you are more. The Caspian Defence Force represents one of the most diverse military organisations the galaxy has yet seen, but we are more the sum of our collective backgrounds. The Union is a promise to the galaxy.

You have something in your favour no [[New Republic|Republic]] or [[Galactic Empire|Imperial]] service member will ever possess. You will never be the representative of some distant government, you will never be in service to some bureaucracy long divorced from the needs and values of its people, you will never represent the might of your government. What you do represent and what Union forces have always represented is a beacon of hope. We are the neighbours of the galaxy, there to lend aid when your house on fire, when slavers are at your door, we provide security in an unsafe universe, and when necessary we will fight even our own selves in defence of our citizens and our values.

Chances are, at some point in your career, you will be sent into harm's way. But I can promise you this, everyone in your chain of command between you and me has your back. We will never send you somewhere we would not go ourselves, we will ask of you something we would not ask of our own children. But remember this chain does not end with you. As you advance in your careers that is the promise you must uphold to those that come next.

I want to conclude by asking you to take a good look at the cadets you are graduating with today. There will be times in your life where you will look for a moral compass in what you are doing. Do not look to your superiors for that, I hate to say it been sometimes we will let you down. Where you need to place you trust is in yourselves and in each other. When I say the Union represents a beacon of hope, that hope isn't me or the people sitting behind me, it is in those of you sitting in front of me. 

It is in you that we have our trust, you will make a better Union and a better galaxy than we have done, just as we have tried to do better than those who came before us. The Union will never be perfect, it never came be. But in you we have a promise of a future, a future that will be more than yesterday was, more than today is. So today, and in every day to come I want to thank you for your service, I want to thank you for being our beacon of hope."

'''Gran''' are beige skinned humanoids with large, black eyes on three eyestalks,
short floppy ears on the sides of their heads and small horns on the tops and
back of their heads. Gran have long, pronounced jaws ending in a tapered, 
almost cow like snout. They have 6 long fingers, ending in blunt, hooflike
claws. There is also a sub-species of Gran that has stumpy fingers.

Gran are a peaceful species that have been a part of galactic society since
the [[:w:c:starwars:Galactic_Republic|Old Republic]]. Their peaceful nature has lead many a space going Gran to
persue diplomatic or honest trade positions. Most Gran hail from their
homeworld of [[:w:c:starwars:Kinyen|Kinyen]], which has had a 10 millennium history of having no
recorded conflict. The Gran have also colonized many worlds, including the
planet [[:w:c:starwars:Malastare|Malastare]]. Kinyen is a lush world with mountains and valleys. The
buildings are painted with bright, lush colours that are reminiscent of the
flowers that bloom all over the planet. Even colony worlds are decorated with
bright colours, especially if the world is drab.

The Gran are social beings and while they are not voilent, they are not
pushovers and will defend members of their species and close friends from
injury &mdash; physical or otherwise.

These aliens are very social and their three eyes give them vision into the
infared range. They have a knack of judging the moods of warm blooded
humanoids by the shifts in their heat patterns. Gran view all members of their
species as family and Gran are seldom seen alone. Exile is a severe punishment
for Gran and if they are left alone in the galaxy without other Gran
companionship they can go mad and lose their pacifistic nature. Other close
companionship may reduce this insanity, but the Gran will always long for his
or her homeworld and people. Gran from colonies are generally better adjusted
to being alone than those from Kinyen.  Grans tend to dress in bright colours
unless they are homesick, as the bright colours remind them of their kin and
can lead them to great distress.

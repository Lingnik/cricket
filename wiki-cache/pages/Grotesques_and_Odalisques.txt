'''Grotesques and Odalisques: Requiem for Basca Starker''' was a party held at the Hall of Heroes at [[Castle Tsabak]] on [[Dreven]] hosted by [[Malif Tal-Tahn]] and presented by [[Zubindi Hakoon]] and the [[Silverwood House of Fashion]] in [[33 ABY]].

==Background==

In [[33 ABY]], [[Athaniss]] based business tycoon and former [[Corporate Sector Authority]] [[Office of the Auditor General|Auditor General]] [[Malif Tal-Tahn]] contacted the [[Silverwood House of Fashion]] in order to hire them for a project. Silverwood's proprietor and chief designer [[Zubindi Hakoon]] along with his protege [[Veruca Violet]] met with Malif in his office on Athaniss. The project it turned out was to put together a party based on the aesthetic of [[Basca Starker]], the man responsible for the Sariphage [[Report:Sariphage Press Releases|pandemic]] on [[Caspar]] which killed 2.8 billion people including Veruca Violet's parents.

Zubindi was quite surprised to find Malif had any reason have an interest in Basca, but was told by Malif that he had acquired some of the data Basca had possessed. While Malif claimed he had no desire to condemn or condone Basca's deeds he felt that his aesthetic was unique and worth exploration. The repeated description Malif provided was 'beauty and beast.' Zubindi had worked with Basca prior to his role in the genocide being uncovered and did find him to be a man of refined tastes, though not necessarily unique.

Both Zubindi and Veruca considered the entire prospect entirely offensive. That being said Zubindi agreed to throw the party for two reasons. The first was the artistic challenge of taking an underdeveloped and controversial theme and actually throwing a decent event out of it. The other reason was to use the party as an opportunity to gain intelligence on Malif and any of the galaxy's rogues that had the poor taste to attend.

Malif gave Zubindi a free hand financially and in location choice. Ultimately Zubindi selected the former Imperial palace on [[Dreven]], [[Castle Tsabak]]. The location fit the grandeur of the egos involved and Zubindi was able to gain permission from the [[Imperium]] to utilise the location.

==The Party==

The official party title was "Grotesques and Odalisques: Requiem for Basca Starker." Taking the theme of 'beauty and beast' to the next level the party was populated by models and staff that were meant to represent aesthetic extremes. Men whose appearances disgusted or frightened conventional human sensibilities and women who represented conventional human ideals.

The centrepiece of the party was the fake corpse that 'Marius Starker' had presented to the [[Caspian Crest|Crest]] claiming it was Basca. It was placed an altar of sorts that was approachable from the front and surrounded on the remaining sides by a holographic garden. Attending the corpse and garden was a model who looked like Basca's lover [[Jessalyn Valios]]. 

A canopy of vines dangled above the hall, two different varieties of vine were utilised: [[:starwars:Bwullin|Bwullin]], a flowering vine from [[Ithor]] with purple blossoms and [[:starwars:Creeper vine|Creeper Vines]] from [[:starwars:Datar/Legends|Datar]] whose leaves are horn-shaped and soft white in colour. A layer of black smoke permeatd the room though it did not rise much above the floor. The smoke was generated by a plethora of discretely located machines which provided coverage to the entire room. The fog itself was largely water based and laced with mild intoxicants.

===Busts===

During its time as an Imperial Palace, the Hall of Heroes contained the busts of various '[[Galactic Empire|Imperial]] Heroes.' Those were placed in storage and replaced with larmalstone busts of some of the galaxy's worst bioterrorists to reflect the fact the party was honouring the aesthetic of one of their number.

Among those so 'honoured' are [[:starwars:Hethrir|Hethrir]], the Imperial Procurator of Justice who unleashed a strain of the [[:starwars:Hive virus|Hive Virus]] on his homeworld [[:starwars:Firrerre/Legends|Firrerre]] to prove his loyalty to the Empire, and in the process wiping out most of his species. [[:starwars:Nuvo Vindi/Legends|Nuvo Vindi]] and [[:starwars:Zeta Magnus|Zeta Magnus]] were 'honoured' for their work recreating the [[:starwars:Blue Shadow Virus/Legends|Blue Shadow Virus]]. And no such collection would be complete without [[:starwars:Jenna Zan Arbor|Jenna Zan Arbor]].

Curiously there was also a bust of Professor [[Daln Bael]] who led the [[Groton Sciences Institute]] on [[Caspar]] during the [[Report:Sariphage Press Releases|Sariphage]] and by extension the [[Caspian Democratic Union|Caspian]] response to plague unleased by [[Basca Starker]] for whom the party is themed. Given his response to the crisis has been widely critised, one can only guess at what message was being sent be his inclusion.

===The Grotesques===

The grotesques represented the expansion beyond the broad category of beast to a specific image of mutation that drew upon the idea of [[Sith Order|Sith]] spawn such as Basca's alter ego. To accomplish this Zubindi drew upon men who suffered severe disfiguring burns or radiation damage. He also made use of a number of [[:starwars:Xa Fel (species)|Xa Fel]] men because of the disfigured appearance of their population owing to the environmental damage on their world.

In a beastly though not-grotesque vein Zubindi also be employed a number of [[Devaronian|Devaronians]] and [[Givin]] owing to their appearances corresponding to devils and ghosts in various folklore. He also employed some members of other races, in spite of their less 'grotesque' nature because they paired well with the activity they were to be engaged in such as [[Kubaz]] and [[Neimoidian]] waiters.

All the grotesques wore black clothes with obsidian jewellery. Some were be more scantily clad than others depending on what aspect of their body was being objectified as 'grotesque.' Some of the grotesques, particularly the Devaronians were armed with 'prop' weapons.

===The Odalisques===

The odalisques represented the expansion beyond the broad category of beauty to a specific image drawing upon the image of harem concubines. In contrast with grotesques and the legacy of Basca Starker, the idea was not only to embody ideal beauty but the degradation inherent in association with Basca's evil. To that end all the odalisques were Humans and Near-Humans with fair skin wearing white and Kubaz Xirkonia jewellery. The outfits varied in exact design but many were quite revealing both to highlight the beauty of their bodies but also to be purposely objectifying.

===Music===

The party's music was largely split by two bands performing diametrically opposed music: Dooku's Droogs a rage-metal band and the Five Elements a band of [[:starwars:Theelin/Legends|Theelin]] [[:starwars:Diva|Divas]]. Poetic lamentations by Jessalyn look-a-like were interspersed between their performances.

====Dooku's Droogs====

Dooku's Droogs are a rage-metal band that takes its name from Count [[:starwars:Dooku/Legends|Dooku]] with a number of their songs themed around the Clone Wars though their repertoire includes songs about other conflicts and events. Each wears black nanosilk robes in emulation of Dooku and is armed with a blunderbuss slug-throwing pistol shaped to look similar to Dooku's curved-hilt lightsaber.

Members include: Count Binface a [[Droid]] who 'sings' and plays a Mood Synthesizer, Power Drum, and Thunder Cannon; Count Ghostface a [[Givin]] who plays a Kintarrian Death Drum; Count Hornface, a [[Devaronian]] who plays an Electric Quetarra; Count Whaleface a [[:starwars:Herglic/Legends|Herglic]] who sings and plays a Nalargon; and the newest member Count Burntface a [[:starwars:Xa Fel (species)|Xa Fel]] who plays a Flaming Rumble Bass.

====The Five Elements====

[[:starwars:Diva|Divas]] were a religious order of female singers within [[:starwars:Theelin/Legends|Theelin]] culture. In the final years of the Galactic Republic, Theelin's were facing extinction as the Theelin genome had mutated to no longer allow Theelin's to successfully reproduce offspring with members of their own species. The infant mortality was high even among pairings with [[Human|Humans]] and other [[Near-Human]] species. By this point in history there were only a few million 'full' and part Theelin left.

In hopes of preserving their culture as they slid towards extinction most Theelin women were given the title of Diva regardless of vocal talent. Some of their greatest works were produced in these years, however, as an epitaph for their dying people. By [[10 ABY]] full Theelin were believed to be extinct.

Given their rarity, Theelin Divas are usually solo acts making The Five Elements a rather unique group. Their members include: Diva Papalaguna (Water) a [[Pantoran]]-Theelin hybrid, Diva Zorajai (Earth) a [[Mirialan]]-Theelin hybrid, Diva Diathim (Air) an [[:starwars:Omwati/Legends|Omwati]]-Theelin hybrid, Diva Eiresia (Fire) a [[Sarian]]-Theelin hybrid, and Diva Leeloominai (Aether) a [[Human]]-Theelin hybrid with orange hair. Their outfits for the gala essentially consist of a thermal bandage look.

==Guests==

To the surprise and consternation of many, particularly [[Zubindi Hakoon]] and [[Veruca Violet]], [[Malif Tal-Tahn]] the man who instigated an paid for the gala did not actually show up himself. Without the presence of the man who had called for a theme around the aesthetic of the notorious [[Basca Starker]], little sense could be made of why the party was happening to begin with.

==Aftermath==

It has been quite a number of years since Dreven had much relevance to the galaxy. Though it is an exporter of electronics, clearly much of its modern existence was centred around being an [[Galactic Empire|Imperial]] throne world. Following the advent of the [[Imperium]], Dreven ended up joining the [[New Republic]]. It was largely ignored by the Republic and ultimately left to join the Imperium, seeing little additional attention there.

The preperations for the gala alone brought a great influx of credits into the Old Imperial City and led to some improvements in civic architecture. The attendance by the Imperator and several business executives, particularly some of the new owners of [[Biscuit Baron]] saw Dreven end up back on the map economically with an increase in trade and outside investment interest.

[[Image:Groton Sciences Institute.jpg|200px|thumb]] The '''Groton Sciences Institute''' is the premier research facility in the [[Caspian Union]], with research going on in a wide range of fields from atmospheric science, to geology, to medicine, and virtually every other field of scientific endeavour. For two decades GSI was consider synonymous top tier research and scientific excellence in the Union. While still the Union's most prominent scientific institution it's prestige has suffered greatly following its controversial role in the response to the [[Report:Sariphage Press Releases|Sariphage pandemic]].

== Background ==

Located in [[Caspar|Caspar's]] Skayla Archipelago, [[Groton]] is the most remote of the planet's cities located in its far north. For much of its history this isolation made it mainly a haven for people who wished to conduct their affairs away from the prying eyes of the rest of society. This changed with the advent of the [[Galactic Empire]] as this isolation proved equally attractive to the Imperial administration who wished to keep their assets away from the greater populace. The city was built up as a citadel with a multitude of new structures built and equipment brought in. When the Empire pulled out the [[Caspia|Caspia System]] in [[2 BBY]] most of the equipment was left behind. In [[4 ABY]], the city would be where [[Admiral]] [[Dergan Roj]] and his followers based themselves out of, repurposing what their predecessors had left behind and attempting to avoid disrupting Caspian society as a whole.

Under the administration of [[Presav]] [[Lyr Dunwell]] in [[5 ABY]] and his [[First Renaissance of Innovation|Renaissance of Innovation]], both the Groton Military Academy and Groton Sciences Institute were founded. Through out the two decades that followed GSI had its hand in nearly every major scientific project in the Union, partnering with both corporations and the military, and operating an number of satellite installations through [[Caspian Sector|Caspian Space]].

== Sariphage Pandemic ==

When the [[Report:Sariphage Press Releases|Sariphage pandemic]] hit [[Caspar]], the [[Caspian Crest|Crest]] placed Professor [[Daln Bael]], the Director of the Groton Sciences Institute, in charge of the response. So great was the authority entrusted to him that he was publicly issue instructions to the [[Caspian Navy]] in terms of quarantining the planet. It is not entirely known, however, whether the members of the government and military command structure were still alive at this point and whether his authority had been delegated by a living government or assumed from a dead one.

Professor Bael incorrectly identified the virus as natural, never knowing that it had been engineered by [[Basca Starker]], the man who became known for curing it. Bael further believed that the risk of the virus spreading off world was too great to allow any outside effort to try to cure it. To this end he instructed the Caspian Navy to blockade the world to stop its spread and allow the sentient population of Caspar to all die out. This position has been widely attacked in the years since as nihilistic and unbefitting of Caspian society.

Chief among Bael's detractors has been [[Crestian Tarasar]] the man who became [[Presav]] after a new permanent government was elected. A key part of the platform of his party [[Praecet]] in the election was to never allow repeat performance of Bael's response. He was so disgusted by it and so afraid of the possibility of another pandemic that he founded his own research institution, [[Twin Suns Hospital]] in [[Trinumvira]] as an alternative.

== Recent History ==

Under the Tarasar administration the Groton Sciences Institute still receives grant money from the government but at a drastically reduce rate. Whenever possible Tarasar attempts to steer money towards other institutions, both based on historical animosity to it as an institution and his belief that relying so much on any one institution is too risky. In the wake of this reduced funding GSI has been forced to sell off some of its facilities to private interests in order to be able to afford maintain the remaining ones.

The animosity towards GSI is not universal and it does have its own advocates within the [[Union Council]] including Vice Presav [[Cas Mos]], herself the Councillor for Groton. As the time of the pandemic grows more distant, GSI has slowly regained some of its reputation. It is still considered the Union's best research institution overall and particularly in a number of specific disciplines including atmospheric science. In other areas such as medicine and mineralogy is has been supplanted by other institutions such as Twin Suns Hospital or groups such as the [[Xi Char]] and their [[Haor Chall Engineering]].

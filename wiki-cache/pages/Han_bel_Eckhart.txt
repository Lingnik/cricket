[[Colonel]] '''Han'bel S. Eckhart''' is a [[New Republic]] [[New Republic Marine Corps|Marine]] officer originally from [[Corellia]]. In [[31 ABY]], Eckhart was based out of [[Dreven]] conducting patrols in the region when he received a distress call from [[:starwars:Gorsh|Gorsh]], an unaligned world in Wild Space. His Republic superiors ordered him to ignore the distress call and return back to base. The incident, one of many where he felt the Republic leadership was abandoning its role in the galaxy, proved to be the final straw. Eckhart could not stand by any longer and chose a different path.

Within weeks, the New Republic confirmed that Colonel Han'bel Eckhart went rogue along with the crew of the ''[[DSV Templar|Templar]]'' taking the ship with them. Though the Republic attempted paint them as dangerous criminals, the public perception was split. Eckhart and the Templar would prove a cause célèbre through much of [[32 ABY]]. Reporting on the good deeds of Eckhart and the ''Templar'', while simultaneously reporting on the corruption that led their defection, and Republic efforts to misrepresent them helped build the brand of [[Thurston Unger]] and his programme [[The Cutting Edge]].

The corruption scandal led to protests and riots on a number of Republic worlds, Dreven itself ultimately left the Republic and turned back to the [[Imperium]]. In spite, of the continued popularity of Eckhart and the ''Templar'', the Republic did not desist in its efforts against the rogue crew. In [[34 ABY]], the ''Templar'' was captured by New Republic forces and Colonel Eckhart placed into custody.

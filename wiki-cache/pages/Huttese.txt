Huttese is the language of the [[Hutt]] species, those species which have become subservient to the Hutts, and nearly anywhere that Hutt influence prevails. Due to the massive influence of the Hutts, Huttese is arguably the second most understood language in the galaxy. The species within [[Hutt Space]] understand it almost universally, whether they speak it or not. 

== History ==
Huttese has apparently remained relatively unchanged for the past five hundred or so years, influencing other languages while refusing to integrate anything foreign into itself. Archaeological evidence of written ancient Huttese dating back 1,000 years exists, proving that it is parsable to the modern reader, and was used for purposes indicating the Hutts' long history in criminal pursuits, the historic record being in the form of ransom notes.

== Speaking Huttese ==
Due to Hutt tongue structure it is not difficult for humanoids to learn to pronounce and even the beaked [[Toydarian]]s can manage understandable Huttese, though true fluency takes years of study. Most species find it easiest to learn to understand the language, but speak in [[Galactic Basic Standard|Basic]] in response, which most Hutts understand though they believe it beneath themselves to actually utter the words.

Some of the best swearing in the galaxy is done in Huttese, which lends itself magnificently to the task by being rife with guttural sounds and palatal fricatives. It is nearly impossible to speak the language without expulsion of saliva, which makes it unsuited for polite company but especially appropriate from the aforementioned swearing or issuance of threats. 

== Grammar and syntax ==
Huttese grammar is as slippery as the Hutts themselves, evidencing no standardization and unquantifiable to non-Huttese speakers. However standards exist and only through immersion in the language can one come to understand how it all works together. Non-fluents are advised to  memorize key phrases in their entirety or hire a good translator in order to avoid error. Perhaps this is why so many non-Hutts understand, but do not attempt to speak, the language.

A shadowed jewel buried in the untamed reaches of Wild Space, '''Induomodo''' orbits at the forgotten fringe of the galaxy, deep within its namesake system. This terrestrial world is cloaked in vast jungles and ancient forests that breathe with their own unknowable rhythm. Beneath the canopy, life thrives in chaotic elegance—roots twist like coiled serpents, and the air itself hums with unseen tension.

Induomodo’s most infamous inhabitants are the [[:starwars:Kouhun/Legends|kouhuns]]—venomous, arthropodal predators that nest in humid subterranean caverns and the marrow-hollows of ancient, hollow trees. Elusive and brutally efficient, kouhuns are known to prey on the mid-sized tree-creatures that scamper across the dense canopy's ecosystem. Their neurotoxic stingers leave no trace—only stillness. It is for this reason that poisoners and assassins from the darker corners of the galaxy have sought them out, braving the jungle’s silence to harvest a death that cannot be traced.

Though located near the terminus of the [[Corellian Run]], Induomodo is rarely mapped and even less often mentioned. It has no colonies, no ports of call, no relevance to trade or diplomacy. It is a place the galaxy forgets on purpose.

One of its rare moments in the galactic eye came in [[22 BBY]], when bounty hunter [[:starwars:Jango Fett/Legends|Jango Fett]] commissioned [[:starwars:Zam Wesell/Legends|Zam Wesell]] to use a pair of starved kouhuns to eliminate [[Senator]] [[:starwars:Padmé Amidala/Legends|Padmé Amidala]] on [[Coruscant]] The attempt failed. The kouhuns were slain by [[Jedi Order|Jedi]] [[:starwars:Anakin Skywalker/Legends|Anakin Skywalker]], and Induomodo slipped once more into the quiet fog of irrelevance.

But the planet endures.

It listens.

And it waits—unchanged, unread, and not without teeth.

== The Landing ==
[[File:Thornmere early days.jpg|thumb|Thornmere camp|left|300px]]
'''Thornmere''' was the first established camp on Induomodo, constructed in [[31 ABY]] by [[Nasiri Zithreej|Nasiri]] and his crew shortly after planetfall aboard the ''Kohoun''. Nestled within a natural basin choked by jungle and bramble-thorns and slow-draining mist, Thornmere earned its name from the dense foliage that had to be burned back daily and the near-total silence that clung to the site like fog. Though meant to be temporary, Thornmere became the nucleus around which later structures formed, including what would become Thornmere Station.

[[File:ThornmereS.jpg|thumb|Thornmere Station|300px]]
Known for its oppressive quiet and erratic magnetic interference, Thornmere was believed by early settlers to be “listening.” Jessa Eiros once referred to it as “a place that does not forget,” though she never clarified the meaning. Some reports from the Landing Year describe strange nocturnal sounds and anomalies near the perimeter, though no formal incidents were recorded. The original landing pad and shelter scaffolds remain, preserved within the deeper sections of Thornmere Station.

== The Happy Hate ==
Shortly after Thornmere was carved from the jungle and its outer base camps surveyed, the skies above Induomodo lit with arrival. The ''Mirth of Malice'' dropped into high orbit like a descending crown—flanked by the ''NightGaunt'' a dreadnought class cruiser and six [[Shearwater-class Frigate|Shearwater-class Light Cruisers]]. What began as a deployment would evolve into the planet’s permanent orbital garrison: a shield of iron and intent.

Over several chaotic days, the surface transformed. Massive dropships ferried colonists from the ''Mirth’s'' lower bays to scattered, pre-cleared settlement zones—each marked for the varied needs of [[Falleen]] anarchists, [[:starwars:Sakiyan/Legends|Sakiyan]] hunters, [[Gamorrean]] labor clans, and the meticulous [[:starwars:Geonosian/Legends|Geonosian]] engineers. The air was thick with unfamiliar languages, bootsteps, and the scent of soil being claimed.

At the core of the security presence stood the Sakiyan troopers—Nasiri’s elite infantry—clad in gleaming chrome armor that turned jungle sunlight into cold brilliance. Silent, calculating, and relentless, they set the tone with their patrols. Wildlife attacks waned. Tensions flared. The settlements held.

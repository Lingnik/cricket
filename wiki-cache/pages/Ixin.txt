'''Ixin''' is an arid planet in the Otus System, in the [[Caspian Sector]], in the Mid Rim. It has generally been considered to be a backwater world. Known for ancient ruins and unique customs, it eschewed membership in the [[Caspian Democratic Union]] for many years. By the time it finally joined in [[21 ABY]], in hopes of stimulating its stagnating economy, the CDU was already faded from galactic relevance. Following the collapse of the CDU in [[28 ABY]] on the heels of the Sariphage pandemic and Mergani pogroms, Ixin became independent once more as the Republic of Ixin. It joined the new [[Caspian Union]] in [[29 ABY]] roughly one month after the conclusion of elections on [[Caspar]] to elect a permanent [[Union Council]] and the inauguration of the administration of [[Presav]] [[Crestian Tarasar]].

== History ==

Original habitation of Ixin dates back more then twenty-five millennia when the world was inhabited by a non-human, non-native civilisation. Little is definitively known about this civilisation, though there is a great deal of speculation among scholars that it was one of the galaxy's great lost civilisations. Whoever they were they left behind a considerable number of temples, which to this day, serve as notable tourist attractions, with that tourism one of the planet's largest source's of income.

[[Human]] civilisation dates back roughly ten millennia, with vast mineral wealth propelling the development of great cities and the colonisation of nearby worlds including [[Mikassa]]. The men who controlled these natural resources began to be styled as [[Prince|Princes]]. It became custom as well for all the sons of a prince to hold that style as well, and all their sons in perpetuity. This proliferation of royalty would ultimately lead to the importation of alien slaves to serve the ever expanding number of royal households. This unrestrained growth would drive mineral exploitation and exportation to the point that the planet suffered devastating and seemingly irreparable desertification four millennia ago.

The environmental devastation saw a great decline in Ix civilisation. Among other consequences of this was the abandonment of their colony worlds. In the case of Mikassa, it became so isolated in this time that is population became differentiated enough from the base Human norm to be classified as a Near-Human race, in spite of the fact that its parent civilisation remained part of galactic society and biological Human norms.

Though the Ix civilisation contracted to fewer and fewer population centres in the centuries and millennia to follow, enough credits and mineral wealth remained to sustain their existence and the comfort of the princes. Though it would never again become a major galactic player it managed to maintain a sufficient level of trade, primarily with [[Hutt Space]], to remain on the star charts.

Over time the number of princes ballooned to a point it was estimated that fully one fifth of all male Ix had the right to the title, though some chose not to use it. In fact, the planet would often be referred to as "the Planet of Princes." The number of mercenaries, retainers, and slaves that accompanied the wealthiest of princes also rose to the point that Humans would eventually only make up half of around half of the planet's population.

By the time of the [[Galactic Civil War]], Ixin's best days were far past. Even within the Caspian Sector, Ixin was not considered very relevant, and it completely escaped the notice of [[Dergan Roj]] and his [[Barren Sector Fleet]]. Though Ixin could be considered within the bounds of the [[Caspian Democratic Union]], the CDU showed no interest in extending membership to Ixin. The Ix, likewise, had little interest in joining the Union. They had no history or interest in democracy. Furthermore, though slavery had been abolished on Ixin by this time, they had little interest in supporting the CDU's strong anti-slavery stands, and continued to view the [[Hutt|Hutts]] as a favoured trading partner. There was also a certain antipathy among many Ix princes toward the [[Yari|Yaries]] of [[Kasputin Yar]]. In addition to a certain amount of pure racism, the Yaries embrace of their environment was antithetical to the proud Ix tradition of despoilment.

It would take nearly two decades before Ix society progressed enough to be open to membership in the CDU, and only then out of economic desperation more than an embrace of values. Their admission to the Union in [[21 ABY]] went little noticed, and the CDU had itself declined to the point that admission provided little of the benefit the Ix had sought in joining. When the Union finally collapsed in [[28 ABY]] following the Sariphage pandemic and Mergani pogroms, the Ix were happy enough to let it disband and resumed independence as the Republic of Ixin, a nominally democratic state that was in practice a feudal state once again.

== Recent Events ==

A major part of the platform of [[Crestian Tarasar|Crestian Tarasar's]] [[Praecet]] party's platform during the Caspian election of [[29 ABY]] was rebuilding the Union. While both Kasputin Yar and [[Rauther]] joined the new [[Caspian Union]] within a fortnight of his government taking power, neither the [[Krittain|Krit Union]] nor Republic of Ixin were part of the initial negotiations. In both cases the reasons were ideological. While there was mutual antipathy between Praecet and the Krit Union, the exclusion of Ixin centred more around their antipathy towards the Yaries. Ultimately, Tarasar considered it impractical to bring both worlds back at once, and reclaiming the [[Lokkai Starworks]] shipyards was considered of far greater importance to Union security.

Nevertheless, Ix reintegration would not follow far behind. Following the Sariphage pandemic and Mergani pogroms a number of members of the [[Caspian Crest|Crest]] who were on Ixin at the time, particularly associated with the [[Naval Intelligence Office]], had faked their own deaths and gone to ground. Among these included [[Sar Admiral]] [[Odie Hood]] and [[Senator of the Union|Senator]] [[Philinda Jorgan]] who considered their native [[Mergansar]] to be a lost cause for the time being following the rise [[Olumi Osahn]] puppet regime. Ixin had served as good a place in their minds to lay low and wait for more favourable conditions.

The election of Tarasar and his administration and its hardline strategy to force Mergani capitulation appealed to these figures. They in turn would work behind the scenes to help facilitate the negotiations between the Tarasar administration and the Princes for reunification. A fortnight after Kasputin Yar and Rauther joined the Union, Ixin followed suit. In turn Hood and Jorgan would soon become involved in the [[Caspian Intelligence Service|Caspian Intelligence Service's]] efforts to undermine the Mergani government. Hood and then later Jorgan would serve in turn as [[Governor|Military Governors]] of Mergansar after the Union began to retake the world, and each would go on to serve as their city's respective representatives in the [[Union Council]].

Ixin has since come to have a somewhat outsized role politically with the Union. The Ix based political party [[Lijsttrekker]] has become the third-largest party in the Union Council and its all-member coalition government. In addition two both Councillors from Ixin, two from Mergansar, one from [[Charros IV]], and ironically enough the one Yari representing Kasputin Yar are members of the party. 

Following the on [[Cloud City]] during the 1st Annual Miners Dinner on [[Bespin]] in which key Union dignitaries were present and the uptick of related terrorist activity, Presav Tarasar tapped [[The Chevalier]], an Ixin based unit, to serve as the security detail for Presavadency. The Chevalier, was foreign legion of sorts, which had been in the employ of several princes for security. The unit also had a strong association with the former Crest officials who had hidden out on Ixin and counted Tarasar's cousin [[General]] [[Siege|Janus Tarasar]] as a member. In its new role as a Union government security force, General Janus Tarasar would take command of the unit. [[Colonel]] [[V1C3]], adjutant and bodyguard to Presav Tarasar, would become its executive officer.

== Geography ==

There is considerable evidence that Ixin was once a lush world until natural climate chance exacerbated by poor environmental management rendered the majority of the world arid approximately four millennia ago. The only significant arable land remaining is on the banks of the few major rivers which survived the desertification. Several salt water seas allow for maritime activities to augment the agriculture possible along the river banks. Most of the planets population is centred around two major cities '''Feyenoor''' and '''Udalooy'''. Archaeological evidences suggests other major population centres existed in past, but only sparse settlements centred around rare water sources now remain.

[[File:Feyenoor.jpg|Feyenoor|left|thumb]] [[File:Zig Al Fidel.jpg|thumb|Prince Zig Al Fidel]] Feyenoor is located at the confluence of the Pearl Ohwen River and the Sapphire Ohwen River. The modern city was founded three centuries ago, though and older city dating back millennia existed to the north near the Temple Al Mahd. The current metropolitan population is roughly 270 million. The city's principle Prince is Doctor [[Zig Al Fidel]], the hereditary custodian of the Temple Al Mahd, he is also serves as the city's representative on the Union Council, and the Caspian Minister of Health. 

[[File:Udalooy.jpg|Udalooy|left|thumb]] [[File:Abdulay O'Jordaan II.jpg|thumb|Prince Abdulay O'Jordaan II]] Udalooy serves at the capital and largest city of Ixin. The city dates back several millennia though its population and prestige has fluctuated considerably in that time. There is a great deal of artefacts and archaeology which suggest it was a thriving metropolis when Ixin was still a lush world. It is also located in relatively close proximity to a number of ancient temples which may suggest it was a site of settlement of the world's previous civilisation as well. It has survived in the millennia since in large part thanks to the nearby Daan River, though its population would plummet in centuries where the river's volume was in decline. The current metropolitan population is roughly 370 million. The city's principle Prince is Abdulay O'Jordaan II, the hereditary custodian of the Ten Temples, he also serves as the city's representative on the Union Council, the leader of the Lijsttrekker party, and the planet's nominal Head of State.

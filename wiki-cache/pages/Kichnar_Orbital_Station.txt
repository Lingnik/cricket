'''Kichnar Orbital Station''' (or '''KOS''') is a huge, multi-component station orbiting high in the tumultuous atmosphere of [[Kichnar]]. It was founded as prospecting platform, with a single test drill-shaft and meager facilities, and then evolved into a mining and harvesting rig, processor, way-station, and finally a bonafide city.

The station is constituted by several modules all bound together. Because of the chaotic, dangerous nature of Kichnar's atmospheric storms, the city is mobile - able to navigate, albeit slowly, to another region. For this reason, there are no hard connections between the famous [[Sloan Drydocks]] and shipbuilding facilities in higher orbit. Corporate and supply offices are located on the station, though.

The station-city acts as the capital of Kichnar's populace, which is divided between the station, the starwright facilities, and Kichnar's moons.

After the short lived Imperial [[2015 Caspar Plot|invasion]] of [[Caspar]] in [[24 ABY]] the station became the main port of the [[Caspian Democratic Union]], and the only port authorized to handle traffic to or from the outside galaxy. The port also became a [[Corporate Sector Authority]] facility, overseen directly by the [[Office of the Auditor General]].

The Sariphage [[Report:Sariphage Press Releases|pandemic]] saw the station catapulted into the spotlight as the central gathering place for those coming to [[Caspian Sector|Caspian Space]] looking to help. The subsequent [[Report:Mergani Pogrom Press Releases|pogroms]] on [[Mergansar]] saw 300 million refugees fleeing the world, with tens of millions going through the station on their way to other worlds. Ultimately 5 million of these refugees stayed on the station, doubling the station's population. Two additional modules were built to accommodate the these refugees, dubbed New Hammerton and New Spanner.

The collapse of the CDU raised questions about the station's future though remained politically under the jurisdiction of the rump [[Caspian Union|Caspar Union]]. After the adoption of a new constitution by the Provisional Assembly of the Caspar Union in [[29 ABY]] elections where held for seats on the new [[Union Council]], which included a seat for Kichnar. The returns for Kichnar were marred by terrorist [[Report:Caspian Election Posts#Kichnar Carnage|attack]] which left 118 dead and 622 wounded. The dead included [[Ulmesar]] candidate Trett Hilo who was one of the terrorists and acted as a suicide bomber, quick thinking by newly elected Councillor [[Sirlahn Inglet]] prevent the deaths of himself and the other candidates.

Following the [[Crestian Tarasar|Tarasar]] administration coming to power, the Union government [[Caspian Repatriation Act (29 ABY)|nationalised]] the station and [[SubPro|Sublight Products Corporation]] which had fallen under Corporate Sector control after the Imperial invasion five years prior. As an unintended side effect of this move most of the [[Griffon Alliance|Griffon]] refugees on the station left, taking the nationalisation as a personal slight against [[Zeak Oppenhiemer]], a Griffon himself.

The station would also cease to be the only Union port open to outside traffic, as the new Caspian government asserted the previous [[CDU Nonaggression Treaty|treaty]] with the [[Galactic Empire|Empire]] was void as both parties had ceased to exist. A new [[Imperium-Caspian Mutual Protection Pact (29 ABY)|mutual protection pact]] between the new Caspian government and the [[Imperium]], the Empire's successor state, was signed in its place.

== History ==

=== Just A Drilling Hobby ===

With the prospect of tapping into another source of marketable gases and substances, a small team of miners and technicians constructed a small, ad-hoc rig in low orbit over [[Kichnar]]. In no small feat, it would actually harvest gas and compress it, albeit in small amounts because of its limited, small-scale capacity. The rig was cyclically disabled by meteor showers and electrical storms, and rebuilt. Meteors were a near-constant threat to anything in this section of the system, as Kichnar is astride two major asteroid belts, as well as having hundreds of smaller satellites and four ring belts of its own. Numerous meteors are pulled down into the gas giant yearly, and the hailstorm-like proportions they arrive in wreak havoc. If this bombardment doesn't squelch a venture, then the electrical and wind storms that ravage the atmosphere certainly do. 

Indeed, the Old Republic prospectors kept pressing on, building onto the ever-to-be-fixed rig as need demanded. When the galactic government changed hands in [[19 BBY]], business and routine just continued, albeit under "new management". Unlike [[Mergansar]] and [[Caspar]], which incurred much more restrictive scrutiny than the virtually unpopulated gas giant, Kichnar did in fact benefit from [[Galactic Empire|Imperial]] presence. Frequently, engineering and technically-sound types on leave from the Imperial garrison at [[Groton]], Caspar, would drop by to see how the rig was holding up, and what improvements could be made to it. Soon, the little makeshift rig was filling out into a much more capable, and survivable, facility. Most of its structure was "appropriated material", scrounged or liberated from Imperial wares that were either surplus, unused, or "never missed".

=== Making It Worthwhile ===

After fifteen years of work, the little rig had become a much more substantial way station. There now was a top-of-the-line drilling system (reclaimed and imported from a damaged and written off station further out in the Outer Rim), as well as various other amenities.

=== Post Imperial Invasion ===

After the short lived Imperial [[2015 Caspar Plot|invasion]] of Caspar in [[24 ABY]], Kichnar came largely under the control of the [[Etti Group]] and [[Zeak Oppenhiemer]]. The treaty ending the conflict, and re-establishing [[Caspian Democratic Union|CDU]] neutrality required the [[Corporate Sector Authority|CSA's]] [[Office of the Auditor General]] to oversee all traffic going into and out of [[Caspian Sector|Caspian Space]]. The influx of new capital and all the traffic resulted in a significant expansion to the station. The population also changed as many CSA employees has emigrated to the station; the exiled [[Griffon Alliance|Griffons]] also benefited from their connexion to Zeak Oppenhiemer, expanding their footprint and playing a large role in new shipping industries.

=== Port in a Storm ===

As the Sariphage [[Report:Sariphage Press Releases|pandemic]] hit Caspar near the end of [[27 ABY]], the normal flow of Union space was upended again. Caspar itself would be placed under quarantine by the [[Caspian Navy]] under the orders of the Director of the [[Groton Sciences Institute]] Professor [[Daln Bael]]. A number of figures would travel the area among them [[Johanna Siri te Danaan]], the ex-wife of Prex Oppenhiemer and Caspian expatriate and [[New Sith Order|Sith]] [[Duke]] and [[Admiral]] [[Crestian Tarasar]]. Arriving with his [[Interdictor-class Immobilizer 418|Interdictor]], the ''[[Leviathan]]'', after keeping it hidden in the [[Dragonflower Nebula]] for a decade, Tarasar also secretly brought with him [[Tyler Damion|Darth Malign]], who had recently been rescued by his sister Johanna from one of the moons of [[Korriban]], where he had been trapped for the past decade.

Tarasar presented himself to [[Sar Admiral]] [[Prae Kichakressa]] to offer what aid he could to his people and was directed to the problem of [[Olumi Osahn]]. Members of the cult were using the pandemic as an excuse to engage in [[Report:Mergani Pogrom Press Releases|pogroms]] on Mergansar aimed at driving out all non-[[Sarian|Sarians]] from the world, placing the blame for the pandemic on them. The Mergani government at the time was comprised largely of members and sympathisers to the cult who offered now resistance to these actions. Tarasar and Malign would attempted to appeal to the cult's association with [[Axel Vichten]] another member of the [[New Sith Order]] to bring the cult in line with the current aims of the Sith. Not only would this effort fail, the cult would later go to the press to expose details of their meeting and Malign's involvement.

Parallel to this effort Crestian, Johanna, and Zeak would work towards finding a solution to the pandemic itself, which Professor Bael had nihilistically suggested should be allowed to run its course. Their efforts coalesced around the presence of [[Basca Starker]] a medical scientist visiting the station who expressed absolutely no humanitarian interest in attempting to find a cure. Only with the offer of a bribe by Johanna in the form of certain data from the archives of the [[Jedi Order]] would Basca get to work on what ultimately proved to be a successful cure. By the time the cure was administered to the survivors on Caspar, 2.8 billion people were already dead, with only 300 million surviving. It was also only be learned over a year later that it was in fact Basca who had developed the Sariphage virus to begin with at the behest of [[Galactic Emperor|Emperor]] [[Aleister Vadim]].

Meanwhile, unable to get Olumi Osahn to join them, Tarasar and Malign set out to beat them. As millions of refugees poured into the station from Mergansar, Tarasar began recruiting for a vigilante movement he called [[Casulmis]]. Tarasar would arm all the refugees he could, with manay willing to overlook his association with the Sith in the face of the violence they had faced from their neighbours on Mergansar. 

''Leviathan'', its support craft, and landing parties of ''Leviathan'' crew, which included Tarasar and Malign themselves, and armed refugees stormed Mergansar. They briefly turned the tide in several cities before the Caspian Navy, freed up from the quarantine with the planet's survivors being inoculated, turned its attention towards Mergansar and went to confront the ''Leviathan'', in spite of Sar Admiral Kichakressa having originally turned Tarasar towards confronting Olumi Osahn to begin with.

The subsequent [[Mergansar Incident|confrontation]] between the Caspian Navy and the ''Leviathan'' would see the bridge of the ''[[CMS Typhoon]]'' blown up killing Sar Admiral Kichakressa, ''CMS Rapace'' and ''[[CMS Prophet]]'' mysteriously crashing into one another, and ''CMS Ad Astra'' suffering extensive damage to her [[HOWL]] Launchers. The battle was a draw, however, as ''Leviathan'' was forced to flee back to [[Silver Station]]. Meanwhile Oppenhiemer responded to the Caspian Navy's distress call with his personal cruiser, the ''Winking Monkey Lizard''.

When the dust had settled 300 million people ended up fleeing Mergansar. Roughly 100 million were assisted by Zeak and the CSA in leaving Caspian Space all together. Another 100 million resettled on Caspar, which had plenty of housing to accommodate them following the decimation of its own population. Another 100 million ended up scattered among other worlds of the now collapsed Caspian Democratic Union, about five million of which stayed on the station itself. Zeak and the CSA would also fund the requisite expansion of the station to hold them, leading to the new modules of New Hammerton and New Spanner.

=== Political Complications ===

Following the collapse of the Caspian Democratic Union, the station ostensibly remained part of the rump [[Caspian Union|Caspar Union]]. Certainly, none of the other member worlds who had declared independence had claimed it. Nevertheless the Provisional Assembly that came to exist on Caspar was focused primarily on Caspar itself, with questions about the status of Kichnar Orbital Station and the ward of [[Mikassa]] almost an after thought. For the station's part, being under operational control of the Corporate Sector Authority meant these political questions had little effect on the station's regular operation.

In adopting a new constitution and preparing for the election of new permanent [[Union Council]], the Provisional Assembly ultimately decided that both Kichnar Orbital Station and Mikassa would have representation. In the case of Mikassa, elevating it for the first time as a full member world. The fact that both Kichnar Orbital Station and Mikassa had taken in roughly five million refugees each from the Mergani pogroms factored heavily in the support they received from the Provisional Assembly.

The new Union Council was to be a unicameral body, serving both executive and legislative functions. Caspar itself would have seven seats, representing city-states centred around the planets seven major settlements. Kichnar Orbital Station and Mikassa which each get a seat as well. The Kichnar election proved interesting in a number of ways, three of the four candidates were in fact refugees from the Imperial destruction of their world: [[Holicet|Holicet's]] Tilla Ulgo was Alderaanian, [[Legacy|Legacy's]] Nevidomo Dodomu was a Griffon, and [[Praecet]] ran [[Sirlahn Inglet]] a [[Sluissi]] engineer at [[SubPro]] who came to the [[Caspian Sector|Union space]] after the destruction of [[Sluis Van]]. The fourth candidate [[Ulmesar|Ulemsar's]] Trett Hilo stood little as their platform centred around the promotion of Sarian culture dominance, with half the voting electorate have specifically fled those attitudes on Mergansar.

The vote tallies we about what was expected 1,071,850 for Ulgo and Holicet, 1,597,663 for Dodomu and Legacy, 2,438,933 and the win for Inglet and Praecet, and 74,328 for Hilo and Ulmesar. The returns were marred by a terrorist attack [[Report:Caspian Election Posts#Kichnar Carnage|attack]]. The attackers were fanactic in favour of a [[Caspia|Caspian System]] that would be purely Sarian. They began with a wave of suicide bombers, followed by another six with blasters. Included in their targets was Trett Hilo, believing his participation in the election at all was apostasy to his race. 

This was ironic because Hilo too was planning his own effort to act as a suicide bomber at the returns. His attempt to blow up his fellow candidates with thermal detonator, along with himself, was thwarted by the quick thinking of newly elected Councillor Sirlahn Inglet. In the confusion following the initial attack, Hilo was slowly in pulling out his detonator. As Inglet spotted this he was able to erect an energy shield between Hilo and his fellow candidates. Inglet had a contingency plan in place and quickly summoned a quartet of [[:starwars:E4 baron droid|E4 Baron Droids]] and a squad of [[Union Marines|Union marines]] to protect the other candidates.

Following the the carnage and confusion of the initial bombings, the terrorist soons met their match. In addition, to the combat droids and marines, there was a significant presence armed and trained Griffon youth, as well as military veterans such as [[Calyx Drogyn]] and the and the soon to be elected [[Presav]] Crestian Tarasar. In the end 118 people are dead and 622 are wounded. The election continues on in the other constituencies, but with heightened security. Out of respect for the dead and wounded all parties agree to halt further campaigning.

=== Renewed Purpose ===

The new Tarasar administration quickly moved to [[Caspian Repatriation Act (29 ABY)|nationalise]] the station, with the terror attack only adding to the desire of the [[Caspian Crest|Crest]] to remove other powers such as the Corporate Sector Authority from the security equation. The Repatriation Act would also nationalise [[SubPro|Sublight Products Corporation]] which had also fallen into CSA hands. While the CSA and most of the Griffons pulled out, the station would get a new lease on life as the centre of the Caspian Navy's rebuilding efforts.

Presav Tarasar was determined to never see the Union's sovereignty undermined again as it had been from the Imperial invasion, or for that matter in being unable score a decisive victory when the Navy had engaged the ''Leviathan'' the previous year. To that end every warship in possession of the Union was to be in service, including those which had been decommissioned or never completed. Sublight Products Corporation, now under the direction of new Chief Executive Officer Sirlahn Inglet, who also became Caspian Minister of Science and Technology, would be working in overdrive refitting and repairing vessels with support from [[Lokkai Starworks]] on [[Kasputin Yar]] as well as the [[Xi Char]] of [[Charros IV]]. Caspian shipyards would also be hard at work producing new warships for sale, with those funds helping to pay for the work on the existing fleet.

The station would no longer have monopoly on outside traffic, as the [[CDU Nonaggression Treaty|treaty]] with the Empire became void as both parties ceased to exist, and the new Union did not consider itself bound by its provisions, particularly in regards to customs. The new Caspian government would, however, sign a [[Imperium-Caspian Mutual Protection Pact (29 ABY)|mutual protection pact]] with Empire's successor state, the [[Imperium]]. This alliance would lead to a considerable increase in trade between the Union and the Imperium, keeping the station as a busy hub.

The massive militarisation of the Caspian Union would see the station being important not just to the construction and upkeep of the navy but also as a base of operations. The [[Caspian 4th Fleet]], which serves as the Union's expeditionary force, is headquarters at Kichnar Orbital Station. And while the administrative headquarters of the [[Caspian 1st Fleet]] and its Defence Group Caspia is at Trinumvira Base on Caspar, in practical terms they are based out of the station as well.

== Modules ==

Rather than existing as singular structure all built at one time, Kichnar Orbital Station is a collection of eight different modules. Built over the span of decades, the various modules have semi-permanently connected with a startram allowing quick travel between them. Each modular has its own thrusters and can be detached and moved, though rather slowly.

=== Central Station ===

What is now known as Central Station began itself life as the original module of Kichnar Orbital Station. Initially constructed as makeshift rig in orbit of the planet Kichnar to harvest gasses, it eventually grew into a bonafide station. In addition to being the site of mining operations on the station, many of the stations key functions occur in this module. The station's operations centre and its public hangar are both based in Central Station.

Central Station is also home to a limited entertainment district with some of the station's oldest venues based here. Both the Blue Nebula Cantina and Cason Hawk's Nest are located in this module. Because of the public hangar this module is always abuzz with activity, but most of the station's residents live, work, and entertain themselves in other modules.

=== Sloan Drydocks ===

The storied [[Sloan Drydocks]] are one of the oldest components of Kichnar Orbital Station, only predated by the mining rig that became Central Station. Built by [[SubPro|Sublight Products Corporation]] after its relocation to the [[Caspia|Caspian System]], the Sloan Drydocks are the largest and most advanced ship construction and repair facility in the Caspian Union. This module is wholly operated by SubPro with their corporate headquarters and sales operation based here as well.

=== Nycad Stardock ===

Part of the second wave of construction of Kichnar Orbital Station, the Nycad Stardock module is the Caspian Navy's primary base in the Caspian System. The vast majority module is occupied by the Kichnar Naval Base. The base is collectively comprised of the Fleet Command and Control Centre, the Kichnar Navy Depot, and the Military Recreation Centre.

The Nycad module also serves as home for Kichnar Medical Complex, the station's main hospital which while civilian run has military staff as well. The hospital treats all inhabitants and guests of the station both civilian and military. Other amenities located here include the Freedom's Dream nightclub.

=== Darsopan Link ===

The Darsopan module was part of the second wave of construction that helped transform the drilling and drydock operations into a bona fide hub. Physically linking the original drilling station, now known as Central Station, and the Sloan Drydocks, this module became known as the Darsopan Link.

The module is largely residential and for many years the station's major residential area until subsequent modules were added for and by various refugee groups. Other amenities include Kichnar Orbital Station's famous arboretum and more recently the Grand Tapani Hotel. The Caspian Foreign Ministry also maintains a large complex in this module.

=== Sluissi Twist ===

After the destruction of [[Sluis Van]], millions of [[Sluissi]] refugees found their way to Caspian Space. With many headed to Kichnar Orbital Station, a new module was added to the station to accommodate the hundreds of thousands of Slussi that would ultimately settle here. Named after a starship [[:starwars:Sluissi twist|manoeuvrer]] that allows a large vessel to squeeze into a tight space, the Slussi Twist module was also squeezed into a tight spot between existing modules.

In addition to housing much of the stations Sluissi refugee and the shops and amenities that support them, several major corporations have facilities here. Most notable among the local businesses is the [[CDMC|Caspian Design and Manufacturing Corporand]] whose main offices and production facilities are here. [[FineLine Spaceways]] and [[Nasiri Zithreej|Zithreej Interstellar]] also have offices in this module.

=== Little Pride ===

After the destruction [[Cochran]], a number of refugees from the [[Griffon Alliance]] found their way into [[Caspian Sector|Caspian space]]. Their numbers were initially small taking up no more than a corridor on the station. In time their presence crew until they came to occupy most of an entire module of the station, which took on the name Little Pride. For many years it was a vibrant part of station life, particular after [[Corporate Sector Authority]] [[Prex (CSA)|Prex]] [[Zeak Oppenhiemer]], a Griffon himself, took over control of the station.

Things changed drastically after election of [[Crestian Tarasar]] as [[Presav]] of the [[Caspian Union]]. After the Union [[Caspian Repatriation Act (29 ABY)|nationalised]] the station, most of the Griffons followed Oppenhiemer in leaving. There is still a small Griffon presence which remains and has slowly regrown some in time as some of the Griffons who left did not find their next homes any happier. Over time though ownership of the various residences and shops have largely changed hands and there are far fewer displays of Griffon paraphernalia to be found.

=== New Hammerton ===

One of the two modules added to the station after the [[Report:Mergani Pogrom Press Releases|pogroms]] on [[Mergansar]] forced millions to flee, New Hammerton is named after the a city on the Dusona continent. The module is mostly residential, serving as home to some of the five million refugees who ultimately stayed on the station. A number of small shops catering to the refugee community helps give the place added character.

A few larger concerns have also chosen Hammerton as a place to set up shop. [[Biscuit Baron]], the galaxy's largest fast-casual dining establishment has a large restaurant located here along with some corporate office space. The Caspian charity known as [[The Mission]] also maintains a sizeable campus in New Hammerton, having been heavily involved in helping refugees who settled here.

=== New Spanner ===

One of the two modules added to the station after the pogroms on Mergansar forced millions to flee, New Spanner is named after the a city on the Dusona continent. The module is mostly residential, serving as home to some of the five million refugees who ultimately stayed on the station. A number of small shops catering to the refugee community helps give the place added character.

A few larger concerns have also chosen New Spanner as a place to set up shop. The [[Caspian Intelligence Service|Caspian Intelligence Service's]] Bureau of Counterintelligence and Bureau of Surveillance are based in a large complex in New Spanner. A large holofilm studio run by Dragonflower Entertainment also calls New Spanner its home. The [[Silverwood House of Fashion]] also adds some class to the community here.

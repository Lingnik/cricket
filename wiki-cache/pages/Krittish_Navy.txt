Properly speaking the term '''Krittish Fleet''' or '''Krittish Navy''' refers to either independent naval forces for the [[Krittain|Krittain System]] prior to the founding of the [[Caspian Democratic Union]] or the independent Krittish naval forces in existence between the fall of the CDU in [[28 ABY]] and Krittain joining the new [[Caspian Union]] in [[30 ABY]]. In an informal sense it may refer vessels of Krittish origin or sponsorship operating within the [[Caspian Navy]]

==History==

The Krittish people have had a long had a proud martial culture and an independent streak. By the advent of the [[Galactic Civil War]] the Krits had acquired a small fleet of capital ships. Most of these were small, acquired second-hand in poor condition, and jury-rigged into useful condition again by [[Aston Aerospace]]. 

The Krits did possess one proper warship, ''Krittainia'', a fully functioning [[Dreadnaught-class Heavy Cruiser|Dreadnaught-Class Heavy Cruiser]] which had been acquired in condition, which served as the flagship of the Krittish Fleet. Though never formal members of the Rebel Alliance, ''Krittainia'' nevertheless managed to assemble an impressive war record prior to [[Krittain]] joining the [[Caspian Democratic Union]]. 

While the Krits would provide backing to the [[Caspian Navy|Caspian Navy's]] [[Daring-class Battlecruiser|Daring Project]], Aston Aerospace would moved beyond the kitbashing work themselves partnering with Hixam Sidney Design on the [[Arsenal-class Heavy Cruiser|Arsenal-class Heavy Cruiser]]. Though these vessels would be part of the Union Navy the would bear the distinct prefix of CMKS, Caspian Military Krittish Ship, and two of the three vessels produced would be operated by exclusively Krittish crews. With the advent of these vessels ''Krittainia'' would be relegated to reserve status.

By the production of the second batch of the [[Shearwater-class Frigate|Shearwater-Class Light Cruiser]], Aston Aerospace would be subcontracted to produce seven of them, all named for Krittish cities. Two of these ''CMKS Lansdowne'' and ''CMKS Aonach'' were launched with exclusively Krittish crews. A number of the vessels of the later [[Mergansar-class Heavy Cruiser|Mergansar-Class Heavy Cruiser]] would be Krit sponsored. Following the commissioning of ''[[KMS Krittain Major|CMKS Krittain Major]]'' the older ''Krittainia'' would be finally decommissioned becoming a museum piece.

''CMKS Krittain Major's'' status as the "flagship" of Krittain would be short lived following the advent of the [[Admiral Roj-class Battleship|Admiral Roj-Class Battleship]]. The fourth vessel of the class ''CMKS King Liam I'' would be named for Krittish folk hero Liam Braemar who became the constitutional monarch of the system in [[:starwars:107 BBY|107 BBY]].

Though Aston Aerospace would again be utilised as a subcontractor for three ships in the third Shearwater batch and one in the fourth batch their main focus would shift to innovating their own designs. This would come into fruition in another collaboration with Hixam Sidney Design, the [[Valorous-class Light Carrier|Valorous-Class Light Carrier]] based on the early Arsenal-class Heavy Cruiser.

==Krit Union==

As the CDU began to splinter the situation for the Krits was more straight-forward than for other CDU worlds. [[Krittain Major]] was already the most populous world of the CDU with a diversified industrial base. The fact that over two dozen warships in the Caspian Navy were already associated with Krittain System made it easy to field a fleet which would pledge themselves to the new Krit Union of Krittain Major and [[Krittain Minor|Minor]]. The CMKS prefix would be replaced by these vessels by the prefix KMS for Krittish Military Ship.

Following the death of [[Sar Admiral]] [[Prae Kichakressa]], [[Admiral]] Davik Nord was the most senior Krittish naval officer. Promoted to the rank of [[Fleet Admiral]] by the new Krittish government and given command of this new Krittish Navy, Nord was faced with a difficult task. While well represented in terms of cruisers, the ''King Liam I'' was the only larger Krittish warship. 

Lacking any [[Hurricane-class Fleet Carrier|Hurricane Class Fleet Carriers]] would also present a problem to the new Krittish Navy as half of the Caspian starfighters were attached to that class, with the Krittish Valorous-Class Light Carriers only containing fraction by comparison. The Krits quickly decided to recommission three Valorous-Class which had been retired, to try to plug the gap.

Few support vessels had been operated by Krit dominated crews before the collapse of the CDU creating further problems for the Krittish Navy. While their habit of fielding such crews had helped in securing those ships, few ships with more mixed crews were willing to consider Krittish allegiance. There was likewise little appetite by the Krits to recruit non-native Krits for their service compared to other former CDU worlds.

The Krittish Navy had no trouble in forming an adequate defence fleet for the Krittain System itself patrolling surrounding space would prove more difficult. Krittain was far enough distant from other former CDU worlds that it was left with comparatively more space to patrol on its own. Later once a new government emerged on [[Caspar]] and began reuniting as the new [[Caspian Union]], the Krits would find themselves more isolated. While the Caspian Union would benefit from military and trade agreements with the [[Imperium]], no such attention was paid to the Krit Union largely limiting them to funding the military they had rather than being able to expand it any.

The limitations in the Krittish fleet would become apparent in early [[30 ABY]] when the [[Ushton-class Heavy Freighter|Ushton-Class Heavy Freighter]] ''Blechley Brook'' which has been employed as transport for a school trip out of Krittain Minor, was [[Report:A Minor Consideration|attacked]] by slavers. By the time the ''[[KMS Lirithose]]'' came on the scene they found this ship wrecked, four dead, and 308 passengers and crew missing. Though elements of the '''[[Caspian 4th Fleet]]''' would soon join in the unsuccessful search for the slavers and subsequent patrolling of Krittish space, this would prove the catalyst which finally brought the two Unions to the negotiating table in earnest.

==Current Status==

Within a month of the attack on the ''Blechley Brook'' the Krittain System joined the new Caspian Union spelling an end to an independent Krittish Fleet. The Krittish Fleet was merged with the '''[[Caspian 2nd Fleet]]''' with the Krittish ships maintain the KMS prefix. The combined fleet would be charged with the defence of the 'northern' portion of Caspian space and headquartered at Krittain Major.

As the 2nd Fleet was larger and possessed more large warships than the Krittish Fleet, the Krits reluctantly agreed to Fleet Admiral Katha Pushtelli of the 2nd Fleet remaining in overall command and assuming command of Defence Group Krittain. In exchange Davik Nord would retain the rank of Fleet Admiral in the Caspian Navy and assume the command of Defence Group Ketterslea with the ''KMS King Liam I'' as his flagship.

Though the majority of the Krittish Fleet would remain as part of the Caspian 2nd Fleet several vessels would be reassigned to other Caspian Fleets. The '''[[Caspian 1st Fleet|1st]]''' and '''[[Caspian 3rd Fleet|3rd]]''' Fleets which each be assigned one of the Arsenal-Class Heavy Cruisers and two of the Valorous-Class Light Carriers for their Patrol Groups. Meanwhile the Valorous-Class ''KMS Intrepid'' and Mergansar-Class Heavy Cruisers ''[[KMS Lirithose]]'' and ''[[KMS Ascension]]'' would join the [[Caspian 4th Fleet|4th Fleet]]''.

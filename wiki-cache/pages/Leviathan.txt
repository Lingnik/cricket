The '''''Leviathan''''' is the personal flagship of [[Caspian Union]] [[Presav]] and [[Sar Admiral]] [[Crestian Tarasar]]. The ship is his personal possession as the Sith [[Duke]] of Dragonflower. Since his election as Presav the ship has been seconded from the [[House Dragonflower]] to the [[Caspian Navy]] and its '''[[Caspian 4th Fleet|4th Fleet]]'''.

Though the ship escaped the spotlight during its time in the [[New Sith Order]], it would grow into the stuff of myth in the decade it spent hidden in the [[Dragonflower Nebula]]. Later the ''Leviathan's'' [[Mergansar Incident|engagement]] against the Caspian Navy while trying to stop the [[Mergansar|Mergani]] [[Report:Mergani Pogrom Press Releases|pogroms]] elevated it to legendary status. Now as a de facto flagship of navy it once fought against, ''Leviathan'' is at the forefront Caspian Union's presence in the galaxy.

== History ==

The early history of the vessel that became known as ''Leviathan'' have been lost to time. As a [[Interdictor-class Immobilizer 418|Interdictor-Class Immobilizer 418]] it was built by [[Sienar Fleet Systems]] and likely had been in service to the [[Galactic Empire]]. When it came into the possession of [[Crestian Tarasar]] it was in the service of the Sith and had been heavily upgraded.

=== Task Force 77 ===

[[File:Tactical Briefing.jpg|thumb|Tactical Briefing]] Following his recruitment into the [[New Sith Order]] by [[Tyler Damion|Darth Malign]] and [[Grand Admiral]] [[Drayson Honos]], Crestian Tarasar would quickly rise in rank, owing to his organisational skills and charisma. Promoted to [[Admiral]] he was given command of '''Task Force 77'''. Conceived as a compliment to the work of the [[Knights of Korriban]], Task Force 77 took on missions not quite important enough to be assigned to a Knight of Korriban or which required a more organised force to execute.

The task force was based around an Interdictor, he named Leviathan after the [[:starwars:Leviathan (Interdictor-class cruiser)|flagship]] of [[:starwars:Darth Malak|Darth Malak]], and four prototype Barb Interceptors. In addition to the naval and marine personnel assigned to the ''Leviathan'' several members of the [[Sith Intelligence Network]] were assigned to Crestian's command. Their mission would vary widely and included everything from artefact collection, clandestine infiltration, false flag operations, and political sabotage among other things.

Crestian would also be granted title of [[Duke]] in the Sith nobility attached to his existing assets around the [[Dragonflower Nebula]] as a mark of Darth Malign's favour. In many respects the Duke of Dragonflower was the least traditionally Sith member of the nobility, but in an organisation filled with power-hungry homicidal maniacs, Crestian managed to carve a spot as a comparatively easy going individual.

While not a force sensitive himself, Crestian was well versed in many of the key lessons of being Sith, the most important being to have an exit strategy. Seeing the writing on the wall when Malign disappeared, Admiral Tarasar took the ''Leviathan'' and parked it in the Dragonflower Nebula to serve as his secret base of operations until Malign returned. With the Empire's purge of the Sith, Crestian saw no other commander who had any hope of standing against them and he had no desire to go down in some suicidal blaze of glory. The fanatics would die and be forgotten, but Crestian, his command, and the [[House Dragonflower]] would survive for future glory.

=== Hidden ===

Following the collapse of the New Sith Order, Crestian would keep the ''Leviathan'' hidden in the Dragonflower Nebula. His public operation would be his control of the nearby [[Silver Station]] and its [[:starwars:Prothium|prothium]] gas extraction. The income from the blaster gas funded the expense of keeping the personnel of his command, some dating back to his time in [[New Republic|Republic]] service, who chose to stay with him in exile. Though some chose to retire and move on, many remained and settled into a more domestic life, some like Crestian himself, even starting families.

Officers and crew would frequently be rotated between the ship and the station so no one was overly isolated. Trips beyond these locations, however, would be limited, occasional mercenary work to pull in extra money and the like. Such contracts, as well as the contracts for the prothium sales, would be carefully chosen to keep a low profile. Crestian cultivated an image as a dandy and 'failed' Sith flag officer running a shadowport.

Rumours would naturally circulate that Sith assets of one form or another were hidden in the nebula. But when Crestian's activities were even noticed, what most of the galaxy saw was an eccentric dandy, hardly the prize most Imperial officers wanted to claim as the last Sith they eliminated, and no one wanted to embarrass themselves bring a fleet to bear against him to potentially find none of what was rumoured. As the years came and went interest in Crestian's Sith past waned. The Empire had, after all, claimed the New Sith Order had been eliminated, pursuit of its former members would conflict with that narrative.

During its decade in hiding the ''Leviathan'' would receive further upgrades to her communications systems and sensors, both reflective of Crestian's skill set and the needs of a ship trying to avoid notice. Power would also be conserved in various ways including dimming the lights in many areas throughout the ship, this low lighting is preserved to this day for its dark ambiance. With few new recruits to the House Dragonflower, when officers and crew died or chose to retire, they would often be replaced by [[Droid|Droids]].

These droids would eventually grow in number to the point of making up a tenth of the crew. The policy of Crestian and House Dragonflower was to treat biological and droid crew as a equals, both entitled to the same pay and rank opportunities. There were no regular memory wipes. The only time a memory wipe would occur would be as a last resort of a droid became too much of a danger to themselves or others, and then only administered by other droids. Usually some alternative to damaged programming would be found.

Promotions for both biological and droid officers and crew alike would continue over the decade. By the time the ''Leviathan'' emerged from hiding, it had a glut of [[Commander|Commanders]] as there was no higher positions for them to be assigned to. It produced an odd dynamic in which senior officers outnumbered junior officers, junior officers outnumbered non-commissioned officers, and non-commissioned officers outnumbered crew.

Throughout it all Crestian work hard to build a sense of community among his crew. His large and opulent personal dining room would be a frequent site of dinners with officers and crew, which he would often cook himself. Though he never abandoned the Sith value of believing his own will and desires to be more important than that of others, he considered his crew his greatest asset and went out of the way to treat them as such. This had the effect of engendering a great deal of loyalty in them for him, which would see most of them continue to follow him over the years and changes in mission.

=== Reemergence ===

Life in exile ended when the [[Jedi Order|Jedi]] [[Jedi Knight|Knight]] [[Johanna Siri te Danaan]] rescued Darth Malign, her half-brother, and Drayson Honos from a moon of [[Korriban]], where they had been trapped for a decade. As Crestian had 'kept the faith' it was to the ''Leviathan'' that she brought Malign. Crestian was quick to pledge himself and his house to their joint cause: the overthrow of [[Galactic Emperor|Emperor]] [[Aleister Vadim|Vadim]] and the end of slavery in the galaxy. Crestian would now busy himself with the notion of building what he conceived of as the Resurrected Sith Order.

Crestian was soon distracted from this mission by the news of a [[Report:Sariphage Press Releases|pandemic]] which struck his homeworld of [[Caspar]]. Determined to do whatever he could to help, he took the ''Levathian'' to the [[Caspia|Caspian System]] and offered his services to [[Sar Admiral]] [[Prae Kichakressa]]. She would assign ''Leviathan'' to a position in maintaining the planetary quarantine, but suggested that Crestian would be most helpful in trying to stop [[Olumi Osahn]].

The dark cult had used the pandemic as an excuse to engage in [[Report:Mergani Pogrom Press Releases|pogroms]] on the [[Sarian]] homeworld of [[Mergansar]], blaming its non-Sarian population for Caspar's pandemic, and attempting to illegally drive them all from Mergansar. When Crestian and Malign's efforts to strong-arm them into submission failed, Crestian would shift to recruiting Mergani refugees into a paramilitary/vigilante organisation he dubbed [[Casulmis]].

The ''Leviathan'' would play an integral role in this operation launching fighters to bomb key Olumi Osahn facilities on Mergansar as well Crestian's personal [[Lambda-class Shuttle|Lambda-Class Shuttle]], ''One Iota'' with a ground team, led by Crestian and Malign to take on cultists at the Hebron starport, the nexus of the pogroms.

=== Mergansar Incident ===

Following a cure to the Sariphage pandemic being developed and administered on Caspar, the [[Caspian Navy|Caspian Navy's]] quarantine of the planet ended. Sar Admiral Kichakressa took the opportunity to take her flagship, ''[[CMS Typhoon]]'', along with ''CMS Rapace'', ''[[CMS Prophet]]'', and ''CMS Ad Astra'' to Mergansar to confront the ''Leviathan''.

This move caught Crestian off guard, as he had considered his efforts as having the tacit support of Sar Admiral Kichakressa. His attempts to defuse the situation diplomatically were quickly complicated by Darth Malign attempting to get the Caspians to surrender. It would be the Caspians who [[Mergansar Incident|fired]] first.

Crestian's reliance on attempting diplomacy was nearly his undoing as the initial attack caused considerable damage. Complete disaster was averted, however, by the presence of Darth Malign. Strike teams led by Malign's mutant servant [[Sentinel]] bombed key locations on the ''Typhoon'', including the bridge, killing Sar Admiral Kichakressa. Meanwhile the ''Rapace'' and ''Prophet'' mysteriously crashed into one another, crippling both ships. The ''Ad Astra'' suffered heavy damage to her [[HOWL]] launchers but little damage elsewhere. With the Caspian force crippled ''Leviathan'' was able to flee back to Silver Station.

=== Transition ===

The ''Leviathan'' would spend months undergoing repairs. Though neither Crestian nor the ''Leviathan'' itself had much to do with their ability to fight a far superior force to a draw, with Malign's involvement unknown to the public, Crestian and the ''Leviathan'' were able to take the credit. Sabotage of the Caspian Navy's investigation by allies at [[SubPro]], ensured they never figured out how it truly happened, allowing Leviathan's legend to grow. Casualties themselves had been light, and with some of the members of Casulmis choosing to flee with the ''Leviathan'', the vacancies in the crew were easily filled by these new allies.

With the [[Caspian Democratic Union]] having collapsed under the weight of the pandemic and pogroms, Crestian now felt that Caspar needed him more than Malign and the Sith did. Once the dust had sufficiently settled on Caspar, Crestian returned his home city of [[Trinumvira]] where he started a charity called [[The Mission]] to support the survivors of the pandemic and Mergani refugees who relocated to Caspar.

Many members of the ''Leviathan'' officers and crew would come to actively support this charity work and the Caspian System soon became ''Leviathan's'' new base of operations. When Crestian later formed a political party, [[Praecet]], and ran for Trinumvira's seat in the new [[Union Council]], it was clear that the House Dragonflower and her assets would soon have a formal role to play in support of the new [[Caspian Union]].

=== [[Caspian 4th Fleet]] ===

Following Crestian's election as [[Presav]] and appointment as Sar Admiral, an agreement was reached to second House Dragonflower assets to the [[Caspian Defence Force]]. The ''Leviathan'' and its support craft would be assigned to the '''[[Caspian 4th Fleet]]''', House Dragonflower ranks would be honoured within the Caspian Defence Force, which would also assume the responsibility of paying salaries. The agreement allowed the Defence Force to utilise Silver Station as a base, with its defences bolstered by additional starfighters and marines. The [[CDMC|Caspian Design & Manufacturing Corporand]] would be able to purchase prothium at cost.

This arrangement had the fact effect of putting droids into senior officer ranks in the Caspian Defence Force for the first time. It would on occasion place officers in the CDF who would normally be too young to enlist. Over the years that followed some of the ''Leviathan's'' officers would move on to opportunities on other ships or units, slowly lowering the glut of senior officers. Meanwhile the ''Leviathan'' would come to play host to officers from the Caspian Navy would consider assignment on the ship a good way to advance their careers.

Crestian himself would continue to use ''Leviathan'' as his personal flagship. The reason was twofold. First and foremost having lived and worked on ''Leviathan'' for over a decade, having customised the amenities to his own needs, the thought of transferring his flag to a mainline navy vessel was less attractive, even if it would afford him a larger personal command. Second, Crestian had lingering regrets about having had to fight the Caspian Navy in past, he felt embarrassed at notion of commanding one of their ships. Whether they liked it or not Caspian Navy personnel had Crestian as the Presav and Sar Admiral, he figured at the very least none of them should be forced to have them as their ship's Commanding Officer.

As Crestian's flagship ''Leviathan'' serves as a de facto flagship for the Caspian Navy, with it frequently serving host to informal leadership gatherings. For more formal functions Crestian will often utilise another ship such as ''[[CMS Renaissance of Innovation]]''. A number of other vessels such as ''[[CMS Hood]]'', ''CMS Repulse'', ''CMS Requiem'', ''[[CMS Resplendent]]'', ''CMS Typhoon,'' ''CMS Warspite'', and ''KMS King Liam I'' sometimes fill this function.

==== Blockade of Mergansar ====

The Caspian 4th Fleet's initial assignment was the [[Report:Mergani Subjugation Posts|blockade]] of Mergansar. ''Leviathan'' would participate in this effort under the day-to-day command of its Executive Officer [[Captain]] [[Drorq]], though it would be frequently pulled from formation to serve Crestian's travel needs as he had frequent diplomatic missions to attend to.

==== Slaver Attacks ====

After receiving another interdictor, the ''[[Dying Sun]]'', from Imperator [[Tyler Damion|Damion I]], Drorq transferred to serve as its Commanding Officer. While the ''Dying Sun'' was still being prepared for service in the Caspian Navy, and its existence still classified, Drorq accompanied the Caspian delegation to the 1st Annual Miners Dinner in [[Cloud City]] on [[Bespin]]. Drorq was serving as Acting Executive Officer of the ''Leviathan'' when the city was [[Report:Bespin Recap|attacked]] by slavers. He was ordered by Crestian to place the planet under interdiction in hopes of catching the culprits. Following the departure of [[Imperium]] [[Fleet Admiral]] Domicor Honos with the grievously wounded Johanna Siri te Danaan to [[Selene]], followed later by Crestian's, Captain Drorq was the senior officer in the system until the arrival of the ''CMS Renaissance of Innovation'' under the command [[Vice Admiral]] [[Kath Samba]] with a team of [[SubPro]] engineers lead Caspian Minister of Science and Technology, [[Sirlahn Inglet]].

==== Operation Requiem ====

In [[33 ABY]], elements of the 4th Fleet participated in the [[Caspian Intelligence Service]] mission code named "Operation Requiem." The ships involve operated in the [[Faylar|Faylar System]] as a fictional Imperium "'''Task Force Faylar'''." Each ship involved had its transponder changed to register as an Imperium vessel, with the ships themselves being the fleets four Imperial made warships. The ''Leviathan'' operated as the the ''"Spire of Victory,"'' the ''Dying Sun'' as the ''"Oubliette,"'' the ''[[Tethys]]'' as the ''"Reborn Order,"'' and the ''"[[Starkiller]],"'' as the ''"Black Talon."'' [[Fleet Captain]] Drorq served as Commanding Officer for the task force, while Captain [[Atsvara Tarasar]] served in place of her father as Acting Commanding Officer of the ''"Spire of Victory."''

== Support Craft ==

When initially given to Crestian to command had a limited number of vessels embarked, four prototype Barb Interceptors, and his personal Lambda-Class Shuttle, ''One Iota''. When Darth Malign took over the Empire and transformed it into the Imperium, the Sith once again access to major shipyards, with Barb Interceptors being built again. Crestian acquired enough of these for ''Leviathan'' and ''Dying Sun'' to each field two full squadrons. ''Leviathan's'' are '''Kojafonas Squadron''' and '''Zudifas Squadron''', meaning 'hunter' and 'killer' respectively in the Sith language. Along with ''Dying Sun's'' Guduma Squadron and Zibeti Squadron, meaning ''dead'' and ''star'' respectively, these are the only Barb Interceptors serving in the [[Caspian Starfighter Corps]]. As Sar Admiral, Crestian felt placing Sith designed craft on mainline Caspian warships would be bad for morale. ''Leviathan'' also now carries a full compliment of four Lambda-Class Shuttles.

== Notable Officers ==

=== Crestian Tarasar ===

[[File:Crestian Sith Uniform.jpg|thumb|Sar Admiral Crestian Tarasar]] As the Commanding Officer of the ''Leviathan'' for nearly two decades, the ship has become synonmous with Sar Admiral '''[[Crestian Tarasar]]'''. This is reflect in the vibe of the ship, part Sith, part Independent, part Caspian. Its culture reflects the many varied roles that he himself has in the galaxy, from statesman and military leader to academic, agent, engineer, entertainer, medic, and scientist.<br><br><br>

=== Drorq ===

[[File:Drorq.jpg|thumb|Commodore Drorq]] Commodore '''[[Drorq]]''' joined Crestian's crew on the ''Argent Drake'' in the period after he left Republic service but before he joined the Sith. Following Crestian into Sith service, Drorq would be one of his initial officers on the ''Leviathan''. He worked his way up the ranks over the years, becoming on of the many Commanders which were legion on the ship prior to its entry into Caspian service. After being elected Presav, Drorq was promoted to Captain and made ''Leviathan's'' Executive Officer. 

Once Crestian acquired the ''Dying Sun'', Drorq was transferred to serve as its Commanding Officer. He was back on ''Leviathan'', however, during the attack on the 1st Annual Miners Dinner in Cloud City on Bespin. For a brief period in time Captain Drorq was the senior allied officer in the system. For his actions he as promoted to Fleet Captain. He later distinguished himself for his role in the Imperium's operation in the Nemares Corridor hunting down escaped prisoners from [[The Oubliette|Spacestation 1138]] and subsquently promoted to Commodore. He is arguably the former ''Leviathan'' officer who has most distinguished himself in later service.

=== Atsvara Tarasar ===

[[File:Atsvara Sith Uniform.jpg|thumb|Captain Atsvara Tarasar]] As Crestian's daughter '''[[Atsvara Tarasar]]''' largely grew up on the ''Leviathan''. And as a vessel in service to House Dragonflower there was always a certain understanding that it served her. At the age of ten, Crestian gave his daughter the title of [[Baron|Baroness]] and effectively recognised her as an adult. She received no military rank at the time, though that did not stop her growing fondness for donning a uniform anyway.

The following year when the ''Leviathan'' participated in Operation Requiem in the Faylar System, Crestian was unable to be present. As Atsvara, however, was due to be present, Crestian gave her a commission as a Captain in House Dragonflower and made her Acting Commanding Officer of the ''Leviathan'', operating undercover as the ''Spire of Victory''. At the age of eleven, this had the effect of also making her a senior officer in the Caspian Navy, at an age several years younger than she would normally be able to join up at all.

The following year she accompanied Crestian and the ''Leviathan'' to [[Tatooine]] to support Caspian relief efforts for the [[Report:Caspian Reponse to Arkanis Famine|famine]] in the [[:starwars:Arkanis sector/Legends|Arkanis Sector]] , serving as Acting Executive Officer. Atsvara's official Caspian Navy file lists her as Commanding Officer of the ''Argent Drake'', Crestian's [[CR90 Corvette]], which he passed on to his daughter for use in role as Chief Executive Officer of [[Biscuit Baron]].

=== V1C3 ===

[[File:V1C3.jpg|thumb|Brigadier V1C3]] Built by Crestian around the time his daughter was born, [[Brigadier]] '''[[V1C3]]''' has become one of the galaxy's most prominent Droids. Initially commissioned as [[Ensign]] and serving as an adjutant and bodyguard for Crestian, Vee-Won worked his way up the ranks to be one of ''Leviathan's'' many commanders by the time it entered Caspian service. His time with Crestian also had the effect of giving Vee-Won a taste for high fashion for which he has become known.

Vee-Won was one of five strike team members on a high profile joint Caspian-Imperium rescue operation, on the resort world of [[:starwars:Spira/Legends|Spira]], to save two force-sensitive youth who had been kidnapped as part of a [[Commission for the Preservation of the New Order|COMPNOR]] programme. Following the mission Crestian transferred Vee-Won into the [[Union Marines]] and promoted him to [[Colonel]].

Following the attack on Cloud City during the 1st Annual Miners Dinner on Bespin and up-tick in other terrorist related activity in the galaxy, Crestian's cousin [[General]] [[Siege|Janus Tarasar]] was appointed as the Commanding Officer of [[The Chevalier]]. The Chevalier were an [[Ixin]] based military unit that was often contracted by various Princes for protection. Now the Chevalier were being tasked to serve as the protection detail for the Tarasar family and along with some other key members of the [[Caspian Crest|Crest]]. To further this aim, Crestian arranged Vee-Won's appointment as the Chevalier's Executive Officer, while continuing his functions as personal aide.

In his capacity a presavadential guard, Vee-Won was involved in assisting Imperator [[Tyler Damion|Damion]], Presav Tarasar, and others in suppressing an attempted [[Report:Corellian Prison Break|prisoner escape]] at [[Coronet City]] Spaceport on [[Corellia]] in [[32 ABY]].

Vee-Won was recruited by Atsvara as an investor in her [[Golden Bantha Group]] in its buyout of Biscuit Baron, becoming the restaurant chain's Vice President of Security concurrent with his duties in The Chevalier. 

On the day the takeover went public, Vee-Won would personally lead a team of [[E5 Baron Droid|E5 Baron Droids]] in an operation on [[:starwars:Ord Cantrell/Legends|Ord Cantrell]] to liberate Biscuit Baron workers who were being trafficked the [[:starwars:Nova Crime Syndicate|Nova Syndicate]]  who were using their Biscuit Baron franchises as a front. For his actions combating slavery, a high profile priority of the Caspian Union, Vee-Won was promoted to the rank of Brigadier.

=== T3-A7 ===

Commander '''T3-A7''' is an ancient utility droid Crestian recovered from an archaeological site, repaired, and reactivated. She serves as Chief Droid Control Officer, placing her in charge of the facilities on ''Leviathan'' that service the unique needs of its droid officers and crew. During Operation Requiem she also served as the Acting Executive Officer under Captain Atsvara Tarasar. She is a minor investor in the Golden Bantha Group and is the girlfriend of Biscuit Baron's Executive Vice President [[Cricket]].

=== Wilhelm Scream ===

[[Wing Commander]] '''Wilhelm Scream''' is a [[:starwars:Myneyrsh|Myneyrsh]] from [[:starwars:Wayland/Legends|Wayland]] who had be the subject of alchemical experimentation by some now forgotten Sith Lord. He was abandoned as either not achieving the desired results of the experiment or because of the Sith Lord's demise. He was later rescued by Crestian who found his skill set suited to starfighter piloting. He is the senior starfighter corps officer on the ''Leviathan''.

== Amenities ==

=== Sar Admiral's Dining Room ===

As the unlikely flagship of the Caspian Navy, the ''Leviathan'' boasts certain amenities that would not normally be found on an interdictor. The Sar Admiral's private dining room is one such amenity. Were it not for the windows opening up to the vastness of space, one could forget they were on a ship.

The top of the walls are covered plaster painted scarlet, the bottom consists of intricately carved [[:starwars:Japor ivory wood|japor ivory wood]] panelling with inlaid [[:starwars:Obsidian/Legends|obsidian]]. The floor is covered with a hand-woven crimson coloured carpet with a leaf design hand-embroidered in gold thread by the [[Silverwood House of Fashion]].

Designed to host both dignitaries and functions for officers it boasts a massive hand-carved dining table, made from [[:starwars:Wroshyr wood/Legends|wroshyr wood]] harvested under dubious circumstances. The table is complimented with forty matching hand-carved worshyr wood chairs upholstered with scarlet coloured cushions. The table is accented by several matching blue coloured reproduction [[:starwars:Junarian vase|Junarian vases]] as centre-pieces, they are filled with local flora when circumstances allow it.

The art and artefacts displayed add greatly to the room's opulence while also reflecting the eclectic tastes of the Sar Admiral. The main entrance to the dining room is flanked by two ancient sandstone sculptures depicting [[:starwars:Massassi/Legends|Massassi]], who look more like bureaucrats than warriors. Meanwhile the side entrance to the Sar Admiral's quarters is flanked by two [[:starwars:Kuati marble|Kuati marble]] sculptures depicting female humanoid fashion models wearing designs from Zubindi Hakoon's collection. The side walls of the room are decorated with the coats of arms of the Sar Admiral's house, House Dragonflower, along with Caspar's Vector Apex.

The walls by the entrance to the kitchen are slightly more practical boast antique [[:starwars:Homogoni|homogoni]] grandfather clocks on each side with antique homogoni drink cabinets on the opposite sides of the clocks. Meanwhile the against the walls by the washroom door are two fishtanks containing fish from the Sar Admiral's former home [[Chandrila]]. The room is lit with [[:starwars:Ice-jewel|ice jewel crystal]] chandeliers.

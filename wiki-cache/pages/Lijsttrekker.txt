'''Lijsttrekker''' is a political party from [[Ixin]] that holds six of thirty-nine seats in the [[Union Council]]. They are the only [[Caspian Union|Caspian]] political party that did not originate on [[Caspar]] itself. In addition to Ixin, they hold seats on [[Charros IV]], [[Kasputin Yar]], and [[Mergansar]]. They are part of the all party coalition along with [[Holicet]], [[Legacy]], and [[Praecet]].

== Background ==

Though [[Ixin]] falls within the [[Caspian Sector]] it did not join the [[Caspian Democratic Union]] until [[21 ABY]]. The planet had long been a feudal state where tribal ties and individual loyalties were far more important than any political or philosophical outlook. It did not possess either the industry or values that would back the Union seek it out as a member nor did the Ix have much use for what the Union had to offer. By the time they did join it was out of economic desperation more than anything else. With Union on the decline by this point, the addition of Ixin went largely unnoticed. Ultimately the Ix never really saw the benefits they were looking for.

When the Union fell apart following the Sariphage [[Report:Sariphage Press Releases|pandemic]] and Mergani [[Report:Mergani Pogrom Press Releases|progoms]] the Ix were happy enough to see it disband. Though they remained nominally a democratic state, in practice feudalism return. Power was back where it always ways, with the [[Prince|Princes]]. Nevertheless, this brief time in a larger political unit, saw a number of Princes look to the future and what sort of Union they might like to see again.

As negotiations to bring [[Kasputin Yar]] and [[Rauther]] back into the new [[Caspian Union]], the more progressive Princes were considering what character the new Union should have. Ixin had spent relatively few years in the old Union. Though it did have a sizeable [[Sarian]] minority and was in the [[Caspian Sector]], it had a limited cultural connexion to [[Caspar]]. In many respects the centuries of trading with the [[Hutt|Hutts]] was still a large cultural tie.

After Kasputin Yar and Rauther rejoined, Ixin followed suit soon after. The Union as it was shaping up was something they wanted to be party of again. But, for figures like Prince Abdulay O'Jordaan II and Prince [[Zig Al Fidel]], they were not enthused at seeing the Union as it had been before, defined by Caspar, less jostled between the [[Imperium|Imperial]] and [[New Republic|Republic]] struggles. They wanted to see a Union which would truly became a real power in its own right, a Union would one day offer a true alternative to galaxy and not just regionally.

== A New Party ==

With this philosophy in mind '''Lijsttrekker''' was born. Roughly translating from an old local dialect as 'List Forward', both Princes would carry this label with them into the [[Union Council]]. The new party quickly gained the interest of politicians on other Union worlds. Perhaps most surprising given traditional antipathy towards the [[Yari|Yaries]] by Ix Princes, the Yari Councillor Tanasha Yarrow of Nenawirth on Kasputin Yar joined the new party. The ruling coalition of [[Praecet]] and [[Legacy]] would invite Lijsttrekker to join them, feeling their differences in political philosophy no more different than the differences they had with each other. Furthermore, the character of an all party coalition was something they wanted to preserve. Lijsttrekker agreed to join, and Prince Zig Al Fidel became the Caspian Minister of Health.

Lijsttrekker's first opportunity to contest election would come on [[Mergansar]] where former [[Senator of the Union|Senator]] [[Philinda Jorgan]] of Charon chose to run under their label much to the surprise of the pundits. Neither Legacy nor Praecet chose to field candidates against her allowing her to win in a landslide with 83% of the vote. Jorgan would later replace [[Odie Hood]] as [[Governor|Military Governor]] of Mergansar and later become the Caspian Minister of Intelligence. With the party now growing far beyond its Ixin roots it began to adopt a more formal structure with Prince Abdulay O'Jordaan II as Party Leader and Philinda Jorgan as Deputy Party Leader.

The admission of [[Charros IV]] into the Union was strongly favoured by Lijsttrekker as a manifestation of its philosophy of the Union as a third great power. [[Xi Char]] Prelate r'ratak-varattak-n'gavanak would himself adopt the Lijsttrekker label for his own party affiliation. The party would pick up one more seat in the Mergani elections with Nanchia Marook taking 44% of the vote in Toresta ahead of [[Ulmesar|Ulmesar's]] Corgan Vir at 31%, [[Holicet|Holicet's]] Brullian Aresko at 24%. The Toresta vote, however, was marred by the assassination of the Legacy candidate Volzang Eiresar by the Praecet candidate Lisal Yutan, and her attempted assassination of Corgan Vir. Legacy, which had already suffered a scandal after Eiresar's ties to the pogroms had been revealed prior to his death, chose not field a replacement candidate. Praecet, likewise did not replace Yutan after her arrest.

== Platform ==

Having chosen a name that translates to List Forward, Lijsttrekker has set itself forth as a progressive party. This is somewhat ironic given Ixin is, by nature, a conservative world, but both Prince Abdulay and Prince Zig are among its most progressive voices, though the loyalty they claim from their constituents has more to do with their roles has hereditary custodians of some of the planet's most important temples.

Their progressive stance is most notable in terms of foreign policy. For Lijsttrekker the end game is to see the Union as a great power alongside the Imperium and Republic, and ideally eventually dropping Caspian from the name. Some of their strongest support from this comes from the more 'alien' demographics within the Union, notably [[Xi Charrian|Xi Charrians]] and Yaries. This position also sees strong support among the [[The Admiralty|Admiralty]] where it is believed a third of the Admirals belong to the party.

In many respects they are not all that different in outlook from Legacy and Praecet, neither really seeks to see Caspian culture dominant as such, but both see that culture as a glue that holds the Union together in a way that Lijsttrekker does not. Lijsttrekker is less enthused by the [[Imperium-Caspian Mutual Protection Pact (29 ABY)|mutual protection pact]] with the Imperium than Praecet is, but less put off by it than the Legacy party. Lijsttrekker considers alliances a normal party of galactic politics but they are keen to now have the Union seen as a junior partner.

On domestic matters Lijsttrekker generally finds common ground with other parties championing causes such as the arts, cultural preservation, education, healthcare, labour rights, and security. One each of these priorities the Lijsttrekker councillors have strong working relationships with their peers in the other parties and strong say in policy and final legislation, in spite of being one of the smaller parties in the Union Council.

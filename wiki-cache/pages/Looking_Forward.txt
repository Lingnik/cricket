On Zhellday [[Caspian Union|Caspian]] [[Presav]] [[Crestian Tarasar]] gave an address at [[Union Hall]] in [[Plaxton]] on [[Caspar]] which among other topics announced a new Youth Council. It is approximately ten minutes.

The [[Presav]] stands at an ornately carved podium in the Council Chambers. The audience appears to be mostly comprised of younglings with a few adult chaperones mixed in.

"Five years ago in this very chamber, the Provisional Assembly of the [[Caspian Union|Caspar Union]] was hard at work trying to hammer out what became our new constitution. For some time that is something that has seemed still like it is still in the present, something that did not happen all that long ago. But five years on we must now acknowledge that time has indeed moved on and that we must start truly looking forward.

There are certain parts of our past that are embedded into to the genetic code of the new Union. The effects of the Sariphage [[Report:Sariphage Press Releases|pandemic]] and the [[Report:Mergani Pogrom Press Releases|pogroms]] on [[Mergansar]] have marked our society in countless ways and inform how we govern ourselves. Our efforts at repatriating our industry both here in the [[Caspia|Caspia System]] and in the [[Charros IV|Charros System]] are also more than just details in an account ledger, they speak to our resolve, and to the new bonds of friendship that have advanced the new Union beyond what the old Union had been.

In fact unity itself is one of our defining characteristics. One reason in fact why our current Union is not the [[Caspian Democratic Union|CDU]] was the choice to emphasise that bit of our character. Not that we had abandoned democracy in any way but rather that it was not a defining characteristic, not what was unique about ourselves. Other places have democracy but what the Empires and Republics have lacked, what makes us different is a true unity of purpose. The bonds that hold us together have proven truly stronger than any efforts to pull us a apart.

Recently, however, I keep finding myself circling back to the idea of our democracy. We are certainly not the only democratic power in the galaxy but when you add in the word functioning, perhaps we are the best example of a function democracy out there. We are certainly not perfect by any stretch. We have made mistakes in these past five years, I know I have made mistakes personally, but at the end of the day we have a government that most of our citizens believe is functioning for them. That is something truly special.

Myself and the others that were elected to this chamber represent a rather diverse bunch, we come from a variety of demographic backgrounds, career paths, and life experiences. We aren't all people you would expect to be working in common cause and yet events we have faced as a people have brought us all to the same table, with differing views on many things yes, but also in a common cause, a higher collective purpose.

When it comes to our next generation, a number of whom sit before me now, it is our hope that it will not require circumstances of great crisis for you to find your leaders and for them to come together in this place and continue the great work. When I look at other societies, some who have served as an example to us over the years, I see that passing the torch was not just left to chance. In the days of the Old Republic there existed an Apprentice Legislature which gathered together younglings from a number of worlds. Some who had quite distinguished careers when they came of age. This is a concept that we could benefit from here.

I am introducing legislation in the coming days to institute a Youth Council in the Caspian Union. The final form this will take will depend upon the input of other members of the Union Council. We will also hold hearings open to those not yet of voting age both here at Union Hall and at other sites throughout the Union. The end result will not a be a chamber with legislative authority. But there is so much more beyond legislating that your elected representatives do. They provide a voice in raising concerns and addressing grievances. This new body will be able to do this and as appropriate bring those issues to the Union Council for action.

Let this be an opportunity for young and old a like to consider what sort of future we want for the Union, consider what characteristics and values we want to look back to and say, this is what the Union is about. It is also my hope that once this new new body exists that it might find the opportunity to interact with similar bodies in other powers. Let us look to bury the hatchet on old conflicts and look instead for new areas to cooperate in across borders. Getting along is not always easy but it is always a worthy goal."

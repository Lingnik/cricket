======================================================================
When the question of whether to develop a new an updated version of the [[:starwars:Variable Geometry Self-Propelled Battle Droid, Mark I/Legends|Variable Geometry Self-Propelled Battle Droid]] or Vulture-Class Droid Starfighter came to the resurgent cathedral factories of the [[Xi Char]] there was much debate. They had been a very popular design which spawned multiple variations by other [[:starwars:Confederacy of Independent Systems/Legends|Confederate]] manufacturers. They also had been a very effective model when employed in large numbers.

The problem was they also were frequently defeated when they encountered the superior skills of [[Jedi Order|Jedi]] pilots or superior tacticians like [[Mitth'raw'nuruodo]]. They also relied on droid control ships which five decades later were not employed by any galactic navies, let alone by the [[Caspian Navy]]. And while the Caspian Navy's philosophy in capital ship deployment was to field as many ships as possible to discourage attacks, it was not based around expendability of assets, even that of [[Droid|Droids]].

The argument that finally held sway in favour of such a design was made by [[Dean]] [[ak]]. The Vulture Droid was [[Haor Chall Engineering|Haor Chall Engineering's]] best hope at restoring brand recognition after fifty years out of operation. More over in spite of the mixed feelings aroused by the name Vulture Droid, particularly as they appeared very similar to the [[Xi Charrian|Xi Charrians]] themselves, ak was narrowly able to convince a [[conclave|designers' conclave]] that the previous more precise name of the craft should be abandoned in favour of making the Vulture Droid name official. The '''Mark IV Vulture-Class Droid Starfighter''' was born.

The immediate design priority was to develop a model that was more autonomous, both to avoid the need of dedicated control ships and to eliminate the key tactics that had been employed to defeat them during the Clone Wars. For inspiration ak and his team looked even further back to the Vulture Droids predecessor the *[[:starwars:Scarab-class starfighter/Legends|Scarab-Class Starfighter]] which featured a cockpit with a Droid pilot rather than the starfighter being the Droid itself.

Though the aim for the Mark IV was a Droid starfighter rather than a Droid piloted starfighter, the schematics of the pilot units from the Scarab-Class would serve the engineers at Haor Chall Engineer along with their partners at [[SubPro|Sublight Products Corporation]] led by [[Sirlahn Inglet]] in developing the new Droid brain for the Mark IV.

No longer built around expendability, the Mark IV would also adopt shielding for the craft in line with the earlier Scarab-Class design. Other design features would hewn close to previous models similar hull strength and maneuverability, and no hyperspace capability. In keeping with five decades of technological advancement since their last production they would feature better weapons. The sensor capability also was improved in line with current [[Caspian Union|Caspian]] design doctrine. 

Efforts would be made to improve the efficacy of the solid-fuel concentrate slugs required for propulsion, extending its time in operation before refueling from 35 minutes to one hour. The plan was to deploy the craft largely from [[Kichnar Orbital Station]] and on [[Daring-class Battlecruiser|Daring-Class Battlecruisers]]. 

On the Daring-Class the Vulture-Class Droid Starfighters and the [[Guardian-Class Starfighter|Guardian-Class Starfighters]] were set to replace the L-200 Halcyon Transports which no longer served the operation assignments of the class. As such the Vulture-Class were not designed to serve in long lasting invasions but rather to defend against invasions. The craft would maintain its ability transform into a ground based combat unit as well though this was not envisioned to be a normal operational role.

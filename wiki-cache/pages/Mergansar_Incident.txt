The '''Mergansar Incident''' was a short naval engagement between the [[Caspian Navy]] and the ''[[Leviathan]]''. Following the [[Casulmis]] attack on members [[Olumi Osahn]] perpetrating the pogroms on [[Mergansar]], the Caspian Navy sent vessels to intercept the ''Leviathan'' and force its surrender. Caspian forces fired first and seemingly had the upper hand on the ''Leviathan'' but the [[New Sith Order|Sith]] counterattack proved devastating to the Caspian forces. Of the four Caspian warships involved, two were crippled by crashing into each other. while the other two were heavily damaged, and [[Sar Admiral]] [[Prae Kichakressa]] killed. The ''Leviathan'', however, was seriously damaged and forced to withdraw.

== Background ==

When the Sariphage [[Report:Sariphage Press Releases|pandemic]] hit [[Caspar]], Caspian born [[New Sith Order|Sith]] [[Duke]] and [[Admiral]] [[Crestian Tarasar]] emerged from his seclusion in the [[Dragonflower Nebula]] and responded with his erstwhile hidden [[Interdictor-class Immobilizer 418|Interdictor]], the ''[[Leviathan]]'', offering his aide to the Caspians. After meeting with [[Sar Admiral]] [[Prae Kichakressa]], she directed Tarasar towards [[Olumi Osahn]], which was using the pandemic as an excuse to drive non-Sarians off of [[Mergansar]]. As Olumi Osahn had previously been led by Sith Thane and Star [[Marshal]] [[Axel Vichten]], it was hoped Tarasar would be able to dissuade them from their [[Report:Mergani Pogrom Press Releases|pogroms]].

The efforts of Admiral Tarasar and [[Tyler Damion|Darth Malign]], who had secretly been rescued from a decade trapped on [[Korriban]] by his [[Johanna Siri te Danaan|sister]], to force the cultists into submission proved fruitless. With the 'diplomatic' path off the table, Tarasar directed his efforts to organise a paramilitary force from among the growing number of Mergani refugees flooding onto [[Kichnar Orbital Station]]. He dubbed the group [[Casulmis]], [[Sarian (language)|Sarian]] for Great Wind.

Once sufficiently organised, Tarasar and Malign would lead Casulmis in an attack on the cultists operating in the city of Hebron, which was the central location from which the non-Sarians were being forced to flee. Their efforts quickly led to the liberation of Hebron, and soon after the city of Goshen as well. Starfighters from the ''Leviathan'' were also engaged in bombing Olumi Osahn strongholds. Though Casulmis faced stronger resistance in the other cities they attempted to liberate, their efforts on Mergansar were off to a promising start.

== The Battle ==

Once the pandemic had been contained, ''[[CMS Typhoon]]'' commanded by Sar Admiral Kichakressa broke for Mergansar along with ''CMS Rapace'', ''[[CMS Prophet]]'', and ''CMS Ad Astra''. Kichakressa ordered the ''Leviathan'' to cease operations and rendezvous with her force, as Tarasar considered his efforts to have the tacit approval of the Sar Admiral, he complied with the initial order. Once the Caspian warships deployed a fighter 'cap', it was clear to Tarasar that Kichakressa had turned on him, so he responded by jamming the Caspian's sensors and refusing to lower shields. He tried to diffuse the situation diplomatically, but when Malign demanded the Caspian's surrender instead, combat became inevitable and ''Leviathan'' was forced to scramble its own fighters.

Tarasar's reliance on attempting diplomacy was nearly his undoing as he allowed the Caspian's to fire on the ''Leviathan'' first, causing considerable damage. Complete disaster was averted, however, by the presence of Darth Malign. Strike teams led by Malign's mutant servant [[Sentinel]] bombed key locations on the ''Typhoon'', including the bridge, killing Sar Admiral Kichakressa. Meanwhile the ''Rapace'' and ''Prophet'' mysteriously crashed into one another, crippling both ships. The ''Ad Astra'' suffered heavy damage to her [[HOWL]] launchers but little damage elsewhere. With the Caspian force crippled ''Leviathan'' was able to flee back to [[Silver Station]].

== Aftermath ==

Though the [[Caspian Navy]] ordered a forensic investigation into how the ''Leviathan'' was able to inflicted so much damage in spite of how overmatched they were, sabotage by allies with [[SubPro]] helped ensure the Caspian Navy was never able to really figure out what happened. The exact details remain one of the few secrets that Tarasar continues to keep from almost anyone who was not already aware.

The subsequent collapse of the [[Caspian Democratic Union]] eliminated the government with a claim against Tarasar. While he was viewed by many on the worlds of the former Union to be an enemy, many others, particularly in the refugee community sympathised with the position he took. The subsequent release of documents linking the late Sar Admiral Kichakressa to Olumi Osahn added to the confusion as to just who was the enemy, though some doubted the veracity of the documents in question.

Months later, after the dust settled, Tarasar returned to his home city of [[Trinumvira]] and established a charity called [[The Mission]]. With The Mission he was able to funnel considerable aid to the survivors of the pandemic and Mergani refugees on Caspar, providing himself some covered from retaliation for his previous actions. He would also publicly stay out of politics for several months in order not to court unneeded controversy. It was only after the Provisional Assembly was nearly done with the new constitution for the new [[Caspian Union|Caspar Union]] that Tarasar formed a political party called [[Praecet]] and began to overtly influence the political process. His election as [[Presav]] and appointment as Sar Admiral ended any potential Caspian retaliation for his earlier actions, though he remained unpopular with some segments of the population.

Though Tarasar does cite his ability to fight the Caspian Navy to a draw as a key reason why the Union has needed to engage in massive rearmament, he also made it clear he finds it a tragic embarrassment for all involved, a battle that did not need to happen. Due to their severe damage both ''Rapace'' and ''Prophet'' were both under consideration to be scrapped. As Sar Admiral, Tarasar made a point of ensuring both ships were fully repaired, at considerable expense, feeling their continued legacy to be an important for reconciliation. ''Typhoon'', ''Rapace'', ''Prophet'', and ''Ad Astra'' now serve alongside ''Leviathan'' in the [[Caspian 4th Fleet]]. Tarasar also promoted one of his former opponents at the battle, Benjawa Cyclos, the commanding officer of the ''Prophet'' to [[Rear Admiral]] giving him command of the recommissioned [[Daring-class Battlecruiser|Daring-Class Battlecruiser]] ''CMS Defiant''.

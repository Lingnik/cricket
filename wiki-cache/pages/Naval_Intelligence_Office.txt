The '''Naval Intelligence Office''' was previously the main intelligence organisation of the [[Caspian Democratic Union]]. It's legacy was considered mixed. Blamed by some to be the principle tool used to dismantle and weaken the [[Caspian Navy]] during the War of Papers and the Taylor Uprising, the Bureau was disbanded. The NIO was then reinstated by Provisional [[Presav]] Thomas Mahon believing it was necessary to maintain the CDU's position as a neutral entity in the ongoing Galactic Civil War.

Understaffed and underfunded in the final years of the Caspian Democratic Union, it was unable to provide effective support to the Union when it got caught in the crossfire of the Galactic Civil War in [[24 ABY]] and subsequently forced to surrender sovereignty over customs enforcement to the [[Corporate Sector Authority]] by the [[Galactic Empire]]. Much of the NIO's databases were deliberately sabotaged during the Sariphage pandemic to prevent the information following into enemy hands. Many of the agents who survived, including NIO Director [[Sar Admiral]] [[Odie Hood]] fake their own deaths, waiting to see how the dust settled.

The function of the Naval Intelligence Office has been replaced in the new [[Caspian Union]] by the [[Caspian Intelligence Service]] which includes many of the survivors of the former NIO.

=== Demographics ===

In its second iteration after being reinstated by Presav Mahon, the Naval Intelligence Office was known for growing species wise. In addition to the [[Human|Humans]] and [[Sarian|Sarians]] who made up the bulk of the agency, significant numbers of [[Bith]], [[Duros]], [[Givin]], [[Gotal|Gotals]], [[Vodran|Vodrans]], [[Vorzydiak]], [[Vultan|Vultans]], and [[Yari|Yaries]] were employed by the NIO. Females outnumber males four to one, which has largely remained true in the modern Caspian Intelligence Service, particularly in leadership roles.

=== Facilities ===
Naval Intelligence was known to keep a forensics and training facility in [[Groton]] on [[Caspar]]. That facility now houses the Technology Section of the modern Caspian Intelligence Service while the Training Section facilities moved to [[Noques Rasala]].

=== Naval Assets ===

Occasionally, a NIO Agent would be placed aboard a warship to further interpret and disseminate the tremendous influx of sensor data Caspian ships typically processed. Prior to the War of Papers, NIO Agents operated aboard ship with far more influence and authority, but this was drastically scaled when the office was reinstated. Many captains maintained caution and wariness regarding Agents, but some did consider their input and advice during assessments. Marines tended to avoid NIO Agents whenever possible.

In reaction to the fallout from the [[Imperial Blitzkrieg|Blitzkrieg of 14 ABY]], the Office intensified their requests for dedicated intelligence-gathering vessels. What vessels they had were either too small or not equipped to adequately perform the tasks the Office were seeking to accomplish. To address these concerns, a half dozen cutters were constructed and outfitted by the [[Krittain|Krittish]] to augment the few [[Lightning-class Sloop|''Lightning''-class sloops]] in NIO's inventory. In addition, a single [[Shearwater-class Frigate|Shearwater-Class Light Cruiser]], ''[[CMS Taciturn]]'', was completely outfitted for the Office's purposes in late 14 ABY. The crew is a mixture of Naval, NIO, and Crest personnel, and the exact manifest and bill are completely undisclosed.

In the modern Caspian Union the ''CMS Taciturn'' serves as part of '''Task Force Resplendent''' of the '''[[Caspian 4th Fleet]]'''. No comprehensive, publicly available list exists of the smaller capital ships in Caspian service, but some of the dedicated Naval Intelligence Office ships found their way back into mainline Naval service while others are operated by the Caspian Intelligence Service.

=== The Prowlers ===

Attracted by the long-range capability of the [[Z-105 Harpoon]] fighter-bomber for the [[Naval Aerospace Arm|NAA]] teamed with the Electronic Warfare packaging employed by the [[Marine Aerospace Division]], the Office retained an undisclosed number of [[Z-105 Harpoon#Prowler Variant|IR-variant ''Prowlers'']] for use as surveillance and intel-gathering platforms. The crews chosen to pilot these craft were often of a "minority" race, not easily identified as being Union in origin, and were kept largely out of the public eye.

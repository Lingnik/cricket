'''Prae Kichakressa''' ([[37 BBY]] - [[28 ABY]]) a [[Sarian]] from [[Krittain Major]] who served as one of the [[Caspian Navy|Caspian Navy's]] [[Sar Admiral|Sar Admirals]] in the final days of the [[Caspian Democratic Union]]. She commanded the [[CDU Home Fleet]] and used the ''[[CMS Typhoon]]'' as her flagship. As she was not on [[Caspar]] itself at the onset of the Sariphage pandemic, she was the only Sar Admiral known at the time to have survived the disease and commanded the blockade of the planet to stop the spread of the virus off world.

When Caspian expatriate and [[New Sith Order|Sith]] [[Admiral]] [[Crestian Tarasar]] arrived in system with his [[Interdictor-class Immobilizer 418|Interdictor]] [[Leviathan]] offering aide, she unofficially tasked him with working to end the pogroms on [[Mergansar]] as [[Olumi Osahn]] had previous links to the Sith. As Tarasar and [[Tyler Damion|Darth Malign]] proved unable to negotiate an end to their activity they instead opted to attack their agents on Mergansar.

As a cure to the Sariphage had been developed, allowing the Navy to end its blockade of Caspar, Sar Admiral Kichakressa turned her attention to Mergansar seeking to force Leviathan's surrender following the attack, in spite of it occurring against a criminal element based on her own unofficial encouragement. The subsequent [[Mergansar Incident|engagement]] saw Leviathan forced to withdraw after suffering heavy damage. 

The damaged suffered on the Caspian side was more extensive. ''CMS Ad Astra'' suffering serious damage to her [[HOWL|HOWL Launchers]]. ''[[CMS Prophet]]'' and ''CMS Rapace'' were crippled when they crashed into each other. Meanwhile ''[[CMS Typhoon]]'' suffered extensive damage including the complete destruction of her bridge, killing Sar Admiral Kichakressa.

In the aftermath of the situation Tarasar presented some evidence to suggest Kichakressa had herself been in league with Olumi Osahn, though in the chaos little could be done to verify the claim. Tarasar himself would stop repeating the claim after his political career on Caspar began to take off, though he has continued to vilify her failure stop the pogroms and attacking his forces after they tried to do so.

Her legacy remains a continued subject of scholarly debate within the Union. Though many agree with Tarasar's assessment of her actions, she is view more favourably by others who feel she did the best job she could in an impossible situation. Within the [[Krittain|Krittain System]] the sentiment tends to be more positive, with Tarasar's role in her demise cited as one of the factors that kept the system from returning to the [[Caspian Union|Union]] for so long after he was elected [[Presav]].

Sar Admiral's Cargo Hold -- Sienar Interdictor-class Cruiser -- Leviathan

To apply the label of cargo hold to the room is a slight exaggeration. The room has no external connexion to the outside of the ship by which it might deliver cargo or take it. Rather than having any purpose in bulk storage, this particular cargo hold serves as storage for the personal effects of the Sar Admiral and on occasion those of VIPs on board. One could be forgiven for thinking it had a large role in the ship's operations as the centrepiece of the room is a small, fully functional carbonite freezing chamber. The pit which extends down into the deck below is large enough to fit most sentients.

Currently the cargo hold is largely serving as a gallery of sorts to some of the Admiral's art collections. Highlights include Eriaduan shellwork jewellery, Mandalorian cubist paintings, Miraluka tapestries, and Wookiee totems.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Wargames had not just been an occasion for Xi Charrian culture to be introduced to the wider Union and galaxy, but an opportunity for other cultures to get introduce to the Xi Charrians. This at least indirectly was what had Cresian and Zubindi meeting in the Sar Admiral's Private Cargo Hold on the Leviathan. The room primarily served to hold his personal art collection. A third opinion had been sought for the matter at hand, with Nara's extended an invitation to come provide it.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nara doesn't keep the pair waiting, though her mode of dress suggests she might have preferred to; she's dressed in a sumptuous shimmersilk lounging robe in a deep rose pink trimmed in white Chandrilan weasel fur at the collar and cuffs and cinched at her waist with a matching belt, her feet shod in dainty matching slippers. Her hair, left loose, spills down her back in pale blonde waves.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She enters at a jog, slowing to a walk only when she approaches her two superiors. "Sorry to keep you waiting, gentlebeings... how may I help you?" she asks, with a slightly sheepish smile.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian chuckles a little as he turns around, "You didn't need to rush it was not that urgent." Zubindi for his part smirks and says, "Well your time might not be valuable Crest. But some of us have places to be." The Presav replies, "Oh oh, suddenly we aren't good enough for you around here. I see how it is."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;They both laugh a little before Crestian remarks to Nara. "So I needed a second opinion, well I guess third. So Councillor cadracak-'r'shak," he gets through the name reasonably well though stumbles a bit at the timing of second 'r.' "You probably have not met him yet, he is not a particular fan of all the military pomp, tries to avoid building weapons and that sort of thing."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian gets lost in a thought for a moment, pausing before continuing, "In any event, cadracak is going to be opening an art gallery in Xi Xin. Wants to show cause some art from the rest of the galaxy. So I am trying to decide what I want to loan him from my collection for the opening. Figured we might benefit from an opinion from someone who hasn't spent years looking at it."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nara giggles at the exchange. "Can you boys behave yourselves, or do you need to go to your rooms for a while?" she asks impishly. "All silliness aside, I don't know a great deal about art. So I suppose it would be best if we avoid anything too martial, given the Councillor's leanings, but otherwise I don't know how much help I can be. Perhaps if you showed me around and told me a little about the items in your collection, I might be able to provide a more informed opinion. Or at the very least get your mind fully on art, and the pieces you enjoy showing off," she suggests, with that warm smile that tends to grace magazine covers and holo-ads alike.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"See you have already offered an important perspective." Crestian smiles a bit then looks pensive, "I had mentioned cadracak's leanings more to explain why you have likely not crossed his path, I had not actually considered whether I that should impact the artistic choices. Mainly trying to keep an eye towards what would captivate Xi Charrian interest." He looks around considering where to start. "I am afraid you missed this 'gallery' at its peak. Atsvara's Biscuit Baron Jolly Meal Toy Collection use to be here, but she has since moved it to a bunker under our villa. I have been slower to consider whether it is time to move any of this home, well this is home, but you know planet side."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"It might be wise. I don't really foresee any fleet conflicts on the horizon, so to speak, but warships are frequently lost to accidents as well," Nara replies thoughtfully. "And are all Xi Charrians of a similar mind to the Councillor? Or would something more martial have less of a risk of offending them? Most patriotic art has a martial bent to it, at least among what artworks I've seen personally, so I felt the need to ask."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian chuckles a bit, "Well most of the Xi Charrian economy is based on selling machines of war. cadracak is more of an outlier. I don't think there is any great worry about offending anyone, cadracak included. His idea is about promoting culture, educating his people on the wider galaxy, things like that. Honestly within my own collection here the only thing that seems overtly martial in nature are the Mandalorian cubist paintings." The Presav motions to the paintings in question adding, "Though honestly they seem like probably the best thing to lend. Given the Xi Charrian religious affinity to their crafting, and examination of Mandalorian culture offers certain parallels."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I see. Perhaps you could lend them, and also something tranquil and peaceful?" Nara suggests. "Not that I know much of anything about Mandalorian cubism. But I must admit, I'm curious about what you mean when you speak of parallels. They sound interesting, and would probably be more so if I knew more about Xi Charrian culture."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Perhaps parallel might be a stretch. But the Xi Char look at their creation's as a why to glimpse upon the perfection of the afterlife. Meanwhile the attachment that Mandalorians place into their armour. I would say that borders on the religious does it not? The perspective is different but both are cultures where the forge is sacred." Crestian looks to Zubindi seeing if he might extend a thought. The Kubaz shrugs, "You are talking neither food nor fashion. Don't expect my expertise to be valuable either."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I see what you mean, a little bit... the armor is both a means to an end and an important part of our cultural identity, not least for our use of beskar'gam metal," Nara muses, pausing before the Mandalorian cubist pieces, studying them thoughtfully. "The craftsmanship is important, but a familial link is also important... Mando'a often wear armor passed down through family lines. Making that armor /fit/ properly is equally important, and ensures that armorsmiths are kept busy."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Sarian stops in front of the Mandalorian paintings, perhaps looking at each more carefully than he has in some time, "My general thought is based on the symbolism present in Xi Char tradition that art that engages in similar practice is what will suit the exposition better. The Wookiee totems seem likewise something that would be fitting. Something my my Eriaduan shellwork jewellery perhaps not so much." Zubindi chimes in, "The shellwork is the best part of your collection Crest."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Possibly. Why don't you show them to me, tell me about them?" Nara asks, her smile growing. "Tell me why they should or shouldn't go. That's what I'm here for. Besides, I'm /learning/ as we do this. I think I've learned more culture in the last ten minutes than in a year's worth of travel."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Poodoo. Now I have to pretend I am an expert. I largely just collect art that relates to cultures I have some affinity towards. Wookiee totems for example seem fairly simplistic. Nothing I consider necessarily pleasing aestheticly in an intrinsic sense. Yet, they are an art meant to capture a likeness, of person or an action. And the Wookiee culture is such a vibrant one so I don't know I guess I see a little bit of that spark in these." Crestian shrugs a little, "It has been a while since I have done much collecting. This collection dates back to quieter years."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Oh, poodoo... you know you claim an affinity for Wookiee culture just so you have an excuse not to shave," Nara teases, chuckling softly. "In all honesty, they're kind of ugly, these totems. But there /is/ an energy to them... a sort of drive to capture the essence of something important to them, like a cultural figure or an event, like you said... and that passion is a truly beautiful thing. I'd bet every one of these totems has its own unique story."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Zubindi interjects, "Okay making a mental note, no Wookiee totems as presents for Nara." He looks them over and adds, "Maybe you should just given them as an outright gift to the new gallery, get something better in your collection?" Crestian smiles, "Perhaps antique shaving equipment, so I have a new excuse not to shave. Can't use antiques after all."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nara laughs at Zubindi's interjection, that same laugh that has won a heart or two in her handful of holoposts. "Way to look out for my sensibilities," she says to Zubindi through her laughter. "Maker forbid!" she echoes in agreement with Crestian. "The totems are sure to be a hit in the exhibit. Wookiee culture is mysterious enough that any glimpse into it should draw quite a crowd."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I should probably just stick to loaning the Mandalorian paintings and Wookiee totems. If I loan too much then it might just look like cadracak could not get enough other interest." Crestian pulls out one of his ubiquitous datapads, "I actually have no idea what else he has actually gotten. I only found about this gallery opening the other day. I don't know him as well as the other Xi Charrians in the Council."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Or perhaps you should see what's been loaned and make a decision once you have a more complete understanding of what he's already gotten. If you feel there needs to be more, you could always donate other items anonymously," Nara suggests gently. "You have two very good collections that feel right for the exhibit, and we all want it to be a success. If the loans and donations come up short, you can still add an anonymous donation to round things out."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian nods, "A sensible thought. Particularly the possibility of anonymous donation. Of course then I would need to make it something folks don't know I have. But I should see at least what he has got arranged already." The Sarian yawns, "He probably has everything he really needs anyway. But it just feels like I should add something since my art collection is already in orbit."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"And because it's just a nice, supportive thing to do, especially for a ruler with a large art collection," Nara adds, smiling supportively. "Were I actually rich, and if I actually had an art collection, I'd loan him something if I were /right here/ with it. And not just because there's a measure of publicity in it, although I'd be a fool to ignore that aspect of it."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Give it time and you will be actually rich. There is a lot of credits to be made yet in modelling and intelligence," remarks to the Kubaz. "And the wargames have not hurt for putting our good works in the spotlight. Though I for one feel like I am going to need to move on from Charros soon. Business to be had on Corellia. If the Confederation keeps doing well I may need to open a location there."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Perhaps. The only reason I even care about money is because it would make my cover a little easier to live up to," Nara observes. "And for what it's worth, I'll be sorry to leave Charros. I find the Xi Charrians fascinating, and I especially enjoyed touring their factory-temple. But the job takes where it takes us, and I may as well get used to that."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Presav smiles once again, "It has been gratifying that so many people have been able to see the splendour of Charros IV. A lot of work went into getting it into the Union. But I think it has truly been to the benefit of both the Xi Charrians and then rest of the Union. The have become such a vibrant part of our community and ak in particular has become a good friend and powerful ally. I am glad others have enjoyed it here as much as I have."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"It /is/ a wonderful society. Sometimes I wish I could be so organized. But I did feel sorry for the little Xi Charrian who couldn't do much in the factory-temple. I hope he does well there in the years to come," Nara observes. "I almost wanted to adopt him, but it seemed like he would be happier in his home."

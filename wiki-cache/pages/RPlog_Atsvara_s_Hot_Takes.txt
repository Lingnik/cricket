&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There are probably two salient features to all serious wargame exercises. The first is that there is going to be a bunch of mock fighting, it is after all in the word wargames. The second is that when you get a bunch of service people together in one place like that, there is going to be a lot of drinking. Add in distinguished guests, and the drinks flow even more. And if the reception and viewing partying on the CMS Renaissance of Innovation were not already an atmosphere of excitement, the victory for the defenders in the first exercise certainly would be enough to create such a vibe. While most of those present had expected the win, in truth some of the Crest, including Presav Tarasar himself were far less convinced, so the final result was a pleasant surprised.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The party on the Renaissance was certainly not the only one going on, but it was A-List one. The second to last of the Admiral Roj-Class Battleships built for the Caspian Navy, it was the site chosen for the premiere party both in terms of its name, which was more fitting to the situation now that a Second Renaissance of Innovation had be declared, but also as a vessel used for foreign aid missions. And now with the first exercise over, some of the participants were starting to make there way to the party here.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But the news was not all good. After stepping out for several minutes Crestian returns to the room and pulls aside Councillor Zaalbacca, "I wanted you to hear this before the news report gets out. Charrs died at Twin Suns this morning, it is going to be on the Brackett's broadcast. I just realised a statement." The normally buoyant Wookiee looks immediately deject, grabbing a pastry item from a nearby plate and throwing it at a window.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Selko had seriously considered returning to her room and staying there instead of going to the party on the observation deck, but she did at least want to say hello to Crestian Tarasar, and possibly his daughter if she was in attendance. She's looking for him now, actually. Though her hair and steel-rimmed, round-lensed plasspecs are the same as the last time they met, her pocket-laden tan jacket and black utility pants are definitely different. Though, judging by the way she keeps covertly tugging at seams, she's still not entirely comfortable with them.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It's really crowded here, too. How will she find him in all this? She ducks between a waiter and two partygoers trying to start an argument about the freshness of the shellfish. Seriously, rich people... she'd be glad to have shellfish at all, especially ones that smell that well-cooked!<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She finds herself near the huge viewport that overlooks the forward hull. She leans on it for a moment, looking out over the massive ship. Imagine how many engineers it takes to keep all /that/ running!<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She realizes quickly that the outside darkness makes the viewport a good mirror. A good look at herself reveals that her hair is a bit mussed, her jacket a little askew, and her square-tailed blouse is coming untucked.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At least nobody's watching her... as far as she can tell. She takes a moment to finger-comb her long hair back into some semblance of order. That done, she unfastens her pants to tuck her blouse back in.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Right about then, something goes *SPLAT* against the window maybe a foot to her right! She ducks instinctively, looking over her shoulder in alarm! "Whoa!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Pilots have a good sense of direction. That's a big part of their job. A /really/ big part! Pilots populate the party in clumps, some still in flightsuits, others in BDUs or civillian clothes depending on if they're stationed on the Renaissance or not. There are other people here, too. But it was the pilots that had won the day. No matter how much planning an Admiral can do, in the end, things happen that no one can anticipate. Then, everything falls on the space jockey no one otherwise takes seriously.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At a long table Elana Tracer sits back in her chair, a Corellian ale in one hand, among quite a few of those A-Listers. Laughter ripples around table as tales as big as a bantha are told. When Tarasar finally walks by, she watches him over the rim of her glass. When the donut hits the transparasteel, she winces and lets out a quiet sound. "Oooo...."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Atsvara goes over to the window and then looks donut at the donut. She pulls our her datapad and types something in while narrating, "One Dark Matter Sprinkled Donut, died of injuries sustained in the battle. I have updated the causality total." This time she manages to speak without any giggle or smile cracking through. She was starting to get better at the comedy. Nevertheless the humour did force Zaalbacca to crack a smile. He looks back to Crestian and growls in Shyriiwook, "Frakkin Death Sticks." Crestian simply nods and pats Zaalbacca on the shoulder. He then looks back to the others nearby and explains, "Friend of his died. He didn't punch a hole in the window so I think he is taking it rather well."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Oh... that explains it," Selko says, unconsciously responding to the wit of the sharp young girl who appears beside her at the viewport. "Casualties are always unfortunate, but the donuts are missed the most."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Over her shoulder she can see the massive Wookiee crack a faint smile. "I'm sorry for your loss," she adds, understanding the quick Shyriiwook words.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She turns back to the window, and realizes that her reflection is holding up its unfastened pants. Oops! At least she kept her grip!<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She does a hasty but covert tucking-in of her blouse, then pulls her pants shut and fastens them. "Shh," she whispers to Atsvara, with an impishly conspiratorial grin.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tracer tugs at the bird on the collar of her slate green BDU jacket and looks at her lap, embarrassed to have been caught watching the exchange until the tension eases. At the explanation from Tarasar, she clears her throat solemnly and rises, raising her glass. The pilots at her table follow suit, others joining them as the whole room quiets. In a loud, clear voice Tracer says, "To those whom we have known!"<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Those in the room who know, know. And they repeat the honorific before they drink. "To those whom we have known."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian misses the moment, perhaps paying more attention to Atsvara, or perhaps just a sign he is still in some ways and outsider in his own military. Whatever the case he pivots to address Tracer, "Thank you for taking command of the exercise. With such legends leading the attack, seemed like we needed one leading the defence. Excellent job by the way. I will be honest I didn't expect our pilots to do as well as they did. I stand behind the craft but most of them have never seen actual combat so pitting them against Axel and Lynae....I expected it to be more humbling." For Atsvara's part her own only reaction to Selko seems to be a bit of giggle she does her best to stifle. After all she was trying to be serious today. Once again wearing her black uniformesque ensemble.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"To those whom we have known," Selko murmurs. She's heard the phrase, but now the meaning sinks in. Those whom we have known... the not-forgotten-but-gone. It's really sad, now that she realizes what they mean.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She turns and gives Atsvara a hug, focusing on the positive, including that giggle that almost got out. "Hi again, Atsvara! Did you enjoy the exercise earlier?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the room around them dissolves into scattered conversations that soon return to the pre-toast, Tracer nods humbly, her free hand flat on her chest with a slight bow. Snatching Crestian's drink he left on the table she walks all the way to him with both of their drinks in her hands, handing his glass to him. "I wouldn't expect anything but excellence from the CDF." Was there some admonishment of the Sarian leader in her tone? Probably.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Oh! I've been meaning to tell you." With this she includes Atsvara as well if she's paying attention, smiling in her direction before looking back at Crestian. And not to be rude she also smiles at Selko. "I'm reopening Marin next month."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Oh I expect excellence from the CDF....it is just now that includes Axel and Lynae again too. Actually looks like Axel wants the Star Marshal job permanently, that was a surprise." Taking his glass from Tracer, Crestian makes an appreciative nod before taking a sip. "And excellent news about Marin Mountain. The galaxy should be ready for more solid tourism and entertainment on Caspar." His eyes dart around for the party's host. Xi Charrians do stick out, but the problem was there were a number still in the room. Finally spotting him, as much by his particular translation droid as anything, "ak get your carapace over here. Elana is reopening Marin Mountain. You have a month to design yourself some skis!"<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;ak who had been in conversation with a certain Devaronian woman who happened to be the ship's commanding officer, makes his way over with the Vice Admiral following him. Once he has closed to a polite distance ak replies, "What makes you think I haven't designed some already?"<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Atsvara for her own part replies to Selko, "It was fun to watch, but would have been more fun to watch if there were real explosions." She looks more excited though at the prospect of Marin Mountain reopening, "Swoop Racing!"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Real explosions mean real deaths," Selko says soberly. "Though, maybe next time they can rig up some holo-explosions, to jazz things up. It /would/ make for more of a show. That'd help with the newscast ratings for sure."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Elana... Elana... the techie's eyes sweep quickly over Tracer's uniform and face, and widen. "You... you're Elana Tracer, aren't you?" she asks, surprised. "Swoop racer, fighter commander. You've got... kind of a temper," she adds, a little sheepishly. She's apparently seen some IGN borderline sludgenews pieces.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Atsvara's happy shout reminds her that the girl and Crestian are there. "Swoop racing?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Well...." Elana chuckles at Atsvara's enthusiasm as ak comes to join them. "Well...nothing sanctioned. Not yet. But everything else will be ready. Boarding, skiing...oh, but not carmteeks. Not until spring. I retired the stock we had before the outbreak. Trainers should have enough rideable by summer!" She lifts her glass slightly in ak's direction. "Ambassador. What a lovely star system you have here, my friend."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then Tracer looks at Selko. Then she blinks.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Well...not anymore," she says regarding the temper. Then she adds flatly, "Motherhood teaches patience."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Did we meet at the opening...?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian looks to Tracer, and then to Atsvara, and then back at Tracer, "I have a feeling it does not matter to her whether there is anything sanctioned. She just wants to do all the things Aunt Elana does. We should probably be grateful she hasn't hijack a starfighter yet....as far as I know." He looks back to her inquisitively. Atsvara replies with some disappointment, "Alas I have had far too much schmoozing to do. Your welcome by the way Tevas, thankfully enough of the people I need to meet with are around for your gaming."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;ak meanwhile is riding the high of being the man of the hour, "Thank you Elana you are too kind. I don't think anyone ever possibly imagined we would turn Charros IV into such a tourist destination....and there are some mixed feelings on that. But I think we are bring more of my people around. Outfitting the Trade Federation and Confederacy of Independent Systems was certainly a larger market for our work than the Caspian Union, but we didn't have such pride of place with that as we do here." He then looks to the Devaronian with him and adds, "I feel like some of you need an introduction, may I present Vice Admiral Kath Samba, Commanding Officer CMS Renaissance of Innovation."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Boarding, skiing, swoop racing... it sounds like this Marin Mountain has a pretty full list of activities. If you're not a bespectacled brainiac, anyway. Something tells Selko she's not cut out for boarding or skiing. Still, boards, skis, and swoops all need to be repaired sometime. Maybe she can contribute that way...<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The thought takes up so much of her brainspace that she almost misses Tracer's reply. "Oh... ISN gets more wrong than I'd thought," she murmurs in reply, scuffing her foot in embarrassment. "We kinda missed each other. I'm Selko," she says, offering a hydrospanner-callused hand.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Madam," Elana greets the Devaronian woman politely. They'd met before, of course. Wargames didn't plan themselves. Then Tracer takes Selkos' offered hands, feeling the familiar callus and grinning broadly. "Great to meet you. You're a fixer, aren't you? I've been known to grease lizard-monkey around myself when I get the chance. But uh yeah...some of those shows are so /old/ but they just keep showing them!" The petite brunette rolls her ice-blue eyes. "But yeah. I was thinking when all this is over, we could get some folks up there to have a test-run for my staff. Of course you're all invited."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"So Crestian...ak...how goes the turf war demos. Everything ready?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian looks a little sheepish, "The Battle Droids will probably represent the closing exercises. The tactical considerations there have needed more planning. Big shift in operations, our pilots may not have a lot of combat experience but starfighters are more the known entity. But battle droids are a newer part of the equation for the marines...." As Crestian trails off, ak interjects, "I think Crest is just nervous. Keeps thinking things aren't going to go well enough. This lack of confidence seems very uncharacteristic of him." ak looks back to Crestian, "I think you made need to drink more." Crestian laughs a bit before dutifully taking another sip of his drink.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Selko has a strong grip, though she doesn't show it off, even in handshakes. Sometimes she forgets, though, like now. "I fix stuff, yeah," she replies, her own answering smile shy but growing stronger. "I guess that explains a lot. Old footage making the rounds over and over again? That does sound like IGN. Though that brickhead did have it coming, the time you punched him in the nose." She sounds almost like she wishes she'd done it, poor mousy girl.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;An invitation to Marin Mountain is not to be missed, and she won't! "I'll be there! I mean, stuff might need to be fixed, right?" And maybe she could try out some of the more exciting stuff without a crowd to watch her fall on her face...<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Her attention returns to Crestian. "Do you have a model in mind? The Neimoidians tried a simple machine, mostly because it could be put back together from salvaged battle-damaged droids, but it wasn't much of a fighter. More of a blaster rifle with legs, and about as smart."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She glances down at Atsvara with a grin. "What do you think, Little Miss Explosions? Want to see the battle droids and living troops fight it out?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tracer grasps Crestian's arm. "It's going to be fine. You've got good people all around you. It's a success! You should enjoy yourself a little." She snaps her fingers at a passing server droid to get him another drink. "She's right, you know. Drink up!"<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tracer's comlink beeps and she glances at it before saying, "I apologize. I really need to take this. I'm sure I'll see you all soon." And with that, without seeming over alarmed, she takes her leave of the party.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian waves to the departing Tracer, before looking back to Selko, "Oh the models have already been decided. These wargames are all about a debut for our new lines. So the starfighters defending earlier were what ak and his people have been designing and building the past couple years, on the Droid end there are two updated designs on Clone Wars era models. The E5 Baron Droid, an update on an Baktoid design, and the IG-240 Hailfire Droid Tank, an update on the old Xi Char design." As to the question of Atsvara's interest in the matter she answers, "Who needs the living troopers, Droids are more fun."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Good luck, Ms. Tracer!" Selko calls after her. "I hope there's a shuttle waiting," she adds, more quietly.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Her attention returns to Crestian. "E5 Baron... what Baktoid design is it based on?" she has to ask, trying to recall some of the less well-known droids. Baktoid had an entire catalog of them, many of them obscure. "I've heard of the Hailfire Tank. It was really effective for a wheeled vehicle. Those do have some advantages over speeders, like not having to be light enough to leave the ground."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She blinks in surprise at Atsvara's answer. "I dunno, 'Vara. I've made friends among soldiers, and they were mostly great people. You lose that using droids exclusively. But using droids does save organic lives. I guess there are tradeoffs, either way."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Atsvara mildly objects to the characterisation, protesting, "I will have you know some of my best friends are Droids and I see no reason why they should be considered any more expendable." Crestian pats Atsvara on the head and explains, "Even money say this one is eventually going to fund and organise a Droid insurrection one of these days. She takes the issue of Droid rights even more seriously than I do. Though I am hoping they will let me live when they rise up."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As to the more technical question posed ak answers, "The E4 Baron Droid was the previous model, though their are some functional similarities to the B-Series. Sirlahn had been tinkering with the design for years but SubPro isn't really in Droid manufacturing the way we are. So it is a joint project in a way. Which is really true of a lot of Caspian designs, particularly now. We are blessed to have a number of corporations to partner with in the Union."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I'm afraid I've never seen any of the Baron series droids," Selko admits, not without some embarrassment. "What are they like? Baktoid had several B-series droids, some of them very different from each other."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She has to giggle at Atsvara's vehement reply. "Well, I guess I don't blame you. Some droids can be really entertaining and companionable. Kinda hard to hug, though."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She looks back toward the huge viewport, catching a glimpse of herself. "On the plus side, they don't have to worry about wearing clothes that get put into disarray," she murmurs, seeing that her blouse is coming untucked again. Next time, she's wearing a cropped one!

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"If Colonel Vee-Won was here he would probably have a word to say on Droid Fashion." Crestian then starts to look around, "Where is that bastard anyway? He blends in too much when he is wearing his uniform and not his pinstripes." The Devaronian who had been quite to this point chimes in, "Last I saw sir, he was playing Sabacc with Rear Admiral 'ak-'lak."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Typical," remarks Crestian, with ak soon adding, "Of them both."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ignoring the technical question for the moment, ak remarks, "'ak-'lak is my cousin. Parties more than I do. Very few Xi Charrians less serious than 'ak-'lak." Crestian chuckles a little explaining, "But a very inspiring commander, he commands the CMS Tenacious, he is our most senior Xi Charrian field commander."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"If Colonel Vee-Won was here he would probably have a word to say on Droid Fashion." Crestian then starts to look around, "Where is that bastard anyway? He blends in too much when he is wearing his uniform and not his pinstripes." The Devaronian who had been quite to this point chimes in, "Last I saw sir, he was playing Sabacc with Rear Admiral 'ak-'lak."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Typical," remarks Crestian, with ak soon adding, "Of them both."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ignoring the technical question for the moment, ak remarks, "'ak-'lak is my cousin. Parties more than I do. Very few Xi Charrians less serious than 'ak-'lak." Crestian chuckles a little explaining, "But a very inspiring commander, he commands the CMS Tenacious, he is our most senior Xi Charrian field commander."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Returning to the question of the Baron Droids, ak says through his Translation Sphere, "Sorry you were asking about Baron Droid similarity with B-Series....different ones in different ways. I could better elaborate another time, it has been long day and I may have inhaled a little too much."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Right at this moment, I'm not liking organic fashion much," Selko mutters, though her attention is on ak and Crestian. She smiles faintly, hearing about Irresponsible Admiral 'ak-'lak. "Some inspiring figures do tend towards habits that responsible people would call frivolous," she observes. "But it sounds like he does his job well in spite of a gambling habit. And this Vee-Won does, too."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian smiles, "Well for many years I cultivated, rather purposely, the image of dandy. I made sure most people didn't take me too seriously. There was a time being underestimated served my purpose. I don't tend to be underestimated too much any more. I would like to think though that we come across as a fun loving people. I mean without frivolity what is really worth fighting for?"<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"It's true. Without the ability to have fun, what is freedom good for? Choosing a different form of suffering isn't much of an advantage," Selko agrees quietly. "But it's true that sometimes it's an advantage to be underestimated. Sometimes the other girl doesn't put forth her best effort against you."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She turns back to the viewport, looking over her image there. "And I think I'd be very easy to underestimate right now," she murmurs, unfastening her pants to tuck her blouse in again. But she's in too much of a hurry; she's not standing close enough to the viewport to hide what she's doing, and she's pulled the closure too wide, leaving the top of the pants drooping away from her hips. Her bright white athletic briefs show, especially in the reflection.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She probably would be easy to underestimate. Or maybe it wouldn't be underestimation.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Sometimes I miss being underestimated though. Life was quieter then," remarks Crestian, oblivious to anything going on with Selko's wardrobe. Atsvara, however, start to giggle little before walking away from the group, having become bored with them. As Atsvara leaves the group Zaalbacca returns stating, "Crest, the Prelate is wondering if you and ak have a few moments to spare?" Turning back to Selko, Crestian says, "I hope you will excuse us, I feel like it would be blasphemous to say no."

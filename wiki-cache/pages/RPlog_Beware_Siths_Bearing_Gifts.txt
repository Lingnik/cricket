'''Crime Moon - Level 23'''

<small>In the pits of the detritus of Nar Shaddaa's nightmare world, exists a place so secretive and so brutal that few dare speak its name. Welcome to Crime Moon, the galaxy's hidden bar, where each visit carries with it a chance of never leaving alive. From the very moment one steps into this dim place, a chill of another world saunters from the walls, where life has no meaning. The door itself is hidden in a back alley, and the lighting inside so dim that only silhouettes can be made out. Each table is made of ferrocrete and is bolted down to the floor, but each has blaster holes peppered through its top. The walls are bare, stripped and scarred in the most bizarre ways, and chairs rarely stand straight.</small>

<small>The bar is a wide duracrete desk, with no drinks visible at all. Behind it, a massive Aqualish bartender - a giant for his race - stares at each customer with ghostly, angry eyes and procures orders from below the bar, but no drink list is visible anywhere. Everyone here is armed to their teeth... those without protection are mugged on sight. Hushed conversations and private meetings are never disturbed, and each walk to the bar - the only way to get a drink - is followed by every pair of eyes. Fights occur daily here, but no security is present. The deals made in Crime Moon can never see the light of day.</small>

Crime Moon does not pretend to be anything but a slaughterhouse with a drink menu, the air thick with the scent of sweat, spice, and ozone burns from discharged weapons. The bodies packed into its dim interior are an assemblage of killers, mercenaries, and fugitives whose lives have long since lost their value and the place hums with the unspoken agreement that violence is the natural order of things.

Mitra sits alone at a table against the far wall, untouched by the disorder around her, a glass of dark liquor in one hand, her expression as unreadable as ever. The cube rests in a satchel at her side, heavy with promise, already beginning to whisper over a frequency only she can hear, the voices within shifting like silt in deep water. She ignores them. They are not for her. Instead, she extends her awareness beyond the filth and frenzy of Crime Moon, out into the ruin beyond, where she feels the dark weight of Malus pressing forward through the wreckage that is the Refugee Sector.

She does not need to turn her head to know the moment he steps into the establishment. The temperature does not change, the air does not still, but something in the room's atmosphere recoils, a ripple in the undercurrent, a sharp inhalation that never quite reaches the lungs. The worst of the criminals here are predators, but they know when something greater has arrived.


It was the second time that Malus meet with one of his liberators here in the Crime Moon. If someone had told him that he would frequent the pinnacle of Nar Shaddaa's underworld as much as he had been, he would not have believed it; yet here he is again. He enters the bar, his mannerisms and posture of someone now comfortable with this setting. Mitra's presence in the Force is stronger than Skawn's, and as such, he has no trouble sensing the woman. His pace not halting, he glides over to the golden-haired woman on his mechanical legs.

Malus' appearance has changed only slightly since he last met with Mitra. The robes are exchanged for a cloak, attached at his collar, exposing the armor which were once hidden. Normally he would stand at the seat, waiting for an invitation, but he does not do so this time. Instead, he slides into a seat opposite the woman offers a polite greeting. "Lady Mitra," Malus says with a subtle bow of his head.


Mitra's grey eyes follow Malus' movements as he slides into the seat across from her, his bow just deep enough to register as deference but not submission. An improvement. Their last meeting had been strained by the edges of his impatience, the sharp glint of a man unaccustomed to being handled. Now, he moves with something closer to consideration; still dangerous, but measured, and that makes him useful.

Beside her, the Cube begins to stir, its whispers no longer distant and drifting, but urgent, churning, an unseen current disturbed by the presence of another Sith. The ancient knowledge inside it knows Malus, remembers him, and its voices press against the edges of her mind in agitated recognition. Mitra allows herself the barest flicker of a smile, tapping one finger against the rim of her glass, letting the moment stretch before she speaks.

"You seem more amenable to a proper conversation than before," she observes, her voice low, unhurried. "That bodes well. Because I have a proposal."


"I am always amenable to conversation. It is something that I rather enjoy," Malus replies through the modulator. He raises his left arm, signaling for an attendant to come to the table. "Our first was rushed and one-sided, unfortunately." He senses a presence, familiar, yet seemingly distance. It gives him a slight pause and the pupil of his right eye dilates and then constricts briefly.


Gesturing with his organic hand towards Mitra, he continues, "What is it that you wish to discuss? I assume that Skawn has spoken with you already."

The blonde exhales a quiet breath, and with it, she eases the tight grip she keeps on the Force. Not by much... just /enough/. Enough for Malus to feel her as she truly is, should he have the sense to reach for it. The darkness uncoils like a serpent, sinuous and cold, not a wild, raging power but something far worse; measured, patient... inevitable. The weight of it lingers between them, a thing not spoken but known, and then, as quickly as it surfaced, it is drawn back beneath her control.

"Skawn serves a purpose," she says, "but I have need of a being who can think further than his next indulgence. One who sees beyond the immediate ruin of things." She lifts her glass but does not drink, watching Malus over the rim, her eyes reflecting the neon glow of Crime Moon's flickering lights. "I assume you are such a being?"

She sets the glass down, hand moving to the satchel at her side. The Cube knows him, and he knows it -- she can see the way his attention shifts, the way his eye dilates ever so slightly. "As a gesture of goodwill," she continues, the word rolling off her tongue like a phrase she's borrowed rather than owned, "I have brought you something."


"I agree with your assessment of him," Malus affirms. The server arrives at the table, to which Malus simply says, "Bring me something. It does not matter what." As the server departs, he reaches up and removes the mask from his collar, setting the device onto the table and allowing his expressions to be fully available to his table companion.

"You do not need to ask a question that you already know the answer to. You would not have gone to the trouble you had, if that was in aby doubt. Already plans are in motion," he says simply. His expression is neutral; he is stating fact and not indulging in self-promotion.

He allows his attention to drift away from her towards the movement of her hand. "And that would be?" he asks, even though he can already sense the answer to his question.


The words bypass the space between them, bypass the air entirely, slipping into Malus' mind as though they had always belonged there. No preamble, no warning... just /there/, the intrusion as easy as stepping through an open door. Mitra does not force her way in. She does not need to. His mind offers only the faintest resistance, something that barely slows her before she has settled inside the architecture of his thoughts. /Leaks cannot be afforded,/ she continues, /I do not tolerate failure./

And then, as suddenly as she entered, she is gone, retreating without fanfare, without a trace save for the lingering knowledge that she could return at any time, if she so wished.

Mitra's lips part as if the previous exchange had not occurred at all. "The object was difficult to locate," she says aloud, her tone measured, polite even. She places one hand on the case at her side, allowing him to anticipate before revealing more. "But I felt it would serve our cause better if you were reunited with something that could teach you, where I cannot. Ahzad's Cube belongs to you, after all."


Malus leans forward at the table, responding to the exchange, rather than the cube. "You truly do remind me of her," he says, the corners of his mouth turning upward. The bloom of a smile doesn't blossom fully, though. "I was about to offer a lesson about unbalancing your opponent," he continues, and his slowly move towards the object near her to emphasize his point. "However, it seems you have some sense of that already. Our goals are aligned for now. Do not be so quick to judge my actions on their face value. Opportunities are being opened; you will come to trust my experience."

Now Malus turns his attention towards the Bantha in the room. "I assumed it to be lost, or locked in a vault somewhere on Coruscant." The bloom of a smile starts to wither, but manages to cling to life for a moment more. "How did you come by it?" he asks with genuine curiosity.


Mitra does not trust. The concept itself is foreign, a relic of childhood long since discarded. But she is willing, willing to let Malus prove his worth. That is the most she will grant him, for now. Whether he succeeds or fails is his own concern; she has no interest in coddling men who speak of experience as though the word alone grants them dominion. Let him /show/ her.

"Our enemies are powerful," she says instead, her voice even as she lifts the satchel and sets it on the table between them. The weight of it is solid, unimpressive to the eye, but Mitra knows better. She watches Malus as she does so, watches for the flicker of want, of recognition. "And twenty years of stasis have left you vulnerable. You have a great deal of catching up to do."

If he wishes to take it, she does not stop him. A quiet, effortless concession. The Cube does not belong to her, it never has. And yet, some of its secrets are hers now, just as they once were his. She allows him to reach, before adding: "Vassily Korolov's vaults are not as hidden or secure as he would believe them to be. And walls," she says, her lips curving at the edges, almost a smile, "are no match for the power of the dark side."


Malus reaches out with the Force, letting his presence touch the cube, while reaching out with his organic hand. It's a gesture of trust to the entities which occupy the cube, for he knows that should they deem him unworthy, it has ways of protecting itself. As such, it is a slow motion, as one might treat a dangerous animal, and finally his hand rests atop of it to physically take custody of it. "You intrigue me, Lady Mitra," he admits, letting his eyes meet hers. "A unique claim that not many sentients can make."

"You are correct, of course, they are powerful. However, such power leads to arrogance and complacence, which in turn makes them vulnerable. Already your actions have disturbed their balance." He breaks eye contact and regards the cube once more. "Have you been introduced to the Spokesman?"


Mitra inclines her head, a slow, deliberate gesture, watching as Malus takes possession of the Cube with all the reverence of a supplicant at an altar. 'You intrigue me, Lady Mitra,' he says, and her expression does not change. She is used to intrigue, to fascination, to the quiet calculations of men who wonder what it is she wants, how far she will go to get it, and what might happen if they stand in her way.

"I have made contact with the Spokesman," she replies, fingers resting lightly against the tabletop, "A strange character. Loquacious, but careful with his truths." A pause, considering. "He did not take kindly to me, though I suspect it had little to do with who I am, and more to do with what I am. I have found that those with a deep-seated disdain for alchemists tend to have... unfortunate personal histories with them. Perhaps he is no exception."

She lets that sit for a moment before moving on, unwilling to waste time on irrelevancies. "But our enemies," she says, grey eyes keen, "are not so easily dismissed. You knew Malign before his ascension, he has now become something else entirely. And his sister..." a faint flick of her fingers, dismissive but not entirely so. "What do you know of their powers? Their limits? They will not fall as easily as most."


Malus fully takes possession of the cube, sliding it into a place within the cloak, where it can be secured. A dark cylinder is briefly revealed, but soon obfuscated by fabric. "I shall speak with them and see if they would be willing to have a reintroduction at a better time. Perhaps along with further discussion regarding your knowledge," Malus says. Sensing the return of the server, he falls silent and accepts the beverage without a word. Truly, he doesn't really care what it is, the cup and liquid it contains is only meant as a prop.

He crooks a smile more akin to a smirk. "Ever since my release, everyone believes my intent is to usurp the throne and to kill Malign... or Tyler... or whatever labels people seem to give him." He takes the cup and leans back into his chair. "What would be the purpose in that, so early in this game? Why should I repeat a task which I was already successful in... take over the Empire. There was not much challenge in that, but...." the smirk fades, "slowly crippling his precious," the next words are spat out, "New Sith Order." There is darkness that falls on his features. "Tearing down piece by piece all the lies he has built up around himself. Watching in rage as all crumbles around him. And then the fear, welling up within him, as he sees the hopelessness of his situation." There is a deep inhale and a slow exhale, as Malus tangibly enjoys such a scenario playing out. "When he has no more, then whatever he is, whatever he will become, can be snuffed out by whomever wishes it. Vadim... you... it matters not to me."


If Malus wishes to believe Mitra's understanding of his motivations to be as shallow as that of the lesser minds who whisper about his inevitable coup, she will allow him the indulgence. In the end, it makes no difference. Men like Malus prefer to be underestimated by their enemies, and overestimated by their allies. If it serves her purposes, then she will play along. But the malice in his voice as he speaks of dismantling the Imperium... now /that/ she appreciates. /That/ is something she can use. She watches him as he describes his methodical destruction of Malign's world, the collapse of his empire, the fear that will take root in the pit of his stomach as he realizes that his foundation was never so solid as he imagined. And when the smirk fades from Malus' face, when he inhales deep and slow like a man savoring the last dregs of a rich wine, she speaks: "Where would you most like to begin?"


"There are opportunities presenting themselves," Malus begins his answer. In the short time he has been released from stasis, he has already began assessing the state of the galaxy. "By releasing me, you have already unsteadied the Imperium and I must continue to take advantage of it. Skawn and I will be traveling to Chandrila shortly. I am sure he will do whatever it is that suits him, but I simply intend to make my presence there known." He pauses a moment curling his fingers around the cup. "If Vichten and the Imperium reacts the same way at Chandrila, as he did here on Nar Shaddaa, then we have established a pattern of behavior. One that can be exploited."


She listens, hands folded loosely in her lap, grey eyes steady on him as he speaks of Chandrila and his intent to establish patterns in the Imperium's responses. A part of her finds this approach inefficient; why seek out breadcrumbs when more decisive action could be taken? But she does not let that thought linger too long. Malus is newly released, a man who has been locked away while the galaxy moved on without him. If he needs to stretch his legs -- to test the waters of his re-emergence -- Mitra will allow it. For now.

Besides, this grants her time. Time to see to matters of greater importance, to set the next pieces in place. He does not yet know that there is another, a shadow still locked away, awaiting release.

"Very well. You will hear from me soon," she tells him, tucking a strand of golden hair behind her ear as she rises from the table, not bothering to glance back as she makes her way through the den of killers and out into the dark streets of Nar Shaddaa.

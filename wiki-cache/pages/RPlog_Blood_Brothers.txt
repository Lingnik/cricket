Lounge - Edgecrusher

This is the main ring of the Edgecrusher. With walls as black as space itself, there really isn't much in the way of color in here. A long black couch lines the wall on the east side of the room and various equipment and other paraphernalia line some of the shelves. There is a booth and table along the western side for playing Sabaac and holochess, along with a number of control consoles with flashing lights; designed to monitor the engineering of the ship. A tall, durasteel cabinet is bolted to the wall beside the couch. Lining the shelves on the upper portion and in the cabinet in the lower portion are many bottles of whiskey, imported from several different planets, but the ones that are seen most are the dark glass bottles of Tatooine ale. This room is clearly used for lounging around and relaxing, but can also be cleaned up quite nicely for casual meetings.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There was, of course, the standard method of getting a hold of Krackor Reincus, famed smuggler. But, that could take days or weeks, depending on the backup the man might have in his queue and Drax Rendolen was in a bit of a hurry. Besides, there was something about that old code and frequency pair from the days of the Knights of Korriban that was just calling out to him, begging to be used. The usual meeting place requested; a little known oasis amidst the chaos of an asteroid field not far off of Corellia. Just on the edge of it, the sleek form of the Arcadia waits for any sign of his counterpart arriving so that he might enter the center of the clearing at the same time and dock.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;An empty area of space inside the asteroid field's safe zone distorts; suddenly twisting and bending upon itself moments before a sleek ILC erupts from it's center. The space-black ILC, designated Edgecrusher, slows as it reverts to realspace. Behind the controls, veteran smuggler Krackor Reincus scans the system to see that the Arcadia has already arrived and is apparently waiting for him.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It had been a long time since he'd been contacted with those old codes and frequencies and the veteran smuggler had been very tempted to ignore the call and disappear for awhile once again. However curiosity had gotten the better of him and here he was. He grinds a cigara out into an ashtray built into his ship's console and then sends a hail to the other ship.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Show off," Drax mutters under his breath before goosing the throttle a bit to drift forward toward the Edgecrusher. Leaning forward in his captain's chair, he flicks on the comms to respond to the hail and try opening up a channel with the other vessel. "Hey there, friend. Fancy a parlay? Got something I'd like to talk to you about. Got a lot to catch up on." With just the momentum from the initial burn done, the Kale keeps drifting forward slowly, her weapons and shields are each powered down as a sign of good faith.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Sure, I've got time to chat. Sure beats the sightseeing out here." Krackor replies. He keeps his ILC in place as the Arcadia begins to move closer. His own shields and weapons are also powered down, so Krackor unlocks his docking tube to allow the other ship to connect. "Just come on over once you're hooked up. We can have a drink and discuss whatever you've got going on."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Drax cracks a grin and laughs into his headset. "Glad to hear it. It always did..." By now, the form of the ILC had grown considerably outside the large cockpit canopy and the former snubfighter pilot works the maneuvering thrusters deftly to swing the back end of the Kale around, stabilize it, and slow his way in to mate the two vessels. "Be right over." He flicks a few switches to button up the vessel and then works his way aft and over toward the airlock. There's that moment of hesitation before hitting the button to raise the door. That age old question of what would really be on the other side.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The airlock door flies out of the way with the smooth *woosh* it always had, revealing the form of Drax Rendolen with his pilot's gloves still on. One hand rests on his hip, his thumb hooked onto his belt while the other is still returning from the button as he strides forward. "Geez, really taking the black theme to heart here, eh? How you been?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;While he waits for the other man to link the ships and make his way over, Krackor busies himself by unlocking his liquor cabinet and setting out some glasses. They're probably clean. Should be, anyway. Finally, the airlock slides open. "What can I say; it goes with everything." Krackor says in response to Drax's comment upon entering. "I can't really complain. Business is up and as far as I know, no one is trying to kill me. Life is good." He gestures to the liquor cabinet and the glasses. "Help yourself. I've got a pretty good selection." he says, pouring himself a glass of some dark liquid.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Green eyes drop to the glasses and Drax holds up a hand to wave off the liquor. "I'm back on the repulsorwagon. Caf machine?" The Alderaanian's grin takes more ground on his cheek as he nods his head. "Indeed it does." Stepping further into the lounge of the Edgecrusher, he turns to either side and takes stock of it, eventually coming to stop in front of the sofa as his eyes wander back over toward that liquor cabinet and lingering there to take inventory of it. "That's always good to hear. I think I can more or less say the same, but who really knows until someone comes out and takes a shot at you, right? Still, things have been pretty good lately. I really can't complain. So where've you been keeping yourself all these years?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Krackor shrugs and closes up the cabinet as Drax declines a drink. He does leave the bottle he's pouring from out, though. "Ah, well. To each their own, I suppose. I think I've got some spice, if that's more your thing. Carsunum? Glitterstim? Deathsticks?" He offers, though he's pretty sure the other man isn't interested, so he moves on. If Drax wants something, he'll speak up. "Oh I've been all over. Stayed on the move for awhile after everything with Malign fell apart." he says, doing his best to stifle a shudder at the name. "But then I got bored and decided it was time to come back. Recently I've been spending a lot of time on Corellia. I think I might call it home for the time being." He pauses to take a drink before continuing. "What about you? Haven't seen much out of you in awhile, either. Seems like you've done alright for yourself over the years. What made you decide to reach out to me all of a sudden?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Still grinning, Drax once again waves a hand and settles onto the sofa with a tug on each of his pantlegs to keep the lines clean. "Corellia, huh? More or less the same for me, for the past month, at least. Before that... well, Malign decided to give me a going away present of a stasis tube before disappearing. Nice of him, eh? On the up side, I look about 6 years younger than I should." Sitting back on the sofa, he spreads an arm out along the back and crosses one leg over the other. "You now, I'm a bit worried about a very close friend of mine. Ran off with a hair-brained idea of lone-wolfing something and I've got a bad feeling about it." As he's talking, Drax rummages through his pocket and pulls out a small disc holoprojector. He taps the button to activate it and tosses it down on the cushion next to him where the image of Andreya Carvalo coalesces in the air. The image is just coming into view as Drax's eyes focus in on the veteran smuggler.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Good ol' Malign. Always looking out for his friends." Krackor says blandly. Clearly, he harbors some hard feelings for the Sith Lord. "Well, I guess that's one way to look at it. Maybe you'll live longer than all the rest of us." He leans against the ship's bar and draws a cigara from his coat pocket as Drax goes on explain why he reached out to the smuggler. As soon as he lights up, the ship's ventilators kick in to draw the excess smoke away. He leans in as the holoprojector is activated and studies the image. "Looks familiar." he says after a moment. "Didn't she work for Karrde?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Breathing in through the nicostick that's been hanging from the corner of his mouth, flopping about wildly as he's been speaking, Drax pulls it away and nods his head. Smoke comes pouring out of his mouth as he says, "Indeed. We both used to work for Karrde back in the day. Incidentally, Malign cut my employment short and nearly killed me there as well. Just the gift that keeps on giving, that man." With the nicostick between two fingers, he points with them toward the image. "Anyways. Word has it someone gave her a lift some place. I've taken a bit of a shine to her and am looking to make sure she's still breathing at the end of her little vendetta." His nicostick comes back in as he takes a drag and leaves it hanging from the corner of his mouth. "Needless to say, I don't expect it to be free. And I'm sure that no one wants to give up a client. But I need that destination."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Krackor pulls an ashtray from behind the bar and passes it to Drax. "Like I said...good ol' Malign." he says, shaking his head and going back to the main topic. "You said she's off to take care of something herself. Maybe she doesn't want help, eh? I worked alongside Karrde myself, and I never knew the guy to hire anyone who couldn't handle themselves." he suggests over the rim of his glass. He then takes a drink, only wincing a little at the strength of the liquor. "Besides, if she's gone to all that trouble, I'm sure she paid well to keep it quiet. If the guy who gave her that lift is worth his credits, I'd wager you'd really have to grease that hyperdrive to get any information."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Absolutely true." Drax stands up from the couch now with a surge of energy, taking the ashtray as he goes. He taps off his ash quickly and holds the nicostick between two fingers, "Thing is, the sabacc table is on tilt. She's playing angry right now and, I don't know about you, but I haven't made my best decisions in times like that." The Alderaanian is walking around the room now, talking things through out loud. "Hell, I worked for Karrde. I can damn well handle myself in a fight just fine. But didn't have my head on straight either when I went after Malign the last time. Rat bastard actually infused his own genetic code in with mine and attached a clone of his own arm onto me. Hurt like a Bantha making tender love to a womprat." The former StarOps General is paused in front of the liquor cabinet, eying it for a moment before turning back to Krackor. "She doesn't know what she's doing. This ain't the kind of job she pulls and she's not going to realize that until she has to do it. If it's money, you know I've got it. If it's a favor, you know damn well I can handle myself on a job or provide connections in the NR. Hell, make it both."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Krackor watches Drax closely as the man rises from the couch and paces the room. He alternates between the cigara and the glass as he listens to what the other man has to say. His brow rises at the mention of the clone arm and the genetic code infusion comment causes an involuntary growl. The Sith did the same to Krackor. Sometimes he still felt this awful itching under his skin that no amount of scratching, booze or spice could sate. Ultimately, its not the concern for the woman that sways him. Its the shared trauma brought by the Sith. Well, that and the offer of credits or connections. He lifts his glass and knocks out the remaining liquid inside. "Ok so here's the deal. I did give your girl a lift. I got a feeling you might've already known that, but guys like me have to stick to certain code, you know? But I'm in a good mood and we go back, so I'll cut you a deal. No credits necessary. I'll tell you where I dropped her, but I want some connections inside the NewRep. I could use someone on the inside who can feed me info or help me out if ever get caught up with customs. Don't know what you do for them these days, but I know you're a guy who can get things done. What can you do for me that'll make it worth my while. She paid well for secrecy."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Drax's grin finally returns at the first bit and he concedes a nod to acknowledge what he had known. "You've got it. A customs hook-up, " The man fixes him with a stare and points toward him, "Nothing over the top on the cargo! But I can help get you on the express lane, get you out of a jam should it come to it, and if I pick up any chatter you'd be interested in, I'll be sure it finds you. I do... consulting for the New Republic. Still have plenty of military connections there." Emerald eyes roll up toward the ceiling for a moment before he pats at his pants pocket, removes a small bag, and then tosses it across the lounge toward the smuggler. "Maybe that'll help ease your conscience a bit. No reason your name even needs to come into it, though. Easy enough to keep that from getting out."

Drax unconceals his Cache of Durindfire Gems.

Drax gives a Cache of Durindfire Gems to Krackor.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Krackor catches the bag as it comes toward him. He tugs it open to take a quick peek inside. "Sounds like a deal to me." he says, making the pouch disappear inside his coat. "I got a message a few days ago from your girl about a job. We met up on Tatooine. She said she had some business to take care of. Sounded important, but I don't ask too many questions. Said she had some stuff going on and needed to get to Coruscant. I took her creds and dropped her off."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"You're the man, Krackor." Drax takes one last pull on his nicostick and then stamps it out as he listens to the smuggler run through the series of events. At the mention of the destination, a wicked looking grin curls its way onto the snubfighter pilot's features. "Lovely. You know, the last time the New Republic took that place I worked undercover there behind the lines beforehand to help weaken it ahead of the assault. Figures she'd wind up heading to an Imperial world, but... of all the ones she could have wound up on, that may well be the best ground to have for a fight. Lots of contacts and places to hide out over there." A gloved hand comes up to point toward Krackor and he walks toward him, the hand opening and extending toward him, "I owe you. Drop me a line anytime. Nothing too big or too small."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Krackor takes the offered hand and gives it a firm shake. "Ah, my favorite phrase. 'I. Owe. You.'. Sounds so nice." he says with a grin; releasing Drax's hand. "If you can survive Malign, you can survive Coruscant. Go take care of your girl." he says, clapping the other man on the shoulder. "Just keep me in the loop as far as what the NR is up to and put in a word for me if they ever manage to stop me on a job. Oh, and keep me in mind if you ever find yourself in need of a smuggler."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A firm shake and Drax's other hand comes in to slap him on the arm as he does so. "Yeah, yeah, don't go rubbing it in." The Alderaanian is grinning like the idiot he is as he starts to walk backward toward the airlock. "Yeah, we'll see. Still not done with that first fight, really..." A quick nod of his head and he fires off a two-fingered mock salute toward the veteran smuggler. "I always keep my promises... and you know damn well I wouldn't settle for anything less than the best on a smuggling job, so you'll be my first call. Keep in touch, Brother." Spinning on his heel, he heads through the airlock and swings a fist back to trigger it closed. Minutes later, the Arcadia decouples itself and soars off into the asteroid field at a full burn of her massive engines. Time to arrange insertion into Coruscant; the world at the very center of galactic society.

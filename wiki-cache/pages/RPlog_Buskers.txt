Yan Square - Old Imperial City: Dreven

Standing as the oldest remaining centres of the Old Imperial City, Yan Square stands, along with the Imperial Palace, as an imposing, yet glorious remnant of an empire whose wealth and power spanned the known universe. People use to walk through the square with a humility rooted in trepidation and awe. With the planet having twice shifted hands in the past five years and the Imperium's government having found no great use for the world the square now stands as a symbol of stagnation. Trepidation and awe in the populous now replaced by a people dispirited and downbeat.

Once the center of business and a rival to the Imperial Courtyard in terms of importance, Yan Square is now a poorly kept museum of the past with imposing structures now largely abandoned. A large archway to the West leads to the Bureau of Operations. A colourful collection of buskers have taken root near the arch attempting to scrape together some meagre living.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yan Square's buskers are especially lively tonight. On one side of the arch , the a cappella stylings of the Yan Yodlers are belting out a popular local tune. A metal box with a simple holo-projector spinning "tips appreciated" in Aurebesh sets in front of the quartet. Across the way, at the other side of the arch, a Figrin D'an and the Modal Nodes tribute band, named Figrin Fans, rolls through the normal setlist of all the cantina band hits. About them are a few random solo performers; some performing juggling and acrobatic tricks, while a few female dancers are skirting the edge of propriety with an exotic show. Towards the edge of the entertainers, a few beggars can be found, shaking their wooden bowls and ceramic cups, looking for a handout.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The fact that the Imperium had not made much use of Dreven since regaining the world had been to the great detriment of the local economy which was largely dependent on its role as a thrown world. It also meant the potential of cheap real estate which brought Biscuit Baron's Chief of Operations out to Yan Square to scope out potential locations for a new corporate headquarters. At least that was Veruca Violet's cover story should any attention be paid to her presence.

It also meant the potential of cheap real estate which brought Biscuit Baron's Chief of Operations out to Yan Square to scope out potential locations for a new corporate headquarters. At least that was Veruca Violet's cover story should any attention be paid to her presence.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Attempting to keep a lower profile, her normal expensive fashions were eschewed in favour of a blue shirt and yellow vest which looked like it derived from some mainstream apparel chain. In her company was also a certain Caspian general in a green leatheris jacket and an albino woman, whose own white coat served as effective camouflage as she was rarely seen wearing so much.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The real aim of the trio though was to check in with their agents in the field about a recent happening. As she surveys the scene Veruca comments to the others, "Nice to see some segments of the economy succeeding."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The appearance of some new marks, that should have some credits to spare, draws the attention of some of the performers. While the singers and the band don't move from their prime locations, the more mobile of the buskers make a flashier show to gather the trio's attention. An excitable Bothan cartwheels across their path, ending with a backflip and repeats the same display back across to the spot he's claimed. He bows and extends an arms towards a box with a few meager chits in it. Not to be outdone, a humanoid dancer whose features and race are concealed with gauzy veils twirls scandalously close, perhaps looking to pilfer an item of value, should they elect not to tip her. Not to be outshined, the beggars make a big show of rattling their vessels, hoping to get some meager treasure dropped within.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Veruca pulls out some credit chits from her pocket but makes sure to hold them close. The attention their appearance was getting served the Caspian agent just fine, the more buskers who thought they could get a payday out of her the less likely any would notice the conversations she might have with her own agents here. Who knows perhaps there were future recruits to be had among those who joined the seen.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The woman in the gauzy veils seems to attract the attention of Vanilla who keeps a close eye on her and on her own credits. For Janus' part though he cannot quite dodge the military mannerisms even in street clothes, seem to efficiently catalogue the scene from a tactical stand point while being unmoved by it artistically.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Veruca deposits her first chit with the Bothan, making sure it outdoes what he had already without seeming coming across overly generous, "Are things always so lively here?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Mmmmm... things could get a lot more lively, love," the dancer almost purrs as she twirls about. She unabashedly places a hand on Janus' chest. It's an exceedingly brief and light touch and she twirls away from him and towards the albino, where a hand briefly flits across her back and touches the coat pocket. "Provided that you have the means to pay for such..." and with a final twirl she stops in front of Veruca. The smile radiating from her face, while obfuscated by the veil, can be heard in her tone, "...an experience." The Bothan grins down at the bowl, perhaps not so much that he received a tip, but that the dancer might have also earned one herself in an unconventional, if illegal, manner.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vanilla feigns ignorance at what just occurred with her pocket, simply smiling as the dancer makes her way in front of Veruca. Readying a credit chit to hand to the dancer, Veruca remarks, "I had heard this place was....a gateway to all manner of destinations. Though I had heard there were some obnoxious elements to the crowd here. But it sounds that I heard wrong, everything here is so....delightful."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The dancers giggles. "No, this place is absolutely horrible. I'd much rather find myself on Selene, or Nar Shaddaa... oh think of the credits to be made there. Or even Caspar." She twirls again with another giggle, "But there are still some treasures that can be unearthed here from time to time. Especially with so many off-worlders coming and going lately, though not as lovely as you three." She comes to a stop and sways to the tune of the Figrin Fans, her right hand making a show of slowly moving towards a piece of gauzy cloth that is meant to be easily removed, "Care for a dance? I can show you just how delightful Dreven can still be to its guests."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Well perhaps there may be more off world traffic. We are checking the area for possible corporate office space. Doesn't look like the Bureau of Operations is using theirs." Veruca follows the dancer, she did not care for a dance but she was sure going to pretend that she did. "I imagine we could bring some jobs here, but that all depends on whether folks would feel comfortable doing business here."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Business talk is just so interesting," the dancer casually remarks and twirls while she removes one her gauzy veils attached to her arm. She winks at the Bothan acrobat while rotating herself towards Vanilla. The veils lands across her shoulder and the dancer draws it lightly across the albino woman's neck as she leans in closer. "Especially when it can bring me more clients." She brings her mouth close to Vanilla's ear, her warm breath whispering something in her ear. She giggles and deftly plucks a coin from the albino's coat and twirls over behind Janus, making a show of tucking the object into her brassiere for the Bothan.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Business talk interesting? Yup this was all very clearly a scam, just as it was intended to be. As the dancer seemed to take particular notice in Vanilla, the albino woman decides to make more spectacle of the situation. She takes off her coat and tosses it to Janus, her attire still remains fairly conservative as she is wearing a white collared shirt. She then precedes to make her own bit of dance around the woman whispering something to her as she does.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The dancer gives a little gasp as Vanilla joins her and almost exclaims, "All three of you? Oh, my dear, I shall have to consider that." She disengages from Vanilla with a series a twirls and lightly touches Veruca's arm, "I am sure that I can melt this one." Then another veil is removed, revealing a supple, toned, fair-skinned arm, which is used to pull her into Janus, "And this delicious one might earn you a discount." Finally she twirls back to Vanilla, re-engaging her in a dance. "Hmmm... how about...." she says and whispers the details of their transaction to the albino woman while removing another veil to expose a bit of cleavage to sweeten the deal.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There was a certain irony in investigating a notable person running off with a beggar by negotiating to do much the same. Seeing how the situation shaping up for Vanilla, Veruca pulls out a high denomination credit chit and tosses it to Vanilla, "Have fun." She looks around to some of the musicians, "I am not doing taking in the sounds here." The Pantoran takes the Sarian man by the arm towards the direction of the Yodlers.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Taking the chit she was tossed, Vanilla places it out for the veiled woman to see and smiles, "So lets see what you can do to earn this." It was another irony for Vanilla, paying for it rather than getting paid for it.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The dancer pouts as two members of the trio walk away, "Oh, and I was hoping for a extra bit of fun tonight." For a brief moment there is a subtle change of expression in her eyes, perhaps that of worry. She makes a show of eyeing the chit and waves the veil at Vanilla. "Grab hold and I will lead you to true paradise, my white haired angel. No touching, though. Not until we've settled accounts, then I'm all yours." While she waves the veil for Vanilla to grab on to, she makes a hand signal to the Bothan.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Well they say three's a crowd, so that would make four right suspicious," Vanilla remarks in a low voice as the other two peel away. Vanilla takes the offered veil following the dancer, "Quite the performance miss." For a moment Vanilla found herself missing the days when exotic dance took up more of her own work time.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The dancer makes quite a show of leading off Vanilla, as if bragging to the other buskers of her night's conquest. "This is nothing, angel. After I'm done with you, even the pleasure domes of Zeltros will seem dull." She turns her head, offering Vanilla a salacious smile. After they exist the square and are away from curious eyes, she lets go of the veil and hurries her pace. Along the way she bends down, without breaking stride, to pick up random bits of detritus to soil her garb. "We must hurry," she says, her voice almost a hiss and darts down a side alley where she waits for Vanilla.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vanilla follows close behind the dancer making mental notes of all of her movements. She was here after particular information, but there was also something to be seen in how this woman managed her own part in the proceedings. There were always to be future assignments to consider. She pondered her own cover in this situation, finding some aspects of her own attire she could rip for effect.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Now secure for the time being, the dancer speaks to Vanilla in a low tone. "Epsilon Omega Gamma Omicron," the dancer, now agent, begins. "We don't have much time before my... partner... arrives. Contact was made between a local beggar named Auntie Maya and Ssanyu Natrulon. Auntie's never been any person of note, just your average elderly beggar. When she saw Ssanyu, she went into hysterics. Called her green one, and then by her name." As she gives her brief, she starts to tear her clothing, intending to make it look like a scuffle took place. "Ssanyu was paying Maya no real attention, but then Maya started talking about a "Dark One" returning."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Had Auntie been working that spot very long or mention the Dark One before?" Dark One could mean so many beings, but there were a few particular suspects in Vanilla's might, not least of which was the former employer to the corpse the recently feted. She is not entirely sure why the scuffle ruse in play but gently asks, "Do you want me to hit your or something?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Yes, punch me here," the dancer, now agent, requests, pointing at the corner of her mouth, so that her lip splits open. "Thank the maker you're not an idiot at this. Oh," the dancer holds out her hand, "give me the cred chit and something extra. A bauble, something that looks expensive that I could have ripped off of you." Her brief list of demands only temporarily interrupts her report. "Yes, Maya has been at Yan Square since I arrived. Never acted like that before. She was always a bit strange, but in that harmless kooky old auntie kind of way. She was very insistent that Ssanyu follow her. We think she took Ssanyu back to her little apartment, but couldn't get a tail without risking a break of our cover. We haven't seem Maya since then; it's fair to say she's probably dead. There's a bit more." The agent waits to continue, presumably for the pop to the mouth and the loot she needs to "pilfer".

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Vanilla carefully punches the woman in the manner requested. Intelligence could be a dirty business after all. At least this felt like demeaning then when she had to indulge in someone's kink for violence. "Will an earring do?" Vanilla hadn't exactly come dressed with currency in mind. "Could Maya have been a force user? We can send in a couple of agents to check out the apartment. A couple in particular that would have stood out too much in the square, but I am sure we could deploy them more quietly to that scene."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A bit of crimson begins to well through the veil, the jab successful in its purpose. The agent readjusts the veil to preserve her anonymity, taking the chit and earring, stuffing it in a makeshift pocket made from one of the veils. "Yes, this should be fine. Now, in case we run out of time, here's what you're going to do. You're going to run down the alley and when it dead ends at the tee, you'll toss that veil I led you with to the right, but go down the left. My partner will think you went back to the local security station near the square. You'll go left and stop when you reach a cafe. Order a stimcaf and when you've finished drinking it, it'll be safe to return to the square. He'll be busy hawking this earring." She takes a breath and shrugs, before slumping onto the ground, "Maya did not seem extraordinary in any way. The bassist in Fingrin Fans can show you her place. Ssanyu seemed spooked about the Dark One. Total change in body language. She asked Maya about someone named Axel, but Maya didn't know anything about that. Oh... and she mentioned something about a man in red. This Dark One she was ranting about was pissed off at him, too. Said that he was angry with all his children." The agent's eyes dart to the entrance of the alleyway. "We got just a few moments left before you need to get out. My partner likes to use a stun pistol on my clients."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In another time or place Vanilla might have asked more questions. Did this Maya have next of kin to be informed, friends that would miss her, or was she just one of the galaxy's many forgotten people. Alas Vanilla was no social worker and there was no time for that, and she had heard enough for the picture she needed to be painted. Taking the veil, she looks back to the dancer, "Be careful, if anything starts going wrong ask for extraction." She starts down the alleyway, that goodness these were work shoes and not other work shoes.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Don't worry about me. Just remember throw veil right, you go left. Drink the stimcaf." The farewells complete, the agent starts cursing up a storm, "That bitch! Blast it! That bitch hit me! Krallik! Krallik! Where are you?!?"

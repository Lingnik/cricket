The Grand Atrium rises through the interior of the Celestine, a vast enclosed garden hidden within the luxury liner's armored hull. Slightly humid air carries the scent of flowering vines, damp soil, expensive perfume, and smoke through an elegant chamber crowded with rare botanical specimens gathered from across the galaxy; most are endangered, some even extinct in the wild. Towering fern-fronds arch over polished walkways of black stone, while orchids in impossible shades of cobalt, silver, and crimson spill from hanging gardens suspended several stories overhead. Bioluminescent moss glimmers softly beneath sculpted trees imported at obscene expense from distant worlds, their roots cradled in bronze planters worked into flowing organic forms. Everywhere, water murmurs quietly through hidden channels and shallow reflecting pools, imbuing the immense space with the atmosphere of a luminous greenhouse designed by aristocrats with unlimited resources and no understanding of restraint.

Far above, the atrium's vaulted ceiling spreads in a breathtaking canopy of stained transparisteel and gilded metalwork inspired by the old palaces of a more elegant age. Amber, emerald, and sapphire panes cast warm pools of colored light across the crowds below. Curving bronze supports twist upward like flowering vines, their surfaces chased with delicate leaves, blossoms, and abstract celestial motifs that catch the light in flashes of gold. At the center of the chamber, beneath the glowing canopy, elegantly dressed guests gather around illuminated gaming tables where fortunes quietly change hands over cards, dice, and whispered negotiations. Beyond the gaming floor, layered seating alcoves and secluded lounges disappear behind walls of greenery, allowing diplomats, celebrities, financiers, and predators of every variety to conduct business in luxurious semi-privacy while orchestral music drifts softly through the air.

Though the Celestine is only scheduled for a brief excursion through nearby space before returning its passengers safely planetside, security aboard the liner is visibly formidable. Uniformed personnel circulate discreetly through the crowds, supported by hidden surveillance systems, biometric checkpoints, private security contractors, and enough armed response capability to discourage all but the most heavily funded and ambitious acts of piracy or interdiction.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tonight the atrium stinks of money. It leaks from every conversation, every glittering wrist and lacquered smile, gathering damply beneath the stained-glass ceiling with the heat of the plants. Guests drift in from the ship's various engineered social exchanges: pharmaceutical summits, antique exhibitions, luxury showcases, political seductions disguised as charity galas, and an unknown number of closed-door corporate negotiations.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Johanna hates negotiations, hates their nauseatingly oily texture, the elaborate theatre required to navigate them a source of irritation even after years of practice. The assistant seated to her right, however, seems entirely at ease among the ritual humiliations.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Across from them, Ailah Vieres reclines into the velvet seating as though born there, empress of an empire one would be wise not to examine too closely.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"So why exactly have the rats of Tatooine come scurrying to me?" the older woman begins, "Especially since I distinctly remember something about dirty money and prosecution and threats being made the last time you deigned to suffer my presence."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Yes, yes. Vent your bile...<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I'd like to apologize for the previous unpleasantries," Johanna replies, loathing the words as they form upon her tongue, "It seems I was... too hasty in my ungenerous assessment of you."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ungenerous, really? Smile, woman. Smile.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A tall man walks through the Atrium, dressed in black and dark grey, a black cloak billowing behind him with its hood drawn up over his head. Walking with an aura that seems to subdue, rather than project, he largely passes unnoticed through the grand atrium of the Celestine, though his posture indicates purpose.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;'Ah, there she is,' he thinks as a smirk forms beneath his hooded features and his path alters to walk along the fringes of the atrium, but clearly in view of Johanna. He deliberately slowing his pace, he turns his head towards the woman, letting the glow of his cybernetic eye grab her attention as he slowly walks, that smirk widening into a smile.

Though the most interesting Xi Charrian in the galaxy is most often imagine in factory setting or occasionally huffing gas in a nightclub, any setting is truly his environment. As the Chief Executive Officer of Haor Chall Engineering since the passing of Prelate r'ratak-varattak-n'gavanak, the need to schmooze has only increased. And it just so happens that as Haor Chall has reemerged as one of the leading shipwrights in the galaxy, there in an increased demand for custom jobs. After recently being approached by a corporate bigwig looking for rocking yacht, ak found himself seeking to do some industrial research. The access to political seductions and corporate negotiations was an added bonus. He stands in the middle of the atrium taking in the sights, as a variety of other patrons look quizzically upon him, Xi Charrians not frequently spotted on such vessels outside of Caspian Union space.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Elsewhere in the atrium in "outdoor" cafe seating are a trio of girls. Two Pantorans and a Firrerreo/Sarian. Dressed in fine fashion, the three are on holiday after many month travelling through backwater world to assess the condition of the local Biscuit Baron restaurants, Making sure the local district managers and franchisees were treating their employees well as a daunting endeavour, but the CEO and COO felt their workers were the backbone to their efforts. The political and business intrigues on this ship might soon turn things into a working vacation as scheming does not take a holiday.

You must really be desperate," Ailah smirks, exposing a line of whitened teeth, "But I can't imagine it's money you need; your grubby little paws are in too many pies for that to be the case." Manicured fingers wrap possessively around the elegant cut-crystal glass on the table beside her, the drink raised to pause scant centimeters from lips twisted into a credible approximation of amusement.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Astutely observed," Johanna begins, wondering what it would feel like to bury her fist in Ailah's offensively perfect face, "In fact, I'm --"<br>

I've rather enjoyed the collectible I won at the auction." And then he continues along his way, weaving in and out of view, playing his silly games like this for his own amusement.

As a petulant and demanding child with rich parents who indulged her every whim, such cruises present an opportunity to give their domestic staff a vacation from such hassles and pay cruise ship workers instead. The loss of her parents and much of her wealth, as well as the guidance and reparenting from Zubindi had done much to disabuse of such traits. Fashion was now her one designated over indulgence. Nevertheless her work with Atsvara and Zubindi, along with her own efforts had returned her to great wealth. And the Celestine seemed as good as any place to take a respite from the boys and the droids.<br>

It seemed to her matter of significance that the girls would want to note. Veruca nods back to Euphoria while making sure Atsvara sees as well.

That would imply surprise, and she refuses to grant him the satisfaction of it, though the sudden awareness of him standing at her shoulder sends a sharp pulse of irritation through her already-thinning patience. The man carries himself with the impossible ease of someone either too dangerous or too insane to care that a million-credit bounty hangs over his head. Perhaps he is both. Still... the faintest corner of her mouth threatens mutiny at his interruption of Ailah's sneering little performance.<br>

"Connections are not the same thing as legitimacy. You may dine at the Imperator's table when it suits him, but everyone aboard this ship knows exactly how exposed you are the moment your brother tires of indulging you."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There it is. The knife at last.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nearby the orchestra swells briefly beneath the humid air of the atrium, strings climbing toward some theatrical crescendo, as Johanna's thoughts turn to the mercies of the airlock once more.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A violent metallic crack suddenly splits the air above them. The sound tears through the atrium hard enough to silence conversations mid-word as guests instinctively look upward, some of them frightened.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For one impossible second, everyone sees only a spreading fracture racing across the colored transparisteel canopy overhead like a spiderweb catching gold light. Then the body comes through, the awful conclusion of its descent reached only a moment later.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"A fair point, your highness, though legitimacy through bloodlines often have the weakness of being snuffed out with a flick of the wrist," Malus replies smoothly. A point that is emphasized by the sudden cacophony of above us and a body wetly impacting onto the deck of the Atrium. "Oh my," Malus says in blas  tone. "Life can be so fleeting at times," he remarks and watches with disinterested curiosity. There's no point in trying to identify the corpse, surely the gossip will spread like wildfire in the next few hours.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;ak, Malus, Johanna, all are forgotten for a moment as Atsvara turns to look at the spectacle. Others, of course, are horrified. Atsvara meanwhile turns to Veruca and excitedly states in a somewhat whispered voice, "This wasn't on the brochure....this should have been on the brochure." The girl is entertained. It is not that she does not care about the loss of life, she was after all a staunch supporter of famine aid and labour unions. But she was also raised as Sith. In the first several years of her life nearly everyone she dealt with regularly had been survivors of the New Sith Order. The Sith language was spoken in her home growing up. Bottom line, she lived in a world where sometimes the solution to a problem was a body hitting the floor. She was not sure what the problem had been but any fear this vacation was going to be too boring was put to rest.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Veruca Violet looks at it with more detachment then her younger friend. She had lived through the Sariphage, she had seen genocide level death all around her, including almost everyone she had known. That created compassion, but it also hardened her to the reality of the universe. And while her public persona was in candies, fashion, and restaurants, she was also a member of the Caspian Intelligence Service since its formation when she was not yet twelve, younger than Atsvara is now. She had risen through the ranks, had field command of their station on Dreven. She had given orders that had killed people and had gotten them killed. What is one more strangers body to that?<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Her cousin Euphoria, was, of course, an agent as well. She wasn't even from Caspar, but when you work for the Silverwood House of Fashion, when your cousin is a Station Master, when your aunt is part of the Intelligence Directorate, such a double life was a given. But she worked as stylist and occasional model, most of her work was surveillance, she was not so hardened as the girls were. Her response simple, muted, "Oh."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Then there was ak, a few moments ago the girls were all calling his name. Now the room was full of screams. He looks back to the girls and remarks, "I am confused are people calling my name or screaming. They sound so alike in Basic."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"WHAT THE FUCK?!"<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The exasperated shout escapes Johanna before diplomacy or self-control can intercept it, the polished theatre of negotiation collapsing instantly beneath the sound of shattering glass, screaming guests, and the catastrophic finality of a body striking the gambling floor below. Because OF COURSE this would happen now! OF COURSE a corpse would come crashing through the ceiling while she was attempting to ingratiate herself with people who considered her a barely civilized bit of flotsam! The universe apparently possesses a sense of humor both vicious and extremely specific.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Panic spreads through the atrium in visible waves. Chairs overturn while guests recoil from the spreading constellation of blood and shattered transparisteel. Somewhere nearby, someone is crying. Somewhere else, someone is trying to film the scene.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;But before chaos can mature into a full stampede, the Celestine's security apparatus asserts itself with ruthless efficiency, armed personnel flooding into the atrium from half a dozen concealed access points as a vessel-wide lockdown begins.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The chaos that erupts draws perhaps an unexpected reaction from Malus. Not an opportunity to revel in it, but rather an opportunity to observe. The calm reactions from the Biscuit Baron entourage - interesting. The outburst from Johanna - expected. The general panic and macabre interest of the guests provides some insights. But of particular interest is in Ailah's reaction and whether an opportunity presents itself there. There is a subtle push of the Force, a trivial matter of moving an inconsequential item, enough to cause a ripple - enough to get Johanna's attention. "Do not let an opportunity go to waste," Malus says and then he slowly turns away, wandering through the chaos, staying out of the way while watching. Watching and smiling.

Brigadier V1C3 had officially assessed their girls trip to be a "low risk" event, and while he had, nevertheless, attempted to assign at least one member of the Chevalier to them, the girls had declined. They were after all always armed and quite capable of defending themselves. Looking around it did not appear that ak had anyone assigned from the Chevalier to him either. Not that such people and droids where always visible, but she was in a position to know what and who to look for.<br>

I am sure there is a relevant regulation that would apply in this situation."<br>

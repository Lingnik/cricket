&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The KDY Spaceview rests high above the domed city below and connects directly with the Shipyard megastructures above the planet spanning around the planet in a ring-like structure. The top floor of the Imperial City's tallest structure thus extends far into space though entry into the shipyard's is currently closed and managed by a security detail.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The entire floor of the building is decorated for a social gathering of the New Order's elite and a list of guests brought by invitation of the politically connected. An additional hundred tickets were auctioned and the credits were distributed to charities including the mission to rebuild Caspar and help the recovery of CDU space.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Moffs, high ranking military and intelligence officers mingle enjoying drinks and discussing their plans for their assigned sectors and other matters of state.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tyler Damion stands with his usual cadre of Drayson Honos and the recently introduced Fleet Admiral Darrian Fel'ya and General Hsskah Agileclaw. They stand in a small circle talking quietly, occasionally pausing to laugh, in some cases sharing old war stories. The Imperator perhaps feeling fatigued from the pleasant exchanges excuses himself from the military brass and finds a quieter corner of the room overlooking the domed city below, Sentinel not far behind keeping a watchful eye on his master and unsettling nearby guests.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Getting into the fete was easier then getting into Imperial space in realistic terms, the new Regime hardly had time to review the millions made outlaw by the select committee and Nasiri just waited out the big spenders at the auction who wanted their invitations first. Auction was actually ideal for him as it allowed him a certain amount of anonymity in his appearance. He suspected that he might fare better with the new Imperator, then he did with the select committee. But that was only a hunch, Sith were by all word and suggestion mercurial in the mood and actions.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;So thus stood Nasiri, by some definitions little more than an animal in Imperial space. But here he was and he was dressed in his usual robelike fashion and quickly looking to get a drink in his hand. At his elbow was his plus one, the top talent from the pleasure palace. In both appearance and skill. she was herself giddy at the notion of being taken to such a highly placed event and perhaps the Falleen himself was just a bit excited for being at the coronation of an Emperor.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A light music begins to play over the room. A live band from Clak'dor VII, plays a series of soft selections of whatever the galactic equivalent of jazz is as the party seems to continue. Staff bring out additional selections of some of the finer alcoholic choices placing them politely on tables, while other waitstaff carry trays with glasses free for the taking. Outside the viewport one of the sentry Imperial Mark 2 Destroyers makes a gradual approach toward the Skyview for the patrons to view. The New Imperium symbols are projected onto its hull as it fires off modified flack that explode into a multitude of colors that seems to be timed with the music inside.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Imperator Damion observes with Sentinel, keeping most of the sentients at bay with the alchemically modified Wookiee's presence alone enough to intimidate most of the room. He nods to himself and turns to the room after a few moments. The music stops and an officer in the new Imperium's uniform steps forward and holds up his glass:<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Gentle Beings, I am honored to present the Imperator of the New Order. Tyler Damion first to the Damion Dynasty, long may he reign!" The room erupts into applause.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tyler steps forward and waves to the crowd, "Enjoy the refreshments and the company. No one is going to throw us out, I know the owner." The crowd offers a polite laugh, "Glory to the New Order, Glory to our Imperium!" he raises a glass and most follow suit in a salute and drink.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The music and the festivities renew and the social mixing and politics begin anew. The Sith Lord now receiving his guests and accepting small gifts from regional Moffs as symbols of their loyalty.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian Tarasar usually enjoyed parties quite a bit. It was where he had tended to shine in the Sith court....also where he projected the persona that had potential rivals dismiss him as unthreatening to them. The Imperium court offered similar possibilities, but he was also here as a representative of Caspar so to speak and made the rounds to thank some of those who had bought the auctioned tickets that were going to benefit the Mission. A lot of idle small talk, with the purposeful casual remarks confiding in some of the donors that Imperator Damion had been quietly supporting aid efforts for sometime. He had already left encrypted files on the Mission's servers that would indicate Tyler and a number of other top figures in the new regime here had donated money and supplies. The Imperator, the Grand Admiral, etc. even Sentinel would be credited with a donation to his fellow Wookiees on the world. Crestian as a skilled cryptographer had naturally left certain vulnerabilities in the file that would allow slicers to get 'lucky' in their hack. Crestian had already crafted the response the Imperium spokespeople would give when contacted for comment. His mechanisations were not as grand as the Imperator's but similarly advanced for their scale. Spotting various Moffs paying homage to Tyler, Crestian began to work out when the best time to join the queue would be.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nasiri ponders a few moments as the brief speech is made and both he and his companion offer applause and the falleen even raises his glass to the chorus of Glory to the Imperium. He did consider a gift, but it was still on the battered Telgas at port. To assume one would actually speak to a rising Emperor was presumptuous at best, still he did inquire with a staff person about sending a bolt of black spider silk cloth from his own pets to the Emperor. He nods as the staff suggested he would look into it. Dewna, his companion had apparently seen something at the tables the tempted her. A quick peck on the Falleen's cheek and she was off to raid the morsels arrayed around the room.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It's comical, really: a polite social gathering on a ruined world with a self-proclaimed Imperator, his lackeys and assorted "lucky winners" milling about, all eager for their chance to lick Malign's ass and secure their footing in the new Imperium. Johanna would just as soon have stayed isolated in her room, drinking and practicing ancient Sith scrying rituals to spy on her traitorous brother from afar, obscured by a veil between watcher and watched... but prudence dictates that she get a grip and reconnoiter in person. Feigning civility comes easily enough, after all, when her children aren't involved. She exits the turbolift composed, nothing in her expression betraying the fact that she might be having stray thoughts of flying her X-Wing up here and firing a well-placed volley of laser fire into the building, vaporizing some unfortunate souls on contact and sending the rest outside to suck vacuum. Lovely damn soiree, indeed.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;An aide stands behind the Imperator and carefully collects the gifts handed to them by Malign as he thanks each Moff, VIP, and sector commander in kind and shares a brief few words and they move back into the crowd. The throne was going to be a challenging place for Tyler who had spent much of his leadership of the Sith Order and the Empire before behind the veil of his military dictatorships, now he was forced to be in the limelight to some degree and to carry a public face when it was necessary. Eventually, much of this would dissolve back into regional and local responsibilities and he would move the core of his actions and activities to his Command Ship which would arrive soon, leaving Selene in the capable hands of Drayson Honos and its appointed Moff.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Oh this is a very nice necklace." Tyler nods to another regional governor and politely examines it before passing it to an aide for scanning and cataloging it as a Dynasty treasure. Some of the newer generations of Moffs were non-human, and as they arrive the Imperator seems to visibly relax. These were likely much closer to his inner circle, beings that were replacements for Thel supporters, bringing their respective sectors additional liberties and freedoms.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Johanna's arrival, Crestian, and even the rogue Falleen are noted in Tyler's mind as the line begins to thin before him after a rather exhausting series of exchanges, "I need a drink." He says finally and one is provided almost immediately.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nasiri picks his way among the guests and watches eyes and listens to tones, there were no doubt jealous sorts and those with envy. Such was the way with power and his recent time spent in far to many Hutt courts, garnered him the habit of watching for the less benignly inclined in social gatherings. There were always the tell tales, those that didnt quite have the bid their time mentality down. The arrival of the alien moffs arrived, prompt a brief look of approval from the Falleen and then a quick scan to see what Dewna was up to. Clearly she had caught the eye of an officer who was chatting her up at the buffet and Nasiri just chuckles softly, she was good.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Imperator's predicament was not off of Crestian's radar either. He had known the Sith Lord long enough and well enough to appreciate the duality of his nature. He was still somewhat Human, capable of partaking in social pleasantries and other behaviour deemed to be normal of sentient biological beings. But much ran beyond what he saw as his boss' comfort level. He was playing a part, a far deeper and more involved cover than the normal subterfuge and half truths Sith told to those they dealt with. He quite expected the Imperator would be glad when he needed to do less of this, but he also appreciated the fact that he took doing it seriously and understood its necessity in larger picture. In time when the politics settled down Crestian hope he would be able to help lift some of this burden from him. Not only as he secured his own position in the Imperium but simply because he had a greater comfort level in such gatherings and his own nature help project an image that befit the friendlier Imperium. There would be other figures that would fill such roles too, Drayson certainly, Johanna if she chose, but some of these new Moffs would become the faces of this new Imperium. He eyes the important figures and begins to make mental notes of who would be profiled in the line of Jolly Meal toys he would arrange Biscuit Baron to release when things settled down.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Have you come for some scraps, Lorda?" Johanna inquires tartly once soft footfalls have brought her within polite speaking distance of the Falleen, "Or are you hoping for something more substantial from the Imperium? You'll have to fight the other carrion-eaters here for it, I'm afraid." She reaches for an expensive brandy on a nearby table, her black-clad form shifting its weight to one hip as she briefly watches him over the rim of her glass before taking a sip. "Mmm," she notes, "They sprang for the good stuff, there may yet be hope that we little people are able to catch whatever falls off the tables of our betters." How she loathes Malign's self-aggrandisement... their bargain had been to destroy Vadim, not have Malign declare himself Imperator so openly. Johanna is accustomed to working in the shadows, after all. "I see Admiral Tarasar is basking in his new-found importance."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nasiri turns an amused gaze at the arrival of Johanna and gives a brief rise of the shoulders. "I came to see...for the most part." he admits and takes a sip from his drink. "My history with the Empire has never been profitable...in point of fact." he gestures to her with his glass. "I am here to support whatever plot you have in the works that has brought you here." he passes a gaze back to the crowd. "But, I am always watchful for a chance to make profit...as are you..." he teases her quietly and grabs another drink as it passes. "The fall of the select committee...is a bit of joy to me."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As much as Johanna loathes the self-aggrandisement, Malign is in his own form of self-loathing as he pushes his own distaste down to continue the formal pleasantries. Eventually Drayson and his war council could take over the day to day and he could return to his work, but events were moving at a pace he had not expected. Many of the former Imperial worlds were left without much direction with the fall of Aldus Thel, the speed the knees had bent to the New Order required drastic action, or so that was their rationale. Now, the lines were being drawn for a coming civil strife with whatever loyalists remained and the newly founded Imperium.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tyler moves away from the greetings area and Drayson steps in as a surrogate quickly finishing the pleasantries with the remaining Moffs. The Dark Lord sulks toward a table to grab additional refreshments, approaching Nasiri and Johanna, "Why fight for scraps when the table is full spread before you? There is enough for everyone, the worlds represented here are not by any stretch of the imagination poor."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He lets his eyes move toward Crestian, "Well most of the worlds that is." Malign had never had much love for the Caspian Democratic Union or its worlds, but he was not the type to block anyone's ambitions, a lesson Johanna learned from her eldest. Now he rests two heart beats from the throne, right after Sonja Damion.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I could have done with a little less overt importance for a while longer," the Sarian remarks as he happens into range of Johanna, "But a wise philosopher once said, 'Adapt or Die.' I am bigger form of the former, like to reserve the latter for others, and with as little of that as possible, give or take." Crestian, of course, had little love for the old CDU. His own family left Caspar as the government formed and moved to Chandrila, his father had felt the attempt to stand alone foolish, a position Crestian always agreed with. But the times had changed and Caspar and the space of the CDU had the capacity either contribute to one side in the growing conflict or be dominated by others. Crestian preferred to be the one to dictate its faith. And as foolish as he considered his people to be. They were his people. Good Sith did not allow themselves too many attachments, practicality in the face of wars were more important than nostalgic concerns, but it was strategicly located area of space."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A couple enters the room, though it is unlike most couples here. The man is young, his hair black just like his red collared uniform. Kipran Jawatha, one of the newest commissioned Commanders, holds his elbow out to be taken by the woman next to him as they enter the party. Beside him, petite, with upswept black hair that shows her strands of silver off in a most intriguing manner, is his mother. Clad all in red velvet Elana Tracer looks the polar opposite of the distraught mother who landed on Selene not too long ago. For one thing, she had had time with Kipran. On the other, she knew how to play the high stakes game she was dipping her toes into. She smiles coolly as the two traverse the room, nodding to those who give them slight bows. As they come upon a passle of other young officers, Tracer smiles then kisses Kipran's cheek softly and he her hand as she lets him depart to be with his mates in the corp. A few steps further along finds her not far from the table where Malign has encamped. Elana snatches a glass from a passing tray and moves to the transparasteel view, not put off by the height or openness in the perpetual night sky. A far away look settles in on her distinct features as she runs a finger along the edge of her glass making it sing slightly.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"My only aim is to get my son to return home with me," the Jedi replies levelly, lest Nasiri get any ideas about profitable subterfuge being the impetus behind her visit, "And I--" She stops herself as Malign joins them, choosing to choke back her contempt with another mouthful of brandy before trusting herself not to say something outrageous in public. "Imperator," Johanna greets, lofting a dark brow as she pronounces the foreign-sounding title, "Lorda Gejalli and I won't be fighting each other for anything." Then, with the arrival of Crestian: "Oh? Well, you certainly had /me/ fooled, Admiral. You 'adapt' a little too well for me to believe your 'overt importance' is anything other than welcomed and enjoyed. I suppose years hiding in a nebula does that to a man."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nasiri tilts his head to the side at Johanna and then when the Tyler arrives. "Imperator." he gives a slight polite bow. "I am not sure what a person such as I could offer the Empire and gain profit from the tables..." his tone is soft and measured, but he does cut a gaze back to Johanna with a bit of a questioning in the gaze. He is wondering how Etiel plays into this.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Sister." Malign states politely, "Lorda Gejalli." He intones toward Nasiri. Johanna's words were like cold daggers and what was unspoken tore at his essence as he stood before the group and Crestian arrives, "Adapt or die. The Damion family has had a target on its back for some time now. Perhaps even Moff te Danaan will explain to his mother when a certain spirit came looking for him and his uncle was there to help shield him from it." He shrugs. He had a selection of barbs for Johanna's own sense of justice and the spread of it through the Outer Rim and into the Coruscanti Underworld. But she was no fool to her own path. For now the Imperator holds his tongue and decides to let a mother go through the process of grief. What is certain is that no one was kidnapped or turned to anyone's will, and that in itself was likely more torturous. The Damion line was one that would always be tainted with the legacy of its progenitor and the art of the Sith.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;His hazel eyes shift to Nasiri, "I am man of production and progress, profit has no meaning to me ultimately. I understand its value only in that others value it. I am keenly aware that some like to accumulate wealth, I am more interested in other pursuits." He smiles, "That is not to say agreements and understandings cannot be reached." He pauses, "Everyone here is in their place by choice and are free to leave when they wish, the Imperium has no need for hostages."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He turns to Crestian, "Is not righteous indignation the mean between envy and spite? Let's commit to a greater understanding of self and others," He raises a glass, "To gathering the means of reducing some of the chaos left in the wake of a failed regime, and to creating a space for other goals."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He drinks as his eyes fall toward Tracer and her son, Malign had begun ironically collecting young Force Adepts. He had not planned for it and yet they come.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian nods in agreement with Tyler most particularly the statement about understanding. The Imperator had always appealed to the Sarian's academic side and continued to do so. He does find himself amused by Johanna's remarks and finds himself thinking less about the mood she is coming from but it has truly meant to transition from a decade in relative seclusion to what he 'enjoys' now. "You are right Lady Johanna that is agreeable to not have my life centred in Dragonflower, but it was my home and a home by choice. It offered safety to myself, my family, and my crew for many years. It required sacrifices, but then military life always does even here in this atmosphere, we all make concessions for the job."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Taking a drink from a passing server he adds, "I certainly do not mind a limelight in and of itself. I merely meant to reflect on some of the complications it has presented in the time table in Caspar. I would have much preferred a version of history that saw no Sariphage strike there and thus no need of my return there. I could be happy enough working with the staff here in the Core or out on the Outer Rim. Caspar requires an approach that is different then it would be elsewhere. I would have preferred more time to shore up things there. But the party is still polling well and I have been told that Presav Lameberchain has recalled the provisional assembly from recess. The new constitution may well be ratified tonight, possibly tomorrow."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nasiri raises a brow at the exchange and does his best to remain neutral in the face, Johanna's ire was up and he wanted no part of adding to that miasma.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Profit has no meaning to you, Imperator?" Johanna scoffs, leaning back into the high heels of her boots and finishing her drink before placing the glass back on the table and folding her arms across her chest, "Oh really? That's funny, I remember you finding /plenty/ meaning in profit when it was my money funding your schemes... and /his/ schemes..." Here she gestures to Crestian with a gloved hand, corners of her mouth downturned in displeasure, ".. and funding the deal with that loathsome scientist to save Caspar from its plague. It was my /profits/ that provided the money for the research and the cure, my /profits/ that kept the Leviathan flying, your personal fighter maintained, and your crew paid. But sure, please go ahead and explain how above money you are now that you've seized the throne. It was all your doing through Sith magic, right?" She snorts in disbelief, shaking her head at the utter gall of it.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Johanna's indignation continues without missing a beat though the redirection from her son to the use of her fortune seems almost amusing to Malign. What he said to Nasiri was indeed truth; the need for funding was built on how the galaxy worked and how they were managing in that economic mode, now that the might of a multisystem Imperium was before them with the opulence of its splendor burning radiant.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Indeed, the wealth you accumulated did achieve great things." He gestures, "With a little sponsorship we can now begin to repay our debts, though strange how you want compensation or glory for the Caspian debacle. Isn't saving those poor beings enough?" Tyler shakes his head, "Resources and power are the currencies of this galaxy. Credits are a means, and meaningless in the grand scheme of things." He gestures upward toward the shipyards, and around to the Moffs, "Now we have the resources. If you want to be reimbursed for our efforts to set the galaxy right.. send Drayson the bill." He scoffs as well.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As he speaks a flash from outside occurs. It was the most ironic of gestures as the dark hull of an Imperial Command Ship suddenly blinks into existence just outside. It was enough for the Sith Lord to begin laughing, "No, sister, better yet send your bill to Moff te Danaan. He has the resources to pay them now. It is all pointless. We have a large mission ahead of us." His laughter stops and he grow serious. "Family First, Johanna. The Threat is real. We are all doing what we must to protect our blood. I will burn a thousand Aldus Thel supporters, I will push through every obstacle to assure our victory over our common enemy-" he pauses, "-including take over a Galactic Imperium so when we move its guns will not be pointing at us."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tracer's maternal reverie is interrupted when she glimpses Malign, Tarasar, the loud woman from the night of her arrival, and of all things a Falleen. It was a crazy mixed up Imp circus Tyler was Ringmaster of here. Definitely no longer the Selene of her brief time as a Moff-under-Protest during the CDU Annexation. They'd brought her here to make sure she knew her role and would stick to it under threat of her children's lives. It was here she'd also learned the man she loved had fallen back in with the Imperials. Her heart aches at the long ago memory and she looks away briefly before steeling herself for the short walk to a necessary evil. The woman slows her approach to assure enough of a lull in the conversation to steal the Sith's attention briefly.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Imperator," Tracer inclines her head and does a perhaps cheeky little dip of respect. "And...Admiral, is it?" she asks looking to Crestian. She wasn't sure what he was really since she thought him just an arse kissing philanthropist up until she had a more in depth talk with her son who explained to her the order of this New Order. She next looks on Johanna and says, "And...I'm sorry, we haven't formally met...did I interrupt something?" she asks, looking around the table calmly. Tension too thick for her vibro to cut is perceived and she flushes slightly.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"The people of Caspar will be eternally in your debt Lady Johanna for your support with paying that....mercenary....Starker." Crestian makes no mention of her support for their other endeavours least of all his ship. He had kept it running and in good working order in a nebula for a decade without her. Sure they were conserving power and not jetting about places, but they were also keeping its remaining existence hidden all that time. Silver Station was the semi-public base of operations. Leviathan was only available to the loyal. Turning to Presav Tracer, Crestian remarks, "Yes Admiral would be my title in this setting. But you may feel free to call me Crestian madam if you wish." Crestian did love all his growing collection of titles, but he also enjoyed employing a congenial atomsphere amongst his peers that saw no need for it. He only really got annoyed at that maker forsaken Sar Admiral that kept referring to him as if he were a mere Captain. Hers is one death he does not mourn an iota.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Johanna's eyes blaze dangerously when Malign accuses her of wanting reimbursement or glory for something she had done out of compassion. It is his blithe suggestion of sending Etiel a bill, however, that causes her right hand to ball into a fist as she considers her options, the leather around her knuckles creaking softly. Oh, how satisfying it would be to smash her brother's face in... here, in front of all assembled! The crunch of bone and spurts of blood would almost be soothing to her jangled nerves. She eyes the distance to a perfect strike, the Force beginning to stir around her, Tracer's arrival not registering fully...<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"What's this I hear about someone sending me a bill?" Etiel inquires politely from behind Johanna, having sensed the impending violence a few minutes earlier and making it his business to keep her from embarrassing herself (and him) further, his arrival in the room not a moment too soon, "Imperator, did I fail to account for an expense?" He's all polite smiles, a calming hand placed on his mother's shoulder.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It's enough to drain the fighting impulse from the brunette, the tension leaving her frame as muscles relax and allow for a more neutral posture. The Force ebbs back into the aether; crisis averted. "It's nothing, I'm sure," she finally sighs, turning to regard her son with a thousand unspoken pleas. "We should probably get going."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The tension was thick, the conditions for conflict were beginning to boil, Sentinel smells it and he and his mutant cohorts were already on the scene in a flash standing behind their master. Drayson was another with a weird sense about these things and was working to close the distance as things are boiling and sparks were preparing to fly, for the Bith band's part the music kept going and most of the party goers were distracted with that. Agents of the Sith were trained to see threats and Johanna was registering as one.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For Tyler's part, he remains stoic after giving a rant. He even feels the pangs of guilt for his outburst at the woman who had done so much to help him. His essence bled guilt toward her, and he deflates his chest and takes a step back. Malign's bones did not break easily since they were alchemically altered beyond recognition, but Johanna could have definitely tested his alchemical prowess with the energies she was tuning toward him, the Sith simply drops his guard as if to let it all occur. Sentinel and the other Wookiees around begin to tighten their muscles sensing violence their claws slowly extending.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Then, Etiel's presence washes over the moment and in an instance everything seems to clear; the Sith, the mutants.

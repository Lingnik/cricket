Goetia Garth - Rive Ga'uche: Nar Shaddaa

Around the boundaries of the Goetia Garden runs a line of Cloisters for people to sit unobserved and share private moments or discreet conversations. The maingate arch is constructed of Velmstone statues of humanoid forms, holding a glow globe overhead as the peak of the arch formed by their by their uplifted arms. Polished cinnabar flags stone wind red pathways throughout the garden. A massive collection of Giant Felucian mushrooms form a forest at the center of the garden, giving the heart of Goetia a purple glow. Scattered about the garden are massive stones covered in Kass Lichen, here and there are high back benches for visitors to relax and lean back into and take in the view. The Basin levee is lined with Zabrak spine, vine plants giving off a red glow to mark the safe edge of the garden. Exotic ferns of rich green spread out in the garden and are broken by Anxynth, or more commonly called Nightmare blooms and Darkkoninn berry bushes. The combination of plants gives the entire garden a pleasant aroma that nothing from the city intrudes on.

The garden is well lit for the moment, floating above it. A large sphere drifts around the garden casting a light all around the garden as it floats a programed pattern. Hard to tell if its a droid or just a device, but its function is clear. Light to nourish the plants needing it. The Lorda is in conversation with several aliens, most armed and rough looking. "Everything is fine, tell the Lorda's that if trouble comes calling. they will know shortly after I do." he has one arm cradled beneath the one holding his goblet of wine, a one of them starts to sputter about needing more and is cut off by a flicker of fingers. "They are getting rich faster these days, and yes. We will revisit slave trade at the next meeting."


It seems like a lifetime ago the last time Jessalyn had walked this path through Nasiri's garden, but it really was not all that long ago at all. And yet everything was different now. She's had a chance to rest and recover, and is once more wearing the cowled black and gold dress that hugs her slender form as she walks. She knows that returning now without any warning might not have been the smartest move, and so she doesn't intrude when she sees the Lorda gathered with several others there. She pauses out of the way, but in sight of anyone who might want to intercept her.


A few more of the majordomos begin to chime in, when the Lorda spies the Jedi and interestingly dressed as well. "Off with you all, I will reach out when I am ready." he waves them off and then with a lift of his chin, invites her over. "Wine?" he offers loud enough to be heard. The gang of majordomos filter out giving he woman a curious look as they do so. The light globe begins to drift north and dims as it does so, like the sun setting.

The Jedi lifts her chin as the Falleen notices her, and smiles graciously, bending her knee in a shallow curtsey of greeting. "Forgive my intrusion, Lorda," she says. "I didn't have time to send word ahead. And I hope you'll forgive me if my meeting has little to do with our prior discussions."


Nasiri smiles and gestures to one of the benches. "The benefit of the world I live in and the shit thats happen...patience." he sits and makes room for her. "That...is not a typical Jedi robe." he observes with a smile and takes a long pull of the wine. "And, as long as your not here to muddle my city...you are always welcome." he resettles the robe over a knee and rests his cheek on his free hand. "So, what is it you want to know?"


"Patience. That's something that a Jedi has in common," Jessalyn says with a small chuckle, following him to the bench and sitting down next to him, tucking the embroidered black cloak beneath her and facing him with her hands folded in her lap. "Do you like it? It's new. And no, I have no intention to interfere in your business, Lorda." Her shoulders rise and fall in a sigh, and she says, "I need your help," realizing the irony that this is almost the only person who might be willing to do so.

The Falleen has in recent times never heard anyone even once suggesting he could be of help to them, that didn't involve a command or some implication of doing the galaxy a moral good so as to look better to the galaxy abroad. "Most come to me for...killing or warring..." he smiles at the notion of a less violent Jedi coming to him, this must be interesting. "So, how can I help you?"


That was the tricky part, and the Jedi shifts her weight and glances away, still not exactly sure what and how much she should reveal. "I don't know if you've been paying any attention at all to the news that's been coming out of Bespin and Caspar," she says quietly. "Let's just say that I've been dealt a rather poor hand in this game, and my family may be in danger. I need... a place where they can be safe. I was hoping you could help me point to some place in Hutt Space where we could find sanctuary."

Nasiri raises a brow and indeed he had not been tracking anything involving the outer galaxy. "A jedi coming to me to find sanctuary in Hutt space." he looks interested and takes a long pull of the wine. "Unusual to say the least...and I totally understand being dealt a bad hand." he muses a bit. "Sanctuary...from?" he heard her mention Bespin and Caspar. "I promise you, whatever is going on out there, means little to me in here."


Jessalyn looks down at her hands in her lap for a long, quiet moment. At least she can feel the Force here and take a measure of serenity there, but it was still not easy. "Believe me, I am as surprised as you are. When we were last on Bespin, it triggered the arrival of Malign's fleet, and we only barely escaped. And now the Caspian Union is joining in and I don't have many options of where to go. Even New Republic space is not an option."


Nasiri rises and bades her to remain seated. "So..very unprecedented to me at least." he pours her an additional wine and returns to hand it to her. "There is a fortified building to the north of this garden on the banks of the Basin." he suggests to her. "I can have it cleaned and made liveable...and I will ask no further questions and answer no future questions about you." he pauses. "And as no doubt you are expecting, a Price." he raises the goblet to signify the offer. "No haggling, no greed...I will do these things and I will guard you...my price...a favor at a later date." there it was. "No I have nothing in mind, but when I ask...it is given."

Pushing back her cowl, Jessalyn watches the Falleen, blinking in surprise at his response. She waves away the wine with a polite smile, and glances north. A hiding place right under the Lorda's nose was not what she had been expecting as a possibility, but it does make a kind of dangerous sense. Who would ever think -- or dare -- to come here. Her lips purse as she calculates, her gaze turning to arch one delicate brow at Nasiri. "That is extremely generous of you," the Jedi admits. "More than I was expecting. I'm willing to make a deal for a later favor. One tiny caveat, if at all possible. I -am- a Jedi... so a favor will need to fit within parameters I can accept."

Nasiri glances at the refused wine and turns to place it upon the table. "I will try to keep it within reason to your conscience, but you may have to stretch slightly as well." he turns and gazes at her. "And I am sure I can trust...your...word when given." he responds and resumes his seat. "Caspar...means Sith, means Imperium...dont worry. I dont much give wits care about them." he smiles at her. "I will also post a guard force at the dwelling, under your command to a limited degree...agreed?"

A crooked smile plays over Jessalyn's mouth. "I can stretch a bit if it is necessary," she says wryly. "Just keep that in mind." As he takes his seat once more she faces him with more of a sense of relief than she would have imagined. "Thank you, Lorda," she says, her voice thick with emotion that she tries to swiftly control. "You don't know how much this means to me. I'll be glad to repay you when the time comes, and then some."

Nasiri nods and raises his wine to her. "My pleasure...family, what sort of rooms will you need?" he inquires as he slides an arm along the back of the bench. "This I believe also brings me potentially at paths with Johanna...also not a concern any more." he grins and begins pondering arrangements. "How comfortable and any special requests?"


"Oh," Jessalyn says, not having thought of any of those details yet, and a flush of color crosses her cheeks. "Just something small-ish and cozy will do fine, mostly it will be just my partner and I and two children." That was satisfactory enough, she decides and nods. "I'm hoping that this won't be too long-term of a situation, but I can't make any promises."

Nasiri chuckles and takes a long drink of his wine. "Rive ga'uche...has tiny and big buildings...it is spacious." he advises her with a grin and finishes off his wine. "Partner..." he then recalls the no questions he offered and waves off the word. "When you and your family are settled, be a guest at my table for dinner?"

"Then it sounds like we should be able to find something to work," Jessalyn says, her voice noticeably lighter. "And I'd be more than honored to join you for dinner, Lorda. You know, I've lived on a ship for a long time. I didn't expect to make a home here, but I'm surprisingly pleased... and relieved. Thank you." She puts her hands together and bends her head over them.


Nasiri rises and smiles. "I renovated this city...from ruin, it is a sanctuary of a sort." refilling his wine and looks across to her. "Here, what you did, where you came from or anything of that sort is unimportant." he turns to examine a plant. " Rive Ga'uche has a syle, it is the dangerous people who don't want to live wretched lives, but with finesse and style...note the fashion here, its not just for looks...it conceals."

Jessalyn shifts on her seat as she listens to Nasiri begin to wax philosophic about this place, her hands folded gently in her lap. "It is quite a unique place," she agrees, glancing around at the strange garden that wasn't like any garden she'd ever grown. "I don't know if I'll contribute much by way of finesse and style, but perhaps I can make myself useful in some ways."

Nasiri nods and drains the wine and returns it to the table. "Feel free to do as you wish, if you want to open a business." he nods and collects himself. "Welcome to Rive." he offers solemnly, signalling that the meeting was over. "And it was a pleasure." he assures her.

Rising in a lithe movement, Jessalyn bows her head to the Falleen, keen not to overstay her welcome. "The pleasure is mine. I will be in contact." She steps close and touches his arm, meeting his gaze in an effort to convey her gratitude. And again -- thank you." She turns with a swirl of her cloak and follows the path back out of the garden the way she came.

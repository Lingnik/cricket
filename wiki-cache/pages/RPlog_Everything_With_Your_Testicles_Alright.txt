Council Chambers - Union Hall: Plaxton: Caspar

The Council Chambers have recently been renovated to better service the needs of the new Caspian Union. This grand hall serves both as the public meeting space for the Union Council as well as doubling as a lecture hall on occasion.

A stage on the far side of the room is occupied by five benches arranged in a semi-circle facing the audience seating. Each bench is constructed in Wansook pine with a hamogoni stain and topped with Vira granite. There are ten place settings on each bench, set widely apart from each other to better accommodate the physiology of some of the non-Humanoid Councillors.

Projectors embedded into the benches allow the Councillors to appear and vote remotely; a particular necessity given the duties of many of the Councillors.

The shape and style of the seating behind the benches differs widely to suit the physiology and preferences of each of the Councillors, though all are limited to a white to grey colour palette to avoid being distracting. A number of place settings lack seats entirely owing both to the actual size of the Union Council currently and the particular physiology of a few of the Councillors.

Behind the benches is an expansive window of Plaxton outside. The fibres of the window can be altered to the touch of a button to become a projection screen.

The seats for the audience ascend up nearly fifty rows up to a projection booth at the top rim, where large lights are affixed. In front of the seats is a single ornately carved podium, of the same hamogoni-stained Wansook pine of the benches, allowing for the audience members to address the Council. On each side there is also a similarly constructed table with three chairs each to allow for more organised hearing and testimony.

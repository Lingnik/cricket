&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A few stragglers in the drunk tank and a would-be thief or two are now what pass for the criminal underworld in Mos Espa; the purge of Tatooine's scum and villainy by Johanna and Malign had been so thorough as to scare off all but the most deranged types, and thankfully the desert world simply isn't enough of a prize to attract the attention of said deranged types. As a result, most days the lockup in Mos Espa is fairly quiet.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Today is not one of those days.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I TAZED HIM IN THE BUTTHOLE! OVER AND OVER!" come the furious electronic screams from none other than the infamous astromech Cricket, "AND HE DESERVED IT!" The droid wheels around his cell seething, his prized tazer having been removed after it was found employed in the molestation of Axel Vichten's unmentionables. "YOU WILL ALL PAY FOR YOUR MISTAKE! I WILL GAIN ACCESS TO YOUR BUTTHOLES AND THEY WILL ALSO BE TAZED!" While it's probably not wise to admit loudly to the assault, his anger has gotten the better of him. He was only engaging in the just punishment of a nuisance, after all! How could they treat him so unfairly, the fuckers?! He would make Johanna pay for this somehow.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;After a certain amount of time to passes the arrest of Cricket attracts attention. The drunk tank in Mos Espa would not normally be a place of public curiosity, but with crime on the minor side these days, the arrest of Warlord Johanna's foul-mouthed astromech, a few bored individuals decide to poke there head into the place to try to gawk.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A different sort of interested party makes their way into the place. Atsvara had taken the time to change her outfit before arriving. The ten, approaching eleven year old golden girl, strolls in dressed in the black uniform that are worn by the crew of the Interdictors, Leviathan and Dying Sun. Different from both Caspian and Imperium uniforms, for those who know it denotes crews in personal service to the Sith House Dragonflower. Though she bears no military rank insignia, the accessories of her outfit mark her as Baroness.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She comes with no obvious guard or staff. Practically pushing through the peasants of Mos Espa blocking her path, she makes her way to the front desk and states, "I am here to see Cricket." Her tone is not raised, but it was clearly stated as a demand and not a request.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Shoo," a hulking IG-RM droid orders as it attempts to herd the gawkers back out onto the street, "Shoo. Shoo." The Devaronian officer behind the desk sighs. They barely pay her enough to deal with whatever rabid contraption one would classify Cricket as, and they /certainly/ don't pay her enough to deal with bossy children. Why, some other weird kid had been through here just a few hours ago, demanding Axel's release. Thankfully social services had retrieved the lad before he could cut anyone with his nails. "Slow your roll, little lady," she drawls, yellow eyes scanning Atsvara for telltale signs of concealed weapons (one could never be too sure when it came to those weird, fucked-up friends of Johanna's), "R2-CT won't be seeing anyone for a little while, he's on the warpath or something. Better to wait until he calms down. Anyway, I can't let kids in here, where's your guardian?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Atsvara is armed, of course. But Chandrilan Datadaggers are designed specifically to not be noticed as weapons. Beyond it she carries no obvious weapons, after all she is not expecting to have to violently break out Cricket, though the possibility has not been entirely discounted.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The girl looks at the Devaronian incredulously. Where could she even begin to process the insults? Little Lady? Where is your Guardian? Why should she fear a warpath? The use of Cricket's official designation seemed unseemly to her as well, like a slave name being uttered upon him.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As a half-Firrerreo she believed that to know someone's name was to have power over them. He own true name was only known to herself and her mother who gave it to her, not even her father knew, nor wanted to. Somehow to have this woman just casually using Cricket's production name was truly galling. "Ma'am I can assure you that Cricket is no threat to me." She not only emphasises the name Cricket, but also the word ma'am like she a frustrated manager talking to annoying customer.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"You may wish to worry more about yourself, Cricket is not libel to be quite pleased when he is released, which he will be. I would be happy to try talk him down and out of your hair." Atsvara takes a deep breath and adds, "As for my age it is irrelevant. I am Baroness Atsvara Tarasar of House Dragonflower. If you insist on me furnishing a 'guardian' they are sure to be unpleasant."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"ATSVARA!" Cricket bleats with an ear-shattering shriek, having recognized the voice of his fellow corporate partner-in-crime immediately, "THEY HAVE TAKEN MY GLORIOUS TASER!" He wheels against the energy barrier, squat cylindrical frame crackling as he's repelled and blasted onto his back. "A PLAGUE UPON JOHANNA AND HER MINIONS," he screams from his beached position within the drunk tank, "THEY WILL KNOW MY WRATH!"<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Devaronian sighs again. "It's your funeral," she shrugs, taking a swig of her caff, "I just don't wanna hear about any lawsuits. Mind the IG-RM over there, I'll let it know you're not a gawker." Truth be told, she doesn't care if Atsvara /is/ a gawker -- she probably is! -- but unless a raise is forthcoming, lippy preteens are not her concern. Let the kid learn a lesson the hard way.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Lawsuit from me or lawsuit from Cricket?" Atsvara asks in a less a hostile tone now that she has begin to make some ground against the poor Devaronian police functionary. "I mean nothing is going to happen that /I/ need to sue over. Cricket, however, has several legal avenues to pursue against your department. Unlawful detention obviously, but you took his taser which being part of his body could lead to suit for assault and maiming."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She steps into the detention area to speak to Cricket, "If I get them to release you into 'my custody' can I offer assurance you will not murder, maim, assault, or launch a lawsuit against the constables and department here?" She looks back towards the Devaronian, and smiles weakly, as if to suggest she better hope that Cricket says yes.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"HNNNNGGHHHH," the astromech replies, his wheels spinning fruitlessly in the air as he lies on the ground and rages, "FUCK THE POLICE!" But even Cricket is smart enough to know a good out when one is presented to him, so after a few more moments of binary swearing, he dials it down a notch or five and stops trying to burn down the station with his mind-vibes. That's how the Force works, anyway, right? Plus it wouldn't be prudent to go out like a bitch. No no, he is destined for a more glorious death! "I... fine. Yes yes, I PROMISE," he zots, "I PROMISE NOT TO VISIT JUSTICE UPON ALL WHO HAVE WRONGED ME. There, that good enough for you fascists?!" Besides, he's not a /lawsuit/ kind of droid, oh no.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Atsvara giggles at Cricket's initial respond adding, "For the record my friend, fucking them is counted under assault...." She looks around at the Devaronian and any other staff, "Unless that is your sort of thing. Not judging." She giggles some more clearly pleased with herself by spinning things into a worse direction. As Cricket calms down she addresses the constabulary again. "Now if you will kindly release Cricket into my custody, he has given you the best assurance you are going to get about avoiding retaliation. Besides I believe still have some headaches in the future ahead of you with Mister Vichten. He is after all a suspect in a myriad of depraved crimes....and a flag officer of an allied power. I mean that really sucks for you guys, and I know if I was dealing with that I would not want to have angry Droid yelling at me too." She pulls out three thousand-credit chits, "This ought to cover your shift's cantina tab for the drinking you all are probably going to want to do after this."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Whatever," the officer scoffs, rolling her eyes both at the bribe and the unbelievably cheeky little git offering it, "Far as I'm concerned, you're all the Warlord's problem. Now put that money away before someone else decides to relieve you of it." Then, "IG, help /Cricket/ back to his... feet, so that he and the nice little lady here can go be on their way and make their ridiculous threats elsewhere. He's not getting back his weapon, though. That's evidence." The barrier deactivates, granting the security droid entrance to the erstwhile enclosure, his large metal frame creaking slightly as he hauls the R2 unit up and sets him back onto his wheels." Whatever thoughts he might have as to the whole fiasco, he keeps to himself. "You are free to go," IG's halting mechanical voice instructs the duo, "Have a nice day." His Devaronian counterpart shakes her head, "Really, IG?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As Cricket is being released, Atsvara says to him in a hushed voice that is purposely not too hushed, "She thinks I should be worried about my pocket change?" The girl though puts away the money. If this woman is too stupid to accept the bribe or try to shake her down for more money it is her own damned fault. The instance in keeping Cricket's tazer gets a long disapproving look from the girl as well before she adds to Cricket, in a slightly more hushed tone, though still purposely audible, "I wonder how she would feel if someone tried to keep her horn as evidence of something. I mean you were really doing them all a favour. I guess we will just have to get you an even bigger taser now? Maybe two." Atsvara begins quietly plotting possible retaliation of her own for these insults. Nothing harmful mind you, just something to one day rub into this woman her mistake in who she has pissed off today.

Caspar Caff #23 - Plaxton: Caspar

This large cafe sits nestled among a recently renovated section of North Mergsanar Avenue. This particular cafe sits in what was once one of the swank residences in this part of the city. Its interior has largely been gutted to fulfil the function it now possesses, though some eccentricities in the design remain. Antique looking windows look out upon the street and other old residences that have found new function. A bar sits on the side. Here patrons can order stim-cafs, teas and other hot beverages from around the galaxy in addition to iced versions of many of the same drinks. A display case showcases deserts from a wide variety of cultures, available in individual servings. A number of small tables are scattered about the space, along with the a number of booths, even though the bulk of the patrons take their drinks to go.

Of particular note are the public holoterminals: each table has an individual holoterminal capable of streaming media or making calls. The booths have the same capability, along with high end noise cancelling equipment to prevent the noise from disturbing other patrons.

There was a great deal of debate among the citizens of Plaxton as to just which Caspar Caff was the superior location. While it was not truly a serious issue, it was one that made it to the halls of government. It was the unofficial policy at Union Hall that group take out orders should alternate between the locations on North and South Mergansar Avenues so as it not to avoid the appearance of favouritism.


But with last night's festivities having driven Crestian to stop for caf before heading to one of his many offices, he went with the location he found superior, Caspar Caff #23 on North Mergansar Avenue. A long night had made this a popular choice with many other people, no doubt amplified by the inability to stop at Biscuit Baron with the strike on, making Caspar Caff more busy than usual. Crestian's own retinue was small, arriving only with his daughter Atsvara and his aide and guard V1C3.

Druze looks sceptical. "Do they serve droidaccinos? Is that a thing?" His caff is black, and the slice of pastry he has alongside it is dotted thoughtfully with candied scallops. Probably smells like a dockworker fell into a cotton candy vat. Nonetheless, it is a dessert that absolutely exists.

Nara arrives within moments of the Presav's party, having swapped her pilot's jacket, warbelts, and officer's cap for an azure military beret with a CU flash and a fatigue jacket in Caspar Armed Forces green. As she approaches the counter, she slows, sniffing the air. "Did someone just come in from the night shift in the local candy factory? I'd swear a stevedore had fallen into a vat of spun sugar..."

Atsvara giggles at the comment from Nara, "Well I can assure you that Veruca Violet would never put Spun Sugared Stevedore on the shelves of her shop. But I am sure there is a market for it somewhere." Crestian for his own part remarks, "I wonder if it is sabotage by the Caspar Caff on the other end of the street. Franchise wars can be brutal." V1C3 back in his normal pin-striped suit attire has a different take, "I have never understood why you biologicals bother with this whole smelling business. You either think things smell bad, or they smell so good as to trick you into other behaviour than you initially intended. Like how is any of this good for you? No Nose. No Nonsense."

Druze clutches his plate nearer, lest anyone try to have a bite. Thieves and vandals, the whole lot! Rapscallions and rascals! He has a forkful, pointing out after chewing thoughtfully: "You can't not have an olfactory sensor, you electroplated simpleton! Speak some Jawa and it'll probably kick itself on!"

Nara receives her order. "Thank you," she says softly, dropping some credits into the tip jar before turning to look for a table. She pauses next to Druze's table, her delicate nose twitching slightly. "Please pardon me, good sir, but have you been working in the local candy factory?" she asks curiously. "That odd smell seems to be coming from here."

Looking toward the Presav's table, she smiles at Atsvara's comment. "I stand assured. Thank you for that," she replies warmly. "And for what it may be worth, good Sir Droid, I suppose a nose is a uniquely organic experience."

Playing on the remark about speaking Jawaese, V1C3 remarks, "Utinni! Do we have a Mister Utinni! here? Your order is ready." He then looks to Druze, "Useless language anyway, how do you count without the number 7, madness." Crestian smirks, "Well clearly he has remembered the important part of his programming. Linguistics." Atsvara takes a bite of some food, "It just is not the same as the Bantha Breakfast Biscuit." Her affectation might imply her statement was somewhat contrived.

Druze queries of Nara: "You've never had a sugar-cured bay scallop from Dac?! Then you simply must!" If it's a trick or a dare like getting someone to eat wasabi or marmite unawares he isn't, for example, trying not to laugh. But underwater cuisine may indeed be a novel beast...

"Um, thank you for your recommendation, but I believe I shall pass on the scallops," Nara replies softly. "I'm not really partial to seafood. But, again, thank you. I'll remember it for another time, perhaps."

She smiles faintly, and sadly, at Atsvara's observation. "Likely not. I'm not much more partial to Biscuit Baron than I am to seafood, but I've grown fond of their caf blends. I'm hoping TaggeCo sees reason and answers the poor workers' demands."

Crestian takes a sip of his caf, it had never been his beverage of choice, but you really can't go native on Caspar without developing at least a begruding affinity for it. "I have never been a big fan of seafood myself. Which is somewhat ironic since I use have a dacha on the Silver Sea on Chandrila and we have a strong maritime industry here. Thankfully the Yacht Club has other things on the menu. Still more appetising than a lot of what Zubindi likes to eat. Antz, drochs, no thank you. He was actually named after a famous chef."

Atsvara meanwhile cannot resist the chance to further discuss Biscuit Baron, "The Tagge Restaurant Association is definitely not playing things smart. And it is just absolutely ruining the launch of the latest Jolly Meal toys!"

Druze shakes his head, wry. "Kids' tables are over there," he points out for Atsvara. "... adult Jolly Meal toys are the maneuvering thrusters on a Xi Char semi-autonomous starfighter that's a test-bed for no fewer than four(!) bleeding-edge upgrades to sensory-neural uplinks in pilot feedback!"

Druze nearly spills his caff, explaining(!!!)

"Tragic, that... I once had a collection of all four Rings of Power, from the holoseries by that name, from Jolly Meals," Nara confides, with a sympathetic smile for Atsvara. "Hopefully they'll make up for lost time once the strike ends. /If/ it ends, anyway." She glances back to Druze, smiling more warmly. "Are you a pilot, good sir?"

Druze hms? "Oh no, not professionally. Engineer, repairman. Bit of a gearhead." He smiles apologetically in Quarrenese.

Atsvara looks at Druze following his comment on a kid's table. She concentrates really hard, but alas still no force powers. Spotting Atsvara's expression Crestian remarks, "Nie zudimas tauta su dvasi" which is followed by a pouting expression by the girl.

"I see. I regret I've never had a head for engineering. Nor for navigation, though I do try," Nara replies ruefully. Looking to Atsvara and noticing her expression of concentration, asks, "Are you feeling all right? You look like you might be feeling ill." Accompanied by a concerned expression.

Druze pulls a pen-light from his belt, checking her pupils and-- perhaps-- counting pulse-beats in her arteries? "Respiration, standard. No sign of venous dissection or thrombosis. Here, have a couple aspirin?" He pulls prescription-strength gelcaps from his uniform. Maybe it's a peace-offering.

Atsvara takes the gelcaps and downs them before her father can comment at all, impishly remarking, "For my headache." I mean she did have one, if only metaphorically. Crestian smirks at the whole matter. He would be more concerned with her taking medications from people she didn't know, but he did trust Quarren. More over he trusted the strength of the Firrerreo physiology which was fairly indestructible. After a moment he realises, "You know where are my manners, I don't suppose you have been formally introduce, Atsvara this is my friend Doctor Druze, Doctor Druze, this is my daughter Atsvara."

Druze *blanches.* "Daughter, ah. I might've chosen my words differently had I known. But my pride prevents me from offering substandard treatment to all persons!"

"A doctor, then? Of medicine? Or perhaps of science or engineering?" Nara asks, smiling warmly at Druze, though her smile falters very slightly as someone comes in and the puff of wind from the opening door fans the cotton-candied-stevedore odor of his dessert of choice right at her face. "I'm Nara. Just Nara. And I'm honored to meet you." She offers him her hand.

Druze shakes. "It's hard to stay busy. Distributed neural networks are... well. A special kind of punishment." He shakes his head. "Doctor, yes. Engineer by way of suddenly couldn't be a doctor for a while, so... ?" He shrugs? "B. Druze: Head in the clouds, mouth off on some tangent." He tries a smile.

The odor of the desert hits Crestian as well, "I suppose if any one can ask I can assure them Caspar Caff offers a proper variety of treats for all palettes. They might survive the labour wars yet." Atsvara for her own part grins simply repeating the word, "Might."

"Hardly a curse, that... what is fashion but flights of fancy made manifest through cloth and the most skillful of hands?" Nara asks, her smile growing a little wider. "A head in the clouds is a wonderful thing, if it has focus enough to make dreams into reality. I've no doubt you've made at least one dream real." She shares a smirk with Atsvara at the girl's reply.

Druze finishes his pie, and then his coffee. "Your seminar was the veritable lightning-bolt, Presav, but I'm fighting the urge to adopt a new identity and melt into the crowd. No Druzes here-- only Fluflurbfloblurb... Fluze, the humble caff-merchant." A wink, at least. "Health to you and yours, but I really ought to get back to my berth for the evening. At least let me pay the check?" This he asks the table. "... my treat."

Crestian smiles, the compliments are always appreciated, but it is added amusement to have someone not obscenely wealthy offer to pick up a tab, albeit a Caspar Caff is a modest one. "I am glad you appreciate the show and I do suppose I can let you pick up the tab this time. But should there be time when we are still both on Caspar you must let me take you to the Yacht Club in Trinumvira. A thank you for always managing to be at the right place in a pinch."

Druze nods. "We'll do that. Have a good night, all."

Nara stifles a giggle behind a ladylike hand. "My thanks... though I must insist you let me treat you sometime, Fluflurb," she replies, with an impish wink of her own as Druze's pretended pseudonym. "And we need not be on Caspar."

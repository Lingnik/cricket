Hangar Bay - Retribution

	The vastness of this hanger bay catches you off guard as you first step into it. The forward end of the cavernous room looks as if it's completely open to the vacuum of outer space, visible through the faint blue haze of the magnetic air lock. The smell of machinery fills the air here, and you notice several flight technicians wearing masks for good reason. Other repair crews work on military shuttles or landing craft. A few private vessels are also parked here and each is overlooked by the six armed New Republic Marines patrolling this deck. Astromechs and load-lifters move about the hangar as their services are needed.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Retribution was a Republic-class Star Destroyer that at some point was captured by the Empire and now serves as Axel's flagship when conducting business as the Inquisitor. The crew was hand-picked by the man, it's soldiers all clad in black stormtrooper armor. An ISB communications bay was set up adjacent to the hangar, and aside from Axel's own ship, there were only a couple of shuttles and multiple racks of TIE Avengers.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Axel was in uniform, sans that damned cape he despised, getting reports from a trio of intelligence officers when the shuttle with Crestian and Lynae arrives. "I'll have to make another damned press release, won't I? By the gods I'm going to strangle someone. I want names, I want anything you can muster on who else got off that fucking station, and I want them yesterday. Now, get out of my face." Axel scowls at the agents, and they nod and depart.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Gathering himself, Axel turns towards the shuttle and begins heading that way. "Your friend is a pain in my ass, Lynae. I don't know what his game is, but I've got a lot of damage control to do now. So, what is his game, and why in the hell are you involved in it?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian was always one to choose his own outfits carefully and for Imperium matters he usually wears black, though his cape is white. "I think she already knows I am a pain in the ass....wait you mean Darth Malus." The Caspian Presav takes a small step away from Lynae, he was not the one to be interrogated here. "And the damage control is not limited to the Imperium here. The crew on the ship he left Caspar on are dead. If he were not such a unique problem we would have warrants out on that alone."

He is a patient, that was escorted to my ship to receive medical care. No questions asked medical services, that remains to be my motto. Second, I don't know precisely why I was selected to be his physician, aside from obvious skill and expertise, I can only speculate. Third, and in no particular order of importance, I don't know exactly what his game is - aside from Revenge, as is his currently stated motivation - which I presume is just the tip of what is probably a poisonous thorn. And," she looks from Axel to Crestian then just as slowly back again, "I'm not 'involved' in it. I facilitated a pair of introductions that led, naturally, to a pair of conversations that I found very enlightening." She flicks a look, again, at Axel, then - just as calmly - around the ship's Hangar Bay and back. "Very. And yes," she angles a nod to Crestian, "you are, but you're not the current pain in the ass that has Axel so annoyed. And if you insist on calling him by that title he's going to continue to think he deserves it."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There's a section of the hangar on the ship that's been cordoned off with a force field and exposed to the vacuum of space. "There's your ship, and the bodies are all aboard it. I didn't want them parked next to my steaks, so I kept them cold another way," Axel says in response to Crestian before looking back to Lynae.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I'd like for you to examine the bodies, Lynae. See if you can figure out how he killed them. I want to know what I'm up against, every damned one of the force-wielding fucks has different tricks after all." Axel sighs, and motions for Crest and Lynae to follow him to an office that is connected to the hangar. "I'm all for stirring the pot, but I need to know everything you do, Lynae. You're my dearest and oldest friend, and I've gotta know how far this shit goes. If I don't get a handle on things, then not only is Caspar potentially at risk, but Malign's gonna put his boot so far up my ass that the water on his knee will quench my thirst."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I suppose you do have a point Lynae. He seemed to read far too much into when referred to Malign as Tyler. But I don't know Mister Rhys just doesn't feel quite right." Crestian smirks, "As for the conversations, I was only in one of the conversations, it was enlightening, but less then I might have hoped. My biggest takeaway is a man who in spite of two decades on ice he still is very determined that certain observations of the universe he had before are still true. It is actually a little amusing. But it does not appear to make him very useful."

She even looks toward the section of the hangar that has the recently-mentioned ship and it's deceased cargo. "Why am I the only one in this conversation who isn't enamored with the Sith? I have no current or RECENT ties to any of them. Point of order, I still think that the ability to consciously interact with and interfere with the force is a genetic aberration that can be filtered out with enough time, effort, focus and credits. &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Not really," Axel says to Lynae's last rant. "The original crew of this ship had a fighter wing commander with peculiarly good tastes," Axel says pulling a couple of dusty bottles out.<br>

"Sorry, Presav, no Ithorian, I do hope this will suffice," he says before filling the other two with Whyren's. One for himself, the other for Lynae.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"By himself, Malus is little to no threat. I tried finding out more while we talked, but that went nowhere, makes me think he doesn't know exactly what he plans yet. Trying to get people to prove their worth to him while refusing to explain his own worth in return."

He takes a sip and does not seem to have a strong reaction either way. "When push comes to shove I don't know how closely my goals align with Tyler and more. But we both want Vadim eliminated, we both are pledge to fight against the scourge of slavery, we both will go to great lengths to protect the things we care about. But I never left the Sith as such. I still control the territory I did before. I am still a scholar on Sithese and Sith history. My title as Duke and my daughters as Baroness are Sith....her name is Sithese. It is still serves me."<br>

I know the detours. I know the lovely scenery. Tyler, Malign, Korolov, what ever his Darth whats-it-name is, and then Vadim at the top of the whole rotting pile. You have goals, both of you, and you're willing to use - and be used - to get where you want to go. I?" she gestures to herself with one hand, "Did not break him out. I did not facilitate any of this. Personally, I think carbonite cold storage is a genius idea to save money in storing extremely dangerous prisoners for long term purposes. This would allow them to flash melt AND fry all at the same time. Melty slag, for the win," she takes another measured drink from the glass.<br>

Superior. And clearly feels very entitled to a great many things. And right now, he's all of that plus twenty years out of sync, which makes him as dangerous as any other rabid and cornered animal." A shift of her attention to Axel, "How many bodies are in there?" then to Crestian, "And if you think Tyler is really anti-slavery, ask him how many of his test subjects are willing participants. Then really think about the answer he gives you. I know him. And you should know better. Both of you."

Regardless, they both have helped me in the past on multiple of occasions," Axel says after Crestian says his little spiel. "It was not that long ago that there was a New Sith Order that came about. In a very short time, we carved out a nice portion of the galaxy, and I had a great deal of power because of it. There is zero reason to do it. There's 8 bodies in about twelve different bags," Axel says between sips. "You mentioned that he was escorted to your ship, who brought him to you?

Silverwood House of Fashion - Trinumvira: Caspar

The Caspar location of Zubindi Hakoon's Silverwood House of Fashion comprises an entire city block of five and six storey buildings surrounded by white cobblestone avenues. The neighbourhood formerly consisted of ground floor shops and living space above but was hard hit by the Sariphage. Each individual building has internal connexions to the building adjacent to it, though not all of these are at ground level. In spite of the labyrinth like structure, the various buildings each retain a sense of individual space.

Keeping with the pattern set at the flagship location on Kubindi the space is divided into rooms centred around the styles of particular worlds whose fashion's Zubindi dabbles in while others host more specialised functions around particular accessories or activities. What marks the Caspar location as unique is the the presence of the few remaining shops that survived the Sariphage. Rather than buying out the owners of these shops, they have been invited to incorporate their businesses integrated into the larger fashion complex. Businesses such as Grant's Star Tours, Out of the Past, Tom's Hair Emporium, and Veruca Violet's Candy Land now do a far greater volume of sales.

The main entrance, insofar as there is one, is found in the building on the southeast corner of the block. One enters into a two story lobby its walls consisting of the same polished Vira granite as the exterior, unadorned except for posters of past campaigns of the fashion house, notably the inaugural campaign of 4 ABY, and the campaigns of 6 ABY, 8 ABY, 14 ABY, 17 ABY, and 24 ABY. The floors are covered with milky white larmalstone from the nearby Roche Asteroids. The only furnishings in the lobby are a series of dolomite statues on obsidian pedestals approximately a half metre tall.

From the lobby the complex opens into the various planetary collections with rooms themed around Kubindi & Mustafar, Alderaan, Bespin, Chandrila, Corellia, Naboo, Neimoidia, Onderon, Pantora, and naturally a Caspian room as well. Other collections centre not on the particular cultures but rather on particular materials with a secluded space devoted to shadowsilk clothing and accessories and elsewhere a building is devoted to Askajian Tomuon cloth.

In one of the newer structures made of Kuati marble near the southwest corner of the block is the jewellery and accessory collection. The upper storeys of the block contain living space for the various designers, crafters, and models as well facilities for more industrial scale production of popular designs. While nothing is truly mass produced some items are produced in some bulk.


Silverwood House of Fashion - Trinumvira: Caspar's Neimodia Room

Because of their obsession with status, the Neimoidians placed great importance in clothing. Among their kind, elaborate clothes were specifically designed to assert an individual's personal wealth and rank and also to instil jealousy or envy in others. Not only did their robes, cloaks, hats and collars all have particular symbolic meanings, but so did their fabrics and colors. Ranks and garments were so closely associated that a Neimoidian disgraced by their peers would be stripped of their clothes immediately.

They were particularly attached to their hats, and a popular opinion was that a Neimoidian would never be seen without it unless they were dead. The numerous types of Neimoidian headgear ranged from simple caps that revealed the complete forehead to a wide variety of mitres and crowns, some of which even comprised wig-like ornaments.

Since the end of the Clone Wars many Neimoidians began adopting the culture of their baseline Duros cousins and fewer left their homeworlds. At the Silverwood House of Fashion, however, they are still considered a touchstone of fashion excellence.

The Neimoidia room somewhat fitting occupies a building which served as a bar called the 'High Hat.' The former bar itself is used to display the wide range of Neimoidian styled headgear available with the liquor cabinets converted for further display and store of the same. The hat selection in this room exceeds that of any of the others.

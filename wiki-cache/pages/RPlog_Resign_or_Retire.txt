Presav's Office - Union Hall: Plaxton: Caspar

A huge Kriin-wood desk dominates the centre of this room and is a whirlwind of papers and datapads. On the front edge sits an etched glass nameplate that reads "Crestian Tarasar, Presav, Caspian Union". The desk has few noticeable momentos aside from a holo-image of the Presav's daughter Atsvara. A large antique chair sits behind the desk, upholstered in a deep forest green. Engraved on the wall behind the desk is the Union's symbol, the Vector Apex.

In front of the desk sit three chairs, each upholstered in a creamy ivory fabric. The floor is carpeted in a similar ivory and has the Caspian Cason Hawk emblazoned in it. The walls are painted in a pale blue, enhancing the overall spaciousness and warmth of the room. The eastern wall is covered from floor to ceiling with rows upon rows of books set into the heavy bookshelves.

On the western wall hang portraits of past Presavs; Wiltmer Gerrin, Lyr, Dunwell, Togryyshyk of Shyykris, Avalyshaar Dusten, Kadgie Rodo, Salonika Batrad, Elana Tracer, Thomas Mahon, Saiane Caitrean, Kilos Sabosulmis, and Nelivve Lameberchain. A potted Pula tree seems to awkwardly hinder easy viewing of the portraits of Mahon, Caitrean, and Sabosulmis. Separately from these hangs a portrait of Admiral Dergan Roj in an Old Republic Navy uniform, standing upon the bridge of his old warship. The Admiral's war-worn features and uncompromising eyes seem to challenge visitors to this office.

The scent of fragrant tea fills the room, wafting from an archaic pot that sits on a small table near the door. Next to it, a plainer carafe contains strong, black caf. Through a large window along the northern wall, you can see the picturesque view of the Government Plaza of Plaxton, and that the night sky is cloudy with patches of clear spots allowing you to see the sky behind them.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian Tarasar, Presav, was living out a political nightmare at the moment. Naturally that had him spending some time in his office. Crestian spent as little time in Plaxton as was necessary, much preferring to carry out what business he could in other locations. He did after all have several offices, just on Caspar alone, not to mention various other haunts on world where he conducted business. But today he was stuck in Plaxton.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As he waited for his next appointment, the Presav eyed the image of his daughter on his desk. It was the key personal keepsake he kept in this office. It was, however, looking a little dated and he pondered to himself whether it was time for a more recent one. Naturally this then led to a question of what sort of image to replace it with if he did, she had so many iconic looks of late. As he ponders he gets up from his desk towards the table with caf and tea.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There's always an admin of some sort. Be it officer, civilian, android, some combination of all of the above, but there's always a flunky. A guardian of the door, the keeper of the schedule, a guard canine. Lynae is moving at a brisk pace as she approaches the office, speaking with a Lieutenant of some sort who is giving a sit-rep on the move and Lynae is signing off on personnel requests, duty requests, general busy-work that has to land on someone's desk, and she's squaring things away as they cross the threshold. A look is spared for the admin, "Is the Presav still in his office?" she asks, pausing mid-conversation stream to confirm this before handing back the datapad to the Lieutenant.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Outside of the Presav's office a few members of his staff are talking. In the middle of them is a Human woman, in her mid forties, wearing maroon suit. She looks some what ill at easy, talking with the others but not as comfortably as the others. The question from Lynae thankfully breaks her out of that discomfort into a completely new discomfort. "Admiral Cassius....Ad....Presav Tarasar is in his office, yes."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Fantastic," Lynae replies as she tucks her hands behind her back, clasping one wrist with the opposite hand, nodding a cordial dismissal to the LT. She waits a beat, then another, a little more then offers a hint of a smile. "Please let him know that I'd appreciate a few minutes of his time, before his next appointment arrives?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Director of the Office of the Presav, turns away from Lynae and opens the door of Crestian's office and takes a step in. Leaving the door open behind her she announces to the Presav, "Admiral Cassius is here to see you."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The news was of no great surprise to Crestian. I mean he was not expecting it at that moment for sure, but he had the feeling she was going to be coming to him. "Send her in Fern."
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She then steps back out of the office and motions towards the open door, saying nothing.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lynae shares a cordial nod with the director of the office before striding for the office, crossing the threshold and pausing for a moment to allow the door to shut. She indulges in a slow sweep of the office, her eyes lingering - with no small amount of regret upon the portrait of Thomas before she turns her attention completely to Crestian. "Have you run into Elana Tracer, lately? I remember being both awed and impressed by her the very first time I met her. She and Thomas were so cordial with each other, it was hard to untangle the history that led from her to him, politically speaking."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"She was at the funeral for Prelate r'ratak. Though I honestly cannot remember if we spoke or not. There was a lot going on. But I suppose there is always a lot going on." Crestian grabs his cup of tea, and motions to the table, "I also have liquor if you prefer something stronger." He take a couple of steps towards his desk, "I am afraid I have little insight to offer on their past myself. I had been away from Caspar for many years at that point. Sort of makes this office a strange place. The people in these paintings....most of them do not conjure up memories for me. I haven't really change the office too much from my predecessors....other than that plant near your ex-husband. I love that plant."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lynae makes a slow circuit of the room, not encroaching on Crestian's space of course, but she does inspect each of the portraits before smiling up at the one of Thomas. "He'd probably approve of the change, but he wouldn't of course say it. His favorite mode of conversation was a mild nod, some carefully measured out words like a conversation miser and a week later you'd get all manner of documentation about the care & feeding of the plant, a history analysis of the genus and what other plants in the same family that might compliment the one you have, along with some soil samples to consider as additives." She turns slightly, away from the portrait, "Caf would be lovely, thank you."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian smiles, he wasn't expecting to smile. And at talk of Thomas Mahon at that. "Well I would happily entertain any advice he might have about the plant. I mean if I am going to obscure his portrait the least I could do is make sure it is with the most fitting foliage. It is the careful balance between honouring our past by keeping all those portraits up and the fact I take more positive inspiration from some of the others on the wall. Probably most from our founding father Admiral Roj, and Presav Dunwell. He really set a lot in motion. The only ones I personally know or have know are Elana of course, and then my immediate predecessor Provisional Presav Lameberchain, sadly he is not in the best of health. But he had come out of retirement after the pandemic. With nearly the entire Union Assembly dead, any one who had been in office before was quite the valuable commodity at the time."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian goes to take his seat, "Well grab your caf and sit down. I am sure you didn't come here to discuss the wall art. And you know if you did it would just get very long and boring I am sure. I don't even know a lot about who even did the art....which is an oversight really....see I am getting boring already."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lynae exhales a chuckle of amusement as she pours herself a cup of coffee, adds a drop - minuscule really - of cream to the cup and takes a seat, carefully angling the cup ever so subtly from side to side to disturb the drop of cream across the surface. "You would, then, not be surprised by the sheer amount of time I spent in this office being not exactly lectured about the history of the union let alone political matters, who was who, when and why, and how they all connected to the next and so on. In fairness," and she pauses to exhale a mild breath across the surface of the caf, "a primer on Caspian politics and history wasn't actually included in my civic reading, back in the day. So his insight was interesting, and welcome," a trace of a smile, "if somewhat long winded."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Well as a student of history I would like to say it is all very important. But people have short memories and so much has happened here since. I can't say the present is all that matters, or even recent history....but twenty year old history only takes any of us so far. And it is something we can only spend so much time on." Crestian sips his tea and sighs. "Though every now and again it suddenly becomes very important."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"A civilization that ignores it's own history has no past and no future," Lynae's tone has the cadence of someone reciting a quote, which she is, and it's said before she sips - somewhat cautiously - from the caf. "And how is your daughter? I forget how old she is, but she can't be that much older than Andrev."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lynae chases her own words a moment later, "Younger? I should know this, but I don't. Forgive me."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian chuckles, "Well don't worry her age is not on any tests. And to be quite honest I do not quite recall Andrev's exact age myself. Atsvara turns 12 next month. She is doing well. We got to spend a lot more time together while we were both on Charros IV than we usually do. For a number of years she came along with me on my main trips throughout the galaxy. And then of course she started to schedule her own meetings wherever we were going. And eventually the places she needed to be and the people she needed to see stop coinciding so much. For a while there I really thought she was going to take after Elana more, I doubted she would ever gun for my job here, but the path of Moff and swoop jockey seemed like where she was heading. But the allure of fast food...."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian cracks a genuine smile, "Before the pandemic and pogroms and coming back here, we had a pretty quite life back in the Dragonflower Nebula. You know needing to keep a lower profile given the Empire's efforts to go after New Sith Order types after Malign went missing. But whenever I travelled away I would bring back Jolly Meal toys. I spoiled her with a collection that included everyone going back decades. And between that an Sith oriented education....she had to go and seize the means of Jolly Meal toy production and take over Biscuit Baron. I am quite proud."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Andrev is turning 17 this fall," Lynae shares the same parental pride smile that is fondness and mystified at the passage of time. "Does she want that education? Sith orientation, I mean, or is it just the natural avenue of progress that comes from your interactions with Malign?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"There are certain elements to Sith philosophy that remained core to us. In this case the belief of the supremacy of one's own will on the universe. Given her successes I think she took to the lesson." Crestian sips his tea, mildly amused at the irony that in this of all conversations he is walking down the road of 'this is what being a Sith means to me.'<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Imposing one's will, however, need not take the dark connotations of Death Stars and Force Choking and all that jizz. We decide whether are goals are good or bad or in between. The name I gave her, Astvara, is Sith for balance. It is a very important concept to me, and the most important of lessons to be instilled. I came to the Sith through way of Malign and Drayson Honos, but the philosophy I ascribe to persisted after they went missing and exist separate from whatever ties I continue to have with them. I care more for the Union than for any Sith group. And it is the Union that needs me. But I am no paragon democratic virtue. I exist to defend certain interests. Mainly to ensure the Union is never as so unprepared as it was."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Balance, aiming for balance, is admirable and ultimately a life long goal," Lynae agrees with a measured nod, having sipped from the cup of caf, seeming to enjoy the flavor as much as the caffeine kick. "For some. I want to believe that it's a goal for most people. But the reality is that 'balance' and 'normal' are variables that can and must be interpreted with the perspective filter of society, culture and all of the nuances that turn a handshake from a gesture of greeting to a threat assessment to ensure your companion isn't carrying a weapon boldly to stab you or shoot you with. That is, in many cultures, the reason for hand shakes in the first place. It was a display of boldness, courage, bravado and threat assessment. The practice of clinking cups together, when drinking, was to spread the liquid around, slosh it, so that not just ONE cup could carry a poison or a drug, it would risk everyone and also expose everyone while spread the risk around. Someone might taste it or fall ill before everyone else. All of these quaint cultural customs of ours, philosophies, hopes, ambitions, life goals - there's a unique perspective filter that needs to be recognized and respected, or we run the risk of using only our alien eyes and ears to understand what drives someone else."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian smiles and sets down his tea, "While balance is the philosophy that drives me, I suppose the true topic of this conversation is in fact threat assessment. And I suppose in coming to me here you have put out your hand as it were to see if there is a blade, blaster, or blight? So I suppose the question is whether I should start asking the real questions now or you. Not that I do not enjoy the philosophy, I do wish more of my conversations could be rooted in it. But we are here with some purpose are we not? I suppose I should start by saying there is no blade, blaster, or blight, but you have not made it easy.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Presav takes a deep breath, "I suppose I do relate to your current path in a certain way. I spent years with the Republic. I became very disillusioned after the Blitzkrieg. And that set me on the path where I was open and eager to be part of Malign's circle in spite of his own association with the Empire I so hated. That understanding though does not eliminate the problem of the fact your path has deviated far from the one that is here."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Live with all of this long enough, they all start to sound and feel the same. Empire. Caspian Union. Malign as Tantris or any of his many other guises, then around to the Republic. Spin out to the far flung words and their local governments, their system governments, and back again," Lynae has finished her caf and sets the now empty mug on the table, briefly displaying her empty hands. "No blade, no blaster, no plague, no blight," a half smile accompanying her words. "We're adults here, and long enough in the tooth to speak on the level."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lynae leans back in the chair, legs stretched forward, ankles crosses, fingers now lacing together over her abdomen. "I've withdrawn Andrev from the fall semester at the academy," speaking plainly. "I have some extended family, estranged but extended, that I will place him with in the interim until he decides where to continue his education. There's no reason to not be reasonable and amicable about this. I've resigned before, without fuss. I've cleared my tasks that were pending, Lt. Kanji is updating everything that needs to finish processing now," speaking of her own uniform assistant, "we'll do this clean and reasonable."
	
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Tantris....that is a really trip in the way back machine I had not thought about in some time." Sometimes a bit of nostalgia really cuts the tension. It was, however, odd sort of nostalgia remembering a false flag cult." Crestian recomposes himself, "At 16 Andrev is legally considered an adult here, however, I certainly can respect /his/ desire to unenrol given the circumstances if that is what he wants."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He picks up his tea again and takes a sip, "However, regardless of whatever impression you may have gotten, it was never our desire to have his resignation nor any intention to....use him as some pawn or whatever scenarios of caution you may considered. It has always been your resignation that was necessary, not desired, but necessary. The concern for him, which is indeed true for other's such as your half-brother, or even your crew was in what way your actions would reflect upon them. Unfortunately the bottom line is the path you have set yourself on has made you a security risk for the Union. Which invariably casts suspicion on others whether anyone intends it to or not. Hopefully with your resignation, that will put some distance out there for everyone concerned."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lynae's head moves subtly in a series of measured nods, "You are quite right, he is of age and considered adult. He will take the time during this off season to make a decision about his education institution future, and if I am hearing you correctly he can remain at the academy here in the fall, should he wish to reenroll?" she makes it a question but forges ahead. "Security risk," another slow nod, "that's a fair phrase. And a not incorrect one, either. Professional integrity, military integrity, it's all a mix of what I won't do. Knowing what I won't do doesn't make others wonder what I'm willing to do, or not to do, is on my shoulders. You'll see that, as I mentioned a few moments ago, everything on my desk is up to date. So," and she straightens in the chair, feet again flat to the floor, "resign or retire, which suits your narrative the best?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian lets out a small smile, "Andrev has quite the complicate origin story to be saddled with, you or Tom alone as a parent would set all sorts of expectation on him but with both, I am not sure I envy him that....I don't mean anything about either of your personally just it casts a long shadow. No matter where he makes his home or career, there is some level of complication that is sure to follow him unless he ends up a nerf herder somewhere under an assumed name....which you know is valid too. But Caspar is a world he has a right to should he wish. I have no desire to see that taken from him if he wants it, but also I will have no less respect for him on another world or another service. If he wants to return I will do what I can for him."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The Presav turns to the question of Lynae herself, "If I did not respect your integrity we would have arrived at this point far sooner and in a far more awkward manner. I take no joy in this result. But there is not getting around the fact that Lord Malus is on a path that is at odds with ours. And your association is too close. Whatever I believe or do not believe about you would or would not protect our security in relation to him, you support of him alone is enough to make a career here untenable."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He takes a deep breath, "I am truly touched that you would ask what suits the narrative more. Either resignation or retirement is fine really. The idea is to spare others the discomfort of you having to be fired and the reasons for it explained. I am not worried about the black eye for you or me. But it is the others in your orbit, I wish to create the softest landing for. So whatever you feel will reflect best on them." He pulls out a datapad and adds, "I have had your predecessor on the Ad Astra, Captain McBride, resume command. Of course now I feel bad about the desk job he has had at Omni Base in the interim. I feel like I owe him something, but alas hurt feelings are not grounds for promotion. But at least I think return to a familiar commander should smooth things over for the ship. If you want to have some transfer command ceremony, I would not consider it out staying your welcome, but nothing I want to push at anyone either."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Children are perfect hostages to ensure the good behavior of the parental units," Lynae replies as she rises to her feet. "Lets make it clean, shall we? I'm nearer to fifty than not and it's a reasonable time to retire," she removes her service weapon and other accompanying service gear as well and comm unit. "Give my regards to Captain McBride. His crew are well trained and the hand off will be without hitch to transition back to a known face and command style." She calls up the necessary form that she'd prepped in advance, presses her thumb to the screen to finish the last leg of official documentation then hands the military issue datapad over to Crestian as well. "There's several commendations and promotions on file marked for your review, I'd appreciate you considering them." She dusts her hands together then extends one to Crestian, "Take care of the Union, she's had a hard time protecting herself in the last couple of decades."

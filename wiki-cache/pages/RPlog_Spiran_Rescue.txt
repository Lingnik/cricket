&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He would have wished the had chance for a longer vacation, but truth was that was likely to never be possible again. Too many people depended on Crestian and Tracer to try to create a better galaxy for them. There was some solace in knowing that on Caspar there would be all the necessary amenities. Orleanon was a far cry from Spira but Sha would be able to host them all just fine there. Though for the rescued children the obvious place to bring them was really Marin Mountain. Its seclusion made it a better citadel than the grand Trinumvira Base for their purposes now. It was weird too just seeing off Atsvara and not his wife. Crestian had had not had many combat missions in recent years and he could think of no occasion in past that had his daughter so in the know. Yet, it was important that she was. A better galaxy meant having the next generation able to build on their gains they made. Regardless of path she chose for herself it could not be from a place of ignorance of the galaxy. As he makes his goodbyes the young girl remarks, "You look funny Tevas."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He did at that. Crestian's position in the Sith hierarchy meant he had his own suit of Hssiss Scout Armour. It was about as elite as one could get, but it was ironically enough designed for use by the New Sith Order's Trandoshan forces. There was much of the Sith's involvement with the Trandoshan's that was questionable. Their culture was archaic and yet still the Sith had manipulated them. Part of Crestian felt he should care more about that, but the sins of the past held little import when the problems of the present were at hand. He was happy enough to kill some Trandoshans today if necessary.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;To the teach the words of encouragement were short and too the point. She leaned on her bowcaster as if in anticipation when Crestian simply stated, "You're in charge." She was now guardian to the hopes of the future of two Moffs. And once does not have a teacher for the young of the Sith officers who does not have an understanding of power. In this moment and in this place Crestian meant to deputise her with all the authority he possessed. The children had to be here, but their safety never far from his mind and he would see Miss Cason burn down the whole of the Core Worlds before harm might befall them.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Finally he came to looking over Commander V1C3 and Councillor Dark. Their combat team was five strong. And he had spent the entirety of their time on their way to Spira and since arriving debating whether it was the right size. Should he have left one of them home, should he have brought more along. It all seemed so imperfect for a situation where every detail was so carefully planned. But they would do. The Droid had built for such missions in mind. The odd mix of politics and battle, not wholly unlike Crestian himself. And Sha would of all of his colleagues on the Union Council the one who could most appreciate what he was trying to do for the galaxy and why. She could accept the Sithification of Caspar as the its path to safety. Seeing her decked out with standard Caspian military armour and weapons he did have mind that more personalised gifts might be in order later.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;For Tracer's part, the size of the rescue squad was perfect. Too few, and failure was inevitable. Too many and you multiply the noise and the errors. With the five of them there were just enough to take the estate quickly and quietly. The plan was clear. Land, gain entry at the eastern door, secure the hall from the main rooms, and then hopefully find the younglings quickly in one of the smaller rooms.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;<br>Tracer's garb looks similar to Councilor Dark's. Her old CDF gear was in near new condition, the slate green camo perfect for their jungle-like environment. With her Kylan-3 at her hip and a blaster carbine rifle strapped to her back, the woman bristles with readiness. Pouches on her armored vest hold smoke and stun grenades. She'd given her instructions to Ashton and Longinus back at the camp - stick with Miss Cason - she's in charge! Though Roi and his Sullustan bride were there as well, the two sticking mostly to kitchen chores and readying everything for a quick departure. It was a shame their stay had to be too short. But she'd had 10 years ignoring the galaxy raising her children in a similar tropic paradise. Now it was time to return herself and her sidearm to usefulness.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tracer is at the boat, anxious to board and be underway.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Kipran Jawatha, dressed in a black jumpsuit, blast vest, and boots has a blaster for backup slung below the lightsaber at his waist. Anxious flutters fill his gut, uncalmed by any amount of meditation as he sits cross-legged on a picnic table. He lets go his thoughts to take in others. First here with the collection of would-be rescuers, then reaching out to the camp, then to the next island closest to them. And then everything beyond that dims. Determined, and trying to have faith in himself that he will be able to sense the children when they get closer, Kipran's mouth is set in a straight line and his blue eyes are nearly black as the dome of the sky darkens and night sets in.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian attempts to offer some reassurance to their embedded reporter as he made his way with her to the boat. Jordaan Mag was now deck out in farm more armour than she ever had been in her life. It was precaution that would hopefully not be needed but her job was not to stand in a business suit with a microphone. In fact with a camera to record she was in a position she normally wasn't in as she tended to be in front of the camera. But any good reporter wants to see their story through to the end and this one looked too good to not follow through, particularly with the confidence Crestian pushed about the importance of the mission and his assurance of success. His own helmet would have a camera too. Some work would have to be done on the trip home to make sure only the best material got used, but so long as the mission was successful it would all be good. A bisected Trandoshan would hardly be anything Crestian would be afraid to show the world. This was about showing the did good deeds, not that they were pansy ass Republic types. Hopefully they would get lucky and be able to loot a datapad or something along the way.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There would, however, be no prisoners, if the Trandoshans lived or died was rather immaterial. Nor was their great concern over what local authorities might find. They would follow the party line he set, the Sith Admiral was not against the notion of offing some local functionaries for lack cooperation if necessary. The fate of the galaxy was more important than the potential whining of planetary governor after all. They were not pansy ass Republic types.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As he boards the ship he looks to Tracer and Kipran. Mother-Son combat was bound to be a good bounding experience, "And now the real family vacation begins." Crestian may not have Dark Side energies to feed off of but he relished the mission and not just its objections. A final look was given towards Nat Pagle. His own armour was slightly more modest than the reporter, he was a little more experienced at this sort of thing, a little more aware of the parameters of what to expect albeit not quite that sort of adventurer himself at his base. "I am ready to go fishing Pat." The order was given.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the small craft starts to move out, Elana begins to feel the weight of her gear in the tropical evening humidity. The air was still offering no measure of relief to the party. They motor on for a few miles before Pagle cuts his engines and drifts towards the destination. It's now that the silence of the party becomes apparent. The air is filled with the sound of tropics - a few nocturnal birds shriek while tach occasionally lend their own shrill sounds to the mix.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Kipran watches the dark water go by as he looks over the back end of the boat, letting the wake's folding and refolding of the water fan out then disappear. Halfway there, he turns towards the front of the boat and closes his eyes. A deep breath, a slow exhale and...there! The young man starts visibly then resumes his concentration to secure the contact.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Elana sees her son's surprise as she was watching him surreptitiously from the front of the small craft. A rise of excitement swells within her and she give Crestian a look. The look is half adrenaline and half certainty and all Marine grit. If anyone had told her several months ago that she'd be here with this crew she wouldn't have believed them but right now she can't deny this is the time and place for her to be. She turns back to watch the imminent landing, ready to disembark as quickly and quietly as possible.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the finally approached the shore Crestian could not help but visit upon his own surprise at how life had ended up. He supposed he should consider himself primarily a naval commander but even that was a later invention for a man who a military academy philosophy professor and administrator. But yet his career had seem in work as marine, pilot, and spy as well. He was pleased by the team he assembled. Kipran's youthful pursuit of his destiny along with Malign's invitation into the Imperium had brought Elana back into the the galaxy and governorance. But Crestian felt like was helping her tap deeper roots and truly blossom in the new order of things. Besides two Presavs was better than one. He wasn't sure the nature of all of Kipran's missions, but the chance to marry noble purpose and power grab at the same time and to do so fully and not simply pay lip service to one or the other seemed an important lesson. It would make him a better officer and better ally.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Sha found herself meditating in a sense too. There was no call to the Force, no religious devotion, but still a peace and calm to be found in their purpose. The children they were here to rescue a product of oppression visit upon them for being different, that was her life. V1C3 too had his own sort of zen in the moment. He was an officer, respected by his peers both organic and inorganic, he was well paid and enjoyed good assignments, he had score this particularly plum assignment of all things where he might finally get to kill things again, his great joy in life. He felt blessed.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nat, of course, was calm. Fishing was a career about patience. After all as he had been known to say, "If you ask me, the best part about fishing is its slow pace. It gives one time to think, to reflect on past events and to plan for future ones. Oh, and it's also a great excuse to drink. Heavily." It was an important night, however, and he was reasonably if not perfectly sober. And emotions could be said to be contagious. For Jordaan she knew this was the calm before the storm, but in the midst of such professionals she was beginning to understand that the storm was not what they would be facing. No, they were there storm.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Five pairs of boots hit the sandy muck a few feet from the water's edge. Having approached from the east and not the main northern bay where guests docked, the beach is littered with debris from the last storms which offers cover for the mostly dark clad group against the white sand shore. Kipran takes point, being the flesh and blood tracking beacon for the children. His mother had asked if he could send thoughts to them when they get close. He said he wasn't sure but she asked him anyway, if he could, to try and reassure them. He could feel his mother's love, not just for him and his family but for the young victims and felt that love flow out towards the oppressed everywhere. It was almost too much to bear, her caring so much. He found himself pulling back at that point, fearing falling into any state of weakness now or when he went back to his peers where he had to keep his feelings hidden.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Elana, from her position further back on the narrow trail, is of course oblivious to the balancing act that is the young commander's life now. So far, he'd not been asked nor ordered to commit the kinds of atrocities his mother had taught them came from Imperial aggression. So far, Tyler Damion was a leader he could follow. Even with his inexperience, he knows that might not forever be the case.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Tracer's focus is on the mission. She slides down the night-vision visor on her helmet as they begin to lose moonlight as the jungle canopy overhead thickens. The jungle is loud. Sounds of insects buzzing and nocturnal creatures rustling through the brush or calling to one another. The sound of the water has disappeared as they reach five minutes inland. Pinpoints of light begin to appear. Now was the crucial time. Now was when they'd learn if the Trandoshans had bothered to post a watch or would run a perimeter check.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There was no patrol outside to compound. Ultimately the Trandoshans had failed to anticipate that there would be such an armed assault on their position. Guards stood outside the main entrance but this too was at least a problem delayed as they would not see their approach and would likely be among the last to have any room to try to stop them. The side entrance was unguarded but that did not mean it was unlocked.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The fancy armour, the targeting sensors, Crestian was sort of hoping for something to shoot. But no instead as followed after Kipran he found a door. They would find the locking mechanism inside easier to bypass but the external lock was where these raiders put some effort. It didn't simply need to be disabled it it needed to be deprogrammed. This was a skills the Caspian leader had honed better than his blaster skill though. The pictographs were vexing but manageable. He hoped the rest of the locks would be less work. The door made a small his as it began to open. Commander V1C3 stood ready with his blaster rifle. If a Trandoshan came out the door it was getting shot in the head.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;It's all Kipran can do to maintain against his drive towards his targets, patience being heavily applied as he watches the Admiral deprogram the exterior lock. The entirety of the party is still and silent in anticipation of the raiding about to happen. Kipran slips his lightsaber from it's position on his belt, letting it fall easily into his hand. Then with quiet, deep breaths he reaches out to the younglings inside, and attempts to channel all the love and caring his mother has to give - that he gives as well.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Elana meanwhile checks the safety on her Kylan, not intending to fire it but lighting up it's power cells just in case. Clicks a whirrs break the silence as the group waits, spring-loaded.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With the door open Crestian made a small hand gesture to indicate Kipran could enter. For his own part the Admiral re-brandished his M434 Blaster Pistol setting himself up to follow the Commander in. There was a patrol of three of the Trandoshan raiders in the hallway but they were out the line of sight of the door Crestian just opened and indeed were just far enough away to not be able to see anyone potentially enter the hallway. The patrol, however, was between their party and the room that had Shen having just moved past it in their approach towards the end of the hallway with the now open door.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Further behind the Zeltron Councillor moved to close distant the others, not wish to get too far behind the more veteran combatants. She had seen action in Crestian's efforts to arm the refugees during the pogroms on Mergansar but compared to the others she was still newer to the world of fighting. Commander V1C3 would continue to pull up the rear, with his blaster rifle could shoot from behind the others and he was ready for anyone attempting to flank their position, perhaps slightly wishing such an attempt would be made so there would be more shooting.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Kipran gives the admiral a quick nod and moves inside silently through the small alcove before hitting the hall. He stops and waits giving a 'hold' hand signal to the group. He was letting the Trandoshans come to him. He flattens himself against the closer alcove wall as the patrol of three absent-mindedly walk and chit-chat in their native tongue. As they approach the alcove, Kipran flicks his finger and with it a small pebble flys to the door opposite with a clacking sound. The three turn their attention in that direction, check to make sure the door is locked, but discuss it briefly and move on down the hall away from the party leaving the way clear between the room holding the boy...Shen...Kipran tells him...don't be scared...friends are here. For a moment, he considers turning right and taking out the three hunters first but he feels Shen's anxiety rise. There'd be time to deal with the dirty Trandoshans later. He takes steps down the hallway towards where it turns left just before Shen's door.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Elana, Kylan held up and ready, follows the Zeltron and leaves watching the door and the Trandoshans who have turned the corner in V1C3's capable sensors.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian was impressed by Kipran's restraint. Patience was an important part of being a successful Sith, far too many foolishly sought to fight sooner than necessary or prudent to satiate a desire for violence, but it was hardly the best tact for victory. The goal was control, power, not wanton violence. Not that he had been expecting Darth Malign or the Grand Admiral to be teaching the young man any different, but youth often had ideas of their own. It require some reminding himself of such tenants to get Crestian himself down the hall but he moved with reasonable stealth, his armour was designed for scouts after all. For Sha and V1C3 the first patrol was a tempting target as well. The Droid in particular ran through calculations of the excuses he could render for firing first. Ultimately the knowledge mission's importance would keep his trigger finger in line. He had a reputation to maintain as a respected officer after all.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With quicker steps Kipran moves to Shen's door, having not seen or sensed any sentients in the estate's open front hall. He reaches a hand towards the much simpler panel lock and within a few moments he smiles as the door slides open. Per their plan, Kip steps in gaining eye contact with Shen, the commander's smile warming at the sight of the frightened little boy. A sadness grips Kipran for a moment, but he boxes it up and puts it away to deal with later. He lifts a gloved finger to his lips urging the boy to stay quiet.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Leaving Crestian to watch the front hall doors, Elana slides in behind Kipran and goes and picks up the boy putting him on her hip. She whispers to him while stroking his hair. "It's okay sweetie. We're taking you to mommy and daddy..." Within seconds she is headed towards the hallway.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As she exits with Shen, Kipran takes a moment to search out the boy's sister. "Rahasia...." Then he starts for a moment. Stepping out into the hall, he looks at Crestian in seeming surprise. "She says she ready for us," he says in a whisper. "...but the guards like to hang out by her door and toss chance cubes...I'll handle this." He gives his superior a moment to object.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There are no objections from the Admiral. Crestian had no reason to doubt Kipran's competency in doing what he stated and more over it served his purpose more to react to the course his subordinate set forth rather than to set one for them. The patrol however begins to turn back towards their end of the hallway and the Droid makes a gesture towards the Zeltron who is in his line of sight. Sha for her part motions for Tracer now holding the boy to not move further down the hallway, placing herself in front of the two, blaster at the ready. Combat was starting to look unavoidable though the change to get patrol into a crossfire was looking like a possibility.<br>

She just needed to get to the alcove to get out into the jungle with her precious cargo. She soothes the younger boy as best she can as the seconds take hours for them all.<br>

He doesn't hesitate. Things needed to stay quick and quiet lest any other Trandoshans from elsewhere on the estate try to violently stop them. Kipran darts between the Ms. Dark and the Admiral and sprints silently back down the hall. Passing V1C3 he leaps upward and forward as the patrol is turning around. Descending, his lightsaber flashes on then in a zigzag motion and the three burly lizards hit the floor as he turns and sprints back towards the party.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Elana tosses her head towards the way they came in, indicating to the Zeltron that she was heading out. Since the kids weren't together, the plan was to take the first rescued immediately to the boat while the others continued to the second child.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As Tracer emerges from the compound Nat Pagle flashes a pre-arranged signal from the boat indicating the path is clear, naturally his blaster is at the ready just in case that changes. For Jordaan this moment provides the first images of what she really came here for as she is able to record the image of Moff Elana Tracer, former Presav of the CDU carrying Shen Nettic to safety from his Trandoshan subduers. It provided a stark contrast to the terrorism that had started her career covering Caspian news she unceremoniously had to get blood sprayed by massacred children. Balance was being restored one youngling at time.<br>

Finding himself closest to Sha Dark, her response was swift plunging her cutlass through him and pushing his falling corpse back in the room. It felt good to end her enemies again. One did not feel authentic as Minister of Justice without administering it on occasion.<br>

"Just like shooting Selkath in a cracker barrel." the Droid remarked to himself. Not wanting to see their escape path flanked Crestian motioned for the Zeltron Councillor to follow Kipran towards the girl while he turned back towards the fire fight unloading his blaster at the raiders as well, "Dude you got to find better holo-films to quote, that one just does not make sense."<br>

The young commander gives the Zeltron an appreciative look before continuing on with her at his back. Seconds burn as he slides up to the first door, unlocks, and sees it slide open. An empty hall, brightly lit and strewn about with crates of supplies means move onto the next door, this one a bit more secure. Kipran hesitates, considers waiting for the Admiral to come use his skill at slicing this sucker but they were running out of time. He takes his lightsaber and jams it into the panel then slides the door open by sheer manual force. With a growl he's through and heading to the girls door.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Outside, Elana runs as fast as she can without falling, darting along the jungle path towards Nat Pagle and the reporter. Breathless, she falls into a seat on the boat, hugs Shen even tighter and exchanges a quick smile with Jordaan.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As Rahasia has related through the Force to Kipran the Trandoshans did enjoy playing with chance cubes outside her door. As Kipran and Sha make their way into the final that hall way their game is obvious though the noise had begun to drive them down the hallway and they had their backs turned as they now were in front of their security room on their way down the opposite of the hallway.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Meanwhile Crestian and V1C3 pushed pressed their advantage on the other end of the compound forcing the raiders back into the security room as they had now doubled the body count to eight in total now with blaster burnt bodies laying just beyond the lightsabered slash victims. That security room was now emptied of the Tranoshan raiders. The Admiral began quickly looking for datachits. That is how all of this start after all it seemed prudent to try to get collect any information they could while they were there. The Droid meanwhile manned a security console unlocking the remaining doors.<br>

In several heartbeats Kipran has the door open and finds a smiling Rahasia waiting for him. It would be best to head back the way they came since it was a known route. He kneels in front of the girl getting down on her level. "This is Sha. I want you to take her hand and don't let go, okay? Shen is safe but we gotta run, alright?" The girl nods and reaches out for Sha's hand as Kipran exchanges a glance with her, showing a cocky smile before leading the way out.<br>

Once he had several datachits packed for later analysis the Admiral joined the Droid Commander with the console creating false alarm at the main door. This would redirect the Trandoshans back away from the side entrance after Kipran, Rahasia, and Sha crossed the point where they were closer to it themselves. The hope was it would pull them around in circles. And indeed none seemed to be blocking their escape out the side.<br>

The hallway smells of burnt ions and blasted Trandoshans as Sha has herself between the girl and any sight of the carnage at the end of the corridor. Out the door they go and into the night, hoping to find Nat Pagle's beacon once they hit the jungle path they took in from the service side of the island.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;In the boat, little Shen is given a blanket to wrap up in and a sweet treat to keep him calm and occupied. "Rashi! Rashi!" the boy exclaims suddenly looking up. Elana's finger flies to her lips and she shushes him so he whispers loudly with a big smile, "Rashi's coming!" Elana peers into the jungle hoping that everyone's children made it out today. Except for the dirty Trandoshan's of course.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Kipran exits first a couple steps ahead of Sha and Rahasia. As Nat flashes the all clear signal the Sith apprentice motions Sha and Rahasia foward staying a step behind them as the three run to the boat. More excellent footage for IGN's Jordaan Mag. Crestian and V1C3 bring up the rear. But by the time the start making their way with the other three now far ahead the signal from the angler changes. The Droid sees it first and two turns to see two Trandoshan's barrelling towards them. The ones inside may be running in circles bit there but the outside guards from the main door seem to have made their appearance. A fire-fight ensues that is difficult to make out from the shore.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A number of close calls on each side before the Droid manages to bring down one of the raiders. The other charges at Crestian using strength to physically knock down the Admiral and crack a couple of ribs. The tussel on the ground, a wrestling match that the Sarian seems ill equipped for until it all suddenly stops with Presav Tarasar stabbing the man in throat with his Rodian Dagger.

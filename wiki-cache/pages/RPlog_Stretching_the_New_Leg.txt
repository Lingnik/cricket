Biscuit Baron - Neon District - Imperial City: Selene

A bright light in Selene's new Neon District, this recently constructed Biscuit Baron was built to serve the casual dining needs of the planets increasing population. Though it has seen a reduction in its market share thanks to increased competition in recent years from Caspar Caff, Biscuit Baron remains the most prolific casual dining restaurant chain in the galaxy. Its offerings vary by time of day with the Bantha Breakfast Biscuit, usually enjoyed with blue sauce, being popular in the mornings. The daytime and evening offerings are often sold in bundles such as the QuickSnack, QuickSnackLite, and Jolly Meals for youngling. The bundles often include toys and other collectable merchandise.

The current decor keeps the primary colour palette of red, black, white, and yellow long employed by the Tagge Company but unlike the previous standard layout which featured white and yellow more heavily, the current design makes great use of red and black. The wide range on items on the Biscuit Baron menu makes it an appealing option for a variety of different beings with that now live and work in growing harmony on Selene. A large sign outside the building depicts the stylized logo of Baron Orman Tagge wearing his trademark goggles. This particular location boasts a line of drive-up on windows on either side of the building.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Biscuit Baron was not classy dining by any stretch of the imagination, though any franchisee of worth kept the place cleaned and well stocked. What it did provide was convenience a quick and affordable meal or snack to the being on the go, with a wide range of options catering to Humans and non-Humans like. It was also valued for its ubiquity. No other dining establishment in the galaxy had so many locations, though in recent years Caspar Caff was giving them some competition in that regard. It seemed obvious enough for Crestian, who was still on Selene coordinating matters with Imperium leadership to suggest an outing for the recovering Johanna, though he couldn't quite recall how the idea of Biscuit Baron entered the conversation. "You have really never been to a Biscuit Baron?"<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Can't say that I have," Johanna replies half-absently, the garish colors and plastic compounds all competing for attention in a cacophony of consumerism, "Uh... what do you recommend?" She's heard tales of the artificial meats and suspicious fats used in the preparation of the "food," and a tiny wave of nausea brings her hand towards her mouth as she squints at the menu. "I'm more the type to cook at home, though the gods only know how many times the kids begged me to take them to a Biscuit Baron," she grunts, her newly-grown leg still somewhat stiff in its hip socket, "I could never see the appeal."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Aye its not generally what I would consider particular quality. A lot of it is far more appealing to the unsophisticated taste buds of youth....hence marketing such as Jolly Meal toys. Suffice to say much of my experience in recent years with the place as been as a father, Atsvara has all the toys going back decades. I have a sneaking suspicion at this point that she has made arrangement with the chain to just send her the toys as soon as they are developed, her collection of the new ones seems to manifest before her mother or I have a chance to get her there." Crestian motions to some of the other people in the establishment, naturally kept distant enough from the Moffs and little phased by the security that as accompanied them, "For many people this is there way to get a quick meal before or after work, folks with out the time or facilities for cooking. I would say standard things to start with would be the Bantha Breakfast Biscuit....though a Jolly Meal is another option. Baby's first Jolly Meal toy and all of that."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"The Breakfast Biscuit seems the less adventurous choice, so let's go with that," the woman decides, managing a brief chuckle at the thought that it has taken her nearly forty years to set foot in one of the galaxy's biggest fast-food chains. "I can't help but feel like we're intruding somehow, Crest. Just busting in on everyone's normal day with a security team... man, that's the part about this whole gig I hate the most." She leans in to say the latter part more quietly, not wishing to sound ungrateful for the protection her brother's Imperium offers. "My habit of going everywhere alone might have encouraged those responsible for the attack on Bespin, though. I was so busy wanting to prove that I was just like everyone else, that I forgot there's those who'd take advantage of it." A twinge begins in her knee, making one of the hard plastic benches seem mighty appealing. "Mind if we sit?"<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Leading Johanna to a table after dispatching one of the security personnel to stand in line for the food, "Well we are on Selene, so for better or worse the people here are use to muckity muck types like us roving through their world. But I know exactly what you mean. The idea of security detail. Even through the election and the attacks at Kichnar vote returns, I eschewed walking around very often in 'friendly' territory with a notable detail. Part of why I have made Commander Vee-Won my adjutant is at least that gives me a body guard that is friend and useful for more than just shooting things." Though the Droid in question is not currently present. "It is amazing how much staff some other Moffs keep around. Even Elana, like I know her house warming was a party....and like she is exactly the right type of person we need, a good friend....I am like you, I prefer to do my own cooking when possible. Sure big party might mean some junior officers are helping out, carrying plates. But I don't employ a cook or anything in my villa....but you know privilege I live in a villa." After their food is brought to their table Crestian adds, "That reminds me, if we can find a quiet enough moment between battles I would like to have you and whatever of your family might want to come for a belated Life Day dinner on Leviathan. As it is I owe Elana's brood some datadaggers as Life Day presents."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Well bring as many as you like my dining room sits forty. I mean it will probably be noisy with the spirits of ancient Sith Damions gasping at a Life Day celebration in a Sith dining room, but you know, I didn't just name my daughter balance because it sounded pretty. I had been working on the menu, but I got a little distracted by you getting yourself blown up." Crestian opens his jolly meal box, unwrapping the sandwich and handing the toy to Johanna, "Like I said Atsvara already has ALL the toys. Anyway I figure we will have your brood, Tracers, naturally a few other people to fill things out if they are available such Kasha and Tiali, Zaalbacca of course he is really my right arm with Caspar, Zubindi....pretty much all the people I left back on Bespin or sent there to help out it seems."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Palladus won't bother you," Johanna snorts, "He answers to me now, and I have no need of his repulsive hide smearing its Dark Side drama everywhere at a Life Day celebration." She takes a careful bite of the biscuit, finding it pleasantly fluffy, the meat within nothing like the frightening, chewy mess she'd imagined. "Not bad," the pilot nods, "I think I get the appeal. Salt, fat, carbohydrates... all the things our physiologies love. Better not get the little one too used to it, though, or I'll have another Jolly Meal beggar on my hands."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian sounds a little surprised, "I had mostly been kidding. Just saying the decor of the represents quite the opposite culture. Then again the aspiration of cultures ought to be defined less dogmatically. But then we also find this sorts of people in the galaxy who go rabid when their petty little ways of running their lives get challenged. At least our Falleen friend is far less chaotic and destructive when he pushes back....a headache for another day. Do you remember anything of import before the explosion?"<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"No," Johanna replies, glancing down at the cheerful toy sitting on her tray before her gaze grows distant, "The last thing I remember is getting into the lift to head down to the refinery. After that..." she shrugs, picking absently at the biscuit, "... it's all a black hole." It's an uncomfortable thing to have such a gap in ones memory, and as she continues picking at the food rather than eating it, it becomes apparent that it's a topic best left for another time. "Anyway," she says a little too loudly, "You were saying, about our Falleen friend?"<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Well he serves his purpose by providing more civilised leadership to Hutt space, but he certainly is not a team player. You and I each have our agenda's that are independent of your brother's but we are still all a team. I fear Nasiri will always ultimately cling back to this idea of culture in Hutt Space having some sort of exceptionalism that whines against our outside morality....but more over his own machinations. Life would be a lot simpler had he brought a different woman with a lightsaber as a date. He has this very foolish habit of not knowing which cards to play face up and which to play face down. Which given his persecution complex and obvious cynicism about any of our noble endeavours proves problematic."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I wish he hadn't brought /any/ woman with a lightsaber as a date," Johanna sighs, rubbing at her forehead and then pinching the bridge of her nose in an attempt to ward off a headache, "The report said that they captured Fett, yes? I can only imagine what Lorda Gejalli has up his very voluminous sleeves for the bounty hunter. Ugh, it makes my head hurt." She picks up one of the chunks she'd pulled off the biscuit moments before, slipping the morsel into her mouth and chewing a few times before fishing the next one out of her little pile of biscuit-bits. "I know he thinks I'm a fool for my crusade against slavery," she says with another shrug, "But then, those who have never felt the oppression of slavery have the luxury of such a viewpoint. You cannot imagine what it was like on Tatooine when I was a girl, Crestian. It was bad enough just before Malign and I began my campaign, but the old days were even worse. Only the most apathetic, disconnected creature could stand idly by and shrug their shoulders had they the means to do something."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian smirks, "Well if nothing else the other woman at least would not bring the deeper questions. My report simply mentions that it was a lightsaber wielder, but suffice it not hard to gather that it was someone in particular rather than he is just great at hiring." The Presav sips his Ardees and ponders the slave issue, "Well on the matter of slavery in this regard there are two thoughts. The first is practical, the morality aside, we have decided to slavery is to be removed from the galaxy. The Hutts need to get on board with that, not the Lorda sitting on a fence claiming some personal higher ground morally while having no real investment in trying to improve the galaxy. The cultures who still don't consider it immoral are irrelevant, they will have to adapt as we all have had to adapt to our societies in various ways to survive. Certainly my embrace of an electoral system and their embrace of a Sith a leader is based on mutual self-interest and the great good not ideologically purity." Another sip of his Ardees and he adds, "And then there is the morality, and it is a horrible. If it were simply a system of poor people selling themselves into contracts....that would be bad but a problem of financial structures in the galaxy. But targetting people, particular by sex and species and kidnapping them. Civilisation cannot exist under such conditions. I can't imagine the life of a slave. But I have seen the fruits of oppression, the genocides on Caspar and Mergansar are still fresh. Enslaving people is just another way to go about genocide. I stand against that most fervently....still in the meantime at least the Lorda makes a good a trade partner, the money from selling him Shearwaters is useful to our economy."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Johanna listens quietly to Crestian's thoughts on Nasiri and the necessity of eliminating the evils of slavery. "I hate fence-sitters as much as you do," she grumbles before popping another few chunks of breakfast biscuit into her mouth, the caff she's ordered with it surprisingly decent, "It's cowardice, not diplomacy. But then I don't think Nasiri is a fence-sitter; he's loudly declared often enough that slavery is simply a fact of life in certain parts of the galaxy and that I shouldn't bother with my fool crusade because some regions and cultures simply can't be changed. He can be very conceited when he wants to be, hiding behind some pretext of being too urbane for the backwater likes of me... but I see him for what he is, an opportunist and someone who is just as in love with his own power as any Sith Lord, his protestations to the contrary notwithstanding." Another sip of caff washes down the crumbs left in her mouth, the brunette glancing again to the toy before pale fingers pluck it from the tray for closer inspection. "Much like Atsvara, Nasiri is a collector, though his toys are all gathered in service to the expansion of his own little empire within Hutt space. I do wish you hadn't told Malign about the woman with the lightsaber, Crest."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"And I wish I hadn't had to tell him. The problem is that she flashed a lightsaber in a crowded room full of people and your brother already has ample reason to want to investigate the situation. It is possible that it would not have gotten back to him that she was there, but if it did it would seem like a glaring absence in the report. I kept it as vague as I felt I could....one of the Lorda's many trouble bringing feats is the fact that he makes his contingency plans against the Imperium and gets too prideful about it all. He feels like his persecuted but really he is just lacking cleverness. It is not like I don't have my own contingencies to continues to preserve what I have in various circumstances."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian sips his Ardees and sighs, "Right now the best thing I feel I can do is it to try to limit how much he feels 'his' people need to do on Bespin. I know he talked about sending ships, but I am trying to have my people help out as much as possible with the investigation and the engineering analysis and such. Hopefully if enough answers can percolate out of there and problems solved without his people having to do it....it will maintain a little more serenity in your world. As for the time when he decides to gets salty about the witch....neither one of his are responsible for keep track of Tyler's toys. He needs you, and clearly he himself has chosen to let Nasiri continue to his thing."<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Your contributions are greatly appreciated, believe me," Johanna replies, a rueful smile forming upon her lips, "My brother's methods are sometimes a little... extreme, perhaps." She doesn't mention that she'd prefer not to have her entire domain groped and sniffed out by the Circle, but the Sarian is already familiar with their methods and can probably deduce as much from her hesitation in bringing the full resources of the Imperium to bear upon Bespin. "If anything, your diplomatic touch will be welcomed. We Damions aren't so good with diplomacy." She sets the toy back down, her other hand moving to rest lightly upon her abdomen.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Crestian smiles as he finishes up his sandwich. "I am not sure how diplomatic I was about anything. I certainly have not had time familiarise myself with whatever governing structures you might have in place but the worlds you control have generally been ones that had traditions of corporate leadership or crime lords so I am afraid I have generally assumed a flying by the seat of our pants sort of vibe....and with the Imperium vibe usually involving Task Forces and agents have tried....well I suppose I gave the Imperator my report to him, I suppose I should give my report to you Grand Moff."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He chuckles a bit clearly state the title more in fun than anything, he knew she was not fond of it and was not making use of it with the sort or respect she would balk at. "Miss Quinne was shaken up when the explosion first occured and Boba Fett broke the wall, but she snapped out of it quickly. In your absence, and without any proper authority to do so, I left her as acting Baroness-Administrator....but you know I am a Duke so I should be able to make a Baroness. In any event given the disaster it seemed like you needed a symbol of state that represented /your/ state. Councillor Zaalbacca has stayed on site to assistant your mine foreman in dealing with the diaster in his capacity as my Minister of Labour and Industry, he is an experience union boss. I had ordered Admiral Honos to interdict the system, but on his departure with you Captain Drorq commanding Leviathan assumed fleet control and kept unauthorised departures to a minimum. I have sent some reinforcements from my 4th Fleet with the CMS Renaissance of Innovation as flag. They brought Councillor Sirlahn Inglet who heads SubPro, so your people will have ample support to make sure the city is still safe and deal with the matter if that proves not to be the case."<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Nibbling on his fried vegetable sticks he adds with a bit of grin, "I cut myself off with Ara in terms of stating who the Section Chief was I have sifting through the evidence left behind in the attacks. I figure she does not need to know and certainly your ex-husband does not need to know that our favourite tailor is also one of the leaders of my intelligence establishment. But it is your space so you know what I am up there. His reports thus far indicate one promising lead from the brothel attack. Some casino chips that were found there are believed to be have been left by the perpetrators but he has traced them back to a variety of casinos closer to my space, the Casino Royale on Vorzyd V, and the Crimson Casino and Grand Casino on The Wheel."<br>

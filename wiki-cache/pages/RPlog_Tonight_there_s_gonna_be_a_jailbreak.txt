'''The Oubliette'''

<small>Spacestation 1138, in the Karthakk System, has a long and storied history as a high security prison. It was once controlled by the Trade Federation prior to the Clone Wars. Now sitting on the outskirts of Imperium space, it serves that power and its allies as a supermax prison with most of the inmates encased in carbonite.</small>

<small>Nicknamed 'The Oubliette' by members of the Moff's Council it is said that no complete manifest of the prison exists and that in fact no one not even the warden is aware of the identity of all of the prisoners housed in it. While the prison's existence is not unknown given its long history any additional detail is a matter considered classified by the Imperium and her allies. While it said that multiple allied powers have sent at least one of their own notaries there to be forgotten, no government has gone on the record to confirm this.</small>

<small>The station itself consists of a central shaft flanked by two twin pylons that rotate around the station. On the ends of each pylon are heavy capital ship turrets, with smaller anti-starfighter laser cannons located along the length of the defense pylons. A droid starfighter hangar bay is located towards the top of the station, though larger starships are accommodated with docking tubes on the outer pylons. On the flat surfaces of the pylons are also a pair of airlock doors, which connect to the inner corridors of the station.</small>

The prison drifts, unmarked on charts, unseen by passing ships, a fortress of black metal folded into the quiet vastness of deep space. No stars shine here-only the cold glow of automated sentries, their sensors sweeping the void with silent, unwavering vigilance. Officially, it does not exist. Unofficially, it houses the nightmares the galaxy cannot quite bring itself to kill.

It is a place without windows, without names, without mercy. Inside, the air hums with the steady pulse of machinery, the hidden lungs of a facility built for containment, for control. Rows of prisoners stand frozen in carbonite, their faces locked in eternal expressions of rage, despair, or something worse. The walls gleam, sterile and unyielding, every surface designed to suppress, to erase, and to hold the galaxy's most dangerous beings in a state of perfect, endless stillness.

And yet, something stirs. A ship slides into the station's shadow, dark against dark, its approach a careful needle threading through the gaps in sensor coverage. Aboard, Mitra watches the prison's rotating bulk, her expression unreadable, her mind already inside those walls. She has studied this place, traced its defenses with the patience of a scholar and the precision of a predator. Beside her, a different energy entirely: Skawn. His crew lurks behind him, a collection of the cruel, the cunning, the entirely unhinged. They do not need to be told what comes next. Inside that fortress of silence and circuitry, an emperor waits, entombed in carbonite, his fate stolen from time. The guards believe him forgotten, believe themselves untouchable, believe in the thickness of their walls and the strength of their weapons. Belief is a fragile thing. In moments, it will shatter.


Mitra's stoicism is not shared by her partner in this crime. Skawn is a creature of chaos and low passions. The Twi'lek will play his role for sure, he does nothing to disrupt the stealth approach, but it is not his style and not how things will go down on his end. His crew is full of freaks of all sorts who have clung to his banner for an excuse and license to satiate their passions unrestrained. Figures like the Neimoidian Doctor Mod and Nez the Killer Kubaz Cannibal. His crew is full of victims, the enslaved, the experimented upon. The top leadership positions in fact held by concubines of Skawn who he has warped to continue the cycle of oppression and violence upon others. In many ways to the Smooth Talker crew the liberation of this prison is a hope to save kindred spirits they image to share their depravity and who they hope will further set the galaxy a blaze for the sheer fun of it.


A new resident to the prison, an unassuming and cold carbonite slab, hangs in wait. Simple readouts dimly illuminate the area around it, indicating that its occupant remains in stasis. The mechanical sentries ignore the object, it is just one of many, their programming focusing them to react to a contingency which should never occur.


1138 breathes in silence a moment longer before the Smoother Talker whispers a signal through the station's skin: a quiet and elegant corruption that snakes through its circuits, unwinds its logic, and hushes the facility with surgical precision. Comms go dark. Life support stutters. Deep in the station's core, its great, unseen heart seizes, skips a beat, then dies.

For thirty seconds, there is nothing. No alarms, no movement, only the frozen figures entombed in carbonite and the distant hum of power bleeding away...


By the time the first redundant system begins to sputter uncertainly back to life, the ship has settled in the hangar and chaos erupts in the half-light of returning power, the first confused flickers of automated defenses stuttering back online just in time to meet the boarding party's violence. First the droids: skeletal sentinels that twitch and lurch into motion, their photoreceptors blinking in confusion before their programming catches up. They lift their weapons... too slow. Blaster fire cuts through them before they can align a single shot, Mitra moving through the chaos without waste, each strike a precise calculation, each step purposeful. A flick of her hand sends a droid spiraling, a crackling white saber carves through another. Even the deaths she grants are efficient. Behind her, Skawn is already laughing, already carving a wilder path through the fray, his crew spilling out behind him like a tide of knives and hunger. Farther in the station, alarms begin their delayed scream, and the facility, so cold and controlled, begins to wake up to the nightmare now inside its walls. More droids will come. Guards, too. But the order of this place -- the absolute control -- has already shattered. Something worse than prisoners has broken in.


Breaking into Imperium supermax could be a defining day in many people's lives, for Skawn it was just a typical Taungsday. Aye he was laughing alright as his purple blade made fast work of man and machine alike. He looked forward to the new friends he was about to make, while ignoring those of his own who might fall behind him. More food for Nez, or spare parts for Mod to play with. His ladies were all unique but all could be replaced with someone new. But they were all trained killers themselves, it was really the only way to survive in his world.

As he reaches the first section of frozen prisoners he shouts back to the Kubaz with glee, "They have Joffrey Damar here. I love that guy! You may have a little competition at the dinner table Nez."


Lighting flickers and the prison's interior is bathed in red, as the backups and infrared projectors come online. Klaxons begin to blare and emergency lights illuminate the deck indicating the designated paths to hangars for evacuation shuttles.


The corridor beyond is a procession of silent ghosts, carbonite slabs standing in perfect rows. Dim readouts on their casings flicker, feeding heartbeats and oxygen levels into the great, unfeeling machinery of the prison. Mitra moves past them without a glance. Somewhere in the maze, buried deep in this mechanical tomb, is Malus. A man who once ruled, who once tore the galaxy into shapes that suited him. She reaches out, stretching her senses across the sterile halls, probing the frozen dark, expecting a whisper... a pulse... some remnant of the power he was said to wield... but there is nothing. No presence, just the dull and voiceless silence of deep hibernation.

She exhales, unimpressed. Perhaps it was all self-aggrandizement, the usual pomp and myth men weave around themselves like cloaks, draping their frailties in the illusion of invincibility. She has met emperors before, heard their grand visions, seen them claw their way to the heights of power only to fall, screaming, when the tide turned. They always believed themselves different, that their rule was destiny, that the Force itself bent to them. And yet... he is somewhere /here/, a slab of carbonite indistinguishable from the wretches around him, locked away like an artifact no one remembers how to use.

She is still considering this when the guards arrive. A small force of four, maybe five, each stepping into the corridor with weapons raised, the weight of their training pressing down against their fear. The hangar would have told them what they needed to know: this is not an invasion meant to be stopped.


Let there be blood? Let there be carnage? Let there be unhealthy amount of chaos, confusion, arson and property damage? Yes, to all of the above. In the wake of Skawn and the rest of the bloodthirsty horde, Vix moves with a silent efficiency that ensures that every sweep of the blade or searing blaster bolt is aimed with precision and zero wasted energy. As this promises to be a slog of epic proportions, or so Vix is given to understand, there’s no sense in wasting energy early on. Something always goes wrong. Somehow there is often a need to move - with haste - back toward the insertion point. And the last thing Vix wants to do is be the last one out the hatch or the one asked to stay behind so the team can live on? Vix is not the noble kind. No one that is part of this crew is the noble kind. They are, all of them, in one form or another, the rubbish and refuse and recycle bin rejects of the universe. This one and probably all other ones, to be fair. And when the guards arrive in that corridor that already reeks of blood and waste and the nasty fluids that seep out of bodies that are no longer sealed within the pressure suit of their skins? Vix angles her head slightly from side to side, popping the upper vertebrae in her neck, a ghost of a smile forming on her face. Oh yes. Let’s dance.


It is not hard for Skawn to keep track of Mitra in spite of the chaos and follow her down the path she takes. His crew would be busy will killing guards, freeing prisoners, killing some of those prisoners for kicks, and so forth, but there was a mission to be done here. And Skawn did have some modest investment in it succeeding as planned. He was many things but he was not about to let down an employer on a contract, that is the kind of bad reputation he did not need. So soon he finds himself behind the guards who had come in response to Mitra's arrival at the slab of the former Emperor.

Beside Skawn is a young Sarian woman clad all in black leather with a vibrosword. The second mate of the Smooth Talker, whom Skawn rechristened as Sia Cia. Once among many youths kidnapped by Skawn on his return trip after the Bespin Brothel brawling, she has now been twisted to a point of no return. She doesn't wait for Skawn starting to slice at the first guard she finds. Skawn laughs some more and takes his blade towards the next.


Blaster turrets descend from a section of bulkhead, sentinels to a room that seals itself shut with blast doors. Barrels swivel and train on organics the sensors detect, the loud whine of capacitors charging breaking through the cacophony of the boarding assault.


Above her, the air erupts with blaster fire. Mitra moves through it untouched, path already mapped in her mind, each inexorable step bearing her closer to her prize. She does not look back to see how Skawn's creatures handle themselves against the storm. It is irrelevant. The strong survive. The useful endure. The rest... well, there is no shortage of bodies in the galaxy.

She reaches the turbolift. A panel flickers red, but she does not wait for permission, doors groaning open in surrender to the unnatural power she wields. She lifts a hand, pointing to Vix. "You there: come with me." Mitra does not ask her name. Does not care. She tilts her head, eyes narrowing for half a second, a thread of thought sent spiraling through the Force. Skawn will know where she is going. If he follows, if he sends more... fine. If not... no matter. The doors slide shut. The lift hums to life. And beneath them, deeper in the belly of this place, Malus waits.


The hum and whine of the capacitors charging is a distinctly noxious sound that Vix recognizes immediately. Anyone who has ever been on the wrong side of those bloody things knows what the sound means. Her mind is already beginning to work trajectory and angles when Mitra points at her. One pale eyebrow arches ever so slightly upward as she steps over a pile of bloody remains and leaves Skawn and the rest to their play. Mitra, for what it is worth, is eyed with the same amount of healthy trepidation that the weapons systems were subject to. She steps into the lift anyway. If she had any amount of self-preservation would she be on this kind of crew? Once the lift hum to life Vix takes the moment to check the charge on the blaster and to wipe the blade off on the sleeve of her jacket. "He won't like it if he doesn't get his quote of blood," her voice is quiet. Sort of gravely, as though she's spent way too many years smoking. She probably has.


After dispatching the guards in front of him and Sia Cia, Skawn turns to find a fresh batch assembled behind them. Choosing to have a little fun he reaches out with the force using it to alter their sense of worth. They soon begin to cower away feeling themselves greatly out matched. As they soon are safely out of the way but not out of sight, Sia Cia trades her blade for a blaster and begins to shoot each one in the back. She knew her cruelty would get a rise out of her master. After the last body falls they continue to follow the path of Mitra with Skawn remarking, "Enough foreplay for now, plenty other people to still be fucked."


Unseen tendrils tug and pull at Mitra and Skawn; a mindless unguided force, perhaps even imperceptible amidst the chaos of the scene. There is no direction, there is no purpose, but there is presence. Meanwhile, the turrets capacitors unless their charge, the energy bolts lancing out at their targets and igniting the hull of the station in a cascade of sparks and molten metal when they miss their targets.


The turbolift glides down, smooth as a knife slipping between ribs. Mitra does not turn to see the way Vix wipes blood from her blade with practiced ease, her words drifting through the quiet, rough-edged and careless. Skawn and his ilk are creatures of chaos, of violence for its own sake. Malign's eldest child has never cared for such indulgences.


Their destination is reached before long, doors parting to reveal a light that is different -- harsher, colder -- the pale blue glow of reinforced containment fields humming at the edges of the corridor. There are no carbonite slabs lining these walls, no rows of frozen prisoners waiting in silence. This place is built for something else...

Defenses react instantly, two turrets emerging from the ceiling with mechanical precision, red lights flickering to life. And beyond them, stepping forward in synchronized unison, more droids. Not the thin, brittle things deployed en-masse in old wars, but something heavier, more deliberate. Dark metal, broad-shouldered, eyes gleaming with the indifferent menace of pure programming. Their servos whine as they raise their weapons.

Mitra moves before they fire. The first bolt is caught, sent whirling back into the eye of the nearest machine, a spray of sparks cutting through the dim. She darts forward, carving through another, every motion clean and economical, and as the scent of scorched circuitry thickens in the air, she /feels/ it-just for a moment, just the barest sliver of something waiting in the dark. Not awake, not aware, but there. A presence, coiled deep beneath layers of ice and silence.

So. Perhaps the stories were not all lies.


Conservation of energy. It is not just a concept. It is not even just a theory. It is a rule to live by. When the lift slides to a halt and the doors ease open to yet another level in this particular cake layer of despair, the first instinct that Vix heeds is to ever so calmly take a nice healthy step to the side of the lift so that she is somewhat shielded by the frame of the lift itself. Her eyes track Mitra as she wades into the field of weapons fire that the weird not-cheap-clunkers unload in her direction.

Only when Mitra has a good amount of scorched metal bits and pieces already littering the floor does Vix follow along in her wake. No one -asked- Vix if she is the trustworthy type to have at your back. Today could just be that day.

In the darkness, the dim glow of a carbonite slab's status panel acts as a beacon drawing attention to the rectangular tomb it is attached to.


For a heartbeat, there is nothing, a strange stillness settling over everything like a shroud as the slab looms before them, sharp edges of the carbonite casting eerie reflections of Mitra's white blade before it is deactivated. The Sith stands motionless, eyes fixed on the frozen form of Malus, her breath steady but her mind turning.

And then... /it/. A faint tremor, a ripple running through the fabric of reality itself, a /presence/ brushing against her awareness. It grows slowly, like a distant storm, the Force around her bending and shifting, a weight pressing against her chest as the awareness of Malus stirs... long dormant but not defeated, waiting, unforgiving. The room, once a sterile prison, now hums with a subtle energy.

She glances at Vix before sending the message to Skawn through the Force, a sharp pulse: It's time. The words resonate in the stillness, as much a signal as a warning.


Vix doesn't have to know what fuck Mitra is doing, and it's not her business to know. It's her job to keep an eye on Mitra - at the moment at least - while Mitra does whatever it is that she's doing. Thawing is a bitch so hopefully he would avoid being too mind fucked right off the bat. Part of his begins to question though why he signed up for this.

And while Mitra is distracted Vix starts helping herself to bits and pieces of the inner workings of the dismembered mechs, there's a market for everything.

Would it be folly to unleash its occupant here in the prison, or at all? Though there is something alluring about this simple slab of carbonite. Any tension in the air is magnified by the silence of the darkened room. No longer can any explosions or blaster fire be heard from within this fortress-like cell.

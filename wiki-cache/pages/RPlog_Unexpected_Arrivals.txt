Chianar Plaza -- New Alderaan: Ord Mantell

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A beautiful plaza is etched of white stone into a large plain nestled between the surrounding hills of New Alderaan. Spanning nearly a quarter mile in diameter, the plaza is framed on almost every side by rolling hills, where other districts of the city can be seen carved into the rich landscape. Beautiful buildings spot the northern and southern hills, with the more utilitarian structures of the starport and military base flanking the west and east, respectively.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The plaza is landscaped with small gardens and art-inspired water features, meant to reflect various significant cultures in the galaxy. An information kiosk stands outside the central rail station, whose elevated maglev tracks snake off into the surrounding districts at various heights, meant to accentuate the decor, rather than obstruct it. A tall memorial structure, carved of synthetic Alderaanian marble, is the center point of a large, central garden, flanked by an open-air pavilion under a transparisteel dome. A tall theater, a wide museum, street vendors, and an open air art gallery are some of the cultural points of interest that mark the plaza.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The storm that had threatened two nights before had delivered upon its rumbling portents. A night, a day, another night, and another day later, Enb'Zik finds himself in Chianar Plaza again. It's the construction of the new Temple not far away that brings him here most days, but this evening, the Sullustan has found a sheltered alcove which he now occupies seated face-to-face with a much larger sentient feline -- his wife Snal'fashtalli. The pair are simply having a quiet conversation between the two of them as the rain falls constantly upon the city.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Soaked to the skin and rain sodden is the only way to describe Lynae's current state, and from the way her chin is tucked down as she walks into the face of the wind it is apparent that this condition does not seem to deter her in the slightest. Accompanied by her standard escorts, both outfitted in much more suitable gear than the doctor is, she is striding across the plaza at a brisk pace. Slogging through puddles she is holding a beaten and rain damaged datapad in one hand, her grip on it so forceful that her hand is white knuckled with the effort.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Although not seriously bothered by the storm itself, Snarl's fur still manages to gradually absorb moisture from the humid air surrounding them. From time to time, the big cat interrupts the conversation briefly to get up and go several paces away in order to shake the collection of droplets loose without drenching her Sullustan mate. Upon returning to her seat, she turns her vaguely luminescent golden eyes shifting briefly outward from their shelter to observe the mostly empty street and walkways nearby. It's just an ingrained habit, or maybe just a nervous quirk of hers. 

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;She hadn't expected to see anything of interest, really, but this time her routine check does come up with something. Narrowing her eyes slightly, the Horansi peers through the downpour with a rather intent expression. The Marines she recognizes by uniform and gear, but the woman with them is ringing a very distant bell. She's seen her before somewhere...


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"So the idea is that, until the Temple is completed, NR Social Services is going to house them on board the...." Enb'Zik's voice quiets to a stop as he sees his wife's gaze direct itself outward to the plaza and remain there. "What is it?" he asks. Over the last five months, he has slowly learned enough that he reaches in that direction with the Force before his eyes follow. The sense of determination that greets Enb'Zik causes him to blink. "Lynae Cassius," he answers Snarl's thoughts, not needing the Force to anticipate that. "Doesn't look like she's out for a leisurely stroll, either."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Zik sits up a little straighter to watch. Is she heading this way? Somewhere else? Briefly, the rain slackens a bit, even as a gust of wind moans its way past the statues and fountains.



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lynae's path takes her around the displays stands and the empty kiosks that are normally crowded during the day with the wares of the vendors that brighten the plaza. In the dark of night, however, it appears more of a ghostly remains of a market than anything else. The falling rain is still unheeded as she heads for the information kiosk, sloshing through another deep puddle in the process before stopping before the kiosk itself and studying it intently. The datapad held in her hand is useless, having been so water logged at this point that it's long since given up the ghost with a fizzle and a rather damp odor of scorched wires. She dumps it unceremoniously into the nearest garbage receptacle and moves towards the news stand and activates it after a few moments of careful study. Surfing rapidly through the available recorded broadcasts she locates the IGN network and hunts through the various feeds for one in particular and lets it play out from beginning to end without making a sound.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Zik's naming of the woman doesn't mean that much to Snarl initially, mostly she is preoccupied with digging around in her recollection for a past event. It takes a few moments, but the Horansi does manage to match the face with the past. "Imperial," she rumbles quietly. "Doctor, I think. Saved my hide once on the KOS when we had a truce called with the Empire. A few of them were wanting to perforate my pelt and I would not draw in response." One side of her muzzle quirks upward to reveal her right upper fang in an amused snarl. Then her gaze shifts back out toward the sodden figure, "What is she doing here? And with a Marine guard?"


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Zik glances aside at Snarl as she recalls finally whence she remembers the woman. He nods, silent for a moment as she comes up. He listens as best he's able to the news broadcast, Sullustan ears being rather good, as he decides Snarl's memory in particular is better than others she /might/ have had. After all, Lynae had also given the order to ram the Crusader with the Conqueror in a maneuver that had nearly killed the Sullustan. "Theseus Station," he murmurs, "but yes." He looks at his wife again, deciding it would be rude to speak further about Lynae when the human woman is at such close quarters.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As the news broadcast reaches a pause, Enb'Zik seems to think it's done. Lifting his voice to a more conversational volume, he calls in Lynae's direction, "Doctor Cassius, good evening. I trust all is well?" By the looks of it, it appears she could be doing better. This is his invitation for her to talk if she'd find it appropriate.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lynae turns abruptly, her eyes wide as she is clearly startled by the sound of anyone speaking her name and she stares first at Zik and then his companion for a moment in silence. Enough of her manners kick in and she manages a, "Good evening, to both of you," a glimmer of recognition finally falling into face as she tips her head in a nod towards the tall Horansi. "I see you're looking well," and good as her memory is it just doesn't do the job to pull up a name fast enough to fill in the blanks. She blinks again, finally running one hand over her face and slicking the hair back from her face at the same time, "Ah, certainly. It's just.. marvelous. I have been having the most wonderful time on this planet of yours. And the news network is just.. marvelous. Relaying news all the way from Corellia. It's just a wonderful thing. " She clears her throat and tries again, "Did you receive the data that I provided to officer Sh'vani?"


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A quiet grunt is Snarl's response to Enb'Zik's correction of the location. That doesn't phase her too much, as she's certain she has the identification correct. "Hmm..." the massive feline mutters just before he calls out to Lynae. "Good evening," she rumbles in reply to the human female, her voice carrying easily with little effort on her part. "I thank you, yes, I am well. If I may be so bold, you seem a tad damp, Doctor. Would you care to join us away from the elements, perhaps?" she invites. 

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Snarl shifts slightly on her bench and curls her long tail to one side so that she makes a slightly more compact bundle under the shelter. At Lynae's last query, she glances over at Enb'Zik to see if he knows anything about the afore mentioned data. Then the Horansi's faintly glowing eyes shift back toward the Doctor. She is of course unaware of the somewhat eerie effect the dim nighttime lighting of the street is creating in her eyes. It's just a consequence of good night vision.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"I am well, yes, thank you," Enb'Zik nods slowly, noting the woman's nervous actions -- and babbling -- as she goes on. When she finally finishes, the last question causes him to tilt his head in a motion that might be more expected of Snarl than of him. "No," he answers, "though I'm only involved with Star Ops's reserve groups presently. Was it something important?" That's just a guess, but one based on that apprehensive behavior.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lynae is silent for a long enough moment for it to become awkward before she nods once, "Thank you," she begins in reply and moves closer to the pair so that she is out of the worst of the rain. "I was certain that it would reach your ears eventually," she says quietly, "I provided officer Sh'vani with a datapad that held a strength assessment of the imperial taskforce along with the names and ranks of the officers that I last knew to be in pertinent areas of command in the fleet. I also included a threat assessment and what I judge to be my best guess as to the next possible movements of the fleet itself," the words are stated in a clear quiet voice that is singularly lacking in inflection but precise in enunciation.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As Lynae is now much closer and upwind, Snarl's long white whiskers bob and twitch lightly as she samples the breeze and with it, the human's scent. "You are welcome," the Horansi replies simply before growing quiet to listen. Once the Doctor has finished, she rumbles lowly, "Doing something productive with your... recent redirection in life, yes? Useful information indeed." 

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Snarl observes her closely for a moment and then shifts her gaze just over Lynae's left shoulder to eye her attendant guards. "Your ward shall remain with us for the time-being," she states. "You are free to take a thirty minute break if you so choose, Lieutenants." Without waiting for acknowledgement, she turns her attention back to the doctor. "You wish to sit?" she enquires, gesturing toward vacant space on one of the benches.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The account of what information she'd provided sketches an expression of captivation on Enb'Zik's face that becomes clearer as Lynae goes on. When she finishes listing what she's done, Ikihsa doesn't answer immediately but leans back in silent thought, letting his wife speak for both of them. When Snarl dismisses the Marine guard, Zik glances at her but doesn't interrupt, for he knows they won't go far.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Finally, Zik motions to a seat near himself and the Horansi when Snarl invites Lynae to join them again. "That was an exceptionally risky thing for you to do, Doctor," he observes. "If the information on the datapad is accurate...." He stops there, letting the rest go unspoken. Two nights previous he'd seen how difficult it had been for Lynae to turn down an apparent offer to return. Iterating that she'd not only burned another bridge but tossed a thermal detonator under it and bombarded it from orbit would be unnecessarily harsh.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"It's as accurate as my last reading of the tactical positioning can make it," Lynae says simply. "I have observed the .. fracas over Nar Shaddaa as well, and while I was on the 'ground' for some of it, I did not take part in the end game exchange that happened between the Fleet, CSA and the rest of the merry band of participants." Her hands find their way into her pockets as her posture relaxes slightly as the Marines do take the offered break, taking the offered seat as well on one of the benches. "I'm under a death sentence if I return to Imperial held space, there isn't much more a risk than that," she states simply. "The offer of Darth Malign not withstanding, I don't wager my acceptance would've been welcomed with a cheer. Captaincy of an Interdictor Cruiser," she pauses to smile faintly, "a nice vessel, believe me. But I'd still be standing on the wrong side of the line. I'm not sure I'd have it in me to say no if he asked me again," she adds in a low voice, "fortunately those kind of offers don't come along every day."


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There's a faint tremor that runs through Snarl's massive frame at the mention of the Sith Lord, but she manages to put a lid on the growl before it actually forms in her throat. "Just as well you did not accept the offer," the big cat says cooly, "Sith are not to be trusted. Ever." Yes, she speaks from personal experience. The Horansi's long tail flicks several times at its apex before she forces it to be still, coiling around her left ankle lightly.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Ikihsa leans forward and props one elbow on the table at which he and Snarl are seated, now with the woman. He raises a hand to his mouth, fingertips resting against one cheek thoughtfully, just above his dewflaps. "Wrong side of the line," he repeats contemplatively, then shakes his head, "An Imperial death mark is not an easy thing to live with. It almost sounds as if you're having a change of heart regarding the Empire. I didn't think several nights ago that you spoke the way most Imperials do." Enb'Zik lowers his hand to the table again, "But be careful. You know as well as any of us that if ired enough, the Empire won't wait for you to come to them. They may come to you."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At Snarl's comment about the Sith, Enb'Zik looks in her direction, then back at Lynae as if in unspoken agreement. "Malign makes this entire entanglement just that much more dangerous."


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Of course not," Lynae says simply, her tone of voice solemn. "But then, I didn't trust them before. I don't trust them now. It was never an issue of trust. It was an offer to go home," she says in a strangely gentle tone of voice. "Something that I can never do again, at this point. Even if you and yours were to win the day, Carida would never be a world I could call home again. My stepping foot in imperial space will cost me not just my life, but that of the lone remaining member of my family that I have left. His life is the price I am willing to pay, and gladly so, and with it the assurance that I will not violate the letter and word of my exile. If his life is suddenly forfeit then there is nothing to keep me from violating the word of my exile," she says in a lower voice, her expression grim. "I am a soldier, uniform or not, billet or not, army or not. I am what I am, no more and certainly no less. They did not take my life then, and taking it now would be a futile gesture lost in the shuffle. They do not wish me dead, apparently I'm still considered a potentially valuable asset. Or Malign wouldn't have come all this way to make the offer that he did," she reasons in a rational tone of voice. "But in light of that, I realize that remaining on this planet is nothing more than added bonus to the temptation to violate your security protocols. To that end, I should be finding out who I need to speak with in order to make my leave. Finian indicated that he had arranged such, is that still true?"


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Something about Lynae's comments about home seem to strike a deep chord with Snarl, and she regards the woman silently with an almost mournful expression for several long moments before venturing to say anything. "At least you have a member of your family remaining," the Hornasi says quietly. "All my blood kin are dead... and I suspect the Sith had a hand in it." She huffs out a soft sigh and glances at the damp duracrete near their feet, "I cannot go home either, so in that you are neither unique nor fully alone. Take comfort if you wish from that fact, Doctor." 

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;A slight shake of the head greets Lynae's thoughts on the Sith. "You may yet be useful to Malign somehow, but that is not necessarily a good thing. For you." she comments, her tone darkening with her own pensive thoughts. With the mention of leave taking, Snarl glances again at Zik briefly before returning her attention to Lynae. "Not that I have heard, however... you were not assigned directly to my charge so I may have missed something." Pausing, the feline wordlessly shifts the conversation to Enb'Zik as she half turns on her bench to again peer out into the rain, ears swiveling alertly.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Enb'Zik gets a bad feeling about this. Literally. As the conversation goes on, there is an odd twinge in the pit of his stomach and an unsettling sense of adrenaline when there shouldn't be that makes his heart beat a touch faster than it should. Listening to the intercourse between Snarl and Lynae, he frowns to himself in response.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lynae's and Snarl's interchange about going home brings Zik a feeling of empathy, for like them, Ikihsa cannot go home, either. Sullust had been his entire reason for fighting, and he had been there personally to lose her. But he doesn't speak of that now.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Instead, he drives away negative thoughts about war and home by acknowledging them for a brief second, then letting them go. Training has taught him that the unprovoked fight-or-flight response can mean something besides indigestion. For a moment he looks aside and seems attentive to something else of importance while the two women talk.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lynae casts a brief glance at Enb'Zik as he falls silent and appears distracted but shifts her gaze back towards the Horansi female, still trying to pull up her name and failing miserably. "That is some consolation, thank you," she says simply. "Odd, that I have spent more than half my life aboard either ship or research station, yet I call Carida home and have not set foot on that planet in nearly a decade. It is odd the way the mind and the heart clings to that which it cannot tough, but will not surrender." A shadow drifts across her face, "I spoke incorrectly there. Last time I set foot on Carida it was to be married. And now my husband is dead," she states in a carefully controlled voice. "Odd that I sought to free him from our marriage once I was declared a traitor, and in the end I could have saved him from the turmoil."


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;There is a span of a few seconds during which both Snarl and Enb'Zik seem to be distracted, but the silence does not linger long before the Horansi glances back toward her Sullustan mate with one set of brow whiskers arching. Noting his distant gaze, she unwinds her tail from her left ankle and lifts it to his shoulder level, giving him a gentle prod with the tip. 

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Turning her attention from Zik, Snarl gazes at Lynae once again. "I am sorry for your loss," she intones gravely, but there is something in her manner that makes it obvious that she is sincere and not just saying the polite thing. There is silence again for a moment, and then the Kasa rumbles, "It is good that you are actively seeking to do something meaningful with your life, rather than remaining in the past."


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lynae smiles faintly, "As long as you don't use the word 'epiphany' on me," she says with a slow shake of her head. I'm really starting to dislike that word. As though a switch was thrown somewhere in my mental processes and suddenly there's this revelation that's grand enough to make the crowd 'Oooh' and 'Ahh'," she mutters. "I keep saying it, but it's true. "


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Stepping off the Maglev, alongside Axel, he takes a few steps along before stopping and giving a quick glance around the plaza, his blue-steel eyes slowly looking over everything as his gaze arcs across the scenery. Right behind Dareus, a droid that stands about the same height as him, dark black with crimson accents maintains a radius within only a few feet of the man that it could obviously, only be assigned to protect. While the breeze catches Dareus' dark nerfhide trenchcoat, he turns to Axel, a rather subdued and cold tone in his voice, "Could have been a better place to try to find her, I suppose. We should try to move along quickly, neither of us have the time to waste here though." His steps begin again as he moves deeper along the plaza, ignoring some of the friendly residents greeting nods, and pretty much anything else that would be considered pleasant and landmark-type; clasping his hands behind his back softly, before he spots the person he had been intent on finding, a smirk crossing his face, but the emotionless voice not changing, "Well, that was easier than I expected." He begins an immediate b-line towards Lynae, quickly glancing back to his metallic bodyguard, "Increase radius to 5 feet, I don't want you nipping at my heels."


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The soft, furry tap against his robes seems to draw Enb'Zik's eyes back into focus. "I'm sorry," the Sullustan states to both of them. For a second it looks like his mind is still on whatever it was on, and indeed it is, but he wraps the thoughts up properly in an expedient manner. He fixes his gaze on Lynae and finally answers the question she'd asked moments before. "I don't know whether Brandis was able to arrange transport as he said, I'm afraid. However, if you will let me, something tells me I should provide such conveyance myself."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Enb'Zik stands, in fact, and seems somewhat unfettered by the calm that had pervaded him early. Signs of urgency show themselves in his posture. The Sullustan looks over at Snarl, "Love, please contact her guard on the Marine frequency and have them meet us en route to hangar seven-seven-nine-three." His eyes turn back to Lynae, "I realize this is rather sudden, but will you trust me?"


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Axel gives a smirk in response to Dareus's comments on better places. "Hey, I got us here in one peace didn't I? I half expect though, that we'll be fighting our way back to the Boomstick. Boy, are the customs guys gonna be surprised when I remote open the cargo hatch." Axel seems to be in a relatively good mood, and why not? He had quit taking drugs altogether, but the threat of danger always got him high. "Could of asked for better weather though. The rain is going to play hell on the fresh paint on the Boomstick. I'm going to have to have some of your crew clean her up when we get back to the Predator."

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;As Dareus spots Lynae and makes his approach, Axel falls in beside him. His own bodyguard droid staying a couple of meters back and a ridiculous fur cloak draped over it's shoulders, which did little to conceal the fact that it was a well armored combat droid.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Cade steps off the transit lift. Stepping out into the open into the open he has his black cloak draped over his bantha hide jacket with the hood up over his head. Even though it is a dark night he still has his glasses on to hide his blue eyes from the rest of humanity. Taking a look around he smiles to himself recognizing Axel and then his smile gets broader as he sees him and another gentleman headed towards the infamous Lynae. Glancing around a little more to make sure there were no dangers he moves forward at a slow gate towards the group.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The reason for Snarl's twitching ears makes itself obvious even as Enb'Zik speaks. "Right," the Kasa mutters under her breath as her golden eyes fix themselves on the approaching party. Her right ear flicks twice and then she mutters again, "Lieutenant Epsion, change in plan. Meet us at hangar 7793 yesterday. Code red. Snarl out." Again, the Horansi doesn't wait for a reply from the Marines and rises smoothly from her seat, effectively blocking access to Lynae with her own bulk. "I suggest we leave. Now," she growls. A glance is thrown toward Lynae, "How are you at beast riding, Doctor?"


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lynae's attention is also drawn to the approaching quartet, her eyebrows lifting upwards as she recognizes first one, then the other, of the two men. Even in the rain, and the dark of night, she'd recognize Dareus anywhere. A frown begins to form on her face as she surveys the following droids, her gaze tugged towards Zik as he stands suddenly, "That's an odd sort of question," she begins before the Horansi woman steps between her and the approaching quartet and asks another question that makes her blink slowly. "That one is as well," she says simply and rises from her seat on the bench . While confused, clearly, Lynae falls back into her trademark of being utterly calm and clear as she shakes her head slightly, a little to the right then back to the left, "No sufficient experience in that area," she states before taking a measured step to the side so as to not be completely hidden, as hidden is something that Lynae simply just does not do. "Of all the gin joints in all the starry universe," she says by way of greeting, "and with such .. interesting escorts. You shouldn't be here."


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Weather is just weather old friend." Dareus doesn't seem to mind the rain beating off his face, aside from a few extra blinks with the moisture in his eyes; "Of course, we got here in one piece, although getting out, that'll be a different story I would imagine. Anytime we're together, there's always trouble. I'll just get those others on the ship to take care of the cleaning, the others are too much tied up with building you your new house...since your landlord evicted you." Only the slightest of chuckles leaves his mouth, running his hand through his wet hair, shaking some of the droplets with a quick motion of his hand. "Watch, I'll be unlucky and them things will rust...that's the last thing I need to go wrong this week." Shrugging that off, Dareus stops a few feet short of Lynae and bows his head a little in greeting, but remains quiet for now, to not interrupt her conversation, actually raising an eyebrow towards all the movements that suddenly happen, turning back to Axel, "I love the New Republic, they make everyone feel so welcome, it makes your heart melt." It comes out spitefully from his lips, but that seems to be how the man intended it; although he turns his attention back to Lynae, "Well, hello to you too Lynae." He looks back at the droids, and shrugs, "Bodyguards, I'm not the most popular man in both the Empire, and well...everywhere. Funny thing about the gin joint; I quit drinking some time ago."


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;At Snarl's suggestion of beast riding, Enb'Zik holds up a hand just to the two of them. "I didn't show up tonight with intent to get involved in a battle," he says quietly, "And I don't intend to fight one now." The Sullustan's eyes lay upon the droid, as well as upon the three men as he tries to gauge their intent. Without blinking, Ikihsa switches languages and speaks very softly in his native tongue, intending to do so loudly enough for only those within a meter to hear: "An nunb den zint dund, mandt?"

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"None of us should be here," Ikihsa notes, then goes on, "As a matter of fact, we were just on our way out. If you'll pardon us, Gentlemen." Zik speaks confidently. Lynae's greeting to the interlopers doesn't lend him much faith in any possibility of benevolence on their part, but as they approach and nervousness knocks on the door to his consciousness, Enb'Zik opens that door... and politely closes it again. He takes a breath and begins walking on a course at a wide angle from the men, intending to leave the plaza at a point different from the one they entered. He steps out into the rain with little heed to it. His eyes turn on the closest of them - Dareus - as he moves to walk past, expecting Snarl and Lynae to follow.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Axel once again nods to Dareus. The two seem to be old friends even though they have really only known each other for a few months. Plus, I would never be able to salute some puppy of a junior officer without the temptation to break a few of his smaller body parts and remind him that it's not the breaking that hurts but the manipulation of the broken shards that proves to be the excruciating pain," a ghost of a smile flickers across her face before she continues. "I appreciate the sentiment, Dareus, and I'm flattered, which is something I don't say very often. We all have our strengths and weakness, I'm a doctor a scientist and a surgeon, a capable interrogator and - at the end - a naval officer whose track record showed more wins than losses. That's why I understand using a small insertion team instead of throwing all the assets at one target and trying to bludgeon it into submission." She pauses as the nav computer indicates their arrival at the programmed coordinates. Swinging her feet down onto the floor she sets her cup aside and brings the ship out of hyper space.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;He smirks at Axel, "I meant the Predator group, not the fleet..." He almost laughs, but it fades rather quickly, "But I stand corrected. You have your ships, I have mine. Boys with toys." The chair swivles slightly as Dareus continues to hold the cup with both hands, allowing it to keep warming them up, "Exile, it seems was more than temporary now. You'll find us far outside the normal Imperial chain of Command, and we only bow to one person...." As the ship exits hyperspace, Dareus stands up quickly and moves to stand by the controls, looking out the window towards the large familiar shape that looms, except for the fact that it's been painted, stark black from fore to aft, "Beautiful isn't she..." The Star Destroyer seems to be unescorted for the time being, unusual, although it appears to be less than easy to quickly spot, due to the color. He reaches to key the comm, "Sorry, I have to let them know it's me, or they'll blow us out of the sky." Clearing his throat, the switch is flicked and he speaks clearly, and coldly, "Predator, this is Red Sky. Authorization S-417-Alpha Tango; alert staff to prepare for my arrival with an important guest." The shields lower and while still on course, the ship slows for docking, letting Dareus move to his seat, "Primary hangar, if you please. The fighter bay is rather stocked to the rafters...literally." He doesn't even laugh at his own jokes, amusingly enough.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Axel remains silent for the most part as Dareus speaks and the ship heads in the direction of the Predator. From the white knuckles, one can tell that the landing approach was bothering him a bit. Once again, not because it was Lynae flying, only because it wasn't him. He couldn't even stand to take public shuttles. As the ship settles in, without problems he loosens his grip on the seat and unstraps. "Ships are ships. Sure, there are two battle groups, but honestly, what is mine without yours and yours without mine? They might have two seperate owners, but they fight as one." He pauses and stands, giving a little stretch. "Lets go ahead and hit the tarmac, that caff has me needing to hit the head pretty badly."


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;With a slow nod, Lynae guides the Red Sky to the Predator and, with the clearance codes provided, guides her ship into the docking bay with ease that demonstrates the many many hours she's put into learning to pilot in the last few months. She chuckles at Dareus's comment, "Interesting paint job," she says in a mild tone which serves to convey that she is impressed by that bit of ingenuity. Once she sets the ship down she runs through the usual procedure to power everything down, checking the systems over as she does so before rising from her seat and stretching slightly. "Important guest? Last time I was an 'important guest' on a ship of the fleet, be it attached to the fleet or no, we ended up strung up like obscene decorations," she comments to Dareus, "If that happens again I am going to make you actually try to hit the drain from where ever you are in the room."

''Hangar Bay -I2SD Predator ''

''The immensity of the hangar is a tribute to the size of this vessel. This facility is able to refuel, repair and rearm vessels from the smallest Lambda shuttle to the larger Correlian Corvettes. It is kept flawlessly clean by the constant presence of scrubber droids removing debris and trash off of the finely polished deck. Very little space is wasted on this mighty warship and this is doubly true of the main hangar. Along the ceiling nestled in launch shrouds are the behemoths of the ground forces, from the fearsome AT-AT and AT-ST walkers to the older Juggernauts and Air Fortresses. Hanging out over the vast vacuum of space is the 'control tower'. From it, the deck chief can supervise the operations in both Hangars. Along either side of it rest the heavy-duty repulsor beams and the passageways to the even heavier tractor beams.''

''The view from the hangar of the microcosm that is a warship might easily be compared to an anthill. Despite the vast numbers of ships that leave and arrive at the ship, crewmembers, pilots and naval security personnel are seen all over the place, from the security by the turbolift doors to technicians and their droids conducting repairs of vital parts on many of the shuttles and combat support craft.''

''Forward of the control tower, the claw-like launchers for the ship's wing of TIE Fighters can be seen, as well as the gantries that lead to the fighter bays. Large blast doors, to the aft, lead to the cargo areas, where the ship's consumables are stored. Several sets of solid black doors mar the othewise light gray finish of the hangar, they are the turbolifts that access the rest of the vessel.''




&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;The hangar bay, looks far busier than a normal Star Destroyer, aside from the obvious looks of outside. There is a distinct and very noticable lack of humans in this hangar bay, basically it looks as though there is only a skeleton crew; although the amount of droids, is astonishing, each of them looking identical to the bodyguards which accompanied Axel and Dareus along to Ord Mantell; and armed with E-11's. As the ramp descends to the deckplates with the normal hiss and venting of gasses, the reception of battle droids stand to attention, their black and crimson painted exo-armor shines as if they were new, 10 on each side as a reception to whomever the Captain said was a guest, no doubt; along with several officers dressed in uniforms that look identical to the Imperial Navy, although there's no rank insignia, and they are nothing but pure black. "Welcome back Captains, there are full reports waiting for you at your convenience, and I've assigned a stateroom on board the Predator should your guest require it." 

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Dareus nods while his boots echo loudly on the ramp that leads to the deck of the hangar bay, nodding with a half grin to the officer, "Excellent Commander. I'm not sure if Ms. Cassius will be staying, but keep the preparations should she decide to be a guest. Maintain TIE Patrols in the sector until we leave. That'll be all." He gives the man a quick nod, letting him disappear into the turbolift, before he turns back to Lynae, "You're thinking of the wrong type of guest, Lynae, that's not going to happen here. Welcome on board His Imperial Majesty's Ship...Predator." Dareus glances around the hangar of the ship, looking proud, after all, it was his ship now...something that shows in his face and in his actions aroun the area, not to mention how the crew of humans and droids react to him.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"His Imperial Majesty's ass," Axel replies. "This may once have been a ship in the service of the Empire, Dareus. It's no longer an Imperial ship though. These droids are not Imperial droids. Welcome to the Sith Flagship, Predator." Axel finally does light his cigarette and looks to the two. "Anyway, please excuse me for about two minutes. I was serious about the caff running right through me." Well, if there was a way to ruin a fancy reception, Axel could do it with a restroom break. Without awaiting a response, the man turns and heads to the Boomstick, happy to see it made it back safe and sound. Using the wrist mounted control pad, he lowers the crew ramp and runs inside.



&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Dareus, either call me Doc or call me Lynae, but calling me 'Ms Cassius' reminds me of a time when I was eighteen, awkward and beset by a penchant for stumbling and falling flat on my face. Pick one, stick with it," Lynae says with a slow smile that settles on her face. She slowly scans the room , her gaze flicking over the droids, one eyebrow arching in silent inquiry as Axel bolts for his ship to reassure himself as to its condition and to take a leak. "Who's controlling this personal armada then? Just Malign at the behest of the Emperor directly? I know, you can't give me all the details, but at least disperse with some of the mystique."


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;"Axel, General Siege and myself report to one man directly, Lord Malign. No one else." His hands clasp behind his back while he stand there, raising an eyebrow to both Axel's quick running off, as well as his comments, "Whatever is between Lord Malign and Emperor Vadim, is none of my business. Nonetheless, they are both Sith; and their titles still deserve my respect." He runs an inspecting finger across the chestplate of a droid, as if seeing if the paint is dry, or any dust lingers on them, "Far more efficient than Stormtroopers...in many ways. Multi-purpose as well." Dareus looks back to Lynae, "Well Doctor, you can officially remain a guest here as long as you like, the executive stateroom is prepared for you, if you wish; a far better reception than the last time you were on board an I2SD, no doubt." A smile crosses his lips, "Although I'd much prefer if you took the Captain's Quarters on board the Embrace, but I also want you to make up your own mind with what time you need."


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Axel returns after a few moments, still refastening his armor as he walks. "Hope I didn't miss much," he says with a stub of a lit cigarette hanging from his lips. "Anyway, what I was trying to say is that this isn't an Imperial ship. The Emperor may be in charge of Tyl.. Darth Malign, but our group is not acting under the command of the Imperial war machine. We are something entirely different and our goals aren't quite the same. We do report to Darth Malign, but as equals. Each of us has our stations, and he the lead tactician but we fight as one. We are going to change the galaxy and our names will be written in the tomes of history for millenia." And that right there was the exact reason Axel was here. Tyler had played on every mental instability the man had, but primarily it was his ego and want for acknowledgement and fame that won the one time drug addict over.


&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Lynae shakes her head slightly, "Decide in haste and repent in leisure, or so the saying goes. I prefer to weigh all the available options before making a final decision. But I would ask a bit of an odd favor of you, Dareus," she says, all traces of humor fading from her expression a look of sorrow settling in place instead. "I caught the IGN report, but I lack the sufficient pull to do anything about it. Could you ask Malign to see to it that Yoseph's remains are cremated and cast into space?"

I'll have to report to him anyway; as we can't leave the bothan system until you've decided, for our own security reasons. Of course you have free roam of the ship aside from the Command deck, Lord Malign's chambers are off limits, but everything else, feel free to look around." With that, he gives Lynae and Axel a polite nod, "If you'll excuse me, I'm sure the bridge has a few things that need my attention before I can sleep as well."

A tall golden-skinned female, economist Professor [[Brackett Benduday]], can be seen sitting behind an anchor's desk in a studio. Images of the city of [[:starwars:Rellodir|Rellidir]] on [[:starwars:Tralus/Legends|Tralus]] are displayed behind her.

"This is [[Brackett Benduday]] for [[InterGalactic News]]. Later on the [[Muun Money Minute]] we will have some news on the fast food industry, with news that workers at [[:starwars:Flangth-2-Go|Flangth-2-Go]] are set to join [[Biscuit Baron]] workers on the picket lines. We will have more on what this means for the industry, the economy in general, and your dining options. But for now we bring you to Tralus in the [[Corellian Confederation]].

With this new galactic power still in its relative infancy, the recent retirement of Councillor Thrackan Tharen of Tralus has opened up a crowded race for the by-election to replace him. Earlier this week my colleague Rick Neel broke the news that [[Corellia|Corellia's]] former [[Governor]] Sir [[Ewan Dain]] had entered the race. Sir Ewan, a former CEO of [[Rendili StarDrive]] and founder of [[Starlight Studios]] as emerged has the immediate front runner in the race.

But this has not discouraged interest in running. Though there are some rumours that some candidates are now planning to drop out, others are continuing to join the race. We are about to go live to the [[:starwars:Navos Center for the Performing Arts|Navos Center for the Performing Arts]] in Relldir where we are told Lady Mithril is about to announce her candidacy."

The screen shifts to the performing arts centre where a podium has been set. A large crowd of mostly [[:starwars:Drall/Legends|Dralls]] and [[:starwars:Selonian/Legends|Selonians]] can be seen waiting for the candidate to emerge. [[Human|Humans]] appear to be a minority in the crowd though clearly quite a few are present as well. After a few moments a tall Selonian woman in a green Ottegan silk skirt emerges from the Navos Centre and walks to the podium.

"Thank you all for coming, I will try to be brief. The advent of the Corellian Confederation has proven a great opportunity for our peoples both here on Tralus and across the sector to have a real voice in our own future. For too long we have a battleground over which larger governments have fought. But, there remains a danger that our voices will be lost again.

Now the larger governments turn our way claiming to be our friends, but will the deals they make truly benefit us or just bring us under their banner under a different classification? As we on Tralus decide who is going to represent us going forward it is important to have a voice that truly stands for Tralus and is not just another voice for [[Selene]], or [[Ord Mantell]], or even [[Corellia]].

The Confederation stands to benefit the whole sector, but it must have voices that truly represent each world in the sector faithfully. If elected I promise to be that voice for Tralus. We are too often forgotten, but I promise you all you will be heard."

== Biscuit Beef ==

A tall golden-skinned female, economist Professor [[Brackett Benduday]], can be seen sitting behind an anchor's desk in a studio. To her left is a [[Wookiee]], the [[Caspian Union|Caspian]] politician and union leader [[Zaalbacca]]. To his left is a cerulean [[Twi'lek]] in her teens. Behind them is a stock image of the exterior of a [[Biscuit Baron]] restaurant.

"We were going to an executive with Biscuit Baron on the [[Muun Money Minute]] later for an exciting new announcement, but they pulled out at the last minute. I am Brackett Benduday and this is where our story begins. Normally such a cancellation would not be newsworthy but according to multiple sources Biscuit Baron's contract negotiations with unions representatives from [[:starwars:Byblos/Legends|Byblos]], [[Caspar]], [[Chandrila]], [[:starwars:Commenor/Legends|Commenor]], [[Selene]], and [[Sullust]] are in the process of faltering. Some believe a strike may be imminent.

While we could not get Biscuit Baron to send someone on the show tonight we do have another special guest, Chairman of the [[Wookiee Workers Union of Anchorron]] and Caspian Minister of Industry and Labour, [[Union Council|Councillor]] Zaalbacca. With him, a senior member of his staff, Calling Teksa."

"Thank you for having us Brackett," replies Zaalbacca in Shyriiwook, no translation is offered into [[Galactic Basic Standard|Basic]] for this statement.

"Well I guess the first question is what are the unions looking for?"

"Respect." He pauses for a moment with again no translation offered before continuing. "Okay but seriously the issue stems from Biscuit Baron taking their employees for granted. In the past five months their restaurants have seen a 20 percent increase in customer volume. And what is their response to this increase in traffic? More staff? Wage increases to reflect the added work their employees are putting in? Apparently it is just to ask their employees to work longer shifts. Its really not unreasonable for their employees to expect something more than that, they are not even being offered a reasonable cost of living adjustment to their wages." This time a translation into Galactic Basic Standard by the Twi'lek girl, which she does without any notable deviation.

Brackett listens to the Wookiee with interest, she is clearly fluent in [[Shyriiwook]] as she is visibly more attentive to the initial speech than the translation offered of it. Once the Twi'lek finishes translating for the audience the anchor asks, "Which brings me to my next question, why do you suppose Biscuit Baron is not making more of an effort to accommodate their workers. Do they feel they have that much leverage over their staff that they don't have to offer some level of compromise?"

"That is an excellent question, Brackett. I think the answer is two-fold. The short answer is yes they think they have the leverage. If you wade past all the subsidiaries, the ultimate owner of Biscuit Baron is the Corporate Sector Authority. But who owns say a competitor like [[Caspar Caff]]? Again it is the [[Corporate Sector Authority|CSA]]. It provides these subsidiaries little incentive to compete amongst themselves for employees, and they probably feel the employees have no better options." This is once again followed by a faithful translation by his aide.

"The longer answer though is that I think there is trouble between the Corporate Sector Authority trying to operate itself as both a government and a mega-corporation. Thus far they seem to be having little trouble with the Biscuit Baron employees in the Corporate Sector itself. The problem is one of the few things the Caspian Union, the [[Imperium]], and the New Republic have in common is pretty strong labour laws. If the corporate structure was more as it had been before the incestuous monstrosity it has become, they might be better poised to react to the fact that they can't get away with shoddy labour practices throughout much of the galaxy like they used to." This time the translation by the Twi'lek aide deviates slightly with the mega-corporation referred to as a convoluted mess rather than incestuous monstrosity.

"We don't have a lot of time so one final question. Do you think this is going to lead to a strike?" asks the golden-skinned anchorwoman.

"As Caspian Minister of Industry and Labour I have to hope that a compromise can be reached. But as Chairman of the Wookiee Workers Union, the workers we represent are tired of being dicked around." After Zaalbacca finishes, Calling translates, "The Crest hopes that a compromise can be reached but from the perspective of the Wookiee Workers Union, we fear a strike may be inevitable."

"I am afraid that is all the time we have. Councillor Zaalbacca and Calling Teska, thanks for being on the programme." The screen fades out and is replaced by a rather ill-timed advertisement from Biscuit Baron.

== Bad Council ==

An image of Union Hall in [[Plaxton]] on Caspar is visible on the screen before it pans out to the anchor's desk in a studio. Behind the anchor's desk is the familiar face of a certain golden-skinned woman who is sitting with three human men, one to her left and two to her right.

"The unions on several worlds in the Inner Rim have given the [[:starwars:Tagge Restaurant Association/Legends|Tagge Restaurant Association]] deadlines to meet their demands; otherwise the workers of the Biscuit Barons on those worlds will go on strike. It is expected more unions will follow suit. But that is not our story tonight, though you could say it ties in. I am Brackett Benduday. And our top story tonight comes from the Union Council where a Taungsday question session that usually is a time Councillor's highlight the concerns of particular constituents saw one of our most recent guests on this show become more part of the story. Let us start with how this all started."

The screen shifts to an image of the previously recorded session of the Union Council. An information box at the bottom of the screen indicates that of the 39 Councillors, 27 are present in person, 9 are present remotely, and 3 are absent with the reasons given: Councillor [[Odie Hood]], Ithaca, absent for naval training exercises; Councillor Lilith Bergen, Spittsbury, absent for local holiday; Councillor [[ak]], Xi Can, absent for trade mission.

The screen zooms on a Human male in his early seventies identified as [[Thomas Keyyaw]] of Lansdowne, [[Krittain Major]]. "Given the likelihood of a major workers strikes likely facing businesses in the Union, and indeed the galaxy at large. Is it not appropriate that the Councillor of Anchorron resign as Minister of Industry and Labour as he has made it quite clear that he cannot represent the interests of the [[Caspian Crest|Crest]] without bias on this matter?"

The other Councillors look in varying degrees of shock at the question. The camera focuses on a few in particular. Zaalbacca himself, normally known for his convivial manner expresses a look of anger intense enough that one might suppose, were he to have force powers, that Keyyaw's telekinetically severed arm would be beating his own face. Next is Vice Presav [[Cas Mos]], the leader of Keyyaw's party Legacy, who looks frustrated at the question. Finally the screen pans to the smirk of Caspian [[Presav]] [[Crestian Tarasar]], for a second it almost looks like he is amused.

The screen returns to the studio, "Before we get into what you just saw let me introduce guests. To my left is Professor Sir [[Curt Johnson]] of Shey Tapani University. To my left Professor [[Clark Rickart]] of Plaxton University and Director of the Plaxton Polling Institute. To his left Professor Emeritus [[Charrs Prego-Roamanoff]] of Plaxton University. Thank you all for coming on the show. Let me pose my first question to you Clark, why was what we just watched so significant?"

"Thank you for having us, Brackett. To understand what we have going on here you need to understand the context of how this government was formed. Before the various worlds of the old [[Caspian Democratic Union|CDU]] returned to the Caspian fold and the [[Xi Char]] joined the Union, the initial election for the Union Council had nine seats, of which Crestian Tarasar's party [[Praecet]] won seven of the nine, one more than they were projected to. [[Legacy]], the only other party at the time to win seats, picked up the other two.

In almost any situation a party having such a resounding control over their legislature would make little effort to compromise, and it was widely expected that Zaalbacca as Deputy Leader of Praecet would be nominated as Vice Presav, but instead Tarasar entered into a coalition with Legacy and nominated their leader Cas Mos as Vice Presav. As other planets joined and rejoined and other parties gained seats they have been invited into this coalition. 

Clearly deals are made to garner each other's support and certain areas of governance where one party has been given more power to enact their platforms. But, that is what goes on behind closed doors. There is still debate in the chamber of the Union Council, still close votes on some matters, but it rarely is what you can describe as partisan or personal. It is the established ethos of the Union Council to be publically cooperative. This makes an attack on the character of one of the chamber's most popular members to be quite an aberration."

"Before we get to our other guests we want to show you more of the debate that followed this."

The screen goes back to where the footage ended before with another Councillor rising to speak, a [[Zeltron]] woman in her early twenties, identified as [[Sha Dark]] of Orleanon, Caspar. "Given the Councillor of Lansdowne's many decades in politics helping to uphold his planet's shameful history of not granting citizen to nearly forty percent of their population while simultaneously taking advantage of those same oppressed people in business, I am not sure where he finds the gall to question the bias of another member of this council. The only reason the Councillor of Lansdowne maintains his place here is because he has not faced election since the injustice around [[Krittain|Krittish]] citizenship was corrected."

The screen briefly pivots to the other Legacy party members of the Krittish delegation, Erskine Braemar, the constitutional monarch of the system and Councillor of Braemar and Maximilian Kritz of Krittain Minor, President of the Krit Union during its brief independence. They do not seem happy with the reply but their attendance via hologram limits the clarity of their expressions.

The screen returns to the studio once again. "My next question is for you Curt, can you unpack Councillor Dark's statement a little?"

"Certainly. Thomas Keyyaw was indeed a key defender of the previous Krittish policy that only granted citizenship on the planet to those who had been there for several generations. So of roughly five billion people, only two billion were actually considered Krittish citizens and had voting rights on the planet among other privileges. This had been a major sticking point that delayed Krittish re-entry into the Caspian Union for some time. Any questions about his business practices are less straightforward. He has never faced any investigation around them, and there seems to be little direct evidence he has taken personal issue with so-called 'alien' residents of Krittain Major. And he remains hugely popular among a large portion of his constituency. But polling figures show his seat to be vulnerable were a new election to happen in the near future."

Returning to the footage the next figure identified as about to speak is a Pantoran woman in her thirties identified as [[Andra Eleria]] of [[Noques Rasala]], Caspar. "The Councillor of Anchorron is one of the figures most responsible for Caspar's perseverance following the pandemic. He has never shown any cause to doubt his commitment to the people of Caspar and the Union. This attack on his integrity is beneath the dignity of the Councillor of Lansdowne, at least it should be."

Then a [[Sarian]] male in his forties rises to speak and is identified as Yoseph Sarlin, of Spanner, [[Mergansar]]. "My friend the honourable Councillor of Lansdowne would be wise to remember that we represent the citizens of the Union, that is the workers, that may in some cases be the franchisees, it is most certainly not the Corporate Sector Authority and its citizens. It is farcical to suggest the Councillor of Anchorron has shown an unfair bias by prioritising the needs and desires of Union citizens."

Back in the studio Brackett now looks farther down her right side, "Charrs your thoughts?"

"Well these were both very interestings statements. Councillor Eleria is known to be one of the more congenial and soft spoken members of the Union Council, so for her to enter the fray so quickly one must suspect she was truly quite offended. But I am more interested in the statement from Sarlin. He is a member of the Legacy party along with Keyyaw, but he also comes from the labour movement. So when the stakes are hot we are seeing class loyalty winning out over party here. I think we will see more contentious debate if this impending strike spreads to the Union."

Now the footage goes back and the next speaker is Caspian Presav Crestian Tarasar himself. "I wish to make it perfectly clear that should the Councillor of Anchorron be bullied out of his post as Minister of Industry and Labour, I will nominate in his stead the Councillor of Hammerton, Chairman of the Dusona Workers Union, [[Valdemar Linane]]. I will not seek to comment on the validity of worker grievances while negotiations are still proceeding. But I remain confident that any involvement the Councillor of Anchorron has in this issue will be handled with the best interests of the Caspian people in mind. And as long as I am Presav, the Labour ministry will remain in the hands of a Councillor who supports Labour, anything less would be betrayal of our voters and our conscience."

The screen briefly turns to show reactions from both Councillor Zaalbacca and Councillor Linane who both seem to be smirking.

Finally, Vice Presav Cas Mos rises to speak and simply states, "The Presav will take the issue under advisement, the matter is closed."

The screen returns to the studio where the anchor asks "Final thoughts?"

Clark answers first stating, "This is a sign of the strong working relationship between the Presav and the Vice Presav, in the end this was not going to make anyone look good and she was clearly trying to do everyone a favour by closing off discussion."

Next Curt adds, "What is also notable here is unlike the old [[Union Assembly]], the Union Council has no presiding officer. When it was only a nine member body there probably was little thought to needing a separate speaker from the Presav, but if we see more debates like this the question of having one may be raised. Vice Presav Mos had no actual authority to end the debate with the Presav present. That said, before her career in politics she was a [[General]] in the [[Union Marines]], and under the current structure of things she is Minister of Domestic Affairs and Defence and a [[Marshal]] in the Union Marines. She is used to exercising power and people listening. And I think she was aware she would not be challenged on it here."

Finally Charrs remarks, "I think we be seeing the beginning of something bigger. A real struggle between labour and business in the galaxy. And in spite of the wealth and business interests of various members of the Union Council it would see clear the Union will come down firmly on the side of labour, but not without more debates like these. Whether they remain rather lopsided is yet to be seen but the gloves seem to be off."

"Professor Sir Curt Johnson, Professor Clark Rickart, and Professor Emeritus Charrs Prego-Roamanoff, thank you again."

== Biscuit Strike ==

Short clips of riots on [[Ord Mantell]] are shown on the screen for a what seems like a full minute without any caption or commentary provided before the last image is frozen on the screen and it camera pans back to show an anchor's desk with a tall golden-skinned female sitting behind, Professor Brackett Benduday of InterGalactic News.

"Our top story tonight is the Republic's capital world of Ord Mantell which is now engulfed in a series of riots. I am Brackett Benduday, and this is InterGalactic News. This is news that hit home for us in the studio. Our own [[Thurston Unger]] was reporting on protests at the Government Complex and was attacked by [[New Republic|Republic]] Security. The end result of this scuffle that has been reported on his own show [[The Cutting Edge]] saw Thurston put in the hospital, and a member of his security detail dead. From all of us at IGN we wish you a speedy recovery and our hearts go out to the family of the dead security officer, we are withholding their name for now confirmation that their family has been notified.

This violence has only seen the crowd of protestors increase with more and more of Ord Mantell's citizens taking to the street to protest a myriad of grievances. One in particular has taken the notice of us here at IGN, calling for the planet's secession from the New Republic. As trust in the New Republic as an institution continues to erode, any planet breaking away is a worry for those in the halls of government, but for its own capital world to possibly one of them would be devastating.

We will surely have a lot more on this story in the coming days, but this brings us to another story we have been reporting in recent days, the breakdown in labour relations between the Tagge Restaurant Association and the unions at a multitude of their Biscuit Baron restaurants.

In a somewhat, though not entirely surprising move, the Ord Mantell Biscuit Baron workers union has officially gone on strike. What makes this surprising is the fact that all reports indicate the local negotiations there had not broken down like they had on other worlds. But apparently both management and workers were willing to walk away from the table tonight. In a brief statement released by the local union they stated quote, "Workers should be able to rely on the support of both their employer and their government, it seems we have neither at the moment and shall be going on strike until this changes.""

The image behind Brackett shifts from the riots on Ord Mantell to workers picketing a Biscuit Baron location.

"Shortly after news broke that the Ord Mantell union had left the negotiating table, the union on the Imperium's capital world of Selene abruptly broke off negotiations with management there and joined the picket line. A statement released by their union states "We are joining the picket line in solidarity with our comrades on Ord Mantell. Biscuit Baron needs to deliver terms satisfactory for the well being of their workers across all powers of the galaxy. And hopefully it is clear by now that they cannot rely on any government to maintain the conditions that have allowed them to exploit us.""

"Rumours from Caspar and Mergansar indicate that Councillors Zaalbacca of Anchorron, Valdemar Linane of Hammerton, and Yoseph Sarlin of Spanner are currently holding a meeting with corporate and union leaders in the Caspia System even as we speak."

The image behind Brackett shifts again from the picketing workers to an image of Union Hall in Plaxton on Caspar. With Brackett looking for a moment like she is receiving new information from her producer.

"We apparently now have a statement on the riots on Ord Mantell from Presav Crestian Tarasar. We take you live to Union Hall now."

The studio image fades and is replaced by an image of Caspian Presav Crestian Tarasar behind his desk in Union Hall.

"This is clearly a dark day for the New Republic. My office continues to receive updated reports on injuries on Ord Mantell, thus far the death count does not seem to be rising but we are unable to verify this. All governments regardless of how we derive our power are ultimately answerable to the people. Passions do not mean every complaint lodged is a reasonable one, Gods knows I have faced picketing constituents before I do not want to make more of this than there is to it. But the quickly devolving situation at the seat of power for the New Republic is unfortunately something that neither the Union nor any other galactic power with a shared border can ignore.

The New Republic has repeatedly avoided developing a real relationship with the Caspian Union, as such we do not have a lot of citizens in Republic space. Nevertheless it is necessary at this time for the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Defence to issue a travel advisory for our citizens. Any Union citizens currently in New Republic space are advised to return and any contemplating a visit to Republic space are strongly discouraged from doing so. The [[Caspian Foreign Ministry|Foreign Ministry]] will work to try to assist any citizens trying to extricate themselves from the situation.

Our Minister of Communication, Councillor Milton Keynes will be drafting an official statement on the assault of free press, but in the meantime I must condemn the attack upon Thurston Unger and his security team in the strongest terms.

I have also instructed the Minister for Refugees, Councillor Niklos Wortham to develop a plan to facilitate the acceptance of potential refugees from New Republic Space. We are not encouraging anyone to flee, but this is a rapidly evolving situation and we wish to remain on top of it.

Ultimately we hope cooler heads and warmer hearts prevail here. The galaxy can little afford the failure of the Republic once again. Get it together."

The screen returns to Brackett in the studio, "We will have more on these situations as they develop."

== The Cost of Breakfast ==

An image of workers belonging to a myriad of different species picketing a location of Biscuit Baron on Selene is shown on the screen before panning out to show an anchor desk at IGN. Behind the desk is anchor and financial analyst Professor Brackett Benduday.

"This Brackett Benduday with InterGalactic News. The stock price of the Corporate Sector Authority is at its lowest levels in three years. The last time CSA stock was so low was on the heels of nationalisation drives by the Caspian Union and Galactic Imperium. But no such efforts are underway currently. So why is the price down and continuing to go down? That is our top story tonight.

When the Corporate Sector Authority reorganised five years ago into a corporate entity itself, it bought majority shares in many of its contributing sponsors in exchange for stock in the CSA itself. The end result was the effective merger of many of the galaxy's corporate giants and two men owning almost half the stock between them [[Galactic Emperor|Emperor]] [[Aleister Vadim]] and [[Prex (CSA)|Prex]] [[Zeak Oppenhiemer]].

The effective takeover of the [[Galactic Empire|Empire]] by the [[Tyler Damion|Tyler Damion's]] Imperium and the loss of many of the assets Oppenhiemer brought into the CSA, is what saw its price go low three years ago. While it had never rebounded to quite the stage it had enjoyed on its formation, the curious move to align more closely with the New Republic had staved off further collapse and kept the CSA relevant in some markets.

With riots expanding on Ord Mantell and indications of unrest on worlds like [[Bothawui]], Chandrila, and [[Dreven]]. This alignment seems to not provide any confidence to the market. Indeed stocks in various individual corporations are seeing individual declines, largely in line with how much their operations are tied to New Republic space. This, of course, is only half the story and for the other half we bring you to our own [[Jordaan Mag]], live from the Pallandorf Astoria in Plaxton on Caspar."

The screen switches to a stately hotel on Plaxton's main thoroughfare Mergsanar Avenue. One can imagine that it was once the symbol of class a century ago but that it has not been sufficiently maintained in recent decades. In front it looks like a press conference is about to occur and an [[:starwars:Arkanian Offshoot/Legends|Arkanian-Offshoot]] reporter stands in front of a camera.

"It has been confirmed that union leaders representing Biscuit Baron employees on the Caspian worlds of Krittain Major and [[Krittain Minor|Minor]], [[Kasputin Yar]], [[Rauther]], [[Mikassa]], [[Charros IV]], [[Kichnar Orbital Station]], Mergansar, and Caspar itself have been meeting for hours here at the Pallandorf Astoria. Rumours are the union representatives from other nearby worlds including [[Ngor Nghal]], [[:starwars:Bimmisaari/Legends|Bimmisaari]], [[:starwars:Danuta|Danuta]], [[:starwars:Xoman III|Xoman III]], [[:starwars:Balamak/Legends|Balamak]], and [[:starwars:Togoria/Legends|Togoria]] are here as well. Hold on it looks like the press conference is about to begin."

The reporter and the cameraman move closer to the front of the hotel where a Sarian and Wookiee emerge, followed then by a variety of people mostly Humans but with several other species featured as well. The Sarian and Wookiee take to twin podiums which have been set up for them while the others stand a respectful distance behind them. They are identified on screen as Valdemar Linane, Chairman of the Dusona Workers Union and Councillor of Hammerton, and Zaalbacca, Chairman of the Wookiee Workers Union of Anchorron.

The Sarian begins to speak first, "A short while ago union leaders representing Biscuit Baron workers in the [[Caspian Sector|Caspian]], [[:starwars:Halla sector/Legends|Halla]], and [[:starwars:Taldot sector/Legends|Taldot]] Sectors voted unanimously to go on strike. The strike at the end of shift for each location affected. Here on Caspar and on Mergansar the strike will begin with immediate effect. Should the corporate management at Biscuit Baron wish to come back to the table and offer reasonable terms our unions will organise with them in good faith, and at least as far this affects Caspian worlds the Crest is eager to work with both sides to find an amicable solution."

The Wookiee takes over speaking with a translation to Galactic Basic Standard feature on the screen, though a few words are not translated faithfully, "We don't want this. I mean look at Valdemar, he is getting too old for this picketing shit. Do you know how often picketing on Caspar ends with getting rained on? [[Ixin]] is arid as fuck. And let's not get started on how truly unenviable walking a picket line on Krittain Minor is going to be. It's not going to be pretty. But one of the basic laws of physics is the fuck around and find out. The Tagge Restaurant Association has fucked around thus forcing the find out stage."

The Sarian, who seems amused at his comrades' words, takes over speaking again, "Our Unions will be working to support the needs of our members during this strike. But Biscuit Baron, the Tagge Restaurant Association, and the Corporate Sector Authority ought to take notice that these people are not without other options for employment. They deserve fair wages, not a proverbial jolly meal toy in lieu of overtime pay."

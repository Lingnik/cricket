On the screen is an image of the interior of [[Biscuit Baron]] restaurant with a diverse selection of beings both behind and in front of the camera. The camera eventually zooms in on the counter with a tray displaying several sandwiches. The lack of wrappers indicates this is indeed a promotional display. A voice over indicates that the product on display is the new "Biscuit Baron Infinite Stack." While clearly not actually infinite, the sandwiches are indeed ridiculously tall, and the being they show eating one definitely has a large mouth. At the end of the advertisement a different voice utters the company's new marketing slogan "Taste the Biscuit."

The screen then shifts to the site of [[InterGalactic News|IGN]] anchor's desk with a golden-skinned women sitting behind it. The backdrop behind the anchor's desk are live images of festive crowds on worlds such as [[Caspar]], [[Corellia]], [[Coruscant]], [[Ord Mantell]], and [[Selene]].

"I am [[Brackett Benduday]] and this is InterGalactic News. With the end of the year upon us we will be bringing you to crowds on varying world throughout the galaxy to show you how they are preparing to ring in the new year, but first we have bit of late breaking news from the Core World of [[:starwars:Rhinnal/Legends|Rhinnal]]. The graduation ceremony of the [[:starwars:Rhinnal State Medical Academy|Rhinnal State Medical Academy]] would normally not be on our sensors, but when one of graduates is also a head of state we take notice."

The screen then cuts to footage of [[Caspian Union]] [[Presav]] [[Crestian Tarasar]] walking across the stage as one of the graduates. Crestian is dressed in traditional academic garb and looks almost nervous. Later the footage jumps ahead to him greeting his wife and daughter, with the latter wearing a black military uniform.

The image shifts back to the studio, "Caspian Presav Crestian Tarasar can now add doctor to his many titles having graduated from the academy and having passed his licencing exam on Rhinnal. Our request for his transcript was quickly approved, though records indicate his actual instruction has occurred at a number of different institutions with the [[Imperium]] and Union in addition to Rhinnal State Medical Academy. Our own [[Jordaan Mag]] was able to briefly catch up with Doctor Tarasar."

The screen transitions back to Rhinnal with the [[:starwars:Arkanian Offshoot/Legends|Arkanian-Offshoot]] speaking to Crestian, still in academic garb. "They you for taking the time to talk to us Doctor Tarasar. I think most of our audience would be surprised to see you here. Your previous academic background was in philosophy as I understand, why the switch to medicine?"

The nervousness Crestian showed on stage seem to be gone now that he is talking to the reporter, "I have pursued a number of subjects academically over the years. I did teach ethics long ago at [[:starwars:Brionelle Memorial Military Academy|Brionelle Memorial Military Academy]] and I also dabble in other areas such as translation, but medicine has become a more practical necessity I would say. As a military man I had known little more than first aid.

When I came back to Caspar during the pandemic, I knew I needed to be able to do more. The way that was handled by the authorities then was truly atrocious. Obviously in hindsight we can imagine that the [[Report:Sariphage Press Releases|Sariphage]], as an engineered virus, threw off our researchers, but the fatalistic attitude was unforgivable. Now virology is not my area of study by any stretch, I will not be curing the galaxy's next great disease, but I have wanted to make sure when the next crisis emerges I could be part of the solution and not reliant on advice from experts I didn't understand.

So when the inoculations against Sariphage were first introduced I made sure I was trained to be able to administer them, and it just a spiral from there. When I came back to Caspar to settle my charity founded a new facility, [[Twin Suns Hospital]], to house the largest research programme in the Union and Mid Rim as a whole. We named it in honour of the home world of [[Johanna Siri te Danaan]] who was instrumental in secure the cure to the Sariphage, by the way.

In many ways that hospital is one of my proudest accomplishments. It is as integral to secure a safe future as anything political or military. But again, serving as executive director of the hospital board, I have felt important to be truly qualified medically. So today is the culmination of that effort."

The newly minted doctor shuts up long enough for the reporter to ask another question, "And how have you managed to find the time for this degree?"

Crestian laughs, "Not easily that is for sure. But I have been at this for nearly six years now. Full disclosure most of my classes were not through Rhinnal State. I have worked in whatever I could at various institutions in the Union and occasionally the Imperium. And the fact I already have a hospital meant I have been able to secure rotations in the various specialties with greater ease than other medical students.

I did make a point of making sure my final course work and licensure happened outside of the Union. Rhinnal State has a very strong reputation, which was important to ensure my degree was not conferred by favouritism. There is this old joke, what do you call someone who graduated last in their class at medical school? Doctor. I did not do that bad, but I just barely avoided being in the bottom third. There are some people here that are going to make excellent doctors, I am just here to be able to support the work of better minds.

If I am really lucky the analgesic research I have been puttering on for years may be of some value, but at the moment my brain is pretty fried. The only reason I was able to even find the time to study for my exams was because my wife and I are empty nesters these days. You know your child grows up, they buyout a major restaurant chain, I am surprised she even had time to be here."

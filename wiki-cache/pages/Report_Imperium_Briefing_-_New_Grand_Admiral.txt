Imperial Press Corps Atrium
[[Selene]] - Capital of the [[Imperium]]
Grand Atrium. Vaulted. Severe. Lit as though the stars themselves cast judgment.

The air in the room was taut, every breath taken with care. The silvery-black banners of the Imperium swayed with calculated grace above the assembled press. No background music, no ambient hum. Just silence. Deliberate. Contained.

At exactly the assigned moment-no earlier, no later-the doors at the rear of the atrium hissed open.

Zephara Val'Riin entered. Her robes trailed behind her like the wake of a warship. Silver-threaded mantle curling at the hem. Her face was a mask of precision. Her eyes-reflective chrome, untouched by warmth-swept across the crowd not to greet, but to assess.

She took the podium like a blade takes its sheath. "Recorders on. Editorializing off."

A beat. Then her voice, smooth as carved obsidian, filled the room. "You have, by now, seen the declaration. Heard the chants. Watched the banners fall behind our Imperator. But understand this-this is not a reassignment. It is not a promotion in the dainty, administrative sense that lesser states adore."

Her tone grew sharper, colder.

"It is an unleashing."

"The Imperator has not exchanged hands. He has empowered a weapon. One forged by necessity, tempered in vigilance, and now-wielded without restraint."

"[[Grand Admiral]] [[Axel Vichten]] now commands not only the Imperium's full military might, but acts as Regent of the Throne in all matters of State, Security, and Corruption. He is the Arbiter of Stability. The Executioner of Hesitation. The final barricade between order and disintegration."

She allowed the weight of those words to settle. "Look to the [[New Republic|Republic]]. Observe the fractures. [[Chandrila]] in flames, [[Dac|Mon Calamari]] rising in revolt, and half the Core pretending their crumbling senate is still a seat of power. That will not happen here."

"Not in this Imperium."

Her voice dropped slightly-cool, focused, lethal. "To the enemies who would test the steel of this state... to the saboteurs who believed delay was immunity... to the opportunists who prey on transition-be advised. Your era of tolerance has ended."She tilted her head slightly. "And no, there will be no disclosure of methods. No trial of words. Only consequence."

She gestured behind her-where the massive banners now bore the sigil of the Grand Admiral, outlined in crimson light. "The Imperator has given Grand Admiral Vichten everything he needs. Forces. Authority. Endorsement. Not for ceremony. For results."

"And results," she finished, leaning slightly into the mic, "will not ask your permission."

She stepped back, her expression unreadable, her robes a falling curtain of silence.

No questions. No applause. Only the soft hum of holorecorders capturing the shape of power.

Location: Hangar Bay, [[Republic-class Star Destroyer]] [[RSD Retribution|Retribution]]

The hangar bay of the Retribution is a stark, steel-gray expanse of military precision, every surface immaculate, every soldier standing at attention. A line of black-armored [[Stormtrooper|stormtroopers]] flanks the perimeter, blaster rifles held in parade stance, while a massive [[Imperium]] banner hangs behind the raised podium where Grand Inquisitor [[Axel Vichten]] stands. The Retribution's crew works diligently in the background, fighters being prepped, officers moving with calculated efficiency-a well-oiled war machine.

Axel, clad in the dark regalia of the Imperium's Grand Inquisitor, approaches the podium with measured steps. His piercing blue eyes sweep over the gathered reporters-an assortment of galactic media representatives, some eager, some nervous, and all very aware that this was not a Republic-friendly affair.

He lets the silence hang for a moment, the tension thick enough to slice with his [[Corellian Rapier]], before speaking. "Let's get straight to the point. The Imperium is not in the business of running clandestine gulags left over from the old Empire's worst days. The [[The Oubliette|facility]] in the [[:starwars:Karthakk system|Karthakk system]] was a relic of [[Galactic Empire|Imperial]] mismanagement and authoritarian excess-one that, regrettably, still existed under outdated bureaucratic oversight. We were in the process of decommissioning it when an unauthorized assault occurred. The perpetrators of this reckless attack facilitated the escape of multiple high-risk individuals. That makes them terrorists, not heroes."

Axel's voice is a cold steel blade wrapped in velvet, controlled but undeniably edged with irritation. He lets his words settle before tapping a control on the podium, bringing up a holographic display that flickers to life beside him. A rotating list of names and images appears, each one stamped with a newly issued bounty. "Here is what we do know. The following individuals have escaped from this facility, and every single one of them is considered armed, dangerous, and an enemy of galactic stability. The Imperium has placed bounties on their heads, payable upon confirmation of capture-dead or alive. The list is not the entirety of those who escaped, as our own records are criminally incomplete."

The list of names scrolls across the display, each accompanied by a brief description and grainy picture:

Karthis Vaan - Black-market arms dealer, assassin, orchestrated two high-profile political assassinations in [[New Republic]] space. 250,000 credits.

Jessa "The Phantom" Trel - Former [[Imperial Security Bureau|ISB]] agent, espionage specialist, suspected of involvement in multiple deep-cover sabotage missions. 200,000 credits.

[[Lorn Rhys]] (Alias: Darth Malus) - Former Imperial [[Warlord|warlord]], orchestrator of multiple planetary subjugations, mass murderer, enemy of civilization. 300,000 credits.

Orlen Mar - Mutinous former Imperial [[Admiral|admiral]], known for war crimes and mass executions of rebel prisoners. 150,000 credits.

Bregg Salvar - Mercenary commander, responsible for raiding and destroying a medical convoy carrying refugees during the last years of the war. 100,000 credits.

Axel turns back to the crowd, eyes narrowed. "Make no mistake, these are not political prisoners, nor are they freedom fighters. They are criminals, warlords, and murderers, and the fact that someone saw fit to release them is an act of unforgivable recklessness. The Imperium will find them, and we will deal with them accordingly."

A journalist from Galactic Standard News hesitantly raises a hand. "Grand Inquisitor, there are rumors that this facility was still being used for more than just holding former Imperial officers. Some sources claim political prisoners were also held there. Can you comment on that?"

Axel's jaw tightens slightly. He takes a breath, then offers a thin, sharp smile that doesn't reach his eyes. "No, I cannot. And quite frankly, I'd be very interested in knowing where you get your 'sources.' We are not in the habit of entertaining wild conspiracy theories. The Imperium is not the Empire of old. We operate differently, and relics like the station in the Karthakk system were remnants of the old regime."

Another journalist, from CoreWorlds Tribune, presses forward. "Can you confirm if this escape is related to recent shake-ups in Imperium leadership, such as the removal of [[Moff]] Octal?"

Axel's expression darkens noticeably. "Moff Octal's failure to maintain order in his own system is precisely why he no longer holds his position. His failure was systemic incompetence, and it was rectified before it could do more harm. That is all you need to know."

He takes a step back from the podium, brushing a speck of dust off his sleeve. "The Imperium is committed to ensuring galactic stability. This will not happen again. We will not tolerate outside interference in our security operations, nor will we entertain foolish speculation about matters that are, frankly, far above your pay grade."

He turns, nodding to the black-armored stormtroopers flanking the stage. "This press conference is over." With that, the hangar bay was filled with the mechanical stomp of trooper boots, the Retribution's personnel resuming their operations as the Grand Inquisitor walked away without a backward glance. The Imperium had spoken, and as far as Axel Vichten was concerned, the discussion was closed for the moment.

[[Imperium]] Press Briefing - [[Selene]], Capital Building

The press room of the [[Imperium|Imperium's]] capital building on [[Selene]] was an obsidian cathedral to authority-its stark white lighting casting no shadows, only truth. High arching ceilings created a sense of scale that dwarfed even the boldest of egos. The rows of vetted journalists, neural recorders, and house-collared correspondents sat in enforced silence, their breaths held beneath the gleaming insignia of the Imperium carved into the black marble above the dais.

At the stroke of the marked hour, the soundless rear doors parted. Zephara Val'Riin entered alone. There was no fanfare. There was no announcement. There was only presence, and it was overwhelming. She glided forward, pale-skinned and clad in midnight robes, [[Sarian (language)|Sarian]] script curling across the fabric like smoke. Her silvered eyes caught the sterile light and reflected it back coldly. She moved without hesitation, her pace unhurried, her path unchallenged. When she reached the podium, she placed her hands together and let the room breathe her silence before speaking.

"Good evening."

The word echoed like a distant chime in a tomb. "Let us dispense with the rituals of civility. You are not here to be comforted. I am not here to reassure. We will not trade pleasantries over poison. What you will receive tonight is clarity, sharpened to an edge, and whether you cut yourself on it is no concern of mine."

Her eyes roved across the assembled members of the press-some shifting uneasily, others frozen as if their thoughts might betray them. "You are no doubt salivating for information regarding the breach in the [[:starwars:Karthakk system|Karthakk system]]. I shall oblige, in so much as it serves the Imperium's interests." She did not raise her voice. She never needed to.

"The structure known as [[The Oubliette|Station 1138]] was a relic. A Clone War-era black-sight installation, decommissioned and archived outside public knowledge. Unfortunately, not all ghosts are content to sleep. The facility was reawakened by actors whose names will be stripped from record, and during that unauthorized reactivation, a security breech allowed for a limited number of detainees to escape."

A brief pause. "Among them: Jessa Trel-a fugitive you know as 'The Phantom.' She was a myth you allowed yourselves to romanticize. I now confirm that she is real, that she did escape, and that she has been recovered."

Murmurs rose-and died just as quickly under her gaze. "Trel was intercepted on [[O'paal]], attempting to make contact with what we can only describe as sympathizers. She was taken alive. No Imperium assets were lost. The operation was, surgical."

She allowed that word to hang in the air like a blade suspended from a thread. "She is now in the custody of the Inquisition. Her voice has been silenced. Her myth has been corrected. You may continue to whisper about her in your little corners of commnet trivia, but understand this: Jessa Trel is over."

"You may ask your questions now. Do so carefully."

A correspondent from The Outer Spine Courier-younger, nervous, but emboldened by curiosity-stood. "Press Secretary Val'Riin, how many escapees remain at large from Station 1138?"

Her expression didn't change, but the air shifted. "Multiple. Those of import were listed in a previous press conference. I do not need to repeat. There were multiple people who are outside of Imperium custody." Another pause. "They will not remain so. Next."

A second journalist from the [[Bothan Federation]] Broadcast cleared her throat. "Will the Imperium release the names of the escapees? The public-"

"-will be protected, not panicked," Zephara interrupted smoothly. "You may feel entitled to names, histories, bedtime stories to clutch to your breast at night, but that is not how the Imperium governs. We do not rule through transparency. We rule through truth, and the truth is this: you are safe. That is all you require."

There was a beat of silence. Then another. She folded her hands again. "If there are no other relevant inquiries, I will now conclude."

No one moved. No one dared.

"Then allow me to leave you with this: power does not always roar. Sometimes, it whispers. Sometimes, it waits. But it never sleeps. The Grand Inquisitor sees all, and through me, he is heard." With that, she turned and departed-her steps as unhurried as they were final.

The press remained seated long after she had gone, as though any movement might shatter the illusion that they had survived the storm. They had not. Only the message had.

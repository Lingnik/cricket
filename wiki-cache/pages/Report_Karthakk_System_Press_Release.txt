Location: [[Imperium]] Command Headquarters, [[:starwars:Karthakk system|Karthakk System]]

A stark, polished conference hall gleams under the white lights of Imperium Command Headquarters. The Imperium crest looms behind the podium, flanked by two armored black-armored [[Stormtrooper|Stormtroopers]] standing at rigid attention. The press, a mixture of eager correspondents and weary veterans of these types of briefings, murmur amongst themselves as they await the Grand Inquisitor's arrival.

When [[Axel Vichten]] enters, the room falls silent. Clad in his high-collared, dark Imperium uniform, he approaches the podium with a slow, deliberate stride. His sharp gaze sweeps over the assembled press like a predator sizing up its prey.

With a press of a button, the Imperium emblem behind him shifts to a star map of Karthakk, pinpointing the soon-to-be restricted area. Without preamble, he begins.

"Effective immediately, [[Moff]] Jenis Octal has been relieved of his duties as the governing authority of the Karthakk system. This decision was not made lightly, nor was it made in haste. It was made because failure to execute the responsibilities of one's station is unacceptable. The governance and security of this system fall upon the Moff, and when the system fails?he fails."

His tone is even, but sharp, allowing no room for misinterpretation.

"Until the [[Moff's Council]] names a replacement, administration of Karthakk will fall to the Office of the Inquisitor. There will be no disruptions. There will be no delays. The Imperium does not tolerate stagnation."

The holoscreen shifts, now displaying what looks to be a decommissioned space station on the outer rim of the Karthakk system. The image itself is unusually grainy, given the technology used.

"Additionally, in the coming days, there will be a scheduled military exercise within this system, which will involve the simulated attack and eventual destruction of this station. That region is now strictly off-limits to all unauthorized personnel. Any trespassers will be dealt with to the fullest extent of Imperium law." There's a brief pause, "In clearer terms, if you enter that space, there will be no warning shots, no requests over comms for you to turn around. You will become a target, and either intercepted and attacked by a squadron of [[TIE Avenger|
TIE Avengers]] or face the destructive potential of a [[Star Destroyer|star destroyer's]] turbolasers. You will not be captured, you will be vaporized."

He leans forward slightly, his steely gaze locking onto the cameras.

"That is not a warning. That is a statement of fact."

A tense silence follows before the first reporter dares to speak.

"Grand Inquisitor, was there a specific event that triggered Moff Octal's removal, or was this an ongoing pattern of failures?"

Axel's jaw tightens. His eyes flick to the reporter as though debating whether the question is even worth acknowledging. After a long pause, he speaks.

"The details are classified. Simply know that Janis Octal failed in his duties, and for that he has been relieved of said duties. Any further action involving the man will be up to the Moff Council or the Imperator himself."

Another reporter quickly jumps in.

"Can you clarify if there was internal resistance to Octal's removal? Was he cooperative?"

Axel exhales sharply through his nose, his patience visibly thinning.

"Octal's cooperation is irrelevant. The decision was made. It was executed. Next question."

A third reporter, perhaps emboldened by the clear agitation on Axel's face, presses on.

"With this military exercise happening so soon after a major leadership shift, is the Imperium concerned about unrest or resistance within Karthakk's military forces?"

Axel snaps his gaze toward the reporter, his expression cold.

"The Imperium does not concern itself with speculation."

Silence falls again. The air in the room is thick with tension.

One final reporter, visibly nervous, raises a hand.

"Is this exercise connected in any way to recent events in [[Hutt Space|Hutt space]]?"

Axel tilts his head slightly, as if considering the question. A tense second passes before he lets out a low, humorless chuckle.

"The two events are unrelated. This briefing is over."

Without another word, Axel turns on his heel and strides from the podium, his dark coat flaring slightly behind him. The guards move in, signaling the end of the conference. The reporters, left with more questions than answers, hurriedly transmit their stories.

The message is clear; Karthakk belongs to the Imperium, and Axel Vichten is making sure everyone knows it.

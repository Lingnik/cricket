The last three minutes of the day's [[InterGalactic News|IGNews]] holocast:

"Finally, our story tonight from the southeastern Outer Rim: two agriworlds in the [[:starwars:Arkanis sector/Legends|Arkanis sector]] have reported full-scale crop failure. It is the result, experts now confirm, of a genetically engineered blight and bears the hallmarks of deliberate sabotage. The first reports emerged five standard days ago from the grain colonies of [[:starwars:New Ator|New Ator]], where countless hectares collapsed within hours under the sudden onset of a fungal pathogen with no precedent in known spore registries. This morning, a second world, [[:starwars:Vasch|Vasch]], declared agricultural emergency after similar symptoms were observed across its orchards and greenhouses."

Footage of ag-droids working in vain to counteract the fungus is followed by shots of dead and withered plants.

"Officials with the independent Agricultural Integrity Commission now state, quote: This is not a naturally occurring organism. The mutation pathways suggest purposeful design."

A map of the sector, with faded red spreading across shipping routes briefly appears.

"Both New Ator and Vasch serve as primary food exporters for several systems and frontier colonies. Now, with shipments suspended, tension is building. Relief vessels report increased orbital traffic as panic has begun to grip the affected planets, a small but steady exodus of individuals making their way off-world.

The majority are making their way to the more fortified planets of the sector, including [[Tatooine]]. There, marshals report growing unrest as locals voice concerns about possible food and water rationing. Tensions escalated earlier today when a water shipment convoy was delayed, and a protest erupted in [[Mos Espa]]. Local [[warlord]] [[Johanna Siri te Danaan|Johanna te Danaan]] has pledged emergency aid, though how it will be distributed remains the subject of intense debate. With demand expected to skyrocket, several planetary authorities have submitted competing requests for relief.

Thus far, no group has claimed responsibility for the blight. For now, border security forces urge travelers to avoid non-essential passage through the area. Shipping lanes are under review, and independent freighter captains are advised to register all cargo with nearest outpost authorities."

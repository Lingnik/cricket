== General Strike ==

A tall golden-skinned female, economist Professor Brackett Benduday, can be seen sitting behind an anchor's desk in a studio. To her left is Professor Clark Rickart of the Plaxton Polling Institute. With an another man, this one an elderly Human to her right.

"This is Brackett Benduday with the latest reports from Mergansar. To my left Professor Clark Rickart of the Plaxton Polling Institute and to my right Professor Emeritus [[Charrs Prego-Roamanoff]] of the history department at Plaxton University. The latest news is the rioting in Ton Mulatta and Ton Echotta has not transformed into a general strike in both those cities and Charon as well. Professor Prego what can you tell us about this?"

"Brackett we can thank the efforts of the Dusona Workers Union for the strike. The Dusona province where these cities are located is the industrial heartland of Mergansar and traditionally the star system as a whole. During the pogroms last year the Olumi Osahn punks tried to convince workers that the non-Sarian were threats to their jobs and livelihoods. This has long been the tactic of capitalists in the galaxy to make their workers more manageable, pit them against each other based on demographics. They had limited success doing so on Mergansar, however, as the non-Sarian population was only a tenth of the total population and was evenly distributed among the classes.

A number of the union leaders had actually been forced to flee at the time including Valdemar Linane. Now that industry had already ground to a halt it has been a lot easier for the remaining union leaders to convince the workers that the Olumi Osahn, Mergansarsolu, the factory owners, the whole elite structure are just a bunch of shits exploiting them, that they not the non-Sarians are their real enemy. At the moment they are demanding the resignation of the entire government with free elections where the collaborators are barred from running and the citizens driven off world may run and vote. But I think we can expect that if the resignations are not forth coming retirement by blaster bolt might be arranged in its place."

"Interesting that you should mention [[Valdemar Linane]]. According to a daily press briefing by the office of Councillor [[Zaalbacca]] of [[Anchorron]], he apparently hosted two our meeting with Mister Linane. Also listed in being in attendance were Councillor Zynski of Breslau, Councillor Starflare of Lurlansk, and Councillor Vilarasar of Vanover as well as to of lead officers on the Mergansar Blockade, [[Commodore]] Manoff of [[CMS Typhoon]] and Captain Shelshasa of the [[Tethys]]. Clark what do you make of this meeting.”

"I think we are starting to see an end to the posturing and the beginning of a real step forward. I don't think anyone expects that authorities on Mergansar are ever going pay up 30 trillion credits to the refugees and the [[Caspian Navy]] isn't going to sit in orbit waiting for it forever. Nor can we expect, particularly now that the union has largely returned its old borders, that the Caspian Union is going to accept a sovereign Mergansar at all, let alone one that is tainted by cultists leading it. One way or another it would seem Dusona province will be rejoining the union sooner rather than later."

== Reunification ==

A tall golden-skinned female, economist Professor Brackett Benduday, can be seen sitting behind an anchor's desk in a studio. She is joined by Professor Clark Rickart of the Plaxton Polling Institute.

"This is Brackett Benduday with news from the [[Caspia|Caspia System]]. After weeks of relative quiet the Caspian Union has passed what they are dubbing the Mergansar Reunification Act. The act formally ends the blockade of Mergansar, though the Dusona region and much of the Ioluma region had already been allowed to resume monitored shipping. The act also appoints Sar Admiral [[Odie Hood]], Councillor of Ithaca as the [[Governor|Military Governor]] of Mergansar pending the full re-establishment of local government. With me tonight is Professor Clark Rickart, Director of the Plaxton Polling Institute. The question I think on the mind of most casual observers of Caspian politics is why did this take so long to happen?"

"In a nutshell, things just haven't gone according to plan at all. The intention of the blockade had always been to force Mergansarsolu, the government that had formed on Mergansar after the fall of the Caspian Democratic Union, to be overthrown internally and for new planetary government or regional governments to petition the new [[Caspian Union]] for membership. The first condition has been met for sometime as the old local government lost effective control weeks ago, even where it still has possessed nominal control it has lacked the ability to enforce it.

Where things have not gone according to plan is in the formation of these new governments. There have been some success stories from the Caspian perspective, Sar Admiral Hood's administration in Ithaca has been recognised and readmitted to the Union. But the only other city that has been politically reintegrated at this point is Hammerton under Chairman Valdemar Linane as Councillor. His union has effective control over Spanner and Charon as well but they are reluctant to assert legal political authority there as they hope to see each city represented in the Union Council, but the work of establishing local administrations there has stalled. Local civic groups have emerged to handle the day to day administrative needs of Vilarasala and Anphora as well, but forming an effective government organisation there has likewise stalled. Meanwhile the cities of the plains of Janina have really had no leaders emerge.

There has even been talk of Councillor [[Sha Dark]] who is originally from Goshen, returning to take charge a the local administration there and triggering a by-election in [[Orleanon]]. Her office has not denied such speculation, but I suspect she is far more comfortable representing refugees on Caspar than trying to bring them back to Mergansar. So appointing a military governor seems aimed at jump starting the process of reforming local government by empowering someone local to establishment parameters necessary for holding elections. The Union Council appears to have other priorities right now than micromanaging this reunification process."

== Earthquake ==

Regular broadcasting is interrupted with the view of [[InterGalactic News|IGN]] anchor and economist Professor Brackett Benduday getting behind the anchors desk for what clearly seems to be an unplanned broadcast.

"Breaking news from the Caspia system. A localised but massive seismic event has hit the city of Hebron on Mergansar. Details are still coming it but it appears that the starport which had once been the planet's main port for interstellar trade has been completely destroyed. Damage has been report in nearby neighbourhoods but thus far it appears that the quake was centred under the starport. 

Miraculously the casualty reports thus far appear to be minimal with few fatalities report. A radiation leak earlier in the day had shuttered most of the starport's operation and only a skeleton crew had been in place while droids we handling the decontamination procedures."

A graphic on the screen shows the logo for the [[Groton Sciences Institute]], the Caspian Union's primary civilian science organisation.

"A spokesman for the Groton Sciences Institute was at a loss to explain this current quake or indeed the recent seismic instability reported across Mergansar. He was quick to point out that no quakes of the magnitude had thus far been reported anywhere else on the planet. That said anecdotal evidence from sources on the planet suggests this may not be entirely true. When asked if the earlier radiation leak may have played a role the spokesman claimed this could not be ruled out, but thus far there is no evidence of an incident with the starport's systems immediately prior to the quake."

The graphic of the GSI is replaced now with the image of the city of [[Noques Rasala]] on Caspar.

"An alternative explanation has begun to emerge from the religious communities in the Caspian city of Noques Rasala. Pointing out that the starport in question had been the focal point of the pogroms which displayed 300 million innocent people from Mergansar during the Sariphage crisis, some religious leaders have been suggesting to their followers that it is now a clear sign that the quakes are retribution from the Gods for the role the world played in the genocide perpetrated by the heretical Olumi Osahn cult. We will have more on this story it develops."

== Charon ==

Professor Brackett Benduday, IGN anchor and economist and Professor Clark Rickart of the Plaxton Polling Institute sit behind the anchor desk.

"This is the IGN with the latest news from the Caspian Union. I am Brackett Benduday and I am joined by Professor Clark Rickart of the Plaxton Polling Institute. Today's news is from Charon on Mergansar where the planet's third Councillor has been elected to the Union Council where they will join Councillors Valdemar Linane of Hammerton and Odie Hood of Ithaca in representing the Sarian homeworld.

Before we announce the winner could you give us a bit of a run down on the race Clark? I understand that this election has come together in a shall we say an unusual manner. In fact isn't this essentially the first real election that Mergansar has had now that it has returned to the Caspian fold?"

The camera then pivots to the Human on the anchor's right.

"That is essentially correct Brackett. The elections of Sar Admiral Hood and Chairman Linane while meeting minimal galactic standards for free elections were considered rather pro forma. The election in Charon was expected to be more competitive. While Spanner and Charon have both been under the effective control of Linane's Dusona Workers Union in recent months, they had failed to provide Presav Tarasar's [[Praecet]] with a particularly energising candidate. The [[Legacy]] party was likewise seeming unable to find a star to improve their waning fortunes. [[Holicet]] and [[Ulmesar]] were set to have a fighting chance to pick up a seat, though naturally given the Sarian nationalist bent of Ulmesar and the suicide-bombing by their [[Kichnar Orbital Station|Kichnar]] candidate, their various candidates and party officials on Mergansar have been under increase scrutiny.

The race was thrown wide open, however, by the last minute entrance of [[Philinda Jorgan]], the Senator of the Dusona province at the time of the Sariphage who had been presumed deceased along with the rest of the Crest. Suffice it to say in spite of questions as to where she has been all this time having the name recognition of a legacy candidate quickly made her the favourite in the race with both the Praecet and Legacy party candidates withdrawing.

There appears to have been some initial confusion about what party Senator Jorgan would be running to represent. She received the endorsement of the shuttered Praecet campaign but their camp has denied any expectation she would be representing their party. Meanwhile the talk from the Legacy camp seems to indicate they thought she would run under their banner. Instead she ran as a candidate for [[Lijsttrekker]], [[Ixin]] based party which also holds one of the seats on [[Kasputin Yar]]."

The camera pivots back to the anchor.

"Unsurprisingly Senator Jorgan was elected Councillor of Charon with 83 percent of the vote. The Ulmesar and Holicet candidates receiving 14 and 3 percent respectively. But, perhaps the bigger news tonight was Councillor Jorgan's inaugural address in the Union Council."

A stock image of identified as Councillor Philinda Jorgan appears on the screen as the anchor continues to narrate. The councillor appears to be roughly fifty years old and is [[Epicanthix]].

"Touching upon the recent destruction of the Hebron starport, Jorgan was expected to introduce a bill related financing its reconstruction. Instead the newly minted Councillor submitted a detailed proposal to instead expand the existing facilities in Charon. The cost of this proposal has not yet been released but it has been reported that the plans would see the new Charon starport exceed the size of the Hebron one and possibly even rival Union Starport on Caspar.

The proposal took several Councillors by surprise with Councillor Greatjon Humber of Humberling on [[Rauther]] quoted as calling it opportunistic, which several other choices words apparently shared off the record. Still others appeared to favour the project with Praecet deputy leader Zaalbacca pointing out the opportunity this presented for good paying jobs that would bring non-Sarians back to Mergansar. The session ended when Legacy leader Vice Presav Cas Mos made a motion to adjourn so the matter could be studied further."

Professor Brackett Benduday, IGN anchor and economist and Professor Clark Rickart of the Plaxton Polling Institute sit behind the anchor desk. An image of the Mergani city of Toresta displayed in the background.

Slightly less expected were Brullian Aresko and Lisal Yutan. Brullian Aresko is a former marine and associate of Corgan Vir's, as candidate for Holicet he is promoting platform of healing and peace. Yutan  meanwhile was an aide to Marook until recently with not a lot known about her or why she has ended up as the candidate for governing party of the Union though it is rumoured that she once had a relationship with Admiral Biker of the [[Caspian 4th Fleet]].

Thus far he has remained free of any claims of profiting off the pogroms and he comes with a great deal of name recognition in the city which no doubt contributed to him receiving the nomination from the Legacy party. He is also apparently related in some fashion to Corgan Vir.

As for Elkanah Temereth no one seems to know much about him other than he is served with Brullian Aresko in the marines. The far it seems his candidacy is more of a vanity project than anything. Our initial polls have Legacy at 43.3%, Ulmesar at 22.1%, Holicet at 14.4%, Praecet at 9.7%, Lijsttrekker at 9.1%, and Mister Temereth at 1.3%."

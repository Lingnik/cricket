{{IGNpost|author=[[Crestian Tarasar]]|date=[[34 ABY]]|topic=Financial News|anchors=[[Brackett Benduday]]|headline=Muun Money Minute - Haor Chall Engineering|synopsis=[[Brackett Benduday]] discusses the state of [[Haor Chall Engineering]] following the death of [[Prelate]] [[r'ratak-varattak-n'gavanak]] with guests [[Atsvara Tarasar]], [[Veruca Violet]], and [[Sirlahn Inglet]].}}

The screen is filled with images of precision manufacturing of starfighters being done at the Xi Chall [[Cathedral Factory]]. Each scene of work seems to be done under the watch of a particularly dignified looking [[Xi Charrian]]. After several factory scenes the screen shifts to showing some of the starfighters in flight. A voice over of a woman speaking [[Galactic Basic Standard]] states, "[[Haor Chall Engineering]], the deity is in the details." Finally the screen shifts to a photographic portrait of the dignified Xi Charrian from earlier with the caption: [[Prelate]] [[r'ratak-varattak-n'gavanak]] [[:starwars:44 BBY/Legends|44 BBY]] – [[34 ABY]].

Following the advertisement the screen shifts to a news studio with three chairs set up and an image of the Xi Chall Cathedral Factory in the background on [[Charros IV]]. In the chairs the programme's anchor, Professor [[Brackett Benduday]], along with a golden-skinned pre-teen girl, and [[Pantoran]] teenage girl, a [[Sluissi]] man is also present though in a relaxed posture. The logo for [[InterGalactic News]] and the [[Muun Money Minute]] financial programme feature prominently in the middle of the screen before shifting to smaller versions at the bottom.

The anchor and her guests appear to be talking to one another inaudibly before turning to the camera and saying, "This is the Muun Money Minute, sponsored tonight by the [[Wookiee Workers Union of Anchorron]] who wish to share a message of sympathy and solidarity with their Xi Charrian comrades." The anchor pauses for a moment and smiles, "That is really beautiful. We are here tonight to discuss Haor Chall Engineering in light of the passing this week of Prelate r'ratak-varattak-n'gavanak. 

With us today we have Baroness [[Atsvara Tarasar]], Chief Executive Officer of [[Biscuit Baron]] and the [[Golden Bantha Group]]; [[Veruca Violet]], Chief Operating Officer of Biscuit Baron, the Golden Bantha Group, and the Silverwood House of Fashion; and [[Sirlahn Inglet]], the [[Caspian Union|Caspian]] Minister of Science and Technology and Chief Executive Officer of [[SubPro|Sublight Products Corporation]].

Let's start off with you Baroness Tarasar, where do you see Haor Chall Engineering as a brand, now and going forward?" The anchor turns to young golden-skinned girl sitting next to her, "That is a really good question Brackett. Haor Chall is just in such a different place than it has been historically. Back during the Clone Wars it was a hugely important part of the Confederacy's war effort and yet that legacy was something that feels almost erased. This was, of course, all way before my time. But all the great holofilms about the war....you hear about the Trade Federation, the Techno Union, the InterGalactic Banking Clan, you hear about [[Neimoidian|Neimoidians]] and [[Geonosian|Geonosians]], and really jack shit about Haor Chall Engineering and the Xi Charrians. You see the products like the [[:starwars:Variable Geometry Self-Propelled Battle Droid, Mark I/Legends|Vulture Droids]], but the beings who made them feel like they were largely forgotten.

The situation now is just the complete opposite. Obviously the [[Caspian Defence Force]] is probably Haor Chall's largest customer and the Union and [[Xi Char]] have worked very hard to make sure that relationship is really front and centre. The [[Charros Wargames|Wargames]], a couple of years ago, for example, were really a huge step toward making the Xi Char and all their efforts known. And that is something I don't see slowing down at all. The next Prelate will be inheriting a company with extensive contracts not only with the Caspian Union, but several independent worlds as well, and private corporations such as Biscuit Baron. I think any major conflict you see in the galaxy in next thirty years is almost guaranteed to feature Haor Chall Engineering products on at least one side."

As Atsvara finishes, the Pantoran beside her interjects, "There is something about this that Prelate r'ratak-varattak-n'gavanak totally does not get enough credit for. Haor Chall Engineering wasn't just some subsidiary that [[Kuat Drive Yards-Sienar Fleet Systems|KDY-SFS]] just gave back to the Xi Char. It was a defunct entity. The Xi Char got back control of designs and factories but there was no real corporate structure to inherit.

Manufacturing may be an act of worship to the Xi Char but managing a corporation, with contracts and customers across the galaxy is not the same as managing a religious organisation. Someone like [[ak]] rightly gets credit for his role negotiating the treaty that allowed all of this to happen, but the organisational efforts to make it all actually work, the work behind the scenes, that was r'ratak."

When the Pantoran finishes the anchor remarks, "Thank for those insights ladies, now turning it over to Minister Inglet. What do you expect to see going forward from Haor Chall Engineering, and how will that fit into the larger Caspian defence industry?"

The Sluissi responds, "Well Brackett, to this point Haor Chall has been producing a smaller portfolio of products on a large scale than it had done during the Clone Wars. A mix of both newer versions of old designs as well as complete new designs. On most these they have been partnering with other Caspian firms like SubPro, [[StarShield]], and [[Lokkai Starworks]]. The fact that there has been little active fighting in the galaxy in recent years has largely informed their focus on producing fewer designs, not to mention all the previous years of conflicts have saturated the market with designs in some areas.

What happens next depends a lot on galactic politics and what the needs of the defence industry both in the Union and elsewhere ends up being. There are some civilian craft we may see resurrected should the galactic economy improve. Haor Chall also had a history of producing designs for aquatic environments. There hasn't been much call for that lately, and I do not put much stock in the rumours around [[Dac]], but it is possible there may be a market for such designs again. Where that to happen I would expect some partnership between Haor Chall and another Union firm, possibly Hixam Sidney Design on [[Krittain Major]].

But I will tell you Brackett what I would really like to see come out of a cathedral factory is a swoop bike. I actually a little surprised ak hasn't produced one yet, but maybe he has one stashed away waiting for the right moment."

Atsvara seems quite please at the Sluissi's suggestion. She turns to the camera and says, "Yes. ak do this, do this right the fuck now." The Pantoran gives her a a bit of glare and she add, "Well after the [[conclave]]." The anchor then interjects herself, "Well clearly we have one enthusiastic customer here." 

The anchor pauses for a moment and then ends with, "As Haor Chall Engineering is not a publicly traded company, nor are its main business partners we cannot report on investor confidence ahead of the conclave, but the financial analysts we have polled seem optimistic that it will be business as usual under the next Prelate. I want to thank my Atsvara, Veruca Violet, and Sirlahn for coming on the show. Our next reporting on this topic will be coming live from Charros IV where ceremonies honouring the late Prelate will be occurring soon."

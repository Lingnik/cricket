{{IGNpost|author=[[Axel Vichten]]|date=[[32 ABY]]|topic=New Republic Politics|anchors=None|headline=NR Disputes Claims of Corruption|synopsis=The [[New Republic]] disputes claims by [[Thurston Unger]], on his programme [[The Cutting Edge]], of corruption within their ranks.}}

The [[New Republic]] press secretary stood behind the podium, her voice trembling as she prepared to address [[Thurston Unger|Thurston's]] recent allegations of government corruption.

"Hello," she began, her tone lacking the natural charisma of Thurston. "We are aware of the recent claims made by [[The Cutting Edge]] regarding government corruption within the New Republic."

She cleared her throat, trying to regain composure. "We want to assure you that the New Republic categorically denies any allegations of corruption within our ranks. We pride ourselves on transparency and integrity."

Her confidence faltered, and she searched for her words. "We understand that people may have concerns, but we want to stress that these allegations require substantial evidence. We believe in due process and the presumption of innocence."

The press secretary's attempts to project confidence only highlighted her unease. "The Cutting Edge claims to have proof of corruption, but we have yet to see any of that evidence. We encourage responsible journalism and fair evaluation of the facts."

She shuffled her papers, visibly anxious. "The New Republic is committed to transparency and fairness. We have systems in place to investigate any legitimate concerns that are brought to our attention."

Her voice grew fainter as she continued. "We want to emphasize that the New Republic is a complex organization with dedicated individuals working for the betterment of the galaxy. Any isolated incidents should not be construed as widespread corruption."

She nervously adjusted her collar, trying to regain her composure. "The New Republic remains steadfast in its commitment to democracy and creating a better future. We encourage citizens to participate and hold their representatives accountable."

Her voice regained some strength as she concluded her statement. "In conclusion, the New Republic stands by its principles and strives to address any legitimate concerns. We are committed to transparency and justice."

As she stepped away from the podium, the press secretary's relief was evident. The absence of Thurston's charismatic presence and the lingering sense of defensiveness remained, underscoring the reluctance to acknowledge the gravity of the allegations.

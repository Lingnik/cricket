[[InterGalactic News|IGNews]] Special Bulletin - Live HoloNet Feed
Location: Senate Plaza, [[Ord Mantell]] - Steps of the [[New Republic]] Government Complex

The [[Ord Mantell]] sky is darkening, both from twilight and the heavy clouds of tension. [[New Republic Senate|Senate]] Plaza is packed-hundreds, perhaps thousands, of protestors fill the streets, chanting slogans and waving homemade signs: "Justice for [[Han'bel Eckhart|Eckhart]]," "Only Heroes Get Silenced," and "Mira Lirien Tells the Truth."

At the foot of the government complex steps stands a simple podium surrounded by flickering holocams and security droids. The [[New Republic|Republic's]] gold-and-blue banner sways stiffly in the wind. A hush falls over the plaza as a worn-looking man in a slate grey government jacket steps forward. He?s in his early seventies, hair thinning, the gleam in his eyes dulled by too many years of compromise and half-truths. His name flickers on the overlay: Press Liaison Oland Brosh.

Oland Brosh clears his throat "Good evening, citizens of the Republic. My name is Oland Brosh, Press Liaison for the Mantellian Office of Civil Communications. I am here today to address the recent... developments surrounding the situation with the rogue vessel Templar, and the statements made by former Senate Aide Mira Lirien."

He fumbles with a datapad, its screen flickering as it syncs with the nearby transmission grid. "Firstly, let me remind you that [[Colonel]] Han'bel Eckhart and the crew of the Templar were, by all official accounts, operating outside the purview of the [[New Republic Military]]. Their actions, though inspiring to some, were in direct violation of military protocol, cross-jurisdictional aid mandates, and chain-of-command statutes outlined in Article Seventeen-C."

His voice lacks conviction. A few protestors boo. Brosh presses on. "It is unfortunate-deeply unfortunate-that during the peaceful detainment of [[Colonel]] Eckhart, resistance was met, and? injuries occurred. He is currently receiving medical attention at a classified facility. The Republic is working diligently to ensure-"

He hesitates. His gaze drifts to the crowd, his voice a bit quieter, "to ensure that the truth? is honored."

A pause. Something shifts in his expression. He no longer looks at the prompter. "I served during the Rebellion. I stood on comm stations while we tracked Imperial warships. I watched friends die so we could build something better. And now? I'm being handed lines to read about a man whose greatest crime was refusing to let red tape stop him from saving lives."

The crowd begins to stir, murmurs of support rippling through them.

"Colonel Eckhart didn't pull a trigger during that arrest. He ordered his crew to stand down. He took the fall for doing the right thing. And now I?m supposed to tell you that he?s a traitor? That we?re all safer because he's in a cell?"

He drops the datapad on the podium with a sharp clack. Security droids twitch. The holocams buzz louder.

"Mira Lirien told the truth. She did what the rest of us were too damn scared to do. And if there?s even a sliver of the old Republic left in this bureaucracy-then we need to listen to her. We need to stop pretending that sending aid without permission is worse than watching people starve."

A red light flashes on the podium. The audio begins to distort. Someone is trying to cut the feed.

Brosh leans into the mic, "You can kill the broadcast. You can erase the transcripts. But the people already know. You've failed them. And I won?t be the mouthpiece for that failure."

The audio cuts completely. The screen glitches. When it comes back, Oland Brosh is already stepping down from the platform, shedding his jacket and walking into the crowd of protestors. They part for him-then close ranks around him with applause and chants of his name.

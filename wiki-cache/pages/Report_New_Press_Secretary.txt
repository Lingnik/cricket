[[Imperium]] Press Briefing - [[Selene]], Capital Building

The press room of the [[Imperium|Imperium's]] capital building on [[Selene]] is a grand hall of polished obsidian and cold white lighting. The ceiling arch high above, designed not just to impress but to make those within feel small. Rows of reporters, holorecorders, and carefully vetted media figures sit before the raised podium, where the insignia of the Imperium gleams like a brand on the universe itself.

At precisely the scheduled moment, the doors at the back of the hall open. The low murmur of conversation dies instantly. [[Axel Vichten]] enters first, his presence enough to demand silence. Dressed in the severe uniform of the Grand Inquisitor, his cold blue eyes sweep across the gathered press. He makes no show of pleasantries. He doesn't need to. His authority was unquestioned.

"Members of the press," Axel begins, his voice as sharp as a [[Vibro-Blade|vibroblade]], "I have no intention of standing here wasting time. You all have questions, and going forward, you will be speaking with someone far better suited to answering-or refusing to answer your inquiries."

His lips quirk in something that might be amusement but hold no warmth.

"It is my pleasure-" The word carries a dangerous edge. "-to introduce Zephara Val'Riin, Press Secretary of the Grand Inquisitor's Office." He steps aside, a predator making room for another.

Zephara Val'Riin moves forward with the grace of inevitability. Clad in flowing midnight robes embroidered with the curling script of her Sarian culture, she was a striking figure of power wrapped in silk. Her silvered eyes, reflecting the glow of the overhead lights, sweep across the room with an intensity that makes even the most seasoned journalists shift in their seats.

She stands before the podium, hands folding neatly in front of her. When she finally speaks, her voice is as smooth as glass yet heavy with an unspoken weight.

"Good evening," she says. A pause. Letting the moment stretch, letting them settle into the realization that every word she spoke will be deliberate, precise, and unassailable.

"I am Zephara Val'Riin. You may not know me yet, but in time, you will learn that I am not in the habit of repeating myself, nor will I engage in the tedious performance of verbal jousting that some of you mistake for journalism."

The silence was absolute.

"I was chosen for this position not to placate you. Not to soften the message. Not to make the Imperium more palatable to those who gnash their teeth at the reality of power. I was chosen because I am capable of dealing with the press so that the Grand Inquisitor can keep his focus on what truly matters, the safety and stability of the galaxy."

She lets the weight of those words settle before continuing, her tone never rising, never faltering. "My role is to ensure that you receive the truth that matters, stripped of the indulgences of speculation and the disease of hysteria. And so, let us set the foundation now: I will speak, and you will listen. If you wish to test the strength of your questions against the weight of my answers, you may do so-but be certain that you are prepared for the weight of my response."

The press corps has never encountered anything quite like her.

A journalist from the [[Chandrila]] News Network hesitates before raising a hand. "Press Secretary Val'Riin, given the recent developments in [[:starwars:Karthakk system|Karthakk]]-"

Zephara turns her gaze upon him, and it was as if the room itself had grown colder. "I was not invited here today to discuss Karthakk," she says smoothly, almost kindly. "But since you have brought it forth like a beggar with an empty cup, let me offer you something to fill it."

She steps forward, each word spoken with the precision of a blade sliding into place. "The Karthakk situation is under control. That is the only truth you require. The details, the nuances, the whispers you so desperately seek? They are of no consequence to the world outside these walls. You may salivate for more, but I assure you, you will leave unsatisfied. The Imperium does not conduct its affairs according to the appetites of the press."

The journalist looks as if he wants to press further but wisely chooses not to. 

Zephara turns her attention back to the room at large, her expression one of serene finality. "Now," she continues, "if there are inquiries relevant to my actual purpose here, I am prepared to entertain them."

The room remains silent for a beat longer than might be comfortable. And then, as if on cue, the first real questions begin.

Zephara Val'Riin had arrived.

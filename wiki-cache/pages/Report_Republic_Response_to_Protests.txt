{{IGNpost|author=[[Han'bel Eckhart]]|date=[[32 ABY]]|topic=New Republic Politics|anchors=None|headline=Republic Response to Protests|synopsis=The [[New Republic]] government responds to protests on [[Dreven]] and [[Chandrila]].}}

"The [[New Republic]] acknowledges the recent protests taking place on [[Dreven]] and [[Chandrila]], and we understand the concerns expressed by our citizens. We believe in the right to peaceful assembly and freedom of speech, which are the foundation of our democratic society."

Press Secretary Harrin stood behind a podium, projecting an air of confidence as he addressed the gathered reporters and news crews. His voice was measured but determined, emphasizing the importance of the New Republic's response to the ongoing situation.

"We are fully aware of the demonstrations occurring on Dreven and Chandrila," Press Secretary Harrin began, his tone resolute. "We recognize the frustration and discontent that some of our citizens are feeling, and we take their concerns seriously."

As he spoke, a large video screen behind him displayed live footage of the protests, showing throngs of people marching through the streets, holding signs and chanting slogans demanding change.

Harrin continued, "The New Republic is committed to addressing the grievances raised by our citizens and ensuring that their voices are heard. We have initiated an internal investigation into allegations of corruption and misconduct within our government, and we will not hesitate to take decisive action where necessary."

Amid the cameras clicking and reporters scribbling notes, Press Secretary Harrin's hand gestured confidently, emphasizing his points. His eyes met the gaze of various journalists, conveying a sense of sincerity and reassurance.

He added, "While we understand the frustration, secession is not a viable solution. The New Republic was formed on the principles of unity and cooperation, and together, we can overcome these challenges. We encourage dialogue and constructive engagement, so that we can work collaboratively toward a stronger and more accountable government."

The press conference continued as reporters asked probing questions, seeking more details about the investigations and the government's plans to address the concerns of its citizens. Press Secretary Harrin answered with a combination of confidence and diplomacy, reaffirming the New Republic's commitment to transparency, accountability, and progress.

As the press conference concluded, Press Secretary Harrin thanked the reporters for their attendance and reiterated the New Republic's dedication to serving the best interests of all citizens.

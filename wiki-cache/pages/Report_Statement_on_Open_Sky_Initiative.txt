Written Statement from [[Caspian Union|Caspian]] [[Presav]] [[Crestian Tarasar]] on the the success of the Open Sky Initiative on [[Selene]]:

"Five years ago saw two key events in galactic politics. Here in the [[Caspian Sector]] a new permanent [[Union Council]] was elected and the work of rebuilding a Union which had been torn asunder under the weight of two genocides began in earnest. Meanwhile outside of our space the [[Galactic Empire]] was replaced by the [[Imperium]] with the Core World of Selene chosen to serve as its capital.

There are a number of worlds the Imperator could have easily chosen to serve as his capital to return to Selene. After the devastation it faced during the War of the Throne, the now Imperator was instrumental in rebuilding the Imperial City and constructing the dome to protect from radiation, the dome that is no longer needed. In choosing to return to Selene, he chose to return to finish the work he had started so many years ago.

Our [[Imperium-Caspian Mutual Protection Pact (29 ABY)|mutual protection pact]] with the Imperium has meant I have had many occasions over these past five years to see the progress made on Selene and it has been truly breathtaking. Not only the environmental work that has just reached a milestone but the transformation of the [[Imperial City (Selene)|Imperial City]] as well. It has developed into a vibrant multicultural hub.

It essentially represents everything that the old Empire was not. A crowning achievement not based in the projection of military might, not based in destructive achievement but a civil works project. A civil works project paired strong social revitalisation. The pockmarked rock of a planet has through the persistence of vision of many has slowly but surely begun to shine like a jewel.

I want to extend both my own personal congratulations and that of the Caspian people to the Imperator, to [[Grand Moff]] [[Elana Tracer|Tracer]], to the [[Ithorian]] scientists who have been the backbone of this project but also to the ordinary citizens of Selene as well. The now breathable air would mean little were it not for the people who have chosen to make Selene their home and who have help it gained its now lustre.

We on [[Caspar]] know how hard to work of rebuilding is and so we salute our fellow travellers who have so brilliantly engaged in the work to make their world better. As for myself I am very much looking forward to seeing these latest results myself and sampling the new invigorating air."

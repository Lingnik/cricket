The holovid is of [[Thurston Unger|Thurston]], wearing the same attire as his report on [[Nar Shaddaa]], though without the jacket. The man is sitting at the controls of a YT-series freighter of some type while the camera is set in front of him, showing him from the chest up.

Darlings, my lovelies, my politically astute little firecrackers - have I got a story for you today! If you thought the Grand Inquisitor's little joyride through [[Hutt Space|Hutt space]] was dramatic, hold on to your sequined capes because we are about to take a sharp turn into the heart of the [[Imperium|Imperium's]] latest very public embarrassment!

Let's talk about [[Moff]] Jenis Octal, formerly the top dog of the [[:starwars:Karthakk system|Karthakk system]], who as of today, is about as relevant as last season's fashion trends. That's right - he's out. Not retired, not reassigned - forcibly removed by none other than [[Axel Vichten]], the Grand Inquisitor himself.

Now, let's take a step back here. Why did this happen? Great question! Do we have an answer? Absolutely not. The Imperium has locked up its lips tighter than a [[Etti IV|Etti]] banker's credit vault, refusing to tell the galaxy why one of their own Moffs was so unceremoniously shown the door.

Oh, but don't worry, kittens, because I have questions.

Is this a Pattern of Power Plays?
This isn't just about Octal. Vichten flew straight from Nar Shaddaa, where he had just flexed his power by snatching a ship right out of Hutt-controlled space. The Bantha Tracks incident alone had everyone on edge, especially when he made it clear that refusing his demand would mean war. And now, before the dust has even settled from that little spectacle, the Inquisitor has booted a sitting Moff with no replacement announced? Coincidence? Please.

What does that mean? For the time being, the governance of the Karthakk system falls to the Inquisition itself. That's right, darlings, the Grand Inquisitor is now the de facto ruler of an entire system, at least until the [[Moff's Council]] scrapes together the nerve to name a successor.

Let's take a moment to appreciate the deliciously awkward silence from the Imperium's official channels. No explanation. No reassurances. No "We wish Moff Octal the best in his future endeavors." Just poof - he's gone, and Karthakk is left dangling in bureaucratic limbo.

What are the Whispers in the Shadows?

Oh, but the rumor mill is churning at hyperspeed! Some whisper corruption, others murmur military failure, and some even suggest the unspeakable, disloyalty to the Imperator himself. But the truth? Still locked away behind Vichten's ever-present, ever-menacing smirk.

So I ask you, my lovelies: Is this just the latest in the Inquisitor's growing list of power grabs? Or is there something deeper, something more dangerous at play?

Because let's be real, when a government starts removing its own people without a word, that's when you know the cracks in the foundation are about to turn into a full collapse. First the [[New Republic|Republic]] with the loss of [[Dreven]] and maybe soon [[Chandrila]], now the Imperium is seeing leaders forcefully removed from office.

We'll keep watching. We'll keep digging. And, as always, my loves, we will keep you on [[The Cutting Edge]].

This is Thurston Unger, signing off, at least until the next scandal drops or we at [[InterGalactic News|IG News]] learn more. Tata for now my loves.

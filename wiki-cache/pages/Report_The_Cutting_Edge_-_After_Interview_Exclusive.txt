[[Thurston Unger|Thurston]] is sitting on the edge of his bed, onboard the [[InterGalactic News|IGN transport]]. A window behind him shows the trails of stars as the vessel is traversing hyperspace. The man looks like he's fresh out of the shower, his hair a wet mess and he's wearing a set of bright red, silk pajamas. He's not his usual energetic self, and there's a look of seriousness on his face as he addresses his audience.

"My fellow beings, I have just returned from a visit to [[Nar Shaddaa]] and I am deeply troubled by what I saw and what I heard while interviewing [[Nasiri Zithreej|Nasiri Gejalli]]. The slave trade is thriving in this lawless region, with beings of all species being bought and sold like commodities. The lack of action against this horrific practice is only driving up the market, and this must be stopped."

"I plead with the[[New Republic]], the [[Corporate Sector Authority]], the [[Caspian Union]], and the [[Imperium]] to put pressure on the [[Hutt|Hutts]] by implementing embargoes on [[Hutt Space]]. The time for inaction is over. It's time for the galaxy to stand up for what is right and put an end to slavery. The beings in Hutt Space deserve better and it is our duty to ensure their freedom."

"I know this won't be an easy task, but I believe that together, we can make a difference. I implore all of you to join me in speaking out against this evil and to put pressure on our leaders to take action. The future of the galaxy depends on it."

"The Hutts and their lieutenants, like Mr. Gejalli, see no wrong in their actions. They truly believe that it will happen regardless of them, so they might as well profit. They create a market, and enterprising criminals are going to do criminal things and victimize innocent people because of it." Thurston pauses, looking straight into the camera. "Thank you for listening. Let's work together to end this archaic practice, once and for all. Remember, my darlings, I love each and every one of you, unless you're a skeevy gutterslug monster like the people I interviewed today."

Broadcast from [[InterGalactic News|IG News]] Studio, [[Etti IV]]

The holofeed opened with sweeping visuals of [[Chandrila]]-rolling green hills beneath slate-gray skies, the once-pristine avenues of [[Hanna City]] now bristling with protest banners and political tension. The feed transitioned smoothly into the gleaming IG News studio on Etti IV, where [[Thurston Unger]]-sharp of tongue and sharper of collar-greeted the galaxy with his signature air of bemused dismay.

Tonight, he wore a dove-grey tunic piped in indigo, paired with a silver-threaded overcloak clasped with a stylized sunburst pin-Chandrilan in origin, though few would dare to wear it now. "My darlings," he began, steepling his fingers, "there are moments in galactic politics when one must pause... and savor the unfolding drama. This, my sweets, is such a moment."

The holoscreen flared to life behind him, displaying a freeze-frame of [[Senator]] Vel Tarnas, Chandrila's senior representative to the [[New Republic Senate]], standing at a podium with her jaw set like [[durasteel]] and a fury one doesn't often see from a Core World aristocrat.

"Earlier today, Senator Tarnas delivered what can only be described as a thunderous ultimatum to the [[New Republic]] High Council-comfortably holed up in their stronghold on [[Ord Mantell]], far from the political theater that once played out on [[Coruscant]]." Thurston gave the camera a knowing look. "Not that the [[Imperium|Imperium's]] current tenants are hosting any less drama in the old palace."

"We will not be dragged down by a government paralyzed by its own cowardice. Chandrila demands action. Restore order, or we reconsider our place at your table." The clip cut to a packed hall in Hanna City, where a mixture of applause and nervous silence followed her remarks. Thurston raised a hand delicately to his chest, as though catching his breath.

"A gasp, a flutter, and suddenly the very soul of the Republic is threatening to pack her bags and call it a day." Behind him, the screen displayed a web of instability: Mon Calamari in protest and Chandrila issuing ultimatums "And one cannot help but notice," Thurston murmured, "that while Ord Mantell's administrators scramble to coordinate crisis responses, there remains a conspicuous lack of leadership. Committees, yes. Subcommittees, naturally. But decisive action? Darling, that's so pre-Clone Wars."

He offered a long, languid sigh, brushing one sleeve smooth. "So here we are. [[Dac|Mon Cala]] is sinking into rebellion. Chandrila is flirting with secession. And the New Republic, still reeling from its own scandals, seems to be asking if it's too early for a lunch break." 

He leaned forward, eyes gleaming. "The real question, my loves, is this: Does anyone actually want peace anymore? Or have we grown so dependent on conflict-so addicted to rebellion, resistance, and rhetoric-that when the guns fall silent, the whole galaxy simply goes mad?"

The holofeed faded to a slow, ominous crawl of headlines:
MON CALAMARI DEFIES PEACEKEEPER MANDATES
ORD MANTELL SENATE DELAYS VOTE-AGAIN
VEL TARNAS: 'THE REPUBLIC MUST CHOOSE ITS FUTURE'

Thurston's voice echoed over the final frame, velvet and sharp:

"Order, chaos, and the ever-spinning wheel between them. I do wonder... which way we're leaning tonight."

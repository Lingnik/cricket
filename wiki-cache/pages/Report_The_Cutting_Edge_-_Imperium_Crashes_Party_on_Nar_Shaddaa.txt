{{IGNpost|author=[[Thurston Unger]]|date=[[34 ABY]]|topic=Inquisitorial Intervention|anchors=[[Thurston Unger]]|headline=The Cutting Edge - Imperium Crashes Party on Nar Shaddaa|synopsis=[[Thurston Unger]] reports on the Imperium's seizure of a ship which recently arrived on [[Nar Shaddaa]] with all its crew having been slain.}}

The intro for [[The Cutting Edge]] fades, revealing [[Thurston Unger]] in a shimmering midnight-blue suit with silver pinstripes, standing in front of a massive screen displaying the hazy, neon-lit skyline of [[Nar Shaddaa]]. He adjusts his cuffs, flashing his signature grin at the camera.

"Oh, my darlings, we have an absolute spectacle to discuss today! And no, it's not another fashion disaster at some Core Worlds gala, though those are always a delight, it's far juicier than that! The [[Imperium]], yes that Imperium, has just waltzed into [[Hutt Space]] like they owned the place and made one hell of a demand."

The screen shifts to shaky holovid footage from Nar Shaddaa's lower levels, showing the ominous silhouette of the [[RSD Retribution|Retribution]], a [[Republic-class Star Destroyer]], hovering over the moon like a great, looming predator. The camera then cuts to a distant view of [[Axel Vichten]], the Grand Inquisitor himself, broadcasting from the bridge of the Retribution in full Imperium regalia, flanked by [[Stormtrooper|stormtroopers]] in their imposing black armor.

"Enter Axel Vichten, the Grand Inquisitor of the Imperium; smuggler, war profiteer, and all-around galactic wildcard. And today's starring role? A man with absolutely no patience for negotiations. What did he want, my loves? A ship. One ship. The Bantha Tracks, a vessel currently in the hands of NSEC, the Nar Shaddaa Security and Enforcement Corps. And when the [[Hutt|Hutts]] and NSEC started getting shifty about it, the Inquisitor made it abundantly clear?hand it over, or the Imperium would take it. By force."

The screen shifts to a close-up of a port official, an aging [[Weequay]], rubbing his temples as he addresses a crowd of reporters, his tone deeply unenthused.

"The Imperium made it clear that failure to comply would be interpreted as an act of war. We're not looking for a war. So? yeah. We turned the ship over."

Thurston presses a manicured hand to his chest, eyes gleaming with amusement. "Oh, how very generous!" he coos. "Just like that, the Hutts and their NSEC lackeys decided that maybe, just maybe, picking a fight with a Star Destroyer was not in their best interests. So, in the blink of an eye, the Bantha Tracks was handed over, no blasterfire, no explosions, just a whole lot of reluctant compliance."

A new clip plays, Axel Vichten himself. His expression is unreadable, his voice clipped and cool.

"The Imperium claims what is ours. This matter is closed."

Thurston sighs dramatically. "My loves, that's it! No explanation. No details. Just 'this is ours now' and an entire criminal empire simply folding under the weight of a well-timed threat. And nobody, not the Hutts, not NSEC, dares ask why this ship was so important."

The camera zooms in on Thurston as he raises a perfectly sculpted brow.

"So, what's so special about the Bantha Tracks? Why did the Grand Inquisitor himself roll into town, personally, to take it? A ship is a ship, but this ship, this ship was worth the risk of making enemies out of the Hutts. And darlings, that means there's a very big secret onboard."

The footage rewinds, playing in slow motion as a squadron of TIE Avengers escort the ship away, the hull of the Bantha Tracks barely visible in the dim glow of Nar Shaddaa's skyline.

Thurston leans in conspiratorially. "And you know that means we are going to find out. Because while the Imperium might be keeping its mouth shut, the galaxy always has ears. And somewhere, someone knows exactly why Axel Vichten came storming into Hutt Space and walked away with a prize he wasn't willing to negotiate for."

He straightens up, flashing his signature dazzling grin.

"So stay tuned, my loves. The truth is out there. And as always, The Cutting Edge will be the ones to cut right to it."

The screen fades to black, leaving only the distant silhouette of the Bantha Tracks vanishing into hangar of the Retribution, easily visible from it's intimidatingly low orbit over Nar Shaddaa, an orbit that is rarely used outside of full ground bombardments.

{{IGNpost|author=[[Thurston Unger]]|date=[[34 ABY]]|topic=Rogue Marines|anchors=[[Thurston Unger]]|headline=The Cutting Edge - Chrome Court Confidential - Temple of Hypocrisy|synopsis=[[Thurston Unger]] points out the hypocrisy of the [[New Republic]] arresting [[Han'bel Eckhart]] for helping people, the job they themselves should be doing.}}

Broadcasting The Mirror Throne, [[Full Frontal Fabulous]]. 
Backlit by furious reds and indigos. The room is pulsing. Every surface gleams. The Mirror Throne is at its tallest setting-currently glowing in righteous indignation. Thurston sits like a storm about to slap.

[[Thurston Unger|Thurston's]] wearing a sheer high-collar synthsilk blouse, shimmering storm-gray with violet accents Hair: platinum-pink, aggressively swept to one side, with holographic streaks flickering like warning sirens. Makeup: battlefield-ready. Expression: a dagger wearing lip gloss.

"Oh no, no, no. We are not doing this quietly."

He leans forward, fingernails flashing like [[Vibro-Blade|vibroblades]]. "You mean to tell me... that the [[New Republic]]-the same bureaucracy that takes nine weeks and three ethics committees to approve a crate of water filters-has somehow found the time to drag a decorated war hero into custody? This is your priority?"

(Holo-footage rolls behind him: [[Colonel]] [[Han'bel Eckhart|Eckhart]] helping evacuate children from collapsed buildings, leading relief drops, firing at pirates.

"You arrested the one man who did your job for you. Not for credits. Not for medals. But because people were dying, and he actually gave a damn. While you-oh, bless your [[transparisteel]] towers and blue carpet press briefings-debated policy implications."

Thurston slams a dataslate to the ground, symbolic obviously. "This is what the New Republic has become: a ceremony of cowardice. A structure so bloated on its own mythology it arrests heroes because they make the council look like what they are. Ineffective. Timid. And frankly, irrelevant."

The Chrome Court enters-Pearletta, Cymbaline-6, and Vel Veela all wearing "#EckhartWasRight" sashes, their couture plating flickering with protest lighting. Tarnish LaRue tosses glitter with theatrical contempt.

"You fear him because he reminds people you've failed them. That when they cried for help, the Templar answered-and not the Republic Navy. That when entire systems were starving, you filed reports, and Eckhart broke the blockade. That he didn't wait for permission because he already had the only thing that mattered: a conscience."

He pauses. Looks directly into the camera. His tone drops to molten steel. "You jailed your conscience, Republic. I hope it fits behind durasteel. And I dare you to try muzzling the rest of us."

Crowd noises and chants begin to echo from the holo-wall. Hashtag overlays rise: #FreeTheTemplar, #RepublicOfRedTape, #YouLockedUpHope.

"Now I'm hearing whispers-oh yes, my sweet sleuths in the back corridors-saying this was about 'chain of command.' That 'no one is above the law.' Really? Tell me, darlings, where was that law when the Senate refused to send aid to the Outer Rim for the third cycle in a row?"

He waves one hand dramatically-cut to holo-feed of abandoned settlements and starving colonists."You don't get to claim moral high ground when you can't even reach the low one. You left people to die. And when someone wouldn't let that stand-you made him the criminal."

He stands now. The Mirror Throne slides behind him like a ghostly cathedral. He holds up a can of Felony Fizz from [[Crimson Nerf Energy|Crimson Nerf]] Dark. "You want to play empire in democracy's clothing? Fine. But don't cry when the capes get ripped off and the cameras are still rolling."

Thurston steps forward. The camera closes in. "Colonel Eckhart didn't go rogue. He went righteous. You've made him a martyr. And I promise you-martyrs get louder after the cuffs click."

(Fade to a grainy holovid of Eckhart waving from the [[DSV Templar|Templar's]] ramp. The crowd around him cheering. Someone shouts: "He saved my village!")

Thurston whispers, like venom poured into velvet. "This isn't justice, New Republic. This is your confession."

[[The Cutting Edge]] newsroom pulsed with the usual energy, the lights gleaming, the holo-screens glowing, and at the center of it all, [[Thurston Unger]] stood, resplendent in a shimmering violet suit with silver trim, an ensemble chosen, no doubt, to highlight his absolute displeasure at tonight's topic. He smoothed his cuffs, then leaned toward the camera with a slow, exaggerated exhale, as if he had just finished the longest, most grating conversation of his life.

"My darlings, my lovelies, my most cherished audience, do you know what I despise more than bad lighting and cheap champagne? A snub." He flicked a hand through the air, as if swatting away the very idea. "And oh, do we have a big one tonight. You see, I-Thurston Unger, your beacon of truth, your intergalactic arbiter of fabulousness-have been denied access to [[Imperium]] press briefings."

He let the words hang in the air before dramatically gasping. "I know! How dare they?!"

The holoscreen behind him flickered to life, showing a still image of the woman responsible-Zephara Val'Riin, the new mouthpiece of the Imperium, draped in midnight and violet, her metallic-sheen eyes as cold as deep space.

"And this, my sweets, is the culprit. The Grand Inquisitor's new pet wordsmith. The Ice Queen of the Imperium herself." Thurston crossed his arms, tilting his head slightly as he studied the image, his lips pursing. "Now, I have faced my share of smooth operators-politicians, crime lords, celebrity chefs-but this one? Oh no, this one is different. She does not merely speak-she rewrites reality."

A dramatic pause, then a grin sharp enough to slice [[Durasteel|durasteel]]. "And guess who has been scrubbed from that reality?" He pointed at himself. "That's right. Moi."

The screen changed to a scrolling list of approved media outlets for Imperium press briefings. [[InterGalactic News|IGN-Politics]], [[New Republic|Republic]] NewsNet, a few select corporate channels-his name, however, was nowhere to be found. "Now, my darlings, we all know I am very popular with certain people in power. Some love me, some loathe me, but they all know better than to pretend I don't exist." He sighed, rolling his eyes. "And yet, the Imperium's newest truth-weaver saw my name and said, 'Oh no, no, we can't have him asking real questions.'"

The screen shifted again, this time showing grainy records of Zephara's past-or rather, the lack of them. "Who is she, you ask? Well, I would love to tell you, but oh, would you look at that-there's practically nothing. Just a name. A birthplace. Caspar, of all places. And a very strong tie to the city of [[Noques Rasala]]. A place the Grand Inquisitor is rumored to call home when he's not terrorizing the galaxy."

The holoscreen shifted to an image of the ancient city, its timeless structures standing in defiant contrast to the modern galaxy. "Now, Noques Ras=la, for those of you unfamiliar, is a place where history is preserved, where tradition is guarded, and where secrets, my sweets, are kept." He tapped a manicured finger against the desk. "She does not come from a line of politicians. She comes from a long, long line of narrative architects-the kind of people who shape stories, who decide what is forgotten and what is remembered."

His lips curled into a smirk, but his eyes held a sharper edge. "So, my darlings, do you really think it's a coincidence that this is the woman standing beside [[Axel Vichten]]? A man who thrives in the shadows, who has rewritten his own past more times than I can count? Please."

He gestured broadly as the image of Zephara returned to the screen. "She's not a press secretary, my loves. She's a mythmaker. And what's the first thing you do when you start crafting a new myth? You erase the inconvenient parts of the old one." He placed a hand on his chest. "Namely, me."

The smirk deepened, his voice dropping into a stage whisper. "So, the next time you hear her silky voice declaring the official truth of the Imperium, ask yourself this-what is she not telling you?" With a flick of his wrist, the screen behind him faded to black. "And I, for one, cannot wait to find out."

The broadcast cut, and the galaxy was left wondering.

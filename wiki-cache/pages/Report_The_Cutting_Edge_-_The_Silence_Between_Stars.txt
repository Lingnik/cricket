{{IGNpost|author=[[Thurston Unger]]|date=[[34 ABY]]|topic=Prison Break|anchors=[[Thurston Unger]]|headline=The Cutting Edge - The Silence Between Stars|synopsis=Having been denied an interview with [[Axel Vichten|Axel Vichten's]] Press Secretary, [[Thurston Unger]] vamps on the state of the Imperium in relation to the prison break on [[The Oubliette|Spacestation 1138.]]}}

The holofeed snapped into focus-not from the polished floors of [[Selene|Selene's]] capital, nor the usual high-gloss studio lighting [[Thurston Unger|Thurston Unger's]] viewers had grown to adore, but from the gently humming cockpit of the General Lesuvius, a [[YT-2400|YT-2400 freighter]] drifting silently through the Mid-Rim. Beyond the [[Transparisteel|transparisteel]] canopy, the stars hung motionless, cold and glittering-silent witnesses to the unfolding storm.

Thurston Unger, ever the vision of star-bound elegance, lounged in the pilot's chair with the casual grace of a man who knew he was being watched. His flightsuit was a custom number-iridescent pearl with dusk-black lapels, and a high collar framed in indigo synth-fur that shimmered faintly with motion. Not a hair out of place. Not a strand unconsidered.

He adjusted a toggle with idle precision, then turned to the holocam mounted on the control cluster, his tone airy and perfectly calculated. "My darlings, I had hoped today's transmission would be one of light. I was scheduled for an interview with the Grand Inquisitor's new pet oracle-what's her name? Zephara? Sounds like a perfume. But alas, it seems someone misplaced my press badge. Again."

There's a brief, cutting pause. The smile didn't reach his eyes. "So instead, I find myself in a far more intimate setting. A drifting ship. A drifting system. And, perhaps, a drifting empire."

The feed shifted-archive footage, overlaid with stark red headers. A corridor within a forgotten Imperial outpost-dark, scorched, riddled with blaster scars. Smoke still curling from shattered panels. A static-bleeding Imperial emblem flickering on the wall. In the corner, the crumpled remains of a peacekeeper droid sparked intermittently. "Yes, my sweets. It's official. The [[Imperium]] has confirmed the catastrophic failure of one of its secret installations in the [[:starwars:Karthakk system|Karthakk system]]-an old [[The Oubliette|ghost station]], repurposed as a detention site for... inconvenient persons. How quaint." 

The screen flashed the names and dossiers already made public. Political dissidents. War-era insurgents. And at the center of it all-a name etched into the very bones of galactic history. "[[Lorn Rhys]]," Thurston said, softly now, almost reverently. "Once [[Galactic Emperor|Emperor]]. Once thought dead. Now fugitive."

The feed returned to the cockpit, Thurston's fingers steepled beneath his chin. The faint thrum of the General Lesuvius's sublight drive purred beneath the moment. "And here's where it gets deliciously murky. The Imperium has refused to release the full number of escapees. No footage. No manifest. Just... silence. A silence so heavy, even Selene's streets are whispering."

He leaned in slightly, the glow of the control panels painting soft shadows across his cheekbones. "My sources-and you know I have them-tell me this wasn't just a prison break. This was a message. Coordinated. Surgical. Someone wanted those prisoners out. And someone wanted the Imperium to bleed."

Another screen lit behind him, displaying a stylized schematic of the Karthakk Drift, with red markers blinking across known hyperspace corridors. "Already, there are rumors of alliances reforming. Old cells reactivating. Even whispers of the Phantom herself, Jessa Trel, recaptured in a sting operation barely hours after setting foot on [[O'paal]]. A little sloppy for a legend, don't you think? Unless..." He smiled slowly. "...she wanted to be caught."

A low warning tone chimed. Thurston casually flicked it off, not breaking eye contact with the audience. "My dears, the Imperium would love for you to believe they have this under control. That the Grand Inquisitor's new mouthpiece can contain the narrative with dramatic silences and clever gowns. But let's not be naive. If they can't even keep their former Emperor locked down, how long before the rest of the galaxy starts wondering-who's really in control?"

The cockpit lights dimmed slightly as the General Lesuvius began to align for a new hyperspace jump. Outside, a looming nebula swallowed the stars in violet mist. "I'm Thurston Unger, and I will keep asking the questions, even if I have to ask them from the edges of civilized space. Because no matter how far the Imperium runs from its truths. I'll be waiting on the Cutting Edge."

The screen faded to black, the last thing seen: a flash of light as the General Lesuvius jumped into hyperspace.

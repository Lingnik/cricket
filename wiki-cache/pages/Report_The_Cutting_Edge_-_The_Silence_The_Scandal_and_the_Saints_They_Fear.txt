{{IGNpost|author=[[Thurston Unger]]|date=[[34 ABY]]|topic=Rogue Marines|anchors=[[Thurston Unger]]|headline=The Cutting Edge - The Silence, The Scandal, and the Saints They Fear|synopsis=[[Thurston Unger]] interviews a [[New Republic]] whistleblower with details of the Republic's attempts to rewrite the history of the actions of [[Han'bel Eckhart]] and the crew of the [[DSV Templar|Templar]].}}

The holo fades in on the Parlor of Probing-[[Thurston Unger|Thurston Unger's]] gilded interview suite aboard the [[Full Frontal Fabulous]]. The set glitters in warm velvet hues, holo-candelabras flicker with soft pink light, and across the gently curving glass wall behind the host, classified Republic memos drift by like damning confetti. Redacted words flash in pulsing crimson as dates and names scroll openly.

Thurston is seated, hair in a luminous fuchsia swoop tonight, lips just this side of scandalously glossy. "Good evening, galaxy. And darling stars above, what a day it's been. While most press outfits are still tripping over their credits to parse the [[New Republic|Republic's]] belated admission about the capture of the so-called '[[DSV Templar|Templar Terrorists]],' we here at [[The Cutting Edge]]-ahem-have never bought the official version. And tonight, we have proof."

"My guest is Mira Lirien. Until three days ago, she was a junior aide to [[Ord Mantell|Ord Mantell's]] senior [[senator]], a member of the Republic's Inner Security Subcommittee. A soft-spoken clerk in a sensible skirt. But then... she opened a file. And everything changed."

The camera pans to Mira. She's early 30s, brown hair in a neat bun, wearing modest professional attire. Her expression is composed, but her eyes carry weight. She is illuminated softly, a deliberate contrast to Thurston's glitz.

"Mira. Welcome. Or... should I say, welcome to the war the Republic tried to bury."

Mira, voice steady, but aching, responds. "Thank you, Thurston. I honestly never thought I'd be here. I just... I can't sit back and let them erase what really happened."

"For those just joining us-where have you been, my scandal-thirsty loves?-Mira is the whistleblower behind this trove of leaked Republic strategy documents now sashaying behind us. Documents that lay bare how the [[New Republic Senate|Senate]] attempted to recast the actions of [[Colonel]] [[Han'bel Eckhart]] and the crew of the Templar from heroic to hostile. Tell us... what did you see?"

The camera shifts slightly as Mira takes a breath. "I worked on internal memos, comm scheduling, briefings-standard assistant work. But last week, documents started surfacing on secure Senate channels. I was supposed to redact and refile them. But they weren't about committee votes or budget amendments. They were coordination messages... spin instructions. Talking points."

She leans forward, hands folded. "They already had Eckhart. The arrest happened weeks ago. But we weren't told. Not even the public-facing offices. When I saw the timestamp-seven days old-and realized it hadn't been released yet... I knew something was wrong."

Thurston responds quietly, "And then the real dagger."

"Yes. The documents show the Republic Intelligence Service recommended delaying public disclosure of the arrest until they had a 'unified narrative'-that's a quote. One that would connect Eckhart's crew to 'anti-state separatist sentiment' on [[Bothawui]], [[Dac|Mon Cala]], and [[Chandrila]]. They wanted to bundle the unrest. To make him the face of the problem-not the hero that thousands, maybe millions, already saw him as."

The scrolling holowall behind them highlights sections: "Narrative cohesion is essential" and "Leak risk minimal-monitor freelance press agents."

Thurston scoffs with mock alarm, "Oh, freelance press agents. Meaning, moi."

"I was disgusted. And ashamed. I've worked for that Senator for four years. My family... they owe their lives to Eckhart and his crew. Nearly a year ago, they were trapped in a labor camp on Deshurr. The Templar showed up-not for credits, not for politics-but because they couldn't not help. They got them out. My parents. My brother."

Thurston reaches out and gently touches her hand, the camera lingering for just a beat.

"So when I saw their names on the list of those captured... and the notes about how to manage Eckhart's health status to 'mitigate martyrdom risk'-I knew I couldn't stay silent."

Behind them, a document illuminates with the chilling note: "Col. Eckhart gravely wounded-condition stable but compromised. Optics of public trial under review. Alternative outcomes being modeled."

Thurston, eyes gleaming with fury, "So let's unpack that-because it's as subtle as a [[Hutt]] in a corset. Colonel Eckhart refused to fight. Refused to let his crew draw arms against the very Republic they once served. And how is that rewarded? He's gunned down during a secret arrest, his people scattered, and his name dragged through a PR sewage tunnel."

"And they won't even release the names of the others captured. Or who escaped."

"Of course they won't. Because if the public knew? The protests outside Republic embassies would look like garden parties."

The feed briefly cuts to footage of protests on Mon Cala and Ord Mantell. Chants echo: "Free the Templar," "We Are Not Their Pawns," and "Eckhart Saved Us."

Thurston, back to camera, "And to the Republic brass watching this right now-yes, I see you, Director Vallane, clutching your pearls-you may control the official record, but you do not control the truth."

He stands slowly, hoisting a can of Glam Shock-subtle product placement-and raises it to Mira. "To Mira Lirien. Citizen. Daughter. Truth-teller. And to Colonel Eckhart-you deserved a medal, not a muzzle."

Studio lights pulse as applause rises from the Chrome Court, all twelve lined up behind the cameras, fashionably silent but undeniably fabulous.

The camera goes in on a closeup of Thurston as he gives his final words. "And to the rest of you across the stars, I say this: if the truth scares your government, perhaps it's not you who should be afraid."

The feed cuts out in a shimmer of static, replaced by a brief [[InterGalactic News|IGNews]] bumper.

The interview between [[Colonel]] [[Han'bel Eckhart|Eckhart]] and [[Thurston Unger]] aboard the stolen [[New Republic|Republic]] ship, [[DSV Templar|The Templar]], was a study in contrasts. Eckhart, a grizzled [[New Republic Marine Corps|marine]] in his starched uniform and military grooming, sat in the captain's chair while Thurston, a flamboyant journalist in a brightly colored, tailored suit and flamboyant hairstyle, stood in front of him with a microphone in hand.

Thurston began the interview with his usual enthusiasm. "Colonel Eckhart, darling, it is such an honor to finally meet you! You and your crew are truly a sight to behold."

Colonel Eckhart gave Thurston a skeptical glance, responding gruffly, "Likewise."

Thurston's smile faltered slightly as he took in the Colonel's rough appearance and demeanor, but he quickly recovered. "So, Colonel, tell me, what led you and your platoon of marines and pilots to steal this Republic ship and go rogue? The Republic is accusing you all of being a threat."

Colonel Eckhart took a sip of his kaf and chewed on his cigar thoughtfully before speaking. "We were tired of following orders from politicians who had no idea what was really going on out here in the galaxy. We saw innocent lives being put in danger and we knew we had to do something to protect them. Our goal is simple, to protect and defend the innocent, no matter the cost. We are not a threat, the real threat is the inaction of a potentially corrupt government and too much bureaucracy keeping soldiers from doing their job."

Thurston nodded, his smile back in place. "I see, and how has the public been receiving your actions? Are they seeing you all as heroes or outlaws?"

Colonel Eckhart responded, "Some understand and support us, others see us as a threat. But our main focus is to protect those who cannot protect themselves."

Thurston: "And what about the future? What's next for the crew of the Templar?"

Colonel Eckhart took a long drag on his cigar and sat back in his chair. "We'll keep doing what we're doing. Fighting for the protection of the innocent, no matter the cost. And anyone who gets in the way of that, had better watch out."

Thurston thanked the Colonel for his time, and the interview ended with Thurston looking impressed and Colonel Eckhart looking determined, as the Templar flew off into the unknown reaches of space. This interview gave a clear understanding of the motivations, attitude and beliefs of the crew of the Templar and Colonel Eckhart, and their noble goal of protecting the innocent, despite the accusations of the Republic.

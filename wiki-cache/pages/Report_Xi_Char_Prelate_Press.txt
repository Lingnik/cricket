== The Prelate is Dead ==

A commercial for [[Corellia|Corellian]] Leather Sofas is abruptly interrupted as the featured pitchman is mid sentence.

The screen goes black for a moment before being replaced by live footage of the Xi Char [[Cathedral Factory]] in Xi Chall. Outside of the cathedral factory are throngs of [[Xi Charrian|Xi Charrians]], though it appears a few members of other species are present as well. This footage stays on the screen without caption or commentary for roughly a minute.

The camera then zooms out to show a news studio with the footage from the cathedral factory in the background. At the anchor's desk if the familiar face of the [[Firrerreo]] anchor Professor [[Brackett Benduday]]. The anchors normal self introduction is entirely omitted, "We are interrupting your normal broadcast with breaking news out of [[Charros IV]]. [[Prelate]] [[r'ratak-varattak-n'gavanak]] of the [[Xi Char]] has died." The Xi Charrian name is spoken with surprising ease by the anchor woman.

An image of an older looking Xi Charrian with some white markings on his carapace is shown in the corner of the screen.

"The Prelate who had recently return from [[Caspar]] died this morning according to Associate Dean kavak-'sa'hak-'arrallak." This name is pronounced more laboriously. "No cause of death has been given and owing to the taboo with which physical imperfections are held by the Xi Char, it is not expected that any additional details on the manner of his passing to be given.

Confidential sources, however, indicate that his passing was not unexpected. King Erskine Braemar, the constitutional monarch of the [[Krittain|Krittain System]], and the prelate's colleague in the [[Union Council]] had recently paid a visit to Xi Chall and has already release a statement that reads in part that":

"The Prelate will be remembered for his empathy, his promotion of inter-faith dialogue, and his service to the Union. His work touched lives throughout the Union and I will remember our many conversations over the years with great affection. My heart felt condolences and sympathy go out to the Xi Charrians."

"There have also been rumours that the Prelate had recently met with a member of the [[New Republic]] [[New Republic Senate|Senate]], but we have not been able to find anyone who would confirm that meeting at this time. We will have more on this story as it develops."

== The Council Reacts ==

The familiar visage of Brackett Benduday behind the an [[InterGalactic News|IGN ]]anchor's desk appears on the screen. "This is Brackett Benduday with the latest news concerning the passing yesterday morning of Xi Char Prelate r'ratak-varattak-n'gavanak."

An image of a building of Xi Charrian design is shown on the corner of the screen, "Members of the Caspian Union Council along with several leading figures in faith and industry attended a private ceremony marking the passing of the Prelate at the Xi Char chapel factory in [[Anchorron]] on Caspar. According to a short press release the ceremony was officiated by [[Dean]] [['radathak-davasak-dannarak]] of Xi Dan.

The Councillors reconvened at Union Hall in [[Plaxton]] later in the day." The screen shifts to a short clip of [[Presav]] [[Crestian Tarasar]] addressing the chamber. In the location where the Prelate who normally stand rests a large wreath consisting of ring of pula tree leaves forged out of orichalc.

The screen then returns back to the studio where the anchor woman adds, "Presav Tarasar put forth a motion to declare a Union wide week of mourning in honour of the Prelate which passed the chamber unanimously. The Council then went into recess to allow its members to travel to Charros IV for the upcoming state funeral there. For more details we bring you to [[Jordaan Mag]] who is coming to us live from Union Hall." The screen then splits to show the studio on the left side of the screen with the [[:starwars:Arkanian Offshoot/Legends|Arkanian-Offshoot]] reporter on the right.

"Jordaan I understand you have some information on the ceremony held earlier in the day?" The reporter nods at the camera and relates, "Yes Brackett and it has to do with the wreath you just saw in the clip. It was designed by Dean 'radathak-davasak-dannarak. It is comprised of 38 pieces of orichalc fashioned in the shape of pula leaves, a tropical tree native here on Caspar. The number corresponds to number of members of the Union Council after the Prelate's passing. Each one was individually forged this morning by the Councillors at the Xi Cas chapel factory.

It should be noted that Dean 'radathak-davasak-dannarak was the principle designer behind [[Haor Chall Engineering|Haor Chall Engineering's]] [[Vector-Class Starfighter|Vector]] and [[Apex-Class Starfighter|Apex]] Class Starfighters whose shape she based on the Union's national symbol. So the choice of the pula tree leaf motif is another example of synthesis between Caspian imagery and Xi Charrian industry that has become central to her work. This can be seen as part of the wider ecumenical outreach that the Xi Char have engaged in under the leadership of Prelate r'ratak-varattak-n'gavanak."

The anchor woman nods as she listens to the reporter and then asks, "And what can you tell us about the general mood there. Clearly the Crest is taking the loss of one of their own quite seriously, but is the loss resonating with the general populace, most of who probably were unfamiliar with Xi Charrians a few short years ago?"

The reporter responds, "Well of the members of the Xi Charrian delegation the Prelate was the one least often seen in Plaxton in person. Of the people have talked to here it has largely been the senior figures in the government, defence establishment, and industrial community who have anything opinion about him personally. What we have heard though from the general populace is a great deal of genuine sympathy for the Xi Charrian people.

In the past five years the Xi Charrians have become truly integrated into every aspect of society in the Union. The Prelate's colleague Dean ak routinely tops polls as the most popular politician in the Union. There is also a great deal of religious diversity within the Union that predates the admission of Charros IV. So even the ordinary citizens who may not have known much about the Prelate himself seem to recognise and respect the importance of the role he played for the Xi Char."

"One last question Jordaan, what do we know about the upcoming funeral services on Charros IV?" The Arkanian-Offshoot responds, "Very little at this point Brackett. We have heard that the [[CMS Renaissance of Innovation]] is being recalled from its current assignment to host members of the Union Council in recognition of the fact it served as flagship for the [[Charros Wargames|Wargames]] at Charros IV two years ago. Given the Prelate's role as a religious leader, political leader, and business leader, the guest list for the ceremony seems quite diverse."

The anchor nods again as the reporter speaks. "Thank you for that report." The report nods back at the anchor before the screen reverts to just showing the studio. "That was Jordaan Mag live from Caspar. We will be back tomorrow with more on this, including some studio guests to talk about what we can expect in the process to replace the Prelate."

== What to Expect ==

Professor Brackett Benduday sits behind the IGN anchor's desk along with three guests. To her left is a [[Near Human]] male, to his left a [[Human]] male, and to her right a [[Pantoran]] woman. "This is Brackett Benduday with more coverage on the Xi Char. With the passing on Primeday morning of Prelate r'ratak-varattak-n'gavanak we turn to the question of what is next for leader ship of the Xi Charrian faith. Tonight with me I have three guests who will help illuminate what we can expect. On my left is Professor [[Sadim Gnik]] of the University of Feyenoor, among other roles. To his left Professor [[Clark Rickart]], Director of the Plaxton Polling Institute. To my right Caspian Minister of Culture and Councillor for [[Noques Rasala]] [[Andra Eleria]]. That you all for coming on the programme.

Professor Gnik let me start with you. The Xi Charrians are, suffice it to say, fairly different from most sentient species in the galaxy. What do we need to know about them to understand their succession process?" The man to her left responds, "First of all thank you for having me on the programme. It is a real honour to be asked on. The short answer is that what you need to know is that there is a lot that we don't know.

Prior to the efforts that ultimately brought Charros IV into the Union, most of what the galaxy knew about the Xi Charrians dated back to the Clone Wars. As a species they were described as being 'hive-minded.' Now that can mean a variety of different things, but it tends to evoke an image, rightly or wrongly, of species with limited individuality. Of course as Xi Charrians have become part of our society the image we get of their behaviour does not seem to even match that. Even among the clergy who have represented their species in the Union Council, they have identified under three different political parties.

If we digger a little deeper into a what a hive-mind really means though it is a matter of communication. Supposedly a collective consciousness guiding a species, but the methodology is often ill understood. Telepathy, pheromones, even physical touch a ways certain species are supposed to achieve this collective consciousness. And when it comes to Xi Charrians there is a lot we really don't truly understand about their communication.

In the past they rarely interacted with outsiders so there was not a great body of research or data on the subject. Of course now their level of outside interaction has changed drastically, but virtually all their communication with the rest of us is being done through translation [[Droid|droids]] of their own design. Speaking as a linguist myself their native language is almost impossible for most species to understand. Even if you were to learn a few words you would have great difficulty in listening to someone speak an picking them out of a sentence.

As far as what we can understand in our observations of Xi Charrians and what they have shared to us, it would seem that among themselves they are able to communicate through pheromones in addition to their spoken language. And we do see this layering of communication evidence in the right circumstances. Say you are at a party with some Xi Charrians present, conversationally beyond the fact that you will be dealing with a translation droid it is exactly the same as talking to anyone else. But say it is an occasion where coordination is called for, a dance performance, a sporting match, a building is collapsed and heavy debris needs to be removed....a group of Xi Charrians will act with great precision and the more of them there are the more it will appear that they are all on the same wave length.

This mindset can also be seen in their use of droids. Now their physical structure is one reason they tend to make great use of droids, they can engage in precision work that Xi Charrians lack the hands for. But the ability of a group of [[Mark IV Vulture-Class Droid Starfighter|Vulture Droids]] to act with a collective consciousness is not something I think is coincidental. It is an extension of the hive mentality. Now whether they are truly hive-minded, that might be an exaggeration. But however you classify it, they do work with a certain harmony. So the election of a new Prelate will not be one in the way we are use to thinking of it. It will not be how gets the most votes, through some form of collective communication and thought, the new Prelate will be unanimously chosen by the electors."

When Sadim is done speaking, the anchor responds, "Thank you Professor for that analysis. I want to bring this now to Professor Rickart. Sadim got us started in understanding who the result will be achieved but what more can you tell us about the process? Who will be voting? Who is a candidate? And how will this connect to the vacancy the Prelate's death created in the Union Council?"

The man on the end of the anchor's desk replies, "The process by which a new Prelate is elected is referred to as a [[conclave]]. At some point after the funeral rituals are all completed, all the senior clergy of the Xi Char will hole themselves up in the Xi Chall cathedral factory to discuss what is needed in a new Prelate, what characteristics, talents, values, and so on.

Technically any member of the faith is a candidate for Prelate, but in practical terms the senior members of the clergy that are participating in the conclave are really under consideration. Given the nature of the species though it is not likely an election as we normally understand it, likely no ballots or recorded votes a such. Nor a case where there is a certain threshold of votes is needed to secure an elected. Through some manner of collective thought and communication the final decision can be expected to be unanimous. This is one reason why it is all but guaranteed to be a senior member of the clergy, anyone else would not be a well enough known individual to emerge as a universal choice.

Now we don't know how long this process will take, historically the process apparently can go on for days or even weeks. From the start of this process the twelve spires of the cathedral factory will release streams of black smoke. As consensus begins to be achieved and they start coalescing around particular candidates the spires will begin switching to white smoke, and when unanimity is achieved all of the spires will be releasing white smoke. Very shortly after that the new Prelate will be publicly proclaimed an installation ceremony scheduled. If it looks as if the process is likely to go quickly some of the dignitaries gathering for the funeral may simply stay on world during the conclave to be around for the installation ceremony.

Xi Chall essentially operates as the capital of Charros IV, but part of the hive culture of the Xi Charrians is manifested in a degree of flexibility as to such details. If one of the Deans of the other cathedral factories is elected as Prelate it is expected they will likely keep that cathedral factory as the seat of their prelacy, particular should Dean ak be elected as Xi Can is essentially as prominent. Should this be the case a new Dean will be chosen for Xi Chall and they are likely to be the candidate to stand in a by-election to represent Xi Chall in the Union Council. If another candidate who is not currently a Dean is chosen as Prelate, they will be based out of Xi Chall and again will likely be the candidate for the Union Council.

It is not a complete given that a new Prelate or Dean will run for the seat. They could in theory choose to delegate that role to another, but it is unlikely. In the Caspian Union power is quite concentrated within the Union Council which serves as both the executive and legislative branch of government. The Xi Charrians to this point have been represented by their most senior leaders, and this is true on other worlds, both the Krittish King and Krittish President are Councillors, as are the two most senior [[Ixin|Ix]] Princes. In most of these cases though they are not serving as ministers, so the new Councillor of Xi Chall will likely not serve in a senior role within the Union Council. We can also expect them to attend more sessions remotely versus in person, particularly when taking on a new religious office.

Unlike the conclave, the election for Union Council will occur just exactly as it does in every other jurisdiction. Given the hive mentality, however, who ever emerges whether new Prelate, new Dean, or designee can count on virtually all the votes of the Xi Charrians and no competition. Three percent of the population of Charros IV are other species, mostly all Humans, they do get a vote, but they are too few in number to truly impact the results. Given the election is likely to be a formality it will not be scheduled until after the conclave and will be scheduled to take place as soon after the conclave as is practicable to allow the new Councillor to take their seat promptly."

When Clark is done speaking the anchor quips, "Well I suppose there won't be a lot of polling work to be done on this one. We will just have to wait for the smoke to clear. Finally I want to turn this over to Minister Eleria. We had hope to avoid calling up Councillors for commentary as this relates to colleagues of yours, but gosh darn it, it is tough to find someone else who would have even half the insight into this matter. What can you tell us about the top candidates for Prelate? And what factors do you think will be under consideration in the choice?"

The Pantoran woman chuckles a bit, "Well I will do my best to remain objective, at the very least I don't think there is any worry that my responses will sway the election any. In order to understand any of this it is important to understand the massive changes that Xi Charrian society has undergone in the past 80 years.

80 years ago Raith Sienar posed as a convert to the Xi Char faith as part of a plan to steal information. This was exposed and the Xi Char swore off allowing any Human apprentices for fear of repeat performance. Then during the Clone Wars the Xi Char were aligned with the Confederacy which resulted in the Republic sending in Clone Troopers to destroy factories, which as we know are the houses of worships for the Xi Char. To add insult to injury what was left of Haor Chall Engineering after the war was lost was taken over by Sienar Fleet Systems of all companies. In short the Xi Char were at their lowest, defeated, effectively under the jackboot of a colonial oppressive, and with every reason to hate outsiders.

Fast forward to a few years ago, a new government on Caspar, a new government on [[Selene]], new leader at [[Kuat Drive Yards-Sienar Fleet Systems]]. And that all led to negotiations with the Xi Char which ultimately saw Haor Chall Engineering repatriated and Charros IV becoming part of the Caspian Union. 80 years ago the Xi Char were despising Humans, now they are increasingly integrated as equals in a Union with Humans and Sarians and a multitude of other beings. 

So I think the biggest question that will be before the conclave is whether this course is indeed the one they wish to continue on or whether a course correction of some form is necessary. And whatever the answer to that question is will inform the selection of a new Prelate. What make this more intriguing is the fact that Prelate r'ratak-varattak-n'gavanak himself presided over an extremely progressive era for the Xi Char, but was not necessarily such a progressive figure himself. The current state of affairs has a had a lot of practical value to the Xi Char which has done much to restore what they had lost since the Clone Wars, but he himself was not at the forefront all these changes. 

I think of it a bit as one would imagine a family. He was not the father of a young children who he had to keep a close eye on and make all the decisions for. Rather he was there to provide guidance to more mature children and give them space to make their way in the world. These changes to Xi Charrian society would not have happened without his blessing, and he lent a dignified air of continuity to it all. He truly was a remarkable man who will be deeply missed, but change on Charros IV much as it is in other places was driven by those younger.

Should the conclave feel their society has been heading in the right direction, the obviously candidate to succeed as the Prelate is Dean [[ak]]. r'ratak-varattak-n'gavanak was the one who signed the treaties, but ak is the one who negotiated for the Xi Char, he is already the Executive Vice President at Haor Chall Engineering, a junior minister in the Union Council, and seemingly a universally loved figure. But, of course, that is how we are looking at him as outsiders to their society. When Charros IV was still under the heel of [[Sienar Fleet Systems]], ak was one of the few Xi Charrians who worked to befriend the Humans they were stuck dealing with. That put him in the position to help restore local control as he understood outsiders well but to older generations that level of comfort with Humans could be considered just short of heresy. So there may be some backlash that prevents him emerging as a consensus candidate.

Then next most likely candidate would be Dean 'radathak-davasak-dannarak of Xi Dan. She is the chief designer of Vector and Apex class starfighters and having just officiated a ceremony on Caspar for the Prelate's passing she has definitely demonstrated her credentials in representing the Xi Char faith in the context of its place in wider Caspian society. Like with ak for her to be elected the conclave would have to voice approval for the current trajectory Xi Charrian society is on. As a less flashy figure, however, she might be better able to win over more conservative clerics. My sense is that her design work may be considered more theologically sound than ak. They both represent a great deal of ecumenical outreach, but as the old Xi Char saying goes, "the deity is in the details," and I think some may feel ak is looser with the details.

In some ways all the top Deans are heterodox choices, where as ak and 'radathak are progressive, Dean [[cadracak-'r'shak]] of Xi Xin would be rather revolutionary. He is effectively a pacifist. Xi Can, Xi Chall and Xi Dan are all producing war materiels Xi Xin is not. While I get no sense that he is preaching against the industry, the course he would chart as Prelate could be radically different. That doesn't mean he doesn't have anything to offer the faith. While we have talked a lot about the impact the Xi Charrians have made on the rest of us, he is someone who has focused on bring elements of outside culture to Charros IV so Xi Charrians can learn from others. I wouldn't complete rule him out as a candidate, but his supporters would have to do much to convince the conclave.

If the conclave feels things are on the wrong track or at least risk heading that way, they may choose a more conservative candidate. Charros IV is a planet of 3.6 billion, there no lack of Associate Deans and Assistant Deans at the cathedral factories. And who knows how many chapel factories, they can now be found on few other Union worlds as well. There are no dearth of Xi Charrian clergy that could be chosen.

That being said should the conclave choose someone more conservative it is likely not someone we know much about on the outside. If there is some figure in the Xi Char preaching against the horrors of ecumenicalism we aren't hearing about. And any figure who were feels Xi Charrian culture is getting diluted, would, by their nature, be less likely to interact with us and be known. So as you said Brackett, we will have to wait to see the smoke clear."

The anchor smirks, "Well it looks like we do not have all the answers tonight, but we have all the answers we have time for. I want to thank my guests Professor Gnik, Professor Rickart, Minister Eleria. I will see you all next on a special edition of [[Muun Money Minute]] where we will discuss what effect this what this all means for Haor Chall Engineering."

== Daring ==

In orbit of Charros IV ten similar, though not identical, battlecruisers are seeing moving into a formation. The complete formation appears more likely ceremonial than tactical in nature. The screen then shifts to an IGN news studio with the image over the battlecruisers in the background. A Firrerreo anchorwoman sits behind an anchor's desk along with a Human woman. "This is Brackett Benduday coming to you live from Xi Chall on Charros IV and with me in the studio is Caspian Vice Presav [[Cas Mos]]. As guests continue to arrive to mark the passing of Xi Char Prelate r'ratak-varattak-n'gavanak, one of the first pieces of pageantry is now taking place with the [[Caspian Navy]] deploying their full compliment of [[Daring-class Battlecruiser|Daring-Class Battlecruisers]] as an honour guard in orbit here. Let's jump right in, Vice Presav Mos, what is the significance of these specific ships?"

The Vice Presav smiles, "Well there are no capital ships being produced here in the Charros System, but the Daring-Class is the next best thing. One of our government's key policies since taking office has been the recommissioning and renovating of all available warships. The Daring-Class date back to the early days of the CDU, but of the ten surviving vessels of the class, only [[CMS Daring]] was still in service by the time we took office.

When [[Kasputin Yar]] rejoined with us in the new Union, [[Lokkai Starworks]], with support from [[SubPro]], was contracted to overhaul the ten battlecruisers now in orbit. The Xi Char were also contracted to partner in this project, with a particular eye towards improving automation. The Darings were notoriously inefficient in terms of their crew compliments which is why the majority had been decommissioned years ago. This cooperation between Lokkai Starworks and the Xi Char actually started while the diplomatic talks to bring Charros IV into the Union were still in their infancy."

"Fascinating," responds the anchorwoman. "As I understand though, the Daring-Class are what engineers refer to as kitbashed. Given the Xi Char's well known pursuit of perfection, it seems like a curious choice to involve them with this particular project."

"It was certainly a provocative choice Brackett," replies the Vice Presav. "You have to keep in mind, however, that before Haor Chall Engineering was resurrected, Xi Charrian factories were largely reduced to producing parts for Sienar Fleet Systems, native designs and creativity had little outlet, the deity was most certainly not in the details.

While the Darings lack uniformity in design what they did represent was ten unique opportunities for Lokkai, SubPro and the Xi Char to make manifest their ingenuity and creativity. Presav Tarasar in particular felt that the differing challenges offered by each ship would offer the Xi Char the best opportunity to showcase their proficiency. In many ways it was also an Alpha Test on how the Xi Charrians would integrated into the Union. 

The fact that the treaty to bring them into the Union took months to negotiate was not because of any great difficulty in convincing any party to it of the value of the end goal nor any excessively quibbling over details. Every one involved want to make sure that the transition was a smooth one. The Daring Project proved that the Xi Char would compliment the cultures already existing in the Union."

"And as I understand it that is not the only unique connexion the Xi Char have with this class?"

The Vice Presav nods, "Part of the reworking of the Daring-Class was to change its starfighter compliments. Each one contains a squadron of Mark IV Vulture-Class Droid Starfighter which are produced by Haor Chall Engineering in partnership with SubPro. They also each have a squadron of [[Guardian-Class Starfighter|Guardian-Class Starfighters]], the first starfighter design made for [[Yari|Yaries]] to pilot, which Haor Chall Engineering partnered with Lokkai Starworks on."

She then points at one of the ships on the screen behind them and remarks, "It also so happens that CMS Tenacious is commanded by [[Rear Admiral]] 'ak-'lak, the highest ranking Xi Charrian field officer. He is got a great sense of humour, he has a really good rapport with his crew."

The anchor smiles a little, "We actually have an interview scheduled with Rear Admiral 'ak-'lak as part of our continuing coverage. But we are at the end of our programme for tonight. I want to thank Vice Presav Mos for coming on our show. Stay tuned for more."

== Tenacious ==

The broadcast starts with the image of a Daring-Class Battlecruiser in space identified with the caption CMS Tenacious with the words Defence Group Mikassa underneath and [[Caspian 3rd Fleet|Iron Torch Fleet]] underneath that. After a few moments a voice over indicates, "Starships are often spoken of in terms as if they were a living being complete with their own personality. This analogy is particular prevalent when speaking of military vessels with the captain, officers, and crew combined with the history and quirks of the ship manifesting as something unique, greater than the sum of its parts. When it comes to the Caspian Navy's CMS Tenacious, you find a ship with quite a personality."

Images of the ship as part of ceremonial flotilla around Charros IV are shown on the screen while the reporter continues with her voice over, "The Tenacious now part of an honour guard of sorts around Charros IV is commanded by Rear Admiral 'ak-'lak, he is the highest ranking Xi Charrian field officer in the Caspian Navy who serves as Deputy Commander of Defence Group Mikassa in addition to commanding the Tenacious."

The footage then shifts to show the reporter, Jordaan Mag walking down a corridor with 'ak-'lak. The Xi Charrian admiral bears some paint decorative paint streaks on his carapace while his articulation conversion sphere bears a paint job that looks straight out of a head shop. The reporter asks, "So how did you get the job commanding the Tenacious?" The Xi Charrian translated through his conversion sphere replies, "They actually were trying to reach my flat mate to off him the job, but they got me on the monitor instead and they couldn't tell the difference and here I am."

Returning to voice over the reporter remarks, "With 'ak-'lak one gets the hence there is some hidden kernel of truth behind the joke." The screen shifts to show the Tenacious being worked on at Nenawirth Yards on Kasputin Yar which is identified by a caption on the screen. "The story of the rebirth of the Tenacious begins in nearly five years ago when it was pulled out of moth balls along with the the rest of the Daring-Class to be refitted by a team of engineers from Lokkai Starworks, Sublight Products Corporation, and the Xi Char."

Next the screen goes to an image of engineers of a variety of species working together on replacing some circuitry on the Tenacious under the apparent supervision of 'ak-'lak. The voice over continues, "The chief engineer assigned to lead the refit of Tenacious was none other than 'ak-'lak. Though Charros IV would not join the Union until several months later, 'ak-'lak was given a [[Captain|Captain's]] commission in the Caspian Navy."

An image of the half torn apart bridge of the ship with a bunch of cadets examining the various stations comes on the screen, "In addition to the engineering team, fifteen senior cadets from the [[Groton]] Military Academy were assigned to the ship to provide it a skeleton crew. The curriculum for their final months in the academy was learning to work under a Xi Charrian commanding officer. When the ship returned to active service after Charros IV's entry into the Caspian Union, now Rear Admiral 'ak-'lak was chosen to remain in command. In a somewhat unorthodox move kept the now graduated officers as his senior officers and command crew."

The screen returns to Jordaan Mag and 'ak-'lak in the corridor but now just about the walk onto the bridge. The voice adds, "Over four years later 13 of those 15 original cadets remain with 'ak-'lak on the Tenacious. Sadly [[Lieutenant|Leftenant]] Skazz Magee was killed in a swoop bike accident a few months later, another has since left the Navy."

As the enter the bridge the distinctive sounds of gonkrock music can be heard being played loudly from the speakers. The officers, many of who are recognisable from the earlier images are largely in violation of uniform regulations in one manner or another. The reporter asks the 'ak-'lak, "Not what I would expect to hear during such a sombre occasion." The Xi Charrian replies, "Why should the Gods want silence? We are sad to have lost the Prelate, but he is now rocking it out in the afterlife. Shall we not try to glimpse that?"

Returning to voice over, the reporter relates, "If there is something about the Rear Admiral's personality that seems familiar, it is worth noting he is the cousin of the Union's most popular politician and the odds on favourite to be elected the new Prelate, Dean ak."

Next the screen goes to the reporter interviewing one of the officers in another location, who unlike many of the others shown before has her uniform in complete compliance with regulations. A caption identifies her as Captain Winter Hatawa, Executive Officer. The reporter asks, "What is it like serving under 'ak-'lak?"

The young women replies, "It was very difficult at first. He seemed to possess no military discipline what so ever which I found very disconcerting. As we all got to know him however, he has the amazing ability to identify the talents of the hive and encourage you to perform in ways you did necessarily see in yourself."

"Hive?"

The young women explains, "That is how we refer to our crew. They are not really that different of a concept. When you are serving together on a ship for such a long period of time it really because a very tight knit community working in unison. This takes a certain precedent over other ties, we became the crew became the new hive for our captain, so it is as term of affection to refer to ourselves as such."

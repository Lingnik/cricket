{{Speech|name=Response to Murders|Author=[[Sha Dark]]|date=[[30 ABY]]|place=[[Union Hall]], [[Plaxton]], [[Caspar]]}}

An advertisement for Dragonflower Entertainment's soon to be open Prothium Club on [[Selene]] is interrupted before announcing the date of the grand opening. The screen is replaced with the Vector Apex of the [[Caspian Union]] before that to fades to display a [[Zeltron]] woman in her early twenties sitting behind a desk. The woman is captioned as [[Sha Dark]], Caspian Minister of Justice, [[Union Council|Councillor]] of [[Orleanon]].

"I am addressing you tonight in regards of the recent murders of members of [[Olumi Osahn]] and [[Sarian]] First organisations. The murders have proven increasingly shocking in their brutality with a growing number of our citizens wondering exactly what the authorities are doing about it. Murder is itself not an unheard of crime in our society. Considering Olumi Osahn is classified by our government as a terrorist organisation and Sarian First a related hate group the suspect pool for any crime where they are the victims is rather expansive.

The [[Constable|Constabulary]] in [[Noques Rasala]] has been chasing down numerous leads in the murders of Sojan Olmensin and Relana Garcon. What we have found suggests their personal culpability in aspects of the Mergani Pogroms. As they are not alive to defend themselves we are electing not to go into further details of that at this time, but it does widen the scope of who would have a motive to see them dead quite considerably. Our efforts over the past two years in eliminating those who orchestrated the illegal removal of 300 million Caspian citizens from their homes hampers our efforts to investigate the internal fighting within their organisations. If we knew more about their current leaders and membership they would already have been arrested and prosecuted for their crimes.

Following the murder of Johannes Barquen on his yacht my office assumed jurisdiction of the case and has taken over direct oversight into the investigation of the previous murders. The continuing investigation will be overseen by Councillor Erl Morgaan in his capacity as Constable General. Having watched the so-called confession tape found with Mister Barquen's body, as one of the many forced from their home on [[Mergansar]] by men like him I have little personal sympathy for him. Nevertheless, no one deserves to die in such a gruesome fashion. Nor do we consider confession brought about under vivisection to be particularly reliable.

The press, of course, seems to have fixated on [[Axel Vichten]] as mastermind or perhaps even perpetrator of these crimes. Our timeline on the first killings counter-indicates his direct involvement as he was spotted on Selene at the time by quite a number of witnesses including [[Presav]] [[Crestian Tarasar|Tarasar]] and myself. At this time, however, we still do not know the identity of Mister Barquen's missing passenger who is considered a possible suspect.

In preparing this address I rewatched the footage of Vichten's recent viral broadcast. It certainly is incendiary and with violence swelling up so afterwards it leaves of very open question legally speaking as to whether he might be held culpable in Caspian courts for the violence whether or not he had any further involvement. My office has not ruled out charging him and seeking his extradition though we are not doing so at this time. We are ordering he be detained for questioning should he be found in Union territory.

Some have questioned how seriously we are taking the investigation. I was actually rather disturbed to see a news reporter cut off by his network before he could finish delivering his full accusations against our government. We value free speech and the implication that we cannot handle some bad press is disturbing. Frankly it seems a little hypocritical to admonish terrorists for killing each other when we have happily engaged in killing them when the opportunity presented itself. The nature and brutality of the killings would suggest that indeed terrorist applies as an apt label to perpetrators of these crimes, regardless of their motivation.

This is ultimately where we must draw some line. As a people we have been trying to move past these dark times, the process of reconciliation is slow. To be honest my first response watching the interview with Artemis Cross was to question why the Cross Consortium and its board had not yet been in court charged with any crimes. But, moving forward means allowing room for atonement which this office welcomes with some level of caution. Such reconciliation is put in jeopardy when our officers are subjected to dealing with PTSD triggering murder scenes."

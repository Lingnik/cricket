A '''Rofalssa stalk''' is a silica-based, sinewy, vine-like genus of giant plant native to the planet [[Kasputin Yar]]. These huge stalks are not single lifeforms, but rather symbiotic conglomerates that make simple categorization nearly impossible through the sheer number of possible combinations.

The pillar of a Rofalssa stalk is a single species, a thick and woody sort of fungus that draws upon ground-suspended methane, nitrates, and electrical current. Their roots run deep, even so far as to reach the bedrock and slowly digest it, drawing up the minerals into the core of each thick and cylindrical body segment, and slowly pumping them upwards, to be incorporated into the heavy sheathing that forms the main structural support of a Rofalssa. Curiously, the root masses of these fungal stalks seem perfectly willing to interlace with each other, and even share materials more or less freely. This process can accelerate the growth of a small stalk, allowing it to reach a height at which its symbiotes can access more solar energy more quickly.

Feeding the fungal pillar as it grows taller and thickens, are photovoric upper symbiotes. Taking on a tremendous variety of shapes as they feed off the minerals pumped up by the pillars, some of these plantlike things form broad and flat plateaus, others vast nests of long stalks, and others form masses of globules that grow and replicate and fall off periodically in their great exuberence to gorge themselves on the radiation-rich sunlight.

Linking the upper symbiotes to the lower are vinelike growths that cling to the outside of the stalk, and leech into them to steal small quantities of chemical energy and nutrients. Running clean from top to bottom, these growths steal metallic minerals from the fungus, and gather them into their bodies. They then proceed to use the constant ionic bombardment of the upper canopy to harvest electrical energy, and fuel massive growth of their own. Their current is stolen back by the fungal stalks, and accelerates their growth as well, dramatically magnifying the rate at which the entire stalk increases its height and mass.

The [[Belshal]] are one of many symbiotes involved with the Rofalssa stalk's life cycle, they are among the world's many lifeforms that feed upon the ionization and more unusual chemicals produced by radiation bombardment in the atmosphere, and they tap into the stalks for their fluid and mineral needs. Believed by many offworld xenobiologists to be a key player in maintaining the methane-rich atmophere of Kasputin Yar, a fair portion of the native Yari scientific community views them as bit players, contributing their share to the gas given off by a vast network of other, less visible lifeforms.

=== Behind the Scenes ===
The Rofalssa were intended to be a combination of [[:wikipedia:Baobab|the Baobab tree]] and many types of large coral.

Before I get into the meat of a response, there are two issues present in this discussion that, while somewhat related and intertwined, have no ''bearing'' on each other. The first has to do with the perception of the Wiki, the role of contributors, and the role of administrators (chief editors) as a whole. The following discourse will deal with that primarily. The second is no less important, but can be addressed once the first is understood. This issue has to do with commenting edits/pages/actions. They are related issues, but ''seperate''.

* Point 1: '''''No one owns any one page.'''''
:Articles are established, edited, and maintained for the good of the community, not for any single user or small group of users. Thusly, not only does the entire community have access to the material for reading, but they also have access to the material for editing it so that the resulting material is in a manner that best ''relates to and satisfies the community''. Any material posted is done so under the [[:w:GFDL|GNU Free Documentation License Agreement]], meaning that everyone who accepts said license ''owns the right to use and improve the article''. (see [[:w:Ownership|Wikia's Policy on Ownership]])

* Point 2: '''''Wikia is <u>not</u> a MySpace site.'''''
:Personal pages are not accepted, nor are requests for personal or small-group Wikis fulfilled. The appeal of a Wiki is to a wide range of editors, not a small select quantity (see [[:w:What Wikia is not|What Wikia Is Not]]). With that said, and keeping in mind Point 1, an article concerning a character is ''not'' a repository for "personal" files, content, or material. The article is supposed to be an encyclopedic entry, in a manner as neutral and factual as possible, ''about'' the character or subject, not an interpretation ''by'' the character or subject. Relevant to SW1 MUSH, the ''only'' type of articles wherein bias is allowed is RPlogs, but even these are encouraged to be as straight-to-the-case whenever possible. Because these are articles/logs ''by a character'', the reader assumes that the character is retelling it from their own point of view.

:It is heavily encouraged to use existing articles as examples or forms to follow when creating articles. "Winging it", or employing a format that is not in keeping with what is established for similar articles on the Wiki is confusing to the community, is counterproductive to the efforts of your fellow editors, and draws unwarranted attention to that article when it shouldn't.

* Point 3: '''''Pages should remain <u>publicly</u> editable.'''''
:As per Point 1, wherein no one owns a page, pages should and shall only be protected if they incur a "revision war" or there is material on it that, if altered, compromises a license that is above and beyond the GFDL (see [[:w:Protection|Wikia's Policy on Protection]]).

* Point 4: '''''Assume Good Faith'''''
:This adage is targetted at the Chief Editors / Administrators of the Wiki, but is certainly applied to any/all editors and contributors. The editing process is about ''intention'', not ''action''. Assume that most edits are undertaken to improve the content of the article for the ''benefit of the community''. (see [[:w:Assume good faith|Wikia's Assume Good Faith]])

There are a good quantity of smaller details to consider within the context of creating, editing, and maintaining articles. However, we need a starting point to base off of, and these four points, and their associated "reading material" should be enough for now. I close this segment by stating that I, in my role as a/the Chief Editor, do so manage the articles and materials of this Wiki in a neutral and impersonal manner so as best to preserve the essence of the Wikipedia and satisfy the needs of the community (or audience) who reads this. (Note: I'm not ignoring Issue 2, as brought up by Wrista(?), but will address that at a later time, perhaps on a different talk-page. Or, after this Issue 1 has been sufficiently absorbed.) -- [[User:Hawke|Hawke / Rtufo]] 03:13, 22 August 2006 (UTC)

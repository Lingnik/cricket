[[Image:Silverwood House of Fashion Logo.jpg|thumb|300px|Silverwood Corporate Logo]] The '''Silverwood House of Fashion''' is one of the galaxy's premiere clothing and jewellery design firms. Originally founded by [[Zubindi Hakoon]] on [[:starwars:Kubindi/Legends|Kubindi]] in [[4 ABY]] it has grown in the decades to have locations in several locations across the galaxy.

Silverwood is known for a unique design aesthetic merging influences from various cultures, particularly, [[Kubaz|Kubazian]] and [[Chandrila|Chandrilan]]. It largely eschews attempts to appeal to mainstream [[Human]] culture though it has managed to cultivated fans across cultures nevertheless. Its main product lines are often noted for surprisingly practical traits though they are equally capable of envelope pushing frivolity.

== History ==

[[Image:Zubindi Triumphant.jpg|thumb|Zubindi Hakoon]] In [[4 ABY]], on the strength of an early design portfolio, Zubindi was able was to secure a loan of 30,000 credits from the [[Bank of the Core]] in order to form the Silverwood House of Fashion on [[:starwars:Kubindi/Legends|Kubindi]]. The name of this new endeavour was inspired the by [[:starwars:Silver Forest of Dreams|Silver Forest of Dreams]] on Kubindi, a forest known for it kilometre-tall silver trees that had 20-metre-wide-leaves and rumoured to be a Force nexus. Zubindi's initial fashions were based on traditional Kubazian designs found on Kubindi and [[:starwars:Mustafar/Legends|Mustafar]]. The line was centred around Kubazian skirts, which had previously been a functional unisex work garment worn by workers in the lava fields of Mustafar and other extreme conditions. What Zubindi's loan paid for was the importation of expensive fabrics which he used to innovate the garment into high fashion. Combined with an ingenious marketing campaign which saw his models in cosmopolitan settings in the galaxy, Zubindi was able to not only pay off the loan but establish a permanent store and atelier.

By [[6 ABY]], the Silverwood House of Fashion had been successful enough to expand, and Zubindi made the unconventional choice to open a second location on Mustafar. Though a sparsely populated world, the [[:starwars:Mustafarian/Legends|Mustafarians]] were a Near-Kubaz race and had been a key market for Kubazian clothing exports. Mustafar also happened to be the source of the metal [[:starwars:Xonolite|xonolite]] and was also one of the galaxy's prime sources of [[:starwars:Obsidian/Legends|obsidian]]. Using the Mustafarian outlet to facilitate the streamlined acquisition of these materials and making use of [[:starwars:Kubaz xirkonia|Kubaz xirkonia]] from Kubindi, along with other materials, the Silverwood House of Fashion expanded to include their own line of jewellery. This new line like the original was a smashing success.

[[File:Zubindi_with Models.jpg|left|thumb]] [[8 ABY]] would see Zubindi and Silverwood take a bold new direction. In spite of his core work in promoting native Kubazian design elements, Zubindi drew inspiration from other cultures as well. Prime among these was the fashions of [[Chandrila]], which would become the next site of Silverwood's expansion and the inspiration for its latest line. While few would have consider the marriage of Kubazian and Chandrilan fashion to be natural, Zubindi saw the opportunity to meld elements from the two in designs that continue to be popular over twenty years later. His use of the lustrous and sheer fabrics popular in Chandrilan designs such as [[:starwars:Fleuréline_weave/Legends|Fleuréline weave]], [[:starwars:Shraa silk/Legends|Shraa silk]], and [[:starwars:Tyrian shimmersilk/Legends|Tyrian shimmersilk]] became as much his signature as the Kubazian skirts. This foray into Chandrila would also put Zubindi in contact with a young Caspian expatriate named [[Crestian Tarasar]] who would ultimately become his best friend and most high profile customer. This friendship would later drive many moves Zubindi and Silverwood would make.

For a number of years Zubindi and the Silverwood House of fashion would continue in this manner, challenging the norms of fashion through the mixture of elements from more [[Human]] dominated cultures those decidedly 'alien.' Nevertheless, Zubindi avoided any deeper political message or provocative statements, carefully skirting around a galaxy still in a civil war. The [[Imperial Blitzkrieg]] in [[14 ABY]] would shake up the galaxy, and particularly in its effect upon Chandrila, shake Zubindi from his complacency. The new line Silverwood would develop in the wake of this upheaval was entitled "Galaxy at War" with designs inspired by from a variety of military sources. Still ignoring the current conflict as such, Zubindi's new designs featured homages from sources such as the [[:starwars:Jedi Civil War|Jedi Civil War]] era [[:starwars:Onderonian military|Onderonian Military]] and the [[:starwars:Invasion of Naboo/Legends|blockade]] era [[:starwars:Royal Naboo Security Forces|Royal Naboo Security Forces]]. Reactions would vary widely among the critics and there was much debate on what deeper meaning might be expressed by the line. The official response from Silverwood and Zubindi that the fashion spoke for itself.

[[File:Zubindi_Drinking.jpg|left|thumb]] While Zubindi's status as a civilian allowed him to maintain his business with its locations throughout the galaxy, it had been nearly a decade since he expanded further. In a move that seemed nearly as unexpected as opening up shop on Mustafar, in [[17 ABY]] Zubindi would open the Silverwood House of Fashion's newest location on [[Silver Station]]. A shadowport and gas mining facility on the edge of the [[Dragonflower Nebula]] in the [[:starwars:Doldur sector/Legends|Doldur Sector]] of the Mid Rim, it had been taken over by his friend Crestian Tarasar after fleeing Chandrila following the Blitzkrieg. At the same time Zubindi would produce a bold new line to match his catering to fringe of civilisation: "Exile." The Exile collection would feature designs influenced by cultures who had been driven into to exile in one manner or another such that of [[:starwars:Alderaan/Legends|Alderaan]] and in truly provocative direction [[:starwars:Neimoidian/Legends|Neimoidian]]. With Neimodian culture having been much maligned since the [[:starwars:Trade Federation of Planets|Trade Federation's]] defeat in the [[:starwars:Clone Wars/Legends|Clone Wars]] the choice to elevate their fashion as a galactic exemplar was perhaps the Silverwood House of Fashion's most challenging statement yet.

[[File:Zubindi_Leaving.jpg|thumb]] Following the defeat of the [[New Sith Order]] the galaxy entered into a time of relative peace with open warfare between the Galactic Empire and [[New Republic]] sporadic. For Zubindi this also marked a period of stagnation at the Silverwood House of Fashion. For a time he lacked the yen for expansion and found himself uninspired for a new major collection. For several years he focused more on private contracts and maintaining the standard offerings from his past collections. Finally in [[24 ABY]] as violence in the galaxy began to uptick, Zubindi finally came up with a novel new campaign. Having spent many years on the fringe, he leaned into this vibe with the "Shadow" collection. The new designs relied on [[:starwars:Shadowsilk/Legends|shadowsilk]], a rare foray into synthetic textiles. Rather than do a single major show for his new collection, Zubindi instead opted for a 'guerilla' campaign. Numerous pop-up shows were held on worlds through out the galaxy with little advanced notice and absolutely no regard for whether the world in question had an actual fashion scene.

The next major milestone for Zubindi came, once again, through his friend Crestian. When Crestian returned to his home city of [[Trinumvira]] on [[Caspar]] following the Sariphage [[Report:Sariphage Press Releases|pandemic]] and [[Mergansar|Mergani]] [[Report:Mergani Pogrom Press Releases|pogroms]], he invited Zubindi to follow him and open a new location for the Silverwood House of Fashion there. Zubindi took to this idea with gusto buying an entire city block less than a kilometre south of Crestian's family estate. This would prove to be the Silverwood House of Fashion's largest and most unique location. The individual structures of the block serving as a single interconnected campus of the fashion house, allowing each specialty of Silverwood its own unique display space. Another novelty of the new location was Zubindi's decision to allow the surviving shopkeepers in the block to maintain their own businesses as part of the complex.

[[File:Veruca Violet Black Eyes.jpg|left|thumb|Veruca Violet]] The move to Caspar would see many changes in Zubindi's life. The block he purchased belonged to [[Veruca Violet|Veruca Violet Eleria]], the orphaned heir of the Elerian Nut Company. Following her parents death she had hidden out in a candy shop in the block that her family owned. The she had a surviving aunt in [[Noques Rasala]], the spoiled Veruca Violet had no desire to give up the life of privilege to life with her Aunt [[Andra Eleria|Andra's]] more austere lifestyle. Sensing something special in Veruca, Zubindi struck an additional bargain with her beyond the property purchase. He would take Veruca Violet on as he his 'ward' if she reopened her candy shop and keep her pursuit of material goods to the realm of fashion. The girl found the terms acceptable, and Zubindi now found himself with a surrogate daughter and apprentice in his various pursuits.

== Silverwood Caspar ==

The size of the Silverwood House of Fashion's complex in [[Trinumvira]] on [[Caspar]] allows for each of the various worlds that Silverwood specialises in the fashions of to each have an entire room devoted to them. Additional rooms are devoted to particular materials such as [[:starwars:Shadowsilk/Legends|Shadowsilk]] and [[:starwars:Askajian/Legends|Askajian]] [[:starwars:Tomuon cloth|Tomuon cloth]] as a well as an entire building devoted to the jewellery and accessories collection. Silverwood Caspar is also unique for having a few business that existed prior to the Sariphage [[Report:Sariphage Press Releases|pandemic]] continue to operate as tenants of the complex.

=== Kubindi and Mustafar ===

The [[:starwars:Kubindi/Legends|Kubindi]] room represents the fashion influences found among Zubindi Hakoon's native [[Kubaz]] as well as those of the [[:starwars:Mustafarian/Legends|Mustafarians]], a Near-Kubaz species. As Kubindi is prone to powerful solar flares which has made the world arid while [[:starwars:Mustafar/Legends|Mustafar]] has been left volcanic wasteland from extreme gravitational forces. These environments motivate clothing choices centred around practicality.

At the Silverwood House of Fashion these realities are not forgotten. Items such as glareshades, scoundrel gloves, and the extreme weather skirts for which the house is most famous are instead innovated to maintain their practical use while utilising imported materials to be stylish. as well. [[:starwars:Kubaz xirkonia|Kubaz xirkonia]] and Mustafarian materials such as [[:starwars:Obsidian/Legends|obsidian]] and [[:starwars:Xonolite|xonolite]] are featured heavily.

=== Caspian ===

The cosmopolitan nature of [[Caspar]] has long since seen native Sarian fashion designs pushed into the background. Where Caspian fashion remains distinct is in the use high quality local materials and the need to account for severe weather. The Caspar room highlights designs featuring four of these materials.

[[Asimi]] is plant based fibre that is among the softest and most durable natural fibres in the galaxy though it has never seen a significant export market. It is a naturally yellow fibre but it dyes very well across the colour spectrum.

[[Jullon]] is cotton-like fibre with a high silica content. The cloth is dyeable, and very fire-resistant. The silica content also makes the cloth an excellent insulator from electrical effects, a handy trait given Caspar's stormy nature.

[[Shamira]] is a plant based fibre and with a smooth, almost slick feel, I's a twill woven fabric that is naturally pale orange and only reliably dyed into the red spectrum. It's nearly waterproof, wrinkles easily, and very comfortable.

[[Warton]] is a wool derived from Serrith a variety of boar native to the highlands of [[Mergansar]] used to make insulated clothing. It is slightly fuzzy, can be felted for waterproofing and stand ups against any synthetic for insulation.

=== Bespin ===

The [[Bespin]] room fittingly occupies the site of a former gambling parlour. Mannequins are seated at or standing near the former gaming tables giving the brief illusion of an active casino until one realises that no one is moving.

The hallmark of Bespin fashion are the accessories; cape, cloaks, coats, jackets, necklaces, sashes, and vests feature prominently.

A wide variety of materials are employed including silks such as [[:starwars:Aeien silk/Legends|Aeien silk]], [[:starwars:Nanosilk/Legends|Nanosilk]], and [[:starwars:Sleedaran silk|Sleedaran silk]]; leathers such as [[:starwars:Bantha hide/Legends|Bantha hide]] and [[:starwars:Thafsalnu|Thafsalnu-hide]]; and other materials such as [[:starwars:Spotted hynax wool|Spotted hynax wool]] and [[:starwars:Tarelle sel-weave/Legends|Tarelle sel-weave]]. [[:starwars:Phil-fiber|Phil-fiber]], [[:starwars:Satyn/Legends|Satyn]], and [[:starwars:Velvoid/Legends|Velvoid]] are utilised for some ready-to-wear pieces.

=== Chandrila ===

[[Chandrila|Chandrilan]] fashion represents Zubindi Hakoon's first forays into [[Human]] inspired design. An environmentally conscious people, which permeates their cultural outlook, Chandrilan designs are noted for a graceful subdued elegance.

Typical pieces include mantles, robes, and tunics. Lustrous and sheer fabrics are favoured with [[:starwars:Fleuréline_weave/Legends|Fleuréline weave]], [[:starwars:Shraa silk/Legends|Shraa silk]], and [[:starwars:Tyrian shimmersilk/Legends|Tyrian shimmersilk]] serving as a fabrics of choice. Embellishment is often done in the same or similar colour as the many piece making it both stylish yet at the same time subtle.

Jewellery is a typical ornamentation to a Chandrilan outfit with solid colours typical here as well. [[:starwars:Hanna pendant/Legends|Hanna pendants]] on ropes are the quintessential for women with metallic headpieces as another popular choice.

=== Corellia ===

As a planet known for producing its fair share of roguish characters, the [[Corellia]] room is, perhaps fittingly, not truly a room at all. It is, instead, a long passageway which once connected two of the buildings on the block, though it is not entirely clear whether its function was to secretly ferry goods or rather function as a discrete corridor for servants.

The walls are lined with a mix of what could be considered smuggler's chic and high fashion robes and capes in materials such as [[:starwars:Nanosilk/Legends|Nanosilk]], an expensive black cloth native to the world and [[:starwars:Ottegan silk/Legends|Ottegan silk]], an expensive purple cloth, but also found in green, a colour often favoured by Corellians.

=== Naboo ===

On [[:starwars:Naboo/Legends|Naboo]], clothing is often used a form of social communication. Symbolism is an omnipresent facet of Naboo fashion with accessories, jewellery, and makeup often marking ties to faith, family, history, and the natural beauty of the world. Even colour is used at times to convey messages about ones feelings.

High fashion consists largely of intricately designed cloaks, coats, dresses, headpieces, and robes. These are often embroidered with ornate Futhork calligraphy script used by the Naboo. Even casual attire from Naboo, while more comfortable, looks elegant compared to most worlds.

A variety of materials feature heavily in Naboo designs include [[:starwars:Cerlin/Legends|Cerlin]], a silver fabric nature to the world. Among other popular textiles are [[:starwars:Chersilk/Legends|Chersilk]], [[:starwars:Cyrene silk/Legends|Cyrene silk]], [[:starwars:Onderon silk|Onderon silk]], [[:starwars:Tomuon cloth|Tomuon cloth]], [[:starwars:Trevella cloth|Trevella cloth]], [[:starwars:Veda cloth/Legends|Veda cloth]], and [[:starwars:Zeyd-cloth|Zeyd-cloth]].

=== Neimoidia ===

Because of their obsession with status, the [[:starwars:Neimoidian/Legends|Neimoidians]] placed great importance in clothing. Among their kind, elaborate clothes were specifically designed to assert an individual's personal wealth and rank and also to instil jealousy or envy in others. Not only did their robes, cloaks, hats and collars all have particular symbolic meanings, but so did their fabrics and colors. Ranks and garments were so closely associated that a Neimoidian disgraced by their peers would be stripped of their clothes immediately.

They were particularly attached to their hats, and a popular opinion was that a Neimoidian would never be seen without it unless they were dead. The numerous types of Neimoidian headgear ranged from simple caps that revealed the complete forehead to a wide variety of mitres and crowns, some of which even comprised wig-like ornaments.

Since the end of the [[:starwars:Clone Wars/Legends|Clone Wars]] many Neimoidians began adopting the culture of their baseline [[Duros]] cousins and fewer left their homeworlds. At the Silverwood House of Fashion, however, they are still considered a touchstone of fashion excellence.

The [[:starwars:Neimoidia/Legends|Neimoidia]] room somewhat fitting occupies a building which served as a bar called the 'High Hat.' The former bar itself is used to display the wide range of Neimoidian styled headgear available with the liquor cabinets converted for further display and store of the same. The hat selection in this room exceeds that of any of the others.

=== Onderon ===

The [[:starwars:Onderon/Legends|Onderon]] room somewhat fittingly occupies a building which served as a stable centuries ago. The smells and sounds of that experience have long past but the overall structure of individual stalls can be glimpsed in the layout.

Silks predominate here, naturally purple [[:starwars:Onderon silk|Onderon silk]] chief among them. Other silks such as [[:starwars:Aeien silk/Legends|Aeien silk]], [[:starwars:Cyrene silk/Legends|Cyrene silk]], [[:starwars:Demicot silk|Demicot silk]], [[:starwars:Lashaa silk/Legends|Lashaa silk]], [[:starwars:Septsilk/Legends|Septsilk]], and [[:starwars:Shraa silk/Legends|Shraa silk]] help fill out the colour palette.

Decorative accessories are a defining characteristic of Onderonian fashion. There are stalls filled with ascots, capes, headscarves, sashes, shawls, tabards, and tippets. Even the more standard items such as shirts, skirts, and pants tend to be embellished.

Onderonian fashion served as a central influence to the redesign of [[Caspian Defence Force]] uniforms by Crestian Tarasar and Zubindi Hakoon.

=== Pantora ===

The most immediately striking feature of [[Pantoran]] fashion is the colour palette it employs. Outfits, often of [[:starwars:Pantora-silk|Pantora-silk]], generally range in various shades of blue, grey, purple, and red, with purple predominate. White and yellow are often employed in accessories, embellishments, and jewellery.

The outfits themselves are often quite elaborate often high collared with ornamental belts and sashes, gloves and headgear are also typical.

=== Shadowsilk ===

Zubindi Hakoon has a great aversion to utilising most synthetic fabrics. Exceptions are made for materials which greatly enhance the practical applications available for his design. Prime among these is [[:starwars:Shadowsilk/Legends|Shadowsilk]], a fabric designed by [[:starwars:Ayelixe/Krongbing Textiles/Legends|Ayelixe/Krongbing Textiles]]. It is most known for its ability to absorb light and dampen sounds made by contact with the material. Making it ideal for stealth applications in low light or nighttime conditions.

Tucked away in a cellar is a space devoted to displaying sample shadowsuits and other accessories made from Shadowsilk. Its existence is not well advertised.

=== Tomuon Cloth ===

One of the most important figures in an [[:starwars:Askajian/Legends|Askajian]] tribe was the weaver, who knew the secret techniques to create [[:starwars:Tomuon cloth|Tomuon cloth]]. Tomuon cloth is a strong, yet soft and wrinkle-resistant fabric that is a high-demand luxury item as far away as the Core Worlds. It was rumoured that even [[:starwars:Palpatine/Legends|Palpatine's]] robes were made of Tomuon wool. As this was the only export the Askajians had, their tribes had been known to fight wars over the services of especially skilled weavers.

The Silverwood House of Fashion line for [[29 ABY]] heavily featured Tomoun cloth with the Askajian weaver Yara Gorgon being in residence. Rumour has it that Zubindi Hakoon instigated a clan war on [[:starwars:Askaji/Legends|Askaji]] in order to instigate her emigration from her homeworld. Rumours also link the two romantically.

Tomuon cloth has also been employed in uniform jackets for senior officers of the Caspian military in the redesign by Crestian Tarasar and Zubindi Hakoon.

=== Jewellery ===

Like the clothes, the jewellery expresses a wide range of planetary influences in both design in material. Nevertheless certain patterns run strong with gems from [[:starwars:Kubindi/Legends|Kubindi]] such as [[:starwars:Kubaz xirkonia|Kubaz xirkonia]] and [[:starwars:Mustafar/Legends|Mustafarian]] materials such as [[:starwars:Obsidian/Legends|obsidian]] and [[:starwars:Xonolite|xonolite]] featuring heavily. One can kind find everything here from [[Chandrila|Chandrilan]] [[:starwars:Datadagger|datadaggers]] to [[:starwars:Corellian love stone|Corellian love stones]] to [[:starwars:Geonosian/Legends|Geonosian]] [[:starwars:Iketa stone/Legends|iketa stones]] to [[:starwars:Hanna pendant/Legends|Hanna pendants]] to [[:starwars:Touranian jumping-stone|Touranian Jumping-Stones]] and everything in between.

=== Grant's Star Tours ===

Recognisable from its blue Wansook pine door on the western side of the block, '''Grant's Star Tours''' is one of the more peculiar businesses in the city. At its most basic it is a bookstore, but one which only carries travel related publications. As an extension of this identity it has become a travel agency of sorts serving as a port of call for those planning trips into the wider galaxy. The store also contains an impressive collection of modern art on display which clearly could not have been financed through the business, the business itself, generates.

=== Out of the Past ===

Located on the western side of the block, ''''Out of the Past'''' was the name of the store. Its products consisted of memories: what was prosaic and even vulgar to one generation had been transmuted by the mere passing of years to a status at once magical and also camp. The great upheavals both on [[Caspar]] and through the galaxy in recent years and decades have added new categories of stock. Memorabilia only a few years old, now serves at witness to an era passed.

=== Tom's Hair ===

A neon sign on outside the second storey of one of the more patched up buildings depicts a bald [[:starwars:Umbaran/Legends|Umbaran]] male captioned with the words, ''''Tom's Hair Emporium.'''' The salon appears to occupy space that was once an apartment. It is relatively small and intimate setting that has three chairs for customers. Nevertheless the equipment and furnishings are all quite modern, equal to that of any salon in the Core. Customers swear by him, whether that is because of the quality of his work or his ability to influence his customers is a matter of debate.

=== Veruca Violet's Candy Land ===

Prior to the Sariphage the Elerian Nut Company operated a confectionery on the northeast corner of the block. When the company chairman and his wife died their particularly spoiled preteen daughter [[Veruca Violet|Veruca Violet Eleria]] retreated here hoping to maintain her decadent lifestyle as best she could.

When the dust had settled and Zubindi Hakoon was buying the block he found Veruca still here, hiding from the likelihood of ending up in the custody of her Aunt [[Andra Eleria|Andra]] in [[Noques Rasala]] and the far more austere lifesytle she embraced.

Zubindi offered her a deal, he would arrange for her to become his ward if she reopened her candy shop as part of his complex and curtailed the pursuit of material goods that had dominated her life previous, excepting naturally fashion.

Now '''Veruca Violet's Candy Land''' serves as an integral part of the complex personally managed by Veruca herself who has also begun learning the side management of the fashion empire.

She has sworn off measuring her life in terms of things choosing instead to collect friends, including her own 'ward' a [[Squib]] orphan she calls Charlie who serves as a clerk. However, fitting her previous materialism and her new life as Zubindi's foster daughter, the [[Pantoran]] teen possesses a wardrobe worth a small planet.

== Caspian Defence Force ==

The current uniform designs for the [[Caspian Defence Force]] are the result of a colloboration between [[Zubindi Hakoon]] and [[Caspian Union|Caspian]] [[Presav]] and [[Sar Admiral]] [[Crestian Tarasar]]. The basic design is based on [[:starwars:Jedi Civil War|Jedi Civil War]] era [[:starwars:Onderonian military|Onderonian Military]], which Zubindi had previously referenced in his "Galaxy at War" collection in [[14 ABY]].

For high ranking officers the uniform consists of a [[:starwars:Tomuon cloth|Tomuon cloth]] jacket with [[:starwars:Silkweed|silkweed]] piping and [[:starwars:Xonolite|xonolite]] buttons, a shoulder-length [[:starwars:Tyrian shimmersilk/Legends|Tyrian shimmersilk]] tippet, Tomuon cloth pants, black [[:starwars:Leatheris|leatheris]] boots, and Tyrian shimmersilk Kubaz Scoundrel Gloves. For the [[Caspian Navy]] the jacket and pants are viridian, for the [[Caspian Starfighter Corps]] they are sky blue, and for the [[Union Marines]] they are forest green. They are produced by the Silverwood House of Fashion itself. For lower ranking officers and enlisted personnel the uniform design is similar but uses cheaper materials and is contracted out to industrial clothiers.

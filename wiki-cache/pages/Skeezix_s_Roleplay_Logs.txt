|logtitle = [[RPlog:The Prodigal Son|The Prodigal Son]]
|author = [[Paul Nighman|Nighman]]
|date = Unknown
|synopsis = After Molari tries to confront Paul over the fact that he is using is work to escape his emotional life, Paul is forced to consider his mentor's words carefully and after a long walk on the beach, comes to the realization that he doesn't have any idea what Shenner is really feeling, and no idea what he is feeling for that matter either. However, he avoids probing his own emotions too deeply by deciding that regardless of how he feels, if he were to get involved with Shenner, he would only end up hurting her. He decides that Shenner really needs to make a more informed decision on what it is that she wants and from a more balanced place. Essentially he decides that he is just the wrong man for her, and the right thing to do is to let her go. However, he also realizes that hiding at the Academy will accomplish nothing, and so the next evening he heads out, returning to the barracks and Shen.}}

{{Template:Logsum
|logtitle = [[RPlog:Return of the Prodigal Son|Return of the Prodigal Son]]
|author = [[Paul Nighman|Nighman]]
|date = Unknown
|synopsis = On the day after Paul and Shenner's huge confrontation, Paul made breakfast for the girl. While he noticed that she spent much of the meal quietly eating and giving him surreptitiously glances, things seemed for the most part, back to normal. Of course he knows that everything is -far- from normal, but Shenner seems stable, and while she can't seem to take her eyes off of him, she seems much recovered from the previous night. Paul lets her go, still a little concerned, but unwilling to bring up the topic again and repeat the previous nights fiasco. Once she has left though, Dr. Canto corners Paul into having a very hard discussion, in which he proves himself to be Paul's friend by telling him all of the things that Paul least wants to hear. Most important of all the topics they discuss, Molari forces Paul to realize that he has spent his entire life running away from relationships just as fast as he rushes into them, never allowing anyone too close while at the same time constantly yearning for a connection. Paul finds himself with the dilemma of realizing that being close to people means taking a risk ... the question being, is it worth the risk?<br><br>On the next day, Paul develops something of an infection, and Molari finally insists that Paul see a doctor, or he can find somewhere else to bunk for the night. Paul reluctantly agrees, deciding that this would be a good time to head into town anyway and get some new clothes, a guitar, and see if he can't find Luke. He manages to find the first two, but it's Trouble that finally finds Paul.}}

{{Template:Logsum
|logtitle = [[RPlog:A Cunning Plan|A Cunning Plan]]
|author = [[Paul Nighman|Nighman]]
|date = Unknown
|synopsis = Having returned to the barracks with Jessalyn the previous night, Paul makes the decision to stop running and try to commit to friendships and caring about people, as well as letting them care for him. It is not an easy decision, as it goes against years of training, but Paul is nothing if not stubborn. Seeing Shenner again, Paul comes to the unpleasant realization that the kid is probably not as "okay" as he thought she was. Her hands have been suspiciously mangled by an eight hour guitar playing marathon, and when she brings up the fact that she is thinking of joining the army, Paul begins to worry that both of these events are due to the night on the cliff. However, he also believes that since he has released her in a sense, that he has no right to tell her what to do with her life, no matter how much he would like to. He doesn't know how to handle the situation. The next day, after having picked up his belongings from Dr. Canto's house, Paul spends most of his time outdoors, playing his guitar and thinking about the upcoming changes in his life. However, when a certain General shows up, he suddenly conceives of a cunning plan ... but there's a saying about best laid plans.}}

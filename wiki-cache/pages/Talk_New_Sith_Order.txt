==General Question==

Quick question- I know that the MUSH doesn't want to be too slavish in accommodating the EU, but would the NSO's core philosophy be more like the "rule by the Strong" Sith under Kaan, or "rule of One" under Darth Krayt? [[User:Quietus|Quietus]] 06:06, 26 December 2007 (UTC)
*It's neither, as far as I can tell. Vadim is the supreme dark lord, but he's not exactly a Sith... Darth Malign is his "apprentice" who in turn has Dareus as his apprentice. Malign calls himself a Sith and acknowledges he's Vadim's subordinate, but it's Malign who founded the New Sith Order. But Vadim isn't actually a part of the NSO. It's confusing to me. The NSO itself only has two Dark-siders, Malign and his apprentice, Dareus. The rest are just non-Force users, soldiers and crew and the like. While this isn't necessarily the truth, but in my opinion, this NSO is only Sith in name... they don't seem to represent the ideals of traditional Sith (orders of Naga Sadow, Revan, Kaan, Bane, etc). While they are evil, Malign seems like a quasi-Sith in that he seems to speak out against Sith-like tactics and Vadim is just a powerful dark side user. But I don't know. -[[User:Danik Kreldin|Danik Kreldin]] 22:17, 26 December 2007 (UTC)

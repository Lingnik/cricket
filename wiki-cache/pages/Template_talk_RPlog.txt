Thanks! I think a compromise for "Date" might be '''Year, Quarter'''. There's folks that have logs that overlap with others, or the RP takes place over several weeks, but only depicts a day, etc. To try and be hyper-specific as to exact day and stuff, that's asking alot. But if we can at least narrow it down to which quarter, I think readers can place it fairly well in their understanding of the timeline. So, '''Date''' would look like &mdash; [[14 ABY]], '''3rd Quarter''' -- [[User:Hawke|Hawke / Rtufo]] 18:07, 14 April 2006 (UTC)
* <p>I was thinking that RL time probably doesn't matter so much.  There's shouldn't really be a difference to the story or the scene if it played out over a single session or was spread out over weeks.  While it's somewhat interesting to be able to place a scene in RL time, it's IC time that matters most.</p><p>As for the date issue.  A scene usually occurs over a relatively brief span of IC time.  We've got IC months integrated into the MUSH now.  I think it would add some flavor to the logs if we used '''IC Month, IC Year''', e.g. ''Selona, 14 ABY''.  Going back and retroactively assigning that specific time frame to older logs wouldn't be necessary, but I think it's a good go-forward standard. -- [[User:Xerxes|Xerxes]] 18:26, 14 April 2006 (UTC)</p>

==Next Speech / Previous Speech==
Why do we not want a way to determine the chronological order of the speeches delivered by a given person within a given year?  It would make more sense to me, personally.  Even if it doesn't happen at the moment, there's certainly the ''potential'' for a number of speeches to be given in a flury of political activity.
:--[[User:AdmiralQuar|AdmiralQuar]] 02:56, 30 May 2006 (UTC)

*Perhaps it's best not to have the previous / next links.  While there is the potential for a series of speeches, it's not the common case.  Most of the time, you're going to have a limited number of major addresses by a limited number of people.<br/>Also, there isn't the same logical connection between one speech and the next -- they can be completely unrelated, e.g. a speech on the Galactic Civil War followed by a speech on taxes and trade policy followed by an address espousing the wisdom in eating your vegetables.  Battles on the other hand have a more logical flow.  Conflicts in a war are all related and one battle can set the stage for the beginning of the next.  That's my opinion anyway. -- [[User:Xerxes|Xerxes]] 12:18, 31 May 2006 (UTC)

*I was inclined to have the same opinion as Xerxes. I don't think there will be a substantial number of speeches to warrant a Previous/Next link section. If you'd like to link to a previous / next speech in a series, I'd encourage you to do so within the article/page itself. -- [[User:Hawke|Hawke / Rtufo]] 16:52, 31 May 2006 (UTC)

The '''''Tethys''''' is a [[Warlord-class Medium Cruiser|Warlord-Class Cruiser]] which, along with its sister ship, ''[[Starkiller]]'', has been indefinitely seconded from the [[Imperium]] to the service of [[Crestian Tarasar]] in his capacity as a [[New Sith Order|Sith]] [[Duke]] and [[Admiral]]. In operational terms it is treated as property of [[House Dragonflower]] and seconded to the [[Caspian Navy]] along with the [[Interdictor-class Immobilizer 418|Interdictors]] ''[[Leviathan]]'' and ''[[Dying Sun]]''.

== Background ==

In [[29 ABY]], [[Tyler Damion]], otherwise known as Darth Malign, the [[Dark Lord of the Sith]], reemerged publicly and declared himself the new leader of the [[Galactic Empire]], now rechristened as the [[Imperium]]. Concurrent with the rise of Imperator Damion was the campaign of [[New Sith Order|Sith]] [[Duke]] and [[Admiral]] [[Crestian Tarasar]] for election to the permanent [[Union Council]] of the [[Caspian Union|Caspar Union]].

When Crestian first arrived on Selene after Malign's takeover, he walked into what became a matter of great family drama. Former [[Caspian Democratic Union|Caspian]] [[Presav]] [[Elana Tracer]] had arrived, having tracked down her missing eldest son [[Kipran Jawatha]], a [[Force|force user]], who has sought out Malign to be trained, and had joined the Sith. Concurrently Malign's half-sister [[Johanna Siri te Danaan]] had come after her own [[Jedi Knight]] son, [[Etiel te Danaan]], who was now serving the Imperium as [[Moff]] of [[O'paal]] and [[Byss]].

Much to Crestian's surprise, this impromptu family reunion saw Malign assign a trio of [[Warlord-class Medium Cruiser|Warlord-Class Cruisers]], ''Imperial Edict'', ''[[Starkiller]]'', and '''''Tethys''''', to reinforce his flagship, the ''[[Leviathan]]''. Kipran was assigned by Malign as commanding officer of the ''Imperial Edict''. Crestian's own hopes to offer command of the [[Caspian Starfighter Corps]] to Former Presav Tracer, following his upcoming election, were dashed by Malign later offering her the roles of [[Moff]] of [[Selene]] and control of the [[Kuat Drive Yards-Sienar Fleet Systems]] shipyards, positions he ironically had imagined he would have been offered had he not returned to [[Caspar]] instead.

As Kipran's training as an apprentice to Malign progressed, the ''Imperial Edict'', would be reassigned back to Imperium service in '''Task Force Shadow'''. The ''Starkiller'' and ''Tethys'' would remain with Crestian and assigned to the '''[[Caspian 4th Fleet]]''' after he became Presav and [[Sar Admiral]]. Though strictly speaking, neither ship was truly necessary, as Crestian now commanded the entire [[Caspian Navy]], given the Imperium's vast resources, the Imperator has never requested their return. Both vessels are now effectively treated as property of Crestian's [[House Dragonflower]], which also provides the Union with the use of the [[Interdictor-class Immobilizer 418|Interdictors]] ''[[Leviathan]]'' and ''[[Dying Sun]]''.

== Captain, Crew, and Compliment ==

As a recent construct, Crestian was in the position to be able to appoint many of the officers of the ''Tethys''. For commanding officer, Crestian made an unconventional choice. [[Captain]] Shelshasa, was an [[:starwars:Arbiter/Legends|arbiter]] from [[:starwars:Manaan/Legends|Manaan]], named after the [[:starwars:Jedi Civil War|Jedi Civil War]] era figures [[:starwars:Shelkar|Shelkar]] and [[:starwars:Shasa|Shasa]]. At some point during these years after the New Sith Order fell, Shelshasa came to [[Silver Station]] in order to negotiate the extradition of a smuggler who was wanted on Manaan for murder. Crestian was impressed by the [[:starwars:Selkath/Legends|Selkath]] and delighted at the presence of someone with a background in law as neither shadowports nor the Sith tended to attract such types.

Once the case that brought her to Silver Station had concluded, Crestian offered Shelshasa a position as a lawyer for Dragonflower Enterprises. Considering her own career prospects back in [[:starwars:Ahto City/Legends|Ahto City]] to be dull and limited, she accepted his offer. Over the years she rose to be General Counsel, and one of the ubiquitous [[Commander|Commanders]] serving the martial forces of House Dragonflower. In choosing Shelshasa to command the ''Tethys'', Crestian felt she was someone who could faithfully and effectively navigate the complexity of its role at the crossroads of Caspian, Imperium, and Sith service.  

Most of the other senior officers of the ''Tethys'' would be drawn from the ranks of the ''Leviathan'', with Crestian taking the opportunity to elevate many officers who had been stalled in advancement during ''Leviathan's'' time in hiding. Among these, Crestian and Shelshasa would choose several officers, who like Shelshasa, were unconvential picks for their posts. Though most of the initial crew would come from the Imperium, as a vessel in Caspian service, subsequent vacancies would be open for Caspian personnel to fill.

As a ship that would be often selected for high profile assignments, postings to it are generally popular with many Caspian officers viewing service on the ''Tethys'' as a good opportunity for advancement, in spite of its status as a Sith ship. Over the years since, ''Tethys'' has slowly grown to be more Caspian in character, though in a number of cases, officers who came by way of the Caspian Navy have come to identify with House Dragonflower. Shelshasa's reputation for impartiality has proven to be integral in bridging the divide.

''Tethys'' is host to '''Harbinger Squadron''' and '''Intruder Squadron'''. Along with Shadowblade Squadron and Longhaul Squadron on the ''Starkiller'', these are the only [[TIE Avenger|TIE Avengers]] serving in the Caspian Starfighter Corps. As [[Sar Admiral]], Crestian felt placing Imperial designed craft on mainline Caspian warships would be bad for morale. ''Tethys'' is also host to three companies of [[Union Marines]].

== Service ==

As part of '''Task Force Renaissance''' of the [[Caspian 4th Fleet]], the ''Tethys'' has participated in various of its operations over the years including the [[Report:Mergani Subjugation Posts|blockade]] of [[Mergansar]]. It has also engaged in smaller scale assignments. In [[30 ABY]], following various attacks by slavers against targets across the galaxy, the ''Tethys'' headed up a flotilla, which included ''CMS Hammerhead'' and ''CMS Mako'', sent to [[Tatooine]]. As the homeworld and seat of power of Johanna Siri te Danaan, a key ally to the Union, ensuring Tatooine was not vulnerable was key to Crestian's strategy in dealing with the [[Hutt|Hutts]]. Though not without ships of her own, Crestian felt Tatooine would benefit from added support, particularly as he feared it might be counterattacked in response to any allied operation against [[Hutt Space]]. Shelshasa would be promoted to [[Fleet Captain]] as part of this assignment.

In [[33 ABY]], elements of the 4th Fleet participated in the [[Caspian Intelligence Service]] mission code named "Operation Requiem." The ships involve operated in the [[Faylar|Faylar System]] as a fictional Imperium "'''Task Force Faylar'''." Each ship involved had its transponder changed to register as an Imperium vessel, with the ships themselves being the fleets four Imperial made warships. The ''Leviathan'' operated as the the ''"Spire of Victory,"'' the ''Dying Sun'' as the ''"Oubliette,"'' the ''Tethys'' as the ''"Reborn Order,"'' and the ''"Starkiller,"'' as the ''"Black Talon."'' Fleet Captain Drorq served as commanding officer for the task force, while Captain [[Atsvara Tarasar]] served in place of her father as acting commanding officer of the ''"Spire of Victory."''

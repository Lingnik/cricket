'''Thomas Keyyaw''' ([[39 BBY]] - ) is a [[Human]] politician, business executive, and philanthropist from Lansdowne on [[Krittain Major]] which he represents in the [[Union Council]]. He is the longtime owner of the Lansdowne Crimson Shoes [[:starwars:Phaseball/Legends|phaseball]] team.

Descended from business executives on both sides of his family, Keyyaw had an upbringing of material comfort. However, his father died while he was still an infant, and his mother when he was a teenager. He was subsequently adopted by an uncle who died a few years later, leaving Keyyaw a considerable fortune.

Once Keyyaw was older enough to access his fortune he used it to buy the Lansdowne Crimson Shoes phaseball team. The team had previously been owned by his uncle, though the team had been sold as part of his will. Under his ownership the team managed to improve its record considerably but has failed to deliver any championship wins. The team has also become publicly associated with several medical charities in the city.

Following the collapse of the [[Caspian Democratic Union]] and the death of the [[Krittain|Krittish]] delegation in the [[Union Assembly]] emerged as a candidate to represent Lansdowne in the new Krit Union. Though he ultimately succeed in his election, he courted some controversy in terms of his stance on Krittish citizenship. To that point to be a citizen of [[Krittain Major]] one had to be descended from citizens, which by this point left over 40 percent of the planet's residents as non-citizens. During the election Keyyaw stood firmly on the side of keeping this exclusionary practice, though it was facing increasing opposition.

As part of the treaty which brought the Krits into the new [[Caspian Union]] in [[30 ABY]], the Krittish Councillors would come from the existing Krittish leadership, but subsequent elections would enfranchise all those who had been residents of [[Krittain Major]] and [[Krittain Minor|Minor]] to this point. In the [[Union Council]] Keyyaw has sat as a member of the [[Legacy]] party. In a chamber usually known for its cordiality, Keyyaw has been known as a vocal opponent of a number of his fellow Councillors, particularly Councillor [[Zaalbacca]].

Keyyaw's pro-business attitudes put him at odds with the pro-labour attitudes espoused by many of his colleagues. And while he has been known to be a philanthropist in private, he has been known to take issue with any government spending that could be considered charity. His opposition to the full enfranchisement of the Krittish population has also served to make him unpopular with a number of Councillors as well.

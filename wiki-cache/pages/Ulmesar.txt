'''Ulmesar''', [[Sarian (language)|Sarian]] for Prime Strength, is a political party that was formed to contest the [[Caspian Union|Caspian]] [[Report:Caspian Election Posts|elections]] for a permanent [[Union Council]] in [[29 ABY]]. They did not win any seats during the initial elections nor in any election since. Their failure to win any seats on the Union Council means they are the only major party in the Union to not be part of the governing coalition though it is doubtful they would be invited into the coalition if they had.

== Background ==

Following the dissolution of the [[Caspian Democratic Union]] following the Sariphage [[Report:Sariphage Press Releases|pandemic]] and Mergani [[Report:Mergani Pogrom Press Releases|progoms]] [[Caspar]] was left in a shambles. Not only was a massive amount of work necessary to support the survivors of the pandemic and the refugees from [[Mergansar]] who had settled, but a new government needed to be established. The new [[Caspian Union|Caspar Union]] was a rump state of what was left after other worlds declare independence, leaving the only Caspian worlds with any significant population [[Mikassa]], [[Kichnar Orbital Station]], and Caspar itself. As the old Union had been conceived around its multi-world membership, the new Union had to do more than simply fill vacated posts, a new government model itself needed to be agreed upon.

A Provisional Assembly was soon organised with representatives from Caspar's seven major cities. The representatives were largely a mix of retired members of the [[Union Assembly]], local leaders, academics, and some representatives from the refugee community. Notably, none of those later elected to permanent [[Union Council]] served in the Provisional Assembly. In many cases, those with the most power and influence were too busy actively directing rebuilding efforts to spend time arguing over a new constitution.

There had been no political parties in the old Caspian Democratic Union, but distinct camps did begin to form within the Provisional Assembly which would subsequently give rise to the political parties that would ultimately contest the election once the new constitution was agreed on and ratified. Ulmesar emerged as as a [[Sarian]] nationalist party of the same ilk as [[Olumi Osahn]] and Mergansarsolu, the Mergani government at the time, though they were careful to avoid any direct or official ties. Ulmesar was also careful to only for Sarian culture to be dominant and not exclusively the Sarian race.

== Elections on Caspar ==

Initial polling done before the election had Ulmesar taking 2% of the vote, with the vast majority of their support confined to the population, though the had token support among some [[Human|Humans]] and [[Near Human|Near Humans]]. The first-past-the-post voting system offered Ulmesar no chance at winning any seats, though their support was too small to have likely benefited from any other voting system. Essentially the party existed as a protest vote for those who wanted denounced Caspian multiculturalism.

Ulmesar's campaign was complicated by a terrorist attack on [[Kichnar Orbital Station]] during the election returns. The attackers shouted about Caspian purity and called out Ulmesar's candidate Trett Hilo as being a sellout, before a number of them acted as suicide bombers. Ironically Hilo himself had independently decided to engage in a suicide bombing as well, with his fellow candidates only saved by the quick thinking of the newly elected [[Sirlahn Inglet]] who was able to deploy an energy shield between Hilo in the other candidates.

The attack left over a hundred people dead with hundreds more wounded. It was defining moment for Caspian democracy as the suicide bombings, particular Hilo's cast suspicion on Ulmesar. There was no evidence at the time that tied the other bombers to Ulmesar, nor any indication Hilo was not acting along. Outgoing [[Presav|Provisional Presav]] [[Nelivve Lameberchain]] met with the leaders of the other parties who agreed that further campaigning should be suspended in the light of the attack, but that vote should proceed and no action taken against Ulmesar. The election returns for the remaining constituencies did, however, move to more secure locations. In most constituencies they got around 1.5% to 2.5%, though they only got about a half percent of the vote on [[Mikassa]]. They would far little better in subsequent local elections.

== Elections on Mergansar ==

Ulmesar had no real base of support on other worlds as they rejoined the Union, until Mergansar was allowed to start conducting elections. Anyone who was found to be complicit in the pogroms or has served the Olumi Osahn backed regime was barred from running for office. This made fielding candidates who were sympathetic to their cause challenging. In an effort to be more publicly presentable, the Mergani candidates looking to espouse a Sarian centred vision ran under the existing Ulmesar label.

Though Ulmesar managed little impact in the first two elections in Ithaca and Hammerton, they managed to gain a little traction in Charon coming in second place with 14% of the vote, with [[Lijsttrekker|Lijsttrekker's]] [[Philinda Jorgan]] winning with 83% of the vote, while neither [[Legacy]] nor [[Praecet]] fielded a candidate. Ulmesar had little impact in Spanner where Legacy's Yoseph Sarlin and Praecet's Leonidas Trettsia both served on the Durona Workers Union executive committee. The race was very competitive between the two, but Ulmesar and other parties were largely shut out. Sarlin would ultimately beat Trettsia 53% to 40%.

Ulmesar's best electoral performance was in Toresta. It started off as crowded race with candidates from all five parties plus one independent candidate. The race was quickly complicated when Legacy party candidate Volzang Eiresar, who had a 21 point lead in the polls, was revealed to have been complicit in the pogroms. He was soon found dead and Legacy did not field a replacement. Later during a candidates debate Lisal Yutan the Praecet candidate tried to assassinate Ulmesar's Corgan Vir. After Yutan was taken into custody it was revealed she was responsible for Eiresar's death, Praecet also declined to field a replacement for their disgraced candidate. [[Lijsttrekker|Lijsttrekker's]] Nanchia Marook utlimately won with 44% of the vote, but Ulmesar's Corgan Vir managed a very respectable 31%, with [[Holicet|Holicet's]] Brullian Aresko at 24%, and independent candidate Elkanah Temereth at 1%. In a sign of some political reconciliation, Vir was given a [[Captain|Captain's]] commission in the [[Caspian Navy]] and command of the [[Shearwater-class Frigate|Shearwater-Class Light Cruiser]] ''CMS Toresta''.

The final four elections in Anphora, Goshen, Hebron, and Vilarasala were dominated by Holicet and Legacy who each picked up two seats. Ulmesar managed a respectable showing in Hebron and to a less extent in Vilarasala, but continued to be shutout of the Union Council. Ulmesar does still hold a few seats on city-state assemblies on Caspar but hold no power or sway in any. In the Mergani assemblies Ulmesar does considerably better generally holding between one fifth and one third of the seats. 

The cooperation between the other parties is less formal and organised at the assembly level than it is at the Union level, it is sufficient to keep Ulmesar in the opposition. Ulmesar does maintain enough sway in some assemblies, however, to achieve occasional compromises in their favour.

== Platform ==

The name of the party translates to Prime Strength which refers to the espousal of Sarian supremacy. To avoid any government retaliation their position is officially about promoting Sarian culture rather than the species itself, and as such they have the occasional Human or Near-Human member who have been sufficiently assimilated into Sarian culture. Nevertheless, anything having to do with anyone or anything truly alien face opposition from Ulmesar. They are against the Union extending beyond the [[Caspia|Caspian System]] particularly the inclusion of [[Charros IV]] and to a lesser extend [[Ixin]] and [[Kasputin Yar]]. 

Ulmesar is against the Union's alliance with the [[Imperium]] because it supports multiculturalism. They are not, however, explicitly against the idea of potential alliances, but would likely only welcome one with a simliarly minded power. In domestic policy they favour things that allow the promotion of Sarian culture. This usually manifests in efforts to promote the Sarian culture, language, and religion in the schools and the arts. As Sarians remain the overwhelming majority on Mergansar, the Ulmesar assembly members find more room for compromise with other parties as they are dealing with issues largely effecting Sarians to begin with.

Historically the members of the Provisional Assembly who joined Ulmesar had wanted the Caspar Union to recognise Mergansarsolu, the Olumi Osahn backed Mergani government. Likewise they wanted Mergansar to allowed to keep a significant portion of the Caspian Navy after the dissolution of the old Union. Ulmesar opposed the Caspian blockade of Mergansar and all its efforts to force Mergansar into to submission. Since the reintegration has been complete, Ulmesar as not attempted to campaign on Mergani independence. This is not on any ideological ground but rather the recognition that they lost battle for independence decisively and any efforts to revive those efforts would be retaliated against harshly by the government.

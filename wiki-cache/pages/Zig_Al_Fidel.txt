'''Zig Al Fidel''' ([[26 BBY]] - ) is a [[Human]] aristocrat and physician from the city of Feyenoor on [[Ixin]] which he represents in the [[Union Council]]. [[Prince]] Zig is the hereditary custodian of the Temple Al Mahd and by extension is the principle Prince of Feyenoor. As the most experienced physician on the Union Council, he also serves as the [[Caspian Union|Caspian]] Minister of Health, a senior position.

== Early Life ==

[[Prince]] Zig Al Fidel Al Zig Abdulay Al Mahd was born in the city of Feyenoor on [[Ixin]] a few years before the start of the Clone Wars. Though being born a prince had become rather common place by that point in time, as the title passed to all male descendants, the Al Mahd's occupied a position of prestige as custodians of the Temple Al Mahd, one of the ancient relics of a previous civilisation which inhabited the world millennia ago. The position made their family the de facto rulers of Feyenoor. As the only son of his parents, Zig could expect in due course to ascend to the role of hereditary custodian of the temple and principle Prince of Feyenoor. 

Though his family's civic importance and wealth meant Zig would not need a profession as such, his parents encouraged him to train for one anyway. They felt Zig would prove a better leader if he had skills to draw upon that were not confined to the realm of court life, and that working in some field might ensure he would possess a humility that many of the Princes of Ixin lacked.

At the age of ten while travelling with his father their caravan got caught in a storm and were forced to seek shelter with a local tribe far from the city. He found the people noble and hospitable, but was saddened by the meagre means by which they lived. In the short time they were with the tribe, Zig witnessed a young girl die from a condition that would have been easily treatable in the city. It was at that point that he decided he would study medicine so he could help bring modern medical care to all the Ix.

When he came of age Zig attended the [[:starwars:Rhinnal State Medical Academy|Rhinnal State Medical Academy]] in the Core, where he would ultimately graduate second in his class in [[1 ABY]]. As Zig considered his opportunities, he learned of a vacancy at the hospital on [[:starwars:The Wheel/Legends|The Wheel]], a huge space station in the Mid Rim. Thinking it was a perfect opportunity to see a wide range of cases and to establish a life for himself separate from his title, he took up the post in [[2 ABY]]. On The Wheel, Doctor Al Fidel as he was known, gained a reputation for being able to handle the toughest cases that reached the station. He would stay for several years, never letting on that he was a man of importance on his homeworld, and rarely encountering anyone from there anyway.

In [[8 ABY]] his grandfather, for whom he was named, passed away. His father Prince Fidel became the custodian of the Temple Al Mahd and principle Prince of Feyenoor. Prince Fidel requested his son come home so that he might now learn more of the ways of court in preparation of this own eventual succession. Zig agreed to return to Ixin, but only if he could establish his new practice among the outlying communities who were in more need of his care than the those in the city. His father agreed to this so long as his son returned to the city regularly to participate in civic life there.

== Prince of Feyenoor ==

[[File:Zig Al Fidel Alt.jpg|thumb]] Prince Fidel passed away in [[21 ABY]] making Prince Zig now custodian of the Temple Al Mahd and principle Prince of Feyenoor. Zig reluctantly shuttered his medical practice and returned to the city to take his place as its de facto leader. Prince Zig supported the efforts of Prince Abdulay O'Jordaan II of Udalooy to bring Ixin into the [[Caspian Democratic Union]], but he did little concern himself with off world politics at the time. What time Zig had outside of court life he devoted to volunteering in medical clinics in poorer areas of the city.

When the Sariphage pandemic hit [[Caspar]] in [[27 ABY]], Zig travelled to [[Kichnar Orbital Station]] in hopes he might be able to help in developing a cure. As the engineered nature of the disease resisted his own skills he ultimately turned his attention to helping treat the refugees from the pogroms on [[Mergansar]]. When the cure was finally made available he participated in the inoculation programme before returning to Ixin. The twin calamities of the pandemic and pogroms had put an end to the CDU, and though Prince Zig personally would have preferred working to preserve the Union, he was forced to accept that there was not enough will to make that happen.

Back in Feyenoor, Prince Zig concerned himself with doing what he could to help the roughly five million refugees who had settled their from Mergansar, preferring to leave the politics of the new Republic of Ixin to others. When a new government formed on Caspar and the new [[Caspian Union]] sought to bring Ixin back into the fold, Zig was an enthusiastic supporter of the enterprise.

Though he had always eschewed politics as best he could, Prince Zig was encouraged to take on the role of Councillor of Feyenoor in the [[Union Council]]. Given the pandemic that had largely destroyed the previous Caspian government, creating a better health service and medical research industry was a major part of the new government's platform, and many felt that Zig was uniquely qualified to lead that task. Reluctantly agreeing to run for office, and facing no real opposition given his standing as the city's de facto ruler, Prince Zig was elected Councillor of Feyenoor and subsequently appointed Minister of Health by the coalition government.

The Minister of Health is considered to be one of the senior ministry positions in the Union Council. Under the leadership of Prince Zig, the Caspian Health Service is one of the best funded healthcare operations in the Mid Rim with cutting edge research occurring in hospitals and universities on multiple Union worlds. The Caspian Health Service also boasts a significant patient treatment capacity, with Caspian hospitals a frequent destination for nearby worlds facing their own health crises.
